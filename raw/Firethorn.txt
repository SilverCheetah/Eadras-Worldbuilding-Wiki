The Legend of the Firethorn
The very first Firethorn grew in the center of a blasted crater where a Terran druid died in a violent magical explosion. When his fellow druids arrived, they found the flower glowing untouched amid the devastation.
Its petals constantly shift between vivid red and bright yellow while glowing with a soft inner light. Its leaves are dark green, and its black thorns glow with a dim, menacing red.
The thorns contain a fast-acting poison that burns the nerves like fire while destroying the blood from within. When the poison leaves the thorn — either drawn out or injected — the empty thorn immediately self-combusts in a burst of magical flame. This is why it’s called the Firethorn.
Even one thorn causes terrible agony. Two or three thorns are usually enough to kill a human-sized creature. For a full-sized Terran, four or more thorns is almost always fatal.
Yet the flower also holds a miracle. The nectar from its petals is a powerful soothing agent that brings instant relief from even the worst pain or burns. When the thorn poison is carefully mixed with this nectar, it creates the Sovereign Antidote — a universal cure that can neutralize any poison known to exist.
Because of this flower, the Terrans named their empire the Firethorn Empire.
To this day, assassins favor the stabilized thorn poison when they wish to guarantee death, while royal Terran houses protect their greatest treasures with traps designed to drive multiple thorns into any intruder.
