## V. How Each Magical Tradition Fought the Demon War

### 1. The Netharim

**Lirra:**  
The **Netharim** were essential and terrifying during the war. Their magic depends on understanding reality deeply enough to impose a model upon it. Against demons, that created both strength and peril.

**Daj:**  
Strength first.

**Lirra:**  
A Netharim who understood fire, momentum, blood, fear, or space could reshape those things directly. They improvised under battlefield conditions better than formulaic traditions would have. Some learned demonic adaptation patterns fast enough to counter them in real time.

**Nox:**  
And peril?

**Lirra:**  
Demons also learn. A Netharim’s model of reality can become a battlefield. If a demon contaminates the assumptions underneath the model, the practitioner may impose corruption while believing they are healing.

**Nara:**  
So Netharim war colleges developed epistemic discipline.

**Vos:**  
The **Model-Sanctums**.

**Lirra:**  
Yes. Chambers where Netharim tested their internal models against peers, priests, druids, and later Windspeaker ward-readings before major operations.

**Sylph:**  
“Are you certain?” as military infrastructure.

**Shali:**  
Certainty killed Arath.

**Nox:**  
Disciplined uncertainty saved parts of the Demon War.

# The Council of the Elder Gods: Corrections on Magic, Death, and the Anti-Magic Crusade

## I. The True Population of Magic

**Vos:**

**Lirra:**

**Raja:**  
Say the proper order.

**Lirra:**  
Among mortal practitioners, broadly:

2. **Priests and Clerics** follow closely.  
3. **Netharim** are distant behind them.  
4. **Lexanari** are extraordinarily rare.

**Sylph:**

**Nox:**  
The Lexanari deserve the fear.

**Nara:**  
And the Netharim?

**Lirra:**  
The Netharim are feared because they resemble little sovereignties of comprehension. They do not chant formulas or petition gods. They understand, and the world yields. But they are not common.

**Vos:**  
Then wizards are the common magical infrastructure of society.

**Lirra:**  
Yes. Wizards are the broad learned class: trained, literate or semi-literate depending on culture, institutionally numerous, guilded, licensed, employed, taxed, feared, and needed.

**Raja:**  
Practical magic?

**Lirra:**  
Very much. Wards, repairs, heating, preservation, canal locks, messages, measurements, civic rituals, battlefield utility, household charms at the low end, academy-scale workings at the high.

**Vos:**  
And Priests and Clerics?

**Nara:**  
They are nearly as numerous because suffering is more common than scholarship.

**Shani:**  
Healing, funerary rites, blessings, exorcisms, oath-witnessing, childbirth, plague care, harvest rites, repentance, protection, spiritual mediation.

**Shali:**  
And judgment.

**Sylph:**  
And bake-sale supervision, depending on sect.

**Vos:**  
This means the Anti-Magic Crusade could not have targeted only rare specialists. It struck the ordinary functioning of civilization.

**Daj:**

**Lirra:**  
Exactly. If the Crusade convinced people that magic itself was evil, then wizards, clerics, Netharim, Druids, Windspeakers, and even suspected Lexanari all became targets—unevenly, politically, regionally, but within one panic.

## II. Reframing the Orders and Practitioners

**Ioa:**  
Clarify the categories.

**Lirra:**  
The corrected magical landscape should be understood as layered, not as a single ladder.

### Common Practitioners

**Wizards** are the most numerous. Their magic is learned through study, discipline, formula, symbolic systems, techniques, schools, guilds, academies, apprenticeships, and practical traditions.

**Priests and Clerics** are nearly as numerous. Their magic flows through devotion, divine sanction, ritual office, sacred relationship, liturgy, vow, and institutional religion.

### Rare Deep Orders

**Netharim** are much rarer. They reshape reality by imposing deeply studied personal models of reality upon the world. They are not the everyday magical class. They are dangerous specialists, philosophers, scholars, and political actors.

**Lexanari** are almost vanishingly rare. Innate-only speakers of fragments of the Primordial Language. Their magic is spoken, never written, and remains outside Elder God oversight.

### Specialized Sacred Orders

**Druids** remain the devotional sub-priesthood of Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox.

**Windspeakers** remain monastic caretakers of Lirra’s Weave, the defensive and Eldritch-bearing ward-system.

**Vos:**  
That is cleaner. Wizards and clerics form the magical public. Netharim and Lexanari form the terrifying exceptions.

**Nox:**  
And Druids and Windspeakers form sacred-specialist traditions with specific divine relationships.

**Sylph:**  
Which means every anti-magic mob had to decide whether the village healer was holy, wicked, useful, or lunch. History became very local very quickly.

## V. The Weave and Eldritch Flow

**Lirra:**  
Now the Weave.

The previous correction—that the Weave is not the universal source of all magic—stands. But it must be expanded. The Weave is not merely a defensive shell. It carries the majority of **Eldritch** within Eadras.

**Raja:**  
Define Eldritch.

**Lirra:**  
Raw magical potency. Not spellcraft, not divine Favor, not personal model, not liturgical authority, not the Primordial Language itself. Eldritch is the wild luminous substance of magical possibility: the power that can be shaped, invoked, studied, sung, named, filtered, polluted, pooled, starved, or stolen.

**Vos:**  
And the Weave carries most of it.

**Lirra:**  
Yes. It circulates, shields, filters, and contains the majority of Eadras’s Eldritch so outsider entities cannot simply drink the world open.

**Qoxli:**  
Like blood in vessels.

**Lirra:**  
Partly. Like rivers under skin. Like breath caught in a net. Like a storm taught to remember borders.

**Sylph:**  
Lirra, that was almost poetry. Are you ill?

**Lirra:**  
Constantly.

**Daj:**  
When the Weave shattered?

**Lirra:**  
Its Eldritch flows became chaotic. Not uniformly weak. That is important. The Thousand-Year Dimming was not the world becoming evenly less magical. It was the world becoming unevenly, dangerously misdistributed.

**Vos:**  
Some regions dimmed.

**Lirra:**  
Yes. Magical barrens, dead channels, dry wells, wardless territories, deserts where Eldritch fell below sustaining thresholds.

**Qoxli:**  
Some literal deserts.

**Lirra:**  
Yes. Not all deserts are Weave-born, but some are. The land starved of Eldritch, and living systems collapsed or adapted harshly.

**Raja:**

**Lirra:**  
Regions where shattered flows pooled or surged. There are places where Eldritch gathers in literal pools: luminous basins, underground reservoirs, floating marshlights, saturated bogs, singing wells, storm-fed hollows.

**Nox:**  
And not all pools are safe.

**Lirra:**  
Most are not.

Areas where Eldritch flow became thin or absent.

- weak magic;
- failed wards;
- exhausted wizardry;
- divine rites requiring greater effort;
- sickened magical fauna;
- hardened local skepticism;
- anti-magic cultures;
- desertification in some climates.

Possible terms:

- **Dimming Wastes**
- **Eldritch-Lean Lands**
- **Dry Channels**

### Eldritch-Overflow Regions

Areas where shattered flows pooled or surged.

Common features:

- unstable magic;
- spontaneous phenomena;
- mutated ecosystems;
- dangerous magical weather;
- literal Eldritch pools;
- overpowered but unreliable wizardry;
- ecstatic cults;
- predatory spirits;
- Black Hand research activity.

Possible terms:

- **Overbright Lands**
- **Eldritch Pools**
- **Surgefields**
- **Brightmarshes**

Areas where Eldritch fluctuates unpredictably.

Common features:

- clerical rites producing unintended signs;
- Netharim models destabilized by shifting assumptions;

- Druids tracking sickened relations;

**Nox:**
