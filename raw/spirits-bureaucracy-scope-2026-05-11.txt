Spirits — cross-realm bureaucratic scope, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture appended to today's spirits canon thread. Josh established
that the Feywild and Shadowdark do not maintain their own administrative
orders; their shell assignments and promotions are handled by the senior
bureaucracy associated with each end of the bright/dark axis.)

== Canon ==

Assignments and promotions of all spirits are handled by one of two
bureaucracies:

  Bright realms (Celestia + Feywild)  -> Divine Bureaucracy
  Dark realms   (Nyxar + Shadowdark)   -> Infernal Bureaucracy

The Feywild does NOT have its own fey-court bureaucracy. Fey shells
(nymphs, brownies, dryads, sprites, genii loci, etc.) are issued, assigned,
and reassigned by the Divine Bureaucracy of Celestia.

The Shadowdark does NOT have its own shadow-hierarchy bureaucracy. Dark
shells (wraiths, shadow-stalkers, banshees, etc.) are issued, assigned,
and reassigned by the Infernal Bureaucracy of Nyxar.

== Structural mapping ==

The two-bureaucracies-two-realms-each structure mirrors the Eadras Mythos's
bright/dark axis:

- Divine Bureaucracy
  - Primary realm: Celestia (standardised shells, mass-produced, rank-coded)
  - Secondary realm: Feywild (bespoke shells, bound to specific places and
    relationships)

- Infernal Bureaucracy
  - Primary realm: Nyxar (standardised shells, mass-produced, rank-coded)
  - Secondary realm: Shadowdark (bespoke shells, bound to specific places
    and relationships)

The standardised-vs-bespoke distinction (established earlier today) maps
onto the primary-vs-secondary realm distinction. Each bureaucracy must
therefore maintain TWO qualitatively different shell catalogues -- a
standardised one for its primary realm, a bespoke one for its secondary.

== Implications ==

- The Divine Bureaucracy is LARGER in administrative scope than the
  Celestia-only framing suggested. It administers two qualitatively
  different domains.
- The Infernal Bureaucracy is similarly cross-realm in scope.
- The "fey courts" and "shadow hierarchies" referenced as institutional
  orders in earlier framings are RETIRED -- they are sub-organisations
  of the parent bureaucracy, not independent administrative powers.
- Back rooms in each realm are infrastructure of the administering
  bureaucracy:
    - Celestial rest-halls = Divine Bureaucracy infrastructure
    - Dreadharrow locker rooms = Infernal Bureaucracy infrastructure
    - Feywild glade-clearings = Divine Bureaucracy infrastructure
      (Feywild Division)
    - Shadowdark quiet rooms = Infernal Bureaucracy infrastructure
      (Shadowdark Division)
- Shells are bureaucracy-specific, not strictly realm-specific. A
  Divine shell cannot fit an Infernal spirit; but a celestial-shell and
  a fey-shell are both Divine products drawn from a single administrative
  tradition (with different shell modes for the two realms).

== Open canon ==

- The internal organisation of each bureaucracy's secondary-realm
  division (Divine Bureaucracy's "Feywild Division" or whatever it is
  internally called; Infernal Bureaucracy's "Shadowdark Division") --
  whether these are formal departments, integrated sub-bureaus, or
  something else -- is open canon.
- Whether secondary-realm spirits ever rotate to the primary realm or
  vice versa (a fey reassigned to a Celestia post? a wraith promoted into
  Dreadharrow filing?) is open canon.

== This refinement RESOLVES an earlier open-canon item ==

The spirits.md page (created earlier this session) listed as open canon:
"The structure of fey and shadowdark institutions. The Divine and Infernal
Bureaucracies are documented; the equivalent institutional order in the
Feywild and Shadowdark (whether also bureaucracies, or courtly hierarchies,
or something else) is open canon."

This is now ANSWERED: there is no separate institutional order. The Divine
and Infernal Bureaucracies are the answer. The open-canon item is replaced
with the new (narrower) question about the internal organisation of each
bureaucracy's secondary-realm division.
