# HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

This document records why the corrected Demon War material is structurally strong. It focuses on trauma, theological horror, magic-system implications, and the narrative value of the Anti-Magic Crusade chronology.

---

# 1. "SURVIVED, NOT WON" SETS THE TONE

[META] The Demon War works best when treated as survival rather than victory. Victory implies closure. Survival implies inheritance.

This allows the Demon War to remain active in modern doctrine, geography, magic, and politics without requiring constant active demons. The war's residue is not only monsters. It is institutions, fear, Dimming scars, anti-magic prejudice, Woundwatch patrols, and rituals for the Unreceived.

---

# 2. DEMONS AS INTERRUPTION OF DEATH

[META] The Unreceived are one of the strongest horror concepts in the session.

Demons do not merely kill. They can consume body, spirit, and soul, stealing victims from judgment, comfort, recalibration, and reincarnation. This transforms demon fear from physical danger into metaphysical theft.

It also gives Shali and Shani enormous emotional and theological weight. Death itself has been violated.

---

# 3. REINCARNATION MAKES CONSUMPTION WORSE

[META] The correction that souls normally reincarnate deepens the horror. If death were permanent anyway, demonic consumption would be annihilation. In Eadras, it is theft from a cycle.

This makes the Unreceived not simply "dead beyond contact," but stolen from future return. Families mourn not only a person, but the road that person should have continued to walk.

The phrase "known cycle" is important because it preserves mystery without false comfort.

---

# 4. THE ANTI-MAGIC CRUSADE CAUSING THE DIMMING IS BETTER THAN THE REVERSE

[META] The corrected chronology is stronger: the Crusade caused the Dimming, not the other way around.

This makes the Dimming a self-inflicted wound. Fear of magic broke the systems that kept magic safe. The Black Hand's success becomes strategic rather than opportunistic: they convinced civilization to murder its own stabilizers, then let the resulting catastrophe confirm the fear.

This is one of the best political-historical patterns in the setting.

---

# 5. THE WEAVE AS ELDRITCH CIRCULATION

[META] Reframing the Weave as Lirra's Ward and Eldritch carrier preserves both corrections:
- magic is not all powered by the Weave;
- the Weave still matters enormously.

This solves a potential problem. If the Weave were not central at all, the Dimming would lose weight. If the Weave powered everything, the four traditions would flatten. The current model is richer: the Weave carries the majority of raw Eldritch and protects it from outsider theft, while different traditions access magic through different relationships.

---

# 6. MAGIC-USER POPULATION HIERARCHY GROUNDS THE CRUSADE

[META] Making wizards the largest magical class and Priests/Clerics a close second makes the Anti-Magic Crusade socially plausible. It targeted the visible magical public, not only rare specialists.

Netharim and Lexanari remain terrifying exceptions. Windspeakers become strategic targets. Druids survive because they are hard to reach and protected by Fey. This creates differentiated trauma instead of one generic purge.

---

# 7. DRUID SURVIVAL PREVENTS OVER-FLATTENING

[META] The correction that Druids were not broadly devastated is valuable because it resists symmetry. Not every magical group suffered the same way.

Druids survived because geography, Fey protection, and judicial legitimacy made them operationally hard to purge. This creates later resentment from urban wizards and makes Druid testimony less corrupted but less accessible.

It also reinforces the existing canon that Druids are judges of Fey society, not generic nature-mages.

---

# 8. BEAST-FOLK LAUGHTER AS DEFENSE

[META] Recognizing laughter-houses as defensive infrastructure is excellent tonal balance. It lets comedy matter without trivializing horror.

Beast-Folk laughter is not comic relief. It is trauma technology and anti-demonic resistance. It counters despair, predatory appetite, and fear organization. This is one of the places where Eadras's humor becomes world-mechanical.

---

# 9. XAR'VION MUST STAY MORALLY UNCOMFORTABLE

[META] Xar'vion works because he remains unresolved. If redeemed, he becomes too easy. If purely villainized, the Black Hand becomes less interesting and divine betrayal loses power.

The correct frame is: perpetrator, savior, danger, prisoner, propaganda object, and wound.

Tiered disclosure allows different cultures to know different amounts, creating story pressure.
