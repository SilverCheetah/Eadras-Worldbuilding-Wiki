Dao — monitor, not overseer; triple role, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture: Josh established the unified role-framing for Dao's
function within the Eadras Mythos. The existing dao.md page documents
many of Dao's acts in detail; this canon provides the FRAME under which
those acts make a single coherent purpose.)

== Canon ==

Dao is a MONITOR, not an OVERSEER.

He watches; he does not govern. The existing "Hidden Balancer" framing
on dao.md is preserved and extended.

== Triple role ==

His function within the Mythos has three distinct components:

1. ENSURE THINGS RUN SMOOTHLY
   Keep the Mythos's operational machinery from breaking. Intervene
   only at the systems level, never as a god giving commands. The
   Mythos is meant to run itself; his job is to ensure it can.

2. KEEP THE MACRO BALANCE
   The cosmological-scale version of (1). Equilibrium between Mythoi,
   between order and chaos, between creation and consumption. The
   Necessary Shadow doctrine (already on dao.md) is the philosophical
   articulation: chaos must be cultivated, never extinguished, because
   perfect order is stagnation.

3. EXPERIENCE LIFE AS AN INDIVIDUAL SEMI-MORTAL, AND TRANSMIT THE
   EXPERIENCE BACK TO THE OMNIVERSAL CREATOR
   This is the genuinely new addition. The existing "humble guises"
   section on dao.md describes Dao adopting forms like farmer,
   craftworker, tinker, village-elder. The new framing is that these
   guises are not merely INCONSPICUOUS -- they are LIVED. Dao adopts
   them to feel what it is to be one tired person among many, to
   know hunger and gratitude and small kindness and small cruelty
   from the inside. The Omniversal Creator, watching through Dao,
   learns what mortal life is LIKE -- not as data, but as experience.

   The mortals who thought they were talking to the man selling them
   turnips were, in a sense, also talking to the Omniversal Creator's
   reading lens for that day's afternoon.

== Implications ==

- The "humble guises" detail on dao.md gains depth: it is part of
  the Creator's mortal-experience pipeline, not just a concealment
  technique.
- The patience-based method (existing canon) is the monitor's
  natural disposition -- monitors watch over long timescales.
- The Necessary Shadow doctrine fits role 2.
- The Game of the Gods (which Dao made) plausibly fits role 1 (keeps
  the gods busy and prevents overly-direct intervention in mortal
  affairs, which would disrupt the smooth running of the Mythos).
  This is extrapolation, not canon -- Josh has not stated the Game's
  purpose explicitly.

== Connection to today's earlier Game canon ==

The Game-of-the-Gods canon established today gains a possible Dao-side
purpose. From Dao's monitor frame, the Game might serve role 1 (keep
the gods busy, prevent over-intervention) or role 2 (maintain macro
balance by giving the gods a structured outlet) or both. The
gods-don't-know-the-rules canon (the gods learned through millennia
of play, never from instruction) is consistent with Dao's
monitor-not-overseer stance -- he didn't sit them down for a rules
seminar; he just gave them the Game and watched.

This is extrapolation pending user confirmation.
