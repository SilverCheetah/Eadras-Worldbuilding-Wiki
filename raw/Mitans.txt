The Mitan were a race engineered by the Tarran as specialized tunnel fighters and miners. Created through in-womb genetic modification, they were designed to excel at underground combat and excavation in the brutal subterranean environments of their era.
As a child race of the martial Tarran, the Mitan inherited a strong warrior culture. They lived almost entirely underground, tunneling, digging, and mining. Their architecture drew from the earlier Rathar (an older engineered child race), but the Mitan adapted the style away from the crystalline underground libraries and archives of the Rathar toward a more robust aesthetic of stone and steel, richly decorated with gold, silver, and gems — the direct precursor to classical dwarven architecture.
The Mitan are the common ancestor of both modern dwarves and halflings. The dwarves are widely considered the true spiritual successors of the Mitan, preserving their underground lifestyle, mining expertise, and martial tradition. The halflings, by contrast, largely abandoned the warlike ways after the Demon War. They moved to the surface and adopted a pastoral lifestyle. It was during or after the Demon War that the halflings developed "The Change" — their racial wanderlust — which drives many of them to a life of travel and adventure.
While the Mitan as a distinct people largely died out long ago, small remnant populations are believed to still exist in the deepest, most isolated underground holds.
