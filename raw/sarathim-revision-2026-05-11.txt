(Session-capture from a Claude Code session in which Josh shared a wholesale

== Canon decisions from the revision (now established) ==

- The Sarathim Empire endured approximately 5,000 years following the Demon
  War (roughly 10,000ya -> 5,000ya), then continued as a fractured diaspora
  to the present day.
- Ottoman imperial administration added as the fourth named cultural-fusion
  influence (alongside Renaissance Italy, Ancient Egypt, Islamic Golden Age).
- The Sarathim Throne was DIVINE, not merely ceremonial. The Divine Emperor
  or Empress was the living bearer of a sacred imperial legacy.
  (This overrides the older "ceremonial Emperor / merchant-princes had real
  power" framing — the Throne held real political authority, specifically over
  the professional military.)
- Succession strictly hereditary, parent-to-direct-child only. Lateral
  succession forbidden — no siblings, cousins, or collateral dynasties.
- When a ruling line died without direct heirs, the event was treated as a
  divine judgment that the dynasty's legitimacy had ended. The noble houses
  then competed for the throne (wealth, influence, alliances, military support,
  bureaucratic favor). The winner formally severed all ties to their original
  house and became sacred -- no longer aristocratic, of the throne alone.
- Three-way political balance: the noble houses controlled wealth, the
  bureaucracy controlled administration, the Throne controlled the professional
  military. None of the three could fully dominate the others; the empire's
  long endurance is attributed to this balance.
- Private armies (house guards, mercenaries, naval squadrons, arcane
  specialists, private marines, caravan armies) were standard for every major
  house BUT capped at one-tenth the strength of the Imperial military.
  Transgressions were among the few infractions that could result in the
  dissolution of an entire house.
- Nobles were forbidden from serving in the Imperial military command at any
  rank -- not officer, advisor, or logistician. The prohibition existed to
  prevent house infiltration of the state military and to keep the army loyal
  to the Throne, not to aristocratic dynasties.
- Imperial Army and Imperial Navy were maintained directly under the Throne
  and staffed almost entirely by commoners.
- The bureaucracy was a brutal meritocratic apparatus led by eunuchs purchased
  from commoner families. Imperial testing officials traveled the empire
  searching for children with exceptional intelligence, memory, discipline, and
  loyalty. Selected children were brutally educated (literacy, accounting, law,
  administration, diplomacy, logistics, political theory, arcane regulation).
  Successes rose into the administrative elite. Failures entered lower civil
  service.
- Over centuries, the bureaucracy evolved into a political force loyal to
  "the continuity of the empire" rather than to any individual Emperor or
  Empress.
- House conflict idioms: intrigue, blackmail, economic sabotage, marriage
  manipulation, assassination by poison, proxy conflicts, information warfare.
  Open warfare between houses inside imperial territory was technically illegal
  but every other form of struggle was constant.
- Sayings:
    "A Sarathim smiles with the mouth and measures your coffin with the eyes."
    "Empires drown. Houses endure."
- Modern mercantile institutions of the Orden and other trade leagues trace
  precursor systems to Sarathim commercial law, accounting practices, and
  merchant-house structures.
- Modern Sarathim stereotypes (as held by other peoples): sophisticated,
  manipulative, cultured, ambitious, arrogant, beautifully mannered, dangerously
  patient.
- The Divine Throne was lost in the chaos of Sarathis's fall; no formal
  imperial succession has been recorded since. Whether any claimant lineage
  survives is open canon. (This resolves the older "current Sarathim ceremonial
  Emperor" open canon item: the question is no longer ceremonial-vs-otherwise
  but whether any legitimate Divine bloodline persists.)

== Preserved canon (not contradicted by the revision; restored to the merged
   page after the AI draft cut it) ==

- Origin and self-image section: the Velrathi-heir manifesto quote and the
  audience reactions paragraph (how the claim is received by Velrathi exiles,
  Katharim, Beast-folk, and Azure Basin scholars).
- Cultural fusion section in the in-character body (the three fusion influences
  from the old page, now extended to four with Ottoman administration added).
- Magic and Scholarship section (High-Firethorn-derived Crystalmancy + Magitek
  descent; refined illusion, sophisticated divination, elegant arcane
  architecture; sponsorship-of-mages-like-Renaissance-painters; close to the
  front rank of post-war traditions but never as deep as the Velrathi at peak).
- Imperial Decree on charity (still binding among legitimate modern Sarathim
  houses; enforcement now reputational rather than imperial).
- "Great house" ideal (wealth without monument = wealth wasted).
- Sarathim festivals (extravagant Renaissance-Italy mode: public theatre,
  masked celebrations, philosophical and rhetorical competitions, scholarly
  debate exhibitions, lavish feasts).
- Sarathim naming patterns (elegant, multisyllabic, vowel-rich; Velrathi-
  influenced syllables vel-/ar-/eth-; Egyptian/Levantine sounds sara-/neth-/
  kal-; specific conventions still open canon).
- "All but one. (See House Sitharis.)" callout in the noble houses section.
- Wikilinks: bold, humans, firethorn-empire, demon-war, lost-primer,
  crystalmancy, magitek -- all restored to Related pages.

== Pending schema refinement (flagged on the page's Open Canon list) ==

The Sarathim Empire spans approximately 10,000-5,000 ya, which has no dedicated
historical_horizon enum value. Josh proposed a refinement to the enum:

  demon-war   (battle through aftermath; existing value)
  pre-modern  (10000-5000 ya; NEW -- between Demon War aftermath and Dimming)
  dimming     (5000-4000 ya; narrowed from current open-ended)
  modern      (4000-now;  potentially renamed from current modern-era)

Until this refinement is performed (which would touch every page using
historical_horizon), the Sarathim page uses [all] as the closest fit, with the
era-refinement question filed to the page's Open Canon section.

== New OOC pattern adopted (CLAUDE.md updated this session) ==

The Sarathim page's bottom OOC block uses a richer structure than the previous
CLAUDE.md "OOC tonal references block" pattern:

  ### Cultural shorthand        - bulleted list of real-world/fictional
                                  inspirations
  ### Intended civilizational
      feel                      - one or two sentences capturing the tonal
                                  register
  ### Visual motifs             - bulleted list of visual elements

CLAUDE.md's "OOC tonal references block" section has been replaced this
session with an "OOC author-side worldbuilding annotations" section that
describes this richer pattern as the canonical form. The simpler tonal-
touchstones-only form remains valid as a degenerate case (single bulleted
list under the same summary, no sub-headings).

The block preserves the Tome conceit by clearly stepping outside it: the
in-universe subject is not derivative of the cited works; the works are cited
because they capture the *feel* the canon is built around.
