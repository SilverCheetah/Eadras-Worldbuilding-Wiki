Trolls
Trolls were created by the Arathi to be the perfect miners — living geological constructs whose bodies are composed of metallic ores and gemstones.
A troll’s body contains some unrefined ore and raw gems that function like fat or gristle, but the main concentrations of minerals and gems are highly refined, serving as their muscles, circulatory system, sensory organs, and power sources.
Trolls reproduce by budding and have only one gender. They grow continuously throughout their lives and consume stone, metal, and gems as food.
Once they reach the age of Elder (roughly 3,000 years old), they enter a long hibernation cycle. During the Arathi Cataclysm, the original ancient trolls that survived were already in hibernation — this is how they endured the disaster. Most of the active trolls were shattered.
These surviving ancient trolls do not remain permanently dormant. Over the millions of years since the Cataclysm, some of them have woken from hibernation multiple times. While there are fewer than a dozen truly colossal, mountain-sized elder trolls in the world, there are likely hundreds of smaller elder trolls currently in hibernation — ranging in size from large hills to half a mountain.
In the 39,000 years since the younger civilizations arose, no elder troll has awakened. However, the oldest dragons still remember the last time one did — and even they speak of it with visible unease.
The dwarves — who have only existed for less than 39,000 years — view trolls not as people, but as repositories of wealth. When dwarves encounter a lone troll, they will often attempt to kill it and break its body apart to harvest the valuable refined minerals and gems inside. They have also repeatedly mined into dormant elder trolls.
The living trolls have never forgiven these acts. They bitterly refer to the ancient ones as “the Sleeping Ancestors” or “Grandfather Stone,” and consider every such killing or mining operation a grave desecration.
