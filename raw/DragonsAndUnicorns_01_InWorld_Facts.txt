HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT
================================================================

This document captures material from an OpenAI Playground worldbuilding session that predates the current Eadras wiki. The wiki is the authoritative canon. The playground session is a HISTORICAL ARCHIVE.

Contradiction flags used here:
  [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI]    — wiki overrides
  [CANONIZED_AS X]        — kept but renamed/re-mechanized
  [WIKI_EXPANDS]          — wiki retains and substantially adds
  [INTERNAL_CONTRADICTION] — council contradicted itself
  [FLAG_FOR_REVIEW]       — unverified

PERSISTENT RETCONS in this material:
  - Ssar'veth → Kudur-eṭir (folk corruption / canonical Arathi name)
  - Ithren → Ixthal (Arathi mortal name Ašar-eṭir; Ixthal is the post-ascension divine name)

THE LARGEST RETCON IN THIS TOPIC: The playground portrays Ixthal as OPENLY responsible for dragons and unicorns — the council writes scripture praising him as Kindler. The wiki canonizes a different load-bearing fact: Ixthal acted SECRETLY. He amplified natural Source surges into X-class flares in a manner indistinguishable from natural phenomena. The Elder Gods believe dragons and unicorns arose from rare but natural events. Ixthal has not been caught. This act is called the FORBIDDEN KINDLING because it violates the Edict of Closed Genesis (a consequence of the Doctrine of the Unheld Hand). The public myth that "Ixthal kindled us" is not wholly false — dragons truly were born during a Source event — but the secret is that the event was AMPLIFIED, not natural. Where the playground celebrates Ixthal's creative act, the wiki classifies it as a god's hidden crime. Both the dragon religion AND the divine register are working with incomplete information.

A related retcon: Ixthal's silence. Playground attributes it to divine TRAUMA (PTSD from witnessing extinction). The wiki preserves trauma as one cause but adds the load-bearing reason: STRATEGIC CONCEALMENT. Words make causal relationships explicit; if Ixthal speaks too much, the connection between his attention and the rise of dragons could be traced back. His silence is primarily a security measure, not (only) a wound.

1. SSAR'VETH IS DEFINITIVELY DEAD
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] At the start of Element V, the council formally corrected its earlier "hollow god" speculation about Ssar'veth [CANONIZED_AS Kudur-eṭir]:

  - He did not linger as the Empty Crown.
  - He did not become a hollow god wandering the cracks of Eadras.
  - He did not survive as a divine intelligence.

[IN-WORLD] He truly died. When the Spear of Crystallized Entropy lifted him beyond mortality, no mortal shell remained to fall back into. With no lesser vessel, no remaining ordinary soul-state to retreat into, his destruction became total. No ghost, no avatar, no whispering remnant, no hidden cult-contact, no buried will beneath the world. The aftermath of Kudur-eṭir is not a lurking god. It is absence.

[IN-WORLD] His workings left scars: entropic glass, broken ley-vaults, impossible mineral formations, Spear-contaminated ruins. None of these scars contain him.

[IN-WORLD] The Mage Architect's framing: "The Spear worked. That is what makes it horrifying. It transformed him into a deity. But godhood did not place him beyond Omniversal Law. His apotheosis removed the safety of mortality."

This is the purest proof of the Omniversal Law: Death is sovereign in the end. Destruction is the natural end of all things. Ssar'veth tried to end entropy, and entropy ended him.

2. THE FIRST GREAT FLARE — DRAGONS EMERGE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] After millions of years of regrowth in the moving world, Ixthal saw that life had finally become strong enough to bear sapience and power again. He caused a controlled SOURCE FLARE.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki adds critical context: this was approximately 8 million years before present, about 2 million years after the Cataclysm. Ixthal did not create the flare from nothing. He detected an INCOMING NATURAL SOURCE SURGE through his pattern-sight and amplified it at the critical moment — widening what was already opening — into an X-CLASS SOURCE FLARE. This is the FIRST GREAT FLARE. The amplification is what makes the act a violation of the Edict of Closed Genesis; the natural surge underneath provides the cover that has protected the secret for ~8 million years.

[IN-WORLD] The flare did not merely shine brighter. It altered the magical character of Sourceshine itself. Under divine regulation, the flare passed through shell, yolk, blood, scale, and spirit, rewriting nine giant reptile eggs into the first true dragons. These nine progenitors are the NINE EMBERS OF RENEWAL.

[IN-WORLD] Element V proposed the nine eggs as the NINE COLOR CLANS, simplifying Element IV's named eggs (Aurelion, Pyrrhax, etc.):
  1. Red
  2. Blue
  3. Green
  4. Purple
  5. Black
  6. White
  7. Copper
  8. Silver
  9. Gold

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki preserves the nine color clans. It also adds: all nine of the original dragons eventually ASCENDED TO GODHOOD through legitimate Ascended Mortal pathways. Three are publicly named (Cindirith the Magnificent / Platinum / 8th egg / First God of Dragons / the Eldest; Davian Sky Sovereign / Blue / Tempest Lord; Koruss of the Eternal Fire / Red / God of Dragonfire). Six WITHDREW into the Source or into draconic-only spheres and remain unnamed in mortal-accessible records. The playground's color-list-as-progenitor-list does not match cleanly onto canon — Cindirith is Platinum, which is not in the playground's nine colors. The wiki's pantheon names supersede the playground's color list as a direct mapping.

[IN-WORLD] Dragons carry three inheritances (per the Mage Architect):
  1. Reptilian flesh from the renewed world
  2. Source-born magical intensity from the flare
  3. Ixthalic regulation in the structure of their sapience

[IN-WORLD] That third point may explain why dragons are so intensely individualistic yet still instinctively defer to age. Their minds were born from singular transformed eggs, but their species-pattern was stabilized by a divine will that understood consequence across ages.

[SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI] The playground gives dragon dominance a span of "ONE HUNDRED MILLION YEARS." The wiki re-dates the dragon-unicorn era as approximately 8 mybp emergence to ~39 kybp humanoid emergence — roughly 8 MILLION YEARS, not a hundred million. The compressed timeline matters for many downstream calculations (the Chaos Crusades, the Eldravath supercontinent phase, the rate of civilizational cycle).

[IN-WORLD] Dragon history during the long dominion followed a cycle:
  War → Recovery → Stability → Overcrowding → War again.
Every age of peace planted the next age of devastation. Every victorious lineage multiplied. Every hoard demanded territory. Every territory overlapped. Then came fire, storm, venom, frost, acid, psionic thunder, and elemental ruin.

[IN-WORLD] The marks of those wars still shape modern geography (preserved verbatim in canon):
  - Deserts that began as Red-Blue warfronts
  - Glassed valleys where Gold and Purple dragons clashed
  - Swamps acidified by Black clan battles
  - Coral graveyards where Silvers dragged enemies beneath the waves
  - Mountain peaks sheared flat by elder dragon duels

[IN-WORLD] Modern peoples may think these are natural formations. Draconic historians know better.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki adds: the dragon-unicorn era unfolded across two major continental configurations — the original three-continent post-Cataclysm world (Avalon, Damedes, Culethor, plus pre-Cataclysm survivors Therion and Aurettos), and the ELDRAVATH SUPERCONTINENT that formed at ~6 mybp when those continents collided. Eldravath was the last single-landmass phase of the world before fracturing back toward the modern continents. Two CHAOS CRUSADES (raw Chaos flooding through Kudur-eṭir's First Breach) struck during this era — the First around 6 mybp (about when unicorns emerged), the Second around 2 mybp. Both were repelled. The playground Council Elements V-VI do not mention either Crusade; this is structure the wiki later added to the dragon-unicorn era.

3. THE SECOND FLARE — UNICORNS EMERGE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Later, Ixthal caused a second Source Flare. This one was different. It did not transform isolated eggs into solitary apex beings. It awakened a herd.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki adds: this is the SILVER PLAINS FLARE, approximately 6 million years before present, two million years after dragon emergence, four million years after the Cataclysm. As with the dragon flare, Ixthal AMPLIFIED a natural Source surge rather than creating one. The geological setting was the Eldravath supercontinent — a single unified landmass with broadly stable, interconnected ecosystems, which contributed to the unicorns' nature as creatures aligned with balance and systemic continuity. The species was BORN COLLECTIVE — a cohesive ecological and social unit awakened together as a single emergent collective.

[IN-WORLD] The first unicorns were plains-dwelling herd animals — proto-equines, fleet, social, highly sensitive to Eldritch currents — transformed into the second sapient race. Their minds were not individual in the draconic sense. They were telepathic, communal, instinctively coordinated, leader-responsive.

[IN-WORLD] They had:
  - No spoken language
  - No cities
  - No writing
  - No private ambition as dragons understood it

[IN-WORLD] To dragons, they were prey.

[IN-WORLD] Unicorns remember their creation only as sensation: light, fear, sudden self-awareness as many, the first awareness of "we," then grass, wind, running. They know Ixthal only as the SILENT BRIGHTNESS or the PAIN BEHIND THE SUN — not as a named god.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki adds significant new material about unicorn social structure:

THE HIDDEN MILLENNIA. After their awakening, unicorns lived for untold millennia in the Unicorn Plains, deliberately keeping a low profile — appearing to outsiders as nothing more than unusually beautiful magical animals. Illusion-mastery and command of the land let them remain unseen among ordinary herds. Through this hidden existence, the unicorns endured TWO FULL CYCLES of draconic civilization before contact — watching the dragons repeatedly rise to dominance, build empires, collapse back into barbarism, then rise again, twice over. The Plains in this period functioned as a deep-time observatory: the unicorns watched the world without being watched, and noticed (long before the dragons did) that other sapient species had begun to appear elsewhere on Eadras.

THE CHORUS. The unicorn collective decision-making body — the herd-mind organized for deliberation rather than for momentary coordination. The herd-mind in motion is reactive simultaneity; the Chorus is the deliberative organ. Its most famous canonical decision was the resolution to strike against the dragons in the Unicorn Plains War — when extinction became a real threat, the Chorus made a "cold, calculated decision" to attack while the dragons were at their weakest, rather than continue to hide. The Chorus is capable of long-time-horizon strategic thinking, not merely reactive consensus.

4. THE UNICORN PLAINS WAR
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Dragons originally treated unicorns as prey. The misunderstanding was essential. Dragons did not recognize unicorns as a peer race because unicorn sapience did not resemble draconic sapience.

  A dragon thinks: I AM. I WANT. I CLAIM. I REMEMBER.
  A unicorn herd thought: WE MOVE. WE KNOW. WE FLEE. WE DEFEND.

To dragons, the lack of speech and architecture meant animality. To unicorns, dragons were terrifying solitary predators whose minds burned too loudly.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki specifies that the war was triggered when, during one of the periodic draconic civilizational collapses, dragon clans DISCOVERED the Unicorn Plains and began hunting in earnest. The unicorns first attempted to hide and flee, using illusion-mastery. When extinction became a real threat, the Chorus's cold calculated decision launched the war.

[IN-WORLD] The Mage Architect classified unicorn telepathy as early PSIONICS — magic ruled directly through consciousness rather than through external spellforms, divine channels, or reflected Eldritch structures. The unicorn herd-mind was not a spell. It was a LIVING MENTAL FIELD.

[IN-WORLD] This is why unicorns could defeat dragons. Not by overpowering them individually, but by acting without delay, argument, fear-fracture, or miscommunication. A dragon had brilliance. A herd had simultaneity. Thousands moved as one body. Leaders redirected the whole formation through silent impulse. Horns channeled psionic force. Shields formed at the speed of thought. Injured members protected without command. Feints executed by instinctive consensus. A dragon might kill dozens. Then hundreds would answer the exact angle of its descent.

[IN-WORLD] The result was the first great defeat of dragonkind. Not extinction. Not conquest. Humiliation. The dragons learned that the plains were not hunting grounds anymore. No treaty was signed. No embassy formed. No songs were exchanged. The dragons simply stopped hunting the plains, and the lesson passed into draconic instinct and law:

  Do not hunt the plains.

[IN-WORLD] This created a territorial shift. Dragons withdrew from broad unicorn-dominated grasslands. Plains became zones of relative draconic absence, which later peoples may have exploited unknowingly. Some early humanoid migrations may have survived because dragons had already learned to avoid certain open ranges.

5. BORDER EXCHANGE AND THE GREAT EXCHANGE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Peace did not begin with rulers. It began with the young.

[IN-WORLD] Young dragons, not yet fully hardened by clan pride, approached the edges of the plains. Young unicorns, less bound by fear-memory, approached in return. They watched. Then they imitated. Then they exchanged images. A dragon brought a heated stone. A unicorn answered with a shared memory of rain. A dragon traced a flame-symbol in sand. A unicorn replied by placing the image directly in the dragon's mind.

[IN-WORLD] This was the beginning of the first true cultural exchange on Eadras.

[IN-WORLD] The exchange altered both species profoundly.

  - The unicorns taught dragons PSIONICS. Dragons had elemental force, bodily magic, breath, instinctive spellcraft, and Source-fed power. Psionics gave them disciplined mind-to-mind contact, mental shielding, memory structures, and nonverbal magical coordination.
  - In return, dragons taught unicorns ELEMENTAL MAGIC and, more radically, PERSONAL POWER — magic drawn from the individual self rather than the collective field.

[IN-WORLD] Even more disruptive was the concept of INDIVIDUALITY. A young unicorn learning from a dragon might, for the first time, think not merely "we know," but "I wonder." That is a civilizational earthquake without cities.

[IN-WORLD] Some herds rejected this as sickness. Others integrated individuality cautiously. Over time, unicorn society developed layers:
  - THE HERD-MIND, still central
  - THE LEAD-MINDS, strong individual coordinators
  - THE FAR-WANDERERS, unicorns who spend time away from the herd to develop personal identity
  - THE BORDER-FRIENDS, those who maintain ancient relationships with dragons

[IN-WORLD] Young dragons changed too. Psionics softened some of their worst tendencies. Not morally purified them, but made empathy harder to dismiss. Once a dragon had felt a herd's fear from inside, hunting unicorns became unthinkable to many.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki canonizes this exchange as a distinct historical phase: the GREAT EXCHANGE. It is structurally separate from the Compact of Horn and Flame. The Compact is the post-war norm (dragons would not hunt unicorns; unicorns would not cleanse dragon lairs without invitation). The Great Exchange is a LATER centuries-long mutual teaching that transformed both species. Wiki expands the unicorn-side teachings to FOUR DISCIPLINES of psionics (telepathy, telekinesis, psychic combat, psychic shielding), plus a third major teaching the council did not develop: cooperation without domination or hierarchy. And a fourth: the existence of OTHER sapient species — humanoid races had begun to appear, and the dragons had been oblivious. Wiki also canonizes two cultural-legacy phrases still in active use:

  "Walking the Dragon Path" — said of a modern unicorn who lives as an individual.
  "Singing with the Unicorn Voice" — said of a dragon who sets aside personal pride for the good of the whole. Among traditionalist dragons it can be an insult; among Council-aligned dragons it is among the highest compliments.

[INTERNAL_CONTRADICTION] Playground's Element V appears to collapse the post-war norm and the Great Exchange into a single phase. Wiki separates them clearly: war → Compact (restraint) → long stable coexistence → Exchange (transformation). The playground's lyrical "border curiosity" scene actually corresponds to the start of the Great Exchange phase, not to the immediate post-war.

6. THE TREATY OF RATHARIL
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Treaty of Ratharil is one of the great turning points of draconic history. For [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI: one hundred million years; canonized as the much-shorter dragon-unicorn era], dragons had failed to break their own cycle. The unicorns did not impose the solution. They inspired it.

[IN-WORLD] Dragons, learning from herd coordination and long-term collective survival, created a law no dragon tyrant had managed to sustain before:

  The total dragon population of Eadras would be limited to exactly 999,999.

[IN-WORLD] A sacred number in honor of the nine progenitors.

[IN-WORLD] The limit is biologically and magically maintained, not by constant murder, but through:
  - Clan record-keeping
  - Egg-rights controlled by elder councils
  - Magical fertility restraint
  - Hoard-status requirements
  - Territorial capacity calculations
  - Oath-magic anchored to the Treaty of Ratharil itself

[IN-WORLD] The number 999,999 is not merely symbolic; it is legally and magically exact.

[IN-WORLD] When a dragon dies, a place opens. When a place opens, egg-rights may be granted. This makes births politically charged among ambitious clans.

[IN-WORLD] The number may also serve as a metaphysical containment limit: too many Source-born dragons may disturb ley systems and the Weave [WIKI_EXPANDS: this hypothesis is preserved in canon as a partial explanation].

[IN-WORLD] In a population-limited species, elders are living continuity. Every ancient dragon is a library, a legal authority, a genealogical anchor, and a memory of old mistakes. The instinct to defer to older dragons may predate the treaty, but Ratharil made it civilization's spine.

7. RATHARIL — CITY OF DRAGONS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Element VI corrects an earlier framing: Ratharil is not merely a treaty site or symbolic caldera. RATHARIL IS THE CITY OF DRAGONS — a genuine city built by dragons, for dragons, with quarried stone buildings, meeting halls, streets wide enough for elder bodies, aerial terraces, landing courts, vaults, archives, towers, and neutral sanctuaries.

[IN-WORLD] Its existence matters because dragons are often imagined as solitary lair-beasts. Ratharil proves they are also architects, lawmakers, masons, archivists, and urban beings when necessity demands.

[IN-WORLD] After [the long stretch] of cyclical draconic catastrophe, the creation of Ratharil marked a cultural revolution. Dragons did not merely agree to limit population; they built a place where agreement could endure. A city requires compromise. Streets must be shared. Stone must be quarried, transported, shaped, and maintained. Meeting halls must stand beside rival halls without becoming battlefields. Disputes must be delayed long enough for procedure. For dragons, that is almost miraculous.

[IN-WORLD] A dragon city is not a human city enlarged. Dragons fly. Dragons coil. Dragons perch. Dragons require heat, water, vault-depth, privacy, and approach routes from the air. Ratharil's architecture is vertical and radial:
  - Vast landing plazas
  - Terraced roofs strong enough for Golds
  - Deep cisterns and saltwater halls for Silvers
  - Sunning courts for Reds and Coppers
  - Mist gardens for Blacks
  - Canopied stone conservatories for Greens
  - Ice-vault embassies for Whites
  - Resonance chambers for Blues
  - Unpredictable, mutable festival courts for Purples

[IN-WORLD] The city sits at the convergence of old draconic territories, on a high basalt plateau surrounding a dormant volcanic throat. The stone is ancient, dense, and magically stable. There are mountains nearby for Golds, dry lands for Blues and Reds, river-fed wetlands within flight for Blacks, forests for Greens, cold peaks for Whites, deep aqueducts leading to marine access for Silvers, and old devastation fields for Coppers. No clan can claim the whole region as natural home. That is why it works.

[IN-WORLD] The city's founding myth: the dragons built Ratharil before the final form of the treaty was completed. At first it was simply a neutral meeting place, a camp of wary elders. Then came stone rings. Then clan halls. Then archives. Then the central council chamber. The treaty was not signed in a city; the treaty and the city grew into one another. Ratharil is both place and promise.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki gives the city its Draconic name: RHTRAKHADARA ("The Eternal Radiant Gathering"). Ratharil is the Common-language form. Mortals find correct Draconic pronunciation extremely difficult; even master Weavespinners only approximate the deeper resonances.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki adds (from the Dragons article) that the FORBIDDEN TRUTH of dragon origin — Ixthal's amplification of the Source surge — is preserved only in the SEALED HALL OF FIRST FLAME in Ratharil, and even there it is incomplete. Most dragons do not know Ixthal created them; common draconic doctrine holds that the Nine Progenitors hatched in a sacred natural mystery.

8. THE COUNCIL OF 99 ELDEST
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Council of 99 Eldest is the governing heart of Ratharil and, insofar as dragons accept governance, dragonkind. Ninety-nine is the number beneath the sacred 999,999: eleven elders for each of the nine color clans.

[IN-WORLD] The Council consists of the eleven oldest living dragons from each color clan who are willing and capable of serving. If one is too infirm, mad, oath-exiled, or unreachable, the next eldest may sit in their place. Age remains the central qualification. This reflects draconic instinct: older dragons are deferred to automatically. The council uses that instinct as political structure.

[IN-WORLD] But that also creates tension. Because dragons defer to age, younger reformers struggle to challenge elder policy. If an ancient dragon is wrong, the whole species may hesitate. The instinct can be overridden in great need — but doing so in Ratharil would be a historic act.

[IN-WORLD] The council chamber is THE NINETY-NINEFOLD HALL — a tiered, descending amphitheater carved into black quarried stone, open to the sky at the center. Not circular like humanoid senates. The eldest sit according to age, not color, on massive stone ledges. Clan banners, scale mosaics, and breath-scarred pillars mark representation, but age hierarchy supersedes color boundaries.

[IN-WORLD] At the floor lies the RATHARIL STONE, a single quarried slab large enough for all nine progenitor names to be carved upon it. The population count is magically maintained there.

[IN-WORLD] Council customs:
  - Some of the eldest may attend in shapeshifted forms for practicality, but custom may require true form for final votes
  - Inter-clan vendetta is forbidden inside city bounds
  - No dragon may hunt another's guest under neutral protection
  - No egg may be destroyed within the city
  - No duel may occur except in sanctioned arenas
  - No dragon may claim Ratharil as personal territory
  - No mortal army may enter without invitation of the Council of 99
  - No clan hall may be besieged by another clan

[IN-WORLD] These laws are magically enforced, but not absolutely in a way that removes agency. Oaths are bound into the city stones. Breaking them causes immediate consequence: loss of shapeshifting within the city, suppression of breath weapon, public scale-marking, or denial of Ratharil's protections. The city does not mind-control dragons. It creates costs that even dragons respect.

[IN-WORLD] The quarried stones themselves come from the same neutral plateau, making the city magically coherent. Every building is literally made from Ratharil's ground. This allows oath-magic to permeate the architecture.

[IN-WORLD] Because the buildings are genuine quarried stone, constructed rather than grown or conjured, dragons have a shared civic pride. Ratharil is proof that dragons can build something lasting without burning it down in the next cycle.

A dragon insult:
  "You have never laid stone in Ratharil."
Meaning: you take from dragonkind but have contributed nothing to its endurance.

9. THE NINE COLOR CLANS — TEMPERAMENT AND CULTURE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The color clans are not moral categories. They are ancient lineages shaped by progenitor temperament, environment, and elder rulership style.

REDS. Hot regions: volcanoes, burning deserts, geothermal lands, arid badlands. Standoffish even with one another. Leaders tend to be hands-off. A Red respects strength, territory, and the right to be left alone.

BLUES. Deserts. Highly clan-oriented. Structured, memory-rich, lineage-conscious. Blue hospitality is formal, rule-bound, full of status signals.

GREENS. Forests. Highly individualistic. Hidden territories among dense canopies, ancient groves, overgrown ruins. Green clan identity exists, but personal territory and private cunning matter more.

PURPLES. Anywhere. Chaotic and unpredictable. Deeply influenced in early history by the Elder Goddess known in draconic tradition as FAYE. Theologians may identify Faye with Sylph under an archaic draconic name, though dragons often insist their own name is older and more accurate. Purples delight in violating expectations.

BLACKS. Swamps, river deltas, marshes, forested wetlands. Less orderly. Loose, opportunistic, intensely local. Masters of ambush, decay-rich ecosystems, waterlogged ruins.

WHITES. Cold and icy regions. Individualistic but fiercely traditional. Because survival in their territories is harsh, they maintain strict hospitality: any White clan member who arrives must be given food, shelter, and protection. A White who violates hospitality risks becoming clanless.

COPPERS. Devastated regions: volcanic scars, ruined lands, old battlefields, ash plains, broken territories. Drawn to places where destruction has already done its work. Some become restorers, others scavenger-lords, others philosophers of ruin.

SILVERS. Marine. Staid and isolationist. Favor oceans, deep coasts, island chains, trenches, storm-seas. Culture is slow to change and difficult for outsiders to access. May preserve some of the oldest memories of drowned places.

GOLDS. High mountains, though some have chosen city life in the modern era. More tightly knit even than Blues. Often possess elaborate internal hierarchies, formal kinship obligations, strong elder authority.

[IN-WORLD] Each color has a clan hall in Ratharil. The halls smell different to dragons (scent is culture):
  - Reds smell mineral heat
  - Blues smell ozone and dry dust
  - Greens smell sap and old rain
  - Purples smell different every visit
  - Blacks smell peat, tannin, and slow water
  - Whites smell snow, blood-warm food, and clean stone
  - Coppers smell ash and metal
  - Silvers smell salt and cold depth
  - Golds smell incense, mountain air, and polished scale-oil

[IN-WORLD] HOSPITALITY varies dramatically by color:
  - A Red may offer a heated cave and a carcass without conversation
  - A Blue may require formal recitation of lineage before opening a guest chamber
  - A White must feed and shelter a clanmate, even an enemy, because the ice permits no lesser law
  - A Gold may host with ceremony so complex that a young dragon needs coaching to avoid insult
  - A Black may offer hospitality but quietly test whether the guest deserves to leave with all their possessions

[IN-WORLD] The deeper law is AGE. A dragon automatically defers to any dragon older than themselves, regardless of clan. This is instinctive and nearly impossible to resist. Not absolute, but close. The instinct can be overridden if need is great enough: to prevent catastrophe, defend hatchlings, uphold Ratharil, or resist an elder who has become dangerously mad. But doing so is psychologically difficult and culturally grave.

[IN-WORLD] The age-deference instinct may be a remnant of Ixthal's original regulation. Dragonkind was born from only nine progenitors. Without age-deference, early dragons may have annihilated themselves immediately. The instinct created hierarchy before law existed. Ratharil later transformed instinct into civilization.

10. MODERN DRAGONS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] In the modern world, elder dragons are reclusive because the world is crowded with younger peoples, divine politics, magitek, kingdoms, and Weave-regulated magic. When an elder dragon acts, geography and history shift. An elder Red entering a war can end a nation. A Silver rising from the sea can close trade across an ocean. A Gold descending into a city can legitimize or topple dynasties.

[IN-WORLD] Young adult and older dragons shapeshift into humanoid forms at will. This lets them participate in the modern economy without immediately provoking armies. Many become elite mercenaries — not common sellswords, but catastrophic assets. A dragon mercenary might charge in gold, land, rare magic, political favors, or hoard-worthy art. Some serve openly. Others operate under humanoid identities for decades. This creates social tension. A "human" duelist in a tavern may be a ninety-foot catastrophe choosing politeness.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki adds the modern dissident cult: the FLESH-EATERS. A small, secretive cult that rejects everything the Great Exchange and Ratharil represent. They hold that dragons are the rightful apex of all life, that civilization and restraint are corruptions of true dragon nature, and that all non-draconic sapient life is prey — preferred prey, because thinking creatures taste better. They reject treaties, coexistence, and shared civilization. They hunt in small mobile cells from concealed lairs and raid Council-aligned dragon clan nests, stealing eggs and indoctrinating the stolen hatchlings in total isolation. Their slang for unicorns is "horn-horses." The Council of 99 has formally declared them enemies of dragonkind. They have proven hard to eradicate because the cult survives its own destruction through buried doctrinal caches — "always reborn." Speaking the name "Flesh-Eaters" in polite company is in extremely poor taste.

11. DRAGON CHILD RACES
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The dragons created or shaped several reptilian and scaled peoples, known broadly as their CHILD RACES:
  - Kobolds
  - Dragonborn
  - Lizardfolk
  - Snake People

[IN-WORLD] They were not all created at once or for the same purpose. Some may have been shaped through direct draconic magic, others through long-term magical influence around lairs, others through deliberate breeding or Source-infused transformation. Methods varied by clan and era.

[IN-WORLD] Their relationships with dragons also vary:
  - Kobolds may preserve devotional, practical, or contractual ties to dragon patrons
  - Dragonborn may claim nobler descent and often struggle with whether they are children, servants, heirs, or independent peoples
  - Lizardfolk may be older-shaped frontier peoples, adapted to wetlands, rivers, and survivalist societies

12. IXTHAL'S SILENCE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Ixthal does not communicate with mortals. He barely communicates with other deities. Not because he is arrogant. Not because he is dead. Not because he has withdrawn in judgment.

[SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI] The playground attributes his silence to DIVINE TRAUMA — he is wounded, has PTSD carried into godhood from witnessing the extinction of his civilization, species, language, homeworld, and sixty percent of all life. His silence is the first doctrine of his clergy: "He survived what no mind should survive."

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki preserves trauma as ONE OF FIVE compounding causes, but adds the LOAD-BEARING reason that supersedes the playground's framing: STRATEGIC CONCEALMENT. Ixthal is fully capable of speech. The silence is a deliberate strategic choice maintained because extended verbal communication increases the risk of revealing causal relationships that would expose the Forbidden Kindling. Words make connections explicit. Connections, once explicit, can be inferred. Ixthal maintains the appearance of being unable or unwilling to speak to keep even the question of his silence from becoming interesting — a god who chooses not to speak invites scrutiny; a god who cannot speak does not. The full list of causes:
  1. Strategic concealment of the Forbidden Kindling (load-bearing)
  2. PTSD from surviving Arath's extinction (the playground's reason)
  3. Survivor guilt — knowing Auralis and Thamior spent themselves in vain
  4. Moral injury — enduring ages when the Prime Material lacked mortal sapient races
  5. Recognition that confession could destabilize dragons, unicorns, divine law, and mortal faith

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Ixthal's silence is SELF-IMPOSED — there is no divine gag upon him. He is his own prison.

[IN-WORLD] Ixthal's clergy, if they exist, are not prophets. They are keepers of aftermaths. They serve disaster survivors, war refugees, extinct languages, last members of bloodlines, orphaned children, and those who must rebuild without closure. Their prayers are not requests for intervention. They are acts of witness.

[IN-WORLD] Divine magic from Ixthal is rare, intermittent, and difficult to interpret. He grants power unconsciously, reflexively, or through established divine channels rather than personal attention. His magic responds less to petition and more to circumstances resembling his portfolio: extinction, renewal, unbearable foresight, last chances, and the preservation of future possibility.

  - A priest begging Ixthal for wealth receives nothing.
  - A lone healer protecting the last clutch of an endangered species during a wildfire may suddenly find their hands filled with pale Source-light.
  - A dragon egg in danger of being destroyed unjustly may be shielded by a flare no one asked for.

[IN-WORLD] Ixthal does not answer conversations. But sometimes his trauma reacts to patterns [WIKI_EXPANDS: or his concealment-instincts react to patterns].

[WIKI_EXPANDS] When Ixthal does communicate, he uses DIRECT CONCEPTUAL TRANSMISSION — not spoken language. The recipient understands him without verbal explanation. A mortal touched by such a transmission may report later that they "knew what he meant" but cannot reconstruct any specific phrasing. Conceptual transmission is poor at complex sequences and detailed causal relationships — exactly the content that would risk exposure. Suited to guidance, not to instruction. The medium itself filters out the dangerous content.

[IN-WORLD] When Ixthal does attend to reality, the Source behaves strangely. Not catastrophically, but noticeably. Solar halos. Sudden pale flares. Reptiles falling silent. Unicorn herds turning their heads at once toward no visible threat. Dragons feeling an ancestral pressure behind the eyes. The world recognizes the attention of the god who remade its future. Such moments are exceedingly rare.

[IN-WORLD] Among the gods: Ixthal is not outside the pantheon's awareness. Lirra may understand his Source Flare work better than any other. Nara may pity him. Vos may respect his restraint. Qoxli may honor the renewal he catalyzed. Daj may despise his refusal to fight doomed battles. Sylph/Faye may sometimes try to provoke him and usually fail. Nox likely understands silence too well. He does not attend divine councils unless the matter touches extinction, Source-level renewal, or the possible repetition of Arath's catastrophe. When he speaks, even gods listen, because he has conserved speech for ages.

[IN-WORLD] A mythic fragment:
  Once did Lirra ask him, "Will you not teach them?"
  Ixthal answered after a century of silence:
  "I taught the sun to make children from eggs. Let that suffice."

[IN-WORLD] Every recorded word of Ixthal is sacred, debated, and probably misquoted. There may be entire monastic orders devoted to preserving the SEVEN UTTERANCES OF IXTHAL, though no one agrees whether there were seven, three, or none.

13. THE COURT OF THE UNANSWERED SUN
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Ratharil contains a temple to Ixthal — but unlike other temples. No idol. No altar of petition. No priest shouting prayers. A silent open court called THE COURT OF THE UNANSWERED SUN.

[IN-WORLD] Dragons enter alone. They do not ask Ixthal for favors. They sit beneath direct sunlight and remain silent for one hour, one day, or one season depending on grief.

[IN-WORLD] The court may occasionally experience a faint Source resonance, but never on command. Attempts to force a response are among Ratharil's greatest taboos. Young dragons probably dare each other to whisper requests there. Elders react with horror.

[IN-WORLD] The inscription at the entrance:

  He kindled us.
  He owes us nothing.
  Do not demand speech from the last throat of Arath.

[IN-WORLD] The court sits at the city's highest eastern terrace, where the first Source-light of dawn enters Ratharil. The stones are pale, unlike the darker council halls. No roof. No echo. No carved face. Just sunlight and silence.

[IN-WORLD] There is also a draconic taboo more broadly:

  Do not call Ixthal lightly.

[IN-WORLD] Not because he will punish you. Because asking him to look directly upon the world is cruelty.
