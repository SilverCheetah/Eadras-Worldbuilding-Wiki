Core Assumption
Magic in Eadras is practiced through four living traditions:

Netharim — the most numerous order; intuitive practitioners who reshape reality by imposing a deeply-studied personal model of how the world actually works.
Lexanari (Name-Bound) — the independent fourth order of innate-only mortal speakers of the Primordial Language.
Druids — the devotional sub-priesthood jointly overseen by Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox.
Windspeakers — the monastic caretakers who maintain Lirra’s defensive Weave.

The Weave itself survives only as Lirra’s defensive ward against outsider magic theft. All other prior functions of the Weave have been retired. Magic is personal, spoken, relational, and protective rather than biological or infrastructural.
The Netharim (The Little Gods)
The Netharim are the most numerous of the four orders. They reshape reality intuitively by imposing a deeply-studied personal model of how the world actually works directly onto the world itself. They use no formulas, no incantations, and no reality-structuring language. They use comprehension.
Netharim magic scales with the accuracy and completeness of the practitioner’s internal model of reality. Most Netharim spend their lives deepening that model through scholarship in physics, philosophy, biology, ecology, mathematics, metaphysics, or any other field. A senior Netharim with a finely-honed model of a specific aspect of reality can rewrite that aspect more or less at will.
They share the same underlying Pillar Magic grammar with the Lexanari (Command Pillars: Control, Transform, Create, Destroy, Perceive; Substance Pillars: Essence, Matter, Flesh, Mind, Spirit), but access it through comprehension rather than spoken command. The order maintains a worldwide scholarly and political institution and is heavily involved in both academia and governance.
The Lexanari (Name-Bound)
The Lexanari are the independent fourth order. They are innate-only mortal speakers of the true Primordial Language — the language in which the Omniversal Creator first shaped reality. Only those born with the Gift can learn even fragments of it.
Their magic is spoken, never written. Naming something with the true Language can bind, heal, unmake, or reveal it. They stand apart from any priesthood and are not under the oversight of any Elder God.
The Druids (Sub-Priesthood of Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox)
The Druids are the devotional sub-priesthood jointly overseen by Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox. They practice a relational, devotional form of magic rooted in instinct, laughter, secrecy, and the living world. Their rites emphasize harmony with ecosystems, holy irreverence, mercy-through-chaos, and the careful stewardship of forbidden or loathsome truths.
The Windspeakers
Windspeakers are the monastic caretakers and stabilizers of Lirra’s defensive Weave. They work through breath, song, and ritual alignment — “speaking to the wind of the Weave” to reinforce the barrier where it frays. Their order is smaller and more personal than its predecessor. The death of a Windspeaker still causes localized damage to the ward.
The Defensive Weave (Lirra’s Ward)
The Weave survives solely as a living, wounded defensive barrier created by Lirra during the Demon War. It prevents outsider entities from stealing or corrupting the world’s ambient magic. It is maintained by Windspeakers through ritual and breath-work and remains partially dependent on mortal existence and belief.
Integration with the Gods
• Lirra feels every breach in the defensive Weave as personal injury.
• Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox jointly oversee the Druid sub-priesthood.
• Lexanari stand apart as an independent order.
• Netharim exist in uneasy but productive tension with all three structured traditions.
Tone Guidance
Magic in Eadras should feel intimate, spoken, relational, and sacred-dangerous. Netharim material is scholarly and quietly godlike. Lexanari material is ancient, precise, and terrifyingly beautiful. Druids feel devotional, wild, playful, and unsettling. Windspeakers and the defensive Weave feel exhausted, protective, and quietly heroic.
Narrative Guidance
Good magic stories emphasize:
• the cost and beauty of true names (Lexanari)
• the lifelong pursuit of deeper comprehension (Netharim)
• the devotional wildness of the Druids
• the fragile necessity of the defensive Weave and its Windspeakers
All prior references to the obsolete Sorcerer/Druid/Weavespinner framework have been superseded. This is the only canon.
