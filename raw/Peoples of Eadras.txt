The peoples of Eadras are varied and come in all shapes and sizes. From titanic Dragons and Titans to diminutive Pixies and short stout Dwarves, there is a huge variety in size, shape, and lifestyle.\

**Pre-Empire Elfen Culture**

**Society** Elfen society was built around small, tight-knit groves of 50 to 300 individuals. They were patient, long-term thinkers who saw themselves as the caretakers and gardeners of the natural world.

**Culture** Their entire culture revolved around balance and harmony with nature. Every decision was made by first listening to the land itself.

**Leadership** Leadership was held by Circles of Wardens — groups of elder elves who would meditate together to hear what the land needed before acting.

**Appearance & Dress** Elfen were tall and graceful with an almost ethereal presence. They wore flowing garments woven from living plant fibers that shifted colors with the seasons and blended perfectly into their environment.

**Cities** Their cities were grown rather than built. Using their voices and connection to Eldritch, they sang living trees and plants into towering spires, graceful bridges, and vast living halls. Large Circles of up to a hundred could raise entire districts in just a couple of hours. They could also vitrify living wood into stone when harder surfaces were needed.

**Pre-Empire Velrothi Culture**

**Society** Velrothi society revolved entirely around the pursuit of magical knowledge. Status was determined solely by arcane achievement and original research.

**Culture** Knowledge was considered sacred. There were no forbidden arts — all forms of magic were studied with equal passion. Necromancy was viewed as a technical discipline rather than a moral one.

**Leadership** Each city or region was governed by a Conclave of the most accomplished mages. Decisions were made through intense debate and contests of logic.

**Appearance & Dress** Velrothi are tall and cadaverous with ashen gray skin and glowing red eyes. They wear dark, layered robes covered in flowing runes that glow faintly when magic is being channeled.

**Cities** Their cities are towering gothic spires of black stone and crystallized magic. Needle-like towers are connected by slender bridges and floating platforms, filled with glowing red runes.

***

**Pre-Empire Tarran Culture**

**Society** Tarran society was strictly disciplined and honor-bound. Status came from martial skill, dedication to duty, and acts of justice. Children were raised with rigorous martial training and discipline from a young age.

**Culture** Their culture revolved around the ideals of **Duty, Honor, and Order**. Self-control was valued as much as strength. Justice was seen in stark black-and-white terms.

**Leadership** They were ruled by a monarchy backed by the Firethorn Throne, which only accepted the rightful monarch.

**Appearance & Dress** Tarrans wore heavy lead-gray cloaks made from the fur of a now-extinct animal. The fur was impossible to dye and perfectly matched the color of their stonework. This animal went extinct during the Demon War. They wore plain, almost undecorated armor forged from a rare, extremely hard-to-work magical metal (mithril placeholder).

**Cities** Tarran cities were grim layered fortresses built entirely around defense. Their streets were narrow and twisting to create choke points. Every building and street was designed as part of the defensive plan. Tall, uniform gray stone structures loomed over the streets below.

At the heart of their capital city stood an enormous domed hall inside the central citadel. The **Firethorn Throne** sat within this vast open hall on full public display.
