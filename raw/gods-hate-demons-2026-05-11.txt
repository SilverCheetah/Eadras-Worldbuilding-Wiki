Gods hate demons — petty grudge canon, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture: Josh established why the gods of Eadras hate demons.
The hatred is real, intense, and PETTY -- a personal grievance about
ruined entertainment, not a noble cosmic stance.)

== Canon ==

The gods hate demons because demons disrupted the Game of the Gods, and
forced the gods to intervene in mortal affairs (which is itself a further
disruption to the Game).

Mechanism:

1. The Game of the Gods (canonically established on gods-of-eadras.md
   under "## The Game of the Gods") is the addictive D&D-like board on
   which all important mortals are pieces. The gods spend most of their
   time playing it in Solarion; intervention in mortal affairs is rare
   precisely because the Game is more compelling than the mortal world
   below it.

2. When the Demon Prince of the Open Wound tore into Eadras through
   Xar'vion's summons, the Demon War began. Game pieces -- important
   mortals -- died in volumes the rules had never been built to handle.
   Whole regions cleared off the board. Carefully-balanced narrative
   cycles the gods had spent millennia cultivating were torn up in
   months. The addictive cycle was BROKEN.

3. Worse: the gods were FORCED to leave the Game and intervene directly
   in the war. Intervention is itself a Game disruption -- moving a god's
   own pieces, breaking turn-by-turn discipline, distorting the board with
   direct action that the Game's rules treat as a last resort. The gods
   who entered the war did so reluctantly, knowing each direct action
   further degraded the Game they were trying to defend.

Result: the gods loathe demons with grudge-intensity. A god unmoved by
mortal suffering on a scale that would shatter mortal minds is FURIOUS
about their interrupted entertainment. The hatred runs deeper than mortal
moral horror because pettiness rooted in personal loss outlasts cosmic
abstraction.

== The Divine Bureaucracy's role ==

The Divine Bureaucracy -- itself outside the Game in the way the
Bureaucracy is outside divine politics generally -- has spent millennia
managing the godly grudge. Bureau heads have fielded divine demands that
demons be hunted, ritualised, anathematised, and framed in mortal-facing
doctrine as cosmic evils. The underlying motive was always "they broke
our game"; mortal-facing doctrine reads better and is what the
priesthoods preach.

A reflective theologian (Lirran clergy especially, who have a tradition
of asking awkward questions about divine motivation) might privately
acknowledge the gap between mortal-facing doctrine and underlying motive.
Few would say so openly.

== Implications ==

- Godly opposition to demons is REAL and INTENSE but not NOBLE. Mortal
  cosmologies that frame godly anti-demon action as cosmic righteousness
  are missing the underlying grudge.
- The Demon War's godly intervention (per the existing demon-war.md
  canon, where Xar'vion eventually turned and joined the alliance
  against the demons) was reluctant, even grudging. The gods did not
  want to leave the Game; they were forced to.
- The doctrine of demonic evil that the priesthoods preach is partly a
  Bureaucratic translation of a petty grudge into mortal-legible terms.
- This pattern fits the broader Eadras tone of "dark fantasy with
  earned hope, not grimdark" -- the gods are not noble guardians of
  mortals; their interests align with mortal survival only as far as
  the Game requires.
