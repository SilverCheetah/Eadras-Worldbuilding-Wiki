The Great Exchange
For untold millennia, the unicorns lived among the vast herds of the Unicorn Plains, sharing the grasslands with wild horses, antelopes, and countless other plains megafauna. They deliberately kept a low profile, appearing to outsiders as nothing more than unusually beautiful and magical animals.
They had already endured two full cycles of draconic civilization — watching the dragons repeatedly rise to dominance, build empires, and collapse back into barbarism.
During one of these collapses, dragon clans discovered the Unicorn Plains and the creatures that lived there. To the dragons, the graceful horned beings were simply another species of exceptionally fine prey. The unicorns first attempted to hide and flee, using their mastery of illusion and the land to remain unseen among the ordinary herds.
When it became clear that the dragons would not stop and extinction became a real threat, the Chorus made a cold, calculated decision. Knowing the dragons were at their weakest, the unicorns struck. The brief but brutal Unicorn Plains War ended with the dragons being decisively driven from the plains.
In the uneasy peace that followed, something unexpected happened on the borders.
Young unicorns — curious adolescents who had begun experimenting with stepping outside the Chorus — and young dragons — restless individuals living at the edges of their fractured society — began encountering one another. Curiosity overcame hatred. These fringe individuals started tentative conversations that slowly deepened.
What emerged from these meetings became known as the Great Exchange.
What the Dragons Taught the Unicorns:

The power and burden of individuality — the concept of a true, singular “I”
New and aggressive applications of Eldritch magic previously unknown to the unicorns

What the Unicorns Taught the Dragons:

The true power of cooperation without domination or hierarchy
The disciplined arts of psionics, including telepathy, telekinesis, psychic combat, and psychic shielding
The realization that other sapient species had existed on Eadras for approximately twenty thousand years

The young unicorns and dragons who met on the border became the bridge between their peoples. Over centuries, these ideas spread through both species and fundamentally changed them.
Modern unicorns who choose to live as individuals are said to be “walking the Dragon Path.”
Dragons who set aside personal pride for the good of the whole are said to be “singing with the Unicorn Voice.”
Though the old war is still remembered, it is the Great Exchange that ultimately defines the ancient and complicated relationship between unicorns and dragons.
