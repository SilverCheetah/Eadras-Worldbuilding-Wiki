In the beginning, the Omniversal Creator birthed two creators for this mythos: Evye , the Feminine, and Fryr , the Masculine. Together they decided every rule, every layer—how it would work, what it would become.

Evye forged the physical world: land, seas, skies, mountains, stars—the solid frame. Fryr shaped the ethereal: Shadowdark , Feywild , Limina (the misty threshold where worlds bleed into each other), every non-physical realm, and the source of all magic.

Unlike other creator deities—who used power but didn't sacrifice themselves—they lent a sliver of their essence to each other: Evye gave Fryr a thread of tangible weight to anchor his realms; Fryr gave Evye a spark of ethereal flow to let her world breathe. Then, to finish it, they poured out everything else—Evye dissolved into earth and storm, Fryr into shadows, dreams, and every spell's core. No remnants, just total commitment. That's why magic here is thick and alive: not borrowed, but their shared pulse, woven into stone, veil, and whisper. Naturally, because magic was so pervasive, the mythos drifted very close to the core, and the Eadras Mythos is one of the seven closest to the core.
