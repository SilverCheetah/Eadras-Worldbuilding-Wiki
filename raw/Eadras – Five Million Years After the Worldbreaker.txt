Eadras – Five Million Years After the Worldbreaker
The Breaking of Eldravath
Five million years after the Cataclysm (and five million years before the present day), the great supercontinent Eldravath has fractured along massive tectonic rifts, splitting into four distinct continents:

Valdaran — The Northwestern Continent
A rugged, mountainous land with deep fjords and cold northern reaches. Future stronghold for Gold and White dragons.
Elyndar — The Western Continent
The large western landmass featuring arid interiors, shifting sands, coastal forests, and broad river valleys. Rich in minerals and destined to become a heartland for humanoid civilizations.
Korvath — The Southeastern Continent
A volcanic, tropical, and mountainous land with lush coasts and active fire mountains. Strong future influence from Red and Black dragons.
Thalindor — The Central Continent
The central core landmass that remains closest to the Worldbreaker Scar. Highly geologically active, magically potent, and unstable. This region will later give rise to the Altean Highlands and the Arathi Golden Age.

The Worldbreaker Scar remains a massive, dangerous oceanic rift to the east, acting as a permanent uncrossable barrier. The breakup has created new ocean basins, jagged coastlines, and chains of young islands along the ancient rift zones.
This division marks the final major geological reshaping of Eadras before the long ages of dragon and unicorn dominance and the eventual rise of the progenitor humanoid races.
