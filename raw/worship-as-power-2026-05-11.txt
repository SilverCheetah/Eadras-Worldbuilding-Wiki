== Canon: worship as a secondary power source (NEW) ==

Gods across the Omniverse derive a **secondary stream of power from
worshippers**. More worshippers = more godpower. Loss of worshippers
shrinks effective capacity.

This is canonical but underdocumented in mortal theology, which
typically frames worship as a duty TO the gods rather than as a
substrate OF their power.

In Mythoi with finite, slowly-draining Wells, worship-power is
LOAD-BEARING for godly capacity:
- Pantheons grow by accumulating worship.
- Pantheons decline when worship shrinks.
- This is the structural reason most pantheons are expansionist.
- Expansion = pushing worship outward into neighbouring Mythoi,
  contesting other pantheons for converts, conducting competing-
  for-souls business across realities.
- Expansion happens through the Outer Realms (which encircle the
  Concordance) and in Nexus itself.
- For most gods, expansion is not ambition; it is SURVIVAL.

== Canon: where cross-Mythos divine business happens ==

- OUTER REALMS (circling the Concordance) -- shared venue for
  inter-Mythos divine business.
- NEXUS -- the megacity where gods of every Mythos can meet.

These are the established mechanisms by which expansion happens.
Gods don't typically invade other Mythoi directly; they accrue
influence through the Outer Realms and Nexus.

== Canon: Eadras pantheon's unique situation ==

The Eadras pantheon is the exception:

- Bastion-fed Well makes Well-power effectively unlimited.
- The pantheon already enjoys the positional advantage among the
  most powerful in the multiverse.
- Worship-power still flows -- Eadras has thriving priesthoods,
  the Divine Bureaucracy's Bureau of Prayer and Bureau of Favor
  process worship, priests gain Favor in exchange for service,
  etc.
- But the Eadras pantheon does NOT NEED worship for survival or
  growth the way most pantheons do.
- This is part of why they can afford to be inward-turned and
  absorbed in the Game instead of expansionist.

For Eadras gods: worship is appreciation / Game-piece-tracking /
institutional engagement, not power-substrate.

== Item 7 deferred (explanation pending user direction) ==

The Skeptic flagged: the Position section confidently attributes
DESIGN INTENT to the Game (it is "the mechanism that maintains" the
inward orientation; it "keeps the pantheon busy without harming them";
it is "benign containment"). The Causation section honestly admits
Dao made the Game IN SECRET and did NOT EXPLAIN it. So the
intent-attribution may exceed what canon supports.

Two readings:

  A. FUNCTION READING: the Game's observable effect is inward
     orientation + pantheon containment. The page is describing
     observable function. No claim about Dao's intent. Fine as
     written.

  B. INTENT READING: the Game's effect is inward orientation; the
     page calls this "not coincidence," "benign containment," and
     attributes purpose to Dao. This goes beyond observable function
     to a claim about Dao's design intent -- which is canonically
     unknown.

The page slides between (A) and (B) without distinguishing them.
The Skeptic objected to (B). User needs to direct whether to:
- (a) canonize Dao's design intent (yes, Dao designed it for
      containment), and the page can keep its current confidence
- (b) hedge the page to describe function only, distinguishing
      observable effect from unstated intent
- (c) preserve the current language under the position that even
      observers in tome-neutral voice can infer purpose from
      consistent observed function

User direction needed.
