Addition — catastrophe sequence:

The Worldbreaker event unfolded as a multi-stage process consisting of impact, global thermal conditions, atmospheric instability, and hydrological reset.

Initial heating was caused by both ejecta reentry and subsurface steam release, producing sustained oven-like conditions. This was followed by a period of global windstorms driven by atmospheric instability, which redistributed heat, debris, and biological material.

As temperatures declined, atmospheric moisture condensed into prolonged global rainfall, forming oceans and reshaping the surface. Tectonic instability persisted throughout these phases, gradually slowing over time to modern rates.
