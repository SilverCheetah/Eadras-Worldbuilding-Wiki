HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT
================================================================

This document captures material from an OpenAI Playground worldbuilding session that predates the current Eadras wiki. The wiki is the authoritative canon. The playground session is a HISTORICAL ARCHIVE preserved because:

  - Most playground material survived to canon, often verbatim
  - Some was renamed, re-mechanized, or replaced
  - The retcons themselves illuminate the setting's evolution
  - Speculative material the council raised but later dropped may still seed future work

Contradictions between playground and wiki are flagged inline with these tags:

  [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI]   — wiki has authoritative version; playground version preserved for historical reference only
  [CANONIZED_AS X]       — idea was kept but renamed or re-mechanized; X gives the canonical form
  [WIKI_EXPANDS]         — wiki retains the idea and adds significant new structure
  [INTERNAL_CONTRADICTION] — the council contradicted itself within these three source files
  [FLAG_FOR_REVIEW]      — material I cannot confirm; check against wiki manually

Two retcons are pervasive across this topic and worth flagging upfront:

  1. SSAR'VETH → KUDUR-EṬIR. The wiki canonizes the original Arathi name Kudur-eṭir ("Seal-Opener"). Ssar'veth is preserved as a post-Cataclysm folk-corruption that became the dominant common name across the millions of years between his death and the present. Where the playground says "Ssar'veth," read "Kudur-eṭir, known in folk-corruption as Ssar'veth."

  2. THE ARATHI CAPITAL'S NAME. The playground proposed "Arathalassa, the Crown of Shallow Seas" as the drowned capital. The wiki renames this to ALTEA. The body of water where the capital was is now called the Arathalassic Sea — the playground name survives as the sea's name, not the city's.

A third major mechanism-correction runs through this material: the Spear is not a ritual instrument. See Section 3 below.

1. THE VERDANT SCAR — A LIVING REGULATING FOREST
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Verdant Scar — known in old accounts as Arath-Veyr, "the Green Wound" — is a vast magically-charged forest in western Eadras. It grew over the ruins of a land-based Arathi installation that survived the Arathi Cataclysm. The forest functions as a distributed magical regulating mind, preventing unstable Eldritch from becoming catastrophic over the broken ley infrastructure beneath. It is not sentient the way a person is; it is more like a dreaming immune system.

[IN-WORLD] The site is geographically a basin where three mountain chains once met around an Arathi ritual complex. After the cataclysm, the central land collapsed into terraces — concentric rings of forest descending toward a hidden inner hollow. Rain flows inward but never floods the center; water disappears into root-chasms and returns as mist.

[IN-WORLD] The forest is divided into named regions:

  - The Green Mantle: outer woodland; villages, Warden paths, Glasshart sightings. Hunters, pilgrims, scholars, and smugglers can enter.
  - The Tilted Miles: middle forest where distance warps. Paths lengthen, hills appear where maps say none should be, ruins surface and sink beneath root mats. This is spatial and perceptual instability caused by damaged Eldritch reflections and Feywild/Shadowdark resonance. Never temporal distortion.
  - The Road of Wanting: an ancient imperial avenue haunted by Want-Wolves.
  - The Sunken Forums: buried civic districts where Marble Mourners gather.
  - The Root-Crucible: the sealed inner hollow over the broken ley-vault network.

[IN-WORLD] The Root-Crucible holds taboo names that vary by faction:
  - Wardens: "The Place Under the First Root"
  - Remnant Houses: "The Last Vault of Arath"
  - Pilgrims of the Closed Wound: "The Shut Door"

[IN-WORLD] What lies beneath the Root-Crucible is not the Spear of Crystallized Entropy itself, but the consequence of its first use: the shape of the desire Kudur-eṭir lost when the curse stripped him. Not his soul, not his ghost — a motivational absence given form. A mage who touches it might not become stronger; instead, they might lose the emotional boundary that prevents them from using the power they already possess.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki adds origin material the council did not develop: the Scar's biology — its ability to fuse crystal with living matter, its self-regulating root-mind — is the legacy of UNCONTROLLED VERDANTITE OVERGROWTH following the Cataclysm. Verdantite is an Arathi crystal lineage used for life-acceleration and biological integration in living architecture. When the regulating singers died and Verdantite went ungoverned, it ate outward through stone, soil, and life until it became its own ecology. The Green Wound is what Verdantite looks like when no one is alive to govern it.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki also clarifies the Scar's place in the modern Weave's geometry. The Verdant Scar is one of FOURTEEN world-piercing conduit ends. Thirteen received Towers of Lirra after the Demon War; the Verdant Scar is the ONE site where no Tower was ever built. The forest's distributed regulating mind was already doing the job a Tower would have done. The Scar's modern antipode is one of the six Towers of Lirra ruined in the Sorcerer Crusade.

2. CREATURES OF THE VERDANT SCAR
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] GLASSHARTS. Tall, pale, antlered deer with translucent bones; antlers grow like branching crystal. At dawn, Sourceshine passes through them and scatters colored light across the forest floor. This is not ornamental — it is defensive magic. Their crystalline antlers refract ambient Eldritch, confusing predators and spellcasters. When threatened, a Glasshart stamps and sends fractures racing through stone, armor, and improperly warded spellwork. They graze on ley-moss, the luminous growth that forms around broken magical currents, preventing unstable Eldritch pools from bursting into wild magic events.

[IN-WORLD] Glasshart antler trade is illegal in Warden territories. Naturally shed shards may be collected only during the SILVER MOLTING, a yearly festival when herds pass through moonlit clearings and drop old growth. Children gather the smallest fragments. Gnomes set them into instruments. Elves use them as tuning pieces for stabilizing song-magic. Orcs carve protective beads for caravan harnesses. Poachers who kill Glassharts are not merely criminals; they endanger the region's magical stability. Overhunting can cause local wild magic surges: trees flowering with eyes, rivers flowing uphill for a few hours, stone singing old Arathi law codes, shadows detaching during eclipses.

[IN-WORLD] WANT-WOLVES. Not true wolves. Descendants of ordinary forest canids twisted by generations of exposure to the Root-Crucible's emanations. Bark-dark, lean, split along the ribs with faint green light. They do not hunt flesh first. They hunt fixation. A traveler obsessed with revenge, lost love, forbidden knowledge, or divine ascension attracts them like blood in water. The pack circles silently until the victim speaks their desire aloud; then they answer in the voices of people connected to that desire. A disciplined mind can evade them; a desperate one cannot.

[IN-WORLD] MARBLE MOURNERS. Arathi funerary statues animated not by souls but by accumulated grief and spirit residue. They wander ruined plazas beneath the roots, cradling broken name-tablets. If approached respectfully, they guide travelers to burial sites. If someone steals from the dead, they become relentless pursuers. Their faces are smooth until they remember a name — then features briefly form.

[IN-WORLD] The Pilgrims of the Closed Wound carry blank clay tablets, asking Marble Mourners to impress lost names upon them. Those tablets are brought back to edge-shrines and added to walls called THE UNFORGOTTEN KILNS.

3. THE ARATHI CATACLYSM — THE EVENT
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Arathi were the FIRST CIVILIZATION of Eadras — a world-spanning advanced civilization predating dragons, unicorns, and the modern peoples. They were created in the earliest dawn of mortal life, before the divine progenitor projects of Mark, Melania, and Rand. Their divine origin is disputed because their own creation scriptures were destroyed with them.

[IN-WORLD] Before the cataclysm, Eadras was a land-heavy world: roughly 70% land, mostly shallow seas, vast continental shields, inland salt basins, warm shallow seas, endless marshlands, broad savannahs. The planet's crust was largely locked: thick, continuous, magically stabilized by primordial cooling and by the lingering order of Evye and Fryr's shaping. There may have been earthquakes and volcanoes, but not modern plate tectonics.

[IN-WORLD] The greatest mind of the Arathi was the archmage SSAR'VETH [CANONIZED_AS Kudur-eṭir; Arathi name "Kudur-eṭir" = "Seal-Opener," the name a foreshadowing of his eventual act; Ssar'veth survives as a post-Cataclysm folk-corruption]. His true motive was not conquest or vanity. He desperately wanted to STOP ENTROPY ENTIRELY — to end the decay of all things, the grinding-down of structures, memories, lives, loves, cities, stars, and worlds under the Omniversal Law of Destruction. His madness was compassion without humility. His blasphemy was not hatred of the world, but refusal to accept the conditions under which the world exists.

[IN-WORLD] A surviving line attributed to him:

  If the pot cracks, blame not the hand that mends it.
  Blame the law that made clay weak.

[IN-WORLD] Because entropy cannot be eliminated within the Omniverse, Kudur-eṭir sought leverage from outside the Mythos — from raw Chaos beyond the rational void cleaved from it by the Great Creator. He drilled a mathematical wound in reality, a conceptual aperture aimed beyond Eadras [CANONIZED_AS the First Breach in the Mythos Shell — the wiki makes this a specific load-bearing cosmological act long predating the Spear]. Through it he did not call a name. He described an impossibility. And the impossibility answered.

[IN-WORLD] What answered was the NEVERBORN — a pre-ontological accident from Chaos. Not born (birth requires continuity), not eternal (eternity requires ordered time), not even properly real. It simply remained where remaining was ontologically impossible.

[IN-WORLD] Contact with the Neverborn occurred at the NULL OBSERVATORY, an Arathi installation built at the still center of the largest landmass to study the boundary between Eadras and Chaos. After contact, local reality became less certain: stones grew internal horizons, shadows fell toward nonexistent lights, mathematical instruments returned answers that were correct but unusable. The Arathi sealed the Observatory three times. Kudur-eṭir opened it four.

[IN-WORLD] The SPEAR OF CRYSTALLIZED ENTROPY was forged through three components:
  1. Arathi high magic — Kudur-eṭir's mastery of ley-vault regulation, celestial alignment, and deep structure
  2. Chaotic impossibility — the Neverborn's contribution from outside the Mythos, allowing entropy to be crystallized as a manipulable principle without directly violating Omniversal Law
  3. Divine vulnerability — the leftover Wellspring of Creation from Evye and Fryr, accessible only during rare alignment

[IN-WORLD] When Dao witnessed the Spear's forging, he secretly laid a curse upon it: the Spear would grant the wielder's deepest desire, but only by stripping that desire from their living identity at the moment of ascension.

[SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI] The playground describes the Spear as a RITUAL INSTRUMENT that Kudur-eṭir used to CALL DOWN A FALLING STAR as the hammer of his ascension ritual — the council debated whether the Falling Star was a true celestial body, crystallized entropy given visible fire, or a fragment from Chaos. The wiki replaces this mechanism entirely. The Spear is NOT a ritual instrument. On God's Day every five thousand years, at noon in a particular time zone, the Spear opens a path for unlimited power to flow from the Source to whoever is holding it. Holding the Spear at that moment transforms the wielder into an Unrestricted Elder God. There is no falling-star call-down ritual.

[SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI] What actually destroyed Altea, in canon, is structurally different. When Kudur-eṭir held the Spear at the God's Day moment, Dao's curse hit him in a UNIQUE MODE because he was the Spear's creator AND had maintained ongoing contact with the Neverborn through the First Breach. Instead of cleanly stripping his motive, the curse SHATTERED HIS CONSCIOUSNESS — leaving him completely insane while preserving his godly power and arcane knowledge. The first Unrestricted Elder God was an insane Unrestricted Elder God.

[SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI] His shattered mind, now empowered by unbounded deific will, reached uncontrollably back through the First Breach. He did not aim. He did not summon a name. His unregulated deific pull simply reached out into Chaos through the existing hole, and what came through — manifested as the unintended product of an insane god's grasp — was the WORLDBREAKER (also called the Worldender): a ten-mile alien object foreign to the Mythos, from pure Chaos. Path: Chaos → First Breach → Limina → shattered PMP shell → Altea.

[IN-WORLD] Before the Worldbreaker arrived, the other Elder Gods confronted Kudur-eṭir and combined their power to bind him fully into his own Icon — an act formalized as TOTAL ICON BINDING, restricting his power to line of sight and concentrating his entire divine continuity in that one anchor. Then the Worldbreaker struck. The impact shattered the Icon. Because Kudur-eṭir's whole divine existence was concentrated there, he was ANNIHILATED — not banished, not exiled, ended.

[SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI] The playground proposes Kudur-eṭir as a "hollow divinity remembered as Ssar'veth the Unwanting, the Empty Crown, the God Without a Cause," lingering after the catastrophe as an emptied Elder God whose followers might encounter purposeless perfection in his divine residue (crystals that resist erosion but slowly erase inscriptions, beasts that collect objects without need, immortal-seeming organisms that cannot reproduce). The wiki replaces this entirely: Kudur-eṭir is FULLY EXTINCT. No ghost. No avatar. No whispering remnant. No buried will. The aftermath of Kudur-eṭir is absence, not lingering. The Spear-curse motifs (purposeless perfection, hollowed motivation) may still apply to other Spear-wielders, but not as a residue of a still-existing Kudur-eṭir.

[IN-WORLD] The Worldbreaker's impact on Altea caused, simultaneously and over years:
  - Direct annihilation of the capital
  - Mantle puncture
  - Subsurface reservoir flash-vaporization (massive high-pressure water reservoirs held within and below the crust)
  - Catastrophic crustal displacement — combined impact shock and steam-flash energy temporarily decoupled the crust from the mantle; the crust slid horizontally over the mantle as a semi-fluid layer
  - Sundering of the Altean Highlands into THREE CONTINENTS (Avalon, Damedes, Culethor) around the new ARATHALASSIC SEA
  - Large Igneous Province formation — lava extruded from the mantle wound for 2 to 8 million years afterward, eventually building the present-day continent of Arathyn at the impact center
  - The WHITE DELUGE — vast subterranean oceans of superheated water bursting upward through the new fissures
  - Sustained oven-like surface temperatures globally
  - Global windstorms, months of rebounding earthquakes, planet-wide firestorms
  - Permanent rainstorm lasting at least a year as the darkened atmosphere condensed planetary moisture
  - Magical deregulation as Arathi crystal mind-regulators shattered

[IN-WORLD] After the event: approximately 70% water with deep ocean basins. Plate tectonics initiated on a previously still world. Approximately 60% of all life on Eadras died. Life required millions of years to regrow.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki adds two long-term consequences the council did not develop:
  - Pre-Cataclysm atmosphere was ~35% oxygen, substantially richer than the modern ~21-25%. The cataclysm's planet-wide firestorms BURNED OFF enormous amounts of oxygen; the atmosphere normalized over millions of years.
  - CLIMATIC DISPLACEMENT. Because the crust shifted under stable poles, surface regions changed latitude essentially overnight in geological terms. Ecosystems dropped into incompatible climates. This contributed massively to biological collapse.

4. ALL ARATHI DIED — THE EXTINCTION ABSOLUTE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Arathi as a people, civilization, and mortal lineage died entire. Their language became a fossil. Their cities became pressure-crushed labyrinths. Their bones dissolved beneath new oceans or were buried beneath continents learning to move.

[INTERNAL_CONTRADICTION] Council Element I introduced TWO NOBLE HOUSES (House Ilvane and House Theryx) claiming Arathi descent. Council Element III then REVISED this: no true Arathi lineage endured. "If later cultures claim Arathi descent, they are mistaken, fraudulent, religiously symbolic, or politically opportunistic." A common saying among educated folk: "Every tyrant eventually discovers he is descended from Arath."

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki preserves House Ilvane and House Theryx as Remnant House FACTIONS in the Verdant Scar region — but treats them as the council's revision suggests: cultures projecting onto an extinction boundary, not literal descendants. House Ilvane's family records contain proof that an ancestor broke one of the focusing pylons during Kudur-eṭir's ritual, lessening the disaster — they seek the Root-Crucible to recover names of the erased dead. House Theryx romanticizes Kudur-eṭir as the "Almost-Elder," fund expeditions for the Root-Crucible's power, and have been known to capture Glassharts to use their crystal antlers as attempted keys to the Root-Crucible seal.

[IN-WORLD] Three categories of Arathi survival were briefly proposed and then rejected:
  - The High Refuges: mountain observatories, sky-temples, elevated colonies
  - The Drowned Continuities: sealed undersea vault-cities
  - The Exile Lines: Arathi-descended families abroad
[INTERNAL_CONTRADICTION] Council Element III withdrew all three. "Every last Arathi perished in the cataclysm or its aftermath." (Subsurface preservation of certain non-Altean Arathi ruins remains canon — see pre-Cataclysm relics — but no surviving Arathi PEOPLE.)

5. THE THREE LAST LIGHTS — ARATHI WHO ASCENDED
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] In the final age of Arath, three individuals ascended to godhood. Two spent all their divine essence trying and failing to save their people. The third saw they would fail, and chose to remain.

[IN-WORLD] AURALIS OF THE THOUSAND SHELTERS [CANONIZED_AS Auralis; her Arathi mortal name in canon is Ra-shakir, "Radiance-Tuner"]. Ascended deity of refuge. She poured her divine essence into sanctuaries across Arath — shielded schools, mountain vaults, seed-halls, temple basements, canal arks. Every one eventually failed. Her final miracle was not survival, but gentleness: many inside her shelters died without terror.

[IN-WORLD] THAMIOR THE UPHOLDER [CANONIZED_AS Thamior; Arathi mortal name Ilum-nāṣir, "Sacred-Light-Guardian"]. Ascended god of burdens and endurance. He tried to hold the crust together. For seven days after the Falling Star [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI: after the Worldbreaker impact], the first fractures slowed because Thamior set his divine shoulders beneath the plates of the world. Then the White Waters burst through, and his essence tore apart. Some mountain roots still contain veins called THAMIOR'S STRAIN, stone folded like muscle.

[IN-WORLD] ITHREN THE LAST ARATHI [CANONIZED_AS Ixthal; Arathi mortal name Ašar-eṭir, "Order-Opener"]. The survivor. Possessed probabilistic foresight — not time manipulation, but pattern-perception so deep he could read how present facts lean toward future outcomes. Because truth is indivisible and interlocking, a sufficiently powerful mind might read how today's flame casts tomorrow's shadow. He saw that no mortal evacuation, no shield, no prayer, no city-vault, no ascended intervention would preserve the Arathi people. So he did not spend himself trying.

[IN-WORLD] Common titles for him (playground variants, in modern usage canonicalized under Ixthal):
  - The Last Arathi
  - The God Who Remained
  - The Ash-Reader
  - Father of Dragons' Dawn
  - Caller of the First Herd
  - The Unforgiven Preserver
  - Dragons remember him as KINDLER

[IN-WORLD] His worship is complicated. Disaster survivors, dragon historians, unicorn-tended sanctuaries, orphans and last-of-lineage families, ecologists and druids, some Lirran mages, and those who must make triage decisions tend toward him. Many reject him. Two named sects were proposed:
  - THE ASHEN ACCUSERS: believe Ithren should have died with his people. Do not deny he gave rise to dragons and unicorns; argue that beauty born later does not absolve abandonment.
  - THE COAL KEEPERS: say his choice was the highest sacrifice — not of death, but of being misunderstood forever so life could have a future.

[IN-WORLD] Failed Arathi sanctuaries left RELIC ECOLOGIES, evidence of love losing to scale (not at Altea, which left nothing, but at other Arathi cities):
  - Valleys where fossils face upward, as if raised by hands
  - Glass domes on the floor of the Arathalassic Sea containing only clean air and dust
  - Mountain peaks where ash never settles because one dying goddess still exhales
  - Reefs grown in the shape of sleeping children

6. THE FIRST RENEWAL — DRAGONS AND UNICORNS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] After millions of years of regrowth, Ixthal acted twice. He caused the Source — the sun and primary origin of most magic in Eadras — to FLARE. Not moving time, not creating life from nothing. A divine modulation of Sourceshine intensity and magical spectrum.

[IN-WORLD] The first flare struck NINE carefully chosen reptile eggs. Those eggs became the first dragon eggs — the NINE EMBERS OF RENEWAL. Ixthal used his divine foresight to select eggs whose embryonic structures, locations, and surrounding Eldritch flows could survive transformation. Under Omniversal Law magic must be ruled by a mind; his divine mind regulated the flare.

[IN-WORLD] The nine were laid in different environments, allowing dragons to be global from their beginning. Playground proposed nine names and aspects:
  1. Aurelion Egg — gold-white; Source-aspected; ancestor of solar and high dragons
  2. Pyrrhax Egg — ember-red; fire-aspected; ancestor of volcanic and flame dragons
  3. Thalrune Egg — blue-green; water-aspected; ancestor of sea and storm dragons
  4. Kaorun Egg — basalt-black; earth-aspected; ancestor of mountain and deepstone dragons
  5. Veyrith Egg — pale silver; air-aspected; ancestor of sky and wind dragons
  6. Nythr Egg — star-black; night/shadow-aspected; ancestor of nocturne and secretive dragons
  7. Qorvash Egg — green-bronze; wild-aspected; ancestor of forest, marsh, and beast-shaping dragons
  8. Lirion Egg — violet-crystal; magic-aspected; ancestor of arcane dragons
  9. Mournscale Egg — pearl-gray; death/remembrance-aspected; ancestor of grave, bone, and ancestral dragons

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki preserves the Nine Embers structure but adds that all nine of the original dragons eventually ASCENDED TO GODHOOD through legitimate ascended-mortal pathways (sustained Source-only worship, divine notice, demonstrated cosmic significance) — they are the eldest dragon gods. THREE are publicly named in the modern pantheon: Cindirith the Magnificent (the Eldest, Platinum, 8th egg in the canonical accounting), Davian Sky Sovereign (Tempest Lord, Blue), and Koruss of the Eternal Fire (God of Dragonfire, Red). The other SIX withdrew — into deep places, into the Source itself, or into draconic-only spheres — and remain unnamed in any mortal-accessible record. The playground's specific names (Aurelion, Pyrrhax, Thalrune, Kaorun, Veyrith, Nythr, Qorvash, Lirion, Mournscale) are NOT canonical for the named three. The names of the six unnamed Embers are open canon.

[IN-WORLD] After hatching, fragments of the eggshells remained. They are among the oldest sacred draconic artifacts: the NINE SHELLS. Each contains regulated Source resonance. They cannot produce another first dragon, but they can stabilize draconic magic, sanctify a lair, or verify lineage myths. False Nine Shell relics are common. Real ones are priceless.

[IN-WORLD] Dragon scripture of the first hatching:

  After the long hunger of the world, when fern and scale had returned beneath the Source,
  Ithren climbed the ashless height and opened his remaining eye.
  He did not command the sun, for the Source is no lantern in a servant's hand.
  He pleaded in the language of endings endured.
  The Source flared.
  Nine eggs burned and did not break.
  Nine shells sang and did not melt.
  From them came claw, wing, horn, and word.

[IN-WORLD] The second flare was DIFFUSE rather than concentrated. The dragon flare was embryonic and lineage-forming; the unicorn flare was harmonic and herd-forming. Ixthal awakened a collective resonance in a herd of pale steppe-beasts already bound by social trust. Their horns became natural conduits for purified Eldritch — not "good magic" in a moral absolute sense, but highly ordered, life-stabilizing magic.

[IN-WORLD] Unicorn horns should not be casual spell components. They are living regulatory structures. Removed violently, they decay magically or become dangerous. Naturally shed horn-slivers, if unicorns shed at all, may be sacred gifts.

[IN-WORLD] The first unicorn herd arose in the FIRST MEADOW, a grassland grown atop an ash plain enriched by volcanic ash, White Deluge minerals, and patient ecological succession. The Source flare turned morning dew into radiant mist. The herd breathed it. Horns of living pearl erupted painlessly from their brows. Their hooves struck the ground, and dormant seeds germinated. Not instant global healing — local acceleration of vitality and purification.

[IN-WORLD] Unicorn descendants trace sacred migration routes called SILVER RUNS, moving through places of ecological harm to stabilize them. They are not domesticated, not mounts by default, and not symbols of virginal purity unless a specific culture imposes that. Their core meaning is restoration, discernment, and untamable grace.

[IN-WORLD] Dragons keep genealogies from the Nine. Unicorns keep PATHS. Dragons began as nine individuals — lineage from singular progenitors. Unicorns began as a herd — community from their first breath. That contrast matters.

[IN-WORLD] The first ancient accord between dragons and unicorns is remembered as the COMPACT OF HORN AND FLAME. Not a written treaty. A mythic accord. Dragons would not hunt unicorns; unicorns would not cleanse dragon lairs without invitation, because some dragon magic is fierce but not corrupt. The Mage Architect made an important clarification: unicorn purification does not erase all dangerous magic. It restores proper order. Fire belongs in a volcano. Death belongs in a grave. Shadow belongs at night. Corruption is not the same as intensity.

[FLAG_FOR_REVIEW] The First Meadow's exact present location was left ambiguous in playground — possibly path-defined ("wherever the eldest herd gathers to renew the Silver Runs, that place becomes the First Meadow ritually and magically") with an original physical location protected by unicorns and dragons together. Wiki cross-reference not performed for this specific detail.

7. FACTIONS AROUND THE VERDANT SCAR
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The VEYRAN WARDENS: mixed communities of elves, orcs, humans, goliaths, and beast-folk who live along the forest edge. They do not own the Scar; they keep bargains with it. Their children learn trail-songs in Elven modes because elven song-magic can soothe minor Eldritch turbulence. Orcish leatherworkers craft warded boots from plains-beast hide to protect travelers from root-bite and ley-moss fever. Gnomes maintain calibrated brass compasses that point not north, but toward stable magical pressure.

[IN-WORLD] HOUSE ILVANE: an Arathi Remnant House preserved in canon as a faction (the literal-descent claim is folk-fiction across the extinction boundary). Their family records contain proof that an ancestor broke one of the focusing pylons during Kudur-eṭir's ritual, lessening the disaster. They seek the Root-Crucible not to reclaim power, but to recover the names of those erased when the capital fell. They sponsor pilgrim expeditions and Marble Mourner tablet-impressions.

[IN-WORLD] HOUSE THERYX: claims Kudur-eṭir was not a monster but a visionary betrayed by cowardly gods and lesser minds. They cultivate his image as the "Almost-Elder." If they seek the Root-Crucible, they misunderstand its magic. They have been known to capture Glassharts to attempt to use their crystal antlers as keys to the Root-Crucible seal — a cruel and practical strategy that gives the Wardens reason to oppose them beyond ideology.

[IN-WORLD] PILGRIMS OF THE CLOSED WOUND: devotees of Evye, Fryr, and Lirra who believe the Verdant Scar is holy evidence that the world protects itself. They enter barefoot, carrying no iron weapons, and tie strips of undyed cloth around trees whose roots have swallowed ruins. Their doctrine: some doors should remain shut, even if treasure lies behind them.

8. MAGIC IN THE VERDANT SCAR — THREE RULES
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Magic in the Scar follows three rules:

  1. NAMED MAGIC IS SAFER THAN IMPROVISED MAGIC. Spells with established forms, gestures, songs, or written matrices are easier for the forest's regulating mind to recognize.
  2. EMOTIONAL CASTING IS AMPLIFIED BUT UNSTABLE. Sorcery driven by fear or obsession risks attracting Want-Wolves or awakening Arathi ruin-engines.
  3. NECROMANCY WORKS UNUSUALLY WELL — BUT NOT CLEANLY. Because so many bodies and animating spirits were trapped during the fall, necromantic rituals can locate remains, speak with death-echoes, or animate guardians. The practitioner must distinguish between a true spirit, a memory imprint, and a forest-generated mimic. Necromancy is not evil here. It is archaeology with moral consequences.

9. NAMES FOR THE EVENT AND ITS PIECES
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Scholarly names for the cataclysm:
  - The Arathi Cataclysm
  - The First Unzipping
  - The Tectonic Birth
  - The Great Inversion

[IN-WORLD] Religious name: The Day the World Broke Its Stillness.

[IN-WORLD] Common speech distinguishes the phases:
  - "When the Star Fell" = the ritual/impact moment [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI mechanism — see Section 3]
  - "When the White Waters Came" = the steam and superheated flood
  - "When Arath Drowned" = the civilization's fall
  - "When the World Began to Walk" = the beginning of plate tectonics; now a saying for earthquakes

10. MATERIALS DEPOSITED BY THE WHITE DELUGE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The White Deluge deposited strange materials that reshape post-Cataclysm economies:

  - STAR-PUMICE: floating impact stone that holds heat and enchantment
  - WHITE SALTS: mineral residues from superheated underwaters, useful in preservation, warding, and necromancy
  - ABYSSAL GLASS: black-blue glass formed where boiling waters met falling starfire
  - ROOT-METAL: deep metallic veins exposed by the unzipping, later mined by dwarves, gnomes, and giants

[IN-WORLD] These created post-Drowning economies. Dwarves and gnomes prize root-metal. Sea peoples control abyssal glass. Surface kingdoms fight over star-pumice deposits because it enables heat-retaining engines and certain forms of magitek. House Theryx might finance expeditions not only for glory but for monopoly over Arathi catastrophe materials. Scaldwyrms nesting in star-pumice fields makes mining dangerous.

12. NEW LIFE AFTER THE CATACLYSM
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The council established (and wiki preserves) post-Cataclysm creatures:

  - ABYSSAL CROWNWHALES: descendants of shallow leviathans that fled into newborn trenches. Crown-like bone lattices sense ley pressure through deep water. Sailors believe they remember the old land-roads now drowned beneath them.
  - VENT SERAPHS: long, translucent, ray-like creatures born around hydrothermal vents. Feed on mineral-rich heat and Eldritch seepage. Wing-fins glow gold and blue. Merfolk and Sea'tua-descended peoples treat them as omens of safe deep currents.
  - SCALDWYRMS: serpentine, armored survivors that nest in boiling fissures, volcanic coasts, and unstable sea-caves. Exhale clouds of mineral steam that can cook flesh inside armor. May descend from dragon-shaped beings or reptilian creations altered by hydrothermal magic, but are not true dragons.
  - CROWN-EELS: enormous silver-black eels that coil around towers and ley-spires. Feed on residual Eldritch leaking from broken Arathi conduits. Their spines are prized illegally because they can hold enchantments underwater.
  - THE LANTERN DEAD: pressure-bound death-echoes trapped in diving bells, crystal corridors, and sealed civic chambers. Appear as drifting lights inside old helmets or glass masks. Necromancers and Sea'tua spirit-guides can speak with them, but only by equalizing pressure physically and ritually. If brought too close to the surface, their echoes disperse.
  - THE BATHYRIC MANDATE: a collective of deep-sea intelligences descended from creatures altered by the Drowning. Proposed as creature, polity, or both; deferred for later development.

[IN-WORLD] PRESSURE ECHOES: residual patterns trapped in abyssal glass, repeating final moments without full personhood. Necromancers cannot simply interview Arathi citizens — most "Arathi ghosts" are imprints, not spirits. After millions of years, bodies are gone unless mineral-preserved, sealed, or magically maintained.

[IN-WORLD] PRE-CATACLYSM RELICS — fossil traces of the old 70%-land world:
  - Arathic Glass Reefs: fossil reefs vitrified by the White Deluge, containing impressions of extinct shallow-sea organisms
  - Stillbeasts: rare pre-Extinction organisms preserved in mineral stasis (not temporal stasis), sometimes awakened by mining. Not adapted to the modern world; usually die violently or tragically
  - Ashseed Forests: petrified forests from the old 70%-land world, their seeds occasionally reanimated by druidic or Qoxlian magic, producing trees that cannot survive modern rainfall patterns without care

ESTABLISHED CANON: THE VERDANT SCAR OF ARATH (from Element I)

  - The Verdant Scar grew over the ruins of the Arathi civilization after Ssar'veth [CANONIZED_AS Kudur-eṭir] first used the Spear of Crystallized Entropy [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI mechanism: the Spear is not a ritual instrument; it activates only at the God's Day moment]

  - The forest acts as a distributed magical regulating mind, preventing unstable Eldritch from becoming catastrophic
  - Major creatures: Glassharts, Want-Wolves, Marble Mourners
  - Important regions: The Green Mantle, The Tilted Miles, The Road of Wanting, The Sunken Forums, The Root-Crucible (The Place Under the First Root / The Shut Door / The Last Vault of Arath)
  - Major cultural factions: Veyran Wardens, House Ilvane, House Theryx, Pilgrims of the Closed Wound
  - Magic within the Scar is safer when named, structured, sung, or ritually stabilized; emotional casting is amplified but dangerous
  - Necromancy is used as a morally complex archaeological and funerary practice
  - The Spear itself is not located in the Verdant Scar; the forest preserves the consequences of its first use


CANON ADJUSTMENT: ARATH AND THE PLANETARY CATACLYSM (from Element II)

  - The Arathi civilization is established as an Atlantis analogue: a vast, advanced civilization of shallow seas, canals, ley-vaults, celestial observatories, and world-scale magical infrastructure
  - Before its fall, Eadras was approximately 70% land with mostly shallow seas
  - Ssar'veth [CANONIZED_AS Kudur-eṭir] used the Spear of Crystallized Entropy during a celestial alignment and called down the Falling Star [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI mechanism — the Spear is not a ritual instrument and the Falling Star (now called the Worldbreaker) was pulled through the First Breach by Kudur-eṭir's uncontrolled post-ascension reach, not summoned ritually]
  - The Falling Star [CANONIZED_AS Worldbreaker] did not merely destroy Arath. It shattered the planetary crust.
  - This event began modern plate tectonics in Eadras
  - The crustal "unzipping" released vast subterranean oceans of superheated water, causing the White Deluge
  - After the event, Eadras became approximately 70% water, with deep ocean basins and active tectonic plates
  - The Arathi capital, Arathalassa [CANONIZED_AS Altea — the body of water is the Arathalassic Sea], lies drowned [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI: Altea was annihilated, leaving no ruins; the Arathalassic Sea is the empty space between three continents born from the sundered Altean Highlands]
  - The Verdant Scar is recontextualized as a surviving inland catastrophe site
  - New geographic features: Arathalassic Deep, Boiling Rifts, Crown Trenches, Steamfall Cliffs, Shattered Shelves, New Mountains
  - New creatures and phenomena: Abyssal Crownwhales, Vent Seraphs, Scaldwyrms, Crown-Eels, The Lantern Dead


CANON CORRECTIONS ESTABLISHED (from Element III)

  - The Arathi were the First Civilization of Eadras
  - The Arathi were not ancestors of any modern people
  - All Arathi died in the catastrophe or its aftermath
  - The Falling Star [CANONIZED_AS Worldbreaker] caused an Extinction-Level Event
  - Approximately 60% of all life on Eadras died
  - The event transformed Eadras from a world of roughly 70% land and shallow seas into one of roughly 70% water with deep ocean basins
  - Plate tectonics began when the planet's crust was shattered and "unzipped"
  - Vast subterranean oceans of superheated water erupted upward during the White Deluge
  - Life required millions of years to regrow
  - Dragons emerged after this long recovery, as the first great race of the renewed world
  - Ssar'veth's [CANONIZED_AS Kudur-eṭir's] true motive was not conquest but the insane desire to stop entropy entirely
  - Ssar'veth [CANONIZED_AS Kudur-eṭir] contacted a Neverborn Chaos Entity, a lingering impossibility from outside Mythoi bubbles
  - Together, through Arathi high magic and impossible Chaos contribution, they created the Spear of Crystallized Entropy
  - The Spear weaponizes or focuses entropy; it does not and cannot abolish the Omniversal Law of Destruction
  - When Ssar'veth [Kudur-eṭir] used the Spear and ascended, Dao's curse stripped away his deepest motivation [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI: in Kudur-eṭir's unique case, the curse shattered his consciousness rather than cleanly stripping his motive]
  - Ssar'veth became a hollow divinity remembered as Ssar'veth the Unwanting, the Empty Crown, or the God Without a Cause [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI: Kudur-eṭir was annihilated when the Worldbreaker shattered his Total-Icon-Binding-anchored Icon; he does not linger]
  - The Neverborn may be partially anchored by the Spear; each use gives it more definition within reality
  - The Verdant Scar remains valid but is now the latest living skin over an ancient Arathi focusing wound, not an unbroken forest from Arathi times


CANON ESTABLISHED: THE THREE LAST LIGHTS AND THE FIRST RENEWAL (from Element IV)

  - The Arathi still died as a people and civilization; no mortal Arathi lineage survived
  - One individual Arathi survived by becoming the first Ascended Mortal Deity
  - Two other Arathi also ascended, but spent all their divine essence trying and failing to save their people
  - Auralis of the Thousand Shelters [Arathi name: Ra-shakir] — ascended deity of refuge who died trying to protect Arathi civilians
  - Thamior the Upholder [Arathi name: Ilum-nāṣir] — ascended deity of endurance who died trying to hold the breaking world together
  - Ithren the Last Arathi [CANONIZED_AS Ixthal; Arathi name: Ašar-eṭir] — the surviving ascended deity, associated with foresight, renewal, last seeds, dragons, and unicorns
  - Ithren [Ixthal] possessed limited precognition through deep perception of interlocking truth and consequence, not through time manipulation
  - He knew saving the Arathi was impossible and chose not to expend himself in vain
  - After millions of years of recovery, Ithren [Ixthal] caused the Source to flare and transformed nine reptile eggs into the first nine dragon eggs
  - Dragons emerged from the First Nine Dragon Eggs, also called the Nine Embers of Renewal [WIKI_EXPANDS: all nine eventually ascended to godhood through legitimate pathways; three are publicly named (Cindirith, Davian, Koruss); six withdrew and remain unnamed]
  - Ithren [Ixthal] later caused another Source flare, transforming a herd of proto-equine or steppe-beasts into the first unicorn herd
  - Dragons are the first great race of the renewed world; unicorns are the second
  - Dragons remember Ithren [Ixthal] as Kindler
  - Unicorns are tied to restoration, living Eldritch regulation, and sacred migration routes called Silver Runs
  - The first ancient accord between dragons and unicorns is remembered as the Compact of Horn and Flame
  - Ithren [Ixthal] is worshiped or feared as: The Last Arathi, The God Who Remained, The Ash-Reader, Father of Dragons' Dawn, Caller of the First Herd, The Unforgiven Preserver
