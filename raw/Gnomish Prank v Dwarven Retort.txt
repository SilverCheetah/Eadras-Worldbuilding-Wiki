Gnomish Prank – “Brewing a Hot Batch”
Among gnomes and dwarves, a long-standing prank has existed for centuries.
Every few years, a group of bold young gnomes will sneak into a dwarven brewery and secretly empty a small bag of their own brewing spices into one of the sealed vats. The blend contains cinnamon, cardamom, star anise, and — almost as an afterthought — a single paper-thin sliver of dried raven’s eye.
To a gnome, this is simply how beer is supposed to taste.
To a dwarf, it is an outrage.
When the batch is finally tapped weeks later, the dwarves discover their honest, malty stout has been transformed into something aromatic, complex, and dangerously warm. The first dwarf to drink it usually lets out a string of curses that echoes through the entire hall.
The dwarves call it “gnome-tainted swill.”
The gnomes call it “improvement.”

Dwarven Retort – Palebone Clay
The dwarves’ revenge is neither quick nor cheap.
After one too many of his own batches were ruined by gnomish spices, the dwarven forge master personally gave permission for a small amount of his rare white clay (known among dwarves as Palebone Clay) to be used. This clay — normally reserved for the highest-quality metalwork — is so powerfully absorbent that even a modest sack can strip most of the flavor and color from an entire brewing vat.
The brewers waited until a large batch of gnomish spiced beer was sealed and fermenting, then added the clay with their forge master’s blessing.
When the vat was tapped weeks later, the vibrant, complex beer had been reduced to a pale, watery shadow of itself — mildly spiced, but so weak it was barely recognizable as beer.
The forge master considered the sacrifice of his rare clay a fair price for justice.
To this day, when a dwarf sees a gnome looking particularly pleased with himself, he’ll simply grin and say:
“We still have more clay.”
