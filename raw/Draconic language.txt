Draconic (language)
Summary: Draconic is the primordial, instinctual language of dragonkind. It is an agglutinative abjad built on triconsonantal roots. Every root consists of exactly three consonants. Meaning is layered through a strict three-vowel template and additional suffixes. The language feels ancient, heavy, and resonant — an extension of the Fire itself. Dragons do not learn it; they awaken already speaking it.
Sources: Dragons.txt, Ratharil entry, Internal Cosmology of the Eadras Mythos.txt

Phonology
Consonants (14 total)
S, Z, Tr, Dr, Rr, Shr, Zr, Rh, Lr, K, G, Ng, Kh, Gh
Transcription Rule (for mortal/Common script only)

Multi-letter consonants have only the first letter capitalized (KKhR, ZKhR, LrRhDr, RhTrKh, TrKhR).
This is purely a transcription convention. The native Draconic runic script has no case distinction.

Vowels
Every root follows the rigid template:
C₁V₁ C₂V₂ C₃V₃
Each vowel position carries semantic or grammatical weight (aspect, relational status, elemental affinity, honor level). Dragons instinctively feel the correct vowels.
Morphology & Grammar

Triconsonantal Roots: Every core concept is a three-consonant root.
Agglutinative: Vowels and suffixes stack to create nuance, tense, social register, elemental resonance, and power level.
Longer, more complex forms indicate greater age, wisdom, or authority.
The language is highly context-sensitive and poetic; a single well-chosen root sequence can carry volumes of meaning.

Cultural & Magical Significance

Dragons consider Draconic sacred. To speak it improperly is an affront to the Fire.
In Rhtrakhadara (Ratharil), formal Draconic is used for all Council proceedings and treaties.
The language carries metaphysical weight. When spoken with true intent, it can subtly influence the Fire (raw Sourceshine / reflected Eldritch).
Mortals find correct pronunciation extremely difficult. Even master weavespinners can only approximate the deeper resonances.
Dragons rarely speak true Draconic to non-dragons unless the matter is of grave importance — either profound respect or a prelude to annihilation.

Writing System
Native Script: A runic abjad of angular, flame-like glyphs that flow and interlock. When carved into stone or traced with breath, the runes can appear to flicker with inner fire. The script is considered sacred; defacing it is a grave offense.
