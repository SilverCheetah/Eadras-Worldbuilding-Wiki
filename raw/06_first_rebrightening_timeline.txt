# Canon Timeline Proposal

## The Divine Discussions Occur During: **The First Rebrightening**

The councils we have been generating should be placed in the **modern era**, specifically during a period called:

> **The First Rebrightening**

This is the first major era after the Thousand-Year Dimming begins to truly recede.

It is not a single year. It is a transitional period, likely spanning several mortal decades, during which:

- the Weave is recovering unevenly;
- magic is becoming industrialized again;
- Bold and other conduit powers are rising;
- Deep Vault archaeology is becoming organized and politically competitive;
- older divine doctrines are being questioned;
- prayer volume and miracle disputes are increasing;
- the Divine Bureaucracy is visibly failing in places;
- mortal civilizations are becoming powerful enough to repeat Arathi-pattern mistakes;
- dragons and unicorns are becoming relevant to ancient-history questions again;
- the Black Hand is using renewed complexity as cover.

The councils themselves occur in a concentrated divine cycle within this era.

Suggested name:

> **The Baldaranic Council Cycle**

or, more formally:

> **The Convocation of the Reopened Ledger**

The second name sounds like something the Divine Bureaucracy would use. The first is better for worldbuilding shorthand.

---

## Why This Placement Works

**Lirra:**  
The timing must follow the Sorcerer Crusade and the Thousand-Year Dimming. Otherwise the Weave reforms have no urgency.

**Vos:**  
It must also follow the rise of modern administrative states. Orden, Bold, Amunorian scholarly networks, Azure Basin merchant law, Katharim death-record systems—these provide mortal machinery capable of receiving divine changes.

**Nara:**  
It must occur while ordinary people still live inside inherited trauma. If the world has become too comfortable, the reforms become philosophy. If it is still too broken, they become noise.

**Daj:**  
And it must occur before the next Demon War-scale disaster.

**Sylph:**  
Ideally. Though history has poor manners.

**Nox:**  
The Black Hand should already be active, embedded, patient, and watching. These reforms threaten them because clarity threatens conspiracies. But they also provide new systems to infiltrate.

**Ioa:**  
Then it is settled. The councils occur when the world is rebuilding fast enough to endanger itself.

# Suggested Relative Timeline

Because exact calendar years may vary by later canon, use relative dating first.

## Before the Councils

### 1. The Arathi Cataclysm  
The world is physically remade. Modern tectonics begin. Intelligent life is absent for roughly two million years afterward.

### 2. Dragons, Then Unicorns  
Dragons arise first. Unicorns later develop as herd-mind beings. The Great Exchange with dragons teaches unicorns temporary individuality.

### 3. The Demon War  
The Weave is created. The Three Who Stood die at the Final Wall. The Beast-Folk emerge from Rathar trauma. Xar’vion is imprisoned after the gods betray him. The Black Hand survives.

### 4. The Sorcerer Crusade  
Black Hand manipulation causes mass killing of Weavespinners, damaging the Weave and triggering the Thousand-Year Dimming.

### 5. The Thousand-Year Dimming  
Magic remains weakened, distrusted, politically constrained, and uneven.

### 6. The First Rebrightening Begins  
The Weave recovers enough for magical industry, conduit systems, vault archaeology, and divine institutional stress to accelerate.

This is where the councils begin.

# The Baldaranic Council Cycle

## Phase I: The Open Palm Council

This is the first major divine discussion we generated.

### Main Decisions

- Reinterpretation of the Doctrine of the Unheld Hand.
- Emergence of the **Doctrine of the Open Palm**.
- Proposal of the **Hearth-Witness Mandate**.
- Revival or formalization of the **Three Bells of Intervention**.
- Recognition that divine restraint must not become divine absence.

### Mortal Echoes

Within 1–5 years:

- priests begin receiving dreams, marginalia, omens, and conflicting instructions;
- Ascended Mortal Gods quietly push procedural reforms;
- Katharim death-priests start recording more **Petitions of Abandoned Circumstance**;
- Orden theologians begin drafting audit-compatible doctrine;
- rural hearth cults gain renewed theological prestige;
- conservative temples accuse reformists of weakening divine authority;
- some desperate communities misread the Open Palm as a promise of more miracles.

---

## Phase II: The Arathi Council

This is the second major divine discussion, corrected by the later clarifications.

### Main Decisions / Revelations

- Arath is reframed as warning, not ladder.
- Arathi “crystals” are correctly understood as **bio-engineered geo-substrate**.
- Pre-Cataclysm Eadras had no active plate tectonics.
- Modern tectonics are a post-Cataclysm condition.
- Deep Vault handling requires substrate ethics and geological caution.
- The **Vault Concordat** becomes possible.
- The **Fallback Doctrine** emerges.
- The **Unsealing Oath** is proposed.
- Unicorn herd-memory becomes relevant to Arathi archaeology.

### Mortal Echoes

Within 3–20 years:

- relic academies split between “crystal” traditionalists and geo-substrate reformers;
- trollish geobiologists, unicorn Herd-Listeners, and cautious Lirran scholars gain importance;
- Bold resists the Fallback Doctrine while claiming compliance;
- Orden pushes for standardized vault classification;
- Amunorian scholars debate whether Arath should be revered, mourned, or feared;
- Black Hand cells pursue Red and Black Vaults while manipulating classification records;
- dragons become harder to ignore as sources of ancient-site knowledge;
- unicorn herds become contested by scholars, priests, and relic hunters.

# How Divine Decisions Enter Mortal History

The gods should not simply announce decisions to the world.

That would violate the setting’s divine logic.

Instead, decisions filter downward through several channels.

## 1. Ascended Mortal Gods

Ascended Mortal Gods understand bureaucracy and mortality better than Elder Gods. They are the primary executive layer.

They may:

- issue revised priestly guidance;
- alter miracle-routing standards;
- sponsor reform synods;
- pressure celestial departments;
- send dreams to trusted saints, prophets, magistrates, or theologians.

Examples: Baldaran, Inki, Din’shadar, Aramet.

## 2. The Divine Bureaucracy

Reform becomes paperwork before it becomes doctrine.

Expect:

- new petition categories;
- revised miracle thresholds;
- audit offices;
- bureaucratic sabotage;
- rival departments disputing jurisdiction;
- delayed implementation;
- clerks inventing abbreviations no mortal should ever see.

The Bureaucracy does not instantly obey divine intent. It metabolizes it.

Often badly.

## 3. Mortal Synods and Councils

Mortal religions receive fragments and argue.

Possible institutions:

- **The First Open Palm Synod**  
  A reformist priestly gathering attempting to define the Open Palm.

- **The Hearth-Witness Hearings**  
  Testimony from widows, veterans, failed magistrates, famine survivors, and recently dead souls if allowed.

- **The Orden Audit of Sacred Procedure**  
  Highly procedural, influential, widely mocked.

- **The Bold Conduit-Theological Congress**  
  Attempts to reconcile divine reform with magical automation.

- **The Katharim Hearings on Abandoned Circumstance**  
  Severe, mournful, politically explosive.

## 4. Dreams, Omens, and Liturgical Drift

The gods often influence theology by pressure rather than proclamation.

Examples:

- old prayers begin changing wording in multiple regions independently;
- priests dream of an open hand instead of an empty sky;
- temple doors refuse certain officials;
- hearth fires burn blue during false miracle audits;
- death-priests hear unprocessed petitions in their sleep;
- unicorn herds refuse passage to relic expeditions lacking proper witnesses.

## 5. Crisis

Most divine reforms become real only after a mortal crisis forces adoption.

Possible triggering crises:

- a Bold petition-engine creates a Favor loop;
- an Arathi geo-substrate vault is misclassified and causes a regional tectonic disaster;
- a Hearth-Witness exposes divine negligence in a famine;
- a Black Hand cell manipulates Three Bells intervention rules;
- a unicorn Long Strayer returns carrying a dangerous Arathi memory;
- an Oru-Sha War-Substrate site awakens;
- a demon remnant exploits weakened Weave channels.

# Recommended Canon Placement

## Best Placement

The divine discussions occur:

> **During the First Rebrightening, roughly one to three generations after the Thousand-Year Dimming begins to lift, but before modern magical civilization reaches full industrial confidence.**

This is the sweet spot.

The world is strong enough to act on divine reform, but fragile enough that reform matters.

## The Councils Are Not Public

The mortal world does **not** know the Elder Gods held these exact conversations.

Mortals encounter the aftermath as:

- new doctrines;
- bureaucratic reforms;
- prophetic dreams;
- religious disputes;
- sudden divine silence in some offices;
- increased miracle audits;
- new relic laws;
- strange consistency across unrelated temples;
- saints saying things that sound suspiciously coordinated.

## Suggested Timeline Anchor

If using a campaign or wiki calendar, define:

> **Year 0 ORP — Open Palm Reckoning**

or

> **Year 0 FR — First Rebrightening**

The first is theological.  
The second is historical.

I recommend using both:

- **FR** for broad historical dating.
- **ORP** for religious reform dating.

Example:

> The Hearth-Witness Mandate was first received by mortal temples in **12 FR / 3 ORP**, though Orthodox Unheld churches deny the legitimacy of that dating.

# Concrete Timeline Draft

## 0 FR — The First Rebrightening Recognized

Major magical institutions agree that the Thousand-Year Dimming is no longer the dominant magical condition, though the Weave remains scarred and uneven.

## 3 FR — The Vault Acceleration

Multiple Deep Vault discoveries occur within a short period. Bold, Orden, Amunorian scholars, and Azure Basin private expeditions compete for access.

## 5 FR — The Prayer-Routing Scandal

A divine administrative failure becomes impossible to ignore. This may involve famine, plague, war widows, or denied Weavespinner protection.

This triggers the Open Palm Council.

## 6 FR / 0 ORP — The Open Palm Council

Divine council produces:

- Doctrine of the Open Palm;
- Hearth-Witness Mandate;
- Three Bells revival;
- increased scrutiny of divine abandonment.

Mortals do not know this directly.

## 8–12 FR — First Mortal Echoes

Priests, death-clerks, hearth cults, and Ascended Mortal Gods begin transmitting reform.

## 13 FR — The First Open Palm Synod

Mortal reformists gather. Conservative priesthoods object.

## 15 FR — Arathi Geo-Substrate Incident

A vault disaster reveals that Arathi “crystal” is living geo-substrate and that old systems may react badly to modern tectonics.

This triggers or follows the Arathi Council.

## 16 FR — The Arathi Council

Divine discussion produces:

- corrected understanding of Arathi geo-substrate;
- Vault Concordat pressure;
- Fallback Doctrine;
- Unsealing Oath;
- greater attention to unicorn herd-memory and dragon testimony.

## 18–25 FR — Vault Concordat Negotiations

Orden supports regulation. Bold resists limitations. Amunorians demand scholarly access. Beast-Folk and Katharim push moral restrictions. Dragons ignore subpoenas.

## 22 FR — Herd-Listener Recognition

Some mortal institutions formally recognize Herd-Listeners as valid witnesses in relic matters.

## 25–35 FR — Demon War Memory Crisis

New evidence, divine pressure, or Black Hand activity forces renewed examination of the Demon War.

This is the best window for the upcoming Demon War council.

Likely developments:

- automated petition-routing;

- audit procedures;

- trade-safe theology.

- trauma-survival rituals;
- sacred laughter;
- warnings against memory tyranny;
- festivals mocking divine paperwork.
