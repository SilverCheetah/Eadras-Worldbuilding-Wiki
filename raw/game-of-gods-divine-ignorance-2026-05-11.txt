Game of the Gods — divine ignorance of causation, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture: Josh established that even the gods do not know the
answer to the Game's causation question. The reason: the Game was made
by Dao in secret; Dao did not explain it; it simply appeared one day
in Solarion, complete and already playing. The mystery is alive, not
historical -- Dao is the canonical creator and the canonical
withholder.)

== Canon ==

The gods of Eadras DO NOT KNOW whether the Game causes Eadras or
Eadras causes the Game (or any of the other positions in the
causation debate).

This is not divine secret-keeping or coyness from the pantheon.
It is genuine ignorance.

The mechanism:

1. The Game was created by Dao in secret. (Existing canon, preserved.)
2. Dao did not explain the Game's nature, rules, or relationship to
   the world it models.
3. The Game appeared one day in Solarion, complete and already
   playing.
4. The current pantheon has learned the Game's rules through
   millennia of play -- by trial, observation, and practice, not by
   instruction.
5. They cannot interrogate the designer in any tractable way. (Dao
   is canonically a maker-figure, an Avatar of the Omniversal Creator,
   not a pantheon member who can be called to a meeting.)

Consequence: the gods who give different answers when asked about
causation are reporting their own private theories formed during long
play -- not authoritative knowledge. The disagreement between them is
not serious in their own minds because none of them claim authority on
the question. The Game's nature is, from any single vantage including
the divine one, OPAQUE.

== Story rationale (author-side) ==

A still-existing secret-maker is a better story than a dissolved-
designer-team. The mystery is LIVE, not HISTORICAL. Dao continues to
exist somewhere in the cosmological frame, and continues not to
explain. This keeps the question generative for ongoing canon rather
than closing it as a sealed past event.

(Earlier in the session a reading was briefly considered in which the
Primordial Duo made the Game as their last creative act before
dissolution. This reading was discarded as making weaker story: a gap
left behind by sacrificed creators is less narratively productive than
an active maker who withholds.)

== Implication for the causation debate ==

The previously-established four positions (Game-causes-Eadras /
Eadras-causes-Game / Isomorphism / Principled-refusal) and the
emerging asymmetric hybrid reading are all positions that mortals --
and gods -- have arrived at by inference from observation. None of
them rests on instruction from Dao. The principled-refusal position
(Doctrine of the Unheld Hand) reads, in light of this canon, as
particularly mature -- it acknowledges what the pantheon also
acknowledges: that the answer is not accessible to anyone currently
inside the system.
