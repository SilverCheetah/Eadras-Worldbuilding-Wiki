Compiled Lore: Velrathi and Their Descendant Races
(Everything you have told me, presented in full without condensing or summarizing)

The three original races created by a set of three sibling gods are the Elfin, the Terran, and the Velrathi.
The Velrathi were the senior race. They experimented on their own in-vitro progeny and engineered three child races from that material:

Orug — created as close servants and warriors for the Velrathi.
Grug — created as the technicians and maintainers responsible for keeping the magitek, semi-magitek, steamworks, and gearworks of Velrathi (and later Rathari) civilization running.
Rathari — created as the scholar-engineers.

From the Orug (Child Race of Velrathi)
The Orug eventually diverged into the Orcs and the Ogres.
Orcs (and Ogres) have a culture centered around plains and animals, similar to historical Mongols or Plains Indians. They are primarily nomadic, following the herds across the plains. They are very skilled blacksmiths and leather workers. They do not have their own cities (except possibly one that has not been detailed yet). Their society is built around mobility and craftsmanship using materials from the herds they follow.
From the Grug (Child Race of Velrathi)
The Grug were the engineered technician class. Over time they naturally split based on differing interests and temperaments:

The more intellectual, careful, and precise ones evolved into Gnomes.
The scrappier, improvisational, scavenging ones evolved into Goblins.

Gnomes

They do not have their own homeland or gnomish civilization with its own territory. They live among the other races, embedded in the cities of other peoples.
They are scholarly artifact-hunters and archaeologists, focused on recovering lost Arathi technology.
They treat the search for Arathi knowledge as almost a religious pursuit.
Their cultural turning point came when the goblin cultural icon Inki (who later became an ascended mortal deity) opened the first Arathi vault in millions of years. This sparked a renaissance of invention.
They have only recovered about five percent of Arathi magitek and crystalmancy knowledge, but even that small amount makes them the most innovative culture in the world.
They puzzle out the technology from ruins, shattered vaults, and fragmented devices in an Indiana Jones-style scholarly investigation.
After the vault opening, they changed their naming conventions to honor the Arathi civilization. They now use names layered over or borrowed from ancient Arathi naming styles (which resemble Akkadian, Egyptian, and Hebrew). They no longer primarily use traditional Grug/goblin-style names.

Goblins

They also do not have their own homeland.
Some live integrated in cities, while others live outside civilization as feral scavengers.
They are considered scavengers on the edge of society. A common rude nickname for them is “shit-pickers.”
They are hoarders who maintain vast stocks of items pulled from trash and ruins that “might be useful someday.”
They have extensive social networks that span multiple cities and civilizations. These networks are not open to civilizations that look down on or mistreat goblins.
When gnomes need a specific ancient part or artifact, they often ask goblins to find it. Goblins frequently already have the item somewhere in their hoards (though it might be in another city).
Gnomes have a mixed view of goblins: some see them as kin, while many others see them primarily as useful tools for dirty work.

Example of a Successful Goblin: Harvey Wash

Goblin name is traditional, but he adopted the human-sounding name Harvey Wash to blend in with the humans of the city.
He dresses like a wealthy human in fine silks and high-quality human-style clothing.
He is known as the King of the Golden Road.
He controls an empire of goblin and other workers who collect thunderpots (chamber pots) at dawn from the city.
The waste is brought to a secondary yard near the city, condensed into larger wagons, and transported to his main processing dump well outside the city walls.
He processes all the collected waste into fertilizer and urea, which he sells. He also sorts and processes all other scavenged junk. Some items go into legitimate businesses, others feed the goblin network.
He does not hoard his profits. Instead, he invests them back into the poor of the city: running soup kitchens, safe sleeping places, and providing fair-wage employment as workers in his operation.
He has a mutual agreement with the Thieves’ Guild: they do not steal from his operations, and once a year he throws them a massive, wild party (“humdinger”) in a secret location where they can fully let loose.
He has been trying to establish a formal Scavengers’ Guild, but the existing guild council refuses to recognize it.

The city where Harvey operates is Bold (B-O-L-D), one of the oldest cities in the world. It contains the Tower of Seven Spires (a major sorcerous fortress) and serves as the capital with the royal castle. Harvey deliberately placed his main dump far outside the expanding city walls so it would not be encroached upon for centuries.
