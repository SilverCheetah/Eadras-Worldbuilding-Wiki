THE ARATHI & THE LOST GOLDEN AGE — IN-WORLD FACTS
World: Eadras
Source: Grok chat session, 2026-05-09
Document type: In-world reference (the Arathi precursor civilization, their relationship to the conduits, the Cataclysm, and the trauma they left for everyone who came after)
Companion documents: "The Arathi & The Lost Golden Age — Meta-Commentary" and "The Arathi & The Lost Golden Age — Principles and Diagnosis"


================================================================
1. WHO THE ARATHI WERE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Arathi were the precursor civilization of Eadras — the most sophisticated and powerful civilization in the planet's history.

They were a POST-SCARCITY society. They had:
- No real material scarcity.
- Effectively no major external enemies.
- Mastery of magic, technology, and infrastructure.
- A civilization that endured for roughly ten million years.

They believed mastery, order, and abundance were permanent features of their existence.


================================================================
2. WHAT THE ARATHI DID NOT CREATE — THE CONDUITS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Arathi did not build the conduits.

The conduits are part of the planet's permanent magical continuity. They are baked into the world's magical bones, fixed in absolute space relative to the planet's core. The Arathi inherited the conduits — they found something ancient and pre-existing already part of the planet itself.

There are fourteen conduit endpoints visible on the surface. These endpoints have existed since before the Arathi and remain after them. The conduits are eternal and unmoving. They cannot be destroyed.

What the Arathi did was build the most sophisticated civilization in history ON TOP OF this gift.


================================================================
3. WHAT THE ARATHI ACTUALLY BUILT
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Arathi built planet-scale magical infrastructure layered over the conduits:

- Seven world-piercing conduit systems they engineered as part of their civilizational network.
- Crystal minds that regulated Eldritch flow across the entire globe.
- Cities built out of living crystal that doubled as both architecture and spellwork.
- Crystalmancy and magitek at a level no later civilization has matched.
- Magitek trains, sophisticated logistics, post-scarcity production.

Modern Eadras civilizations have access to only fragments of this. They can build a Tower of Lirra at some conduit endpoints. They scrape together what local magic they can. Compared to what the Arathi had, modern societies are working with scraps.


================================================================
4. THE PRE-CATACLYSM PLANET
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] During the Arathi era, the planet's crust did not undergo continental drift. There were no shifting plates and no continent-scale movement.

The planet was not geologically dead — there were earthquakes, and there was constant hydraulic activity from massive underground oceanic reservoirs exchanging water with the surface and going back underground. But the crust itself was stable.

To the Arathi, the world was a stable, predictable thing. The continents were where they had always been. The conduits were permanent. The supremacy of their civilization was permanent.

They never imagined the planet could betray them on a continental scale.


================================================================
5. THE WORLDBREAKER AND THE CATACLYSM
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Worldbreaker was triggered by Kudur-eṭir — an Arathi who reached into something he should not have reached into.

The impact and its immediate aftermath included:
- The White Deluge.
- Continent-spanning firestorms.
- The crust decoupling from the mantle.
- The continents physically sliding and reshaping.
- The Arathi highlands tearing apart into multiple new continents.

By the end of the first day, most Arathi were already dead. Eventually the effects spread across the entire planet and killed all of them.

The Arathi never witnessed the aftermath of what they had done. They were vaporized in the first moments. They died believing they were untouchable.


================================================================
6. THE CONDUITS SURVIVED — BUT THE GROUND DID NOT
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The conduits remained exactly where they had always been, locked to the planet's core in absolute space.

The crust, however, slid. The land above the conduits physically moved.

The result: the Arathi had built their greatest works directly on top of what they believed was permanent — and they were right, the conduits ARE permanent — but the surface above the conduits walked away from them. Their greatest cities ended up sitting on dead ground while the real conduits poked up somewhere else entirely.

This crustal movement happened AFTER nearly all Arathi were already dead. There was no one alive to watch it.


================================================================
7. THE TIMELINE OF DEATH
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Arathi did not experience the slow tragic fall of their civilization. They experienced:

- The impact of the Worldbreaker.
- The White Deluge.
- The firestorms.
- Death.

Then nothing.

The continental drift, the slow reshaping of the planet, the oceans forming where the highlands used to be — all of that happened to a world without Arathi in it. The civilization did not collapse poetically. It was instantly erased, with no one left alive to understand what had happened.

The Arathi never got to be traumatized by their own fall. They died inside the moment of realization, before any cultural processing could occur.


================================================================
8. THE TRAUMA INHERITED BY EVERYONE WHO CAME AFTER
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Arathi themselves carried no scar. They did not survive long enough to develop one.

The trauma was inherited by every civilization that came after.

Every later civilization on Eadras is secretly haunted by the story of a people who:
- Had mastered the world.
- Had no scarcity.
- Had no real enemies.
- Vanished overnight because one of their own decided the world was not perfect enough.

This produces the foundational cultural anxiety of post-Arathi Eadras: that even mastery, order, and abundance offer no protection when reality decides to break.


================================================================
9. THE PROVERB
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Modern cultures on Eadras carry the echo of the Arathi catastrophe in their language.

The proverb "Proud as Arath before steam" means roughly: "arrogant enough to think nothing could ever go wrong." It is used to describe a person, a faction, or a civilization that has become complacent about the permanence of their position.


================================================================
10. WHY LATER SOCIETIES KEEP RETURNING TO THE CONDUIT ENDS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Modern civilizations on Eadras keep building near the fourteen conduit endpoints — not just because of the power available there, but because of an unconscious cultural drive to prove they are not Arathi.

Every later civilization is unconsciously trying to demonstrate:
- That they will not make the same mistake.
- That they will be the ones who finally get it right.
- That mastery of the eternal can be safe in their hands where it was not in Arathi hands.

They are simultaneously chasing power and terrified of succeeding. The fact that the Arathi held this same power and still managed to break the world means that climbing toward Arathi capability is itself climbing toward the same precipice.


================================================================
11. THE SCAR ON THE ETERNAL
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The deepest fact of the Arathi legacy is not that they fell. It is that they fell on top of something that literally cannot fall.

The conduits are permanent. Eternal. Baked into the world's magical continuity since before the Arathi ever existed.

And the Arathi still managed to turn that permanence into a planetary extinction event.

That is not just failure. That is profanity — the demonstration that mortal arrogance can scar even the eternal. The fourteen conduit endpoints stand as permanent monuments to this fact.


================================================================
12. THE BURIED CHAOS WAR — A DEEPER LAYER
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] There is a secret about the Arathi golden age that even the Arathi themselves never knew.

Centuries before the Worldbreaker, the Arathi faced an earlier catastrophe: a Chaos incursion that struck their civilization from nowhere, with no warning, in violation of everything Arathi magical law would predict.

It started small. Localized glitches in reality:
- A crystal spire that started growing wrong.
- A conduit that briefly output raw Sourceshine instead of regulated Eldritch.
- A magitek train that arrived before it departed.
- A street where time ran slightly backwards for six seconds.

The Arathi response was pure arrogance: "This is clearly a malfunction in our system. We will study and correct it."

Then the incursions grew. Districts where physics stopped behaving. Living geometry that should not exist. Things that looked at observers with too many eyes.

By the time the massive breach finally opened, it was too late.

The Arathi lost. The greatest empire in history was on the verge of being swallowed by Chaos. The crystal spires, the mastered conduits, the perfect civilization — none of it was enough.

The only reason the timeline still exists is because the universe sealed the entire event in a closed time bubble (see "Cosmology of Naming & Observation" for the metaphysics of how this is possible). The event was surgically excised from reality. The Arathi golden age that history records is, technically, the second one.


================================================================
13. WHAT REMAINS HIDDEN
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The buried Chaos War is recorded in the Tome of Beldoran, because the chosen observer player characters preserved it through their memory. However, the Tome has chosen to keep that chapter sealed and unopened in this age.

The Tome is therefore not just hiding a war. It is hiding the fact that the Arathi golden age was ALREADY LOST ONCE, and that even at their absolute peak the Arathi were not enough to stop Chaos.

The only beings who carry that knowledge are the chosen observer PCs — who could, theoretically, still exist in the modern era as ancient, half-mad figures muttering about a war no history book acknowledges.
