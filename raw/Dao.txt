Dao is an avatar of the Omniversal Creator, the God of Gods.

Dao adopted three children when he noticed that they had the potential to be great Adepts. Their mother was being burned at the stake as a witch, but Dao gathered the children and made the village forget they existed. The children were triplets, named Rand , Mark, and Melania .

Over the years, Dao taught them many things, including the use of Eldritch and they observed the creation of both the Runes of Power and the Runeswords.

All of Dao’s creations exist outside of any magical strictures that might later develop, they are written into the very fabric of the omniverse (and work in every Mythos.)

Dao typically appears as a humanoid in his mid-sixties, sturdy but lined—like a man who's seen decades of work and wonder. Silver-white hair, shoulder-length and tied back loose, gray beard trimmed neat. Face creased with laugh lines and old scars, eyes sharp and twinkling behind half-moon spectacles (lenses scratched, perched low). He wears clothing that's clean, fitted, and right for whatever he's doing—practical over flashy. When he's not in "work mode," he defaults to a patched linen tunic (stitches from thorns, burns), rolled cuffs on pants, waistcoat over top—buttons worn smooth, brass pocket watch tucked into the waistcoat's left chest pocket, chain hooked to a buttonhole and dangling just enough to glint. Cloak slung over one shoulder if wet—dark wool, hood up. Leans on a gnarled stick only when he feels like it; otherwise moves steady, deliberate. To mortals: soft voice, "Easy now, lad"—he appears harmless, like a village elder who makes folks listen or laugh, not fight.
