Ssarvath and the First Breach
Long before God’s Day, Ssarvath — while conducting forbidden research into ways to permanently eliminate entropy — drilled the first hole through the Mythos Shell.

Through this breach he made contact with the Neverborn (the singular entity relevant to Eadras).
This contact, combined with the fact that he was the creator of the Spear of Crystallized Entropy, made him uniquely vulnerable when the curse later took hold.


God’s Day and the Worldbreaker (10 Million Years Ago)
On God’s Day, Ssarvath claimed the Spear of Crystallized Entropy and ascended to Unrestricted Elder Godhood. Empowered by this ascension, his already fractured and insane mind reached uncontrollably into the breach he had created in the Mythos Shell.
He did not deliberately summon the Worldbreaker.
Instead, his unregulated deific will simply reached forth into Chaos through the existing hole. What came through was the Worldbreaker — an alien object from pure Chaos that manifested as the unintended result of his insane, directionless pull.
Once inside the Mythos, the Worldbreaker then shattered the exterior shell of the Prime Material Plane and was used to strike the world itself, creating the massive Worldbreaker Scar.
The Curse’s Unique Effect
Because Ssarvath had previously contacted the Neverborn and was the creator of the Spear, Dao’s curse interacted with him differently than with others. It did not simply strip his deepest desire — it shattered his consciousness, leaving him completely insane while still allowing occasional flashes of his former brilliance and knowledge.

Kuo-toa – The Fracture Cult
Summary
The Kuo-toa are a deeply divergent, fanatical branch of the Seatua lineage. They are obsessive worshippers of the Gaps — fractures in reality that lead directly into Limina, the liminal plane between what is and what could be. They are chaos cultists, but their worship is coldly intellectual: they believe that by widening these fractures and studying what comes through, they can eventually force existence to reconcile its contradictions.
Origin & Belief
During and after the Demon War, certain Seatua colonies near major rifts were exposed to raw energies leaking from Limina. They became convinced that the universe is fundamentally broken and that the only path to true order is through perfect understanding of its fractures.
They do not worship Chaos itself, nor demons as gods. They worship the Gaps as sacred doorways. Demons are simply the most obvious entities that have successfully crossed from Limina into the Prime Material — living proof that the boundary can be breached. The Kuo-toa study them the way a scholar studies a particularly dangerous equation.
Key Cosmological Truth
All major fractures in the Prime Material — whether created by the Chaos Crusades or the summoning of the Worldbreaker — ultimately open into Limina first. From Limina, entities can then spill into the Prime Material. This is the same path the Worldbreaker followed when Ssarveth summoned it from pure Chaos.
The Kuo-toa believe that if they can map, widen, and ultimately stabilize enough Gaps, they can achieve “Final Reconciliation” — merging Limina and the Prime Material into a single, perfected reality with no contradictions.
Appearance & Society
Pale, rubbery, fish-like humanoids with bulging eyes and slick skin. Many display shifting, liminal mutations — extra limbs, flickering forms, or patches of unreal matter. Their cities are nightmarish coral cathedrals built directly into rift walls, filled with constantly shifting geometries and obsidian tablets carved with “corrections” from Limina.
They speak in overlapping, hyper-precise layers of prophecy. A single Kuo-toa can argue multiple possible versions of reality simultaneously. Their logic is flawless within their own broken framework, which makes them deeply unsettling to deal with.
Role in the World
Other sea races give Kuo-toa territories a very wide berth. They are sometimes tolerated near major rifts because they act as early warning systems for Limina bleed-through. Surface nations fear them — Kuo-toa raiding parties occasionally surface to “collect corrections” (living subjects for their rituals and observations).

Sahuagin – The Blood Navigators
Blood Fleets
They are submarine / underwater fleets, not surface ships.

Massive, living war-constructs grown from coral, bone, and the shells of ancient sea beasts.
They move through the water column like predatory schools — sleek, silent, and terrifyingly fast.
Some of the largest Blood Fleets have “sail” structures made of enormous living manta rays or jellyfish colonies that catch deep currents.
They rarely surface. When they do, it is usually to raid coastal settlements or to fulfill a particularly strong “blood prophecy.” Surface dwellers describe them as “islands that hunt.”

The Sahuagin see the open surface ocean as a shallow, noisy, and dishonorable place. True navigation and worthy combat happen in the deep currents.

The Five Modern Oceanic Races
1. Merrow – The Tideweavers
Symbiotic coral architects and patient gardeners who treat the ocean like a vast, breathing garden.

Elongated, elegant bodies covered in living polyps and anemones that function as clothing, tools, and mood/status displays.
They sing entire coral megastructures and kelp cathedrals into existence over decades.
Society revolves around Growth Circles — massive, multi-generational collaborative projects.
Most open to surface trade, exchanging living coral art and current-tuned navigational shells.
Speak in layered, overlapping harmonies that sound like an entire choir.

2. Sirens – The Echo Weavers
Sonic architects who literally reshape underwater geography with complex layered song.

Tall and willowy with subtle feather-like fins and haunting multi-tonal voices.
A choir of Sirens can collapse cliffs, carve new trenches, or create stable “song tunnels” through dangerous currents.
Live in floating, ever-shifting cities of giant hollow shells and bubble-glass domes suspended in the water column.
Social status is determined by vocal range and harmonic complexity. The greatest are called Deep Conductors.
Have a dry, cutting sense of humor and love impossibly complex puns only other Sirens understand.

3. Shimmercloaks – The Echo Traders
Living trade networks who treat memory and emotion as physical currency.

Long, sinuous, semi-translucent bodies with shifting iridescent patterns. Can flatten paper-thin or balloon into temporary trading halls.
Peel off pieces of their own skin containing perfect living memories/emotions and trade them as currency.
Their entire society is one vast, ever-shifting web of debts and favors. Breaking a deal causes visible, painful discoloration for years.
Ultimate neutral middlemen of the oceans. Surface captains sometimes find one waiting at their anchor chain with the offer: “One memory for one favor.”

4. Ink Lords – The Story Eaters
Communal octopus-like scholars who travel among other societies to collect and consume stories.

Octopus-like upper bodies with heavily armored crab-like lower shells. Can expel massive clouds of magical ink that serve as both defense and libraries.
They feed on well-told, emotionally true stories for nourishment. Feeding does not remove or erase the memory from the teller.
Many live for years among other races (surface or oceanic) specifically to gather new stories.
Maintain hidden Memory Groves where particularly delicious stories are stored in suspended ink droplets.
Sardonic, hungry, and surprisingly honorable — they will never break a story-debt.

5. Tideclad – The Current Wardens
Disciplined martial protectors who weaponize and endure extreme currents, riptides, and pressure.

Heavily armored, robust merman forms with natural bone plating and powerful fin structures.
They “clad” themselves in living water forces, forming temporary armor and blades from redirected currents and pressure waves.
Battle is viewed as “releasing the sea’s tension” — they fight by turning an opponent’s own momentum and the ocean’s force against them.
Highly disciplined and honor-bound. They guard sacred boundaries, dangerous rifts, and important migration routes.
Live in fortified trench-cities and roving warden schools that patrol the most volatile waters.

Important Canon Note
No entirely new sapient races have been created in the last 10,000 years (except for golems, homunculi, and other artificial constructs). All modern races descend from the ancient progenitors through natural devolution, cultural divergence, or the special cases of Katharim and Beast-folk.

Xarvion
Xarvion was a brilliant and tragic Velrothi archmage who lived during the final centuries of the Firethorn Empire. Born a sickly orphan, he was fiercely protected by his elder sister Velena (replace Elena). After Velena was raped and murdered by a gang of Tarran noble youths, the young Xarvion swore two vows: he would never be powerless again, and he would find a way to bring her back.
Centuries of ruthless political, magical, and military maneuvering were all in service to one goal — claiming the Spear of Crystallized Entropy on God’s Day so he could ascend to Unrestricted Elder Godhood and resurrect Velena. He did not create the Spear (it was created long before by Ssarveth). He simply positioned himself to wield its ultimate power.
His actions destabilized the already decaying Firethorn Empire. When he finally claimed the Spear on God’s Day, his ascension triggered the War of Domination, a desperate summoning of a Demon Prince, and the catastrophic Demon War. The empire’s collapse was a side effect of his obsession, not his primary intent.
After the Gods, Dragons, and Unicorns ended the Demon War, the divine punishment for the chaos fell upon the elder races as a whole. Xarvion himself was bound into his Icon and driven mad by Dao’s curse, which stripped him of his deepest desire.

Velena
Velena was a Velrothi woman and the elder sister of Xarvion. She was strong-willed, fiercely protective, and served as her sickly younger brother’s only shield and family in the brutal final days of the Firethorn Empire.
She was raped and murdered by a gang of arrogant Tarran noble youths during the empire’s degenerate decline. Her death became the central tragedy that drove Xarvion’s entire life.
Velena currently resides in the Verdant Fields of Solarion, a peaceful pastoral afterlife of gentle golden light and quiet happiness. She is content and does not (yet) wish to return to the living world. Like all souls, she knows she will eventually reincarnate when the time is right.

The Fall of the Firethorn Empire (10,000 years ago)
The collapse of the Firethorn Empire was not the result of gradual decline alone, nor was it Xarvion’s direct goal. It was the catastrophic final blow — the equivalent of the barbarian invasions that ended Rome — delivered during an empire already rotting from within.
For nearly a millennium the Firethorn Empire had been sliding into decadence, debauchery, and profound ennui. The once-disciplined Tarran martial culture had grown corrupt and entitled. Noble youths abused their power with impunity. The grand ideals of Order and Duty had become hollow slogans. The empire was collapsing under its own weight long before Xarvion rose to prominence — much like Rome in its final centuries.
Xarvion, a brilliant but broken Velrothi archmage, did not set out to destroy the empire. His sole obsession was obtaining the power to resurrect his murdered sister Velena. Through ruthless political maneuvering, revolutionary magical discoveries, and calculated military campaigns, he positioned himself to claim the Spear of Crystallized Entropy on God’s Day.
When he finally ascended as an Unrestricted Elder God, the War of Domination, the desperate summoning of a Demon Prince, and the ensuing Demon War tore through the already weakened empire like a wildfire through dry grass. What little structural integrity remained was obliterated.
In the aftermath, the Gods held the Three Elder Races collectively responsible for the hubris and chaos. The Great Exile banished the majority of the Elfen, Tarran, and Velrothi from the Prime Material Plane to the Green Moon Gaia.
The Firethorn Empire did not fall because Xarvion willed its destruction. It fell because it was already dying — and Xarvion was the final, devastating catalyst.

Updated Canon – 10,000 Years Ago
Dissolution of the Firethorn Empire & The Great Exile
Ten thousand years ago, the Firethorn Empire finally collapsed amid corruption, rebellion, and catastrophic internal conflict.
In the aftermath, the Three Elder Progenitor Races — the Elfen, Tarran, and Velrothi — were collectively exiled from the main Prime Material Plane continents.
They were banished to Gaia, the lush Green Moon, along with portions of their populations in the Bright Mirror (Feywild) and Dark Mirror (Shadowdark).
Details of the Exile:

The exile was not total for every individual, but the vast majority of the elder races were forced (or chose) to leave the main world.
Gaia became their primary new home — a world that closely resembles pre-cataclysm Eadras: temperate forests, swamps, shallow seas, and rich natural magic.
Some enclaves remained behind in hidden places on the main world (especially Velrothi archives and Elfen deep wilds), but the bulk of their civilizations relocated to the moon.
This event marks the definitive end of the “Elder Age” and the true beginning of the rise of the younger races (humans, orcs, dwarves, gnomes, beast-folk, etc.) on the main continents.
