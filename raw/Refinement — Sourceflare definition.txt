Refinement — Sourceflare definition:

Sourceflares are not a distinct phenomenon, but are standard solar flares observed within the full spectrum of reality.

In addition to electromagnetic radiation, solar flares emit energy within the Eldritch spectrum. This component is not directly observable by conventional means but interacts with magical systems and planetary conditions.

Under most circumstances, solar flares have no significant magical effect. However, in rare cases, the Eldritch component of a flare interacts with specific environmental and systemic conditions, resulting in discrete manifestations such as the emergence of new species.

The form of these manifestations is determined by the state of the world at the time of the flare, including planetary stability, ecological maturity, and systemic structure.


Addition — anomalous flare outcomes:

Certain historical Sourceflare events produced outcomes that do not align with expected environmental conditions. These anomalies are not currently understood and are generally attributed to unknown variables within Eldritch interactions.

Refinement — Sourceflare atmospheric filtering:

Most solar flares do not produce significant magical effects at the surface due to atmospheric attenuation.

The planet’s atmosphere, magnetic field, and existing magical structure (including the Weave in later eras) act as filters that absorb or disperse the Eldritch component of incoming radiation.

Only exceptionally high-intensity flares are capable of penetrating these layers sufficiently to produce measurable magical effects at ground level.

As a result, while solar flares occur frequently, only rare high-energy events have the potential to influence planetary systems, ecological stability, or trigger emergent phenomena such as species manifestation.

Addition — Sourceflare threshold conditions:

For a Sourceflare to produce significant effects, multiple conditions must align, including flare intensity, planetary atmospheric state, and systemic stability.

Pre-Weave conditions allowed greater penetration of Eldritch radiation, increasing the likelihood of high-impact events. Post-Weave conditions further restrict surface-level effects, making such events even rarer.

This threshold requirement explains the long intervals between major manifestation events such as the emergence of dragons and unicorns.
