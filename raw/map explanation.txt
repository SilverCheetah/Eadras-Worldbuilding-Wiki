
Eadras – One Million Years After the Worldbreaker
One million years after the Cataclysm, the world is still raw and unstable. The Worldbreaker Scar burns brightly at the center of what will become the Central Rift Ocean. Three major proto-continents have begun to stabilize:

Avalon in the north
Damedes in the southwest
Culethor in the southeast

Smaller island chains dot the young oceans. This is the age in which the first dragons awaken from the amplified First Great Flare. The planet is still recovering, its crust fractured and its climate volatile.

Eadras – Two Million Years After the Worldbreaker
Two million years after the impact, the continents have drifted further apart. The Worldbreaker Scar has begun to cool and sink, now known as the Buried Worldbreaker Scar, though its influence still distorts ocean currents and ley lines.
The landmasses are beginning to take on more recognizable shapes. Ocean currents are becoming well-established, creating distinct climate zones. This is the era in which the first unicorns awaken on the plains. The long age of dragon and unicorn dominance is beginning.

Eadras – Three Million Years After the Worldbreaker
By three million years after the Cataclysm, Eadras has settled into a configuration closer to its modern form. The three major continents — Avalon, Damedes, and Culethor — are clearly distinct, each with its own mountain ranges, rivers, and coastal features.
The Worldbreaker Scar has become a deep, stable oceanic rift, still geologically active but no longer a blazing wound. New geographical features have emerged, including fracture caps, stormbreak points, and shifting currents. This marks the height of the ancient Dragon-Unicorn Wars and the long era before the rise of the progenitor humanoid races.

Eadras – Four Million Years After the Worldbreaker
Eldravath – The Forged Land
Four million years after the Cataclysm, the scattered continents have collided once more through the slow violence of plate tectonics, forming the great supercontinent Eldravath.
This is a world in the midst of violent geological rebirth. The Worldbreaker Scar still glows at the heart of the Central Rift Ocean, though it has begun to cool and sink. The formation of Eldravath has triggered massive mountain-building events, new volcanic arcs, and extreme climate gradients across the single landmass.
Key Features

Towering central mountain ranges dominate the interior.
Vast interior basins, shifting sands, and newly formed inland seas create dramatic environmental contrasts.
Active coastal rifting and volcanic activity mark the beginning of the forces that will eventually tear the supercontinent apart again in another million years.

Significance
This is the age in which the long Dragon-Unicorn Wars reach their peak intensity. The reunification of the continents forces massive migrations, resource wars, and uneasy alliances as old territories vanish and new borders form. Eldravath represents the last time the world existed as a single landmass before fracturing into the modern continents.
