Core Assumption
As of 2026-05-09 the magic system of Eadras was fully and retroactively redefined. The following four living traditions have always been the canonical orders of magic:

Netharim (the Little Gods) — intuitive practitioners who reshape reality by imposing a deeply-studied personal model of how the world actually works.
Lexanari (Name-Bound) — the independent fourth order of innate-only mortal speakers of the Primordial Language.
Druids — the devotional sub-priesthood jointly overseen by Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox.
Windspeakers — the monastic caretakers who maintain Lirra’s defensive Weave.

The defensive Weave itself survives only as Lirra’s ward against outsider magic theft. All other prior functions of the Weave have been retired.

Canon Change Declaration
The previous Sorcerer/Druid/Weavespinner framework no longer exists in any form. It was a pre-2026-05-09 drafting stage and has been entirely superseded. All wiki pages, stories, and prompts must now reflect only the four living traditions above. There is no in-universe “evolution” or “historical period” in which the old system was true — the new system has always been the truth of Eadras.
Update Instructions for Editors
When editing any page:

Replace every instance of “Sorcerer” / “Sorcerers” with Netharim
Replace every instance of “Druid” / “Druids” (when it referred to the old standalone order) with Lexanari
Replace every instance of “Weavespinner” / “Weavespinners” with Windspeaker / Windspeakers
Leave all references to the new Druids (sub-priesthood of Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox) completely untouched
Update any mechanical or historical references to the Weave so that it is described only as Lirra’s defensive ward

Tone Guidance
Treat the change as a clean, authoritative retcon. No apologies, no transitional lore, no “used to be.” The new system is and always was canon.
