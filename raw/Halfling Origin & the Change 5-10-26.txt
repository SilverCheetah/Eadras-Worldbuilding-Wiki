Halfling Origin
The halflings initially formed as a subrace of the Mitan. The underground-dwelling Mitan wanted reliable access to fresh agricultural products, so they sent a group of their people to the surface to serve as farmers.
These farmers liked living on the surface and decided they no longer wanted to return underground every day. Instead, they built hole houses near their fields and gradually established permanent surface communities.

Halfling “The Change” Origin
During the Demon War, they lost their food supply for a time. Their farms were destroyed or overrun, and many halflings became refugees. The trauma of starvation and displacement while living under open sky triggered a deep racial reaction.
A few years after the war, “The Change” began to manifest — their now-iconic racial wanderlust. This is understood to be a psychological coping mechanism for the combined trauma of:

Living under the terrifyingly open sky (having evolved underground)
The fear of running out of food and having no safe place to store reserves

The constant movement and inability to stay in one place for too long is their race’s way of ensuring they never again find themselves trapped without food or shelter.
Modern halflings are not agoraphobic. They are quite comfortable under open sky. The Change is simply a lasting behavioral adaptation born from that specific moment of crisis during the Demon War.
