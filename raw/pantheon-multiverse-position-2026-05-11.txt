Eadras pantheon — multiverse position and the Game as containment, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture: Josh established the cosmic context for the Eadras
pantheon's power and the Game's true function as benign containment
against expansionism into other Mythoi.)

== Canon ==

1. EADRAS IS ONE OF THE MOST MAGICALLY RICH MYTHOI IN THE OMNIVERSE.
   Magical richness is the source of deity power; rich Mythoi produce
   powerful gods.

2. THE EADRAS PANTHEON ARE AMONG THE MOST POWERFUL DEITIES IN THE
   MULTIVERSE.
   Their power derives from where they live -- Eadras's magical
   richness -- not from anything intrinsic to them. They are powerful
   because of position.

3. STANDARD HIGH-TIER DEITY PATTERN (across other Mythoi) IS EXPANSIONIST.
   Other powerful gods push worship outward into neighbouring Mythoi,
   contest other pantheons, recruit or convert populations across
   realities, and accrue influence beyond their home plane.

4. THE EADRAS PANTHEON DOES NOT DO THIS.
   They are turned almost entirely inward. They are absorbed in their
   own affairs and especially in the Game of the Gods.

5. THE GAME IS THE CONTAINMENT MECHANISM.
   It keeps the pantheon busy WITHOUT HARMING THEM. It is not a chain,
   not a binding, not a diminishment. The gods remain at full power;
   they simply spend that power inward, because the Game is more
   interesting than anything outside Eadras would be.

6. FROM DAO'S MONITOR FRAME, THE GAME SERVES BOTH ROLE 1 AND ROLE 2:
   - Role 1 (smooth running of Eadras) -- gods absorbed in the Game
     don't over-intervene in mortal affairs
   - Role 2 (macro balance of the Omniverse) -- the most powerful
     pantheon in a magic-rich Mythos isn't pushing into neighbouring
     Mythoi to destabilise them.
   The Game is multi-purpose containment.

== Implications ==

- Eadras's exceptional pantheon power is a STRUCTURAL CONSEQUENCE
  of the Mythos's magical richness, not a virtue of the gods.
- The Game (per earlier today's canon: created by Dao in secret, gods
  don't know how it works) is now contextualised: Dao designed (or at
  least put in place) a benign containment for what would otherwise
  be an Omniversal expansion problem.
- The pantheon themselves do not experience the Game as containment.
  From inside the Game, they experience it as the most interesting
  thing in existence. The system is stable because it is genuinely
  satisfying to the contained.
- The "deeply addictive" descriptor of the Game (existing canon) now
  reads as DESIGN INTENT (or at minimum, as a feature Dao has chosen
  not to mitigate) rather than as accident.
- The grudge canon (gods hate demons because demons disrupted the
  Game) reads sharper too: demons did not just disrupt entertainment,
  they threatened to break the containment that maintains macro
  balance.
