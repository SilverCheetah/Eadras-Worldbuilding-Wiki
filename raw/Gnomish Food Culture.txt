Gnomish Food Culture – The Vault’s Gift
After the gnomes discovered the Arathi vault, it took them decades of painstaking work to translate its language. At first, they didn’t understand what the strange device was. Only after they finally deciphered the ancient text did they realize it was a food fabricator — and that in low-power survival mode, it could still generate seeds.
Over the following generations, gnomes began cultivating dozens of previously unknown crops, herbs, and spices from that single regional Arathi culture. Although they could never fully replicate the original cuisine (which had relied heavily on the meat of giant insects that no longer existed), the new ingredients dramatically transformed gnomish food culture.
What began as basic medieval fare gradually evolved into richly spiced, complex dishes that blended ancient Arathi flavors with traditional gnomish techniques.
When the gnomes finally translated the cookbook, one of the first ingredients Inki tried was something listed simply as a “raven’s eye” — a small, glossy black pepper about the size of a fingernail. The cookbook described it as pleasantly warm.
Inki popped one in his mouth expecting something mild.
It nearly blew his head off.
That single pepper became the spark that helped change gnomish cuisine forever. To this day, tasting a raven’s eye is still the traditional first test given to any young gnome who wants to become a master chef.
