## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

This document distills the in-world canon for **Velveth**, the Ascended Mortal Velrathi Goddess of Betrayal, Love, and Duty. Velveth was the Velrathi princess Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion, fiancée to Emperor Thoramis Firethorn (the last Firethorn Emperor), who poisoned the Emperor in his sleep on the night that sealed the Great Betrayal's bargain with the Elder Gods. Her Apotheosis is the canonical reference case for Designation-Over-Acquisition Ascension in the setting. All `[CANONIZED]` claims establish new canon for the wiki; `[WIKI_REFINED]` claims extend or sharpen existing pages.

## 1. Velrathi High-Title Morphology (Linguistic Canon)

`[CANONIZED]` Velrathi high titles operate on a **two-axis morphology** that produces a clean four-form paradigm for every titular stem:

**Gender axis:**
- **-ar** suffix = masculine, reigning
- **-eth** suffix = feminine, reigning in her own right

**Diminutive axis (child-of-title-holder, heir-by-lineage):**
- **-i** suffix appended to either gendered form = "child of [title]" / heir-presumptive / not-yet-invested holder of the lineage-title

This grammar applies systematically to all Velrathi high titles. For the senior territorial / princely / ducal stem **Vorand-**:

| Form | Composition | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Vorandar** | Vorand- + -ar | Prince/Duke (masculine), reigning |
| **Vorandeth** | Vorand- + -eth | Princess/Duchess (feminine), reigning in her own right |
| **Vorandari** | Vorand- + -ar + -i | Prince's son / male heir-by-lineage, not yet invested |
| **Vorandethi** | Vorand- + -eth + -i | Princess's daughter / female heir-by-lineage, not yet invested |

`[WIKI_REFINED]` The morphology extends to other Velrathi high titles with their own stems. The full Velrathi title-stem catalogue is open canon; the morphology itself is now fixed.

## 2. Mortal Identity

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's pre-Apotheosis mortal name was **Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion**.

`[CANONIZED]` Kathira was a Velrathi princess of the Northern Velrathi Titular Monarchy — the senior of the two surviving Velrathi titular monarchies during the late Firethorn era. House Valtharion was the ruling house of the Northern Monarchy; Kathira was the second-eldest daughter of the reigning Northern Vorandar at the time of her engagement.

`[CANONIZED]` Her father was **Vorandar Narand of House Valtharion**, reigning prince of the Northern Monarchy throughout Kathira's youth and through the late Firethorn era. He survived the war and accompanied his people into the Great Banishment; canon on his death-date in Veyr'Keth is open.

`[CANONIZED]` Kathira held the title **Vorandethi** throughout her mortal life. She would have inherited as Vorandeth if her father had passed and the succession had fallen to her, but the Demon War and her subsequent Apotheosis made the succession question moot; the Northern Monarchy passed sideways to her elder sister's line after the Banishment.

`[CANONIZED]` She bore the cognomen **Spellsinger** — earned in young adulthood as a recognition of her unusual gift for *song-channeled spellwork*, a relatively rare Velrathi discipline combining vocal magic and ritual architecture.

`[CANONIZED]` Kathira's age at the time of the Great Betrayal is established as **approximately 612 years** — Velrathi-prime-adult, fully mature, having held the Spellsinger cognomen for roughly four centuries and having been engaged to Emperor Thoramis Firethorn for approximately eighty years at the moment of the poisoning.

`[CANONIZED]` Her full formal address in pre-Banishment Velrathi court usage was:

> **Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion, daughter of Vorandar Narand of the Northern Monarchy.**

`[WIKI_REFINED]` Kathira's depth as a Spellsinger was the reason the Emperor's protective enchantments could be bypassed through *intimate contact* in the way the Great Betrayal canon describes. The poison was *calibrated by song-channel adjustment to her own arcane signature* in addition to being chemically calibrated; the protective field could not distinguish her vocal-arcane signature from the Emperor's own field-resonance after eighty years of formal engagement-rituals.

## 3. The Emperor and the Engagement

`[CANONIZED]` The Emperor whom Kathira poisoned is named **Thoramis Firethorn, the Last Firethorn**. Earlier canon recorded only the title; the personal name is now fixed.

`[CANONIZED]` Thoramis was the seventh Emperor of the Firethorn Empire's late period. He had reigned for approximately three hundred and forty years at the time of his death. The earlier emperors of the Firethorn line had been competent if increasingly distant; Thoramis was the one in whom the Empire's accumulated arrogance crystallized into refusal-to-listen.

`[CANONIZED]` The political marriage between Thoramis and Kathira was arranged approximately one century before the outbreak of the Demon War, as a cementing of the Velrathi-Tarran alliance at the highest dynastic level. The engagement was formally consecrated in three separate ceremonies across eighty years of pre-war and early-war diplomacy. By the time of the poisoning, the marriage itself had not yet occurred; Kathira was Thoramis's *fiancée of long standing*, with the wedding repeatedly deferred by the war's pressures.

`[CANONIZED]` Across the eighty years of formal engagement, Kathira and Thoramis came to **love each other**. This was not a political fiction. The slow accreting love of two long-lived Velrathi-and-Tarran minds discovering each other over decades was real, witnessed by their courts, and is preserved in Velrathi-archival correspondence in Veyr'Keth. Velveth's portfolio of *love-and-betrayal-and-duty as inseparable* is rooted in this fact: **she loved him. The betrayal was not of an enemy.**

## 4. The Night of the Poisoning

`[CANONIZED]` The poison was administered in the Imperial command tent at the Eastern Front, six weeks after the Battle of the Broken Conclaves and three days after the Treaty of Article Four was signed by the Coalition of Disillusioned High Command.

`[CANONIZED]` The substance was a **tasteless, colorless Velrathi alchemical preparation**, derived from the deep tradition of Velrathi poison-craft. It was specifically engineered for this single use, by hands the Coalition has never publicly named.

`[CANONIZED]` The poison was administered through **a shared cup of warmed wine**, taken in private after the Emperor had retired from the day's command meeting. Kathira poured. Thoramis drank. He died within approximately two hours, in his sleep, with Kathira beside him. *He did not wake. He did not know.*

`[CANONIZED]` Kathira remained at the Emperor's bedside through the night. She did not raise an alarm until dawn. The interval between the death and the alarm — *approximately six hours* — is preserved in Velrathi-archival memory as **the Long Quiet**, and is the period most central to Velveth's eventual portfolio as goddess. *She sat with what she had done.* The night is, in some Velrathi mystical traditions, treated as the silent root of all subsequent love-and-duty discipline.

`[WIKI_REFINED]` The wiki canon that Kathira *combed her long gray hair as the life left him* is now sharpened: she did so as a deliberate small ritual of composure, holding the Velrathi-discipline architecture against the grief of having just killed the man she loved. The combing was not a flourish; it was the load-bearing internal practice that allowed her to survive the next six hours intact.

## 5. The Bargain Sealed

`[CANONIZED]` The Emperor's death triggered the immediate collapse of the Firethorn Throne's protective field. Within hours, the Elder Gods' constraint was lifted, and direct divine intervention became cosmologically possible at scale.

`[CANONIZED]` The Gods, in fulfillment of the bargain sealed by Kathira's hand, did the following within the next several months:

- **Destroyed the Demon Prince of the Open Wound** through coordinated divine action; the Demon Prince was reduced, banished, and its anchor in Eadras unmade
- **Drove back the demonic legions** that had penetrated the Demonfall Marches; most were destroyed, the remainder fled back through the gate
- **Sealed the Demonfall Gate** — the breach through which Xar'vion had originally summoned the Demon Prince — closing the major demonic incursion route into Eadras for the duration of the present age

`[WIKI_REFINED]` The bargain was *honored in full* by the Gods. This is canonically significant: it stands as one of the very few cosmic-scale bargains between mortal coalition and Elder Gods that was completed precisely as agreed, with no later revision, dispute, or punitive amendment. The Great Banishment that followed was *not* a punitive amendment but a separate cosmological judgment rendered alongside the bargain's fulfillment, with the Coalition having pre-accepted it under Article Six.

`[WIKI_REFINED]` The **Weave was not a deliverable of the bargain**. Lirra wove it early in the Demon War as a defensive structure specifically to prevent demonic energivore-siphoning of Eadras's magical infrastructure. By the time the bargain was sealed, the Weave was already operational. What the Throne's protective field had been blocking was the Elder Gods' *direct intervention against the Demon Prince and its legions*, not the structural-defensive magic Lirra was already maintaining. The bargain's full-honoring concerns the active-intervention deliverables (destroying the Prince, driving back the legions, sealing the Gate), all of which were performed exactly as agreed.

## 6. From the Banishment to Apotheosis

`[CANONIZED]` Kathira survived the war. She accompanied the Velrathi into the Great Banishment, departing for **Shadowdark Gaia** and the geode-city of **Veyr'Keth**, where she resumed political duties as a senior figure of the Northern Monarchy's exiled court.

`[CANONIZED]` She was not assassinated, exiled-within-exile, ostracized, or marginalized by her surviving people. The Velrathi understood, in their cold-clear way, that **she had paid the price they could not pay**. She was treated with the precise dignified respect accorded to those who have done what the people required of them at terrible personal cost.

`[CANONIZED]` During this period of continued mortal life in Veyr'Keth, she neither remarried nor took another lover; this is preserved in Velrathi archives as part of *her continuous discipline of bearing*. The duration of this period is not specified in any open record — *Kathira's life-as-mortal-in-Veyr'Keth had some length, and it ended at Apotheosis*, but the exact span is not preserved.

`[CANONIZED]` Apotheosis occurred *at some point during her Veyr'Keth life*, by mechanism specific to her Ascension and not shared with mortals. At that moment, the mortal Kathira **ceased to exist as a continuing mortal individual**: her body became Icon (manifesting on the Prime Material at the moment of transformation), her mind became divine, the Hall of the Long Quiet appeared in the outer planes, and her divine simulacrum appeared in Solarion. The Vorandethi who had walked Veyr'Keth corridors for some unknown number of years was, in that instant, a goddess.

`[WIKI_REFINED]` The wiki canon that says Kathira "is said to still live somewhere in the blood-red crystal halls of Veyr'Keth" is now understood as **mortal misremembering of the Apotheosis transition**. To mortal observation, the long-walking Vorandethi simply ceased to be seen in Veyr'Keth corridors at some indeterminate point; the rumor that she still lived there was the simplest mortal interpretation. The truth — that her Apotheosis had occurred and that her presence was now distributed across the Hall of the Long Quiet, her Solarion simulacrum, and her Prime-Material Icon — was not announced and was not deduced by mortal observation.

## 7. The Apotheosis

`[CANONIZED]` Apotheosis can only happen to living mortals. A soul that has died must be reborn into the cycle and have its candidacy reset across new lifetimes. Kathira's Apotheosis occurred during her continued life in Veyr'Keth, not posthumously.

`[CANONIZED]` **The mechanism of each Apotheosis is different from every other Apotheosis.** No single procedure exists. The Tome of Baldaran does not record the mechanism, not because of a Decree of silence, but because *there is no single mechanism to record*. Each Ascended Mortal God's transition was its own private cosmological event. Similarities across cases certainly exist, but the Gods have never compiled them — Ascended Mortal Gods are characteristically reluctant to discuss the moment of their own Apotheosis, even with one another. The experience is regarded as **intensely personal**, possibly the most personal experience the cosmology contains.

`[CANONIZED]` A small number of fragmentary details are preserved in *scattered mortal myth*, distributed across the religious traditions of the peoples who worship various Ascended Mortal Gods. No single tradition contains a description of Apotheosis; together, the fragments would form a partial mosaic if anyone were to compile them. Such a compilation has not been attempted in known canon.

`[CANONIZED]` **What is established about the moment of Apotheosis itself is this:**

- The mortal body **becomes an Icon** — the Prime-Material anchor required for any god to directly interact with the Mythos
- The mortal mind **becomes divine** — qualitatively transformed, not merely augmented
- **A divine realm is instantly created in the outer planes** — a new outer-plane region specific to the Ascended
- **One or more divine simulacra appear in the spirit realms** — wherever the Ascended's divine power has reach

`[CANONIZED]` **The universal Icon-and-Simulacrum framework**: Every god in Eadras — Elder or Ascended — operates through the same two-part mediation structure.

- **An Icon on the Prime Material is required for direct interaction with this Mythos.** **All Icons are on the Prime Material, without exception** — never on Mirror Planes (Feywild, Shadowdark, Veil-Gaia), Upper Planes (Celestia and its layers), Lower Planes (Nyxar and its layers), or Elemental Planes. The Prime Material is the load-bearing reality-substrate of the Mythos; Icons must anchor to it specifically. **Most mortals do not know that Icons exist.** The concept is held by inner clergy, the gods themselves, the Order Sharanel, the Tome of Baldaran's curatorial scope, and very few others. **Most Icons are hidden** in protected sanctuaries, deep vaults, or sealed chambers known only to the highest priestly inner circles. **A few are hidden in plain sight** — for example, embedded as the central altar-object in the nave of a main temple, where worshippers pass by daily without knowing what they are seeing.
- **One or more Simulacra exist in the spirit realms wherever the god's divine power reaches** — Solarion (Celestia), Nyxar, the Feywild, the Shadowdark, the Elemental Planes, or any combination. Simulacra conduct institutional business and cross-pantheon work.

`[CANONIZED]` **Why this framework exists**: Eadras cannot bear **Avatars** — direct partial divine presences carrying full reality-weight, which are routine in many other Mythoi across the Omniverse. **Manifesting a genuine Avatar in Eadras would risk cracking the narrative foundation of the world**. The Mythos is too cosmologically wounded (observer-hungry from the Primordial Sacrifice, scarred by the Worldbreaker-wound, weakened by multiple Crusades, the Demon War, and the Anti-Magic Crusade) to bear that weight without structural failure. The Icon-and-Simulacrum framework is what makes divine operation in Eadras possible at all. **The mediation is not chosen by any god; it is required by the Mythos's condition.**

`[CANONIZED]` **Consequences of Icon destruction**: If a god's Icon is destroyed, the god **loses Prime-Material influence for approximately 100 years** — the time required to reform the Icon. During this interval, the god's **domain becomes unruled**: the portfolio continues to operate cosmologically, but its *governance* falls away. *Magic without a mind becomes chaotic rule in effect.* The severity varies by god: most cases produce **bad but bearable** consequences during the 100-year recovery; some are **catastrophic**. Lirra's case is the canonical extreme — if Lirra's Icon were destroyed, *magic in Eadras would cease entirely for 100 years*, because magic's governance falls to her, and without her ruling presence the entire system loses its mind. **Kudur-eṭir is the unique exception**: his Total Icon Binding pinned his entire divine continuity to a single anchor, making the Worldbreaker's destruction of that anchor *terminal* rather than recoverable. For all ordinary gods, Icon-destruction is catastrophic but recoverable. This is why **Icon-protection is one of the most serious institutional responsibilities** in Eadras.

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's portfolio was settled at Apotheosis as **Betrayal, Love, and Duty** — the three terms inseparable, listed in that order in formal Velrathi prayer-and-vow contexts, and never broken into single-term invocations. To pray to *Velveth-of-Love* alone is to mishandle the form; the three terms are one domain, addressed together. The order is itself doctrinally fixed: betrayal first, love second, duty third — *the act, the bond, the requirement*.

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's divine realm in the outer planes is named **the Hall of the Long Quiet** in the small Velrathi mythic-fragments that reference it. The realm is described in those fragments as *a Velrathi-Gothic chamber of indeterminate scale, lit by a single low oil-flame that never burns out, in which the goddess holds the architecture across all the moments of all the bargains under her domain*. Whether this is literally true of the realm or whether it is the mythic-reading of an experience the surviving fragment-tellers could only approximate is open canon.

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's Icon-form retained the appearance of Kathira: three meters tall, ashen-gray, red-eyed, with the long gray hair Kathira had combed on the Long Quiet, wearing formal Velrathi robes in deep midnight-blue with House Valtharion sigils at the collar. The partly-veiled face — the visible half showing *resolve-grief-steadiness held as one feeling* — is consistent across all known mortal accounts and temple-iconography.

`[CANONIZED]` **The location of Velveth's Prime-Material Icon is open canon, but the Icon must be on the Prime Material** — there are no exceptions to this rule, despite the Velrathi Banishment. Velveth's Icon manifested on the Prime Material at the moment of her Apotheosis, even though Kathira herself was in Veyr'Keth at the time. The Icon's location is a Velrathi state secret — known to the inner clergy of her cult, to whatever protective arrangement maintains the wider Velrathi pantheon's Icons on the Prime Material, and to Velveth herself, but to almost no one else. Plausible candidate locations include a sealed enclave-archive of House Valtharion, a hidden chamber in a deep-vault location maintained by remnant-Velrathi-keepers, or some other protected Prime-Material site established at the time of her Apotheosis.

`[CANONIZED]` **Consequences if Velveth's Icon were destroyed**: For the 100 years required to reform it, the domain of *betrayal-love-duty* would operate unruled. Bargains-honored-in-full would lose their governing presence — *betrayals would happen without weight-bearing, loves would fracture without duty-anchoring, duties would compel without the bond-integrity that makes them bearable*. The Mythos would survive this loss (Velveth's portfolio is significant but not load-bearing in the way Lirra's magic is), but the era of her absence would be characteristically chaotic in the relevant ways. Velrathi vow-prayers would fail their cross-pantheon routing for the duration. Cross-cultural impossible-choice petitioners would face their decisions without any divine office accepting their case.

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's case is the **clearest known example of *Designation-Over-Acquisition*** (Principle 17 of the In-World Principles). Kathira did not seek Apotheosis. She did not perform rituals to acquire it. She did not know it was a possibility. Whatever Apotheosis-event occurred to her was unsought; the experience was hers alone, and she has not described it. What can be observed is the outcome: the Hall of the Long Quiet exists; her Solarion-simulacrum maintains her office; her Prime-Material Icon anchors her reach to the Mythos; the prayers are received; the bargain has been honored eternally since.

## 8. Office and Working Practice

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's primary Solarion presence operates through her **Solarion simulacrum**, which occupies the **Office of Velrathi Affairs** on the divine main street. The simulacrum is fully conscious, fully Velveth-in-character, and fully empowered to act on her behalf in cross-pantheon institutional business. The full goddess remains in the Hall of the Long Quiet in the outer planes; the simulacrum handles the Solarion working-day.

`[CANONIZED]` The Solarion building is in formal Velrathi-Gothic style — narrow, dark, with runes faintly glowing on the facade and the House Valtharion sigil set above the lintel.

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's Solarion-simulacrum office is **three doors down from Imma Who-Did-Not-Stop-Laughing's small wagon-style office**. Both goddesses operate through their simulacrum-presences in Solarion. They cross-sign Velrathi vow-prayers regularly and maintain a daily corridor-nod that constitutes their full bilateral working relationship.

`[CANONIZED]` Whether Velveth maintains additional simulacra in other spirit realms (Nyxar, given her domain's overlap with duty-bound choices that touch infernal-bureaucratic matters; Shadowdark Gaia's adjacent realm, given the Velrathi exile location) is open canon. Most Ascended Mortal Gods with cross-realm portfolio reach maintain multiple simulacra.

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's iconography includes:

- **A sealed treaty-vial** in one hand — representing the architectural-discipline side of the Great Betrayal
- **A wine-cup** in the other — representing the instrument of the act itself
- **The partly-veiled face** — representing the Decreed silence about the Long Quiet and what passed through her in those six hours
- **House Valtharion sigils** — the family-and-line dimension; her duty was performed as a Vorandethi of her House and a princess of her people

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's prayer-traffic comes primarily from:

- **Velrathi in Veyr'Keth and other exile-locations** making vows of duty-against-personal-cost
- **The Gray Matriarchy** (descended from exiled Tarans who fled at the Banishment) addressing the lineage's collective shame about the betrayal
- **Cross-cultural petitioners** facing the rare class of impossible-choice in which betrayal of a loved one is the only ethical path; these prayers cross from many peoples' offices to Velveth's, and she signs them with care
- **Katharim death-priests** at the deaths of those who died bearing impossible loyalty-conflicts
- **Modern Prime-Material peoples** (Tarran-descended humans, Bold merchants, Orden trade-compact members, others) invoking her for bargain-honored-in-full commercial and political agreements

`[CANONIZED]` Velveth's tea preference, maintained across her Apotheosis, is **Clarity Thorn** — the same memory-bright variety Kathira drank during her mortal life as an aid to political-architectural thinking. She drinks it daily, in the formal Velrathi tea-ceremony register, and accepts visitors only when she has finished the day's first cup.
