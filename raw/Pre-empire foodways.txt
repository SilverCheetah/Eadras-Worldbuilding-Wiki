Elfen Food Ways – Pre-Empire Period
The Elfen eat no meat whatsoever. Their cuisine is entirely plant-based and extraordinarily diverse thanks to their powerful nature magic.
They can grow plants with virtually any flavor profile they desire and have a deep love for spices, herbs, and complex layered tastes. They particularly enjoy stews, soups, and elaborate salads.
Elfen meals function as communal workshops. While eating together, they openly discuss problems, air grievances, settle disputes, share news, and make group decisions. Eating is therefore both nourishment and a social/political act.
For scouts, rangers, and long journeys, they carry Waypetals — small, compact, nutrient-dense wafers made from compressed fruits, nuts, seeds, and herbs. One wafer provides complete daily nutrition while still delivering rich, pleasant flavor.
Valrathi Food Ways – Pre-Empire Period
Most Valrathi view food primarily as fuel for the mind and body. They eat quickly, often while still working or studying, and show little interest in flavor or variety.
Their regular diet consists of preserved foods — thick nutrient pastes, fermented broths, and jars of concentrated fungal stews. A common staple is bone-meal bread — a dense, dark, earthy loaf made from ground bone meal, subterranean fungi, and hardy grains. They usually tear off chunks and dip them into strong fermented liquids or thin broths.
However, a small but dedicated group of Valrathi treat cooking as arcane research. These experimental mages take ingredients normally used for potions — mana-infused organs from magical monsters and heavily magically-charged plants — and deliberately use them as food. Their goal is to create meals that function like potions, granting magical effects, enhanced cognition, or temporary arcane abilities through eating. These experiments can be unpredictable or even dangerous.
Terran Food Ways – Pre-Empire Period
Pre-Empire Terrans were highly disciplined and viewed food primarily as fuel to support their martial duties.
Their daily meals were simple, hearty, and efficient: large amounts of roasted or grilled meat (mostly from herd animals and large game), seasoned only with salt and basic herbs. Sauces and elaborate preparations were considered unnecessary indulgences. Meals were usually eaten quickly, often in silence or while discussing duty and training.
Only during major celebrations (victories, oath ceremonies, etc.) did they loosen their restraint. In these feasts they would enthusiastically borrow elaborate dishes, ingredients, and cooking styles from both the Elfen and Valrathi, enjoying the variety even while normally considering it “foreign frippery.”
