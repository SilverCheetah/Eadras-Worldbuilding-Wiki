Taran

Created by Melvina.

Child Species: Titans (Giants), Extans (Superior Humans), Mitans (Dwarves, Gnomes, Halflings)

**Tarran History**

The Tarran were created 39,000 years ago as the Keepers of Order. For the first 21,000 years they performed their duty honorably and were respected by the Elfen and Velrothi.

Between **20,000 and 19,000 years ago**, the Three That Are One withdrew from the world. With no direct divine guidance left, Tarran arrogance began to grow unchecked. They gradually came to believe that their role maintaining order meant they were destined to rule over all other races.

This belief led them to declare the **Firethorn Empire** at 18,000 years ago, ending the long cooperative age between the three progenitor races.

**Pre-Empire Tarran Society, Culture & Cities**

**Society** Tarran society was strictly disciplined and honor-bound. Status came from martial skill, dedication to duty, and acts of justice. Children were raised with rigorous martial training and discipline from a young age.

**Culture** Their culture revolved around **Duty, Honor, and Order**. Self-control was valued as much as strength. Justice was black-and-white, and public trials and oath ceremonies were important events.

**Appearance & Dress** Tarrans wore heavy lead-gray cloaks made from the fur of an extinct animal that was impossible to dye. The fur perfectly matched the color of their stonework, making them nearly invisible against the walls. They wore plain, almost undecorated armor forged from a rare, extremely hard-to-work magical metal (mithril placeholder).

**Cities** Tarran cities were grim, layered fortresses built entirely around defense. Their streets were narrow and twisting to create choke points and force attackers to bunch up. Every building and street was designed as part of the defensive plan. Tall, uniform gray stone structures loomed over the streets below.

At the heart of their capital city stood an enormous domed hall inside the central citadel. The **Firethorn Throne** sat within this vast open hall on full public display. Behind the hall were the private royal quarters, accessible only to the royal family and their chosen servants.
