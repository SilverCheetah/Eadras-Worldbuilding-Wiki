**Basic Information** Orcs are powerful, muscular warriors who live for battle and tribal honor. They’re tough, relentless, and built for raw strength and survival. They are an honor-bound people who live as both fierce nomadic clans on the plains and as a respected side culture in civilized cities.

**Anatomy** Tall and heavily muscled, often six to seven feet, with rugged features and scars worn like badges. They favor rough leather, bone, and metal armor that shows off their might.

**Ecology and Habitats** They roam vast plains in nomadic clans and have also settled among civilized peoples while preserving their distinct identity.

**Society** Strong ties remain between plains orcs and city orcs. Each group sends their young to live with the other for one to two years — city children go to the plains to learn the old ways, while plains children go to the cities to learn negotiation and trade.

City orc neighborhoods feature heavily defensive stone walls. Inside, the homes are surprisingly warm and organized like a tent with furs, low tables, and hanging fabrics. They wear modified city clothing with a martial cut. They never go unarmed, and some have armor plates sewn into inside pockets.

They’ve been around long enough that they’re not seen as threats — it’s understood that you don’t mess with an orc’s honor.

**Trades** Orcs excel as caravan guards and mercenaries. They are master leatherworkers, especially anything to do with horses — saddles, bridles, and ropes. They make very sturdy leather clothing. Their weapons tend to be ugly but functional.
