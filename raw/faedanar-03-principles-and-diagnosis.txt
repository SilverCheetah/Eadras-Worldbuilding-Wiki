# HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

This document extracts setting-wide principles from the Fae-da'Nar and frontier trade material. These principles are useful for hidden societies, borderlands, mixed-species law, trade towns, and urbanization.

---

# PRINCIPLE 1: SURVIVAL LAW IS NOT THE SAME AS JUSTICE

[DIAGNOSIS] The Four Laws are necessary, but not morally complete. They preserve a hidden society. They do not answer every question of fairness.

This tension should remain visible. Fae-da'Nar is old because its laws work, not because they are gentle.

---

# PRINCIPLE 2: COEXISTENCE REQUIRES RESTRAINT ON BOTH SIDES

[DIAGNOSIS] Woodkin must not provoke human fear. Humans must not provoke hidden retaliation.

The border survives because both sides learn what not to do. Hospitality customs and Fae-da'Nar law are two halves of one living treaty.

---

# PRINCIPLE 3: JUDGMENT WITHOUT SOVEREIGNTY CAN STABILIZE LIMINAL SOCIETIES

[DIAGNOSIS] Druids are judges, not rulers. Their authority works precisely because they are not kings.

This principle may apply to:
- Fey society;
- threshold cities;
- frontier compacts;
- Druid grounds;
- mixed-species disputes;
- hidden magical orders.

---

# PRINCIPLE 4: ETIQUETTE IS INFRASTRUCTURE

[DIAGNOSIS] Peace-brass, guest-bowls, Green Doors, and fair-measure witnesses are not flavor. They are material supports for peace.

If the spoons disappear, the treaty weakens.

---

# PRINCIPLE 5: CONTEXT TURNS DANGER INTO CONSENT

[DIAGNOSIS] Iron is not simply forbidden. At table it threatens; in trade it may be accepted. Wrapped, named, priced, and requested iron becomes tool rather than insult.

This principle applies broadly to dangerous substances, names, true language, relics, blood, contracts, and magical knowledge.

---

# PRINCIPLE 6: SUCCESS CAN BREAK FACE-TO-FACE TRUST

[DIAGNOSIS] Threshold towns grow by personal trust, then become cities that attempt to spend that trust impersonally.

Growth does not automatically mean betrayal, but it forces the compact to scale. If the compact cannot scale, it either relocates, becomes ceremonial, or breaks.

---

# PRINCIPLE 7: WITHDRAWAL CAN BE PUNISHMENT

[DIAGNOSIS] The worst consequence of broken hospitality may not be attack. It may be absence.

No warning before winter. No herb for fever. No lost child guided home. No Shunned tracks diverted. No paths remaining true.
