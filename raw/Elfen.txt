Elfen
Created by Mark

Child Races: El'mer (Elves), Sidhe (Sapient Fae Creatures), Seatua (Aquatic Races)

**Pre-Empire Elfen Culture**

The Elfen were created as the wardens of all wildlands. They possessed a profound, innate connection to the land and to all living things — feeling the pulse of forests, rivers, and mountains as if they were part of their own body. They also had strong natural connections to the elemental planes of earth and water.

Their building materials of choice were living plant matter, along with rock and crystals drawn up from the living earth. They never worked with dead materials.

Using their voices and their connection to Eldritch, they could sing directly into living matter and reshape it from within. A lone Elfen could slowly grow a home over days or weeks. But when real power was needed, Circles of up to a hundred Elfen would sing in harmony and raise entire districts or cities in just a couple of hours.

They could also vitrify living wood — literally transforming it into stone while preserving its natural grain and structure. This let them create fireplaces, pillars, or any hard surface they needed without ever using dead material.

Leadership came through Circles of Wardens, groups of elders who listened to the needs of the land itself before making decisions.

**Pre-Empire Relationship Between the Three Progenitor Races**

For over twenty thousand years, the Elfen, Velrothi, and Tarran lived in a stable, cooperative civilization. Each race had a clear and respected role:

*   The **Elfen** served as the Wardens of the Wildlands, shaping and protecting all natural ecosystems.
*   The **Velrothi** were the Masters of Magic, devoted to studying, harmonizing, and advancing all forms of arcane knowledge.
*   The **Tarran** were the Keepers of Order. They protected the world from both external threats and internal corruption — hunting down dangerous magical beasts and punishing members of any race who committed serious crimes.

The three races got along well because they saw each other as necessary parts of a balanced whole. There was mutual respect and almost no conflict between them.

This harmony began to break down in the centuries leading up to 18,000 years ago. The Tarran gradually grew arrogant. They started thinking, “We are the ones who maintain order… so why shouldn’t we also rule?”

That shift in attitude directly led to the founding of the Firethorn Empire.
