Spirits — universal true form + uniforms + back rooms canon, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture from a Claude Code session in which Josh established the
universal canon governing spirits across all four non-mortal realms of the
Eadras Mythos.)

== Universal true form (all spirits, all realms) ==

All spirits share the same basic true form:
- A smooth, nearly featureless, androgynous humanoid figure
- Roughly mortal proportions
- Minimal facial details
- No hair
- A glow emanating from the body itself

The form is identical across all four spirit realms. What distinguishes one
realm's spirits from another's is the COLOUR of the glow. What distinguishes
individual spirits within a realm is the BRIGHTNESS of the glow plus the
uniform they wear.

== Glow colour = realm of origin ==

| Realm                | Spirits            | Glow                                                    |
|----------------------|--------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| Celestia (upper)     | Seraphim           | Bright golden-white, like sunlight or holy radiance     |
| Feywild (middle)     | Nature spirits/Fey | Vibrant emerald green, sunlight through deep forest     |
| Shadowdark (middle)  | Dark spirits       | Blacklight ultraviolet-violet, cold and unnatural       |
| Nyxar (lower)        | Devils             | Deep crimson-red to orange, molten metal or hellfire    |

The Feywild and Shadowdark together comprise the "Middle" tier of spirit
realms (per the Upper/Middle/Lower trichotomy used in the back-rooms canon).

== Glow brightness = raw spiritual power ==

A spirit's brightness indicates its raw inherent power. A junior clerical
imp glows like banked embers; a senior pit-fiend's glow scorches the air
around it. A celestial functionary glows softly; a high-tier Seraph's
radiance fills the corridor. Brightness is intrinsic to the underlying
spirit; it does not predict role -- a bright spirit assigned to a low rank
wears a low-rank uniform, and the role's visible appearance is the
uniform's.

== Uniforms ==

Spirits ALMOST NEVER appear in true form in the mortal world or in most
areas of the spirit realms. They are almost always wearing a uniform.

The "types" mortals encounter -- imps, wraiths, dryads, seraphs, banshees,
pit-fiends, sprites, nymphs, shadow-stalkers, will-o'-the-wisps, etc. --
are NOT biological variations. They are uniforms worn over the universal
true form. The uniform:

- Reshapes the body when worn (adds horns, wings, vines, mass, scales,
  feathers, mist, etc., as the role specifies)
- Carries the powers associated with the role
- Encodes rank insignia legible only to other spirits of the same realm
- Reverses cleanly when removed -- the underlying spirit returns to the
  universal true form

Each realm has its own uniform code, maintained by its institutional order:
- Celestia: the Divine Bureaucracy
- Nyxar: the Infernal Bureaucracy
- Feywild: fey courts/hierarchies (specific institutional structure open)
- Shadowdark: shadow hierarchies (specific institutional structure open)

A uniform from one realm cannot be worn by a spirit of another realm.

Mortal demonological/angelological taxonomy systematically catalogues
uniforms as if they were species. The taxonomy is wrong.

== Back rooms (canonical to every spirit realm) ==

All spirit realms -- Upper, Middle, and Lower -- contain special areas
known as BACK ROOMS. These are private spaces where spirits can remove
their uniforms and rest in their true form.

Cultural framing: the spirit realms function as vast bureaucracies and
institutional orders where every spirit has a role to perform. The back
rooms are the necessary structural counterweight -- "even spirits cannot
remain on duty for eternity." Forcing a spirit to remain in uniform
continuously for too long degrades it in ways the institutions take
seriously.

Canonical back-room examples:
- The locker rooms of Dreadharrow (Infernal Bureaucracy's back rooms;
  already documented on the devils page; the canonical example)
- The celestial rest-halls of Celestia (Divine Bureaucracy's equivalent)
- Fey glade-clearings of the Feywild (open as to specific form/structure)
- The quiet rooms of the Shadowdark (open as to specific form/structure)

A mortal who stumbled into a back room would see dozens of glowing
humanoid figures, all nearly identical except for the colour and brightness
of their glow, casually existing without the dramatic costumes of mortal
iconography.
