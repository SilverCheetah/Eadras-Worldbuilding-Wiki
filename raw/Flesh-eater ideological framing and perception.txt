Ideological Framing and Self-Justification

Summary:
Flesh-Eaters do not view their behavior as immoral, deviant, or even unusual. They interpret all actions—predation, secrecy, flight, and concealment—through a framework of freedom and survival. Within this framework, they see themselves not as aberrations, but as the only dragons living correctly.

Core Belief

Flesh-Eaters believe:

Survival is the only meaningful measure of strength
Freedom is defined as the absence of imposed restraint
Predation is the natural and proper expression of dragonhood

All actions are judged according to these principles.

Interpretation of Behavior

Flesh-Eaters do not perceive:

secrecy as hiding
relocation as retreat
avoidance as fear

Instead, they interpret these as:

strategic movement
efficient survival
intelligent predation

👉 Remaining unseen is not cowardice.
👉 It is control.

Perception of Dragon Society

Flesh-Eaters view mainstream dragon society as:

constrained
weakened
fundamentally compromised

They believe other dragons:

have surrendered their dominance
have accepted artificial limits
are bound by fear of consequence

👉 In Flesh-Eater terms:

civilized dragons are cowards.

Internal Contrast

Flesh-Eaters define themselves in opposition:

Others obey laws
They obey only necessity
Others restrain themselves
They act freely
Others coexist
They dominate

👉 This contrast reinforces their identity as superior.

The Freedom Doctrine

Flesh-Eaters summarize their worldview in simple terms:

Freedom is the right to take what you can
Survival proves legitimacy
Anything that limits predation is weakness

This applies equally to:

hunting sapient prey
avoiding stronger enemies
abandoning territory

👉 There is no contradiction in their view:

fleeing a superior force is survival
hunting the weak is freedom

Lack of Self-Awareness

Flesh-Eaters do not recognize contradictions in their beliefs.

They do not perceive:

that secrecy implies fear
that avoidance limits their freedom
that their actions are constrained by necessity

👉 Outcomes define truth:

if they survive, they are correct
if others die, those others were weak

Irony

From an outside perspective:

Flesh-Eaters are highly constrained by circumstance
their survival depends on secrecy and avoidance

👉 However:

they do not perceive this.

To them:

they are the only dragons who are truly free
all others live in fear

Perception and Identification

Summary:
The distinction between Flesh-Eaters and civilized dragons is not widely understood among ordinary sapient populations. Most people do not know the term “Flesh-Eater” and instead classify dragons based on observed behavior.

Common Understanding

Among most civilized peoples:

Dragons are known to exist
Dragons are generally understood to be:
intelligent
capable of restraint
not inherently hostile

However:

Few people have direct experience with dragons
Fewer still understand draconic divisions

Practical Classification

Most populations divide dragons into two categories:

Civilized Dragons

Do not attack on sight
May observe or ignore
May speak or communicate
Can exist near settlements without predation

Monstrous Dragons

Attack without warning
Kill and consume sapient beings
Display no restraint

👉 Flesh-Eaters fall entirely into this second category.

First Contact Rule

To an ordinary observer:

A dragon that does not attack immediately
→ is assumed to be civilized
A dragon that attacks immediately
→ is assumed to be monstrous

👉 This is often the only distinction that matters.

Lack of Terminology

Most sapient populations:

do not use the term “Flesh-Eaters”
do not understand their doctrine

Instead, encounters are described simply:

“It came out of the sky”
“It took them”
“It ate them”

👉 Knowledge is experiential, not theoretical.

Draconic and Scholarly Knowledge

Among dragons and informed scholars:

Flesh-Eaters are recognized as:
a specific cult
a known threat

However:

this knowledge is not widely shared
and is often poorly communicated

Perception by Others

Summary:
While Flesh-Eaters view themselves as free and correct, other dragons and sapient races perceive them as a profound corruption of dragonkind—something not merely dangerous, but fundamentally sick.

Draconic View

To mainstream dragons, Flesh-Eaters are:

not simply criminals
not merely ideological deviants

👉 They are considered:

warped
degenerate
fundamentally wrong

This judgment arises from:

predation on sapient beings
deliberate cultivation of suffering
rejection of all restraint

Predation Boundary

Among dragons:

hunting is natural
predation is accepted

However:

👉 Flesh-Eater behavior is seen as:

a violation of proper predation
a corruption of natural order

Cultural Taboo

Among dragons:

direct reference to Flesh-Eaters is avoided
indirect language is preferred

Common euphemisms include:

“those who have not heard the Council”
“the egg-thieves”
“the hunters who forgot themselves”

👉 The name itself is considered distasteful.

Sapient View

To non-dragons, Flesh-Eaters are not a defined group.

They are perceived as:

monstrous dragons
aberrations
proof that dragons can become predators of civilization

Their behavior is experienced as:

predation without limit
violence without restraint
cruelty without explanation

👉 They are not categorized—they are feared.

Emotional Response

Common reactions to confirmed activity:

revulsion
disbelief
denial
panic

Even those familiar with dragons struggle to reconcile:

known dragon behavior
with Flesh-Eater actions

Perceptual Effect

👉 Flesh-Eaters break expectation before they break bodies.

They force observers to confront:

that dragons can violate assumed limits
that safety is conditional

Summary Rule

Flesh-Eaters justify all actions as expressions of freedom and survival, viewing all other dragons as cowards. In contrast, they are universally regarded as a profound corruption of dragonkind—recognized by behavior rather than name, and feared as something not merely dangerous, but fundamentally sick.
