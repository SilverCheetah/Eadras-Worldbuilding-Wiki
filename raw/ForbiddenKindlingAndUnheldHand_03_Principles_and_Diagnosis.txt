================================================================
PART I — SETTING-WIDE PRINCIPLES
================================================================

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 1 — DIVINE LAW IS NOT OMNISCIENT.

  The Doctrine of the Unheld Hand and the Edict of Closed Genesis are real, formal, enforceable. They are also undetected when violated by someone careful. Ixthal violated both, twice, and the Elder Gods believe both races arose from natural events. Divine law in Eadras operates through inference and detection like mortal law, not through perfect cosmic perception.

  The bureaucratic-delegation gap formalizes this: gods are not continuously aware of all actions taken in their name. Routine prayer-traffic and divine-magic requests are processed bureaucratically rather than by direct divine attention. The same delegation that allows divine governance at scale also creates the gap that ambitious violators can exploit.

  Story consequence: divine attention is a SCARCE RESOURCE in Eadras. Operating below the threshold that draws it is a viable strategy for entities with patience and discipline. Ixthal has done it for ~8 million years. The Black Hand attempted to do it during the Sorcerer Crusade (and was eventually detected, but only after enormous damage). The Forbidden Kindling is the canonical example of long-term success.

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 2 — INSTITUTIONAL SECRECY HAS DIFFERENT KINDS.

  This material distinguishes three kinds of secret:

  - SELF-IMPOSED (Ixthal): The violator conceals themselves. No institution covers for them. Their silence is their own strategy.
  - INSTITUTIONAL (Ratharil's Sealed Hall of First Flame): The dragons collectively suspect or know parts of the truth and seal records under institutional discipline. Not divine cover-up; dragon-side discretion.
  - DIVINE (the playground's mistaken model): A coalition of gods deliberately conceals a divine crime. Per the wiki, this layer DOES NOT EXIST in the Forbidden Kindling case. It exists as a theoretical capacity (Vosian Redaction, Noxian Counter-Secrecy, etc.) but was not deployed against Ixthal.

  Generalization: when a character in Eadras encounters concealed truth, the question is not just "what is hidden" but "WHO is hiding it." The same buried fact can be the product of any of the three kinds. Each implies different things about who else knows, what the cover-up costs to maintain, and what could break it.

  Story consequence: investigation in Eadras is a multi-layer activity. Penetrating one layer (dragon institutional secrecy at the Sealed Hall) does not get you to the next (Ixthal's own concealment) and does not put you in conflict with the third (the gods, who don't know to be concealing). A character could in principle become an expert in dragon institutional secrecy while remaining oblivious to Ixthal's deeper game.

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 3 — DIVINE NONINTERVENTION IS NECESSITY-DRIVEN, NOT INDIFFERENCE.

  The Doctrine of the Unheld Hand exists because Eadras has tested the alternative. The Arathi catastrophe and Auralis and Thamior's failure proved that direct divine intervention at species-scale produces either catastrophe (Kudur-eṭir) or ruined gods (Auralis and Thamior). The Doctrine codified the lesson: even compassionate intervention fails at planetary scale and can ruin the gods who attempt it.

  This makes Eadras's divine restraint LEARNED, not innate. The gods are not constitutionally incapable of macro-intervention. They have chosen to refrain because they have evidence that macro-intervention produces worse outcomes than its alternative.

  Generalization: the setting's gods are PRAGMATIC. Their restraint is calibrated to outcomes, not to abstract purity. They could in principle intervene; they choose not to because intervention at macro-scale has track record of disaster. They DO intervene at smaller scales (clerical magic, limited miracles, blessed champions, omens) where the track record is acceptable.

  Story consequence: prayer in Eadras is not metaphysically futile. Gods do hear; they do sometimes answer; they do empower mortal agents. What they do not do is solve civilization-scale problems by fiat. Characters who understand this have a more accurate prayer-life than characters who expect either total responsiveness or total silence.

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 4 — GOOD CAN COME OF UNLAWFUL ACTS.

  Dragons and unicorns are real, valuable, foundational. Their creation was unlawful. Both can be true. The Great Moral Knot makes this explicit: the resulting lives are not illegitimate because the creation was unlawful, yet that does not make the unlawful act safe.

  Generalization: the setting refuses the easy ethical move of treating outcomes as judgments of acts. A wrong act that produced good consequences is still a wrong act. A right act that produced bad consequences is still a right act. Eadras is consequentialist in its physics (causes have consequences) but DEONTOLOGICAL in its moral law (acts are judged by their nature, not their results).

  Story consequence: a character who argues "look what good came of it" is not making a valid defense of a violation in Eadras. The setting will accept the good as good and the violation as violation. This is the structural answer to the Genesis Seekers' slogan ("Every race was forbidden once"): yes, every race was forbidden once, and that does not mean the next attempt should be permitted. The setting expects characters to hold both halves of this without collapsing them.

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 5 — METHODS CAN BE MORE DANGEROUS THAN OUTCOMES.

  The Mage Architect's argument for divine concealment of the Forbidden Kindling: "The method of Source Flare genesis is dangerously reproducible." The outcome (dragons, unicorns) cannot be undone. The METHOD, if known, can be replicated — badly, by less competent actors. The wiki carries this through: the Genesis Seekers are catastrophic antagonists not because they would create more dragons but because their imitation would likely produce "mass mutation, planar rupture, or Source sickness."

  Generalization: secrecy in Eadras protects METHOD more than OUTCOME. The Spear of Crystallized Entropy is unique; the world contains only one. But the method of its forging, if recovered, could produce more. The Spear must be hidden specifically because methods are imitable. Similarly with Source Flare genesis, with deep crustal manipulation (Kudur-eṭir), with binding rituals.

  Story consequence: research into Eadras's catastrophes is dangerous specifically because successful research produces methodological knowledge that can be re-used. This makes scholars potential threats. A historian who reconstructs the Arathi crystal regulator network in too much detail is closer to repeating Arath than to merely commemorating it. The setting watches its researchers.

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 6 — THE GENESIS-BY-MANDATE SLOT EXISTS BUT IS NARROW.

  The Doctrine forbids unauthorized creation but permits Genesis by Mandate. Mark, Melania, and Rand used it. The requirements are specific: Elder Pantheon approval, defined boundaries, Lirran regulation if magic-involving, mortality-acceptance, no divine puppetry. The slot is real but narrow.

  Generalization: the setting has LAWFUL PATHWAYS for most things that might seem permanently forbidden. The Spear of Crystallized Entropy is the worst artifact in Eadras and yet exists; there are no plans to destroy it because destruction is more dangerous than containment. The Forbidden Kindling is the worst divine crime and yet did not invalidate the Doctrine; the Doctrine continues to operate with the Genesis-by-Mandate slot intact. Every aspect of cosmology has SOMEWHERE that the otherwise-forbidden act could in principle be done legally.

  Story consequence: characters who want to do prohibited things can in some cases find legal pathways. A creator-class god could request mandate for a new race. A scholar could request authorization for restricted research. The pathways have high gates and political costs, but they exist. This rules out the binary "forbidden vs. permitted" frame the playground sometimes lapses into. Reality is mostly gradient.

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 7 — THEOLOGICAL POSITIONS PRODUCE POLITICAL FACTIONS.

  The five theological movements (Open Hand Heresy, Closed Hand Orthodoxy, Coalbearers, Ashen Accusers, Genesis Seekers) are positions on a single underlying question: when, if ever, should divine restraint yield to direct intervention? They map onto five different answers, each defensible, each producing different action-recommendations.

  Generalization: Eadras's theology generates politics directly. There is no separation of theological belief from civic-political action in the setting. The Open Hand Heresy would push mortal religion toward demands for divine intervention. The Closed Hand Orthodoxy would buttress conservative legal traditions. The Coalbearers would support quiet humanitarian violations of religious code. The Ashen Accusers would oppose any veneration of Ixthal. The Genesis Seekers would actively pursue forbidden methodology. These are political movements as much as religious ones.

  Story consequence: any plot that touches Eadras's religion will produce factional alignment. A character investigating the Forbidden Kindling will find allies and opponents among each of the five movements (and among the much larger group that does not yet know the question exists). A character defending the Doctrine will find tactical allies in the Closed Hand and uneasy ones in the Coalbearers. The theology is alive enough to take sides.

[DIAGNOSIS] PRINCIPLE 8 — IXTHAL IS NOT A VILLAIN.

  The setting deliberately refuses to make Ixthal one. He violated divine law. He has not repented. He believes his act was justified. He continues active concealment. By most ethical frameworks the setting offers, these are damning. And yet the playground and the wiki both protect him from being read as villain.

  The Lorekeeper: "He broke divine law because he believed inaction would doom the future in a slower way." The wiki: "His private conviction: 'The law forbade interference. But the law also permitted a world where only gods remembered speech.'" Ixthal's defense is taken seriously enough to be preserved. His argument is not refuted, only countered.

  Generalization: Eadras allows its most consequential criminals to be MORALLY INTELLIGIBLE without being morally exonerated. Kudur-eṭir tried to abolish entropy; this was monstrous and also compassion. Xar'vion will trigger the Demon War; this will be catastrophic and also love for his murdered sister. Ixthal violated the Doctrine; this is illegal and also why mortal life resumed on Eadras after the cataclysm. The setting does not flatten its perpetrators into evil.

  Story consequence: when stories engage these figures, they engage GENUINE MORAL COMPLEXITY rather than puzzle boxes. The reader is not invited to figure out "what really happened" with the discovery that it was simpler than it looked. The complexity is the truth. Multiple defensible readings exist simultaneously.

PART II — LIVE WIRES
================================================================

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 1 — DOES THE GAP CLOSE?

  The bureaucratic-delegation gap is canonized as inherent to the Doctrine. "The gap cannot be closed without abandoning bureaucratic delegation altogether (impossible at the volume of mortal religious activity)." But the gap-condition is a state of cosmology, not a permanent law. It could in principle close. What if the gods became aware of the gap's exploitation pattern and developed compensating mechanisms? What if Lirra's Source Wards were upgraded to detect amplified Source surges? What if Nox were dispatched to investigate the gap itself (he is well-suited to studying secrets)?

  Stories that involve gap-closing initiatives — either from the gods or from mortal forces seeking divine oversight — are structurally available. They are also conservative dystopia material: a tightening of divine surveillance could end Ixthal, end the Black Hand's working space, end the Genesis Seekers, end most cult activity. It could also end the Doctrine's space for mortal agency. The gap is unsettling and also load-bearing.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 2 — IXTHAL CONFESSES.

  The Genesis Seekers' slogan "Every race was forbidden once" is dangerously persuasive. Ixthal himself, if he ever broke his silence, could utter a version of it. His defining possible statement (per wiki): "There were no mortals. I changed that." If Ixthal ever decided concealment was no longer worth the cost — if he aged past patience, if he saw an emerging crisis that demanded his testimony, if his pattern-sight showed the Genesis Seekers about to succeed at imitation — he might speak.

  His confession would destabilize four institutions (divine law, dragon religion, unicorn self-understanding, mortal faith). It would also vindicate the Open Hand Heresy. It would put the Closed Hand Orthodoxy in an awkward position. It would empower the Genesis Seekers' replication attempts. It would also potentially invite divine punishment after 8 million years of evasion. A story in which Ixthal CHOOSES to speak is one of the largest narrative bombs the setting allows.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 3 — LIRRA RECOGNIZES THE PATTERN.

  Lirra is described as understanding Ixthal's Source Flare work "better than any other" of the gods. She is the goddess of magic, the Weave's architect, the one most attuned to Source manipulation. If anyone among the Elder Gods would eventually piece together the Forbidden Kindling, it is Lirra.

  Stories in which Lirra begins to suspect — perhaps studying old records of the First Great Flare, perhaps investigating the Silver Plains Flare's "too-precise" pattern that the wiki notes "may have been what finally prompted divine suspicion" — open the divine-detection scenario. The wiki's "may have been what finally prompted divine suspicion" phrasing is open: divine suspicion may have been raised, may continue, may have died down. Lirra is the natural focal character for this thread.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 4 — THE SHARD OF FIRST SHELL.

  A memory-crystal in the Sealed Hall of First Flame, allegedly containing a fragment of one of the original nine eggshells and recording the magical signature of the first Source Flare. "Lirra's seal prevents anyone from deriving the method from it." But IF the gods do not know about the Forbidden Kindling, then Lirra's seal cannot specifically target Source Flare methodology. The seal might be a general precaution. The method might in principle be derivable by a scholar who breaks the seal.

  Story scaffolding: a Genesis Seeker who gains access to the Shard (through theft, alliance with a dissident dragon, or unusual circumstance). The seal might not hold. The setting's "Lirra's seal" claim is from the playground; the wiki's "even there it is incomplete" framing of the Sealed Hall's records suggests the seal is not infallible. The Shard is a high-value MacGuffin.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 5 — UNICORN ORIGIN-MEMORY SEALS.

  Divine seals exist around unicorn origin-memory. The playground reads them as part of the divine cover-up; the wiki's "Ixthal has not been caught" frame requires them to be either Ixthal-placed (masquerading as divine), self-generated by the Mythos's response to forbidden creation, or placed by gods for general precaution.

  Stories that involve a powerful psionic scholar — dragon, unicorn, or mortal — pushing too deep into ancestral herd-memory could brush against the truth. The wiki notes some modern unicorn mystics seek the "First Bright Fear" — the ancestral memory of awakening. "If they push too deep, they may brush against the sealed truth that the brightness was forced." This is the unicorn-side equivalent of the Shard. The investigative path is different (psionic descent rather than physical access to an archive) but the discovery would be functionally similar.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 6 — THE GENESIS SEEKERS' CURRENT ORGANIZATION.

  Listed as one of five theological movements that would arise if the truth emerged. But the truth has NOT publicly emerged. Are the Genesis Seekers currently dormant? Are they pre-existing without the full theological framework, simply ambitious mages trying Source manipulation experiments? Are they organized? Are they tied to other dangerous groups (the Black Hand, the Flesh-Eaters, certain Velrathi factions)?

  This is open canon. The wiki preserves all five movements as potential futures. A current low-level Genesis Seeker presence — perhaps research groups under Lirran academies investigating amplified Source events, perhaps fringe sorcery cults, perhaps individual ambitious mages — is structurally available. The full movement awaits the Knot becoming public knowledge.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 7 — THE COALBEARERS' OATH.

  The Ratharil oath proposed by the Lorekeeper:

    By scale, by Source, by the nine first shells,
    I shall not call the Kindler by plea or command.
    I shall not teach the forbidden flare.
    I shall not unmake my people with the truth of their making.
    I shall carry the coal and close my jaws.

  This is sworn by dragons who learn the truth at Level 3. The phrase "carry the coal and close my jaws" echoes the Three Last Lights mythology — Ixthal carried the coal. The dragons who know the truth become carriers of his secret. There is a structural parallel: Ixthal carries the coal of survival; oath-bound dragons carry the coal of Ixthal's secret.

  Stories about a dragon who BREAKS this oath — for sufficient cause, in extremis, deliberately — are structurally available. The oath has no metaphysical enforcement mechanism (no automatic spell-cost). It is honor-bound. A dragon willing to bear the dishonor could in principle reveal what they know. This puts the secrecy of the Sealed Hall on a knife edge: it depends on the discipline of every oath-taker, across millions of years.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 8 — THE PLAYGROUND'S DIVINE SCHISM, AS HYPOTHETICAL.

  The playground generated a detailed prediction of how each Elder God would respond if they knew. The wiki cancels this as fact but preserves it as POTENTIAL response. If the truth ever emerged, this is roughly how the divine schism would unfold:

  - Ioa would condemn the violation of sovereign order
  - Vos would demand law, record-sealing, enforceable prohibitions
  - Lirra would be horrified by unauthorized Source manipulation but recognize the brilliance of the regulation
  - Qoxli would defend the resulting life, if not the method
  - Nara would pity the newborn races
  - Raja would judge by endurance
  - Daj would respect boldness and despise secrecy
  - Sylph/Faye would ask: if the forbidden children live, was the law wrong or merely broken well?
  - Nox would keep his counsel and remember where secrets breed power
  - Shali and Shani would insist Ixthal did not cheat death, only made new life after it

  These predictions remain useful. A story about divine reaction TO the revelation can use them as character templates. The gods are well-developed enough that their responses are predictable in shape.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 9 — IS IXTHAL ALONE?

  Auralis and Thamior spent themselves and died. Ixthal survived. But other Ascended Mortals exist in the wider pantheon (per Topic 1's "Three That Are One" passage about Mark, Melania, and Rand, and from various other Eadras canon). Are any of THEM Ixthal-aware? Did any other gods, in any era, suspect or come close to discovering the Forbidden Kindling? The dragons' three-level knowledge structure exists; does any similar structure exist among gods or ascended mortals?

  Open canon. A high-stakes story space: a single other being who suspects Ixthal but cannot prove it. Perhaps Nox (who understands silence too well, per the council's prediction). Perhaps a divine archivist with unusual records. Perhaps another ascended mortal who experienced something during a Source surge that suggested artificial amplification. This single hypothetical character could be either ally or threat to Ixthal.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 10 — THE METAPHYSICAL CONTAINMENT HYPOTHESIS.

  The 999,999 limit may be metaphysical, not just political. If true, exceeding it damages the Weave. The wiki preserves this as hypothesis. If it is true, then the Flesh-Eaters' raiding of Council-aligned clans for eggs has a hidden cost — the Treaty-population maintenance depends on every birth being legally accounted for. Stolen eggs raised in Flesh-Eater cells might or might not register with the Ratharil Stone's count.

  Story scaffolding: a Council investigation into the Ratharil Stone's accuracy. A discovery that the Flesh-Eater population, hidden, has pushed the total dragon count to or past the threshold. Subtle Weave damage as evidence. This connects the Forbidden Kindling indirectly to current Flesh-Eater problem: Ixthal's act made dragons; the Treaty contains them; the Flesh-Eaters threaten the Treaty's containment function.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 11 — THE OPEN HAND HERESY DURING ACTIVE CRISES.

  If a major mortal crisis is unfolding (plague, war, famine, ecological collapse) and the gods are not intervening, the Open Hand Heresy could spread rapidly. Disaster survivors and refugees are its natural recruitment base. A protracted disaster — months or years of unanswered prayer — could shift mortal religion toward the heresy.

  This is a recurring possibility, not a single story. Any era in Eadras with sustained suffering and minimal divine response could see the Open Hand emerge. The wiki preserves it as one of five movements; in any given era, depending on conditions, it might be ascendant or dormant. A scholar character tracking its prevalence over time could map mortal-religious health across eras.

[DIAGNOSIS] LIVE WIRE 12 — THE CLOSED HAND ORTHODOXY AND CIVIL POWER.

  The Closed Hand Orthodoxy — Vosian-Ioan doctrine insisting divine restraint is the foundation of mortal freedom — would be the doctrinally orthodox position in any developed Vosian-Ioan church. Its political consequences could be significant: a church that argues divine restraint enables mortal freedom is also a church that may resist calls for divine intervention even when those calls are widely felt. It might therefore be in tension with popular religion, especially during crises.

  Stories that put a Closed Hand priest in conflict with their congregation during a disaster are structurally available. The priest's theological correctness (the gods cannot solve civilization-scale crises by fiat) collides with the congregation's emotional need. This is the kind of intra-religious conflict the setting supports cleanly.
