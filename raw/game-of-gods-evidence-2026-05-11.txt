Game of the Gods — evidence in the causation debate, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture: Josh provided canonical evidence on both sides of the
causation debate established earlier in the day. The debate is now
empirically grounded, not just philosophical.)

== Canon: evidence FOR "Game causes Eadras" ==

Direct-effect actions: cases where a god has plainly moved a piece on
the board and the corresponding mortal has acted accordingly without
prior mortal cause.

The flagship example: MOVING HEROES.

"Hero" appears to be a Game-specific term for the rare mortal piece
that has personal divine attention. (Cf. D&D characters as protagonists
the divine players invest in.) When a god moves a Hero, the
corresponding mortal Hero in Eadras acts -- sets out, confronts an
enemy, makes a choice. The correspondence is:
- Direct
- Tight in timing
- Often acknowledged by the Hero retrospectively as "I don't know why
  I did that"

Other examples are implied but not specified ("etc.").

== Canon: evidence AGAINST "Game causes Eadras" ==

Emergent events with no deific cause: major Eadras-events that no god
initiated through Game-play.

The canonical example: THE DEMON INVASION (Demon War).

The Demon Prince of the Open Wound was summoned into Eadras by Xar'vion
through a RITUAL operating outside the Game's mechanics -- not through
a Game move. No god in Solarion played the piece that produced the
war. The board UPDATED to reflect the demonic incursion after the fact,
but the originating event was not a Game move.

If the Game caused Eadras in a pure form, no such emergent event
should have been possible. The Demon War is therefore canonical
counterevidence to pure Game-causes-Eadras.

== Implication: the four positions weakened ==

The evidence on both sides makes pure versions of both Game-causes-
Eadras and Eadras-causes-Game difficult to maintain. The isomorphism
position is consistent with both bodies of evidence (the board and
Eadras update in step, regardless of which "causes" the other). The
principled-refusal position is unaffected (it doesn't take a stand).

== Emerging hybrid position (extrapolation from the evidence) ==

A growing number of modern scholars hold an ASYMMETRIC reading:
- Divine moves cause downward for pieces the gods are actively
  playing (Heroes, etc.)
- Emergent Eadras-events update the board reflectively (the Game
  accommodates what it did not initiate)

This is a hybrid of the first two positions, not yet a fifth canonical
school. The Lirran clergy (who already favour the reflective reading)
and Tarran philosophers (who have a taste for formal ontological
nuance) are the most likely adopters.

== New Game terminology surfaced this turn ==

HERO -- a Game-specific category of mortal piece, marked by personal
divine attention. Heroes are the rare pieces the gods actively play
on a turn-by-turn basis; other mortals are presumably pieces on the
board but not Heroes. The mechanical and social distinction between
Heroes and ordinary mortal-pieces is open canon.
