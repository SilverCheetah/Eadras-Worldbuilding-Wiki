**Summary**: The Sarathim are a proud, mostly-human civilization that rose centuries after the [[demon-war|Demon War]], publicly identifying themselves as the **true heirs of the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] legacy** — the people who kept the flame of Velrathi scholarship, ambition, and arcane mastery alive while the [[katharim|Katharim]] retreated into death-priesthood and the [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] embraced chaos. Sarathim
The Satharim Empire was a major civilization that rose in the aftermath of the Demon War. It endured for approximately five thousand years before its collapse during the Sorcerer’s Crusade. Many of the later Trade Leagues, including the precursor to the Orden, originated from institutions and practices developed during the Satharim period.
Government
The empire was ruled by a Divine Emperor or Empress, who was regarded as carrying a literal divine legacy. Succession was strictly hereditary and limited to direct parent-to-child descent. Lateral succession (such as brother to brother) was not permitted.
If the reigning bloodline died out with no surviving direct offspring, this was interpreted as a divine signal that the current legacy had ended. In such cases, the noble houses would compete for the throne. The richest house with the most capable candidate would have their chosen individual elevated to the position of Divine Emperor or Empress.
Upon ascending the throne, the new ruler formally severed all ties with their original noble house and became a divine figure standing above all houses.
Noble Houses
The empire had no traditional guild system. Instead, the noble houses directly controlled and administered the various trades and industries. Each noble house typically dominated two or three specific trades.
The noble houses were highly competitive and engaged in constant political maneuvering. They resolved disputes primarily through intrigue, negotiation, and poison rather than open violence against one another.
Noble houses were permitted to maintain their own standing military forces, including both land and naval units. However, the combined strength of any single house’s military forces was legally prohibited from exceeding one-tenth the size of the Imperial military.
The Bureaucracy
Day-to-day governance of the empire was managed by a powerful professional bureaucracy. All top-ranking officials within this bureaucracy were eunuchs.
These eunuchs were recruited exclusively from commoner children. Testing squads dispatched by the bureaucracy traveled the empire seeking children who demonstrated high intelligence, loyalty, and aptitude. Selected children were purchased from their parents and placed into intensive training programs. Those who successfully completed training were made into eunuchs and entered the bureaucracy. Those who failed were assigned to regular imperial service as workers or lower functionaries.
The highest-ranking eunuchs owed their primary loyalty to the bureaucracy itself, rather than to the Divine Emperor or Empress.
Military
The Divine Emperor maintained a large professional Imperial Army and Imperial Navy. These forces were composed entirely of commoners. Nobles were strictly prohibited from serving in the Imperial military at any rank. This policy was designed to ensure the military’s absolute loyalty to the Emperor and to prevent noble houses from infiltrating or undermining the Imperial forces.
In contrast, noble houses maintained their own private military forces for the protection of their commercial interests. These house armies were legally limited in size to no more than one-tenth the total strength of the Imperial military.

The Sarathim

Summary: The Sarathim were a proud, mostly-human civilization that rose centuries after the [[demon-war|Demon War]], publicly proclaiming themselves the true heirs of the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] legacy — the people who preserved Velrathi scholarship, ambition, mercantile sophistication, and arcane mastery while the [[katharim|Katharim]] turned inward toward death-priesthood and the [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] embraced joyful chaos.

For nearly five thousand years, the Sarathim Empire dominated the warm trade corridors of the [[azure-basin|Azure Basin]] and surrounding regions through a fusion of merchant-prince politics, divine monarchy, bureaucratic administration, and arcane scholarship. Their civilization blended Renaissance-style mercantile culture, ancient monumental grandeur, and the scholarly traditions of the Islamic Golden Age into a single distinctive identity.

At its height, the empire centered on the golden city of Sarathis, built atop one of the fourteen [[seven-conduits|conduit]] termini beneath the shadow of a colossal sorcerous tower. The empire fell during the [[sorcerer-crusade|Sorcerer Crusade]], when Sarathis was consumed by religious riots, anti-sorcerer fervor, and coordinated assaults by the three [[black-hand|Black Hand]]-influenced military religious orders.

Modern Sarathim survive as a fractured diaspora scattered across the [[azure-basin|Azure Basin]] and neighboring lands — wealthy, cultured, politically connected, and haunted by the memory of a civilization that once believed itself eternal.

The Sarathim Empire

The Sarathim Empire endured for approximately five thousand years following the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. It became one of the defining postwar civilizations of Eadras, shaping trade, banking, administration, diplomacy, and magical scholarship across much of the known world.

Many later mercantile institutions — including several precursor systems that eventually contributed to the rise of the [[orden|Orden]] and the modern trade leagues — originated from Sarathim commercial law, accounting practices, and merchant-house structures.

The empire's identity rested on three intertwined convictions:

That civilization survives through knowledge and organization
That wealth and power are inseparable
That the Sarathim alone properly inherited the disciplined greatness of the [[velrathi|Velrathi]]

This belief produced both extraordinary achievements and immense arrogance.

Government

The empire was ruled by a Divine Emperor or Empress, regarded not merely as a monarch but as the living bearer of a sacred imperial legacy.

Succession was strictly hereditary and passed only from parent to direct child. Lateral succession — sibling inheritance, cousin branches, and collateral dynasties — was forbidden. The Sarathim viewed the imperial bloodline as a sacred continuity rather than a merely political office.

If a ruling line died without direct heirs, the event was interpreted as a divine judgment that the dynasty's legitimacy had ended. In such periods, the noble houses entered intense political competition for the throne. Wealth, influence, alliances, military support, and bureaucratic favor all determined which house would prevail.

Once elevated, however, the new ruler formally severed all ties to their original house and became a figure above the noble order entirely — no longer merely aristocratic, but sacred.

Noble Houses

The Sarathim lacked a traditional guild system. Instead, the great noble houses directly controlled commerce, industry, banking, shipping, and production.

Each major house typically dominated two or three sectors:

Maritime trade
Banking and finance
Textile production
Arcane industries
Spice and luxury importation
Agricultural estates
Shipbuilding
Relic recovery
Alchemical production

The noble houses were intensely competitive. Open warfare between houses inside imperial territory was technically illegal and culturally frowned upon, but political conflict was constant.

Sarathim noble culture became infamous for:

Intrigue
Blackmail
Economic sabotage
Marriage manipulation
Assassination by poison
Proxy conflicts
Information warfare

A common saying in later centuries:

“A Sarathim smiles with the mouth and measures your coffin with the eyes.”

Private Armies and the Imperial Military

Every major house maintained private military forces to defend its commercial interests and prestige.

These included:

House guards
Mercenary companies
Naval squadrons
Arcane specialists
Private marines
Caravan armies

However, strict imperial law prevented any one house from becoming militarily dominant. No noble house was permitted to field forces exceeding one-tenth the strength of the Imperial military.

The Imperial Army and Imperial Navy were instead maintained directly by the Divine Throne and staffed almost entirely by commoners.

Nobles were forbidden from serving within the Imperial military command structure at any rank. This prohibition existed specifically to prevent house infiltration of the state military and to ensure the army's loyalty belonged to the Emperor rather than to aristocratic dynasties.

The result was an unusual political balance:

The noble houses controlled wealth
The bureaucracy controlled administration
The throne controlled the professional military

The system endured remarkably long before eventually calcifying into corruption and factional paralysis.

The Bureaucracy

The true day-to-day functioning of the empire rested in the hands of an enormous professional bureaucracy.

The highest administrative officials were exclusively eunuchs, recruited from common-born children identified for exceptional intelligence, memory, discipline, and loyalty. Imperial testing officials traveled throughout the empire searching for suitable candidates.

Children selected for the bureaucracy were purchased from their families and entered into brutal state educational systems focused on:

Literacy
Accounting
Law
Administration
Diplomacy
Logistics
Political theory
Arcane regulation

Those who succeeded rose into the administrative elite. Those who failed entered lower civil service positions instead.

Over centuries, the bureaucracy evolved into a political force almost independent of the throne itself. Senior bureaucrats often viewed their true loyalty as belonging to the continuity of the empire rather than to any individual Emperor or Empress.

This produced a constant low-level tension between:

The Divine Throne
The noble merchant houses
The bureaucratic apparatus

The Sarathim state survived as long as it did largely because none of the three could fully dominate the others.

Sarathis

The imperial capital, Sarathis, stood atop one of the fourteen [[seven-conduits|conduit]] termini and was crowned by a colossal sorcerous tower visible for miles across the surrounding basin.

The city embodied the Sarathim worldview:

Marble plazas and monumental stairways
Vast libraries and observatories
Merchant palaces covered in mosaic and gold
Arcane universities
Bureaucratic archive-citadels
Crowded canal districts and spice markets
Hanging gardens and rooftop waterworks
Great harbor-fortresses controlling regional trade

Sarathis was simultaneously:

Imperial capital
Financial center
Magical center
Cultural center
Diplomatic crossroads

Its destruction effectively shattered the empire beyond recovery.

The Sorcerer Crusade and the Fall

The Sarathim collapse came during the [[sorcerer-crusade|Sorcerer Crusade]].

Religious panic surrounding sorcery spread rapidly through the empire. Existing tensions between the throne, the houses, the bureaucracy, and the common population exploded into violence.

The three military religious orders — covertly manipulated by the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] — turned anti-sorcerer hysteria into organized destruction.

Sarathis fell to:

Riots
Purges
Political betrayal
Religious mobs
Coordinated military assault

The great sorcerous institutions were burned. Libraries vanished. Merchant dynasties fled. Bureaucratic archives were looted or destroyed. Entire bloodlines disappeared in weeks.

The empire never recovered.

Modern Sarathim

Modern Sarathim survive primarily as a diaspora civilization rather than a unified state.

They remain heavily concentrated throughout the [[azure-basin|Azure Basin]], particularly in:

Merchant republics
Banking houses
Maritime trade cities
Diplomatic circles
Arcane scholarship
Luxury industries

Many Sarathim houses still preserve fragments of imperial etiquette, architecture, cuisine, and political culture.

Even impoverished Sarathim communities often maintain:

Obsessive record-keeping
Elaborate family genealogies
Formal education traditions
Mercantile networks
Strong cultural pride

Other peoples frequently stereotype them as:

Sophisticated
Manipulative
Cultured
Ambitious
Arrogant
Beautifully mannered
Dangerously patient

The Sarathim themselves tend to view their civilization not as dead, but as temporarily diminished.

A common saying among the old diaspora houses:

“Empires drown. Houses endure.”
