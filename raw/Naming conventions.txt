
Racial Naming Conventions

1. Elfen (Created by Mark)

Style: Elegant, melodic, flowing, nature-inspired with a strong Hawaiian phonetic influence (soft consonants, abundant vowels, rhythmic and musical quality).
Feminine names: Always end in -a (or a diphthong ending in an “a” sound, e.g. -ia, -ua, -ea).
Masculine names: Always end in -o (or a diphthong ending in an “o” sound, e.g. -io, -uo, -eo).
Common sounds: l, m, n, r, s, h, w, th + lots of vowels.
Examples: Elenara, Liraena, Thalira, Miralua, Kaelana, Thalor, Elorio, Silvaro, Aulani, Kaelo.

2. Tarran (Created by Melania)

Style: Strong, martial, disciplined, authoritative, solid.
Common sounds: Hard consonants (t, k, r, th, d, v).
Patterns: Short, powerful syllables. Often starts with Tar-, Kor-, Val-, Thor-, Dar-, Mar-.
Endings: -an, -ath, -or, -ak, -un, -ak.
Examples: Korvath, Valdrak, Tharun, Daramis, Markor, Tarveth, Tarran.

3. Velrothi (Created by Rand)

Style: Arcane, scholarly, mysterious, slightly gothic and esoteric. Feels ancient and weighty.
Common sounds: Complex clusters with v, r, th, x, k, n, z.
Patterns: Starts with Vel-, Roth-, Vex-, Nar-, Kath-, Zor-, Mel-, Vor-.
Endings: -othi, -ar, -eth, -un, -or, -ith, -and, -ex.
Examples: Velena, Xarvion, Rothena, Narveth, Kathira, Vexara, Rothandar, Melaroth.

Halfling Naming Conventions
Core Structure

Given Name: Practical and sturdy (Daric, Thomen, Garrick, Miraeth, Alden, Brenna, etc.).
Clan Name: Usually ends in -ric, -bar, -well, -den, -field, -carver (Deepbar, Stonewell, Rootcarver, Hillric, etc.).

The Cognomen System
When a halfling feels The Change and takes to the road, they earn cognomens — descriptive honorifics granted by others based on notable deeds, personality, or events during their travels.
Key Rules:

Cognomens are always given by others — never self-chosen.
A halfling can accumulate many cognomens over a lifetime of travel.
When introducing themselves formally while traveling, they list their most relevant or prestigious cognomens.
Upon returning home after The Change, most halflings set their cognomens aside (though family and close friends may still use them affectionately).

Formal Address Examples

Settled Phase:
Thomen of Deepbar Hillric
Traveling Phase:
Thomen Farwalker, Stormrider of Deepbar Hillric
or simply Thomen Farwalker Stormrider among those who know them.

Common Ways Cognomens Are Earned

Surviving a dangerous journey (“Farwalker”, “Stormrider”, “Riftcrosser”)
Skill in trade or negotiation (“Brightcoin”, “Fairdeal”, “Longbarter”)
Helping a community (“Rootfinder”, “Bridgewarden”, “Hearthfriend”)
Notable personality or quirk (“Quicklaugh”, “Silentstep”, “Twicebold”)

A well-traveled halfling might eventually carry 4–7 cognomens, though they usually only use 1–3 at a time depending on context.
Flavor
Cognomens are a source of quiet pride. When two traveling halflings meet, they often exchange cognomens as a form of respect — much like warriors comparing battle scars. A halfling with many respected cognomens is treated with deference even by taller races.

Mitan Naming Conventions
Overall Style
Heavy, resonant, and mineral. Mitan names feel like they were carved from stone or forged in deep fires. They are deliberately weighty and imposing, reflecting their role as the first underground warriors and laborers. Names often evoke metals, ores, gemstones, or the deep earth itself.
Key Sounds & Patterns

Hard, heavy consonants: K, D, R, Th, G, B, M, N, V
Deep vowel sounds: long “o”, “u”, “ar”, “or”
Names tend to be 2–3 syllables, often compound or ending in strong stops.
They sound ancient, durable, and slightly martial.

Male Names (Common)

Iron examples: Korthun, Durak, Tharok, Gromnir, Borak, Valdrak
Stone/Gem examples: Granthar, Obsid, Flintar, Basalt, Onyxar, Rubar
Metal examples: Adamar, Mithrak, Steelorn, Copperak, Ironthar

Female Names

Slightly softer but still heavy: Duraka, Tharona, Gromira, Valdris, Kortha, Granthara
Gem/Mineral examples: Emeraldis, Sapphira, Onyxia, Rubara, Amethystar

Cognomens (Earned Titles)
Like halflings, Mitans earn cognomens through deeds, especially in battle or mining. These are always granted by others and can accumulate.
Common Mitan Cognomens (Heavy & Mineral Themed):

Deepvein, Stonebreaker, Ironfist, Riftcarver, Obsidion, Graniteheart, Mithralforge, Bloodvein, Underlord, Cavemaster

Full Formal Address

Settled / Clan Phase: [Given Name] of Clan [Clan Name]
Example: Korthun of Clan Deepvein
With Cognomen (especially honored warriors/miners):
Korthun Stonebreaker of Clan Deepvein
or Korthun Stonebreaker, Riftcarver of Clan Deepvein

Clan Names (Mineral / Metal Inspired)

Deepvein, Ironvein, Stoneforge, Obsidian, Granitehold, Mithralcore, Rubark, Basaltar, Underforge

Flavor Notes
Mitan names feel like they belong underground — heavy, resonant, and built to last. A Mitan with many cognomens is treated with great respect, as each title represents proven endurance in the deep places. Modern dwarves still carry echoes of this heavy naming tradition, though theirs has softened slightly over time.

Rathar Naming Conventions (Final)
Overall Style
Scholarly, precise, arcane, and resonant with stone and hidden knowledge. Names feel formal, ancient, and intellectual — as if spoken in vast crystal-lit underground halls.
Core Rules

Rathar identity is defined first by House (there are only 10 ancient Houses).
Second by Hold — the specific underground enclave, archive-city, or scholarly fortress they belong to.
No cognomens. Names are strictly formal and tied to lineage and location.

Given Names

Male: Valthar, Rathandar, Korthalion, Pharzen, Thalorak, Xarvenor, Narvethian, Lithor, Archivon, Zenthir
Female: Valthara, Rathandri, Korthalia, Pharzene, Thalira, Xarvena, Narvethia, Lithara, Archivara, Zenthira

Rathandar of House Valtharion, Stone Memory Hold

Everyday Usage

Among other Rathar: Often shortened to Given Name + House (e.g. “Valthar Korthal”)
When dealing with outsiders: Full formal name is used to show respect and clarity.
In scholarly correspondence: Usually signed with House and Hold.

Here are the Ten Houses:

House Valtharion (The Eldest House)
Focus: Theoretical Arcana and Metaphysics
The oldest and most prestigious House. They specialize in pure theory, ley line mapping, and the fundamental laws of magic. Many of the greatest advances in understanding the Weave and Sourceshine came from Valtharion researchers.

House Korthal
Focus: Crystallomancy and Magical Architecture
Masters of growing, shaping, and stabilizing magical crystals. They designed and maintained many of the grand underground archive-cities and crystal conduits.

House Narveth
Focus: History, Memory, and Temporal Studies
The great archivists and chronologists. They maintain the most complete historical records in Eadras and study time anomalies and memory magic.

House Pharzen
Focus: Alchemy and Transmutation
Renowned for their work with magical reagents, potions, and the transmutation of matter. They were instrumental in developing many of the early mana-infused metals.

House Lithorium
Focus: Geomancy and Stone Magic
Specialists in underground construction, stabilization magic, and stone-based enchantments. They perfected the techniques for carving safe, vast underground holds.

House Deepcrystal
Focus: Divination and Far-Seeing
Masters of scrying, prophecy, and long-distance communication. They maintain vast crystal networks used for observation across great distances.

House Runewarden
Focus: Rune Magic and Warding
Experts in permanent enchantments, defensive runes, and sealing dangerous knowledge. They created many of the most powerful wards protecting Rathar vaults.

House Archivault
Focus: Secure Knowledge Preservation
The guardians of forbidden and dangerous lore. They maintain the most heavily protected archives and develop new methods of storing information that even time cannot erode.

House Voidscribe
Focus: Shadow, Void, and Liminal Studies
They study the boundaries between planes, shadow magic, and liminal spaces. After the Demon War, many Voidscribe researchers were instrumental in early attempts to understand Limina.

House Echoheart
Focus: Resonance, Sound, and Sympathetic Magic
Specialists in vibrational magic, echo manipulation, and sympathetic links. They developed many communication and harmonic-based magical systems used deep underground.



Additional Notes

Each House maintains its own grand Hold (underground enclave/archive-city), though some smaller branches may share space.
Loyalty to one’s House is extremely strong, but Houses frequently collaborate on large research projects.

The Rathar – Current Status
The Rathar as a distinct people no longer exist in any meaningful numbers after the Demon War (10,000 years ago).
What Happened:

The vast majority of the Rathar perished during the Demon War.
Of the survivors:
Many chose the Veil of Forgetting and became the Beast-folk (the Laughing Third).
Many others refused the Veil and became the Katharim (the Weighted Third / Rememberers).

Only a very small number of Rathar survived as traditional Rathar. They sealed themselves into a handful of extremely well-hidden, heavily warded underground holds. These enclaves remain isolated to this day, rarely interacting with the outside world.

The Ten Houses Today
Of the original Ten Ancient Houses, only fragments remain:

Most Houses were completely wiped out or fully transitioned into either Katharim or Beast-folk lineages.
A few Houses (particularly House Valtharion, House Archivault, and House Voidscribe) still have tiny surviving populations in sealed holds, jealously guarding what remains of their ancient knowledge.
These surviving Rathar are extremely reclusive, paranoid, and protective of their archives. They view the outside world as irreparably tainted by the events of the Demon War.

Modern Identification
Because so few true Rathar remain, the old House + Hold naming system is now mostly of historical interest. The surviving Rathar still use it among themselves, but it is almost never heard by outsiders.
Examples of Surviving Rathar Names (extremely rare):

Valthar of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault (Sealed)
Thalira of House Archivault, Deep Memory Hold
Narvethian of House Voidscribe, Riftward Sanctum
