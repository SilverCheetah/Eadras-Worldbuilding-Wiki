1. THE FOUR ORDERS ARE DERIVED, NOT INVENTED
================================================================

[META] The four orders are derivable from the language hierarchy established by the naming cosmology. They are not four separately invented magic systems.

- Wizards study the Arcane Language.
- Priests appeal to beings who speak the Divine Language.
- Lexanari are born able to speak the Primordial Language.
- Netharim bypass language entirely.

Each order corresponds to a specific relationship with the language hierarchy. Wizards work at the tier mortals can fully access. Priests work by proxy through beings at a higher tier. Lexanari work directly at a tier above what most mortals can survive. Netharim sidestep the whole hierarchy.

This is the same derivation principle the Draconic analysis identified: when a system feels well-designed in Eadras, it is almost always because it was DERIVED from a deeper rule rather than invented from scratch. The four orders are a textbook case.


================================================================
2. THE THREE LANGUAGE-USERS PLUS ONE NON-USER
================================================================

[META] Three of the four orders work through language (Arcane / Divine / Primordial). The fourth deliberately does not.

This is structurally important because it prevents the system from being a single-axis power slider. If all four orders worked through language at different tiers, they would form a linear hierarchy: weakest to strongest, with Wizards at the bottom and Lexanari at the top. The Netharim break this line.

The Netharim are not stronger or weaker than the Lexanari. They are operating on a DIFFERENT AXIS. They cannot be ranked against the language-users because they are not playing the same game.

HIDDEN CONNECTION: This is the same multi-axial principle the Modernity framework uses to refuse a single "tech level" slider, and the same principle the Cosmology uses to define Chaos as negative space rather than as an opposing force on the same continuum as reality. Eadras consistently refuses to collapse distinct dimensions into a single ranking. The four orders are this principle applied to magic.


================================================================
3. LANGUAGE TIER PREDICTS RARITY AND DANGER
================================================================

[META] The three language-using orders follow a clean inverse relationship:

- Wizards: most accessible, lowest tier of language, most numerous.
- Priests: mediated access to a higher tier, common but dependent on relationship with a deity.
- Lexanari: direct access to the second-highest tier, born only, lethal training, vanishingly rare.

Higher language tier means greater power, smaller population, and higher cost. This is not arbitrary game balance. It follows naturally from the cosmology: the closer to the True Language, the more reality-shaping the speech, and the fewer beings can survive holding that capacity.

This is the same pattern as the cosmology's overall corruption cascade. The True Language is unspeakable by mortals. Each tier down opens more access but at lower power. The four orders are the mortal-tier expression of the cosmological gradient.


================================================================
4. WHY THE PRIESTS-AS-CHANNELS DESIGN WORKS
================================================================

[META] Priests do not wield magic directly. They call upon gods who use the Divine Language on their behalf.

This is a structurally elegant choice because it does several things at once:

- It explains why divine magic has the moral and political character of negotiation rather than command. The priest is asking, not doing. The god decides whether and how to respond.

- It prevents the Priest order from being a redundant "lesser Wizard." Priests are not weaker language-users than Wizards. They are not language-users at all. They are negotiators with beings who use the language for them.

- It generates faction politics naturally. A Priest's effectiveness depends on relationship with their deity, on the deity's compatibility with the request, and on the deity's willingness to act. This is a richer power structure than "the Priest casts a spell."

CRAFT PRINCIPLE: Divine magic in fantasy is often handled clumsily as "Wizards but they pray for spells instead of studying them." The Priest-as-channel design avoids this trap by making the Priest a genuinely different kind of practitioner with different politics, different limits, and different opportunities.


================================================================
5. THE LEXANARI PRECISION PROBLEM IS A CRAFT TRIUMPH
================================================================

[META] The Lexanari mechanic — mortal minds cannot hold the full complexity of reality the way a deific mind can, so vague commands produce dangerous results — solves a notorious problem in fantasy magic systems.

Most "reality-altering wish magic" in fantasy collapses into one of two unsatisfying outcomes:
- It works as intended, making the system arbitrarily powerful and boring.
- It backfires catastrophically as a GM punishment for the player wanting too much.

The Lexanari solution is neither. The mechanic states: precision works. Imprecision is dangerous. The danger is not arbitrary punishment. It is the metaphysical consequence of trying to use a language that requires a mind larger than the speaker has.

A Lexanari who can hold more complexity in mind gets more precise results. A Lexanari who shouts "Stop!" while in danger might stop their attacker's heart or stop all motion in the surrounding area — both are reasonable parsings of "stop" with no further specification.

This is the rare case of a magic system where the limitation is INTELLECTUALLY interesting rather than just mechanically constraining. It makes the Lexanari work like negotiators with the language itself.


================================================================
6. THE LEXANARI ARE TRAINABLE BUT NOT LEARNABLE
================================================================

[META] The distinction matters. The Lexanari gift is innate. It cannot be acquired. But those born with it must be TRAINED to survive using it.

This separates the Lexanari from two common fantasy magic-system tropes:

- "Anyone can learn this if they study hard enough" (the Wizard model). Lexanari magic explicitly cannot be acquired by study.
- "Bloodline confers automatic mastery" (the Sorcerer model). Lexanari magic explicitly DOES require survival of brutal training. Being born with the gift is necessary but not sufficient.

The combination means Lexanari are simultaneously rare-by-bloodline AND rare-by-survival. The order is not a hereditary aristocracy of automatic adepts. It is a tiny number of survivors who had to earn the use of an innate gift through deadly discipline.

HIDDEN CONNECTION: This is the same structural shape as the chosen observer principle in the Cosmology. The Lexanari, like the chosen observer, are DESIGNATED — by birth in the Lexanari case, by reality's need in the chosen observer case — and must then survive the consequences of their designation. Both roles are about being chosen for something costly and irreversible.


================================================================
7. THE NETHARIM AS PHILOSOPHY-AS-MAGIC
================================================================

[META] The Netharim mechanic is the strongest single innovation in the four-order system. Reality is reshaped by the practitioner's understanding of how reality works. The more rigorous, coherent, and deep the understanding, the more precise and powerful the effect.

This is genuinely fresh territory for fantasy magic.

A physicist Netharim does not start a fire the same way a poet Netharim does. A hunter Netharim does not heal the same way a metaphysicist Netharim does. Each practitioner's INTERNAL MODEL OF REALITY is the actual instrument.

This produces several rare qualities for a magic system:

- It rewards genuine scholarship over arbitrary spell-list memorization.
- It makes character backstory and personal philosophy mechanically meaningful.
- It allows two Netharim of the same "level" to produce visibly different magic.
- It makes the practitioner's WORLDVIEW the actual source of their power.

THEME: A Netharim's magic is the externalization of their inner coherence. If their understanding of reality is muddled, their magic is muddled. If their understanding is sharp, their magic is sharp. This makes the Netharim the most ethically loaded of the four orders — they are literally as good as their philosophy is.


================================================================
8. WHY THE NETHARIM AVOID THE COPY PROBLEM
================================================================

[META] The Netharim were initially developed as a replacement for Sorcerers borrowed from another author. The original concern (raised explicitly in the source session) was that the Netharim might be too close to that author's Sorcerers in concept, since both were intuitive practitioners.

The resolution that made the Netharim genuinely distinct: the original-author Sorcerers PULL THREADS from a Weave and tie them into knots. They are spellweavers. The Netharim, by contrast, do not pull anything from anywhere. They impose their personal understanding of reality directly onto the world.

These are mechanically different acts:
- Spellweaving: manipulate external magical material into spell-shaped knots.
- Reality-shaping by understanding: impose internal mental coherence onto external reality.

The flavor difference is also genuine:
- Spellweavers are ARTISANS working with magical material.
- Netharim are PHILOSOPHERS whose philosophy is itself the instrument.

CRAFT NOTE: This is a worked example of how to address the copy problem in worldbuilding. Don't change the surface and hope. Change the underlying mechanism so the flavor difference is structurally grounded.


================================================================
9. DRUIDS AS NATURE-PRIESTS, NOT NATURE-WIZARDS
================================================================

[META] The decision to demote Druids from a full order to a sub-order of Priests (specifically, Priests of Quetzal, Sylph, and Nox) does important work.

It solves the borrowing problem. The original-author Druids had the "extremely powerful but destroyed by violating nature's laws" mechanic. That mechanic has been MOVED to the Lexanari (with a different trigger — imprecision rather than nature-violation). The Druid name and role survive, but the mechanic is reassigned.

It grounds the Druid in the existing pantheon. By making Druids serve specific deities, the order is no longer a free-floating "nature magic" tradition. It is a tradition rooted in specific theological commitments to specific gods.

It distinguishes Druids from regular Priests. Druids are field-priests — self-sufficient, wilderness-trained, mediators between mortal society and the fey realms. They are doing different work from temple-bound Priests of urban gods, even though they share the same underlying magical mechanism.

HIDDEN CONNECTION: This is the same structural move the cosmology makes with the Tome and the closed time bubble — different scales of the same protective function. Here, Druids and regular Priests are different specializations of the same fundamental relationship (mortal channel to divine speaker). They are not separate orders. They are different applications of one order.


================================================================
10. THE WEAVE QUESTION REMAINS OPEN
================================================================

[META] With Sorcerers replaced by Netharim, the original mechanical reason for the Weave is gone. The Netharim do not pull threads from anything.

The source session reached this realization but did not resolve it. The Weave's PROTECTIVE FUNCTION — preventing demons / interdivores from consuming raw magic during the Demon War — may still be canonically necessary. The Weave's WEB STRUCTURE is no longer mechanically required.

This is an UNRESOLVED CANON ISSUE. The Weave may need to be:
- Kept as a residual cosmological structure with its protective function preserved and its web-shape de-emphasized.
- Replaced by a different mechanism that performs the same protective function.
- Removed from canon, with the Demon War history rewritten to account for the change.

The choice has implications for the Demon War splat (one of the four planned eras). The Weave's status should be settled before that splat is written.


================================================================
11. THE SYSTEM AS COSMOLOGY MADE PRACTICAL
================================================================

[META] The complete four-order system is the cosmology of naming made operational for ordinary use.

The cosmology says: reality is shaped by language tiers, from True down through Primordial, Divine, Arcane, and ordinary mortal speech. The four orders are the practical answer to "how do mortals actually engage with this hierarchy?"

- Engage with the Arcane tier directly (Wizards).
- Engage with the Divine tier by proxy through a being who speaks it (Priests).
- Engage with the Primordial tier directly, at extreme cost (Lexanari).
- Bypass the hierarchy entirely through internal understanding (Netharim).

The four orders together exhaust the legitimate ways a mortal can relate to the language hierarchy. There is no missing fifth option. The system is COMPLETE.

CRAFT PRINCIPLE: A well-derived magic system feels complete. The four orders feel complete because they exhaust the cosmological options. Any future addition to the magical landscape would need to either fit one of these four categories or expose a gap in the cosmology — and there does not appear to be such a gap.
