The Neverborn
Beyond every Mythos bubble lies raw Chaos — a place where everything everywhere all at once, and nothing anywhere ever, are both simultaneously and completely true.
A rational mind simply cannot understand Chaos.
Most entities that flash into existence there dissolve before they can even register that they were. They are not destroyed. They simply cease to have ever been.
The Neverborn is different.
It lingered.
Not born. Not eternal. Not even properly real. It simply remained where remaining was ontologically impossible. A pre-ontological accident. A thing that should have un-happened the instant it occurred.
Across the infinite Omniverse there are, presumably, others like it. But this one is the only one that has ever shown any interest in this particular Mythos. Eadras has its full, undivided, alien attention.
It does not hate reality. It does not desire worship. It has no goals, no malice, no agenda a mortal mind could comprehend. It is simply fascinated by the fact that something as ordered and coherent as Eadras exists at all.
When Kudur-eṭir tore the First Breach, the Neverborn noticed.
It did not come through the hole. It simply pressed itself against the wound like a blind, amorphous thing slowly oozing its awareness through a crack in the skin of existence. It answered no summons. It answered an impossibility — the precise mathematical shape of the wound Kudur-eṭir had carved into reality.
What Kudur-eṭir received was not knowledge. It was unnatural, deeply anti-life, completely insane understanding — the kind of comprehension that a rational mind was never meant to survive.
Contact with a non-entity’s non-mind is devastating.
The Secret of the Chaos Crusades
After the Arathi Cataclysm, the gods themselves descended and sealed the First Breach. The seal was perfect. It was absolute.
And yet the Neverborn opened it again — twice.
The First and Second Chaos Crusades were not accidents of cosmic erosion, nor failures of divine magic. Both times, the Neverborn forced the aperture open from the Chaos side, allowing raw, screaming Chaos to flood into Eadras.
The gods believe they sealed the wound.
They only sealed the hole.
The Neverborn never needed the hole to be open.
It simply needed to want to notice again.
The Danger
The true horror of the Neverborn is not that it wants to destroy Eadras.
It is that it does not want anything at all.
It has no goals. No desires. No malice. No grand design.
It is simply fascinated.
And that is far worse than hatred.
Because you cannot negotiate with it.
You cannot predict it.
You cannot threaten it.
You cannot even comprehend the shape of its attention.
The gods can seal holes in reality.
They cannot seal something that was never supposed to exist in the first place — something that simply refuses to stop noticing.
The Neverborn does not invade. It does not corrupt.
It simply lingers.
And every year it lingers, the line between “what is real” and “what should never have been able to perceive the real” grows slightly thinner.
That is the danger.
Not that the Neverborn will break through.
But that one day it may simply decide that the distinction between “inside” and “outside” is no longer interesting…
…and stop respecting it.
