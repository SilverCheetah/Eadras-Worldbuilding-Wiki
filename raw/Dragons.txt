**Rise of the Dragons**

The first dragons were created when the ascended mortal god Ixthal caused a Source Flare. Nine giant reptile eggs were transformed, giving rise to the first true dragons — one from each of the nine color clans: Red, Blue, Green, Purple, Black, White, Copper, Silver, and Gold.

From these nine progenitors, dragonkind spread across Eadras. For the next hundred million years, dragons dominated the world, living in a destructive cycle of war, recovery, stability, overcrowding, and war again.

**The Unicorns**

Later, Ixthal caused another Source Flare, creating the unicorns — the second sapient race on Eadras. The unicorns lived as telepathic herd animals on the plains, with no spoken language or cities. To dragons, they were simply another kind of prey.

Eventually, war broke out between the dragons and the unicorns. Though individual dragons were far more powerful, the unicorns fought as a coordinated herd, instinctively following their leaders. Against this unified force, the dragons were defeated.

After their defeat, dragons simply stopped hunting the plains. No formal treaty was made and no direct contact occurred between the two races. The dragons simply passed down an unspoken understanding among their kind: the plains were no longer to be hunted.

Shortly after the war ended, curious young dragons and curious young unicorns began cautiously approaching each other along the borders of their territories.

**Cultural Exchange**

Through these border meetings, the young dragons and young unicorns slowly began to share knowledge that neither race had previously possessed.

The unicorns taught the dragons **psionics**, a branch of magic dragons had never discovered. In exchange, the dragons taught the unicorns **elemental magic** and the concept of **personal power** — magic drawn from the individual rather than the collective herd. These exchanges also introduced the unicorns to the radical idea of individuality itself, something completely foreign to a herd-based species.

Over time, these friendships led to greater understanding between the two races. Inspired by what they learned from the unicorns, the dragons eventually created the **Treaty of Ratharil**, which limited the total dragon population to exactly 999,999 — in honor of the original nine progenitors — to finally break their ancient cycle of overcrowding and devastating war.

**Modern Dragons**

In the modern era, elder dragons have become extremely reclusive. They rarely involve themselves in the affairs of the world, but when they do, their actions are monumental and world-changing.

Young adult and older dragons possess the ability to shapeshift into humanoid form at will. Many of them have adapted to the current age by working as highly sought-after mercenaries, using their immense power to build their hoards and gain status.

**The Nine Color Clans**

Although dragons are deeply individualistic, each color clan has developed its own distinct culture, traditions, and preferred environments over time. These tendencies are shaped by the personality and rulership style of each clan's eldest dragon and are passed down through generations.

*   **Blues** are highly clan-oriented and prefer **deserts**.
*   **Golds** are even more tightly knit than the Blues and prefer living **high in the mountains**, though some have chosen to live in cities.
*   **Reds** tend to be standoffish with one another. Their leaders are generally hands-off. They prefer **hot regions**, including both volcanic areas and arid hot lands.
*   **Blacks** are notably less orderly and prefer **swamps, river deltas, and forested wetlands**.
*   **Purples** are chaotic and unpredictable. Their entire clan was heavily influenced in its early history by the Elder Goddess Faye. They can be found living in almost any environment.
*   **Coppers** tend to prefer areas of devastation, such as volcanic regions or ruined lands.
*   **Silvers** are staid and isolationist, preferring **marine and oceanic environments**.
*   **Greens** are highly individualistic and tend to live in **forested regions**.
*   **Whites** are individualistic but fiercely maintain clan traditions. Because they inhabit cold, icy regions, they uphold a strict custom of hospitality — any clan member who arrives is welcomed and given food and shelter.

**Clan Hospitality**

All nine dragon clans practice some form of hospitality toward members of their own color, though the specifics vary greatly between clans. Some offer only the bare minimum, while others maintain elaborate traditions of food, shelter, and protection.

**Respect for Elders**

Age is one of the most respected aspects of draconic society. A dragon will automatically defer to any dragon older than themselves, regardless of clan. This deference is instinctual and nearly impossible to resist. However, this instinct can be overridden if the need is great enough.
