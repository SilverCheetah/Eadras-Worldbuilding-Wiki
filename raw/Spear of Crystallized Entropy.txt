Spear of Crystallized Entropy
**The Creation of the Spear of Crystallized Entropy**

In the final days of the Arathi civilization, the archmage **Ssar’veth** sought to master decay itself — to reverse entropy, to freeze the natural unraveling of all things and stop the universe from winding down.

To achieve this, he committed an act that was considered not only forbidden, but utterly mad. He reached beyond the boundaries of any mythos, into the raw **Primordial Chaos** — the formless void where nothing can hold shape and everything constantly devours and becomes everything else.

There, he made contact with an unspeakable godlike entity that shouldn’t exist — a being of pure, chaos and madness that would later be known across countless Mythos as the neverborn primordial **Cthosh**. With its impossible aid, he forged the Spear of Crystallized Entropy.

Unknown to him, the omniversal creator, through his avatar **Dao**, witnessed the rod’s creation and laid a terrible curse upon it: the rod would grant the wielder’s deepest desire, but only by stripping that desire away and replacing it with its twisted opposite.

When Ssar’veth activated the rod, his noble goal of reversing entropy was torn from him and replaced with an all-consuming hunger to spread chaos and destruction.

He became the first unrestricted Elder God.

The other Elder Gods confronted him in a cataclysmic battle that tore open the land. In the final moments, they managed to trap Ssar’veth inside his own **Icon**. As the only other ascended mortal god began to shatter the Icon, Ssar’veth did not fight to save himself. Instead, with his last moments of power, he called down **the Worldbreaker** — a colossal falling star from the heavens — directly onto the Altean capital city, intending to destroy the world rather than let the Elder Gods claim it.

The moment his Icon was shattered, Ssar’veth ceased to exist entirely.

The Worldbreaker struck. The Arathi civilization was annihilated, the planet’s crust was shattered, and the world was plunged into two hundred million years of desolation.

The elder gods took the spear of crystallized entropy and set it into a volcano at the heart of Darkmirror, the dark reflection of the prime material plane. This, they thought it should secure its evil and chaos away from mortal realms. Instead, over the next 200 million years it changed Darkmirror into a twisted reflection of itself, Shadowdark, tunneling deep into the ground and creating twisted mazes and chambers. Roughly 350 million years after its first use, Tarloth the cruel sought it out to become a god. She was tired of being told no by the gods, and denied her rightful place in the pantheon. So she sought out the spear as a shortcut.

After Tarloth, they imprisoned the Spear in the Brightmirror, and it twisted that plane into what would later be called the Feywild. Darkhoof retrieved it and used it, so the Elder Gods once again sought to find a better prison. This time they imprisoned it within the heart of the Tower of Stars, inside the conduit, in a sealed sub-basement filled with magical traps, illusions, bound guardians, and a chapter of sorcerers led by a Weavespinner.

It was not enough. Xar'vion was able to overcome the protections, destroying the city and the tower in the process, killing all of the sorcerers and the weavespinner, claiming his prize. He gained the power of an unrestricted Elder God, but suffered the curse as well.

Xar'vion set off the Demon war when he was losing the war following his ascension, when the other Gods turned all of the mortal races against him and his followers. He was on the verge of losing when he foolishly summoned a Demon Prince. This rash action allowed demons access to Eadras for the first time, and had terrible consequences. Half of all living creatures died in the war, and several races were completely wiped out. Xar'vion was punished by being restricted to his Icon and his power lessened by being line of sight only.
