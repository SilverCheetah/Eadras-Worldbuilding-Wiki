Spirits — gods as primary-realm non-bureaucratic layer, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture appended to today's spirits canon thread. Josh clarified
that the primary realms -- Celestia and Nyxar -- have gods operating
outside the Bureaucracy in the same role that local courts play in the
secondary realms.)

== Canon ==

In Celestia and Nyxar there are GODS as well as the Bureaucracy. The
gods are OUTSIDE the Bureaucracy -- not subject to it, not part of its
hierarchy in the administrative sense. They have their own:

- Goals
- Desires
- Power
- Capacity to create intrigue and problems for the Bureaucracy

This parallels the secondary realms' interior-governance layer
(established in the previous clarification). All four realms now have a
consistent structural pattern:

  REALM        | BUREAUCRACY (admin)            | NON-BUREAUCRATIC LAYER
  -------------|--------------------------------|------------------------
  Celestia     | Divine Bureaucracy             | Gods (Elder, Ascended)
  Nyxar        | Infernal Bureaucracy           | Senior infernal powers
                                                   (gods of Nyxar)
  Feywild      | Divine Bureaucracy             | Fey courts, regional
                  (shell-administration)           powers
  Shadowdark   | Infernal Bureaucracy           | Shadow councils, regional
                  (shell-administration)           powers

== The pattern across all four realms ==

Every realm has TWO layers:

1. The Bureaucracy: shell-issuance, assignment, promotion processing,
   shell-catalogue maintenance, back-room infrastructure. Plane-wide
   per the Bureaucracy's two-realm scope.

2. A non-Bureaucratic political layer:
   - In primary realms: gods, with personal goals and the power to
     create intrigue
   - In secondary realms: local/regional courts and councils, with
     place-bound politics that varies region to region

The two layers cooperate at handoff points (a promotion needs both a
sponsor and a new shell), but they are different institutions doing
different work, and neither has authority over the other's domain.

== Implications ==

- The Bureaucracy is the CONSTANT across all four realms; what changes
  is the kind of non-Bureaucratic actor operating beside it.
- Primary realms feel POLITICALLY ALIVE because of divine intrigue,
  even though the daily workforce experience is heavily bureaucratic.
- Secondary realms feel POLITICALLY ALIVE because of fey court /
  shadow council intrigue, even though their shells are issued by a
  distant central Bureaucracy.
- The Bureaucracy in primary realms does what bureaucracies always do
  under capricious sovereigns: it manages, it routes around, it
  generates paperwork to explain why the new policy could not be
  implemented this quarter, and it complains in the back rooms.
- The Bureaucracy in secondary realms is more EXTERNAL to its assigned
  realm: it processes shell-changes that local courts have decided to
  confer, without being a participant in the politics that produced
  the decision.

== Correction of an under-statement ==

In a Claude Code session-reply after the previous clarification (the
"interior governance" one), I summarised the asymmetry by saying primary
realms feel "claustrophobic with paperwork" while secondary realms feel
"politically alive." That was an under-statement of the primary realms:
they have their own non-Bureaucratic political layer too (the gods),
which is just as capable of generating intrigue as fey courts or shadow
councils. The asymmetry between primary and secondary realms is about
the SHAPE of the non-Bureaucratic layer (centralised divine pantheon
vs. distributed local courts), not its presence or vigour.
