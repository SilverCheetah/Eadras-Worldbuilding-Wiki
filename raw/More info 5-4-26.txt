More info 5-4-26

Addition — Order Sharanel:

The sorcerer order is known as the “Order Sharanel.”

The name reflects a blended linguistic structure derived from the three progenitor humanoid races, without belonging to any single tradition.

It is generally understood to denote those aligned with order, structure, and the maintenance of the Weave.

Addition — Order Sharanel founding context:

The Order Sharanel was founded during the height of the Demon War, at a time when the world faced catastrophic instability and mass loss of life.

This period represented the greatest crisis in the history of the Mythos following the Worldbreaker event, as demonic forces consumed both life and Eldritch energy at scale.

Addition — creation of the Weave:

In response to the uncontrolled consumption of Eldritch energy by demonic forces, Lirra, goddess of magic, initiated the creation of the Weave.

The Weave gathered all free Eldritch energy, previously distributed through natural flows, into a structured and regulated system.

This act fundamentally altered the nature of magic, replacing an unstable, natural distribution with a controlled network.

Addition — first sorcerers:

Simultaneously with the creation of the Weave, Lirra performed a large-scale act of transmutation affecting approximately 10,000 mortals.

These individuals were transformed into sorcerers, becoming the first members of what would later be known as the Order Sharanel.

The group included:

- trained practitioners such as wizards
- some priests
- a majority of ordinary individuals with no prior magical training

This transformation represents one of the most significant acts of magic in the history of the Mythos.

Addition — sorcerers as living elements:

The sorcerers created during this event became integral components of the Weave itself.

Each sorcerer’s continued existence contributes to the stability and strength of the Weave.

This establishes a direct relationship between living beings and the functioning of the global magical system.

Addition — racial composition:

No members of the three progenitor humanoid races were included among the initial 10,000 individuals transformed into sorcerers.

The founding membership of the Order Sharanel was drawn entirely from other populations present at the time.

Summary rule — Order Sharanel:

The Order Sharanel was formed during the Demon War through Lirra’s simultaneous creation of the Weave and transmutation of 10,000 mortals into sorcerers, who became living components sustaining the global magical system.

Addition — weave doctrine (public understanding):

The Weave is understood by most cultures as a stable, self-sustaining structure created by Lirra to regulate Eldritch energy.

Under this view, the Weave operates independently of individual lives, and fluctuations in magical strength are attributed to environmental factors, Source activity, or historical disruption.

The role of individual sorcerers is seen as interaction with the Weave rather than participation in its structure.

Addition — weave hidden mechanism:

The true nature of the Weave is not widely known.

Sorcerers function as distributed living anchors within the Weave, and their continued existence contributes directly to its stability and capacity.

This relationship is not publicly taught and is known only to a limited number of individuals or groups.

As a result, the connection between sorcerer mortality and Weave integrity is generally unrecognized.


Addition — weave degradation:

Large-scale loss of sorcerers results in measurable degradation of the Weave.

When a sufficient proportion of anchors is removed, the Weave’s ability to carry high-order magical processes is reduced.

Effects include:

- failure of high-level spellcasting
- collapse of complex or sustained magical effects
- destabilization or destruction of powerful magical artifacts

Refinement — sorcerer crusade impact:

The Sorcerer Crusade resulted in the deliberate or incidental killing of a large number of sorcerers.

This caused a severe weakening of the Weave, reducing its capacity to sustain higher-order magic.

The resulting period, known as the Thousand-Year Dimming, was characterized by the inability to cast or maintain high-level spells, as well as widespread loss of advanced magical infrastructure.

Addition — weave recovery:

The Weave recovers over time as the number of sorcerers increases and stability is gradually restored.

This process is slow and dependent on the emergence or creation of new sorcerers.

Full restoration of high-order magical capacity requires a sufficient density of living anchors to support complex magical structures.

Addition — knowledge control:

Knowledge of the relationship between sorcerers and the Weave is restricted or suppressed.

In some traditions, it is actively concealed to prevent targeted harm against sorcerers.

In others, it is simply not understood, as the visible effects of Weave degradation do not clearly indicate their cause.

Summary rule — sorcerers and the Weave:

Sorcerers serve as living anchors within the Weave, and large-scale loss of sorcerers reduces the Weave’s capacity to sustain high-order magic, though this relationship is not widely known.

Addition — Black Hand orchestration of the Sorcerer Crusade:

The Sorcerer Crusade was covertly instigated by the Black Hand as a strategic diversion.

As a Grand Conjunction approached, the Black Hand required reduced scrutiny to conduct a coordinated search for the Spear of Crystallized Entropy. To achieve this, they redirected institutional and popular attention toward sorcerers as a perceived internal threat.

Addition — doctrinal framing of sorcerers:

The Black Hand promoted a doctrinal argument within certain churches: because sorcerers were created during the Demon War, they must be demonic or demon-influenced.

This framing did not rely on established theology but on suggestive association and fear. It was adopted most strongly by militant religious orders predisposed toward decisive action.

Addition — mobilization of martial orders:

Martial orders within affected churches acted as primary enforcers of the anti-sorcerer doctrine.

Through sermons, edicts, and direct action, they legitimized hostility toward sorcerers and provided organizational structure for persecution.

Their authority transformed suspicion into sanctioned action, enabling rapid escalation.

Addition — popular uprising dynamics:

Once legitimized by religious authority, hostility toward sorcerers spread into the general population.

Communities began targeting individuals identified as “evil magic users,” leading to widespread violence, denunciation, and purges.

This phase was characterized by decentralized action driven by fear, rumor, and moral certainty rather than coordinated command.

Refinement — hidden system impact:

The large-scale killing of sorcerers during the Crusade had unintended systemic consequences.

Because sorcerers function as living anchors of the Weave, their loss reduced the Weave’s capacity, leading to failure of high-order magic and the onset of the Thousand-Year Dimming.

This relationship was not understood by the participants in the Crusade and was not the primary objective of the Black Hand.

Addition — plausible deniability:

The Black Hand maintained plausible deniability throughout the Crusade.

By acting through existing religious institutions and social tensions, they avoided direct attribution. The resulting events are widely recorded as religious conflict rather than coordinated manipulation.

Summary rule — Sorcerer Crusade causation:

The Sorcerer Crusade was a Black Hand–engineered diversion that leveraged religious doctrine and popular fear to target sorcerers, enabling covert operations during a Grand Conjunction while inadvertently weakening the Weave.


Refinement — divine bureaucracy:

Divine power within Eadras is distributed through a structured bureaucratic system.

This system processes the vast volume of mortal prayers, rituals, and invocations, allowing gods to delegate routine interaction and avoid constant direct attention to mortal affairs.

As a result, gods are not continuously aware of all actions taken in their name, particularly when those actions are framed within accepted doctrine or institutional authority.

Addition — oversight gap during Sorcerer Crusade:

During the Sorcerer Crusade, the churches whose martial orders participated operated within the bounds of their interpreted doctrine.

Because divine interaction is mediated through bureaucratic structures, the gods associated with these churches were not immediately aware of the scale or nature of the actions being taken.

This allowed the Crusade to escalate without direct divine intervention in its early stages.

Addition — weave disruption trigger:

As the killing of sorcerers intensified, the Weave began to degrade due to loss of its living anchors.

Lirra, as the deity directly responsible for the Weave, detected this disruption and recognized it as a systemic threat rather than a localized conflict.

This condition exceeded the threshold for passive oversight and triggered direct communication to the other gods.

Addition — divine intervention during Crusade:

Upon being informed by Lirra, the gods associated with the participating churches became aware of the actions carried out in their names.

In response, they intervened directly by manifesting avatars within the affected institutions.

These manifestations occurred publicly, including appearances before high priests and leaders of martial orders, and were used to immediately halt the persecution of sorcerers.

The intervention was decisive and left no ambiguity regarding divine disapproval.

Addition — doctrinal consequence:

The direct intervention of the gods during the Sorcerer Crusade resulted in significant doctrinal correction within affected churches.

Actions taken during the Crusade were reinterpreted as misapplications or distortions of divine will rather than valid expressions of it.

This event reinforced the principle that institutional authority does not guarantee alignment with divine intent.

Refinement — doctrine of unheld hand application:

The divine response to the Sorcerer Crusade represents an exception to the general principle of noninterference.

While gods typically refrain from direct involvement in mortal affairs, large-scale disruption of the Weave constitutes a systemic threat to the stability of the Mythos.

Such conditions justify direct intervention, particularly when caused by actions carried out in the gods’ own names.

Summary rule — divine intervention in Crusade:

The Sorcerer Crusade escalated due to delayed divine awareness within a bureaucratic system, but was ultimately halted when Lirra detected Weave degradation and alerted the gods, prompting direct avatar intervention to stop the persecution.
