Creation of the Eadras Mythos (Creation Myth/story)

The Omniversal Creator  created the shell of the Eadras  Mythos , encapsulating void and potential within. Then He created Evye  and Fryr  to create and govern however they chose. Evye and Fryr created the 10 Elder Gods, and turned their entire beings to creating the Eadras Mythos, giving all of their power and even their essence to complete this great work.

In the beginning, there was naught but chaos—a seething sea of possibility without structure or meaning. But lo, did the Great Creator, in his boundless wisdom and mercy, correct this injustice: he forged a bubble of rational void, cleaving Chaos from the not-chaos. This place he named Eadras, a Mythos. Yet the Mythos was but boundless emptiness—devoid of light, love, laughter, and all that makes life worth living.

So the Great Creator shaped his two children: Evye and Fryr, feminine and masculine, to birth everything else.

Evye looked across the endless black, voice like dawn breaking.

"Brother, we need land first—solid ground, seas to cradle it, skies to roof it all. Something they can walk on, breathe in."

Fryr turned, hands already flexing like they held hammers.

"And the unseen. Realms of spirit—quiet places where minds can wander, wonder grows, magic sparks. Without that, the land's just dirt. They need souls to dream it alive."

She nodded slow.

"Then we weave them together. Earth underfoot, spirit overhead—like lungs and heart. But..."

He finished it, quieter.

"But if we pour everything—our light, our iron—into this... we'll burn out. No one left to tend it. We need help. Someone to carry on. Not copies—children. Strong enough to guide what we start."

Evye's eyes met his.

"Ten. Our first act. They'll steer the seas, tune the winds, guard the quiet rooms. And when we're gone..."

Fryr laughed—dry, almost sad.

"Then we did it right. They'll feel us in every tide, every dream. Not as gods on thrones—as the wind that moves them. Ruling's for mortals who fear dying. We're not afraid."

He paused, softer.

"Are you afraid, Evye?"

She didn't answer right away. The dark pressed in.

"Afraid?" she echoed, voice cracking just once. "Of fading? Yes. Of being nothing? Yes. But more... of leaving them without a hand to hold. Still—yes. Let's do it."

Fryr's jaw set.

"Then no half-measures. We empty. All of it."

The dark waited. They turned to call the elders.

Joining together, Evye and Fryr birthed ten Elder Gods into the void. They emerged—not summoned, not forged—like the first breath after silence, rising from the dark's own quiet heart. Forms took shape: vast, luminous, edges still trembling with what their parents had poured.

They stepped forward—ten, then eleven. The tenth had split in the moment of coming into being: one half gentle, offering rest to the dead; the other stern, demanding endless reckoning. Death, born torn.

The ten Elder Gods rose from the void, one by one claiming their names like they were always meant to be.

Ioa stepped up first: "I am Ioa—god of rulership."

Nara next, soft: "I am Nara—goddess of femininity."

Raja, firm: "I am Raja—god of masculinity."

Voss: "I am Voss—god of civilization."

Qoxli: "I am Qoxli—goddess of the wild."

Daj: "I am Daj—god of war."

Nox: "I am Nox—god of night."

Sylph, grinning: "I am Sylph—goddess of foolishness."

Lirra: "I am Lirra—goddess of magic."

Shali stepped up, chin high:

"I am Shali—goddess of death."

Shani shoved in right after:

"No, you're not. I'm Shani—goddess of death."

Shali: "See? I'm Shali, goddess of death, and she's a mistake."

Shani: "No, I'm not!"

Shali: "Yes, you are!"

Shani: "No, I'm not—"

Ioa's voice cut through, low and final:

"Be quiet, both of you."

The twins froze, glaring sideways. The void went still.

Evye watched, light steady:

"You know yourselves. Good."

Fryr nodded:

"Now we begin creating the mythos. We'll need your help. Each of you—your specialty. Guide the power we push out. Make it well. Make it real."

Then they turned to the void and began their great task.

No one can say it really say how long it took because time wasn't invented until the end of the creation of the mythos, and, but it took either the blink of an eye or an eon.

Evye and Fryr had utterly spent themselves into the mythos, giving everything—light, iron, breath, will—until nothing remained. No trace. No echo. They weren't gone; they became it. The ground, the wind, the quiet between stars.

The ten Elder Gods took what poured out. Ioa set the laws. Nara warmed the homes. Raja forged the tools. Qoxli ran the forests. Daj sharpened the blades. Nox hid the secrets. Sylph laughed at the rules. Lirra wove the magic. And Shali and Shani—still bickering—split the first soul, one to light, one to dark.

They shaped it all. Not perfect. Not finished. Just... alive. A place where things grow, break, love, fight, die, and start again. This is how the mythos came to be.
