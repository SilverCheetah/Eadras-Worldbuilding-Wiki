**Omniversal Laws**

Installed by the Omniversal Creator—universal, unbreakable, apply to every mythos.

1.  **Truth is truth, indivisible, interlocking, unquestionable.**\
    *What it really means*: Truth isn't opinion—it's bedrock. Every fact links to every other fact like gears in a machine. Lie once, the whole system grinds. Mortals twist words, gods hide truths, but the law doesn't care. If something *is*, it stays true forever. No "alternative facts"—just what happened, what is, what will be. Break it? Reality starts unraveling, one contradiction at a time.
2.  **Time flows in one direction only—it is sacred and finite. None may bend, hasten, or stall its passage.**\
    *What it really means*: Time's arrow points forward. No rewind, no fast-forward, no loops. You can't cheat death by jumping ahead, can't undo a murder by going back. Gods might slow a moment for a whisper, but even they can't stop the clock. It's why prophecies feel heavy—once said, the future's locked. Mortals age, worlds crumble, stories end. Time's not cruel; it's honest. It gives you one shot.
3.  **Magic must be ruled by a mind. Without regulation, anything can happen.**\
    *What it really means*: Magic isn't some free-floating energy—it's only safe when a thinking mind, a sapient being, is in the driver's seat. If a wizard, a god, or even a clever beast casts it, that's "ruled" magic: intentional, contained, predictable. But if no one—god or creator—is holding the reins? Then it's wild. In some mythoi, whole zones go haywire: spells twist, portals pop open, reality glitches like bad code. In others, the entire world runs on uncontrolled magic—random surges, curses that backfire, miracles that kill. Outside mythoi, like in pure chaos realms? That's the ultimate wild: no mind, no rules, just raw, endless "anything." The law's warning: let magic run free, and you're rolling dice with existence itself.
4.  **Justice must be swift and complete—when the scales tip, they seek balance, though the greater the imbalance, the longer it takes to right itself.**\
    *What it really means*: The cosmos doesn't ignore wrongs—it just doesn't rush. A petty lie? Balance corrects fast: bad luck, a slip-up, gone in days. But a genocide, a broken oath that shatters worlds? That takes time—weeks, years, even generations—because the scales are heavy. Shally and Shani still ferry souls, but for epic crimes, the reckoning builds slow: empires rot from inside, curses linger like smoke. It's not mercy; it's physics. The law says: tip too far, and gravity drags you down—eventually. No one escapes, but no one gets a lightning bolt every time. And if the tip's extreme? The Three Loom Sisters step in—quietly snipping threads, weaving back to center, no fanfare.
5.  **Death is sovereign in the end—death eventually claims everything.**\
    *What it really means*: Death wins. Always. Mortals fade fast. Gods linger. Mythoi crumble slower. But nothing escapes the final curtain. Immortality? Just a delay—buying time until time itself dies. The Deathbringer can hurry it along if needed, but even without it, the end comes. Not necessarily soon. Could be eons. Could be now. But it comes. The law's not a threat; it's a fact. Live like it matters, because one day... it won't.
6.  **Destruction is the natural end of all things—it crushes the weak fast and grinds the strong slow, but everything breaks eventually.**\
    *What it really means*: The mythos promises erosion. Every sword dulls, every empire cracks, every spell fades. Weak things—like a flimsy alliance or a cheap blade—snap quick. Strong things—like a hero's will or Damascus steel forged in endless layers—get hammered harder, tested longer, until the cracks finally show. No one's unbreakable. Pressure builds, metal flakes, rust creeps. Even immortals erode—time just stretches the grind. Destruction isn't hate; it's the slow polish that turns junk into dust. The law says: hold on as long as you can, but the end's coming. We don't know when. Just... when.
