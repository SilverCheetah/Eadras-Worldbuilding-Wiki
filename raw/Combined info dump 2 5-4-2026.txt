Refinement — chaos definition:

Chaos is not disorder or contradiction, but the absence of reality.

It is an unreality in which the structures that define existence—time, space, identity, and causality—do not exist in a stable or meaningful form.

Chaos predates the Omniversal Creator and the formation of all Mythoi. Both chaos and the Omniversal Creator are understood to have always existed.

Chaos exists between all Mythoi and surrounds them. Each Mythos is a bounded region of imposed structure within this unreality.

When chaos intrudes into a Mythos, it does not replace reality but undermines the conditions that allow reality to exist, producing instability and incoherence.

Refinement — aberration origin:

Aberrations arise from the interaction between reality and residual chaos.

They are not beings of pure chaos, but the result of matter or life exposed to conditions in which stable structure cannot be maintained.

As a result, aberrations exhibit inconsistent, alien, or structurally incorrect properties relative to the normal rules of the Mythos.

Residual chaos pockets created during the First Chaos Crusade serve as ongoing sources for such entities.
