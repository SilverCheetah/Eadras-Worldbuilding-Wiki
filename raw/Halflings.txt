Halflings
**Basic Information** Halflings are small, cheerful folk with two opposing sides to their nature. One side loves the comfort of home and hearth, while the other is happy and restless on the road. They are easygoing, sociable, and well-liked by other races.

**Anatomy** Small of stature with a friendly, rounded appearance. Their short legs mean they have a small stride length, so when traveling with taller races they usually ride a horse, beast, or wagon to keep up with the group.

**Ecology and Habitats** Halflings do not have a single homeland. They live in small villages or towns, never in large concentrations. Their settlements adapt to wherever they are granted guest rights — resembling cozy hobbit-holes in human lands or built into the roots of giant trees beneath elven cities.

**Society** Halfling society is built around a recurring cycle called **“The Change”**. Every halfling periodically feels a growing restlessness that builds over roughly six months. When it becomes too strong, they must leave their current life, shut everything down, and switch to the other side — either heading out to travel or returning home.

Halfling sense of property is semi-communal. When an adult feels The Change coming on, they give their property to the community to redistribute before they leave. Shops, homes, and belongings are then offered to the next halfling who arrives in the village. A newcomer is likely to be offered suitable work and an empty home (“hole”) that fits their needs.

*   **Homebound halflings** focus on farming and traditional village life.
*   **Traveling halflings** join caravans or work as crew on other races’ ships. They are happy on the road and very good at traveling. They can pack up a camp faster than most races and have a great deal of experience living on the move. Their wagons look ordinary from the outside but contain two full floors of living space inside.

Pure halfling caravans are rare. Because they are not strong warriors, most prefer to travel with mixed-race merchant trains for protection.

The family is the most stable unit in halfling society. Parents do not feel The Change while raising children. Once the children are grown, the whole family often experiences The Change around the same time.

Halflings nearly always give birth to twins, which helps keep their population stable despite so many adults constantly moving between home and travel.

**Trades & Relations** Halflings are respected farmers and courteous hunters. Other races gladly grant them guest rights because their villages provide reliable agricultural goods in exchange for manufactured items. They are known for being reasonable, easy to get along with, and excellent cooks — any caravan lucky enough to have a halfling in the group eats very well.
