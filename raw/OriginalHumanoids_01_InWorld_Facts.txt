ORIGINAL HUMANOID CULTURES — IN-WORLD FACTS
World: Eadras
Source: Grok chat session, 2026-05-09
Document type: In-world reference (the three original Elder Humanoid races — Elfen, Tarran, Velrathi — their cultures, alcohols, customs, and signature practices)
Companion documents: "Original Humanoid Cultures — Meta-Commentary" and "Original Humanoid Cultures — Principles and Diagnosis"


================================================================
1. THE THREE ORIGINAL ELDER HUMANOID RACES
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Three created the three original Elder Humanoid races. Each race was created by one of the three primordial figures:

- ELFEN — Created by Mark.
- TARRAN — Created by Melania.
- VELRATHI — Created by Rand.

These three races are the foundational humanoid stock of Eadras. All later humanoid races descend from or were shaped by them. The Velrathi and Tarran are described as effectively siblings, with deep cultural and biological ties between them.


================================================================
2. ELFEN — OVERVIEW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elfen are the original race created by Mark. They are ancient, proud, and somewhat mystical.

There are no Elfen on modern Eadras. They have been banished to the Green Moon Gaia in the Feywild. References to "Elfen" in this document refer to the historical/banished race, not their modern descendants.

Elfen are organized into clans, each with its own variations on shared cultural practices.


================================================================
3. ELFEN BLOSSOM WINES
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The signature alcoholic drink of the Elfen is a family of fermented wines made from a combination of berries, edible flowers, and herbs. Every Elfen clan produces some variation, with closely guarded recipes, aging processes, and traditional names.

Characteristics of Elfen blossom wines:
- Light, fragrant, and complex.
- Floral notes dominate the flavor profile.
- A subtle herbal layer, with each clan adding slightly different herbs.
- Refreshing, delicious, and slightly intoxicating.
- The finest versions are aged for decades.

Variations:
- STANDARD — fermented, strained, refined, aged.
- DISTILLED — refined further into a stronger spirit.
- MAGICAL — high-magic clans infuse batches with mana-rich berries grown in powerful ley line zones, giving the wine a subtle magical resonance.

The Elfen magically remove the alcohol from versions intended for children using nature magic.

Some known clan variations include: Starblossom Wine, Lunapetal Wine, Aetherbloom, Dawnpetal Reserve, Celestine Gold.

Offering a bottle of another clan's blossom wine is considered both a gesture of respect and a subtle status display.


================================================================
4. ELFEN AQUA VITAE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Elfen aqua vitae is a base ingredient used in most potion-making. Elfen aqua vitae is light and subtly bubbly, with a delicate floral-herbal character. It is especially prized for potions involving:

- Nature
- Growth
- Healing
- Life magic

The natural character of Elfen aqua vitae enhances and harmonizes with natural forces in potions, making it the preferred base for any potion drawing on those domains.


================================================================
5. TARRAN — OVERVIEW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Tarran (sometimes Terran) are the original race created by Melania. They assumed a protector role for the other two races early in their history.

Because their protector role exposed them to dangerous and potentially poisonous animals, plants, and substances, they developed a deliberate cultural response over generations: building cumulative immunity to poisons and toxins.


================================================================
6. TARRAN POISON IMMUNITY
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Tarran are famous for their near-total immunity to poisons and toxins.

This resistance was originally developed over approximately TEN THOUSAND YEARS through a deliberate cultural ritual: starting in early childhood, Tarran parents gave their children gradually increasing doses of various poisons. The purpose was both to build resistance and to toughen the children.

Over thousands of years, this practice became encoded in the Tarran bloodline as an inherited genetic trait. By the Imperial era, Tarran were born with the immunity.

THE RITUAL IS STILL PERFORMED. Even though the trait is now genetic, the ritual continues as an important cultural rite of passage. Most Tarran do not realize the trait has become inherited — they simply continue the practice as a routine part of childhood.

There remain EXTREMELY RARE INDIVIDUALS who fail to develop full immunity. These exceptions are uncommon and viewed with suspicion or pity. One such individual is canonical: the princess who poisoned the emperor used her own lack of immunity in some part of the plot.


================================================================
7. TARRAN VENOM-AGED LIQUORS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Tarran poison immunity has strongly shaped their drinking culture.

Tarran traditionally distill liquors from wine, then add carefully selected ANIMAL VENOMS AND TOXINS during the aging process. While the venom largely denatures over the aging period, it leaves behind a distinct and complex flavor that Tarran prize.

CLASS DISTINCTIONS BY THE IMPERIAL PERIOD:
- Liquors aged for only SIX MONTHS were considered common swill, fit only for commoners. (Six months is the minimum aging time.)
- Nobles and the upper class would not drink anything aged for LESS THAN TWENTY YEARS.
- The finest, most expensive venom-aged liquors are aged FIFTY YEARS OR MORE.

These drinks are completely safe for Tarran. They are MILDLY TO MODERATELY DANGEROUS for other races, often causing sickness or hallucinations in those without Tarran immunity.

Because of this, venom-aged liquor is considered a distinctly Tarran drink and is sometimes used as a subtle TEST of whether someone is truly Tarran.


================================================================
8. TARRAN PET CULTURE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Tarran have a strong cultural appreciation for venomous and poisonous animals. The more dangerous the creature, the higher its status as a pet.

However, the Tarran themselves lack the magical expertise to create venomous animals. They commission the Velrathi to do this magical work. Over the centuries, the Velrathi have created several breeds of magically-engineered venomous creatures specifically for Tarran use.

Notable Tarran venomous pets:

- VENOMFANG WARHOUNDS — Massive, powerful war dogs based on traditional Tarran burly warhound stock. Their saliva produces a potent neurotoxin. Fiercely loyal, incredibly strong, lethal to non-Tarran. The favored pet of military officers and nobility.

- BLOOD PARROTS — Vibrant crimson parrots with deadly toxin in their beaks and claws. Known for high intelligence and aggressive personalities. Favored by nobles and mages.

- IRONSCALE MONITORS — Large, heavily armored monitor lizards that deliver a powerful hemotoxin through their claws.

The Tarran are pragmatic about their venomous animal program. They have rejected proposals for creatures that cannot be controlled — for example, venomous fleas were proposed but rejected because they could spread uncontrollably and harm the Tarran population.


================================================================
9. VELRATHI — OVERVIEW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Velrathi are the original race created by Rand. They are ancient, scholarly, deeply ritualistic, and obsessed with knowledge and intellectual perfection.

The Velrathi are the scholarly and ruling class of the original three races. They are the ones who:
- Discovered tea.
- Created the Gruug (their engineered labor race).
- Provide magical services (such as venomous-pet engineering) to the Tarran.
- Maintain the strictest tradition of intellectual contribution to society.

The Velrathi do not consume alcohol.


================================================================
10. VELRATHI REJECTION OF ALCOHOL
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Velrathi do not drink alcohol. They believe it clouds the mind and impairs rational thought, which is intolerable to a culture organized around intellectual rigor.

They turned instead to tea after discovering that carefully rationed consumption of specific teas during periods of study and learning produces a measurable improvement in mental acuity.


================================================================
11. THE DISCOVERY OF TEA
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Velrathi were the first to discover tea — even before the Elfen.

Tea was discovered through a typical Velrathi experiment. They had no boundaries on their experimentation, and at some point a Velrathi scholar asked: "What happens if I dry these leaves and steep them in hot water?"

That single experiment created the first cup of tea. It was a Velrathi experiment, not an Elfen tradition.


================================================================
12. THE ELFEN TEA COMMISSION
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] After discovering tea's ability to enhance mental acuity during study, the Velrathi commissioned the Elfen to create new varieties. The Velrathi expected perhaps ten or fifteen varieties at most.

The Elfen fell deeply in love with the project. For centuries, they returned every decade with 12 to 15 BRAND NEW, INTERESTING, DELICIOUS, AND DISTINCTLY DIFFERENT tea varieties — each genuinely new and different from those that came before.

Eventually the Velrathi were forced to firmly tell the Elfen: "This is getting ridiculous. Thank you — but that is quite enough."

By this point, roughly 500 distinct varieties of tea existed.


================================================================
13. WHAT MAKES THESE TEAS UNIQUE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] These are NOT flavored teas. They are not blends or chai mixes with added ingredients.

The Elfen magically engineered the tea leaves themselves so that when steeped, a single type of leaf naturally produces complex flavor profiles that would normally require blending multiple ingredients.

Some varieties taste like spiced chai using only the tea leaf alone. Others taste naturally smoked, fermented, floral, fruity, or earthy — all emerging from the leaf itself when bloomed in hot water.

KNOWN VARIETY CATEGORIES AND EXAMPLES:

FLORAL / FRUITY:
- Moonpetal Bloom
- Starblossom
- Dawnberry
- Lunefruit
- Silverpetal
- Nightrose

FERMENTED / SOUR:
- Bitterthorn
- Sourvein
- Acidroot
- Tangbloom

SMOKED / EARTHY:
- Sagesmoke
- Ashpetal
- Emberroot

GNOMISH TEAS — Spiced-style teas that taste like Gnomish spices. The category is named "Gnomish" rather than "spiced" because conventional spice descriptions feel too Earth-like. Examples include Emberspice, Flamepetal, and similar warming/fire-themed names.

Other named varieties: Dawn Ember, Starveil, Memory Bloom, Silverroot, Lunareth, Thoughtpetal, Mindweft, Everbloom, Voidpetal, Clarity Thorn, Echo Blossom.


================================================================
14. VELRATHI TEA CULTURE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Tea plays three major roles in Velrathi society:

1. THE TEA CEREMONY — A formal and highly disciplined ritual, particularly when a SUPERIOR visits an INFERIOR. Selecting the correct tea for the specific context is considered a refined intellectual art. Choice of tea encodes social relationship, occasion, and intention.

2. PERSONAL PREFERENCE — Within the home, tea choice is deeply personal. Many Velrathi spend decades sampling different varieties to discover their single favorite tea for long-term cognitive enhancement.

3. MEDITATIVE PRACTICE — Tea is used as a tool for deep introspection and focus, helping the drinker enter their own memory palace.

The Velrathi view tea preparation and consumption as completely rational, in contrast to food, which they view purely as fuel. Tea provides a tangible benefit to mental performance, so the significant time and care invested in its study is considered intellectually justified.


================================================================
15. THE ELFEN TEA CULTURE — DIFFERENT FROM THE VELRATHI
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elfen, having fallen in love with tea while creating it, developed their own tea culture — but a very different one from the Velrathi.

Elfen tea culture is:
- More casual.
- More personal.
- More expressive.

The Elfen drink tea for enjoyment and for personal expression, not as a discipline or formal ritual. Their tea customs lack the rigid hierarchical formality of the Velrathi ceremony.


================================================================
16. VELRATHI COGNITIVE FROGS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Among the Velrathi upper class — particularly scholars, nobles, and mages — it is common to carry small, brilliantly multicolored frogs that resemble poison dart frogs but display vibrant, multi-colored patterns rather than a single hue.

These frogs secrete a potent toxin on their backs. To most other races, this toxin is HIGHLY POISONOUS — even lethal.

The Velrathi have developed a limited immunity to the deadly effects of the toxin through generations of controlled exposure. Rather than distilling or processing the toxin, many Velrathi simply LICK THE BACKS OF THE FROGS DIRECTLY to receive a dose that enhances mental acuity and focus.

The frogs are commonly carried in ELEGANT SMALL GLASS CONTAINERS, often worn on the belt or kept in a pocket. This allows the owner to lick them whenever they need a boost in concentration.

To outsiders, the sight of a dignified Velrathi casually licking the back of a frog is DEEPLY UNNERVING. This practice has contributed significantly to the Velrathi's unsettling reputation among other races.


================================================================
17. VELRATHI BATHING CULTURE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Velrathi developed sophisticated communal bathing practices early in their history. When they first started constructing buildings to bathe in, they discovered that communal bathing SPREAD SICKNESS.

Their response was characteristically thorough. They instituted:

- Magical protections and cleanliness measures on all water entering and present in the building, killing any sickness in the water.

- PROPER HYGIENE STATIONS — essentially shower cubicles for everyone before entry to communal bathing areas. A Velrathi steps into the shower cubicle, is hit with water and a soap-like substance, is auto-scrubbed, dried, and then permitted to enter the communal bathing area.

The communal bathing area is then a place to SOAK and SOCIALIZE for about an hour. The Velrathi view communal bathing as a pleasant opportunity for social and intellectual exchange.


================================================================
18. RATHARI BATHING — INHERITED FROM VELRATHI
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Rathari inherited their bathing culture from the Velrathi. They maintain the underground bathhouse infrastructure, the auto-scrub stations, and the daily bathing expectation.

However, the Rathari have shifted the emphasis. Most Rathari today are MORE ABOUT THE AUTO-SCRUB than the communal soak. They use the bathhouse efficiently as a hygiene tool and return quickly to their studies.

Some Rathari are so absorbed in their work that they forget to bathe at all for extended periods — sometimes weeks. Their relatives typically have to remind them. A family member appearing at the archive saying "you smell like a tomb, bath now" is a recognized social ritual.

The more disciplined Rathari quietly judge the ones who must be reminded.


================================================================
19. PHILOSOPHY OF CONTRIBUTION — VELRATHI VERSION
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Velrathi share a core cultural belief with the Gruug and Goblins (their descendants and engineered race): every capable individual must contribute to society. Freeloading is not tolerated.

The Velrathi version is the LEAST EXTREME of the three:

- They expect every citizen to contribute to society in some meaningful way: intellectually, artistically, or through skilled labor.
- They do NOT require physical labor from everyone.
- They strongly reject anyone who contributes nothing.

The Velrathi are pragmatic, not extremist. Sensible exceptions apply: those who are injured, ill, elderly, or disabled are not expected to contribute beyond their ability. In such cases, the community finds other ways for them to participate or supports them. The general rule: if you are capable of contributing and choose not to, there will be consequences.

(Compare with Gruug and Goblin versions in the "Descendant Cultures" companion document.)
