# The Anti-Magic Crusade

**Summary**: A violent persecution of **all magic users** that occurred **roughly 9,000–10,000 years ago**, in the decades immediately preceding the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]. It was **covertly engineered by the [[black-hand|Black Hand]]** as a strategic diversion to enable their search for the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]. Over the preceding century the Black Hand orchestrated the “Century of Sorrows” — hundreds of major magical disasters and thousands of smaller ones — to turn the world against all magic. The Crusade catastrophically damaged the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] (every magic user’s life sustains it), but this damage was largely unintended. It was halted by what mortals perceived as divine intervention when the gods inhabited their Icons. Three [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] were killed, triggering the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]].

## Overview

Roughly 9,000–10,000 years ago, a violent persecution of **all magic users** erupted across much of Eadras under the doctrine that **all magic is wicked**. The Black Hand spent the preceding century engineering ever-worsening magical disasters (the Century of Sorrows) to create the conditions for the Crusade. The persecution swept up Netharim, wizards, druids, priests, Lexanari, psionic practitioners, hedge-witches, and more before it was halted.

## The Engineered Catastrophes (The Century of Sorrows)

The Black Hand’s campaign was a deliberate, long-term operation that slowly ramped up over ~100 years, reaching a crescendo in the final 15–20 years. There were **hundreds of major incidents** and **thousands of smaller ones**, each intended to provoke horror, revulsion, and ultimately anger at magic itself.

**Representative major disasters** (crescendo period):
- A magical poison cloud over the city of Vaelis killed 18,000 in a single night.
- A plague of hungry undead ravaged the trading town of Dun Lira.
- A chaos pocket ruptured at the caravan village of Threeforks, dissolving reality before hundreds of witnesses.
- Rivers transformed into liquid shadow; fields grew screaming flesh-crops; wild magic surges warped entire districts.

Once the Crusade became self-sustaining, the Black Hand largely stopped manufacturing new disasters and focused on directing the resulting violence.

## The Core Crusader Claim: "All Magic Is Wicked"

> **“All magic is wicked. Recent events have proven it beyond doubt.”**

## The Doctrine of the Cold Hearth

**Public name**: The Doctrine of the Cold Hearth  
**Critics’ name**: “Wickedness to Combat Wickedness”

Core tenets:
- All magic is inherently corrupt and dangerous.
- The Century of Sorrows proved that any magic inevitably leads to horror.
- No exceptions — every magic user must be purified, bound, exiled, or slain.
- A cold hearth is safest.

## Magic-User Slurs and Reclaimed Names

- Demon-seeds, Hearth-killers, Breach-born, Spellblights, Shadow-hands, The Bringing-Down, etc.
- Some have been reclaimed by modern magical orders.

## The Hidden Truth

Every living magic user sustains the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]]. Mass death weakens it. The death of a Windspeaker without a ready successor causes structural damage to a conduit junction.

## Black Hand Intent — Diversion, Not Sabotage

The Crusade was engineered purely as a distraction for the Spear search. Weave damage was an unintended consequence. The Outer Hand knew nothing. The Inner Hand only suspected a possible connection and could never prove causation due to chaos.

## Divine Intervention — How the Crusade Was Halted

The Crusade was stopped by what mortals perceived as direct divine intervention: the gods fully inhabited their **Icons**, making them appear and act as living deities. 

#### Punishment and Reparations

- Participating priesthoods and martial orders were stripped of divine power for 100 years (the Silent Century).
- Each order donated one-third of its membership to form the **Knights of Lirra**, a new order dedicated to protecting magic users as bodyguards, companions, and a unified martial force. They draw power from their original gods plus Lirra.

## A Political Revolution Against Tower Rule

The Crusade also served as a political revolution against Windspeaker tower rule. Long-simmering resentments were weaponized, leading to the Fall of the Spirelords and the Shattering of the Tower Thrones.

## The Three Windspeaker Deaths

Each assassination required an enormous operation: 100 coerced magic users (wizards, priests, Netharim, Lexanari), dozens of assassins and guards, poisons, curses, and a massive frontal assault as distraction. The three slain Windspeakers are remembered as the **Three Cut Anchors**.

## The Thousand-Year Dimming

The three vacant anchors caused catastrophic damage. Six Conduit Towers were destroyed and magical potency across Eadras collapsed for a thousand years.

## The Ethical Dilemma

Secrecy did not fail. The Black Hand never *knew* the truth — only a very few suspected, and even they could not establish a positive causal link amid chaos. The uncertainty (“Was it the magic users? The Windspeakers? The general slaughter?”) continues to protect the Weave.

## Aftermath: The End of Open Windspeaker Rule

The three slain Windspeakers have **never been reborn**. Their anchors remain vacant. The damaged conduits have regenerated over the millennia but are **still not at full power** without living Windspeakers to regulate them. The world’s magical defenses remain permanently diminished at those three junctions.

The Crusade marked the permanent end of open Windspeaker political rule and left deep cultural, political, and metaphysical scars that still shape Eadras today.
