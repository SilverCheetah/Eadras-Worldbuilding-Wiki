Eadras Race Deepening Protocol (v1)
Purpose
This is a standardized, reusable framework for systematically expanding any race in the Eadras setting. It ensures depth, consistency with existing canon (creation history, god tiers, etc.), and integration across pages. Apply it section-by-section when developing or revising a race.

1. Origins & Creation

How was the race created or descended? (Directly by one of The Three? Engineered by another race? Natural evolution?)
What is the layered creation history (e.g., Rand → Velrathi → Orug → modern orcs)?
Key historical events that shaped the race (wars, cataclysms, banishments, etc.)?
How does the race itself understand and mythologize its own origins?

2. Biology & Appearance

Physical build, height range, skin tones, distinctive features (tusks, horns, etc.).
Lifespan and aging process.
Sensory or biological advantages/disadvantages.

3. Society & Social Structure

Major political or social divisions (e.g., Six Great Clans + 17 minor clans).
Leadership and succession systems (merit-based? hereditary?).
Key institutions (Great Gathering, cultural exchange program, clan-holds).
Urban vs. nomadic/rural splits and how they interact.

4. Spirituality & Religion

Primary tier of devotion (Mortal Gods / Ascended / God-born).
Secondary tier (Elderborn).
Distant acknowledgment (Elder Gods).
Major deities or patrons with domains, symbols, and how they are invoked.
Key rituals, festivals, and holy days (Night of the Gray Wall, etc.).
Daily practices, shrines, offerings, and omens.

5. Material Culture & Daily Life

Core economic resources and subsistence (the Five Jewels herds, etc.).
Signature crafts, trades, and hand-made goods.
Food & Drink (including cheeses, fermented products, brewing traditions).
Clothing, armor, and decorative arts.
Housing (gers, clan-holds, city neighborhoods).
Family life, child-rearing, and rites of passage.

Orc-specific example (new detail):
Orc cheese exists in two main forms:

Fermented mare’s milk cheese (light, tangy, produced in saddle-bags during long marches).
Blended yak and sheep milk cheese (richer, firmer, made in clan-holds for storage and trade).

6. Economy & Technology

Primary occupations (herding, craftsmanship, mercenary work, trade).
Trade goods and reputation in the wider world.
Level of magitek or traditional technology.
How wealth and status are measured.

7. Relations with Outsiders

How other races/peoples refer to them (neutral terms, respectful titles, derogatory slurs).
Common stereotypes and misconceptions.
Diplomatic, trade, or military relationships.
Cultural exchange programs or integration patterns (city orcs).

Orc-specific example derogatory terms: Graybacks, Tuskers, Ash-Eaters, Dirt-Bloods, Brute-Kin, etc.
8. Language, Naming & Communication

Naming conventions and cognomens.
Any unique linguistic features or dialects.
How they communicate with other races.

9. Subgroups & Variations

Major internal divisions (plains vs. city orcs, the Six Great Clans, etc.).
Regional or cultural variations.

10. Open Canon & Future Hooks

What remains deliberately undefined or flexible?
Plot hooks or unresolved tensions that could drive stories.
