Refinement — Arathi language:

The Arathi language is logographic and tonal, resembling certain structural aspects of human languages such as Chinese, but with significant differences.

Written Arathi consists of complex symbols that encode meaning directly rather than representing phonetic components.

Spoken Arathi is constrained by the species’ reptilian physiology, resulting in a limited phoneme set characterized by reduced labial sounds and increased use of sibilant and fricative tones.

Tone plays a critical role in distinguishing meaning, and correct pronunciation requires precise control of pitch and resonance.

This combination of logographic writing and physiologically constrained tonal speech makes the language difficult for other species to reproduce or fully understand.

Addition — language and magical function:

Arathi written language is closely tied to their use of crystalmancy and magitek.

Symbols are not purely linguistic but also encode functional and resonant properties, allowing them to serve as control structures within crystal systems.

This dual role of language as both communication and operational logic contributes to the difficulty of reconstructing Arathi technology in the modern era.
