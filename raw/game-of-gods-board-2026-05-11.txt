Game of the Gods — board nature and subtle play, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture: Josh extended the Game of the Gods canon with details
about the board's nature and the way gods play it.)

== Canon ==

1. THE BOARD UPDATES IN REAL TIME.
   It continuously reflects the state of Eadras-below as that state
   changes. There is no separate "input" or "rendering" step; the board
   IS a live view of the world.

2. ALL DATA SHOWN IN PICTOGRAPHIC FORM.
   The display is visual -- symbols, shifting iconography, signs whose
   grammar is godly. The gods read it fluently.

3. MORTALS CANNOT COMPREHEND THE BOARD.
   A mortal scholar shown the board would see an unintelligible mass
   of moving signs. The pictographic form is what permits an immortal
   player to take in the whole board at once; the information density
   of Eadras-at-any-instant is beyond any mortal mind's capacity.
   Reduce it to anything a mortal could read and the Game's grain
   disappears.

4. GODS CAN MAKE SUBTLE MOVES.
   Using the Game's rules, a god can make moves whose immediate effect
   on the board is far too subtle for a mortal to register -- a nudge,
   an inflection, a small adjustment to one piece's standing in a
   system of relationships.

5. SUBTLE MOVES PRODUCE MAJOR RESULTS TURNS LATER.
   Consequences land turns after the originating move, often as major
   events whose causal chain has long since vanished from observable
   history. Divine plans operating across centuries is, in the Game,
   mechanical truth: a god setting up a delayed payoff fifty turns out
   is playing the rules AS DESIGNED, not bending them.

== Implications ==

- The causation question (established earlier in the session) is
  deepened: even if a mortal scholar accepted "Game-causes-Eadras",
  they could not trace any particular mortal event back to its
  originating divine move. The moves are too subtle and the chains
  too long. Causation, if it exists, is REAL but UNRECOVERABLE.
- Even if a god TOLD a mortal which Game move produced a particular
  outcome, the mortal could not verify it -- the move would have been
  one of many on its originating turn, and the chain to the present
  is opaque.
- Divine plans in Eadras are not theatrical announcements followed
  by visible miracles. They are quiet, layered, and almost invisibly
  long-term.
- A god who suddenly intervenes OPENLY is doing something OUTSIDE
  their usual play -- and (per the earlier-established canon) such
  interventions further disrupt the Game and are themselves regarded
  as disruptive even by the intervening god.

== Connection to today's other canon ==

The gods-hate-demons grudge (gods-hate-demons-2026-05-11.txt) becomes
sharper in light of this canon. The demons did not just disrupt
casual entertainment -- they disrupted plays that had been set up
across many turns, possibly across millennia. Setups that would have
landed beautifully became unrecoverable in days. From the gods'
internal frame, this is a far more catastrophic loss than mortals
appreciate.
