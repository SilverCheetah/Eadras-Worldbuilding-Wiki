
1. Arathi Civilization (Ancient History, ~10+ Million Years Ago)
The Arathi were the first true sapient civilization of Eadras. Almost everything known about them comes from ruined crystal cities, shattered Deep Vaults, broken magitek fragments, and the rare testimony of surviving Troll Servitors.
They were elegant insectoid-reptilian hybrids with iridescent scales, feathered crests, and glowing chest Focus Crystals. Their society pursued advancement and perfect knowledge — complete understanding of magic, matter, biology, and reality itself. They believed they were close to this pinnacle but had not yet reached it. This made them a patient, methodical people who built slowly and refined their works over centuries or millennia.
Ssar’veth was a hidden societal outlier. While the Arathi as a whole were willing to wait, he secretly believed the current era was already the pinnacle of success and sought a shortcut to make everything perfect now.
Technology & Magic
Masters of crystalmancy and magitek. Magical metals served as perfect conductors but were not capable of mana storage (crystals handled that). Their cities were breathtaking fusions of living crystal spires, golden metallic circuits, floating platforms, and symbiotic megaflora.
Servitors
Dozens of lineages existed.

Trolls were the hardy general-purpose constructs for deep mining, processing, vein detection, and excavation. They are the only surviving lineage.
Household Servitors were extremely common — often elegantly engineered from insectoid stock (mantis-like limbs, beetle carapaces, iridescent wings). They handled domestic duties, personal assistance, laboratory work, and fine craftsmanship.
All other lineages were organic or semi-organic and died out completely during the Cataclysm.

Deep Vaults
Secure ancient repositories.
Current Status: 12 known to exist. 5 opened (1 by Darkhoof, 3 by gnomes, 1 undersea). 7 remain sealed (6 underwater, 1 on land). 1 additional vault on the red moon.

2. Pre-Cataclysm Atmosphere & Fire Ecology
The pre-Cataclysm atmosphere contained ~35% oxygen. This made spontaneous combustion and rapid wildfire spread a constant, serious danger. The Arathi managed it through crystal-tech fire-suppression fields, humidity control, non-flammable construction, rapid-response Household Servitors, and Lirra’s stabilizing lattices. The Cataclysm burned off enormous amounts of oxygen; the atmosphere gradually normalized over the following millions of years to ~21–25% O₂.

3. The Seven Conduits
The Seven Conduits are vast, natural planetary-scale channels of Eldritch energy created by Evye and Fryr. They are placed roughly equidistantly across the globe (not aligned to the north-south axis). Lirra has governed and stabilized them since their creation — both before and after the Weave. The Arathi built around their surface termini but did not create them.

4. The Cataclysm & Birth of Tectonics (~10 Million Years Ago)
The Worldbreaker struck with planetary-scale Chaotic force. For the first year, continents moved at kilometers per year due to impact energy and runaway subduction. Motion then slowed to meters per year, then centimeters per year. The Ignic Continent rose only 1 million years ago.

5. Trolls (Only Surviving Servitors)
Trolls are sapient, self-repairing, self-replicating constructs of living stone and crystal. All other Servitors were organic/semi-organic and died out completely.
Population: Exactly 2,000 total (Arathi programming).

Active: 300–400
Hibernating: ~1,600

Hibernation Horror: Elders can grow to hill or small-mountain size. Settlements sometimes unknowingly build beside them. Awakening is a rare, terrifying event in which the mountain splits open, the elder emerges, reshapes into towering humanoid form, walks the land (sometimes into the ocean), and eventually returns to dormancy.
Trolls remain minor figures in world events — rare, ancient echoes of a lost age.

6. Varkhul, the Dark Continent (The Exile Lands)
Varkhul is the southwestern continent, a harsh, fractured land roughly the size of two major continents combined. It is dominated by the Scorched Waste (roughly one-quarter of its surface) and serves the rest of the world as a permanent, heavily guarded prison continent — the ultimate dumping ground for the dangerous, the irredeemable, and the unwanted.
The Continental Ward
Twenty-five miles offshore, encircling the entire continent, stands the Continental Ward — the strongest magical barrier on Eadras. It is the cumulative ward formed by the combined magical efforts of nearly every major nation and power on the planet. Hundreds of casters from across the world rotate in continuous shifts, feeding the barrier without pause.
Ward Features

Solid and unpassable physical/magical barrier.
Dozens of concentric layers alternating hard (brute resistance) and soft (absorption, redirection, illusion).
Constantly shifting frequencies to defeat any attempt at tuning or bypassing.
Reflective inward — any outward force or spell is reflected back toward Varkhul with amplified strength.
Designed to block every known form of magic.

The nations of Eadras maintain this Ward with grim determination. They really don’t want anything to get out.
Inhabitants

Dragonkin (Mendelian-bred descendants of dragons in humanoid form) provide natural territorial hostility.
Turkorcs — wholly individualistic, powerful warriors and casters. They have no concept of honor and consider it stupid. Amoral and extremely dangerous. Strictly contained on Varkhul.
Exiled clans of dwarves, goblins, orcs, and other outcasts.

The Obsidian Sanctum
An ancient Arathi Deep Vault located deep within the Scorched Waste. No formal guardians watch it. Access is blocked by the lethal environment, roaming Turkorcs, territorial dragonkin, and the sheer difficulty of reaching the interior of a continent sealed behind the world’s strongest magical prison.

7. Beast-folk (The Laughing Third)
Summary: The Beast-Folk are the Laughing Third — descendants of the Rathar who begged Sylph for the Veil of Forgetting during the Demon War and were transmuted into a single unified people of anthropomorphic mammals, birds, and amphibians whose culture is built around deliberate joy, communal laughter, and the sacred refusal of perfect memory.
Origin
Beast-folk are not a divinely created race in the conventional sense, nor are they corrupted animals or war-shaped refugees. They are one people — not scattered adaptations, but a single, deliberate act of divine mercy wrapped in laughter.
Ten thousand years ago, when one-third of the Rathar lay dead in the Demon War and the survivors' perfect memories had become instruments of torture, Sylph answered the plea of another third: "Let us forget enough to live."
The transmutation was ecstatic, chaotic, and final. The Veil of Forgetting settled over them like warm fog at dawn — dulling the endless replay of horror while sharpening every heartbeat of the present. They emerged as the Beast-Folk: unified in blood, origin, and the quiet knowledge that they chose joy over perfect recall.
They call themselves the Laughing Third, the Veiled, or simply Sylph's Kin.
Core Philosophy
Their cultures, while regionally flavored by ecology, share one unbreakable core:
Life is too short and too sweet to waste on what cannot be changed. Memory is a tool, not a chain. Laughter is sacred. Forgetting is holy.
Forms
The transmutation gave the Laughing Third forms drawn from animal kin: mammalian (wolf, cat, bear, fox, deer, bovine, rat, bat), avian (hawk, raven, owl, crane, songbird, vulture, parrot), and amphibian (frog, salamander, newt, toad). These forms are inherited through bloodline.
Social Structure – Laughing Circles
Beast-folk organize around Laughing Circles — fluid, instinct-driven communities of 30 to 300 souls. Leadership is situational and joyous. Decisions happen in Feast Debates. Grudges are rare — the Veil makes old slights feel distant and ridiculous.
The Change
Every few years adults feel a sacred restlessness. Circles throw farewell feasts and welcome them back changed (or not at all).
Traditions

Festival of the Laughing Choice
Flyting (sacred insult duels)
Veil Walks
Forgetful Hearths in every home

Housing, Clothing & Food
Settlements are joyous hybrids of lost Rathar precision and wild exuberance. Clothing is practical, expressive, colorful, and kinetic. Food is sacrament and celebration (Veil Cakes, Laughing Broth, Memory Burn desserts).
Relationships
They maintain warm, mutually beneficial ties with the Shada’din (dwarves) and deep sibling bonds with the Katharim.
Modern Role
Many guard lands near the Demonfall Marches. Their instinctive pattern-breaking subtly strengthens the Weave.

8. Katharim (The Weighted Third)
Summary: The Katharim are the Weighted Third of the Rathar — the gray-robed undertakers, crypt keepers, and divine-only priests who refused Sylph's Veil of Forgetting and chose instead to remember the dead with unflinching precision, becoming Eadras' foremost archivists of mortality.
Origin
The Katharim are descended directly from the third of the Rathar who refused the Veil of Forgetting during the Demon War. While the Laughing Third chose to forget so they might live brightly, the Rememberers took upon themselves the sacred, crushing duty of never letting the dead be forgotten.
The Refusal of Arcane Magic
They refuse all arcane magic — the raw manipulation of Eldritch through personal will, Weave-threads, sorcery, wizardry, or scholarly formula. They practice only divine magic, channeled through priesthood and ritual obedience.
Kharathen – City of Unforgotten Names
Their central holy site is Kharathen, a vast necropolis known as the City of Unforgotten Names. It is a sprawling complex of white stone tombs, memorial halls, silent gardens, and endless rows of carved name-stelae.
While Kharathen is their spiritual heart, Katharim tend graves and maintain memorial sites all over Eadras. Small groups travel constantly as wandering caretakers, ensuring that no fallen soul is forgotten.
Daily Life & Traditions
Lifestyle is ascetic and rhythmic. They maintain Memory Circles, perform undertaker rites with solemn precision, and serve as neutral arbiters in mixed communities.

9. Spear of Crystalline Entropy
Forged by Ssar’veth with aid from a neverborn chaos entity. Grants unrestricted Elder Godhood but strips the wielder’s deepest motivation. Four known wielders. Currently hidden by the gods for the fourth time. Xar’vion’s Icon (mobile statue) is carried by the Black Hand.

10. Order Sharanel
The formal name for sorcerers sworn to Lirra and the protection of the Weave. They wear the Sworn Eye amulet.
