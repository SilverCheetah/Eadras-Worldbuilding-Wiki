Mithril is a rare silver that has been naturally transformed by mana, becoming lighter, stronger, and capable of storing mana within its structure. It forms under intense mana exposure, typically deep underground or near mantle activity. It is highly prized by enchanters for its ability to hold complex spells.
Orichalcum is a rare gold that has been naturally transformed by mana. It is heavier than normal gold but far stronger and also stores mana. It has a distinctive reddish-gold color.
Kylum is mana-infused copper. It has a bright, almost glowing appearance and is an excellent conductor of both electricity and magic. Mages often use it in wands and magical circuitry.
Valthyr is mana-infused iron. It is significantly stronger and more durable than normal iron while remaining workable. It is the preferred metal for high-quality weapons and armor that need to hold enchantments.
Valthrene is the mana-steel alloy made by combining Valthyr with carbon. It is exceptionally tough and holds a sharp edge extremely well. It is the standard material for masterwork weapons and heavy armor.
Argentus is the alloy of mithril and kylum. It has a beautiful silvery-white color with a slight blue sheen and is excellent at conducting and shaping magical energy.
Aurelius is the alloy of orichalcum and kylum. It has a rich golden-red color and is particularly good at storing large amounts of mana for long periods.
Elyrium is the alloy of mithril and orichalcum. It has a brilliant platinum-like appearance and is considered one of the most powerful magical metals, capable of holding extremely complex and long-lasting enchantments.
Magisterium is the highest-tier alloy, made from mithril, orichalcum, and valthyr. It is extremely rare and powerful, said to be able to hold permanent, self-sustaining magic.
