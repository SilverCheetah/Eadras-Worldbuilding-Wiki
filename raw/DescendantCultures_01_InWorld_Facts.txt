DESCENDANT & OTHER CULTURES — IN-WORLD FACTS
World: Eadras

================================================================
1. THE DESCENDANT-CULTURE LANDSCAPE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Several major Eadras cultures descend from or are descended-FROM the three original humanoid races:

- RATHARI — Cultural descendants of the Velrathi. Proud, scholarly, ritualistic, deeply isolated in modern times.
- GRUUG — An entire race created by the Velrathi for heavy labor and engineering. The Goblins descend from them.
- GOBLINS — Descended from the Gruug. The most radically egalitarian of the contribution-philosophy cultures.

The ELFIN are not descendants in the same sense — they are the surviving moderns of the original Elfen line, with their own distinctive relationship to nature.

The ORDEN are a separate culture entirely, sharing no direct ancestry with the original three races. They are notable here because of their unique alcohol culture (whale honey mead) and their long-standing alliance with the Marrow (Sea Elves).


================================================================
2. THE PHILOSOPHY OF CONTRIBUTION — CULTURAL THREAD
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Velrathi, Gruug, and Goblins share a core cultural belief: every capable individual must contribute to society. Freeloading is not tolerated.

However, all three cultures are PRAGMATIC, NOT EXTREMIST. They make sensible exceptions for those who are injured, ill, elderly, or disabled. Those who are unable to perform labor beyond their ability are not punished — the community finds other ways for them to contribute or supports them.

The general principle: if you are capable of contributing and choose not to, there will be consequences.

The three cultures express this belief at different intensities:

VELRATHI (least extreme) — Everyone must contribute, but contribution can be intellectual, artistic, or skilled. Physical labor is not required of everyone. (See "Original Humanoid Cultures" for full Velrathi version.)

GRUUG (more extreme) — Created by the Velrathi specifically for physical labor and engineering work. Even high-ranking Gruug are expected to regularly work with their hands.

GOBLIN (most extreme) — The most radical expression. No matter your role or status — king, archmage, high priest, common laborer — every goblin is required to regularly serve on the work gangs doing manual labor.


================================================================
3. GRUUG — OVERVIEW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Gruug are an entire race created by the Velrathi.

Their purpose was specific: to perform the HEAVY PHYSICAL LABOR and ENGINEERING WORK required to maintain Velrathi society's machines and magical infrastructure. They were the engineers and the heavy labor force of the Velrathi civilization.

Because their entire existence was built around labor, the Gruug developed a deep cultural value around physical work. Even after acquiring full cultural autonomy and producing their own descendants (the Goblins), the Gruug maintained the cultural respect for hands-on labor.

In the Gruug version of the contribution philosophy:
- All capable Gruug are expected to contribute to society.
- High-ranking individuals are expected to REGULARLY engage in physical labor alongside common citizens.
- The same sensible exceptions for injured, ill, elderly, or disabled apply.


================================================================
4. GOBLIN EGALITARIANISM
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Goblin society is RADICALLY EGALITARIAN. Regardless of station, every goblin is expected to contribute physical labor.

Kings, merchants, wizards, priests, and warriors alike are all SCHEDULED to serve regular shifts on the work gangs. When the labor roster is posted (roughly every week), no one is exempt. A king might be making critical decisions for the tribe one day, and the next day he is working shoulder-to-shoulder with common laborers digging ditches or repairing walls.

The Goblin position: different roles require different skills — a wizard must study, a king must govern — but no role makes anyone BETTER than anyone else. The society is a MERITOCRACY built on the belief that everyone is a goblin first. Status is temporary and earned, never inherent. A king is simply a goblin who has proven himself worthy of greater responsibility.

This philosophy is so ingrained that goblins find the rigid hierarchies of other races both strange and slightly ridiculous.

The same sensible exceptions for injured, ill, elderly, or disabled apply. Goblin egalitarianism is extreme, but not extremist.


================================================================
5. GOBLIN BATHING CULTURE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Goblins actually ENJOY bathing. They like being clean.

However, they are also practical. They spend their days crawling through scrap heaps, working in machinery, and handling grease, oil, and rust. They have developed a working philosophy:

"If I'm working, I'm gonna get filthy. That's just part of the job. But when the day's done? Yeah, I'm gonna sit in a nice hot bath and scrub the grease off."

A goblin will happily sit in a bath for an hour after work, but will not lose sleep over smelling like machine oil during the day. The bath is the reward, not the constant state.


================================================================
6. GNOME BATHING — CONTRAST
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Gnomes do not have an active bathing culture. They do not LIKE being dirty, but they do not particularly care about being squeaky clean either.

Most gnomes bathe roughly once a week, sometimes twice. They consider this sufficient. They are too lost in their own heads — too absorbed in their next invention or some obscure mechanical principle — to give the matter much thought.

This is consistent with the broader gnome pattern of intellectual absorption at the expense of housekeeping. Dishes pile up at gnome workshops. Soap goes unused. Cleanliness is not opposed; it is simply not prioritized.

7. ELFIN — OVERVIEW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elfin are the surviving modern-era line of the original Elfen, with their own distinctive relationship to the natural world. Their culture is built around being GUARDIANS OF NATURAL BALANCE.


================================================================
8. ELFIN RELATIONSHIP WITH ANIMALS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elfin do not keep traditional pets. Their villages and settlements are nonetheless constantly filled with wild animals, because the Elfin do not hunt for sport or food.

HERBIVORES are particularly comfortable around them. Deer, rabbits, birds, and other plant-eaters will often approach Elfin openly, sometimes even begging for food. The Elfin frequently feed these animals by hand.

CARNIVORES are generally not welcome in Elfin villages. If a predator enters the settlement, the Elfin will kill it — though they do so with regret. They do not kill carnivores for food or sport, but to protect the herbivores and the safety of their communities.


================================================================
9. ELFIN AS GUARDIANS OF NATURAL BALANCE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elfin see themselves as the GUARDIANS OF NATURAL BALANCE in the wild regions of the world that remain untouched by civilization.

This means that when necessary, the Elfin WILL hunt — both herbivores and carnivores — to maintain the health and balance of the local ecosystem. They do not hunt for food. They hunt for ecosystem management.

These ecological killings are done with reluctance and with RITUALISTIC RESPECT. The Elfin do not approach this work as predators or as gamesmen. They approach it as caretakers performing a difficult duty.


================================================================
10. ELFIN AGRICULTURE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elfin do practice agriculture, but it bears little resemblance to human or dwarven farming.

They NEVER CLEAR-CUT LAND. Instead, they plant and tend crops between existing trees in the natural forest. Their nature magic ensures that the planted crops receive sufficient sunlight and nutrients without harming the surrounding forest.

This produces a harmonious agricultural pattern in which Elfin food production is woven INTO the existing forest rather than imposed on top of it. The forest itself remains intact and healthy while sustaining additional plant growth that supports the Elfin community.


================================================================
11. ORDEN — OVERVIEW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Orden are a separate cultural lineage not descended from the original three humanoid races. They are notable here for two reasons:

- Their signature alcohol culture (whale honey mead) is unique.
- They have a long-standing alliance with the Marrow (Sea Elves) that shapes both cultures' lives.

The Orden culture has a strong "Viking-inspired" flavor — seafaring, hardy, rite-of-passage oriented, with deep cultural pride centered on dangerous communal voyages.


================================================================
12. WHALE HONEY — THE FOUNDATION
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The signature drink of the Orden is a strong, golden mead made from WHALE HONEY — a thick, sweet, amber substance produced by massive whales that live in the cold northern seas.

The whales produce this honey-like substance when hunting in cold, scarce waters. They squirt it out to attract small life forms (fish, krill) which they then feed on. The whales' honey production is biologically unusual but is well-established as part of the canonical Orden cultural ecology.

"Whale honey" is a shorthand name. The substance is not technically honey. It is a thick, sweet, sugar-rich substance produced by whales that ferments into excellent mead.


================================================================
13. THE HARVESTING METHOD
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Whale honey cannot be collected from the open water — once a whale releases it into the sea, it dilutes too rapidly to be useful.

Instead, the Orden have developed a specific cooperative harvesting method:

- The Orden cannot easily lure whales to the surface on their own.
- The MARROW (Sea Elves), allied with the Orden, use their innate connection to the sea to gently guide the whales up to the surface.
- The whales are brought alongside the Orden's ships.
- Orden harvesters then carefully insert long SYRINGES MADE OF IVORY AND TEAK, SEALED WITH WHALE BLUBBER, into the whales' honey glands.
- The honey is drawn directly from the gland while the whale is feeding.
- Only a portion is taken from each whale, never all of it.

A typical harvesting voyage lasts SIX DAYS and can harvest honey from DOZENS of whales over that period.


================================================================
14. THE RITE OF PASSAGE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Whale honey harvesting is not just an economic activity for the Orden. It is a deeply tied CULTURAL RITE OF PASSAGE.

Every young Ordon is expected to participate in at least one whale harvesting voyage as a coming-of-age trial.

The voyages are genuinely dangerous. If a whale becomes agitated and begins thrashing while the harvesters are alongside in small boats, the results can be deadly. The small boats can be crushed or capsized.

Surviving a whale harvesting voyage is considered a major milestone that earns young Ordons respect among their people.


================================================================
15. SHARING WITH THE MARROW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Once the mead is finished and bottled, the Orden ALWAYS SHARE a portion of it with the Marrow.

The Marrow take the bottles back down to their underwater cities. This sharing is a fundamental and unbroken part of the Orden-Marrow alliance. Without the Marrow, the whales could not be lured to the surface. Without the Orden, the honey could not be drawn or fermented. The mead itself is the product of the partnership, and the sharing of finished mead is the renewal of that partnership.


================================================================
16. THE ORDEN-MARROW ALLIANCE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Orden and the Marrow share an old and stable alliance:

- The Marrow are Sea Elves who live in underwater cities.
- They have an innate connection to sea creatures.
- They have been allied with the Orden for long enough that the whale honey harvest is a routine cooperative practice.

The alliance is mutually beneficial:
- The Marrow gain access to the finished mead (which they cannot brew themselves).
- The Orden gain access to whales they could not otherwise call to the surface.

This is a stable cultural partnership of the kind that Eadras consistently treats as more interesting than rivalries. Neither culture resents the other's specialization. Both gain.
