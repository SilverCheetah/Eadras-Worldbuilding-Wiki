WHAT GOLDENWOOD IS ================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Goldenwood is an alien-origin tree species that does not fully conform to the physical and metaphysical assumptions of Eadras.

It is not merely a magical tree.

Its substance was formed under the laws of another Mythos and therefore exhibits material behaviors that native Eadras biology and forestry cannot fully explain.

Goldenwood is one of the clearest known examples of alien life coexisting within Eadras without fully belonging to it.

================================================================ 2. APPEARANCE

[IN-WORLD] Mature Goldenwood trees possess:

Greyish bark with a faint golden sheen.
Green-gold trefoil leaves.
Dense golden heartwood.
Extremely slow growth.
A subtle metallic warmth in direct sunlight.

The bark often appears dull at a distance but reflects warm gold tones when viewed closely or under angled light.

The leaves do not shimmer unnaturally, but observers often report that the coloration seems “slightly more vivid than it should be.”

================================================================ 3. DURABILITY

[IN-WORLD] Goldenwood possesses extraordinary structural durability far beyond native Eadras wood.

Documented properties include:

Extreme resistance to cutting.
Exceptional resistance to splitting.
High resilience under stress.
Very slow degradation.
Long-term structural stability.

A sharpened blade may require dozens of resharpenings merely to cut through a sapling or thick branch.

Traditional axes dull rapidly against mature Goldenwood.

Because of this, harvesting Goldenwood is slow, specialized, and labor-intensive.

================================================================ 4. LIVING CUTTINGS

[IN-WORLD] Goldenwood remains alive after separation from the parent tree for extraordinarily long periods.

A cut branch, stave blank, or bow stave may remain biologically viable for years with minimal care.

Requirements appear minimal:

occasional water,
indirect sunlight,
and protection from total desiccation.

Even worked lumber may continue exhibiting low-level living behavior long after cutting.

This has led to the widespread phrase:

“Goldenwood never fully believes it has been cut.”

================================================================ 5. PROPAGATION

[IN-WORLD] Severed portions of Goldenwood can be planted and may eventually grow into new trees.

This is true even for relatively small viable cuttings.

The process is extremely slow.

Goldenwood propagation behaves more like continuation than reproduction. Some philosophers and Druids argue that the species does not conceptualize separation the same way native Eadras biology does.

================================================================ 6. USES

[IN-WORLD] Goldenwood is highly prized for:

bows,
staves,
ritual implements,
heirloom tools,
reinforced handles,
long-duration structural pieces,
and rare magical applications.

Goldenwood bows are especially valued because the wood maintains elasticity and structural memory over immense timescales.

Many Goldenwood objects remain functional for centuries when maintained.

Some old bows and staves are rumored to still be technically alive.

================================================================ 7. CRAFT TRADITIONS

[IN-WORLD] Working Goldenwood requires specialized techniques.

Common practices include:

rotational cutting,
alternating blades,
slow shaving instead of deep chopping,
heat-softened scoring,
and long curing periods.

Some Druidic circles insist that Goldenwood responds poorly to anger, haste, or violent harvesting.

Certain crafters leave cut staves partially rooted during shaping so the wood “understands continuity.”

Whether this is superstition or practical technique remains debated.

================================================================ 8. RELATIONSHIP TO ALIEN OBJECTS

[IN-WORLD] Goldenwood is categorized as alien-origin material.

Alien objects originate outside the local Mythos and therefore do not fully obey Eadras physical and metaphysical assumptions.

Goldenwood demonstrates that alien-origin matter is not automatically hostile.

However, it remains ontologically non-native.

Its unusual durability, persistence, and biological continuity are believed to derive from physical laws that differ subtly from those governing native Eadras life.

================================================================ 9. DRUIDIC INTERPRETATIONS

[IN-WORLD] Druidic opinion on Goldenwood varies.

Some Druids view it as:

a tolerated immigrant species,
an adopted lifeform,
or proof that alien life can coexist peacefully with Eadras.

Others remain uneasy, arguing that Goldenwood obeys ecological assumptions not negotiated into the living relationships of the world.

A minority consider large unmanaged Goldenwood groves metaphysically dangerous.

================================================================ 10. SCHOLARLY THEORIES

[IN-WORLD] Common theories regarding Goldenwood include:

It evolved in a Mythos with different entropy conditions.
It possesses distributed biological continuity.
It experiences injury differently from native life.
Its cellular or magical structure partially resists local decay assumptions.
It may preserve foreign cosmological rules at microscopic scales.

No consensus exists.
