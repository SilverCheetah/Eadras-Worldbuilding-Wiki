Spirits — interior governance clarification, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture appended to the spirits canon thread. Josh clarified the
scope of the cross-realm Bureaucratic administration: it covers shell-
issuance, assignment, and promotion, but NOT all governance of the
Feywild and Shadowdark.)

== Canon ==

The Feywild and Shadowdark each maintain their own INTERIOR GOVERNANCE,
distinct from the Divine and Infernal Bureaucracies' shell-administration
layer. This interior governance is LOCAL OR REGIONAL, NOT PLANE-WIDE --
the secondary realms are NOT unified under any single internal authority
the way Celestia and Nyxar are.

Interior governance includes (non-exhaustive):
- Fey courts (multiple regional courts, not one fey-wide council)
- Shadow councils
- Regional fiefs and local powers
- Place-by-place political arrangements

The Bureaucracies do NOT run the day-to-day politics of these realms.
The Bureaucratic scope is precisely:
- Shell issuance
- Shell assignment
- Promotion processing
- Shell-catalogue maintenance
- Back-room infrastructure (where shells are removed)

Everything else in the secondary realms is the secondary realm's own
affair.

== Reading the split ==

The Bureaucracies are the EMPLOYMENT AND CREDENTIALING layer.
The local fey courts and shadow councils are the POLITICAL AND SOCIAL
layer.

They cooperate at the shell-handoff: a promotion requires both a local
sponsor (interior governance decides the spirit deserves elevation) and
a new shell (Bureaucracy issues the upgraded credential). But they are
different institutions doing different work, and neither has authority
over the other's domain.

== Implications ==

- Primary realms (Celestia, Nyxar) ARE largely unified under their
  administering Bureaucracy. Day-to-day life there IS Bureaucratic
  day-to-day. (Though even the primary realms surely have non-
  Bureaucratic elements -- this is unspecified canon for now.)
- Secondary realms (Feywild, Shadowdark) have a dual structure: shell
  layer (Bureaucracy) + interior governance (regional/local courts and
  councils).
- This makes the secondary realms politically RICHER than the primary
  realms in some ways: fey courts can scheme, regional powers can
  contest, alliances can shift independently of Bureaucratic oversight.
- The Bureaucracies are somewhat external to the secondary realms in
  daily life: a Divine Bureaucracy clerk processing a fey promotion is
  NOT a participant in the fey court politics that produced the
  promotion -- they are just issuing the shell that the court has
  decided to confer.

== Earlier framing that needed softening ==

The spirits.md page (in its earlier "Administration: two bureaucracies,
four realms" section) said the bureaucracies "administer" the bright/
dark realms -- "administers" was too broad and implied all-governance.
The new framing says the Bureaucracies "handle shell-administration"
specifically, with the secondary realms' interior governance remaining
the realms' own affair.
