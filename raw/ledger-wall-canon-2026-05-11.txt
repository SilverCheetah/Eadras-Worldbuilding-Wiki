== Canon: Ledger Wall ==

The Tarran institutional record of FAILURES, DEBTS, and UNKEPT COMMITMENTS.
Counterpart to the existing canonical Boast Wall (a long hallway of carved
deeds going back to the founding, per firethorn-hold.md line 95).

TWO LOCATIONS:
- ORIGINAL — at the imperial capital of the Firethorn Empire on Eadras
  (pre-Banishment). The specific city/building/curatorial offices remain
  open canon (depend on canonising the Firethorn Empire's imperial capital,
  which the wiki does not yet do).
- SURVIVING — at Firethorn Hold on Prime-like Gaia (exile equivalent),
  paired with the Hold's existing Boast Wall.

ANIMATING PRINCIPLE: *The office is the act.* A Tarran who claims an office
(Protector, Steward, Emperor, Mother) and does not perform the act has
failed both the office and its witnesses. The Ledger names what the act
was supposed to be, what was done in its place, and at what cost.

ENTRIES REMAIN. Not erased by later success, not softened by mitigation.
The Tarran historical conscience does not lighten its debts.

CENTRAL ENTRY (at both locations): the failure to reinforce the Final
Wall in year 15 of the twenty-year Demon War.

CULTURAL FUNCTION:
- Conscience-anchor for the Maternal Line.
- Counterweight to the Boast Wall (deeds vs. debts paired).
- Doctrinal articulation: "the office is the act."
- The Matriarchy's "males brought us to ruin" lesson is recorded on the
  Wall but framed as "true but too small" — the fuller lesson is "a
  Protector who does not arrive is no longer a Protector."

CUSTODIAN: one of the Seven Thorn-Daughters (already canon on
firethorn-hold.md line 70) in her role as keeper of the exile Wall.

(Adjacent Tarran-multi-POV material — the "15th-year recitation by
daughters of the Maternal Line," the "Empty Claim" formal-rite naming,
the specifically-titled "Thorn-Daughter of Memory" — remain candidates
for explicit canon promotion but are not directly canonized this turn.
Note: the Rite of Empty Claim is *already* canonized at firethorn-hold.md
line 54.)

== Canon: Demon War timeline ==

DURATION: twenty years total. The Demon War lasted twenty years from
the opening of the Demon Prince's portal to the war's end.

EXISTING CANON THAT STAYS: Xar'vion's turn at year 5 (he summoned the
Demon Prince, then "after five years, finally understood what he had
unleashed" and turned to the Elder Gods). The new 20-year duration is
consistent with this — Xar'vion fought against demons for years 5–20.

NEW CANON THIS TURN: The Battle of the Broken Conclaves (Final Wall)
occurred in YEAR 15 of the twenty-year war. The war continued
approximately five more years after the Final Wall before the Demon
Prince was sealed back through his portal and the major incursion ended.

This SUPERSEDES the existing demon-war.md line "lasted at least five
full years" (an open-ended framing). The new line is precise: twenty
years.
