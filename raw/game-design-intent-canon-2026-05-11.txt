== Canon ==

The Game of the Gods is **designed by Dao for containment**, serving
Roles 1 and 2 of Dao's monitor function:

- Role 1: ensure things run smoothly (gods absorbed in the Game do
  not over-intervene in mortal affairs).
- Role 2: keep the macro balance (the most powerful pantheon in a
  magic-rich Mythos does not push outward into other Mythoi).

This is canon at the Tome / authorial-narrator level, not at the
in-Eadras-mortal-scholar level. Eadras inhabitants do NOT know this:
- Dao made the Game in secret.
- Dao did not explain it.
- The gods who play the Game do not know it functions as
  containment; from inside they experience it as the most
  interesting thing in existence.
- Mortal scholars cannot verify the design intent from within the
  Mythos.

The Tome (and the wiki's tome-neutral narrator voice) knows the
design intent. Inside-Eadras characters do not.

== Implication for page voice ==

The Position section's confident language about the Game ("the
mechanism that maintains [inward orientation]" / "benign containment"
/ "not coincidence") is correct and need not be hedged. The Causation
section's epistemic humility about WHAT THE EADRAS INHABITANTS KNOW
remains correct.

The two sections operate at different epistemic levels:
- Position section: Tome-narrator knowledge level (knows design
  intent canonically).
- Causation section: Eadras-internal knowledge level (does not
  know what the gods don't know).

This reconciles the apparent tension. The Skeptic's flag was valid
within a more restrictive epistemic model than the Tome operates at;
the Tome operates at a broader level and can know what the
inhabitants don't.
