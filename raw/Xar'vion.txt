Xar'vion

Xar'vion grew up as a homeless waif in a nearly forgotten imperial capital with his older sister, whose name has been lost to history. She protected the weak and sickly Xar'vion from predators on the streets, and he loved her for it.

One night, a group of young noblemen from a sadistic club caught her. They brutalized her for sport while Xar'vion could only watch, too weak to stop them. She died trying to shield him one final time.

That night broke something in Xar'vion forever. Kneeling beside her body, he made a vow: he would never be weak again. He would never be helpless again. He would gain so much power that no one could ever take from him what he loved.

Three years later, now a young man in the middle of his teens, Xar'vion took a job as a waiter in the very tavern those noblemen still frequented. He spent weeks studying their habits. Then, on the exact anniversary of his sister’s death, he served them all drinks laced with massive doses of poison. The moment he set the cups on their table, he turned around, walked out the back door, and never looked back.

He didn’t need to watch them die. He already knew they were dead.

That single night became the seed of the man who would spend centuries building a massive arcane empire and the Black Hand spy network, all for one purpose — to claim the Spear of Crystallized Entropy and bring his sister back.

He succeeded. He used the Spear and ascended to an unbound Elder God with no restrictions. No one knew about the trap — not even Xar'vion — so when it triggered, it caught him completely by surprise. In a single instant, the Spear ripped away his desire to resurrect his sister — the one thing that still mattered to him.

Even though that desire was gone, he kept the power and continued his conquests. When the world rose against him, he made a terrible mistake and summoned a demon lord. Together they tore open a portal to the demon mythos.

It took Xar'vion five full years to realize the demon had no intention of honoring any promises. The demon simply wanted to consume everything in this mythos. The war lasted at least five years.

Only then, when Xar'vion finally understood the full horror of what he had unleashed, did he turn to the Elder Gods and say, “I have made a terrible mistake. Let me join you.”

The gods accepted. Xar'vion immediately turned all his forces and all his divine power against the demon lord, forcing him back through the portal. The moment the portal was sealed shut, the Elder Gods betrayed Xar'vion and trapped his entire essence inside his own icon.

If that icon is ever destroyed, Xar'vion is permanently gone. Until then, he still has unlimited power within line of sight of the icon and continues to secretly run the Black Hand from hiding.
