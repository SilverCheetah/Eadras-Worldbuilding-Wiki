Game of the Gods — nature and causation, 2026-05-11

(Session-capture: Josh extended the Game of the Gods canon with three
new aspects -- its structural nature as a miniature of Eadras, the godly
complexity of its rules, and the unresolved causation question that
mortal philosophers debate.)

== Canon ==

1. THE GAME IS A PERFECT RECREATION OF EADRAS, GAMIFIED.
   The board is not an abstract gameboard; it is an exact-scale model
   of the Eadras Mythos in miniature. All important mortals are pieces
   on this miniature Eadras. The Game and the world below correspond
   structurally and dynamically.

2. THE RULES ARE GODLY-COMPLEX.
   The rules are intricate, layered, and appropriate to immortal
   players. They are beyond the capacity of any mortal mind to hold in
   full. The mechanics resemble D&D in form (turn-based, piece-moving)
   but operate at an entirely different order of strategic depth.

3. THE GAME SEEMINGLY PRODUCES OUTCOMES ON EADRAS.
   When a god moves a piece, the mortal-world correlate of that piece
   appears to move with it: a king's army marches, a lover changes
   their mind, a city's fortunes turn. The correspondence is reliable
   enough that the gods plainly believe they are playing reality.

4. CAUSATION IS UNCLEAR FROM THE MORTAL SIDE.
   Whether the Game causes Eadras, Eadras causes the Game, both are
   true simultaneously (isomorphism without directional causation),
   or the relationship is asymmetric (some moves cause, others
   reflect), cannot be resolved from inside Eadras. This is one of
   the great philosophical questions of the world.

5. PHILOSOPHERS ARGUE.
   The debate is approximately as old as recorded scholarship. Mortal
   schools have lined up on every position. The gods themselves do
   not always agree on the answer; dispatched divine agents asked
   about it have given different answers depending on the god in
   question.

== Positions in the debate (in-universe schools) ==

(These positions are the ways mortal traditions have organised the
debate; specific named "schools" are open canon for future fleshing-out.)

- "GAME-CAUSES-EADRAS" position: every meaningful mortal event is the
  consequence of a divine move. Free will is an illusion mortals tell
  each other. The real action happens above and is enforced below.

- "EADRAS-CAUSES-GAME" position (reflective reading): the board is
  an observer, a mirror, a divine instrument that REFLECTS what
  mortals do but does not direct it. Mortal moral responsibility is
  preserved. Lirran clergy generally favour this reading because of
  its preservation of moral agency.

- "ISOMORPHISM" position: the Game and Eadras are isomorphic at every
  moment; to ask which causes the other is to misunderstand the
  relationship. Some Tarran philosophers (the Deep Learning progenitor
  race, with a tradition of formal ontological investigation) have
  argued for this position.

- "PRINCIPLED REFUSAL TO SPECULATE": mortals cannot resolve the
  question from inside Eadras; asking is not piety. The Doctrine of
  the Unheld Hand is the canonical example.

None of these positions are settled, and the debate is expected to
persist as long as both mortals and gods exist.

== Notes ==

- The Game pre-existed the Demon War (which destabilised it; see
  gods-hate-demons-2026-05-11.txt) and presumably pre-existed most
  of Eadras's recorded history. Dao created it in secret; nobody
  knows when exactly.

- The Game is not separate from Eadras in any sense that mortals can
  identify; it is the same world, viewed from the divine realm in
  playable form. Whether the divine view is "the real Eadras" or
  "a representation of Eadras" is itself part of the causation
  debate.

- The connection to free will, divine intervention, prayer
  efficacy, and divine omniscience is rich and largely unexplored
  in this session.
