HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT
================================================================

This document captures the council's most theologically ambitious work: the recognition that Ixthal's creation of dragons and unicorns was illegal under divine law, and the formulation of the divine law it violated. The wiki preserves an unusually large amount of this material verbatim — these two elements may be the most-preserved single block of playground material in the entire ingest sequence.

Contradiction flags:
  [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI]    [CANONIZED_AS X]    [WIKI_EXPANDS]
  [INTERNAL_CONTRADICTION]    [FLAG_FOR_REVIEW]

THE CORE RETCON OF THIS TOPIC. The playground assumes a specific institutional fact: the Elder Gods KNOW about Ixthal's Forbidden Kindling. After his violations, the council proposes that the gods divided on response (a divine schism), placed a Veil of Closed Genesis upon Ixthal's communication, sealed records, censured him, and chose containment over destruction because the new races could not ethically be erased.

The wiki overrides this. Per the wiki: "Ixthal has not been caught. The Elder Gods believe both races arose from rare but natural Source events." There was no divine schism over Ixthal because there was no recognized divine crime. The gods are operating with incomplete information. The Sealed Hall of First Flame in Ratharil and the divine seals on unicorn origin-memory are not products of a divine cover-up; they are products of IXTHAL'S OWN concealment, supported by the gods' lack of suspicion and by selective dragon institutional secrecy.

This retcon does not contradict the playground's THEOLOGICAL STRUCTURE — the Doctrine of the Unheld Hand and the Edict of Closed Genesis are real, formal, and survive into canon almost verbatim. The retcon contradicts the INSTITUTIONAL HISTORY — whether the gods successfully caught Ixthal, debated his case, and imposed restraints. They did not. They do not know.

The persistent retcons from earlier topics apply here too:
  - Ssar'veth → Kudur-eṭir
  - Ithren → Ixthal (Arathi mortal name Ašar-eṭir)


================================================================
1. TWO KINDS OF LAW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Mage Architect distinguished two categories of law that govern divine action:

  OMNIVERSAL LAW. Established by the Omniversal Creator. Universal, unbreakable, applying to every Mythos. No one can violate it — not Ixthal, not Ssar'veth/Kudur-eṭir, not the Elder Gods, not even the creator deities of a Mythos. The Omniversal Creator's laws are absolute.

  DIVINE LAW within Eadras. The order established by the gods of a Mythos: restrictions, mandates, covenants, jurisdictions, taboos, and cosmic governance. It can be broken, though never without consequence.

[IN-WORLD] Ixthal did NOT violate Omniversal Law. He did violate divine law within Eadras.

[IN-WORLD] This distinction matters across the whole setting. It means divine law is not omniscient enforcement of cosmic principle. It is a political-theological order, made by gods, enforceable only when violation is detected.


================================================================
2. THE DOCTRINE OF THE UNHELD HAND
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elder Gods of Eadras uphold the foundational principle of divine noninterference: the DOCTRINE OF THE UNHELD HAND. Not because they are indifferent. Not because they do not love, pity, envy, judge, or favor mortals. But because Eadras has already paid the price of godlike power applied too directly to mortal history. The Arathi Cataclysm proved that when beings of overwhelming power seize the wheel of the mortal world, even noble motives can become extinction.

[IN-WORLD] Core teaching:

  The divine hand may bless the road, light the lantern, and strengthen the traveler.
  It may not carry the traveler to the destination.

[IN-WORLD] The principle: MORTALS MUST LIVE MORTAL LIVES. The gods may guide. They may inspire. They may empower priests under covenant. They may answer through omen, miracle, or chosen vessel in limited ways. But they must not seize the whole fate of a people in their hands.

[IN-WORLD] The Doctrine emerged from divine reflection on the cataclysm and on Auralis and Thamior's failure to save the Arathi. Before the Falling Star, the Elder Gods had customs of restraint but not a fully articulated doctrine. The two ascended Arathi spent their entire divine essence trying to save their people and failed catastrophically. The gods saw that even compassionate direct intervention could not necessarily save mortals, and might draw divine beings into ruin. Afterward, the Doctrine was codified.


================================================================
3. WHY THE DOCTRINE EXISTS — SIX REASONS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Elder Gods hold to the Doctrine for six distinct reasons:

  1. PROTECTION OF MORTAL AGENCY. Mortals must make choices that matter.
  2. PREVENTION OF DIVINE TYRANNY. A god who can "save" a people can also shape, own, or control them.
  3. AVOIDANCE OF COSMIC IMBALANCE. Direct intervention at species-scale creates ecological, magical, and historical consequences that cascade for millions of years.
  4. RESPECT FOR OMNIVERSAL LAWS. Death and destruction cannot be abolished; attempting to do so leads to horror.
  5. CONTAINMENT OF DIVINE CONFLICT. If one god saves a favored race, another may empower its enemies; mortal wars become divine wars.
  6. MEMORY OF ARATH. Eadras has already been broken by someone trying to impose will upon fundamental reality.

[IN-WORLD] Reason 5 is especially important. If Qoxli saved a forest people from extinction, Daj might argue he can preserve a warrior nation. Nox might preserve an undead cult. Vos might permanently sustain a city-state. Sylph might create chaos-touched prankster races whenever bored. Soon mortal history becomes a divine arms race. The Doctrine prevents that.


================================================================
4. WHAT IS PERMITTED
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Doctrine permits limited divine influence:

  1. DIVINE MAGIC THROUGH MORTAL AGENTS. Clerics, paladins, or chosen servants may heal, protect, curse, bless, and act — but they remain mortal actors within the world.
  2. OMENS AND VISIONS. Gods may warn, but mortals must interpret and act.
  3. LIMITED MIRACLES. A god may save a village, cleanse a spring, heal a plague outbreak, or protect a child if the act does not rewrite the fate of entire peoples or species.
  4. EMPOWERMENT UNDER COVENANT. A deity may empower a champion, but the champion bears risk, choice, and consequence.
  5. PLANE-SPECIFIC ACTION. Gods may rule their domains and act more directly within divine realms — Celestia, Nyxar, or other planes — depending on jurisdiction.
  6. CORRECTION OF DIVINE OR EXTRAPLANAR VIOLATIONS. If demons, rogue deities, Neverborn contamination, or forbidden artifacts interfere, gods may respond more forcefully because the mortal order has already been breached.

[IN-WORLD] Practical examples (Cultural Weaver's formulation):
  - A temple healer can save a child from fever without violating divine law.
  - Nara cannot make an entire race immune to disease because she pities mothers.
  - Vos can inspire law codes and empower judges, but cannot descend and personally govern every corrupt city.
  - Daj can bless warriors, but cannot fight every mortal war himself.
  - Lirra can create the Weave in response to demons siphoning Eadras' magic because that is a mythos-scale magical violation threatening all mortal agency. But she cannot casually rewrite every sorcerer's soul to prevent misuse.

[IN-WORLD] The Lorekeeper's summary: the gods may protect the conditions under which mortal life continues. They may not live mortal life on mortals' behalf.


================================================================
5. WHAT IS FORBIDDEN
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Doctrine forbids acts that alter the macrostructure of mortal destiny:

  - Creating a new sapient mortal race
  - Preventing a mortal race from going extinct by direct divine preservation
  - Rendering a mortal civilization permanently indestructible
  - Directly overthrowing or founding mortal empires by divine force
  - Eliminating an ordinary mortal disease from the world by fiat
  - Granting universal immortality or removing the need for food, birth, death, or adaptation
  - Rewriting a species' nature without consent and mandate
  - Directly manipulating the Source to reshape life
  - Altering ecosystems on a continental or planetary scale for mortal preference


================================================================
6. THE EDICT OF CLOSED GENESIS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The EDICT OF CLOSED GENESIS is a specific legal instrument under the broader Doctrine. It targets the creation of new sapient races and unauthorized Source manipulation.

[IN-WORLD] Central command:

  No ascended mortal, lesser deity, spirit sovereign, or divine servant shall create a new sapient race, alter the Source, or kindle world-bearing life without the mandate of the Elder Pantheon.

[IN-WORLD] Formal wording:

  By decree of the Elder Pantheon, after the Breaking of Arath and the White Deluge:
  No god, ascended mortal, divine servant, spirit sovereign, or power of lesser throne shall take into their hand the continuance or genesis of a mortal people.
  No race shall be made as tool, comfort, answer, weapon, heir, or remedy without the full mandate of the Elder Gods.
  No doomed people shall be preserved entire by divine seizure from death, for death is sovereign and mortal fate must not be worn as a god's garment.
  Let the divine hand remain unclosed.

[IN-WORLD] The phrase "divine seizure from death" is essential. It does not forbid saving individuals. It forbids grabbing an entire people away from mortality because a god cannot bear loss. That was Ssar'veth's [Kudur-eṭir's] madness in another form: refusal to accept endings.

[IN-WORLD] The phrase "as tool, comfort, answer, weapon, heir, or remedy" closes loopholes. A god cannot say, "I did not make this race as a tool; I made them as a memorial." Still forbidden.

[IN-WORLD] Ixthal may have made dragons and unicorns as renewal, not replacement — but the law covered that too.


================================================================
7. GENESIS BY MANDATE — THE LAWFUL EXCEPTION
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] A lawful path exists: GENESIS BY MANDATE. Required:

  - Approval of the Elder Pantheon
  - Defined ecological and metaphysical boundaries
  - Consent or participation of appropriate divine domains
  - Regulation by Lirra or other magical authority if magic is involved
  - Acceptance that the new race remains mortal and subject to death
  - No attempt to create a permanent divine puppet species

[IN-WORLD] MARK, MELANIA, AND RAND — The Three That Are One — created the Elfan, Taran, and Velroth lawfully under this exception. They did not act secretly or unilaterally. They were recognized creator gods operating under divine permission after the world had stabilized enough for new mortal peoples.

[IN-WORLD] Ixthal bypassed this entirely. He did not ask permission. He did not submit design. He did not wait for mandate. He altered the Source directly.


================================================================
8. THE FORBIDDEN KINDLING — IXTHAL'S CRIME
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The creation of dragons and unicorns by Ixthal was SECRET. And it was FORBIDDEN.

[IN-WORLD] His crime had two parts (per the Mage Architect):

  First: UNAUTHORIZED SOURCE MANIPULATION. He caused Source Flares. That is not ordinary magic. The Source is the primary magical engine of Eadras. To alter its output, even briefly and locally, is an act of terrifying scale.

  Second: UNAUTHORIZED SAPIENT GENESIS. He transformed existing life into new sapient races: dragons first, unicorns later. That crossed a sacred boundary.

[IN-WORLD] The fact that his work succeeded does not make it lawful. And success was not guaranteed. The first Source Flare transformed nine giant reptile eggs into the first dragons. But if Ixthal had miscalculated, those eggs might have become monsters, unstable demigods, living magical plagues, or ruptures in the recovering biosphere. The second Source Flare created unicorns as telepathic herd-sapients. If mishandled, he might have produced a continent-wide psionic swarm, mindless agony, or a collective intelligence unable to coexist with any other life. Ixthal did not merely "make animals smarter." He gambled with the future of a world still healing from extinction.


================================================================
9. IXTHAL'S MOTIVE
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Not pride. Not conquest. Not loneliness, though loneliness surely gnawed at him.

[IN-WORLD] He had precognitive pattern-sight. He could read consequence. Perhaps he saw that without powerful stabilizing sapient species, Eadras' recovered biosphere would eventually collapse into repeated magical catastrophes. Or that later races could not emerge safely unless something first dominated, regulated, and tested the renewed world.

[IN-WORLD] Dragons were not merely apex predators. They were world-shapers. Unicorns were not merely prey who became people. They were psychic stabilizers of plains, herd-minds capable of collective resistance, and the source of psionics.

[IN-WORLD] Ixthal may have believed the Elder Gods were waiting too long. Divine caution after Arath was understandable. But excessive caution can become its own disaster. Life had returned. The world was ready for sapience again. The gods hesitated, perhaps debating mandate, jurisdiction, risk, and hierarchy. Ixthal acted.

[IN-WORLD] This makes him neither innocent nor villainous. He broke divine law because he believed inaction would doom the future in a slower way.

[IN-WORLD] The Lorekeeper's mythic formulation:

  The gods said: "Not yet."
  Ixthal saw the long road empty and answered: "Then never."
  So he stole no fire, for the Source was not his to steal.
  He bent no time, for time cannot bow.
  He spoke no command, for his throat was ash.
  But he opened his wound toward the sun,
  and the sun flared back.


================================================================
10. THE DIVINE COVERING — PLAYGROUND VS WIKI
================================================================

[SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI] The playground proposes that the gods CAUGHT Ixthal and chose containment over destruction.

The council's reasoning: by the time the gods understood the full result, the dragons already existed. Destroying the dragons to punish Ixthal would be monstrous. Undoing them was impossible without killing them. The same was later true of unicorns. The Elder Gods could punish Ixthal, conceal the method, bind future attempts, and forbid disclosure — but they could not ethically erase his creations.

The council proposed a DIVINE SCHISM in response:
  - Ioa condemned the violation of sovereign order
  - Vos demanded law, record-sealing, and enforceable prohibitions
  - Lirra was horrified by unauthorized Source manipulation, but understood the brilliance of the regulation
  - Qoxli defended the resulting life, if not the method
  - Nara pitied Ixthal's grief and the newborn races
  - Raja judged the work by its endurance
  - Daj respected the boldness and despised the secrecy
  - Sylph/Faye laughed, then asked the question no one wanted: if the forbidden children live, was the law wrong or merely broken well?
  - Nox kept his own counsel and remembered where secrets breed power
  - Shali and Shani insisted that the dead Arathi were not restored; Ixthal had not cheated death, only made new life after it

The council proposed that Lirra and Ioa together placed the VEIL OF CLOSED GENESIS upon Ixthal's divine presence — not a prison exactly, but a divine gag-law and containment lattice. He could exist, perceive, rarely act in matters of extinction or renewal — but he could not teach, describe, or reveal the method of Source Flare genesis. Given his trauma, Ixthal welcomed silence.

WIKI CANON: NONE OF THIS HAPPENED. "Ixthal has not been caught. The Elder Gods believe both races arose from rare but natural Source events." There was no divine schism over Ixthal because there was no recognized divine crime. The Veil of Closed Genesis does not exist. The various enforcement tools (Mandate Seals, Source Wards, Ioa's Censure, Vosian Redaction, Noxian Counter-Secrecy, Qoxlian Balance Signs, Death Witnesses of Shali and Shani) exist as DOCTRINAL ENFORCEMENT TOOLS in canon, but they were not deployed against Ixthal. They are theoretical instruments that could in principle be brought against a violator IF a violator were detected.

Ixthal's silence is SELF-IMPOSED for strategic concealment plus four compounding emotional reasons (see Topic 2 InWorld_Facts, Section 12). There is no divine gag upon him. He is his own prison.

The playground's institutional layering (Common Dragon Belief / Elder Lore / Forbidden Council Record) survives in canon — but the FORBIDDEN COUNCIL RECORD is dragon institutional secrecy at Ratharil, not a divine enforcement structure. The Sealed Hall of First Flame is a dragon institution. Lirra's seal on the Shard of First Shell is dragon-institutional, not the wider pantheon's coverup.

11. WHAT DRAGONS KNOW — THREE LEVELS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Most dragons do NOT know that Ixthal created them through a forbidden Source Flare.

[IN-WORLD] Their public myth says the Nine Progenitors hatched during the FIRST GREAT FLARE, a sacred natural event of the renewed world. Some clans say the Source itself chose them. Others say the Elder Gods blessed the eggs. A few say dragonkind arose because the world needed rulers strong enough to survive it. Ixthal is present only as a shadow in certain esoteric traditions.

[IN-WORLD] The Cultural Weaver proposed three levels of draconic knowledge:

  1. COMMON DRAGON BELIEF: The Nine Progenitors were born from the First Great Flare, a sacred mystery.
  2. ELDER LORE: Ixthal was somehow present at dragonkind's beginning and is owed silence, not worship.
  3. FORBIDDEN COUNCIL RECORD: Ixthal deliberately caused the Source Flare in violation of divine law.

[IN-WORLD] Only the oldest among the Council of 99, certain Gold archivists, Silver deep-memory keepers, and perhaps Purple heretics suspect the full truth.

[IN-WORLD] The Court of the Unanswered Sun in Ratharil remains, but its public meaning differs from its hidden meaning. To most dragons, it is not openly "Ixthal's temple." It is a silent court honoring the unknown force behind the First Great Flare. The inscription "He kindled us. He owes us nothing. Do not demand speech from the last throat of Arath" has a hidden referent only the initiated recognize. To the uninitiated, "he" may be interpreted as the Source, the First Progenitor, the Sun-Father, or a poetic mystery. To the initiated, it is Ixthal.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki preserves the public-myth/hidden-truth structure and adds: the full forbidden truth is preserved only in the Sealed Hall of First Flame, and even there it is incomplete. The Sealed Hall is dragon institutional architecture, not divine punishment.

12. WHAT UNICORNS KNOW
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Unicorns have no ancient written records and originally had no spoken language. Their memory is telepathic, emotional, and herd-patterned. They do not preserve origin stories as dragons do.

[IN-WORLD] Their oldest memory of creation is not a narrative. It is a sensation: light, fear, sudden self-awareness as many, the first awareness of "we," then grass, wind, running.

[IN-WORLD] They know Ixthal only as THE SILENT BRIGHTNESS or THE PAIN BEHIND THE SUN — not as a named god.

[IN-WORLD] Unicorn psionics may contain the most dangerous evidence of the Forbidden Kindling. Their collective memory-field might still hold an imprint of the Source Flare. If a powerful psionic scholar, dragon, or deity entered the ancestral herd-memory deeply enough, they might reconstruct the forbidden truth.

[IN-WORLD] Certain divine seals exist around unicorn origin-memory. NOT to oppress unicorns, but to prevent the method of their creation from being rediscovered.

[FLAG_FOR_REVIEW] The wiki preserves the existence of "divine seals" around unicorn origin-memory but does not specify whether they were placed BY THE GODS (knowing of Ixthal) or by Ixthal himself disguised as divine seals, or whether they emerged naturally from the Mythos's response to the Forbidden Kindling. The playground reads them as part of the divine cover-up; the wiki's "Ixthal has not been caught" framing requires either Ixthal-placed seals masquerading as divine work, or seals from a different cause. Open.

[IN-WORLD] Some modern unicorns influenced by individuality might resent this if they learn it. A species awakened into selfhood by a hidden crime, then prevented from fully remembering its own birth — that is fertile ground for conflict.

13. THE SEALED HALL OF FIRST FLAME
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] Ratharil's public city contains clan halls, markets, landing courts, archives, and the Ninety-Ninefold Hall. Beneath or behind the Council archives lies the SEALED HALL OF FIRST FLAME, where the oldest records of the Nine Progenitors are stored. Its stones are quarried from the same plateau but lined with gold-veined black basalt that does not echo. No sound leaves it. No flame burns naturally there except Source-lamps.

[IN-WORLD] Entry requires representation from multiple clans, so no single color controls the secret. At least one elder from each of three opposed clan blocs must consent.

[IN-WORLD] The records inside are layered:
  - Draconic scale-script
  - Psionic impressions learned from unicorns
  - Elemental seals
  - Divine redactions
  - Empty spaces where names were removed by order of the gods [FLAG_FOR_REVIEW: if the gods don't know, these redactions and empty spaces must be either older institutional self-censorship or the result of dragon-side discretion attributed to the gods]

[IN-WORLD] The deepest record is not a book but a protected memory-crystal called the SHARD OF FIRST SHELL. It contains a fragment of one of the original nine eggshells and records the magical signature of the first Source Flare. The Shard proves dragons were made. It does not teach how to make them. Lirra's seal allegedly prevents anyone from deriving the method from it. [FLAG_FOR_REVIEW: same caveat — if Lirra does not know Ixthal kindled dragons, the seal on the Shard may be a precautionary measure for any high-Source-resonance artifact, not a specifically-anti-Forbidden-Kindling seal.]

[IN-WORLD] A Ratharil oath for those who learn the truth:

  By scale, by Source, by the nine first shells,
  I shall not call the Kindler by plea or command.
  I shall not teach the forbidden flare.
  I shall not unmake my people with the truth of their making.
  I shall carry the coal and close my jaws.

14. THE TREATY OF RATHARIL AS METAPHYSICAL CONTAINMENT
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Treaty's population limit of 999,999 gains a deeper layer in this material.

[IN-WORLD] Publicly: limits dragon population to prevent overcrowding and catastrophic war.

[IN-WORLD] Secretly: may also serve another purpose — preventing dragonkind from growing so numerous that their Source-born nature destabilizes Eadras. Dragons were created by forbidden Source manipulation. Too many dragons may place strain on ley systems, ecosystems, magical weather, or the Weave itself. The population limit is not only social policy. It may be a containment measure.

[IN-WORLD] 999,999 is sacred, political, and metaphysical. Above that number, dragonkind does not merely overcrowd territory. Their collective magical mass begins to disturb the world. This explains the severity of Ratharil law. Illegal hatchings are not just unauthorized births. They are potential breaches in planetary equilibrium.

[IN-WORLD] A dragon would hate this if widely known. To learn that one's entire species is under a hidden containment regime would provoke rage. Some would accept it as necessary. Some would call it divine treachery. Some would demand the limit be broken. Some would ask what else the gods concealed.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Wiki keeps this as HYPOTHESIS ("may also serve as a metaphysical containment limit") — not confirmed, but preserved as possible truth.

15. THE GREAT MORAL KNOT
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Forbidden Kindling creates one of Eadras' greatest theological crises:

  If dragons and unicorns were born from a violation of divine law, then divine law is not the same as moral good.
  A forbidden act produced two great sapient races.
  But that does not make the act safe.

[IN-WORLD] This is the razor's edge of Ixthal's legacy.

[IN-WORLD] The Lorekeeper's final summary:

  Ixthal broke divine law.
  The Elder Gods were right to forbid such interference.
  Ixthal may also have been right that the world needed renewal.
  Dragons and unicorns are not illegitimate lives because their creation was unlawful.
  The gods concealed the truth to prevent repetition [WIKI_EXPANDS: Ixthal conceals the truth, the gods know nothing to conceal].
  The concealment itself creates future danger.

[IN-WORLD] This is not a contradiction to solve. It is the wound from which stories grow.

[IN-WORLD] Closing imagery from the council:

  A dragon soaring over Ratharil is both miracle and evidence.
  A unicorn herd crossing the plains is both restoration and trespass.
  A mortal praying for divine rescue stands beneath a heaven that loves them enough not to take their life away from them.
  And Ixthal remains silent, because if he spoke, someone would ask:
    "Was it worth it?"
  And no answer could be lawful.

16. THEOLOGICAL MOVEMENTS — THE FIVE RESPONSES
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] If the truth ever emerges, the council proposed five movements would arise:

THE OPEN HAND HERESY. A mortal religious movement arguing that the gods are morally obligated to intervene directly when suffering becomes too great. They cite Auralis, Thamior, and Ixthal as holier than the Elder Gods because they acted.

THE CLOSED HAND ORTHODOXY. A Vosian-Ioan doctrine insisting that divine restraint is the foundation of mortal freedom. Views Ixthal as tragic but guilty.

THE COALBEARERS. Secret Ixthalian sympathizers who believe breaking divine law may be justified to preserve future possibility — but only at unbearable cost.

THE ASHEN ACCUSERS. Those who condemn Ixthal for surviving Arath and later daring to create new peoples while his own remained dead.

THE GENESIS SEEKERS. The most dangerous: mages, cults, or rulers who seek the hidden method of Source Flare genesis, arguing that if Ixthal did it, so can they. Slogan: "Every race was forbidden once." Horribly persuasive. Horribly wrong.

[IN-WORLD] The Mage Architect on the Genesis Seekers: they may not understand that Ixthal was an ascended mortal deity with Arathi knowledge, divine-scale foresight, and unique access to Source regulation. A mortal imitation would likely produce mass mutation, planar rupture, or Source sickness.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] All five movements survive in canon as "Theological Responses" — but they exist as potential futures conditional on the truth becoming known, not as currently active sects. The wiki also adds the Ashen Accusers to the Three Last Lights material in Topic 1 as a sect that already exists in some form, even without the full truth being public.

================================================================
18. THE SORCERER CRUSADE EXCEPTION
================================================================

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The wiki adds an exception to the Doctrine the playground did not formulate: LARGE-SCALE DISRUPTION OF THE WEAVE CONSTITUTES A SYSTEMIC THREAT TO THE STABILITY OF THE MYTHOS AND JUSTIFIES DIRECT DIVINE INTERVENTION — particularly when the disruption is being caused by actions carried out in the gods' own names.

[IN-WORLD/WIKI] The canonical instance is the SORCERER CRUSADE. When the Crusade's killing of sorcerers reached a sufficient threshold, the Weave began to degrade due to loss of its living anchors. Lirra detected the disruption and recognized it as systemic rather than localized; she alerted the gods of the participating churches. The gods of those churches then MANIFESTED AVATARS PUBLICLY before high priests and the leaders of martial orders to immediately halt the persecution. The intervention was decisive and left no ambiguity regarding divine disapproval.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Avatar manifestation is far stronger than the Doctrine's normal permitted forms (omens, visions, blessed champions, limited miracles). It is reserved for cases where four conditions are met:

  1. A systemic-scale threat to the Mythos itself is unfolding (Weave degradation qualifies; ordinary mortal wars do not)
  2. The threat is being caused by actions framed as expressions of divine will (i.e., the gods' own institutional authority is being weaponized)
  3. The threat exceeds the capacity of mortal institutions to halt internally
  4. Continued bureaucratic delegation is failing to surface the situation to direct divine awareness in time

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The Sorcerer Crusade met all four. The avatar-intervention precedent is preserved in modern theology as the legitimate use-case for what would otherwise be a doctrinal violation.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] The Sorcerer Crusade also exposed a structural feature of the Doctrine: GODS ARE NOT CONTINUOUSLY AWARE OF ALL ACTIONS TAKEN IN THEIR NAME. Divine power is distributed through a structured bureaucratic system; routine prayers, rituals, and divine-magic requests are delegated to that system rather than processed by direct divine attention. Actions framed within accepted doctrine and routed through legitimate institutional authority can therefore escalate substantially before reaching the relevant deity's direct awareness. This delay was the gap the Black Hand's diversion operation exploited; it is an inherent risk of the Doctrine's reliance on bureaucratic delegation.

[WIKI_EXPANDS] Modern theological consensus following the Crusade: the gap cannot be closed without abandoning bureaucratic delegation altogether (impossible at the volume of mortal religious activity), but the avatar-intervention precedent ensures that catastrophic exploitation will eventually be detected and corrected — at the cost of whatever damage was done before the threshold was crossed.

19. DIVINE ENFORCEMENT TOOLS
================================================================

[IN-WORLD] The Doctrine is enforced by divine covenant, not Omniversal compulsion. The Elder Gods can punish violators, strip privileges, bind divine channels, seal knowledge, empower agents, or intervene collectively against an interfering deity. Because divine law can be broken, enforcement is political, magical, and theological.

[IN-WORLD] Enforcement tools include:

  - MANDATE SEALS placed on divine acts of genesis
  - SOURCE WARDS monitored by Lirra
  - DEATH WITNESSES of Shali and Shani, who mark attempts to seize entire peoples from death
  - IOA'S CENSURE, a sovereign condemnation that isolates a god from pantheon councils
  - VOSIAN REDACTION, lawful sealing of records

CANON ADDITIONS ESTABLISHED (from Element VIII — The Doctrine of the Unheld Hand)

  - The Elder Gods of Eadras uphold the DOCTRINE OF THE UNHELD HAND.
  - Gods may guide, bless, warn, empower, and inspire mortals, but must not directly seize control of mortal destiny.
  - Creating a new mortal race is considered direct divine interference on a grand scale.
  - Saving an entire race from extinction by direct divine action is also forbidden interference.
  - The EDICT OF CLOSED GENESIS is a specific law under this doctrine.
  - The Edict forbids:
    - Creating new sapient mortal races without Elder Pantheon mandate
    - Preserving doomed races from extinction by divine seizure
    - Manipulating the Source to kindle mortal life without authorization
  - Auralis and Thamior's failed attempts to save the Arathi helped inspire the formal doctrine, though their actions occurred during the catastrophe before the law was fully codified.
  - Ixthal violated the later formalized law by secretly creating dragons and unicorns through Source Flares.
  - The Elder Gods concealed Ixthal's role [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI: did not detect his role; the concealment is Ixthal's]
  - Permitted divine influence: clerical magic, omens, visions, limited miracles, empowered mortal champions, responses to extraplanar or divine violations.
  - Forbidden: macro-scale interference (creating races, preventing race-wide extinction, making civilizations indestructible, directly ruling mortal nations, eliminating mortality or natural struggle by fiat).
  - Mark, Melania, and Rand's creation of later progenitor humanoid races was lawful because it occurred by GENESIS BY MANDATE, with Elder Pantheon authorization.
  - Ixthal's violation remains one of the greatest divine secrets in Eadras [SUPERSEDED_BY_WIKI: one of the greatest UNKNOWN-TO-DIVINE-LAW secrets — not secret because gods conceal it, but secret because gods don't know it exists].
