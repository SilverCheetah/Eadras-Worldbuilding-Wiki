Velrothi Servant Race Naming Conventions
During the height of the Firethorn Empire, Velrothi servant races followed a structured naming system that clearly showed allegiance while allowing each race to retain its own cultural flavor.
General Structure
Standard Servant Form: [Given Name] ser [Velrothi House] [Own Clan / Workshop / Hold]
Elevated Form (Rare Honor): [Given Name] of House [Velrothi House] [Own Clan / Workshop / Hold]

1. Orug (Civilized Servants & Honorable Retainers)
Style: Formal, honorable, martial, and dutiful.
Phonetic Profile: Strong Velrothi influence with heavy, reliable consonants. Names feel solid and trustworthy.
Typical Sounds: Hard consonants (K, R, Th, G, V, B, D); steady vowels (a, o, ar, or).
Patterns: Two or three syllables, often ending in strong stops (-ak, -or, -ath, -ash).
Male Given Names: Varak, Thragor, Korvash, Narok, Grendar, Borvath
Female Given Names: Varaka, Thragara, Korvasha, Naroka, Grendra, Borvatha
Cognomens (Earned through honorable service): Oathkeeper, Steadfast, Ironshield, Trueblade, Longwatch
Examples:

Varak ser Velroth Stoneward
Thragara ser Narveth Oathbound
Korvash ser Korthal Ironshield


2. Grog / Grug (Technical Maintainers & Inventors)
Style: Clever, sharp, inventive, and slightly chaotic.
Phonetic Profile: Quick, technical, and mechanical-sounding with sharp edges.
Typical Sounds: Sharp consonants (Gr, Kr, X, Z, Tik, Rik, Phr, Grix); short, bright vowels.
Patterns: Short, punchy syllables. Often include “-ix”, “-zar”, “-rik”, or “-tik”.
Male Given Names: Grix, Vexor, Narkim, Tikzar, Rixul, Phrikon
Female Given Names: Grixa, Vexara, Narkima, Tikzara, Rixula, Phrikara
Cognomens (Invention / Skill related): Sparkfinger, Gearwhisper, Lockbreaker, Overclocker, Quickfix
Examples:

Grix ser Korthal Sparkworks
Vexara ser Valtharion Gearvault
Tikzar ser Kathor Deepcoil


3. Rathar (Scholarly Assistants & Researchers)
Style: Scholarly, precise, arcane, and formal.
Phonetic Profile: Heavy, resonant, and intellectual. Long, flowing but structured.
Typical Sounds: Velrothi classics (V, R, Th, K, X, L, N, Ph, Z); rich, deep vowels (ar, or, eth, ian).
Patterns: 3–4 syllables, often ending in -ar, -ion, -eth, -or. No cognomens — identity is strictly by House and Hold.
Male Given Names: Valthar, Rathandar, Korthalion, Pharzen, Thalorak, Xarvenor
Female Given Names: Valthara, Rathandri, Korthalia, Pharzene, Thalira, Xarvena
Examples:

Valthar of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault
Thalira of House Narveth, Deep Archive of Thalor
Pharzen of House Korthal, Runeward Hold


General Cultural Notes

The “ser [House]” structure emphasized loyalty to a specific Velrothi House.
The Own Clan / Workshop / Hold portion allowed servant races to maintain pride in their specific contributions.
Formal induction into a Velrothi House (dropping “ser”) was one of the highest honors a servant could receive.

After the banishment, the cultures were no longer servants and slowly dropped the "ser"
