## HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT

This document distills the **setting-wide principles** surfaced or sharpened during the Imma dialogue. Each principle has a `[DIAGNOSIS]` explaining the insight and a brief note on where else in canon it applies.

## 1. The Unselfconsciousness Principle

> *Cosmological work performed at scale by mortals is load-bearing precisely because it is unselfconscious. The moment the doing becomes deliberate, the doing is contaminated by the deliberation.*

`[DIAGNOSIS]` This principle organizes much of how the Mythos actually maintains itself. The beast-folk maintain the Veil through unconscious daily joy. The wizards sustain the Weave through unconscious daily practice. The peasants anchor reality through unconscious daily naming. In each case, *the function depends on the performer not knowing they are performing a function*.

The corollary: **the gods, the Order Sharanel, the Decree-keepers, and the Tome of Baldaran all keep specific secrets in service of preserving the unselfconsciousness**. This is not paternalism for its own sake; it is the cosmological mechanism by which the largest body of structural work gets done.

**Applies elsewhere**: every secret-from-mortals piece of canon (the Weave-secret, the Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa, the sealed Tome chapters, the Order Sharanel's compartmentalization) operates on this principle. Reframing any of these as *cruel withholding* misreads them; they are *load-bearing kindnesses*.

## 2. Conscious-Anchor vs Unconscious-Contribution

> *Cosmological labor on Eadras is performed in two complementary modes: a large diffuse population of unconscious contributors, and a small number of conscious anchors who deliberately carry bounded burdens of awareness.*

`[DIAGNOSIS]` Each load-bearing cosmological function has both layers running in parallel. The Weave's diffuse defensive coherence is the unconscious work of every magic user; the Weave's load-bearing junction-anchoring is the conscious work of the Windspeakers. The Veil's diffuse cultural renewal is the unconscious work of every Beast-Folk Circle; the Veil's institutional maintenance at the divine layer is the conscious work of Imma. The Decree's silence is preserved at the diffuse level by all gods who do not speak it, and at the conscious-anchor level by Shali, Shani, Ioa, and the Tome.

**The conscious anchors can bear conscious knowledge because their burden is bounded**. Unbounded awareness would flood and break them. Boundedness is what permits conscious load-bearing without leakage.

**Applies elsewhere**: the Katharim institution as conscious-anchor for the diffuse work of mortal grieving; the Order Sharanel as conscious-anchor for the Weave-secret; the Tome of Baldaran as conscious-anchor for the curated cosmological record.

## 3. Form Follows Self-Perception (Across All Tiers)

> *The spirits show in pure form what is true at every tier: the form of a being is a direct expression of the being's settled self-perception, and form changes when self-perception changes.*

`[DIAGNOSIS]` The wiki-canon spirits-page principle (form follows self-perception, with realm-of-origin and raw-power fixed) is now understood as the **purest demonstration of a principle operating at every cosmological tier**, not as a spirits-only mechanic.

For mortals: the gap between perception-and-form exists at the flesh-tier, but at the Mythos-tier — at the level of naming, observing, anchoring — the gap closes. Mortal naming-and-observing reshapes *what is real in the world*, even if it does not reshape the namer's own body.

For Ascended Mortal Gods: their form is anchored by their self-perception at the moment of Ascension. They retain mortal shape because changing self-perception would shift the form and degrade the office. This is why Baldaran is human-shaped, Aramet is Elfen-shaped, Ixthal is Arathi-shaped, and Imma is fox-folk-shaped.

**Applies elsewhere**: the Velrathi-architecture spell-lattice and bound wardspirits (a willingly-bound observer holding a referential lattice in attention); the Tome of Baldaran's appearing as a culturally-relevant document to whoever encounters it (form matching the perception of the reader); the dragons' single-Fire perception of magic (no internal distinction = no internal distinction in how the magic operates).

## 4. The Soul-Thread Continues in Both Directions

> *An Ascended Mortal God's later form can rest attention on an earlier soul-life of the same thread. The earlier life sometimes feels the attention. The soul is continuous in both directions.*

`[DIAGNOSIS]` The cycle of reincarnation is not strictly one-way. The Ascended end of a soul-thread can look back along the thread with deliberate warm attention, and the mortal lives at the other end can register the looking, however dimly. The receiving mortal often mistakes the attention for an Elder God's notice — frequently Sylph's, because the warm-amused-quiet-pride quality matches her register.

This produces a small but real piece of cosmological mechanic: **prior-life attention is one of the kindnesses available to Ascended Mortal Gods that the cycling lives cannot return in kind**. It functions as the Ascended's *backward-facing daily practice*, complementing whatever forward-facing portfolio they bear.

**Applies elsewhere**: any Ascended Mortal whose backstory the setting develops can be assumed to do this; the practice would explain the *moments of unexplained warmth or recognition* that mortals occasionally report at otherwise unremarkable times. The Tome of Baldaran can also rest curatorial attention on lives the Tome has recorded; this is the closest spirit-realm equivalent to soul-thread attention.

## 5. The Curator-Not-Speaker Loophole

> *The Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa forbids speaking; the Decree does not forbid the curated showing-of-a-record to a properly-chosen reader. The Tome of Baldaran is the cosmological loophole — the artifact that holds what the gods cannot tell, available to be read by those who can bear it.*

`[DIAGNOSIS]` This explains the Tome's structural role in the cosmology with greater precision than the wiki has previously articulated. The Tome is **not just an archive with curatorial discretion**; it is the **load-bearing vessel for the decreed-silent knowledge that some beings in the Mythos must occasionally access**.

Without the Tome, the Decree would either *be broken regularly* by gods who needed to discuss decreed matters with junior staff, or *would isolate the decreed-silent material so totally that institutional continuity would fail*. The Tome solves the problem by being neither a speaker nor an institutional bureau; it is *an artifact that may be read*. The reading does not violate the Decree because the Decree binds *speaking*, not *being-read-from*.

**Applies elsewhere**: any future cosmological-secret canon that must be accessible-but-not-speakable will route through the Tome's curatorial layer. The same logic explains why senior Velrathi archivists, certain Lirran clergy, and the deepest Katharim memory-priests are *permitted* to know things they may not *say*. The discipline is read-only-acknowledgment, not full disclosure.

## 6. Theological Position Produces Office Architecture

> *A people's answer to the question of weight produces both their cultural practice and the architectural shape of their divine office (or absence of office) at the Solarion level.*

`[DIAGNOSIS]` This is the **deep principle** that organizes why different peoples have different pantheon shapes. Each people's theological-position-on-weight produces a corresponding shape at the divine-bureaucratic level:

| People | Theological answer to weight | Divine office shape |
|---|---|---|
| Velrathi | Hold weight in architecture | Velrathi pantheon proper (Rand, Velveth, and others); Velveth's Office of Velrathi Affairs |
| Beast-Folk | Daily forgetting; laughter renews the Veil | Single small Imma office; no pantheon |
| Katharim | Hold the names forever | Katharim institutional priesthood + Shali/Shani interface |
| Orcs | Communal ritual grief; honor strength | Orcish honored-progenitor lineages; minimal active office |
| Trolls | The Great Balance | No divine office of any kind |
| Dragons | Grim satisfaction over hubris | Cindirith and the Nine Embers; dragon pantheon proper |
| Unicorns | Watchful sorrow | Three unicorn deities (Starwyn, Faylune, Valenor) |
| Dwarves / Halflings / Humans | Various | Various; not catalogued here |

**Applies elsewhere**: when developing a new people in the setting, the question to answer is *what is their theological position on the question of weight*. The pantheon shape will follow.

## 7. Solarion as Inhabited City

> *Solarion is a polity, not an abstraction. The divine corridors have streets, offices, neighbors, and ongoing silent working relationships between adjacent offices.*

`[DIAGNOSIS]` The wiki's existing solarion.md page treats Solarion as a settlement; this principle sharpens that into *Solarion as concretely inhabited working city with bilateral office-relationships*.

Specifically: divine offices are *neighbors*. Adjacent offices do *daily corridor business with each other* in the form of cross-signing, mutual acknowledgment, and the silent recognition of shared institutional history. The Game of the Gods occupies one part of Solarion; the Bureaucracy occupies another; and a great deal of working divine collegiality happens *in the corridors between offices, not in either of those two named spaces*.

**Applies elsewhere**: future Solarion-located scenes (any divine council, any priest's vision, any Bureau-routing-of-prayer story) should treat the divine geography as *populated and architecturally specific*. The street-level reality of Solarion is now canonical texture available to any future material.

## LIVE WIRES

- Are there other Ascended Mortal Gods whose offices line the same corridor as Imma's? What would a complete corridor-map of Solarion's divine main street look like?
- How does the conscious-anchor / unconscious-contribution distinction apply to the *Bureau spirits themselves*? Do junior spirit-clerks bear their work consciously, or is the Bureaucracy partly an unconscious-contribution layer too?
- If form follows self-perception even at the Ascended level, what happens to an Ascended Mortal God whose self-perception drifts significantly over the course of millennia? Does Baldaran look slightly different now than he did in the Third Age?
- The Tome-as-loophole principle implies *other artifacts* might exist in similar functional roles. Are there decreed-silent matters with their own analogous archive-artifacts that the wiki has not yet identified?
- The theological-position-produces-office principle predicts a *Goblin Ascended Mortal God* with some specific portfolio. What is that portfolio? Does it exist, or is it a gap to fill?
- Could the soul-thread-attention mechanic be exploited adversarially? Could an entity with the right kind of access *impersonate* an Ascended Mortal's backward-facing attention to manipulate a target mortal who feels the attention?
- The unselfconsciousness principle has dark applications: if institutional discipline relies on practitioners not knowing what they are doing, what happens when *the secret gets out at scale*? Is there a scenario in which mass-revelation degrades the Mythos's structural integrity by collapsing the unconscious-contribution layer?
