== Modern range (feral Titans) ==

Feral Titans range "wherever they want" -- they are NOT anchored to fixed
regions. They are mobile because their calorie demand forces them to keep
moving once a region's food sources are exhausted. A feral Titan in a region
is a transient threat: the region empties of accessible prey, the Titan moves
on. Frontier villages near a Titan's drift line are buried periodically until
the Titan passes through and onward.

== Genetic bottleneck ==

Most Titans died in the Demon War. Not all did, but the survivors were too few
and too narrowly drawn to preserve the breeding diversity of the pre-war
population. The pure-Titan stock that exists today -- both feral and
Gray-Matriarchy -- is the descendant of that bottleneck. It has never
recovered. The species is in slow-motion decline: devolution pressure drains
individuals into the Giant lineage and onward to Goliath, while the surviving
pure-Titan base cannot replenish itself through natural variation alone.

== Civilized Titans in the Gray Matriarchy ==

The exiled Tarran of the Gray Matriarchy DID keep Titan lineages with them
after the Great Banishment. They maintain a small, stable breeding population
of civilized Titans in their exile.

This population has itself lost most of its genetic diversity in the centuries
since the Banishment. The Gray Matriarchy sustains the population through
careful stewardship rather than natural variation.

The Gray Matriarchy USES these civilized Titans as instruments of terror
against their own commoners. Paraded through villages. Deployed against
rebellious districts. Held as a standing reminder to commoners of what the
Tarran can still field. This is one of the defining political facts of
Gray Matriarchy rule -- the exiled Tarran did not become kinder after their
exile; they doubled down on instruments of dominance.

== Sapience baseline and developmental disabilities ==

ALL Titans (civilized or feral) have fully sapient brains. Cognition is
fundamentally that of a person: a Titan can be as quick or as dull as any
human or any other sapient mind. There is no inherent cognitive limitation
to the species.

The complication is that a Titan child's nutritional needs are extreme. The
calorie demand of a ten-meter child outstrips what most civilizations can
sustain consistently -- even Tarran civilization at its height could not
always feed Titan children sufficiently. Even attentive lineages produce
adults whose minds are limited by what they did not eat as children.

Consequence: developmental disabilities are common among Titans of every
era. A fully-developed, fully-sapient adult Titan is rare and precious -- the
survivor of a lineage whose stewards managed to keep its young properly fed
all the way through growth.

The Gray Matriarchy's small stable breeding population is, among other things,
a deliberate practice of producing and protecting these uncommon individuals.

For feral Titans the picture is worse: chronic childhood under-feeding is
nearly universal among the wild population, meaning that even by feral-Titan
standards a cognitively-intact adult is uncommon. Most feral Titans are
sapient but developmentally damaged.
