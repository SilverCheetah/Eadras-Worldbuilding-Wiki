Orcs
Orcs are a hardy, nomadic people who dwell primarily on the vast grasslands beyond the southern mountain ranges. Descended from one of the three original humanoid races created by the sibling gods, they are divided between plains orcs, who follow great herds across the steppe, and city orcs, who live as master artisans and traders in humanoid cities across the known world, including human, elven, and dwarven settlements.
Despite the geographic distance, the two groups maintain strong cultural ties through a tradition of exchanging adolescents for several years so each may learn the ways of the other.
Orc society is built on practicality, mastery, and personal honor. They value skill above all else and express this through constant competition. Every craft, trade, and martial art becomes a contest judged by Grandmasters — those who have proven themselves so thoroughly that they retire from competition to teach and judge instead.
Each clan maintains a permanent clan-hold, usually located in nearby hills or mountainous terrain so the open plains can be reserved entirely for grazing herds. Most of the people spend the majority of the year following the herds. Leadership is merit-based: potential chieftains undergo years of testing and apprenticeship before taking power, and a wise chieftain steps down once they are no longer capable of performing the role.
The high point of orc life is the Great Gathering, held every three years. Nearly the entire population of every clan attends, leaving only a small guard behind. It is a time of trade, diplomacy, matchmaking, and above all, competition. Everything from blacksmithing to horsemanship to swordcraft is contested, with Grandmasters serving as judges.Though fearsome warriors when threatened, orcs are not a conquering people. Their honor is rooted not in domination of outsiders, but in self-mastery, protecting the clan, and proving superiority in skill.
