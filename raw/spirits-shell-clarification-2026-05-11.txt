Spirits — shell clarification (still put-on / take-off), 2026-05-11

(Session-capture appended to today's spirits umbrella canon and the
shell-terminology refinement. Josh clarified that shells, despite their
deeper-than-clothing mechanism, are still put-on / take-off and that
spirit identity persists across shell changes.)

== Clarification ==

A shell is still something the spirit PUTS ON AND TAKES OFF, like any
uniform. The deeper-than-clothing aspect is that while WORN, the shell
provides the full bundle (appearance + powers + responsibilities) and the
spirit functions as that kind of being. It is not a permanent
transformation. Remove the shell, and the spirit returns to the universal
true form, free of the previous role's powers and duties.

== Promotion: a shell upgrade, not a re-creation ==

A spirit's identity persists across shell changes. Promotion is administrative:
a spirit receives a new shell when reassigned upward in the institutional
hierarchy. A characteristic fey-hierarchy promotion notice reads:

  "Congratulations on your promotion, spirit. You have been promoted
   from Pixie to Brownie!"

Reading this canonically:
- The spirit is the same spirit (addressed as themselves)
- "From Pixie to Brownie" is shell-change, not species-change
- The reputation, work history, and standing accumulated under the
  pixie-shell travel with the spirit into the brownie role
- The new shell carries the powers and duties of brownie; the old
  pixie-shell goes back to the institutional channel
- The spirit is now "Brownie So-and-so, who used to be a fine Pixie"

The administrative continuity is identical in structure to a mortal
promotion at a bureaucracy: the same employee gets a new role, new
authority, new responsibilities, and a new set of badges/uniforms; the
employee themselves persists.

== What this clarifies in earlier canon ==

The morning's shell-terminology refinement said shells "fundamentally
change what the spirit is while they are wearing them" -- that phrasing
remains correct, but the WHILE qualifier is doing important work. Outside
the shell, in the back rooms, the spirit is just a glowing humanoid figure
again. The change is operative only while the shell is on.

Specifically:
- A nymph reassigned to a brownie shell is functionally a brownie while
  the brownie shell is on. The same spirit, the next morning, may go to a
  back room, take off the brownie shell, exist as their true form for the
  rest day, then return to the brownie shell for the next shift.
- A fey reassigned from a stream to a hearth functions as a different
  kind of being from the moment the new shell is donned -- different
  body, different powers, different duties, different place-binding --
  but the underlying spirit is the same individual.
- Career history and reputation are spirit-attached, not shell-attached.

== No additional contradictions ==

This clarification does not contradict any prior canon. It tightens the
phrasing on a particular point that I had over-emphasized in the
shell-terminology page: the "fundamentally changes what the spirit is"
clause is true but operative only WHILE the shell is worn, and the
spirit's continuous identity is preserved across shell changes.
