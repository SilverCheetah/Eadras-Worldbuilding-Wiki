# Spirits

**Summary**: *Spirits* are the native populations of the four non-mortal realms within the [[eadras-cosmology|Eadras Mythos]] — the [[seraphim|Seraphim]] of [[celestia|Celestia]], the [[devils|Devils]] of [[nyxar|Nyxar]], the **nature spirits (fey)** of the [[feywild|Feywild]], and the **dark spirits** of the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]. Mortals encounter them as wildly varied beings — angels, imps, dryads, wraiths, pit-fiends, sprites, banshees, and a hundred more named "types" — but the dramatic variety is largely an artefact of *uniforms*. The **true form** of a spirit is the same across all four realms; only the colour and brightness of its native glow distinguishes one realm's spirit from another.

## The universal true form

The **true form** of a spirit — the form that is displayed when the spirit is not wearing a shell — is **a direct reflection of self-perception**. The spirit looks like what the spirit perceives themselves to look like, *exactly*, with no intermediation, translation, or filtering between the perception and the form. Self-perception and true form are not two different things in a causal relationship; they are the same thing expressed in two registers. This is the deep cosmological mechanism that organises every variation discussed in the following subsections; it is the load-bearing principle of the spirits-canon.

Two features of the true form are *not* self-perception and remain fixed regardless of how a spirit thinks about themselves:

- **Glow colour** is determined by [[#Glow colour: realm of origin|realm of origin]] (see below).
- **Glow brightness** is determined by [[#Glow brightness: raw spiritual power|raw spiritual power]] (see below).

Everything else about the true form — body shape, facial features, gendered aspects, posture, the texture of the glow, the sense of presence the spirit projects — answers to the spirit's own settled self-image.

### The cosmological baseline default

A newly-created spirit, before any self-perception has had a chance to develop, manifests at the **cosmological baseline default** that all spirits share when their self-perception is at or near the starting point:

- A smooth, **nearly featureless, androgynous humanoid figure**
- Roughly mortal proportions
- Minimal facial details
- No hair
- A glow that emanates from the body itself

This baseline is what the vast majority of spirits look like in true form, because the vast majority of spirits — particularly those whose duty cycle never produces sustained mortal contact or other identity-shaping experience — never develop a self-perception that diverges from the default. The baseline is the spirit equivalent of a starting condition the spirit has not yet pressed against.

When a spirit's self-perception *does* shift — most documented case: the development of gendered aspects through long mortal-interaction work — the true form **shifts with it**, because the true form is the self-perception itself, made visible. There is no delay, no intermediation, no need for the spirit to *do* anything to update their form once their self-perception has settled into a new state; the form simply *is* the new perception. The form is identical across spirits whose self-perception is at the default; it diverges from the default for spirits whose self-perception has settled somewhere else. Most observable spirit-variation in [[back-rooms|Backroom]] settings is therefore variation in self-perception, not variation in underlying nature.

The form is otherwise identical whether the spirit is a junior clerk in the [[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal Bureaucracy]], a senior dispatched [[seraphim|Seraph]], a great fey lord, or a high-ranking dark spirit of the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]: institutional rank does not change true form (only shell). What changes is the spirit's self-perception, and (for the underlying spirit itself) the colour and brightness of its glow.

### Initial androgyny and chosen gendered aspects

The canonically-documented example of self-perception driving true form is **the development of gendered aspects**.

**All spirits begin androgynous** — that is, all spirits begin at the cosmological-baseline default described above, whose form is androgynous by virtue of being featureless-humanoid. Most spirits — particularly those whose duty cycle keeps them entirely within the spirit realms or in institutional roles that do not require sustained mortal interaction — remain androgynous for their whole institutional lives, because their self-perception never develops past the baseline.

Some spirits, however, **choose to take on aspects of gender**. The deeper mechanism: **gendered traits are an aspect of self-perception**. A spirit develops gendered identity by coming to perceive *themselves* as gendered — not by being assigned a gender, not by performing a gendered role for a job, not by being labelled gendered by mortals or by the bureaucracies. The change happens inside the spirit's own sense of who they are. Spirits whose duty cycle puts them in close, sustained interaction with mortals are the spirits in which gendered self-perception most commonly develops, because the mortal-facing work brings them into long proximity with gendered modes of being and that proximity *gives them the material* against which their own self-perception can shift. The mortal interaction is the **context**; the self-perception is the **mechanism**.

A Seraph who repeatedly dispatches to comfort grieving mortals may, over time, come to perceive herself as motherly — and once she perceives herself that way, she *is* that way at the true-form level. A devil whose long assignment puts him in regular contact with a particular mortal scholar may come to perceive himself as a particular kind of man, and *be* that. A fey who interacts with a single mortal community for centuries may settle into whichever gendered self-perception the community's vocabulary has made available to her. None of this is performance; all of it is identity.

This intersects cleanly with the [[observer-and-naming|observer-and-naming]] cosmological principle: in Eadras, what is perceived is part of how things are. A spirit who has settled into self-perceiving as gendered has, by that act of self-perception sustained over time, *become* gendered in their true form. Reality in this Mythos does not require an external observer for this kind of change — **the spirit's own observation of themselves is sufficient**. This is the same observer-driven mechanism that organises the rest of the Eadran cosmology, applied specifically to the spirit's relationship with their own form.

Gender is the documented case. The broader implication, since the true form *is* self-perception, is that **other forms of self-perception-derived variation are theoretically possible** — a spirit whose long experience or settled identity has shifted them in some non-gendered way (a particular bearing, an aspect of size or substance, a sensory characteristic) would show that shift in true form too, by the same mechanism. Gender is canonically documented because gendered self-perception is the most common form of self-perception-shift that mortal-facing duty produces. Other forms of self-perception-shift, and what they look like, are open canon.

The load-bearing point: **the chosen gendered aspect becomes part of the spirit's core identity, not a work-overlay shed when the shift ends.** When a spirit's self-perception settles into gender, the change is *real* and *permanent at the true-form level*. The androgynous default was the spirit's starting state; the gendered state is the spirit's new actual identity. There is no putting it back. A spirit who has settled into a feminine self-perception across centuries of mortal-interaction work *is* feminine, in true form, in the [[back-rooms|Backrooms]], at any moment when their shell is off and their identity is what shows.

The shell mediates the gendered identity *outward* in mortal-facing interaction — a uniform a feminine-identified spirit wears that shapes how mortals perceive that femininity through the role — but the shell **does not create the gender; the underlying spirit's self-perception does**. Personality is revealed through the shell, not produced by it. A spirit's true form is who they actually are at the level of self-perception, and once gendered aspects have settled into self-perception, those aspects are part of true form.

In the [[back-rooms|Backrooms]] (off-duty, shells removed), a spirit shows their *actual* true form. For the majority of spirits whose duty cycle has never produced sustained mortal contact, that means androgynous. For the minority who have adopted gendered aspects, that means showing those aspects in the Backrooms exactly as they show them anywhere else — because the aspects are not work clothing. A devil and a Seraph relaxing in a lounge in true form may look androgynous-and-androgynous, gendered-and-androgynous, or both-gendered, and any of those configurations is normal. The lounge does not see *off-duty erasure of gender* because gender was never on-duty work.

This is the canonical mechanism behind mortal religious iconography: the angels mortals describe as beautiful men, the demons mortals describe as seductive women, the dryads mortals describe as feminine and the satyrs mortals describe as masculine — these are all spirits whose long mortal-interaction histories have produced settled gendered self-perceptions that the spirits themselves carry, and that the shells those spirits wear express to mortal observers. The folk theology was reading the spirits accurately at the identity level; what folk theology got wrong was *categorising spirits by gender as a class property* rather than recognising that the gender belongs to the individual spirit's self-perception.

### Glow colour: realm of origin

The colour of a spirit's glow indicates which realm it comes from. The realm determines its nature; the nature determines (with rank) the uniforms available to it.

### Glow brightness: raw spiritual power

The **brightness** of a spirit's glow indicates its raw spiritual power. A junior celestial functionary glows softly enough to read by; a high-tier celestial of great seniority glows with a radiance that fills a corridor end to end. A senior infernal of pit-fiend rank carries a glow that scorches the air around it; a clerical imp glows like the embers of a banked fire.

Mortal scholars sometimes call this the *visible authority* of a spirit — the brighter the glow, the more inherent capacity for shaping outcomes the spirit holds. Brightness, however, **does not predict role**. A bright-glowing spirit assigned to a low-ranking role wears the uniform of that role; the underlying brightness is unchanged but the visible appearance is the uniform's appearance. A mortal scholar with the right craft to see through a uniform can therefore find the rare spectacle of a brilliantly-glowing spirit doing menial work — a politically meaningful event in the realm in question, and almost always a sign of disgrace, exile, or a deliberate institutional message.

## Shells

Spirits **almost never appear in their true form** in the mortal world, or in most areas of the spirit realms themselves. They are almost always wearing a *shell* — a physical and magical construct that **fundamentally changes what the spirit is** while they are wearing it.

*Shell* is the canonical term. *Uniform* is a useful shorthand — and in the bureaucratic realms ([[celestia|Celestia]] and [[nyxar|Nyxar]]) shells genuinely do operate as uniforms, with rank insignia and standardised institutional codes — but the mechanism is deeper than clothing. A spirit placed into a new shell does not merely *appear* to be a different type of being; they functionally *are* one. The role assignment is literal:

> *"You are now a nymph."*
> *"You are now a brownie."*
> *"You are now the genius loci of this forest."*

A shell is still something the spirit **puts on and takes off**, like any uniform — but it is a put-on/take-off that completely changes the spirit's functional identity for the duration. The spirit's underlying identity is continuous across shell changes; what changes is the shell they wear from that moment forward. A promotion notice in the fey hierarchies reads, characteristically:

> *"Congratulations on your promotion, spirit. You have been promoted from Pixie to Brownie!"*

The spirit is the same spirit, addressed as themselves. What changes is the shell they will wear in the new role: the old pixie-shell goes back to the appropriate institutional channel; the new brownie-shell is issued and donned; the reputation and career-history accumulated under the previous shell **travel with the spirit** into the next. A promoted brownie is *Brownie So-and-so, who used to be a fine pixie* — not a wholly new being.

While the shell is worn, the spirit takes on a complete bundle: a new appearance, new powers, *and* new responsibilities. The underlying spirit beneath the shell is unchanged in its true form (the glow remains the same colour and brightness), but **what the spirit can do, where it belongs, and how mortals perceive it** are all properties of the shell, not the spirit. While the shell is on, the nymph **functions** as a nymph in every operational sense — but it remains a shell that can be taken off at the end of the shift, in the back rooms, and a new one donned the next morning if the assignment has changed.

The "types" of spirits mortals catalogue — imps, wraiths, dryads, seraphs, banshees, pit-fiends, sprites, nymphs, brownies, shadow-stalkers, will-o'-the-wisps, the genii loci of named landscapes, the dramatic field-forms catalogued in every demonological and angelological text ever produced by mortals — are **not biological variations of the underlying spirits**. They are **shells** worn over the universal true form, each calibrated to mortal expectations and to institutional or place-bound role.

A spirit's shell:

- **Is a physical and magical construct**, not mere clothing. When donned it reshapes the body — adding mass, growing wings, sprouting horns or vines or shadow-trails, layering scales or feathers or bark or mist as the role specifies.
- **Carries the powers associated with the role.** A devil dispatched to tempt a mortal soul wears a shell that includes the relevant temptation-craft; the same devil reassigned to filing wears a shell without those powers.
- **Carries the responsibilities of the role.** A *genius loci* shell binds the spirit to its specific landscape and to the duties owed by that place's guardian. A nymph shell binds the spirit to its waters; a brownie shell to its household. The role, the powers, and the duty are a single bundle.
- **Encodes rank insignia** legible to other spirits of the same realm (and almost never legible to mortals). In bureaucratic realms, this insignia function is foregrounded — which is why *uniform* fits as shorthand there.
- **Reverses cleanly when removed.** The spirit underneath returns to the universal true form, freed of the shell's powers and responsibilities alike.

### Administration: two bureaucracies, four realms

The administrative layer — **shell-issuance, assignment, and promotion** — is not handled by each realm independently. Two senior institutional powers each cover two realms apiece, along the cosmological bright/dark axis:

- The **[[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]]** handles shell-administration across the **bright realms**: [[celestia|Celestia]] (its primary realm) and the [[feywild|Feywild]] (its secondary realm).
- The **[[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal Bureaucracy]]** handles shell-administration across the **dark realms**: [[nyxar|Nyxar]] (its primary realm) and the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] (its secondary realm).

Each bureaucracy issues both kinds of shell its scope requires, maintains both kinds of catalogue, and processes assignments and promotions across both realms. A fey-hierarchy promotion notice — *"Congratulations on your promotion, spirit. You have been promoted from Pixie to Brownie!"* — is therefore a Divine Bureaucracy document; the same document type processed by the same institution that issues seraphic dispatch-uniforms in [[celestia|Celestia]]. Likewise a Shadowdark reassignment is an Infernal Bureaucracy procedure, even though the spirit involved glows ultraviolet and not crimson.

**This is the administrative layer only.** Every realm has another political layer operating alongside the Bureaucracy — distinct from it, not subject to its authority, and entirely capable of creating intrigue and problems for it.

In the **primary realms** ([[celestia|Celestia]] and [[nyxar|Nyxar]]), the non-Bureaucratic political layer is the [[gods-of-eadras|gods]]. Elder Gods, Ascended Mortal Gods, and the senior infernal powers of [[nyxar|Nyxar]] sit *outside the Bureaucracy* — above it, beside it, with their own goals, desires, alliances, grudges, and political projects. The Bureaucracy nominally serves them — routing prayers, processing souls, enforcing doctrine — but it does not control them. A divine grudge, a sudden change of policy from a god who has decided to favour or disfavour a particular initiative, a quiet alliance between two thrones forming behind the Bureaucracy's back, a celestial mandate handed down with no warning and no explanation — these are non-Bureaucratic political acts happening in parallel to the routing and filing the workforce does day to day. The Bureaucracy in turn does what bureaucracies always do under capricious sovereigns: it manages, it routes around, it generates paperwork to explain why the new policy could not be implemented this quarter, and it complains in the back rooms.

In the **secondary realms** ([[feywild|Feywild]] and [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]), the non-Bureaucratic political layer is **interior governance** — fey courts, shadow councils, regional fiefs, local powers, and the political arrangements that vary place by place across each realm. This governance is **local and regional, not plane-wide**: there is no fey-wide council, no shadow-wide throne, no single authority unifying either realm. Each region runs itself.

What the Bureaucracies do *not* do is run the day-to-day politics of *any* realm. Which fey court decided a spirit deserves promotion, which regional Power signed off, which Elder God grew impatient enough with a delay that the relevant Bureau heads suddenly received a visit — those decisions happen entirely outside the Bureaucracy's purview. The Bureaucracy is invoked only when an administrative action (a shell-change, a routing-decision, a record-update) must formally be issued: the gods and the courts do the politics, the Bureaucracy does the paperwork.

A way to read the structure: the Bureaucracies are the **employment and credentialing** layer. The gods (in primary realms) and the local courts and councils (in secondary realms) are the **political and social** layer. They cooperate at handoff points — a promotion needs both a sponsor *and* a new shell — but they are different institutions doing different work, and neither has authority over the other's domain.

### Standardised shells vs. bespoke shells

The two shell modes map onto the primary-vs-secondary realm distinction inside each bureaucracy:

**Standardised shells** are the form used in each bureaucracy's primary realm ([[celestia|Celestia]] for the Divine, [[nyxar|Nyxar]] for the Infernal). They are issued from a code, organised by department and rank, mass-produced for the workforce. A pit-fiend shell is structurally similar to every other pit-fiend shell; a seraphic dispatch-uniform is structurally similar to every other dispatch-uniform of the same rank. The bureaucracies maintain their standardised catalogues the same way they maintain everything else — meticulously, with revisions tracked and audited. The *uniform* shorthand fits cleanly here: in the standardised-shell context, a shell **is** a uniform, with all the institutional rank-marker implications the word carries.

**Bespoke shells** are the form used in each bureaucracy's secondary realm ([[feywild|Feywild]] under the Divine, [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] under the Infernal). Bespoke shells are **bound to specific landscapes, hearths, streams, groves, or relationships**. A nymph is not just "a nymph"; she is the nymph of *this* stream. A brownie is not just "a brownie"; he is the brownie of *this* household. A *genius loci* is, by definition, the spirit of one specific named place. The shell binds the role, the place, and the relationship as a single unit, and reassignment changes all three together — a fey reassigned from a stream to a hearth functions as a different kind of being from the moment the new shell is donned, even though the underlying spirit is the same individual. For bespoke shells the *uniform* shorthand fits less well; a shell tied to one specific forest isn't a uniform so much as a *vessel* for the role-in-place.

Each bureaucracy therefore maintains **two qualitatively different shell catalogues** — a standardised one for its primary realm and a bespoke one for its secondary. Shells are bureaucracy-specific, not strictly realm-specific: a celestial shell and a fey shell are both Divine Bureaucracy products drawn from a single administrative tradition, even though their forms and bindings differ. What a shell from one bureaucracy cannot do is be worn under the other — a Divine shell cannot fit an Infernal spirit, and vice versa. The complete catalogue of named shell forms across both modes and both bureaucracies is open canon.

A consequence: **mortal demonological and angelological taxonomy is wrong about almost everything**. When a mortal text catalogues "barbed devils," "ice devils," "pit fiends," "wraiths," "dryads," and "banshees" as distinct species, it is doing exactly what would happen if a foreign visitor catalogued *the police, the firemen, the postal workers, and the soldiers* of a mortal nation as different species on the basis of their uniforms — except worse, because shells aren't just costumes. They are the literal functional identity of the wearer for the duration of the role.

## Back rooms

Spirits from all eight spirit realms share access to the [[back-rooms|Backrooms]], a **single universal off-duty layer** with hidden entrances distributed throughout each of the eight realms (Disney-service-entrance-style — discreet doors in regular institutional architecture, all leading to the same shared underground): shared lounges, locker rooms, dining areas serving mana-as-food, and serene parks in one consistent [[arathi|Arathi]] [[crystalmancy]]-and-[[magitek]] architectural vocabulary, where spirits shed their [[#Shells|shells]] and roles and interact as equals across cosmological divides their on-duty roles impose. **Not segregated by realm** — a celestial spirit and an infernal spirit, off-shift, use the same room. The Backrooms exist because **even spirits cannot remain on duty for eternity**, and because **Spirits are Spirits**: the universal Backroom layer is the visible expression of the principle that there is no fundamental difference between spirits no matter the role. See [[back-rooms]] for the full treatment.

The Backroom canon also formally names **the four elemental planes** as spirit realms — the Backrooms are accessed by all *eight* spirit realms' populations, including Elemental Fire, Water, Earth, and Air. The four non-mortal realms covered in detail elsewhere on this page (Celestia, Nyxar, Feywild, Shadowdark) are therefore *four of eight*, not the full set; the elemental spirits' glow colours are canonized in the table above, but their shell catalogues remain open canon (see [[back-rooms#Implications (downstream work)|Backrooms — Implications]]).

The elemental spirits are administered by the **Elemental Bureaucracy** — the third institutional bureaucracy of the spirit realms, parallel to the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]] (which administers Celestia + Feywild) and the [[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal Bureaucracy]] (which administers Nyxar + Shadowdark). The Elemental Bureaucracy's full internal structure, headquarters, leadership, and major bureau organisation are open canon, but the institutional fact of its existence — and its parity with the other two bureaucracies — is established.

### Cross-bureaucracy mobility

Spirits **can swap bureaucracies** — celestial to infernal, infernal to elemental, elemental to celestial, and so on. The transition **is not easy**: each bureaucracy issues its own shells, maintains its own promotion records, and has its own institutional culture; a spirit transferring across must surrender the prior realm's accumulated shell-history and start the new bureaucracy's records from a clean slate, with all the friction that implies. But it is *possible*. This is one of the cleanest cosmological expressions of the [[back-rooms#Spirits are Spirits|Spirits are Spirits]] principle: if spirits are fundamentally the same kind of being regardless of role or realm, then in principle any spirit can wear any bureaucracy's shells. The institutional difficulty of the swap is therefore a friction layer, not a fundamental barrier.

The specific procedural mechanics of a cross-bureaucracy transfer — who approves it, what the receiving bureaucracy requires, how long the transition takes, whether the spirit's glow colour shifts when their bureaucratic affiliation changes — are open canon.

The previously-documented per-realm rest-space names on this page (*the Dreadharrow locker rooms*, *the celestial rest-halls*, *the Feywild glade-clearings*, *the Shadowdark quiet rooms*) are now understood as **bureaucracy-internal vernacular** for the universal Backrooms — each workforce uses a folk-name for "the place we go off-shift" that references its home realm's geography metaphorically. The physical layer is shared.

## Distinctions

Spirits are not interchangeable with several adjacent categories of being, and the distinctions matter:

- **Spirits are not [[demons|Demons]].** Demons are chaos-bred war-forces from outside the [[mythos|Mythos]] proper, not native to any of the four spirit realms. They do not have the universal true form. They do not wear realm-issued uniforms. They do not, in any sense, work for the institutions. See [[devils]] for the longer treatment of the devil-versus-demon confusion that mortal religions almost universally make.
- **Spirits are not gods.** The [[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]] and Ascended Mortal Gods sit at the top of the institutional hierarchies that the spirits staff; they are categorically different beings. A Seraph does not become a god through promotion; a god does not become a spirit through demotion. The categories are distinct.
- **Spirits are not the engineered mortal races.** The [[velrathi|Velrathi]], [[tarans|Tarran]], [[elfen|Elfen]] and their engineered descendants are biological mortal beings who descended from progenitor lineages. They are not spirits and have nothing to do with the universal true form or the uniform mechanic.
- **Spirits are not [[archons|Archons]].** Archons serve the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]] and operate across the entire [[omniverse|Omniverse]], not within any single Mythos. Their presence in Eadras would be a cosmological event. Spirits are native to Eadras's realms specifically and are utterly bounded by them.

## Origin: how new spirits come into being

Spirits **cannot create life, and cannot create new spirits.** This is a hard cosmological constraint. No combination of two spirits, no Backroom-internal arrangement, no bureaucratic procedure, and no act of will by any spirit — however senior, however brightly-glowing, however institutionally powerful — can produce a new spirit. The capacity simply is not there. A spirit can shape its own identity (adopt a gendered aspect, accept a promotion, transfer between bureaucracies), but it cannot bring another spirit into being.

**New spirits come into being by the will of a god.** A divine act of will is the canonical mechanism by which a new spirit enters existence. The act is rare on cosmic timescales — the gods do not produce new spirits casually — and the specific procedural form of the act (whether the god speaks the spirit into being, shapes it from some pre-existing substrate, draws it from some divine reservoir, or simply *wills* it without any intermediate motion) is not preserved in open Tome chapters. What is preserved is the load-bearing constraint: **the gods make new spirits; spirits do not**.

This constraint distinguishes spirits sharply from the engineered mortal races: mortals reproduce biologically, generation after generation, with no divine involvement required after the original lineage was founded. The engineered races are *biologically* self-sustaining populations; the spirit populations are *cosmologically* self-sustaining only insofar as no spirits are being unmade — the gods top up the population through new creations whenever and however they choose.

The constraint also explains the relative stability of spirit-realm headcount across cosmic time. Spirits do not breed exponentially; their numbers grow only when a god wills it. The [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine]], [[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal]], and Elemental Bureaucracies all operate with workforces whose total size is — over long enough timescales — divinely-rate-limited rather than reproductively-driven. This is why bureaucratic over-staffing (see [[divine-bureaucracy#Size and the sinecure problem|Size and the sinecure problem]]) is the form the staffing imbalance takes: too many spirits assigned to too few real jobs, rather than chronic understaffing — the bureaucracies have what the gods have given them and arrange the work around that.

The corollary is also true: **the death or destruction of a spirit is correspondingly serious.** A spirit removed from existence cannot be replaced except by a fresh act of divine will. The bureaucracies treat irreversible damage to a spirit as a major matter for that reason. Routine spirit-removal mechanisms — promotion-out, retirement, reassignment — do not unmake the spirit; they shift its assignment. Actual destruction of a spirit is rare, theologically grave, and (in canon currently) not documented in any institutional procedural manual the wiki has recovered.

## Open canon

- **The full uniform catalogue** for each realm — the complete list of named "types" of celestial, infernal, fey, and shadowdark spirits, with the rank and department logic that maps each to a position in its realm's institutional order. Partial coverage exists in the [[devils]] page; the other three realms' codes are open canon.
- **The internal organisation of each bureaucracy's secondary-realm shell-administration.** The [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]] handles shell-issuance for the [[feywild|Feywild]] in addition to [[celestia|Celestia]]; the [[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal Bureaucracy]] handles shell-issuance for the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] in addition to [[nyxar|Nyxar]]. Whether the secondary-realm shell-administration is structured as a formal Department, an integrated sub-bureau, a parallel institutional column, or some other organisational form is open canon for both bureaucracies.
- **The structure of interior governance in the [[feywild|Feywild]] and [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]].** Both realms maintain local and regional governance distinct from the Bureaucratic shell-administration layer — fey courts, shadow councils, regional fiefs, and the political arrangements particular to each landscape. The specific institutions, the political map of regional powers, and the relationships between them (and with the administering Bureaucracy at the shell-handoff points) are open canon. What is established: there is *no* plane-wide authority in either realm; whatever governance exists, it is local or regional.
- **Whether spirits rotate between primary and secondary realms within the same bureaucracy.** Whether a Seraph can be reassigned to a fey-shell in the Feywild, or a Devil to a wraith-shell in the Shadowdark — or whether spirits are bound to one realm for their working lives — is open canon. Existing canon establishes only that shells are bureaucracy-specific (a Divine shell cannot be worn by an Infernal spirit); it does not establish whether shell modes are exchangeable within a bureaucracy's scope.
- ~~**Spirit reproduction and population.**~~ **RESOLVED 2026-05-14.** Spirits cannot create life or new spirits; new spirits come into being only by the will of a god. The procedural form of the divine act (spoken into being / shaped from substrate / drawn from reservoir / pure will) and the question of *which* gods can perform the act, with what frequency, are not yet documented. See *Origin: how new spirits come into being* above.
- **Which gods can create new spirits, and how often.** The constraint says *by the will of a god* (singular) but does not specify whether any god, only the gods who created the relevant realm, only specific portfolio-holders, or some other restriction applies. Open canon.
- **Cross-realm interaction.** Whether spirits of one realm can recognise spirits of another in true form (presumably yes; the glow colour is unambiguous), whether they have established protocols for inter-realm encounters, and what the institutional politics of such encounters look like — open canon.
- **What happens to a spirit kept in uniform too long.** The cultural framing says the institutions take it seriously; the specific mechanism of the degradation (does the uniform fuse to the true form? does the underlying spirit weaken? does the glow dim?) is open canon.
