# Racial Naming Conventions

**Summary**: The canonical phonetic, structural, and formal-address conventions for the major peoples of Eadras. Each progenitor race has its own characteristic style; descendant races inherit echoes of the progenitor pattern. This page is the master reference; individual race pages link here rather than duplicate.

**Sources**: Naming conventions.txt; Velrothi Servant Race Naming Conventions.txt; Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

## [[arathi|Arathi]] Naming

The naming canon of the pre-Cataclysm Arathi civilization is **Ancient Near Eastern in flavor** — an evocative blend of Akkadian, Egyptian, and Hebrew, drawing on theophoric and arcane compound forms appropriate to a literate, civic-magical culture that wrote on clay tablets, temple walls, royal seals, and astronomical ledgers. (source: Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt)

In their own tongue the civilization called itself the **Nerathi** — a word from this same naming convention. *Arathi* is the modern Eadran exonym (a post-Cataclysm outsider name worn smooth by ten million years of foreign tongues); *Nerathi* is the endonym preserved in their own surviving inscriptions. *Arathi* serves as both singular and plural form — there is no separate plural. The Tome and most modern scholarship use *Arathi*. (source: notes on various topics 2.txt)

Arathi names should sound *older, more solemn, and more imperial* than later races' names. **Avoid** elven/modern-fantasy-flowery forms (Aelarion, Vaelithas, Thalorien) — they are wrong for the Arathi.

### Phonetic feel

- **Vowels**: a, e, i, u (with long forms: ā, ē, ī, ū)
- **Consonants**: m, n, r, l, s, sh, z, k, q, t, d, b, p, h
- **Emphatic-looking consonants** (for scholarly transliteration feel): q, ṣ, ṭ, ḫ
- **Common endings**: *-el, -il, -um, -u, -a, -at, -et, -ia, -aya, -nadin, -iddin, -nāṣir, -eṭir, -mukin, -shar*

### Name structures

**A. Theophoric / arcane** — invokes sacred or magical forces.

`[Sacred Element] + [Verb / Role]`

Examples: *Ilu-nāṣir* ("Keeper of the Sacred Light"), *Ra-iddin* ("Given by Radiance"), *Nabu-mukin* ("Wisdom Established"), *Qiru-shakir* ("Crystal-Tuner"), *Nēr-eṭir* ("Opener of the Conduit"), *Ašar-kalib* ("Measurer of Order"), *Etem-nāṣir* ("Keeper of Memory"), *Bāb-eṭir* ("Gate-Opener"), *Kudur-mukin* ("Establisher of the Seal"), *Kudur-eṭir* ("Seal-Opener" — see [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]]).

**B. Royal / civic** — authority + civic role.

Examples: *Bēl-Ašar* ("Lord of Order"), *Adan-Kudur* ("Lord of the Seal"), *Nabu-Etem* ("Record of Memory"), *Ilum-Mat* ("Light of Truth"), *Kha-Nēru* ("Manifestation of the Conduit"), *Amun-Qiru* ("Hidden Crystal"), *Ra-Shar-Mukin* ("Radiance Establishes Order").

**C. Descent** — lineage constructions, used especially among high families, civic offices, or master crystalmancers.

`[Name] ben/bat [Parent/Lineage]` or `[Name] aplu ša [House/Office]`

Examples: *Ilu-nāṣir ben Qiru-mukin*, *Nabu-etem bat Ra-iddin*, *Ašar-kalib aplu ša Bāb-Nēru*, *Nabu-mukin, aplu ša Etem-Rā* ("heir of the House of Memory-Radiance").

**D. Egyptian-feeling beloved names** — common among artists, singers, temple-functionaries, memory-keepers.

`Meri-[Sacred Element]` or `[Name]-meri-[Element]`

Examples: *Meri-Rā*, *Meri-Qiru*, *Meri-Amun*, *Nabu-meri-Etem*, *Kha-meri-Nēru*, *Ašar-meri-Ilum*.

### Example Arathi names

**Masculine-leaning**: Ilu-nāṣir, Ra-iddin, Nabu-mukin, Bēl-Qiru, Adan-Kudur, Nēr-eṭir, Qiru-shakir, Ašar-kalib, Kudur-nāṣir, Etem-mukin, Zēru-nadin, Kha-Nēru, Amun-Qiru, Ra-Shar-Mukin, Nabu-Etem.

**Feminine-leaning**: Ilat-nāṣira, Ra-nadītu, Nabītu-mukin, Bēlat-Qiru, Adanat-Kudur, Nērat-eṭira, Qirat-shakira, Ašarat-kaliba, Kudurat-nāṣira, Etemat-mukina, Zērat-nadīta, Khat-Nēru, Amunet-Qiru, Meri-Rā, Meri-Qirat.

**Neutral / scholarly / civic**: Nabu-Kalib, Etem-Nāṣir, Bāb-Eṭir, Qiru-Mukin, Nēr-Shakir, Ašar-Mat, Ilum-Kudur, Zēr-Etem, Kha-Ilum, Amun-Shar.

### High names and full formal address

The Arathi often had layered names — appropriate for an advanced civic-magical culture. The full formal address combined personal name + house/lineage + professional or civic title:

> *Ra-iddin, aplu ša Qiru-Mukin, kalib nēri of the Third Terminus*

This multi-tier structure is bureaucratic, priestly, and imperial — fit for the Arathi.

### Canonical Arathi-named characters

- **[[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]]** ("Seal-Opener") — the great archmage and Spear-creator known to later folk memory as **Ssar'veth**. The Kudur-eṭir form is canonical; "Ssar'veth" is the corrupted form that survived in post-Cataclysm tradition.
- **[[three-last-lights|The Three Last Lights]]** — their Arathi mortal names are **Ra-shakir, Ilum-nāṣir, and Ašar-eṭir**. They are best known by their post-ascension divine names (Auralis, Thamior, [[ixthal|Ixthal]]).

## Progenitor Races

### [[elfen|Elfen]] (created by [[the-three|Mark]])

- **Style**: Elegant, melodic, flowing, nature-inspired with a strong **Hawaiian phonetic influence** — soft consonants, abundant vowels, rhythmic and musical.
- **Common sounds**: l, m, n, r, s, h, w, th + lots of vowels.
- **Feminine**: always end in **-a** (or a diphthong ending in an "a" sound — *-ia, -ua, -ea*).
- **Masculine**: always end in **-o** (or a diphthong ending in an "o" sound — *-io, -uo, -eo*).
- **Examples (F)**: *Elenara, Liraena, Thalira, Miralua, Kaelana, Aulani.*
- **Examples (M)**: *Thalor, Elorio, Silvaro, Kaelo.*

### [[tarans|Taran]] (created by [[the-three|Melania]])

- **Style**: Strong, martial, disciplined, authoritative, solid.
- **Common sounds**: Hard consonants — t, k, r, th, d, v.
- **Patterns**: Short, powerful syllables. Common opening syllables: **Tar-, Kor-, Val-, Thor-, Dar-, Mar-**.
- **Common endings**: *-an, -ath, -or, -ak, -un.*
- **Examples**: *Korvath, Valdrak, Tharun, Daramis, Markor, Tarveth, Tarran.*

### [[velrathi|Velrathi]] (created by [[the-three|Rand]])

- **Style**: Arcane, scholarly, mysterious, slightly gothic and esoteric. Feels ancient and weighty.
- **Common sounds**: Complex clusters with v, r, th, x, k, n, z.
- **Patterns**: Common opening syllables: **Vel-, Roth-, Vex-, Nar-, Kath-, Zor-, Mel-, Vor-**.
- **Common endings**: *-othi, -ar, -eth, -un, -or, -ith, -and, -ex.*
- **Examples**: *[[velena|Velena]], [[xarvion|Xarvion]], Rothena, Narveth, Kathira, Vexara, Rothandar, Melaroth.*

(The canonical names of [[velena|Velena]] and [[xarvion|Xarvion]] are listed by the convention itself — both fit the Velrathi pattern explicitly.)

#### Velrathi title morphology

Velrathi high titles operate on a **two-axis morphology** that produces a clean four-form paradigm for every titular stem. The morphology is setting-wide canon and applies systematically to all Velrathi high titles.

**Gender axis**

- **-ar** suffix = masculine, reigning
- **-eth** suffix = feminine, reigning in her own right

**Diminutive axis (heir-by-lineage, not-yet-invested)**

- **-i** suffix appended to either gendered form = "child of [title]" / heir-presumptive / not-yet-invested holder of the lineage-title

For the senior territorial / princely / ducal stem **Vorand-**:

| Form | Composition | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Vorandar** | Vorand- + -ar | Prince/Duke (masculine), reigning |
| **Vorandeth** | Vorand- + -eth | Princess/Duchess (feminine), reigning in her own right |
| **Vorandari** | Vorand- + -ar + -i | Prince's son / male heir-by-lineage, not yet invested |
| **Vorandethi** | Vorand- + -eth + -i | Princess's daughter / female heir-by-lineage, not yet invested |

The canonical example is **Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion** — *Vorandethi* is the title (second-eldest daughter of the reigning [[velveth#Mortal life|Vorandar Narand]] of the Northern Monarchy), *Kathira* the personal name, *Spellsinger* the earned cognomen, *Valtharion* the House.

The morphology extends to other Velrathi high-title stems with their own stems (magisterial, conclave-presidential, military, scholarly). The full Velrathi title-stem catalogue is open canon; the *morphology itself* is fixed. The resonance between the divine name **[[velveth|Velveth]]** and the feminine-reigning suffix *-eth* — *the goddess's name grammatically echoes the form of feminine reigning authority* — is a load-bearing piece of Velrathi liturgical recognition.

## [[halflings|Halfling]] Naming

### Core structure

- **Given name** — practical and sturdy: *Daric, Thomen, Garrick, Miraeth, Alden, Brenna.*
- **Clan name** — usually ends in *-ric, -bar, -well, -den, -field, -carver*: *Deepbar, Stonewell, Rootcarver, Hillric.*

### The cognomen system

When a halfling feels The Change and takes to the road, they earn **cognomens** — descriptive honorifics granted by others based on notable deeds, personality, or events during their travels.

- **Cognomens are always given by others — never self-chosen.**
- A halfling can accumulate many cognomens over a lifetime of travel.
- When introducing themselves formally while traveling, they list their most relevant or prestigious cognomens.
- Upon returning home after The Change, most halflings set their cognomens aside (though family and close friends may still use them affectionately).
- A well-traveled halfling might eventually carry 4–7 cognomens, though they usually only use 1–3 at a time depending on context.

### Common ways cognomens are earned

- **Surviving a dangerous journey** — *Farwalker, Stormrider, Riftcrosser*
- **Skill in trade or negotiation** — *Brightcoin, Fairdeal, Longbarter*
- **Helping a community** — *Rootfinder, Bridgewarden, Hearthfriend*
- **Notable personality or quirk** — *Quicklaugh, Silentstep, Twicebold*

### Formal address

- **Settled phase**: *Thomen of Deepbar Hillric*
- **Traveling phase**: *Thomen Farwalker, Stormrider of Deepbar Hillric* — or simply *Thomen Farwalker Stormrider* among those who know them.

### Flavor

Cognomens are a source of quiet pride. When two traveling halflings meet, they often exchange cognomens as a form of respect — much like warriors comparing battle scars. A halfling with many respected cognomens is treated with deference even by taller races.

## Mitan Naming (and inherited [[dwarves|Shada'din / Dwarven]] echoes)

The [[dwarves|Shada'din (Dwarves)]] are the modern descendants of the Mitan; their naming traditions are softened echoes of the Mitan original below.

### Overall style

Heavy, resonant, and mineral. Mitan names feel like they were carved from stone or forged in deep fires. Names often evoke metals, ores, gemstones, or the deep earth itself.

### Sounds and patterns

- **Hard, heavy consonants**: K, D, R, Th, G, B, M, N, V.
- **Deep vowel sounds**: long *o*, *u*, *ar*, *or*.
- **Length**: 2–3 syllables, often compound or ending in strong stops.
- **Tone**: ancient, durable, slightly martial.

### Given names

- **Iron-themed**: *Korthun, Durak, Tharok, Gromnir, Borak, Valdrak.*
- **Stone/Gem-themed**: *Granthar, Obsid, Flintar, Basalt, Onyxar, Rubar.*
- **Metal-themed**: *Adamar, Mithrak, Steelorn, Copperak, Ironthar.*
- **Female (slightly softer but still heavy)**: *Duraka, Tharona, Gromira, Valdris, Kortha, Granthara.*
- **Female gem/mineral**: *Emeraldis, Sapphira, Onyxia, Rubara, Amethystar.*

### Cognomens (earned titles)

Like halflings, Mitans earn cognomens through deeds — especially in battle or mining. Always granted by others. Common Mitan cognomens (heavy and mineral themed): *Deepvein, Stonebreaker, Ironfist, Riftcarver, Obsidion, Graniteheart, Mithralforge, Bloodvein, Underlord, Cavemaster.*

### Clan names

Mineral or metal inspired: *Deepvein, Ironvein, Stoneforge, Obsidian, Granitehold, Mithralcore, Rubark, Basaltar, Underforge.*

### Formal address

- **Settled / Clan phase**: *Korthun of Clan Deepvein.*
- **With cognomen** (especially honored warriors / miners): *Korthun Stonebreaker of Clan Deepvein* — or *Korthun Stonebreaker, Riftcarver of Clan Deepvein.*

### Flavor

Mitan names feel like they belong underground — heavy, resonant, and built to last. A Mitan with many cognomens is treated with great respect, as each title represents proven endurance in the deep places. Modern dwarves still carry echoes of this heavy naming tradition, though theirs has softened slightly over time.

## Velrathi Servant Race Naming

The Velrathi House system itself **formed during the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] era** (~18,000 to ~10,000 years ago), as a lineage/patronage structure layered on top of the older Velrathi [[velrathi#Culture|Conclave]] governance. (User direction 2026-05-01: *"Velrathi houses formed during the empire."*) **All Velrathi are associated with a House.** (User direction 2026-05-01.) The engineered servant races [[orugi|Orug]] and [[grugi|Grug]] — already centuries to millennia old by the Empire's founding — were sworn to specific Velrathi Houses, and a **structured naming system** developed that simultaneously displayed allegiance to a House and preserved each servant race's cultural flavor. (source: Velrothi Servant Race Naming Conventions.txt)

The [[rathari|Rathar]] are a special case: they were Velrathi-engineered as a servant-scholar race **and** they had their own self-governing House system in parallel — analogous to **Oxford University colleges** within the broader Velrathi academic "university." (User direction 2026-05-01.) Each Rathar served a Velrathi House in their day-to-day work AND was a fellow of a Rathar House (their College — see [[rathari#The Ten Houses|the Ten Ancient Rathar Houses]]). Their formal address **combines both** in a single string: `[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] of [Rathar House], [Hold]`. (User direction 2026-05-01.) The "ser" particle also applies to Orug and Grug servants, but those races do not have a College system and therefore do not have the "of [House]" elevation — see below.

### General structure

- **Standard Orug / Grug servant form**: `[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] [Own Clan / Workshop]`
- **Rathar combined form** (Empire-era full): `[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] of [Rathar House], [Hold]` — the Rathar uniquely have both a Velrathi master House (servant status) AND a Rathar House (College / fellow status), and display both. (User direction 2026-05-01.)
- **Elevated Orug / Grug form** (rare honor — formal induction into a Velrathi House): `[Given Name] of House [Velrathi House] [Own Clan / Workshop]` — drops the "ser" particle entirely.

The "**ser**" particle marks the servant's loyalty to a specific Velrathi House. For Orug and Grug, formal induction into the Velrathi House was the highest honor and dropped "ser" entirely. The Rathar already had "of [Rathar House]" as part of their standard address (their College membership), so for them "ser" never disappeared in the Empire-era form — it sat alongside the College designation.

### What the parts mean

- **Given name** — the servant's personal name in the phonetic style of their race.
- **Velrathi House** — the House the servant or their lineage was sworn to. Many Velrathi Houses overlap with the [[rathari#The Ten Houses|Ten Ancient Rathar Houses]] (Korthal, Narveth, Valtharion, etc.); other Velrathi Houses appear in canon examples (Velroth, Kathor) but are not in the Rathar Ten — see *Velrathi Houses vs. Rathar Houses*, below.
- **Own Clan / Workshop / Hold** — the servant's specific contribution structure: an Orug honor-clan, a Grug workshop, a Rathar Hold. Preserves servant pride in their own work.

### After the Banishment

After the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] 10,000 years ago, the servant cultures were no longer servants. They **slowly dropped the "ser"** as the social structure that gave it meaning fell away. (source: Velrothi Servant Race Naming Conventions.txt)

For Orug and Grug, this means modern descendants ([[orcs|Orcs]], [[gnomes|Gnomes]], [[goblins|Goblins]]) typically don't carry the "ser" construction. Echoes of it may survive in ceremonial or historical contexts.

For the surviving sealed-Hold Rathar (House Valtharion, House Archivault, House Voidscribe — see [[rathari#Modern status]]), the **"ser [Velrathi House]"** portion of the combined address dropped because their Velrathi-House service ended. What survives is the College half — `[Given Name] of House [Rathar House], [Hold]`. The canonical surviving-Rathar examples in *Naming conventions.txt* (e.g. *Valthar of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault*) reflect this post-Banishment short form, not the Empire-era full form.

### Velrathi Houses vs. Rathar Houses — two separate systems

The Velrathi and the Rathar maintain **two parallel and entirely distinct House systems**. (User direction 2026-05-01: *"Rathar houses are not Velrathi houses."*)

- **Velrathi Houses** are the institution to which all Velrathi belong, and to which Orug and Grug servants were sworn under the "ser" particle. Canonical examples include *Velroth, Kathor, Korthal, Narveth, Valtharion* (full roster not yet documented). The system formed during the Firethorn Empire era.
- **Rathar Houses** are the [[rathari#The Ten Houses|Ten Ancient Rathar Houses]] — Rathar institutions only, with their own scholarly specialties and Holds.

Some names appear in both rosters (Korthal, Narveth, Valtharion). **A Velrathi House Korthal and a Rathar House Korthal are different organizations that happen to share a name** — not the same House from two angles. Other Velrathi Houses (Velroth, Kathor) have no Rathar counterpart at all.

### 1. [[orugi|Orug]] — Civilized Servants and Honorable Retainers

- **Style**: Formal, honorable, martial, dutiful.
- **Phonetic profile**: Strong Velrathi influence with heavy, reliable consonants. Names feel solid and trustworthy.
- **Common sounds**: Hard consonants (K, R, Th, G, V, B, D); steady vowels (a, o, ar, or).
- **Patterns**: Two or three syllables, often ending in strong stops (*-ak, -or, -ath, -ash*).
- **Male given names**: *Varak, Thragor, Korvash, Narok, Grendar, Borvath.*
- **Female given names**: *Varaka, Thragara, Korvasha, Naroka, Grendra, Borvatha.*
- **Cognomens (earned through honorable service)**: *Oathkeeper, Steadfast, Ironshield, Trueblade, Longwatch.*
- **Examples**:
  - *Varak ser Velroth Stoneward*
  - *Thragara ser Narveth Oathbound*
  - *Korvash ser Korthal Ironshield*

### 2. [[grugi|Grug]] — Technical Maintainers and Inventors

- **Style**: Clever, sharp, inventive, slightly chaotic.
- **Phonetic profile**: Quick, technical, mechanical-sounding with sharp edges.
- **Common sounds**: Sharp consonants (Gr, Kr, X, Z, Tik, Rik, Phr, Grix); short, bright vowels.
- **Patterns**: Short, punchy syllables. Often include *-ix, -zar, -rik, -tik.*
- **Male given names**: *Grix, Vexor, Narkim, Tikzar, Rixul, Phrikon.*
- **Female given names**: *Grixa, Vexara, Narkima, Tikzara, Rixula, Phrikara.*
- **Cognomens (invention/skill related)**: *Sparkfinger, Gearwhisper, Lockbreaker, Overclocker, Quickfix.*
- **Examples**:
  - *Grix ser Korthal Sparkworks*
  - *Vexara ser Valtharion Gearvault*
  - *Tikzar ser Kathor Deepcoil*

### 3. [[rathari|Rathar]] — Scholarly Assistants and Researchers

The Rathar are a special case: they were Velrathi-engineered servants **and** organized their own self-governing **House (College) system** in parallel — see [[rathari#The Oxford-college model|the Oxford-college model]]. Their formal address **combines both**: `[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] of [Rathar House], [Hold]`. (User direction 2026-05-01.) Empire-era examples:

- *Valthar ser Korthal of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault*
- *Thalira ser Valtharion of House Narveth, Deep Archive of Thalor*
- *Pharzen ser Velroth of House Korthal, Runeward Hold*

They do not use cognomens; identity is strictly by Velrathi House service and Rathar House + Hold. See the [[#rathar-naming|Rathar Naming]] section below for full details.

## [[rathari|Rathar]] Naming

### Overall style

Scholarly, precise, arcane, and resonant with stone and hidden knowledge. Names feel formal, ancient, and intellectual — as if spoken in vast crystal-lit underground halls.

### Core rules

- Rathar identity is defined first by **House** (there are only **10 ancient Rathar Houses**, distinct from the Velrathi House system — see [[rathari#The Ten Houses]]).
- Second by **Hold** — the specific underground enclave, archive-city, or scholarly fortress the Rathar belongs to.
- **No cognomens.** Names are strictly formal and tied to lineage and location.

#### Singular vs. plural

The race name has distinct singular and plural forms (analogous to *Arathi* / *Arath*, where the bare form refers to the people collectively):

- **Singular:** *Rathar* — *"a Rathar scholar entered the Hold."*
- **Plural:** *Rathari* — *"the Rathari of House Valtharion preserved the deepest archives."*

(User direction 2026-05-02.) Older raw sources sometimes use *Rathar* for both — that is acceptable for short reference but the distinction is canonical going forward.

### Given names

- **Male**: *Valthar, Rathandar, Korthalion, Pharzen, Thalorak, Xarvenor, Narvethian, Lithor, Archivon, Zenthir.*
- **Female**: *Valthara, Rathandri, Korthalia, Pharzene, Thalira, Xarvena, Narvethia, Lithara, Archivara, Zenthira.*

### The Ten Ancient Houses

See [[rathari#The Ten Houses]] for the canonical specialties of each House. The list itself: **House Valtharion, House Korthal, House Narveth, House Pharzen, House Lithorium, House Deepcrystal, House Runewarden, House Archivault, House Voidscribe, House Echoheart**.

### Holds (underground enclaves / archive-cities)

Examples: *The Ninth Crystal Vault, The Deep Archive of Thalor, Stone Memory Hold, Riftward Citadel, The Obsidian Library, Underforge Sanctum.*

### Formal address

**Empire-era full form** (combining servant status + College membership):

**[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] of [Rathar House], [Hold Identifier]** — for example:

- *Valthar ser Korthal of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault*
- *Thalira ser Valtharion of House Narveth, Deep Archive of Thalor*
- *Pharzen ser Velroth of House Korthal, Runeward Hold*

**Post-Banishment short form** (used by surviving sealed-Hold Rathar today):

**[Given Name] of House [Rathar House], [Hold Identifier]** — the "ser [Velrathi House]" portion drops because the Velrathi House service ended. Examples:

- *Valthar of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault (Sealed)*
- *Thalira of House Archivault, Deep Memory Hold*
- *Narvethian of House Voidscribe, Riftward Sanctum*

### Everyday usage

- **Among other Rathar**: shortened to *Given Name + Rathar House* — e.g. *Valthar Valtharion*.
- **With outsiders**: full formal name, for respect and clarity.
- **In scholarly correspondence**: signed with House and Hold.

### Modern usage

After the Demon War, the Rathar as a distinct people are nearly extinct. Surviving Rathar use the post-Banishment short form among themselves — but most outsiders never hear it. See [[rathari#Modern status]].

## [[goblins|Goblin]] Naming — the rough sibling branch

Goblin names are **Grug names made shorter, meaner, funnier, and more accident-prone** — clipped, dented, and turned into names that sound like they were *shouted during a fire*. (source: Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt)

### Sound traits

- Hard consonants
- Doubled letters (Grizz, Tikk, Vexx)
- Clipped endings
- Comic violence
- Harsh nicknames
- Earned **insult-cognomens**

**Common endings**: *-ix, -ik, -ikk, -zak, -zog, -ruk, -tik, -nix, -grix, -x.*

## [[gnomes|Gnomish]] Naming — the precise sibling branch (with a major historical arc)

Gnomish naming has **two distinct historical layers** divided by the moment **Inki opened the First [[arathi|Arathi]] Vault**. (source: Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt)

## Pre-Vault Gnomish

Before the First Vault, gnomish names were still recognizably **Grugic but smaller, neater, and more precise** — sharp but not savage, clever but not grand. Tool-bench names; practical, domestic, craft-focused. **Not yet Arathi-influenced** — pre-vault gnomish names should not use Arathi-style forms, vault imagery, crystal terms, or Babylonian grandeur.

**Sound traits**: Grugic roots softened.

| Grug Root | Pre-Vault Gnomish |
|---|---|
| Grix | Grixel, Grixi, Grixen |
| Vexor | Vexin, Vexel |
| Narkim | Narik, Narikel |
| Tikzar | Tikzar, Tiksel, Tizari |
| Rixul | Rixil, Rixel |
| Phrikon | Phrikem, Phrinel |

**Common endings**: *-i, -in, -el, -em, -ik, -il, -a, -ara, -ima, -ula, -ela.*

**Pre-vault given names — masculine/neutral**: *Grixi, Grixel, Grixen, Vexin, Vexel, Vexorin, Narik, Narkim, Narikel, Tikzar, Tizari, Tiksel, Tazrik, Rixil, Rixel, Rixul, Phrikon, Phrikem, Phrinel, Inki, Inkrik, Tinkar, Zarik, Mextil, Krixen, Briktel, Zintik, Rikven.*

**Pre-vault given names — feminine**: *Grixa, Grixina, Grixela, Vexara, Vexina, Vexela, Narkima, Narika, Narkela, Tikzara, Tizara, Tiksela, Rixula, Rixela, Rixina, Phrikara, Phrika, Phrina, Inkara, Inkima, Tinkara, Zarika, Mextila, Krixela, Brikta, Zintika, Rikvena.*

**Pre-vault cognomens** — practical craft titles in Common: *Quickfix, Sparkfinger, Gearwhisper, Locklistener, Hinge-Eye, Springhand, Finefinger, Smallgear, Truefile, Needlewit, Latchfriend, Copperthumb, Kettlewise, Wirewise, Tinkerhand, Measuremark, Brightscrew, Softclick, Clocknurse, Cogmender, Keyturner, Underlatch, Littlehammer, Threadgear, Boltsetter, Crankwise, Ratchet-Ear.*

**Examples (pre-vault)**: *Inki Quickfix, Grixel Locklistener, Vexin Hinge-Eye, Narik Finefinger, Tikzar Measuremark, Rixil Springhand, Phrikem Gearwhisper, Grixina Kettlewise, Vexara Copperthumb, Narkima Softclick, Tizara Latchfriend, Rixela Clocknurse, Phrika Truefile.*

This is what gnomes sounded like before they touched Arathi history.

### Post-Vault Gnomish — Arathi-Babylonian prestige style

After [[inki|Inki]] opens the First Vault, gnomish names **change dramatically**. Gnomes begin imitating recovered Arathi naming forms — half translation, half reverent mispronunciation, half cultural reinvention. *(Gnomes insist the arithmetic permits three halves.)*

Names become longer, more formal, more ancient-sounding, and more tablet-like. Cognomens are no longer translated directly into Common — they take on Arathi-style elements (Bāb, Eš, Qir, Zēr, Nēr, Etem, Kudur, Mukin, Nāṣir, Iddin, Eṭir).

A pre-vault name like *Narik Locklistener* becomes formally *Nariqu-Bāb-Nāṣir* — "Narik, Guardian of the Gate."

**Important distinction**: post-vault gnomish names are **NOT true Arathi names** — they are gnomish reconstructions from recovered tablets, manuals, and lab inventories. Gnomes recovered only ~5% of Arathi technology and culture, and their Arathi-style names are sometimes technically wrong but always reverent. (See [[#arathi-naming|Arathi Naming]] above for the genuine Arathi style.)

| Element | Gnomish-Arathi usage |
|---|---|
| **Bāb / Bab** | gate, sealed door, vault-door |
| **Eš / Esh** | vault, sanctum, hidden chamber |
| **Qir / Qiru** | crystal, cut stone, harmonic stone |
| **Zēr / Zeru** | seed, preserved life, inheritance |
| **Nēr / Neru** | conduit, light-road, flowing power |
| **Etem** | echo, memory, recording |
| **Kudur** | seal, boundary, protected tablet |
| **Ilum / Elum** | light, shining source, sacred radiance |
| **Šar / Shar** | order, regulation, correct arrangement |
| **Mukin** | establisher, one who makes firm |
| **Nāṣir** | guardian, keeper, preserver |
| **Iddin** | given, granted, received |
| **Eṭir** | opener, rescuer, releaser |
| **Shakir** | tuner, one who sets in harmony |
| **Kalib** | measurer, tester, calibrator |
| **Rabu** | great, masterful, senior |
| **Aplu** | child, heir, apprentice-line |
| **Belu** | master, possessor, lord of a craft |

### Post-vault structure (one gnome may have two or three names)

- **Everyday name** — short and Grugic-derived. *Grixel, Vexin, Narik, Tikzar, Rixil, Phrikem, Tizara, Rixela.*
- **Formal name** — Arathi-Babylonian style: `[Arathicized Given Name] + [Vault/Craft Compound]`. Examples: *Nariqu-Bāb-Nāṣir, Tikzāru-Nēr-Mukin, Rixilu-Qir-Shakir, Vexāra-Zēr-Iddin.*
- **Full guild or ritual name** — `[Given Name] + [Arathi-style Cognomen] + [Bench/Lineage/Host Affiliation]`. Examples: *Nariqu-Bāb-Nāṣir, aplu of the Copper Bench*; *Tikzāru-Nēr-Mukin, of the Dwarven Deep-Forge Compact*; *Vexāra-Zēr-Iddin, keeper of the First Seeds*; *Rixilu-Qir-Shakir, under the Third Vault Measure.*

Because gnomes have no cities of their own, these affiliations refer to **benches, workshops, host cultures, compacts, or vault lineages** rather than homeland.

### The arc

> *Grugic spark → pre-vault craft → Arathi-vault grandeur.*

The arc of gnomish naming is the arc of gnomish civilization itself: a clever scavenging people who, after Inki opened the First Vault, found themselves reverently dressing their workshop names in the language of a dead empire.
