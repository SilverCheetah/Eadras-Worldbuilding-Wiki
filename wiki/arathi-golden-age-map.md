# Eadras — Golden Age Map

**Summary**: An illuminated [[arathi|Arathi]] cartographic masterpiece depicting the world of Eadras at the absolute peak of Arathi civilization, **roughly a generation before [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] raised the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]** — created in the classic Arathi style with glowing crystal inlays, gold leaf, and living ley-line illumination, centered on [[altea|Altea]] as the radiant *Heart of the Conduits*.

**Sources**: Eadras – Golden Age Map.txt and Eadras – Golden Age Map.png (raw/, 2026-04-30)

**Last updated**: 2026-04-30

---

![[Eadras Golden Age Map.png]]
*An Arathi cartographic masterpiece from the late Golden Age, showing the world of Eadras roughly a generation before the Cataclysm. Altea sits at the center; the [[magic-in-eadras|conduit ends]] and surface ley-roads radiate to numbered termini around the world.*

## What it depicts

This is a **late Golden Age** map — produced *roughly a generation* (decades) before [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir's]] use of the Spear, which means it shows Eadras at the height of [[arathi|Arathi]] civilization, on the eve of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] that would erase nearly all of it. The map predates:

- The destruction of [[altea|Altea]] (still depicted as a radiant capital at the world's heart)
- The sundering of the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]] into three continents
- The rise of [[arathyn]] from the Worldbreaker's impact wound
- The subsequent plate tectonics that drifted the planetary surface relative to the conduits

The world it depicts is the **70%-land Eadras** that existed before the Cataclysm flipped the planet's water-to-land ratio.

## Style — Arathi cartography

The map is a worked example of the Arathi illuminated cartographic style:

- **Glowing crystal inlays** as legend points and key features
- **Gold leaf** detail across the cartouche, border, and major architectural icons
- **Living ley-line illumination** — the conduit and ley-road lines themselves glow with stored Eldritch
- **Arathi-script borders** running around the cartouche edges as decoration and provenance
- A **compass rose** with the cardinals marked in crystalline inlay

The combination of artistic illumination with functional magical infrastructure (the actual conduit lines on the map are *live* with stored Eldritch) is typical of Arathi work, where ornament and operation are inseparable.

## Geography depicted

### The center

- **[[altea|Altea]]** — the capital, depicted as a radiant city at the world's geographic center. Epithet: *"Heart of the Conduits."*
- **Altean Plains** — the surface plain immediately surrounding Altea, on top of the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]] continental upland.

### Termini (numbered fortress-cities at conduit ends)

The map labels conduit-end fortress-cities as numbered Termini. Eleven are named in the cartographer's notes (with two more unnamed implied by "and others"):

- **Northwatch**
- **Frosthold**
- **Skyreach**
- **Sunspire**
- **Dawnward**
- **Emergate**
- **Starfall**
- **Southwatch**
- **Stoneward**
- **Westwatch**
- **Stormpear**

These are pre-Cataclysm fortress-citadels at the surface ends of conduits radiating from Altea. Most or all were destroyed when the Cataclysm shattered the Arathi conduit infrastructure and shifted the planetary surface; ruins of some may persist in the modern world (see [[arathi|Arathi]] for what remains).

### Major regions

- **The Haelings** and **Cloudhelm Mountains** — frozen northern range
- **Veridian Wilds** and **Evergreen Vale**
- **Eldrim Forest**
- **Golden Savannas** and **Aurelian Plains**
- **Sea of Glass**, **Sea of Mirrors**, **Sea of Origins**
- **Kharosian Waste**, **Crystal Wastes**, and **Obsidian Highlands**
- **Thalorian Range** and **Sea of Giants**

(source: Eadras – Golden Age Map.txt)

These regions are pre-Cataclysm. They were the surface geography of the Altean Highlands and the surrounding world before the Worldbreaker. Some surface features at these names *may* persist today — modern peoples are unlikely to know the original Arathi names for them — and others were obliterated entirely. Mapping the modern continents onto these old names is open canon (the user is developing post-Cataclysm map snapshots iteratively; see project memory).

## Errata — the map's text and image may not reflect canon precisely

Both the cartographer's accompanying text and the map's visual depiction contain elements that **do not match established canon**. Canon takes precedence; treat the map as a useful reference for naming and for the general shape of pre-Cataclysm Eadras, but not as authoritative on the structural facts below.

### Conduits — canon is **seven, not thirteen**

The map text says: *"From Altea radiate thirteen major Crystal Conduits."* **Canon: there are seven conduits / fourteen exits** — each conduit pierces the world like a pole, producing two surface exits. See [[magic-in-eadras]] and [[order-sharanel]]. (User confirmation 2026-04-30: *"7 conduits, 14 exits."*)

The map's *visual* depiction may show more than seven radiating lines from Altea. Some of those lines should be read as **ley roads and ancient highways** (which the map text correctly mentions separately as pre-Cataclysm transit infrastructure linking cities), but the visual may not cleanly distinguish them from the true conduits. *The map may not have this indicated correctly.* When the map's depiction conflicts with the seven-conduit canon, canon wins.

### Verdant Scar — canon is **antipodal to Altea**

The map text says: *"the cataclysm that would shatter the continent, birth the Verdant Scar where Altea once stood."* **Canon: the Verdant Scar grew over [[arathi|Arath-Veyr]] (the inland Arathi receiving array) at the antipodal point from Altea — the opposite end of one conduit, on the opposite side of the planet.** Altea's own site became [[arathyn]] from the Worldbreaker impact, not the Verdant Scar. (User confirmation 2026-04-30: *"antipodal."*)

The map's visual may also not indicate the Verdant Scar's antipodal position correctly. Same precedence: canon wins.

### Bottom line

The map is **canonical as a record of pre-Cataclysm geography and named places** — Altea, the Termini, the regions, the Arathi cartographic style. It is **not authoritative** on the structural facts of conduit count or Verdant Scar location, where its text and possibly its visual lines diverge from canon.

## Common usage

A canonical caption for the map, suitable for citation in other contexts:

> *Map of Eadras during the Golden Age of the Arathi Empire, showing Altea, Heart of the Conduits, at the world's center, and the named Termini at conduit ends and along the ley-road network.*

## Major errata (user direction 2026-05-01)

The map is **not entirely accurate** — it is a best-guess reconstruction of pre-Cataclysm geography. Two specific errata go beyond the textual claims already flagged:

- **The depicted geography is approximate**, not authoritative. Specific shorelines, mountain ranges, and Terminus positions are reconstructions, and where they conflict with other canon, other canon takes precedence.
- **The Termini and region names are not the names the Arathi used.** They were **assigned by later civilizations** — post-Cataclysm scholars, surviving non-Arathi peoples, modern cartographers — attempting to label sites whose original Arathi names were lost. The actual Arathi names for Northwatch, Frosthold, Skyreach, Sunspire, Dawnward, Emergate, Starfall, Southwatch, Stoneward, Westwatch, Stormpear, and the regions are not preserved in any current source.

This treatment is consistent with the Cataclysm's total annihilation of Arathi records: nothing survived to fix the original toponymy. The Golden Age Map is therefore valuable as **an inherited cartographic tradition** but is not — and was never claimed in canon to be — a primary Arathi artifact.

## What's resolved

- **All named Termini were destroyed in the Cataclysm.** No Terminus magitek network survived. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

## Open canon

- The original Arathi names of the Termini and regions — most likely permanently lost.
- How the map's pre-Cataclysm regions correspond to modern continents — pending the iterative plate-tectonics map work; see [[eadras-tectonic-history]].
- Whether any Termini carried specific cultural roles (military outposts, trade hubs, observatories) beyond being conduit-end fortress-cities.
