# Deep Vaults

**Summary**: The Deep Vaults are secure ancient repositories built by the [[arathi|Arathi]] before the Cataclysm. **Thirteen are known to exist on Eadras**: six have been opened and seven remain sealed. **One additional vault is known to exist on the red moon ([[daj]])**. They are believed to contain pristine [[magitek]], stored Eldritch crystals, and lost Arathi knowledge.

**Sources**: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt; notes on various topics 2.txt; Grok Chat 5-8-26.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-08

## Function

The Vaults were Arathi-era secure repositories. Their canonical contents — across all twelve, broadly and with variation — are believed to include:

- **Pristine magitek** preserved against millions of years of decay
- **Stored Eldritch crystals** holding pre-Cataclysm magical charge or knowledge
- **Lost Arathi knowledge** in whatever recording media the builders chose

What survived inside any specific vault depends on the vault. The opened five are the only ones whose contents have been even partially observed; the sealed seven (and the Daj vault) are speculative.

## The opened six

### The Lost Primer vault (Velrathi-Rathari, early High Firethorn)

The earliest known vault opening — by a **joint Velrathi-Rathari expedition in the early High Firethorn Era** (well before the Demon War). The expedition recovered a foundational [[arathi|Arathi]] **primer on [[crystalmancy]] and [[magitek]]**: low-to-mid-level instructional material whose extrapolation seeded both the Velrathi and Rathari magitek/crystalmancy traditions across the High Firethorn. The vault was overrun and **emptied by demons in the Demon War**. Modern scholars cannot relocate the site, and no fragment of the Primer itself has survived. **Grug attendants on the original expedition kept meticulous notes that they passed down to their child races, the [[gnomes]] and [[goblins]]** — the canonical reason for the gnomish racial obsession with vault-archaeology. See [[lost-primer]].

### Darkhoof's vault

The unicorn [[unicorns#Darkhoof|Darkhoof]] opened one of the Vaults during his career. This is the same Darkhoof who later wielded the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]; the vault opening predates his Spear use.

The vault contained a **construct workshop and a near-complete personality matrix** that Darkhoof encountered there. (source: notes on various topics 2.txt) The matrix was a [[arathi|Nerathi]] crystalmind — preserved across the ten million years since the Cataclysm — sufficiently intact to constitute a functional sapient pattern. What Darkhoof did with the encounter, what the matrix knew, and whether the pattern persists today are open canon. The construct workshop itself is one of the most important surviving Nerathi facilities, and is the strongest direct line of intact Arathi-era construct technique to any post-Cataclysm civilization. Modern gnomish [[golems|golem-craft]] traditions recovering Arathi crystal minds may trace partly to information that flowed outward from this vault, though the chain of custody is undocumented.

### The three gnome openings

Three Vaults have been opened by **[[gnomes|gnomes]]** in separate operations. Gnome aptitude for [[magitek|magitek]] reverse-engineering and gnome scholarly persistence make them the natural fit for vault-opening work; whether the three openings were coordinated efforts or independent finds is undefined.

### The undersea opening

One **undersea** Vault has been opened. The opener is not yet specified in canon; the [[merfolk|Five Modern Oceanic Races]] (and the rogue [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]] / [[sahuagin|Sahuagin]] branches) are the natural candidates given the location, but no attribution is fixed.

## The seven sealed

Six of the seven sealed Vaults are **underwater**. Their specific locations and identities are open canon. They share, presumably, the deep-ocean preservation conditions of the rest of the Arathalassic and post-Cataclysm seafloor — pressure, darkness, and the layered ruins of the drowned 70%-land world.

The seventh sealed Vault is the only one on dry land: the **[[obsidian-sanctum|Obsidian Sanctum]]**, deep within the Scorched Waste of [[varkhul|Varkhul]]. It is sealed by environment rather than by guardian — the Scorched Waste, the [[turkorcs|Turkorcs]], the [[dragonkin|dragonkin]], and the [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] form four overlapping layers that have kept it untouched. See [[obsidian-sanctum]].

## The Daj vault

A **thirteenth Vault** is known to exist on the red moon, [[daj|Daj]]. This is **off-Eadras** and is counted separately from the twelve. Its physical accessibility is essentially zero for ordinary mortal effort; reaching Daj at all is a feat of high magic or divine cooperation.

The fact that Arathi placed a Vault on a moon — and which moon, and whether other moons hold further unrecorded vaults — is one of the more striking implications of this inventory. See [[daj]] and [[four-moons-of-eadras]].

## Open canon

- **Specific locations** of the five opened vaults and the seven sealed ones.
- **Specific contents** recovered from the opened vaults (Darkhoof's: construct workshop + near-complete personality matrix; the others: still undocumented).
- **Identities** of the three gnome openers and the undersea opener.
- **Why the Arathi placed a vault on Daj** — and whether further off-world vaults exist on the other three moons.
- **Total count of vaults that ever existed** — the "12 known" framing leaves space for unknowns.
- **Standard vault construction and warding** — whether all twelve share design features.
