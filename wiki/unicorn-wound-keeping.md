# Unicorn Wound-Keeping

**Summary**: **Wound-Keeping** is the [[unicorns|unicorn]] herd-memory discipline that preserves recognition of wounded places, dangerous [[arathi-geo-substrate|substrate]], and old magical harm â€” *not* for use, but as warning. The doctrine treats certain categories of knowledge as **hazard rather than asset**: the wound is remembered *so it is not repeated*, not so it can be reactivated. Wound-Keeping is one of the [[unicorns#The Herd is the Truth|five memory layers]] in the herd-mind, and is the layer most often translated into mortal institutional language by [[herd-listeners|Herd-Listeners]].

**Sources**: unicorns-01/02/03 (2026-05-15 ingest from Elder Gods chat 1.txt).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-15.

## Knowledge treated as hazard

Most mortal traditions treat knowledge as *asset* â€” something to acquire, accumulate, deploy, monetise, defend, and pass on. Unicorn Wound-Keeping is built on the opposite premise: **some knowledge is hazard**. The wound is held because forgetting it would cause its repetition; the wound is *not* held because it would be useful to recreate.

The discipline therefore has an unusual epistemic structure:

- **Recognition** is taught
- **Avoidance** is taught
- **Reconstruction is not taught**

A unicorn herd may know that a specific Arathi war-substrate site is dangerous, and may know what the wrongness of that site *feels* like, and may know what avoidance behaviour is appropriate â€” without knowing how the war-substrate was made, what it does in detail, or how an opener might defeat its defences.

This is not ignorance. It is **deliberate non-reconstruction**. The herd has chosen to remember in a way that prevents reactivation rather than enabling it.

> ***"Wound-Keeping is not anti-knowledge in a simple sense. It is knowledge treated as hazard."***

## What is wound-kept

Categories of memory typically maintained in the Wound-Keeping layer:

- **[[arathi-geo-substrate|Wounded Arathi geo-substrate]] sites** â€” particularly Oru-Sha War-Substrate, Black-Vault-class substrate, and Context-Bound systems with destructive failure modes
- **[[deep-vaults|Dangerous vaults]]** â€” not specific opening procedures, but recognition that opening is unwise
- **Old-world magical residues** â€” places where pre-Cataclysm magical infrastructure still produces effects modern peoples should not interact with
- **Places where land and substrate still suffer** â€” wound-marks that have not healed and where mortal presence might disturb the healing
- **Patterns of consequence from past exploration** â€” when the herd has watched mortals open something and observed the result, the pattern is wound-kept for future Herd-Listener warning
- **Taboos against certain forms of activation** â€” specific procedures that the herd remembers as having gone catastrophically wrong somewhere

The catalog is non-exhaustive. Each herd's Wound-Keeping is shaped by what *that herd has witnessed*. Different herds maintain different wound-records, sometimes overlapping but never identical.

## How wound-keeping works in herd-memory

Wound-kept memory is **distinct from herd-deep memory** in its handling:

- **Herd-deep memory** is collective continuity â€” the shared substrate every unicorn carries
- **Wound-kept memory** is held *somewhat apart* â€” accessible to the herd but *flagged* as hazardous
- Returning [[unicorns#Modern unicorn life patterns|Seasonal Strayers]] who carry new wound-information may have their experiences wound-kept rather than fully integrated, particularly if the experience is corrupting or destabilising
- The [[circling-of-the-returned|Circling of the Returned]] is partly a wound-kept-quarantine ritual: experiences that arrive at the herd's edge are tested for whether they should enter full herd-memory or be held in the Wound-Kept layer

The result is that wound-kept memory **carries a quality of warning** alongside its content. A unicorn who consults wound-kept memory feels *both* the memory *and* the flag that says *this is held because it should not happen again*.

## Mortal applications

[[herd-listeners|Herd-Listeners]] are the principal translation mechanism between Wound-Keeping and mortal institutions. A Herd-Listener consulted about a particular [[deep-vaults|vault]] receives the herd's wound-kept assessment of the site and translates it into language the [[vault-concordat|Concordat]] can use.

Specific institutional applications:

- **Concordat vault classification** â€” Wound-Kept warnings inform Red and Black Vault designations
- **[[woundwatch|Woundwatch]] patrol routing** â€” herds adjacent to Demonfall Marches share Wound-Kept memory with Woundwatch about specific demonic-residue sites
- **[[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] research caution** â€” Sharanel scholars studying ancient magic consult Herd-Listeners before activating recovered substrates
- **[[druids|Druid]] judgment proceedings** â€” where Druid grounds intersect Wound-Kept territory, herds may convey relevant warnings
- **Mortal civic planning** â€” settlement, agriculture, and infrastructure decisions in unicorn-adjacent regions sometimes route through Herd-Listener consultation

## Limits of the discipline

Wound-Keeping is **not infallible**:

- The herd may **not have witnessed** a particular wound â€” if no unicorn was present, no wound-kept memory exists
- The herd may have **forgotten over deep time** â€” Wound-Kept memory persists but is not eternal; very old wounds may have faded
- The herd may **misrecognise** â€” a feeling of wrongness can come from sources other than what the herd assumed
- The herd may **be deceived** â€” the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] is presumed to have attempted to confuse Wound-Kept signatures, though documented cases are rare
- The Herd-Listener may **mistranslate** â€” the translation is craft, and craft can fail

The discipline's strength is not infallibility but **bias toward caution**. A Wound-Kept warning may be wrong, but it almost never produces false-negatives in the direction of *this is safe when it is not*. The error mode is over-caution, not under-caution.

## Relation to dragon memory

[[dragons|Dragon memory]] is centred, possessive, technical (where the dragon has chosen to learn), and idiosyncratic. Unicorn Wound-Keeping is distributed, embodied, consequence-focused, and dangerous to access. The two complement each other:

- **Ask a dragon how it opens.**
- **Ask a unicorn whether opening it harms the land.**

A [[vault-concordat|Concordat]] hearing on a difficult [[deep-vaults|vault]] ideally hears both. The dragon explains technical mechanism; the unicorn-translation explains consequence-pattern. Neither knowledge system is sufficient alone.

## Wound-Keeping as cultural template

The principle has been recognised in other Eadran traditions as a transferable model:

- The [[katharim|Katharim]] [[unreceived|Unreceived Ledger]] is wound-kept memory in mortal form â€” names held *so theft is remembered*, not so the theft can be undone
- [[absence-traditions|Formal Absence Theology]]'s refusal of invented continuity is wound-kept-shaped â€” the absence is honoured *as absence*, not reconstructed into false memory
- [[trolls|Trollish]] geobiological memory of substrate distress operates on similar lines â€” held *to recognise*, not to recreate
- The [[woundwatch|Woundwatch]]'s warning function is institutional Wound-Keeping for demonic-boundary risk

These convergences suggest the principle is a general Eadran wisdom that the unicorns instantiate most cleanly.

## Open canon

- The **catalog of major Wound-Kept sites** across Eadran herds â€” currently informal; whether to formalise into a Concordat-accessible registry is contested
- Whether Wound-Kept memory can be **deliberately transferred** between herds (working canon: yes, partially, through Seasonal Strayer carriers)
- The specific **mortal protocols** for receiving Herd-Listener Wound-Kept warnings â€” institutional procedure is emerging
- How Wound-Kept memory interacts with **demonic interference attempts** â€” case studies are open canon
- Whether a herd can **release** a Wound-Kept memory back into general herd-deep memory after the danger has passed, and what that looks like
