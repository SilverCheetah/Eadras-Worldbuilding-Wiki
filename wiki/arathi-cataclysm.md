# Arathi Cataclysm

**Summary**: The Arathi Cataclysm â€” **10 million years before present** under the canonical [[eadras-timeline|10my timeline]] â€” is the world-ending event in which the archmage Kudur-eá¹­ir, driven insane after ascending to Unrestricted Elder God via the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] on God's Day, reached uncontrollably back through the **First Breach** he had drilled long before in the Mythos Shell and pulled the alien [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] through into Eadras. ## The Event

### Kudur-eá¹­ir's Goal, the First Breach, and the Spear

In the final days of the [[arathi|Arathi civilization]], the archmage **Kudur-eá¹­ir** sought to **end entropy entirely** â€” believing that if he could abolish decay, he could perfect Arathi civilization and make sure it lasted forever, just as it was. **Long before God's Day**, in the course of forbidden research toward that goal, he drilled the **First Breach** through the Mythos Shell. Through that breach he made contact with the [[neverborn|Neverborn]], and in their long collaboration he forged the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]]. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

When [[dao]] witnessed the Spear's forging, he secretly laid a terrible curse upon it: the Spear would grant the wielder's deepest desire, but only by stripping that desire from their living identity at the moment of ascension.

The Spear is **not a ritual instrument**. On **God's Day every five thousand years, at noon in a particular time zone**, the Spear opens a path for unlimited power to flow from the [[eldritch|Source]] to whoever is holding it. The flow transforms the wielder: physical form into an [[icons|Icon]], soul into the soul of a God â€” an **Unrestricted Elder God** (one not bound by any rules agreed to by other gods). When Kudur-eá¹­ir held the Spear at the proper moment, he became the first such being. (source: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt)

But because Kudur-eá¹­ir had previously contacted the Neverborn through the breach **and** was the Spear's *creator*, Dao's curse hit him in a unique mode: it did not cleanly strip his motive â€” it **shattered his consciousness**, leaving him completely insane while preserving his godly power and arcane knowledge. Occasional flashes of his former brilliance still surfaced through the wreckage. (sources: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt; New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt)

### The Worldbreaker Pulled Through the First Breach

Kudur-eá¹­ir's already-fractured mind, now empowered by the unbounded will of an Unrestricted Elder God, reached **uncontrollably** back into the First Breach. He did not aim. He did not summon a name. His unregulated deific pull simply reached out into Chaos through the existing hole, and what came through â€” manifested as the unintended product of an insane god's grasp â€” was the **[[worldender|Worldbreaker]]**: a ten-mile object foreign to the [[mythos|Mythos]] from pure Chaos. The Worldbreaker came through passively, as an unintended consequence of his unregulated reach. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

Once inside the Mythos, the Worldbreaker traversed [[limina|Limina]] (the connecting plane that fills the Mythos interior), then shattered the exterior shell of the Prime Material Plane (a separate, inner membrane between Limina and the Prime), and was used to strike the Arathi capital **[[altea|Altea]]** directly â€” annihilating it. The full path: Chaos â†’ First Breach (Mythos Shell) â†’ Limina â†’ shattered PMP shell â†’ Altea.

### The Healed Shell and the Microfracture Network

The Prime Material Plane's exterior shell â€” the membrane that separates the Prime from [[limina|Limina]] â€” did not stay shattered. Across the **~10 million years** since God's Day, the shell has substantially **healed**, closing the gross wound the Worldbreaker tore open on its way to Altea. (canon clarification, curator 2026-05-02)

The healing was incomplete. The shell still bears a dense network of **microfractures across the entire sphere** â€” too small for entities of any meaningful size to traverse, but real, persistent holes between Limina and the Prime. They are not a localized scar at the impact site; they are a planet-wide field, a cracked-glass pattern across the whole shell.

This microfracture network is the foundational object of [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]] worship. The Kuo-toa call them **the Gaps**, and their conviction that *existence itself is fundamentally broken* is grounded in the literal fact that the membrane around the Prime Material Plane is shot through with reality-cracks everywhere. Larger fractures from the [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]] and from [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir's]] First Breach in the [[mythos|Mythos Shell]] are also Gaps in the Kuo-toa cosmology, but the omnipresent ambient microfracture network is the load-bearing one. (See [[kuo-toa]] and [[limina]] for the modern theology and the cosmological consequence.)

The other Elder Gods confronted Kudur-eá¹­ir and combined their power to bind him fully into his [[icons|Icon]] â€” an act now formalized as **Total Icon Binding**. With Kudur-eá¹­ir's god-spirit fully contained in the Icon, his power was limited to line of sight and his entire divine continuity depended on that one anchor. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt) The Worldbreaker's impact then shattered the Icon. Because Kudur-eá¹­ir was Icon-bound and his entire divine existence was concentrated there, the Icon's destruction completely annihilated him â€” not banished, not exiled for a century, but ended. **He perished with Altea.** (sources: Playground generation 2nd part.txt; New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt)

### Pre-Cataclysm baseline atmosphere

Before the impact, Eadras's atmosphere was approximately **35% oxygen** â€” substantially richer than the modern ~21â€“25%. This high-oxygen atmosphere made spontaneous combustion and rapid wildfire spread a constant, serious danger across the entire planet, and it shaped Arathi civic infrastructure: every Arathi city ran active fire-management systems (crystal fire-suppression fields, humidity control, non-flammable construction materials, rapid-response Household Servitors, [[lirra|Lirra]]'s stabilizing lattices). The Cataclysm itself **burned off enormous amounts of oxygen** in its planet-wide firestorms; the atmosphere then gradually normalized over the following millions of years to its modern range. The oxygen burn-off is one of the under-appreciated long-term changes the Worldbreaker imposed on Eadras â€” it altered the very air. See [[pre-cataclysm-relics#Pre-Cataclysm Atmosphere & Fire Ecology]] for the full pre-Cataclysm picture. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

### Continental drift deceleration curve

The Cataclysm initiated plate tectonics on Eadras and did so violently. The newly-fragmenting continents moved through three rough speed-regimes (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt):

- **First year** â€” kilometers per year, driven by the impact's residual kinetic energy and runaway subduction at the freshly-opened mantle wound.
- **Subsequent decades-to-millennia** â€” meters per year, as the initial energy dissipated.
- **Modern regime** â€” centimeters per year, the present-day plate-tectonic baseline that has been stable across most of the post-Cataclysm period.

This deceleration is consistent with the four one-million-year tectonic snapshots ([[eadras-tectonic-history]]) â€” the rapid early phase produces the dramatic 1mya configuration; the meters-per-year phase carries continents into the 2myaâ€“3mya layouts; the cm/y phase governs everything from then to now.

### Physical Consequences

The catastrophe unfolded simultaneously and over years (sources: Playground generation 1st/2nd part.txt; Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt; New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt; Large page of notes 5-4-2026.txt; Addition â€” catastrophe sequence.txt; Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt). For the deep geophysical mechanism â€” the steam-flash cascade, the crustal decoupling, the polar-stability anomaly, and the post-hoc reconstruction of all of the above â€” see the dedicated [[worldbreaker-mechanism]] page.

1. **Direct annihilation of [[altea|Altea]]**. The capital was the impact point. Nothing of Altea survived â€” no ruins, no vaults, no echoes.
2. **Mantle puncture**. The Worldender drove through the planetary crust into the mantle, creating an open wound briefly covered only by inrushing seawater.
3. **Subsurface reservoir flash-vaporization**. Massive high-pressure water reservoirs held within and below the crust were exposed by the impact's fracturing shock and flash-vaporized to superheated steam, releasing combined pressure and thermal energy on a scale far greater than the impact's kinetic shock alone. (See [[worldbreaker-mechanism#Stage 3 â€” Steam Flash and Pressure Release]].)
4. **Catastrophic crustal displacement**. Combined impact shock and steam-flash energy temporarily decoupled the crust from the mantle; the crust slid horizontally over the mantle as a semi-fluid layer ("butter on a hot skillet"), carrying the entire surface to a new orientation relative to the deep interior. The rotational axis and magnetic poles remained fixed throughout. (See [[worldbreaker-mechanism#Stage 5 â€” Crustal Displacement (Slide)]] and [[worldbreaker-mechanism#Polar Stability]].)
5. **Sundering of the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]]**. The continent-sized upland that held Altea split into **three continents** that drifted apart as plate tectonics initiated. The cavity between them became the [[arathalassic-sea|Arathalassic Sea]].
6. **Large Igneous Province formation**. Lava extruded from the mantle wound for **2 to 8 million years** afterward, building a new Large Igneous Province at the impact center. The LIP eventually breached the surface and became the present-day [[arathyn]] at the heart of the Arathalassic Sea.
7. **The White Deluge**. Vast subterranean oceans of superheated water burst upward through the new fissures â€” the immediate hydrothermal flood.
8. **Sustained oven-like surface temperatures** persisted globally for an extended period from compounding ejecta re-entry and steam venting.
9. **Global windstorms**. Atmospheric instability driven by extreme temperature and pressure differentials produced sustained worldwide windstorms that redistributed heat, debris, and biological material.
10. **Months of rebounding earthquakes** swept the planet in waves as the crust resettled into its new tectonic configuration.
11. **Planet-wide firestorms** ignited from impact ejecta and re-entering debris.
12. **Permanent rainstorm lasting at least a year** followed, as the darkened atmosphere condensed planetary moisture (much of it from the vaporized reservoirs); this rain filled the new ocean basins.
13. **Magical deregulation**. Arathi crystal mind-regulators shattered or went mad, causing uncontrolled wild magic.
14. **Climatic displacement**. Because the crust shifted under stable poles, surface regions changed latitude essentially overnight in geological terms. Equatorial regions transported to high latitudes; polar regions transported to lower latitudes; ecosystems dropped into incompatible climates. This contributed massively to biological collapse and to the failure of Arathi cities that had survived the impact and deluge but could not survive the climate inversion. (source: Large page of notes 5-4-2026.txt; user direction 2026-05-04)
15. **Conduit-surface misalignment**. The seven [[seven-conduits|conduits]] are eternal magical structures anchored to the planetary core, not physical shafts. They were **not destroyed** by the Cataclysm â€” they cannot be. What was destroyed was the surface infrastructure built around them: the Arathi terminus cities, the [[crystalmancy|crystalline control structures]] for regulating local flow, the towers and archives. The conduits themselves did not move. The surface, however, did â€” and so for the immediate post-Cataclysm period the surface points where the conduits emerged were misaligned with where civilization had been built. As tectonic motion stabilized over millions of years and post-Cataclysm civilizations emerged, settlement re-centered on the conduits' new surface termini (see [[oldest-continuous-settlements]]). This is why historical pairings of conduit-end sites (e.g., [[altea|Altea]] and the [[verdant-scar|Verdant Scar]] as Arathi-era Far-Kin) are not preserved in the modern Weave geography. (sources: More corrections.txt â€” clarifying conversation 2026-04-30; user direction 2026-05-04 clarifying that the conduits are magical, not physical, and survived intact)
16. **Extinction (partial)**. Approximately **60% of all life on Eadras died**. The greatest losses were among complex organisms and surface ecosystems; survival was concentrated in dormant plant seeds, subsurface organisms, sea creatures and plants, and life protected within insulated environments. These survivors formed the basis for ecological recovery during the long post-Cataclysm reset. **No Arathi survived** beyond [[ixthal|Ixthal]] himself; civilization-bearing species were reduced to the few non-Arathi peoples of the surviving 40%. The only sapient survivors were [[trolls|trolls in deep hibernation]]. (sources: Refinement â€” biological survival.txt; user direction 2026-05-04)

Before the event: Eadras was approximately **70% land** with mostly shallow seas.
After: Eadras became approximately **70% water** with deep ocean basins.

There is **no Arathi descent, no Arathi echoes, and no remnants of Altea whatsoever**. Remnants of *other* Arathi cities are rare-but-existent (source: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt).

## Geological Features Created

- **[[arathalassic-sea|The Arathalassic Sea]]** â€” The great ocean filling the cavity between the three sundered fragments of the Altean Highlands.
- **[[arathyn|Arathyn]]** â€” Large Igneous Province at the impact center that grew over 2-8 million years into a continental landmass on the basement of the [[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]].
- **The three sundered continents** â€” The pieces of the former Altean Highlands: **[[avalon|Avalon]]** (north), **[[damedes|Damedes]]** (southwest), **[[culethor|Culethor]]** (southeast). They stabilized as distinct landmasses within the first million years post-Cataclysm and are visible across the [[eadras-tectonic-history|tectonic snapshot series]].
- **The pre-Cataclysm survivors** â€” Two pre-Cataclysm highlands, **[[therion|Therion]]** and **[[aurettos|Aurettos]]**, survived the impact intact. They are *not* fragments of the sundered Altean Highlands; they were already separate landmasses before the Worldbreaker fell.
- **The [[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]]** â€” The persistent geological wound at the impact site that endured across millions of years of plate tectonics: blazing wound at 1mya post-Cataclysm, buried at 2mya, deep oceanic rift at 3mya, glowing rift-center within Eldravath at 4mya, eventual basement of [[arathyn|Arathyn]].
- **The Boiling Rifts** â€” Active hydrothermal scars from the unzipping; haunt of Vent Seraphs.
- **The Crown Trenches** â€” Deep fractures radiating outward from the impact zone; migration paths of Abyssal Crownwhales.
- **The Steamfall Cliffs** â€” Coastal escarpments where land collapsed and superheated waters burst out; nesting sites for Scaldwyrms.
- **The Shattered Shelves** â€” Former shallow seas dropped into broken continental margins, rich in fossils and ruins.
- **The New Mountains** â€” Mountain ranges born as plates began to collide.
- **[[verdant-scar|The Verdant Scar]]** â€” The overgrown ruins of the land-based Arathi receiving array (Arath-Veyr); on one of the surviving non-Altean ruin sites.

### Post-Cataclysm tectonic timeline

The Cataclysm did not produce the modern continental geography in a single event. Plate tectonics â€” newly ignited by the Worldbreaker â€” reshaped the world across the millions of years that followed. The post-Cataclysm world has been mapped at four one-million-year snapshots ([[eadras-tectonic-history]]):

- **1mya post-Cataclysm** (9 mybp): three Altean fragments stabilized, plus pre-Cataclysm Therion and Aurettos.
- **2mya post-Cataclysm** (8 mybp): continents drift apart; Worldbreaker Scar buried; **first dragons awaken**.
- **3mya post-Cataclysm** (7 mybp): continents distinct; Scar a deep oceanic rift; dragon era established.
- **4mya post-Cataclysm** (6 mybp): plates collide into the supercontinent **[[eldravath|Eldravath]]**; First Chaos Crusade approximately at this horizon.

Eldravath is "the last time the world existed as a single landmass before fracturing into the modern continents" â€” the supercontinent breaks apart at ~5 mybp (5mya post-Cataclysm), producing modern Eadras's continental layout. The **first [[unicorns|unicorns]] awaken at ~6 mybp** (4mya post-Cataclysm), on Eldravath itself â€” the supercontinent's broadly stable, interconnected ecosystems are the foundational reason unicorns are inherently herd-oriented and system-attuned. The Cataclysm became the central dividing line in Eadras history. Common calendrical markers:

- *After the Drowning*

Common sayings:
- "Older than the deep sea."
- "Built when roads crossed where ships now sail."
- "Proud as Arath before steam."

## Religious Interpretations

Different faiths interpret the event through their own lens (sources: Playground generation 1st part.txt; VerdantScarAndArathCataclysm_01_InWorld_Facts.txt):
- **Followers of Ioa**: Kudur-eá¹­ir violated Sovereign Order by ascending outside divine sanction.
- **Followers of Lirra**: Proof that magic must be governed by mind â€” and that governed magic becomes monstrous when the governing mind is prideful.
- **Followers of Qoxli**: The old land-heavy world was overmastered by civilization; the Drowning restored wildness at unimaginable cost.
- **Followers of Nara**: Mourn the lost homes.
- **Followers of Raja**: Praise those who rebuilt â€” the post-Cataclysm civilizations that rose from ash are the doctrine's true achievement; the catastrophe is read as the test that produced them.
- **Followers of Sylph**: Forbidden jokes circulate among Sylphic-fae communities about Arathi architects who designed *"perfectly permanent"* seaside villas. The joke is structurally a Sylphic protest against the Arathi conviction that any human work could be eternal.
- **Followers of Nox**: Preserve darker claims that some Arathi elites knew the Star would fall and built undersea sanctums beforehand.
