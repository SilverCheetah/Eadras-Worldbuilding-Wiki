# Mitans

**Summary**: The Mitans were a [[tarans|Tarran]]-engineered subterranean people â€” tunnel-fighters, miners, and underground architects â€” created via in-utero magical modification (see [[peoples-of-eadras#How the engineering worked]]). They are the common ancestor of both modern [[dwarves]] and [[halflings]]. Catastrophic Mitan losses in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] left the survivors with too narrow a genetic base to recover, and the species has been in slow decline ever since; small remnant populations are believed to persist in the deepest, most isolated holds.

## Origin

The Mitans are a [[tarans|Tarran]]-engineered child race â€” created during the cooperative pre-imperial age of the three progenitor peoples through the same canonical mechanism used for every engineered race: **magical modification of the progenitor's own babies in the womb, accumulated over a few generations**, until the offspring stabilized as a distinct people fitted to a defined role (see [[peoples-of-eadras#How the engineering worked]]). The Tarran themselves did the work; [[the-three|Melania]] did not direct, assist, or oversee the engineering (canon clarification 2026-05-10).

The Tarran shaped the Mitan trunk for two interlocking purposes: **subterranean combat** and **mining and excavation in deep, hostile environments**. The Tarran needed both â€” their architecture was already shifting toward layered fortresses and frontier strongholds (see [[taran-architecture]]), and their military doctrine demanded specialists who could fight in tunnels, ventilation shafts, and collapsed rubble where heavy infantry could not deploy. The Mitans answered both needs.

## Form and bearing

Mitans were physically smaller and stockier than their Tarran progenitors but built for the underground: dense bone, deep lung capacity, exceptional low-light vision, hands shaped for both tools and weapons. They carried the [[tarans|Tarran]] martial inheritance â€” a strong warrior culture, an instinct for discipline, an ethic of duty â€” but channelled it into a domain the Tarran themselves never inhabited daily. A Mitan was not a Tarran-in-miniature; a Mitan was a tunnel professional with a Tarran's bearing.

## Architecture

The Mitan architectural tradition is the **direct precursor to classical dwarven architecture**. It descended in turn from an older lineage: the [[rathari|Rathari]] underground tradition, which the Mitans encountered, learned from, and adapted away.

The Rathari built crystalline underground libraries and archive-cities â€” vast vaulted halls supported by slender rune-etched columns, designed around scholarship, recitation, and the storage of magical knowledge (see [[rathari#Architecture]] and [[kharathen]]). The Mitans inherited the engineering â€” the geometric precision, the deep-stone confidence, the rune-supported vaults â€” but had no use for the Rathari aesthetic. Their work was tunnel warfare and ore extraction, not the preservation of theorems.

Mitan architecture therefore moved decisively toward a **stone-and-steel idiom**, richly decorated with **gold, silver, and gems**. Fortified gates, ribbed vaults, broad utility corridors, weapon-friendly turning radii at every chamber junction, ventilation engineered to survive sieges, smelting halls integrated with living quarters. The decorative weight shifted from crystal-and-recitation to mineral display: a Mitan hold announced its prosperity through metalwork and gemwork, not through illuminated archives. This is the tradition modern [[dwarves]] inherited and refined.

## Warrior culture

The Mitan inherited the [[tarans|Tarran]] martial tradition and adapted it to subterranean warfare. They were not the heavy-infantry shock troops of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] â€” that was [[aetherins|Aetherin]] work above ground. The Mitans were the underground specialists: tunnel ambushers, sapper-warriors, mining-claim defenders, deep-hold garrisons. Their tactical doctrine emphasized terrain mastery, patience, and disciplined small-unit action.

This warrior culture is what the modern [[dwarves]] preserve and what the modern [[halflings]] explicitly do not.

## Descendants

The Mitan trunk gave rise to **two modern peoples**:

- **[[dwarves|Dwarves (Shada'din)]]** are the spiritual successors of the Mitan. They preserved the underground lifestyle, the mining and martial expertise, the stone-and-steel-and-precious-metal architectural tradition, and the disciplined warrior culture. Modern dwarven culture is what the Mitan tradition became when it was given the time and pressure of the post-Demon-War millennia to refine itself. Their two-mode Work-and-Relaxation cultural pattern (see [[dwarves]]) is a post-Mitan development; the Mitan trunk had not split that way. (Per the **2026-05-10 canon correction**, [[gnomes|Gnomes]] are *not* Mitan descendants â€” gnomes descend purely from [[grugi|Grugi]] stock; the earlier "dual-progenitor" gnomish framing has been retracted. See [[gnomes#Origin]].)
- **[[halflings|Halflings]]** are the surface-divergent branch. They formed initially as a **subrace within the Mitan**, sent to the surface as agriculturalists to supply the deep holds with fresh produce, and gradually established permanent surface communities of their own. By the time the [[demon-war|Demon War]] broke, halflings had been a recognizably distinct folk for many thousands of years â€” separate communities, separate architecture, separate culture, separate aesthetic. They shed the warrior tradition almost entirely, and the Demon War-era trauma of losing their surface farms produced "[[halflings#The Change|The Change]]" â€” the racial wanderlust that defines them today. (See [[halflings#Origin as a Mitan subrace]].)

## The slow decline

Mitan losses in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] were catastrophic. As Tarran-aligned tunnel-warfare specialists, the Mitan were committed wherever subterranean fronts opened â€” and demonic incursions found, opened, and exploited many such fronts. Mitans fought in collapsing tunnels, against demonic things bred for darkness, in close-quarters work that consumed warriors faster than the surface campaigns. They held, in many places. They saved deep populations, in many places. But the cost was civilizational.

When the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Final Wall]] ended the war, the surviving Mitan population was too small and too inbred to recover. The species came out of the war with **a genetic base too narrow to stabilize at scale**. There was no outright extinction event for the Mitan â€” no equivalent of the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Final Wall]] for the [[aetherins|Aetherin]] and [[orugi|Orug]]. Instead, the trunk slowly thinned over the millennia that followed: holds emptied one by one as their populations dropped below sustainable numbers; founder lines died out; entire Mitan lineages went silent across centuries. The decline was quiet, and it was very long, and it has not yet ended.

In the modern era the Mitan are not absolutely extinct. **Small remnant populations are believed to persist in the deepest, most isolated holds** â€” places too far from modern surface routes to be visited, too difficult to provision for outside contact, and (in some cases) deliberately concealed by their inhabitants from a world that has largely forgotten them. These remnants are the species' last living trunk. Whether they will recover or whether the slow decline is now terminal is one of the quietly-held tragedies of modern Eadras.

The dwarven Shada'din regard the deep-hold Mitan remnants with a complex compound of reverence, guilt, and reluctance to disturb. Dwarven exploration tradition includes **the Mitan-hold protocol** â€” when a sealed deep complex is identified as plausibly Mitan-inhabited, it is **not opened**. The decision to leave them their dignity is treated as a baseline ethical commitment, not a topic for negotiation.

## Naming

Mitan naming is the original tradition that modern **dwarven** naming descends from â€” heavy, resonant, mineral, evoking metals, ores, gemstones, and the deep earth. See [[naming-conventions#mitan-naming|Naming Conventions â€” Mitan]] for the inherited pattern (lightly softened by the modern dwarves, but still recognizably Mitan in its phonological weight). Halfling naming, by contrast, diverged hard during the agricultural-divergence period and bears almost no Mitan signature â€” see [[naming-conventions#halfling-naming|Halfling naming]].
