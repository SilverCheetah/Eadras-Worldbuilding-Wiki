# The Hearth-Witness Mandate

**Summary**: An administrative reform under the [[doctrine-of-open-palm|Doctrine of the Open Palm]] that requires major divine offices processing mortal petitions to maintain **mortal or recently mortal auditors empowered to observe consequence, not merely procedure**. Hearth-Witnesses exist because [[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods|Ascended Mortal Gods]] become divine too quickly to fully preserve mortal time-sense — and a system comprehensible only to immortals inevitably forgets urgency. The Mandate was approved in principle by [[ioa|Ioa]] during the [[baldaranic-council-cycle#Phase I — The Open Palm Council|Open Palm Council]]; its mortal-institutional implementation is ongoing throughout the [[first-rebrightening|First Rebrightening]].

**Sources**: open-palm-01/02 (2026-05-15 ingest from Elder Gods chat 1.txt).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-15.

---

## The mandate

Major divine offices that process mortal petitions must maintain **at least one Hearth-Witness**: a mortal or recently mortal observer whose role is *to observe consequence, not merely procedure*.

The distinction is deliberate. The [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]]'s existing auditing apparatus already tracks procedure — which clerk routed which petition, which deity received it, which response was returned, what records were kept. Procedural auditing is what the Bureaucracy is good at. It is also what the Bureaucracy has historically used as cover for institutional failure: *the procedure was followed; therefore the outcome was correct*.

The Hearth-Witness audits *the outcome*. Was the consequence what mortal time would call adequate? Did the procedural compliance produce a result a widow would recognise as response, or only as record? The Mandate inserts mortal time-sense into divine institutional time-sense at the point where the two diverge most catastrophically.

## Why the role is necessary

The diagnosis the Council reached: **immortal institutions cannot be trusted to measure delay correctly**. A century can feel like procedure to gods and like apocalypse to mortals. The same procedural step that reads as *appropriate caution* to a god who has been in office for ten thousand years reads as *unconscionable delay* to a mortal whose child is dying while the routing decision proceeds.

This is not a failure of divine compassion. [[gods-of-eadras|Most Elder Gods]] possess immense compassion in their portfolios. The failure is one of *calibration*. An immortal cannot consistently track the cost of time the way a mortal can, because the cost of time *for the immortal* is structurally different.

Ascended Mortal Gods partly correct this — they remember being mortal — but only partly. Ascension shifts time-sense even when memory is preserved. The Council observed that even relatively recently Ascended figures (within the past few thousand years) show some drift from mortal urgency.

The Mandate addresses this by **placing actively-mortal time-sense inside the institution permanently**.

## Who can serve

Potential Hearth-Witnesses include, among others:

- **Widows** — particularly widows whose grief involved bureaucratic-routing failure, who therefore have first-hand institutional anger calibrated to the failure mode the Mandate addresses
- **Veterans** — particularly veterans who have lost comrades to delayed response (medical, magical, divine)
- **Farmers** — whose seasonal-clock urgency tracks consequences across years not centuries
- **Midwives** — whose entire profession is *cannot wait*
- **Failed magistrates** — those whose institutional failures gave them an outsider's clarity about institutional failure mechanisms
- **Repentant tax collectors** — bureaucratic-trained but with personal experience of the procedure-vs-consequence gap from the wrong side of it
- **Recently dead souls who willingly serve before final placement** (controversial — see below)

The list is not exhaustive. The qualification is **calibrated mortal time-sense + institutional understanding**, not any specific prior career.

## The recently-dead-souls controversy

The Mandate's most contentious provision: **recently dead souls may serve as Hearth-Witnesses before being placed in their final afterlife position**.

The case *for*: some dead souls have particular insight (institutional failure from the receiving end of the failure), and the dead may want meaningful service before rest. Service rendered prevents the bitter death-state of *the routing error that killed me had no consequences for anyone*.

The case *against*: [[shali|Shali]] objects strongly to exploiting the dead as clerks. The dead earned their rest. Asking the dead to work — even willing dead, even briefly — risks reproducing the bureaucratic exploitation pattern the Mandate is supposed to fix.

[[shani|Shani]]'s position is more nuanced: some dead souls *crave* meaningful service before rest, and refusing them violates their agency in a way the Open Palm doctrine itself should oppose. Forced dead-service is exploitation; voluntary dead-service may be honour.

The Council flagged this for **ongoing doctrinal and afterlife-administrative tension**. The Mandate is approved in principle; the specific mechanism for voluntary dead-service Hearth-Witnessing remains unresolved.

## What Hearth-Witnesses do

The role's operational structure (still emerging during the [[first-rebrightening|First Rebrightening]]):

- **Receive petition records** as they come in, in parallel with the divine office's normal routing
- **Maintain independent consequence files** — written from the mortal-time-sense perspective, tracking outcomes rather than procedure
- **Certify or refuse to certify** major outcome categories — *this petition was answered*, *this petition was dropped*, *this petition's procedural compliance was achieved at unconscionable cost*
- **Testify in [[petition-of-abandoned-circumstance|Petitions of Abandoned Circumstance]]** — the Hearth-Witness's consequence-file may be decisive evidence
- **Escalate** to the relevant Ascended Mortal God, the office's senior staff, or directly to the [[katharim|Katharim]] when procedural compliance has clearly missed mortal-time consequence

The role is **not** to issue rulings, redirect petitions, or substitute mortal judgment for divine judgment. The Witness observes and records and certifies. The divine office still decides. But the Witness's certification (or refusal) becomes part of the record the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] curates, and the [[gods-of-eadras|gods]] cannot pretend the Witness's certification did not happen.

## A Hearth-Witness testimony, illustrative

The form of a Hearth-Witness consequence-file entry, in the working register the Council suggested:

> *Petition #4471-Z. Routed: 3 days. Answered: 17 days. Consequence: the child died on day 11. Procedural compliance was complete. The procedural answer arrived 6 days after the consequence the procedure was supposed to address. The petition's response was therefore institutionally null. The Office of Health Returns is hereby asked to consider whether 17-day routing for childhood fever cases constitutes good-faith intervention. I will not certify this petition as answered.*
>
> — Marra of Drakthorn, Hearth-Witness, Office of Health Returns

The tone is documentary and stubborn. The Witness does not call the office negligent (that is the Petition's job). The Witness records what time did to the petition, and refuses certification.

## Resistance

The Mandate has institutional opponents:

- **Conservative temple bureaucracies** view it as miracle inflation — a precedent for mortal oversight of divine offices that, once established, will be impossible to bound.
- **Some Ascended Mortal Gods** view it as an insult to their work — the Mandate's premise is that *Ascended Mortal Gods cannot be trusted to track mortal time*, which many find difficult to accept.
- **Specific divine offices** with poor procedural-vs-consequence records have institutional reasons to resist.

The reform's proponents respond that the **failure mode the Mandate addresses is structural, not personal**. No individual Ascended Mortal God is being accused of bad faith. The Mandate exists because *the structure itself drifts* without an actively-mortal corrective.

## Open canon

- **First office** to formally implement the Mandate (Office of Childbirth Mercies is the leading working canon — high-stakes, high-volume, high-urgency).
- **Procedural mechanism** for selecting Hearth-Witnesses — election? Lottery? Recommendation by Katharim? By Ascended Mortal Gods? Some hybrid?
- **Term length** for a Hearth-Witness — single-petition? Years? Until death? Until they themselves Ascend?
- **What happens when** a Hearth-Witness refuses to certify a high-profile petition and the office disagrees — institutional escalation procedure is open canon.
- **The Mandate's specific application to [[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani]]'s offices** — given the Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa, can a Hearth-Witness observe what they may not be told?
- **Hearth-Witness compensation**, if any — service is presumed to be its own honour, but living Hearth-Witnesses presumably still need to eat.
