# Din'shadar

**Summary**: Din'shadar is the Ascended Mortal God of mining and the [[dwarves|Shada'din]] people — the divine patron whose blessing manifests in the rare gift of **Rock Sense**, the ability to feel and read stone with great precision.

**Sources**: May 1 revisions.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-01

---

## Domain

**Mining** and the **Shada'din people**. Din'shadar's reach covers every aspect of working stone and metal: the deep prospecting that finds the right vein, the steady labor that opens it, the safety of the crews under the mountain, and the long generational craft that keeps a hold's halls habitable. He is also patron of the Shada'din *as a people* — their unity, their endurance, their dignity. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

## Worship pattern

As an Ascended Mortal God, Din'shadar **actively desires and encourages worship**. His faith is among the most fervent in Eadras: every Shada'din hold maintains a foundry-altar or deep-shrine, and every clan recognizes Din'shadar as a household name even when that household's primary devotion lies elsewhere. See [[religion-in-eadras|Religion in Eadras]] for the broader pattern of how Ascended Mortal gods receive worship.

Worship of Din'shadar is **not racially exclusive**. Surface miners of any race who labor in dwarven holds, gnome engineers working in dwarven-built factories, and human stone-masons trained in Shada'din techniques all commonly carry his sign. He is, however, distinctly the patron of the Shada'din *as a people* — a role no other god holds.

## Rock Sense

A **rare few** Shada'din are born with **Rock Sense** — the ability to feel and "read" stone around them with great precision. They sense fault lines, ore concentrations, hollow chambers, magical resonance in bedrock, and the slow movements of deep earth in ways no instrument can match. This gift is **considered a direct blessing from Din'shadar.** (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

Rock Sense individuals are valuable beyond price: they identify safe excavation paths, locate veins of [[mana-metals|mana-metals]] and gem deposits, and serve as living instruments for the Shada'din mages who specialize in stabilization and structural magic (see [[dwarves#Magic|Shada'din magic]]). Rock-sensed children are typically apprenticed early to senior structural-magic practitioners; misuse or neglect of the gift is treated, theologically, as ingratitude toward Din'shadar himself.

## Open canon

- Din'shadar's pre-ascension biography. Canon establishes him as an Ascended Mortal of Shada'din origin but does not yet name his mortal life, his deeds, or the circumstances of his ascension.
- His symbol and iconography. (Hammer-and-anvil and the cleft mountain are obvious candidates; not yet canonical.)
- His relationship to the Elder God [[vos|Vos]] (technology, craftsmanship in the broader urban sense), to [[raja|Raja]] (craftsmanship more generally), and to [[inki|Inki]] (the gnomish ascended god of invention and craftsmanship). The Inki ↔ Din'shadar parallel is especially natural given the close gnome-Shada'din factory partnership.
