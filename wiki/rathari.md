# Rathari

**Summary**: The Rathar were the pinnacle scholarly race engineered by the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] from refined Velrathi stock — gray-skinned, red-eyed, perfect-memory researchers who served as Velrathi thought-partners for millennia before the [[demon-war|Demon War]] shattered them into three branches: the dead, the [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]], and the [[katharim|Weighted Third]].

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Playground generation 2nd part.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt, Naming conventions.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-02

---

## Singular vs. plural

The race name has distinct singular and plural forms (analogous to *Arathi* / *Arath*):

- **Singular:** *Rathar*
- **Plural:** *Rathari*

This distinction is canonical (User direction 2026-05-02). Older raw sources sometimes use *Rathar* as the plural; that usage remains acceptable for short reference but *Rathari* is the canonical plural going forward. See [[naming-conventions#Singular vs. plural|Naming Conventions — Rathar singular/plural]].

## Origin

The Rathar were not directly created by [[the-three|Rand]]. They were the **deliberate masterpiece of the [[velrathi|Velrathi]]** — engineered through generations of meticulous arcane ritual using refined Velrathi stock and carefully selected elemental essences. They are living embodiments of Velrathi curiosity given independent form (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

In the long golden age of cooperation between the Three Progenitor Peoples, the Velrathi looked upon their own scholarly labors and desired assistants who could match their intellectual hunger. From the deepest arts of their arcane science, the first Rathar were shaped: tall, gray-skinned, red-eyed, cadaverous (the grey-and-red phenotype **inherited from their already-greyed Velrathi makers** — see [[velrathi#The greying|the greying]], which by the cooperative age was a well-established acquired trait), with **minds even sharper at pattern recognition** than their makers, **bodies slightly hardier** for long laboratory hours, and voices tuned for perfect recitation.

They were created to serve as ideal research companions, archive guardians, and thought-partners. They were never slaves. They were living tools — proud extensions of Velrathi will, granted sapience, dignity, and the satisfaction of purpose.

## Pre-War Society

### Thought Conclaves

Rathar society was organized around **Thought Conclaves** — meritocratic circles of 12 to 48 scholars bound by shared research projects rather than blood. Status came solely from original insight, flawless memory, and the elegance of one's theorems. A brilliant young researcher could rise faster than an elder if their work was sound.

Conclaves were lifelong but not insular; members frequently transferred between projects, carrying knowledge like pollen. Families existed but were secondary — children were raised communally in **scholarchives**, taught to recite lineages and theorems before they could walk. Debate was passionate yet ritualized: no shouting, only escalating precision until one side's logic could no longer hold.

### The Great Recitation

Life revolved around the **Great Recitation** — nightly assemblies where every Conclave member reported progress, admitted errors, and offered new lines of inquiry. Sleep was scheduled and minimal. Children competed in memory games and logical duels from infancy.

The highest honor was to have one's theorem added to the **Living Archive** — a vast, ever-growing crystalline matrix that stored perfect copies of every discovery. To have one's name struck from the Archive was worse than death.

### Architecture

The Rathar were the first to master true underground cities. Their greatest settlements — the **Deep Conclaves** — were carved into stable bedrock with impossible geometric precision. Vast vaulted halls supported by slender, rune-etched columns that seemed too delicate to hold the weight above. Crystal veins in the stone were coaxed to glow with soft, steady light. Libraries and laboratories formed the true heart of every district; living quarters were austere alcoves off the main scholarly chambers.

This is the skill that later bloomed into the construction of [[kharathen]], the [[katharim|Katharim]] necropolis.

### Diet and Daily Life

Meals were functional yet precise: nutrient-dense fungal stews, bone-meal breads, fermented broths, and crystal-infused waters that sharpened cognition. Eating happened at long communal tables while research was recited aloud. Flavor was secondary to nutritional perfection and symbolic alignment with ongoing projects (see also [[pre-empire-foodways|Velrathi food culture]], from which Rathar cuisine descended).

### Magical Practice

Pre-war Rathar were among the finest **arcane scholars and ritualists** in Eadras, second only to the Velrathi themselves. They excelled at ritual precision, long-term enchantment weaving, and the mathematics of [[magic-in-eadras|ley-line stabilization]]. Their laboratories produced some of the most stable, enduring magitek in history.

## The Ten Houses

Rathar identity is defined first by **House** and second by **Hold** (the underground enclave or archive-city). There are exactly **ten ancient Rathar Houses**, each with a defined area of magical specialty. Loyalty to one's House is extremely strong, but Houses frequently collaborated on large research projects. (source: Naming conventions.txt)

### The Oxford-college model

The Rathar Houses functioned like **Oxford University colleges** within the broader Velrathi "university." (User direction 2026-05-01.) Each House is a sovereign self-governing scholarly community — its own fellows, focus, traditions, archives, Hold, and admissions practices — but the Houses collectively served the Velrathi and operated within a Velrathi academic-cultural superstructure. A Rathar belonged to **both** the Velrathi civilization (as engineered servants of a particular Velrathi House) **and** to their own Rathar House (as scholars of that College).

This is why the Rathar are correctly described as a Velrathi servant race **and** as having their own House system — both are true at once, and the Oxford analogy unifies them. A given Rathar might serve a particular Velrathi House in their day-to-day work while remaining a fellow of, say, House Narveth — and would be addressed formally by their Rathar House (the College), not by the Velrathi House they served.

### Distinct from Velrathi Houses

**The Rathar Houses are not the same as Velrathi Houses.** (User direction 2026-05-01: *"Rathar houses are not Velrathi houses."*) The two systems are parallel and distinct. Some names happen to match across the two systems (Korthal, Narveth, and Valtharion appear in both rosters), but a Rathar House Korthal and a Velrathi House Korthal are *different organizations* that share a name — much as a college and the university faculty it sits within can both bear a founder's name without being the same institution. Other Velrathi Houses (e.g. *Velroth*, *Kathor*) have no Rathar College counterpart at all.

### Formal address

Rathar formal address **combines both** their Velrathi House service and their Rathar House (College) membership in a single address. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

**Empire-era full form:**

`[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] of [Rathar House], [Hold]`

The two particles each carry weight:

- **"ser [Velrathi House]"** — marks the Rathar's service to a particular Velrathi House (the same particle Orug and Grug use; see [[naming-conventions#velrathi-servant-race-naming|Velrathi Servant Race Naming]]).
- **"of [Rathar House]"** — marks College membership in one of the [[#The Ten Houses|Ten Ancient Rathar Houses]].
- **[Hold]** — the specific Rathar Hold (underground enclave or archive-city) the Rathar belongs to.

This combined form shows both dimensions of Rathar identity in one address: a fact about their work (servant of a Velrathi House) and a fact about who they are (fellow of a Rathar College).

**Examples (Empire-era full form):**

- *Valthar ser Korthal of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault*
- *Thalira ser Valtharion of House Narveth, Deep Archive of Thalor*
- *Pharzen ser Velroth of House Korthal, Runeward Hold*

**Post-Banishment form:** After the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] 10,000 years ago, the Velrathi House structure that gave the "ser" particle its meaning was broken — and surviving Rathar gradually dropped the "ser [Velrathi House]" portion, retaining only the College designation:

`[Given Name] of House [Rathar House], [Hold]`

**Examples (modern surviving Rathar):**

- *Valthar of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault (Sealed)*
- *Thalira of House Archivault, Deep Memory Hold*
- *Narvethian of House Voidscribe, Riftward Sanctum*

The shorter post-Banishment form is what surviving Rathar use today, and is the form that appears as the canonical example in source naming material — but the Empire-era full form is the historical canonical address.

Rathar do not use cognomens; identity is strictly by Velrathi House service (when applicable) and Rathar House + Hold.

| House | Focus |
|---|---|
| **House Valtharion** *(The Eldest House)* | Theoretical Arcana and Metaphysics — pure theory, ley line mapping, fundamental laws of magic. Many of the greatest advances in understanding the Weave and Sourceshine came from Valtharion researchers. |
| **House Korthal** | Crystallomancy and Magical Architecture — masters of growing, shaping, and stabilizing magical crystals. Designed and maintained many of the grand underground archive-cities and crystal conduits. |
| **House Narveth** | History, Memory, and Temporal Studies — the great archivists and chronologists. Maintain the most complete historical records in Eadras and study time anomalies and memory magic. |
| **House Pharzen** | Alchemy and Transmutation — magical reagents, potions, and the transmutation of matter. Instrumental in developing many of the early mana-infused metals. |
| **House Lithorium** | Geomancy and Stone Magic — underground construction, stabilization magic, stone-based enchantments. Perfected the techniques for carving safe, vast underground holds. |
| **House Deepcrystal** | Divination and Far-Seeing — scrying, prophecy, and long-distance communication. Maintain vast crystal networks used for observation across great distances. |
| **House Runewarden** | Rune Magic and Warding — permanent enchantments, defensive runes, and sealing dangerous knowledge. Created many of the most powerful wards protecting Rathar vaults. |
| **House Archivault** | Secure Knowledge Preservation — the guardians of forbidden and dangerous lore. Maintain the most heavily protected archives and develop new methods of storing information that even time cannot erode. |
| **House Voidscribe** | Shadow, Void, and Liminal Studies — the boundaries between planes, shadow magic, liminal spaces. After the Demon War, many Voidscribe researchers were instrumental in early attempts to understand [[limina\|Limina]]. |
| **House Echoheart** | Resonance, Sound, and Sympathetic Magic — vibrational magic, echo manipulation, sympathetic links. Developed many communication and harmonic-based magical systems used deep underground. |

Each House maintained its own grand **Hold** — an underground enclave or archive-city — though some smaller branches shared space.

## The Schism

When the [[demon-war|Demon War]] came, the Rathar's perfect memory became both their greatest strength and their undoing. The horror of demonic invasion replayed endlessly in minds engineered for flawless recall. The Rathar fractured into three distinct answers to suffering. See [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar]] for the full account.

| Third | Choice | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Fallen Third** | Died in the war | Killed defending breached libraries; some at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves\|Final Wall]] |
| **Laughing Third** | Begged Sylph for the **Veil of Forgetting** | Transmuted into the [[beast-folk\|Beast-Folk]] |
| **Weighted Third** | Refused the Veil; chose to remember | Became the [[katharim\|Katharim]] — undertakers and divine-only priests |

A very small number of pure Rathar bloodlines survived by retreating into the deepest, most fortified undercities and sealing themselves in. They are the only direct survivors of the original people.

## Modern status

The Rathar as a distinct people no longer exist in any meaningful numbers after the Demon War (10,000 years ago). (source: Naming conventions.txt)

- The vast majority of the Rathar perished during the Demon War.
- Of the survivors, many chose Sylph's **Veil of Forgetting** and became the [[beast-folk|Beast-folk]] (the Laughing Third).
- Many others **refused the Veil** and became the [[katharim|Katharim]] (the Weighted Third / Rememberers).
- Only a very small number of Rathar survived **as traditional Rathar**, sealed into a handful of extremely well-hidden, heavily warded underground holds. These enclaves remain isolated to this day, rarely interacting with the outside world.

These surviving Rathar fit the canonical "Velrathi archives" exception to the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] — the Velrathi line was exiled in the vast majority, but enclaves remained behind on Eadras, and the surviving Rathar are part of that hidden continuity.

### The Ten Houses today

Of the original Ten Ancient Houses, **only fragments remain**:

- Most Houses were completely wiped out or fully transitioned into either Katharim or Beast-folk lineages.
- A few Houses — particularly **House Valtharion**, **House Archivault**, and **House Voidscribe** — still have tiny surviving populations in sealed holds, jealously guarding what remains of their ancient knowledge.
- These surviving Rathar are extremely reclusive, paranoid, and protective of their archives. They view the outside world as irreparably tainted by the events of the Demon War. (source: Naming conventions.txt)

Examples of surviving Rathar names — extremely rare in occurrence:

- *Valthar of House Valtharion, Ninth Crystal Vault (Sealed)*
- *Thalira of House Archivault, Deep Memory Hold*
- *Narvethian of House Voidscribe, Riftward Sanctum*

### Pure Rathar Enclaves Today

The few remaining pure Rathar are **rare, reclusive, and fiercely traditional** — living archives who refused both the Veil and the full weight of public mourning.

They preserve the old Conclave structure almost unchanged. They rise at the exact hour their ancestors did, recite the same theorems, and tend the same preserved specimens. They view both descendant peoples with complex emotion: pride in the Beast-Folk's survival and joy, sorrow for the Katharim's burden, and quiet determination to preserve the original scholarly flame.

Their hidden enclaves still receive occasional pilgrims from both descendant lines — gray figures meeting colorful ones at sealed vault entrances to exchange knowledge and sorrow.

To the outside world, the Rathar legacy is felt more than seen. Beast-folk bring chaotic vitality. Katharim bring solemn memory and the care of the dead. The hidden pure Rathar enclaves remain quiet wells of ancient knowledge that surface only in times of dire need — a living bridge between the old world and the new.

## Naming

Rathar names are scholarly, precise, and arcane — *Valthar, Rathandar, Korthalion, Pharzen, Thalira, Xarvena.* Identity is defined first by **House** and second by **Hold**: the formal address pattern is *[Given Name] of House [House Name], [Hold Identifier]* — e.g. *Valthar of House Korthal, Ninth Crystal Vault.* Among other Rathar this shortens to *Given Name + House.* The Rathar do **not** use cognomens; their tradition is strictly formal and tied to lineage and location. See [[naming-conventions#Rathar Naming|Racial Naming Conventions — Rathar]].

## The Velrathi Brood

The Rathar were not the only race the Velrathi engineered. They also created:
- **[[orugi|Orug]]** — strong, honorable guardians and laborers (extinct after the Demon War)
- **[[grugi|Grug]]** — clever, mechanically adept maintainers and inventors

All three crafted races shared the Velrathi template (gray skin, red eyes, long-lived, precision minds) but were optimized for different roles. They formed a unified society with the Velrathi as apex, until the war broke everything.
