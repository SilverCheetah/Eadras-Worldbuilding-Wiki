# Grand Conjunction

**Summary**: The Grand Conjunction is the rare celestial alignment — recurring on a roughly 5,000-year cycle — that activates the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] by unlocking the Wellspring of Creation, allowing it to transform a wielder into an Unrestricted Elder God.

**Sources**: Playground generation 2nd part.txt, Spear of Crystallized Entropy.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-29

---

## Overview

The **Grand Conjunction** is a rare celestial alignment under which the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] can unlock the **Wellspring of Creation** and transform its wielder into an Unrestricted Elder God. Without the conjunction, the Spear cannot perform its central transformation — it remains a dangerous artifact, but not an apotheosis engine. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Cycle

The Grand Conjunction occurs on a roughly **ten-thousand-year cycle** (user direction 2026-05-15 morning ingest, decision B1: 10,000 years final). Two windows are historically significant in the current age:

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| ~10,000 years ago | [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] used the Spear and ascended at the Grand Conjunction of that horizon; Demon War followed. The [[divine-concordat|Divine Concordat]] was sealed shortly after; the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] ended the empire era. |
| ~9,000–10,000 years ago | [[lost-zenith|Lost Zenith]] — the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] failed to retrieve the Spear at the same Grand Conjunction; in the decades immediately preceding the window they engineered the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] (after the hundred-year [[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]] ramp-up) as a Spear-search diversion. The Crusade's catastrophic side-effects produced the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]. |
| Imminent (modern era) | Next Grand Conjunction — the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] races to deliver the Spear to Xar'vion before this window |

(sources: Playground generation 2nd part.txt; user direction 2026-05-15 — replacement-pass timeline and B1 cycle-period lock)

The recurrent ten-thousand-year period explains why each previous Spear-wielder was separated from the next by deep time. The Spear sits inert through the millennia between alignments. When the conjunction approaches, Source pressure increases, and the Spear may begin to resonate. Note that **Xar'vion's Spear-use and the Lost Zenith both fall within the same Grand-Conjunction window** roughly ~10,000 years ago — Xar'vion used the Spear early in the window; the Lost Zenith was the Black Hand's failed attempt to recover it during the same window (or a closely-adjacent window of the cycle), in the decades preceding which they engineered the Crusade as cover.

## Astronomical Mechanics

The conjunction calculations require ancient Arathi-derived astronomy, [[lirra|Lirran]] Source theory, and possibly stolen [[ratharil|Ratharil]] star charts. Predicting the exact moment of alignment is difficult enough that:

- The Black Hand maintains continuous calendrical obsession through hereditary archive (the **Bone Ledger**)
- A Lirran observatory was sabotaged before the Lost Zenith
- Dragon star-charts have been stolen for cult purposes
- Modern crisis indicators may include unicorn herds becoming agitated near ancient plains and Demon War battlefields showing Weave instability

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Why It Matters: Xar'vion's Second Ascension Plot

The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] has spent ten thousand years preparing to deliver the Spear to [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] during the Grand Conjunction. Their plan, called the **Second Crown Doctrine**, requires:

1. The Spear be recovered from its hiding place (where it has remained since 10,000 years ago)
2. The Spear be brought into Xar'vion's line of sight or grasp
3. Both to occur during the Grand Conjunction

If successful, Xar'vion would undergo a second apotheosis layered over his first — a recursive ascension never before attempted. The Black Hand believes this would make him uncontainable.

Their hidden maxim: *"What bound the First Crown shall break upon the Second."* (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## The Wellspring of Creation

The Wellspring of Creation is the cosmic source of pure creative power that the Grand Conjunction makes momentarily accessible through the Spear. Kudur-eá¹­ir's ritual was designed to pierce reality and reach this Wellspring; the [[worldender|Worldender]] was called down in part to break open the world's crust to expose it. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

Under conjunction conditions, the Spear can:
- Unlock the Wellspring
- Channel its raw creative force into the wielder
- Trigger Unrestricted Elder God ascension

The conjunction is therefore not merely a calendrical curiosity. It is the only window in which the Spear's most catastrophic function operates.

## Modern Crisis Indicators

As the next Grand Conjunction approaches, signs may appear before any institution understands them as a single threat:

- Old [[black-hand|Black Hand]] symbols appear in multiple countries
- Funeral records vanish
- An [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] observatory is sabotaged
- Dragon star-charts are stolen from [[ratharil|Ratharil]]
- Unicorn herds become agitated near ancient plains
- A [[demon-war|Demon War]] battlefield shows Weave instability
- Familiars fall ill; magical creatures react before mortals understand
- Beast-folk near ancient breaches sense conduit-hum through marsh water

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Defensive Forces

The defenders of the world act on a ticking clock:

- The [[white-palm|White Palm]] hunts Black Hand cells, tracks the movement of Xar'vion's Icon, and intercepts pilgrims seeking audience with him
- The [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] tracks conduit health and protects [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]
- Dragon star-watchers monitor astronomical alignment
- Unicorn herds use psionic harmonics to sense ley disturbances

The Elder Gods themselves are constrained by the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] until the threat clearly crosses divine-scale thresholds.
