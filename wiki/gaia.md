# Gaia

**Summary**: Gaia is the **Green Moon** — the largest of [[four-moons-of-eadras|Eadras's four moons]], lush and mostly temperate, resembling pre-Cataclysm Eadras — and the only moon reflected across all three planes of the Mythos, with a Prime, a Wyldglimmer, and a Glimmerdark Gaia, each of which became a place of exile for one of the three progenitor races after the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]].

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, More corrections.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

## What Gaia Is

Gaia is the **Green Moon** — the **largest of Eadras's four moons** (see [[four-moons-of-eadras]] for the full set: Gaia, [[daj|Daj]], [[shali|Shali]], [[shani|Shani]]). Unlike Eadras's other moons, Gaia is reflected across all three planes of the Mythos, with **all three Gaias being moons** — there is a Prime Gaia, a Wyldglimmer Gaia, and a Glimmerdark Gaia, each orbiting its respective Eadras.

Each reflection has its own coherent geography, its own ecology, and its own metaphysical character — a smaller-scale parallel to the way the [[eadras-cosmology|Prime Material of Eadras]] has its own [[wyldglimmer|Wyldglimmer]] and [[glimmerdark|Glimmerdark]] reflections.

### Climate and surface character (Prime Gaia)

Gaia is **lush, with mostly temperate climate**: forests, swamps, shallow seas, equatorial jungle, and polar regions. Standing on Prime Gaia is closer to standing on **pre-Cataclysm Eadras** than standing anywhere on present-day Eadras. The Cataclysm did not reach Gaia, and Gaia has not been geologically reordered by any later event of comparable scale; what would have been ordinary 70%-land Eadras topology is preserved here as a Green Moon. (source: More corrections.txt)

The other two reflections of Gaia diverge dramatically from this base character — see Wyldglimmer Gaia and Glimmerdark Gaia below.

### Reflections

- **Prime Gaia** — the Green Moon proper. Lush, mostly temperate. Original creation site of the Tarran progenitor stock under [[the-three|Melania's]] hand.
- **Wyldglimmer Gaia** — bright, positive-tainted reflection.
- **Glimmerdark Gaia** — dark, negative-tainted reflection.

## Gaia After the Banishment

At the conclusion of the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the [[great-banishment|Elder Gods banished]] all three progenitor races into the three reflections:

| Reflection | Exiles | Capital | Modern Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Prime-like Gaia** | [[tarans\|Tarran]] | [[firethorn-hold\|Firethorn Hold]] | Strict matriarchy ([[gray-matriarchy\|Gray Matriarchy]]) ruled secretly by [[she-who-shall-not-be-named\|a hidden dragon]] |
| **Wyldglimmer Gaia** | [[elfen\|Elfen]] | (unnamed) | Bright wild-magic realm; rampant pattern-breaking; ecology twists delightfully |
| **Glimmerdark Gaia** | [[velrathi\|Velrathi]] | [[veyr-keth\|Veyr'Keth]] | Jagged crystal spires, inverted mountains, labyrinthine caverns where light feels reluctant |

Each exiled people has been there roughly ten thousand years, adapting (or refusing to adapt) to its mirror-environment.

## Prime-like Gaia

This was the [[tarans|Tarran]] progenitors' **original creation site** — [[the-three|Melania]] placed the initial Tarran stock here during the early ages of creation, before they were brought to Eadras proper. Returning them here at the Banishment was a deliberate gesture by the Elder Gods: *"Go back to where your line began. Try again."*

The Prime-like aspect resembles the original pre-catastrophe Eadras. The exiled Tarran rebuilt a fortified gray fortress-capital they call [[firethorn-hold|Firethorn Hold]] — the seat of the modern [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]]. The vanished [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] would have stood here had it not withdrawn from history; the Throne's empty dais remains as a sacred shrine.

The **Door to the [[hall-of-kings|Hall of Kings]]** normally manifests within the Tarran Royal Citadel of Firethorn Hold. The [[runeswords|Runeswords]] rest in their sanctum just one threshold away from the matriarchy — a fact the Gray Matriarchy guards but cannot reach.

## Wyldglimmer Gaia

The Wyldglimmer aspect is **bright, positive-tainted, and chaotic**. Wild magic runs rampant, distances twist delightfully, and **living architecture blooms with impossible speed**. The exiled [[elfen|Elfen]] adapted by leaning fully into the wild-song magic of their pre-war culture — their descendants there are even more attuned to growing things and song-magic than the original Elfen were.

Their descendants' diluted blood flows through modern elves and fey on the Prime — though the pure exiles in Wyldglimmer Gaia still produce occasional envoys.

## Glimmerdark Gaia

Glimmerdark Gaia is **no gentle prison**. It is a mirror-realm of:

- Jagged crystal spires
- Inverted mountains
- Labyrinthine tunnels where **light itself feels reluctant**

The exiled [[velrathi|Velrathi]] claimed the largest stable cavern systems and rebuilt pale echoes of their old Deep Conclaves — black stone halls lit by **cold red crystal**, now reinforced against the constant psychic pressure of the Glimmerdark.

Their new capital, **[[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]]**, is a fortress-archive suspended within a vast geode whose facets refract the few sources of light into blood-red patterns.

The Velrathi have turned to stranger arts there: shadow-reflected rituals, negative-energy harmonics, and study of how the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear's]] lingering corruption warped the Glimmerdark itself.

## The Teleportation Platforms

The teleportation network that linked all three Gaias to their respective planes were locked by the Elder Gods post banishment. It is still possible to use them with the proper (extremely rare) key.

Some unicorn seers and powerful [[beast-folk|beast-folk]] shamans report dreams of all three reflections during multi-moon conjunction approaches — wild, true, fragmentary glimpses.

## Inhabitants Beyond the Exiles

Gaia is not solely populated by the exiled races. Each reflection has its own native ecology and lesser sapient peoples — though the canon is sparse here. What is established:

- **Prime-like Gaia** has surviving native fauna comparable to pre-catastrophe Eadras Prime, plus whatever the Tarran have brought or domesticated
- **Wyldglimmer Gaia** has fae creatures native to its bright reflection, intermingled with the exiled Elfen lines
- **Glimmerdark Gaia** has shadow-adapted creatures and aberations — dangerous enough that the exiled Velrathi must constantly defend their territories

## Why the Banishment Sent Them Here

The Elder Gods chose Gaia for three reasons:

1. **Distance** — far enough that the exiles could not easily return or interfere with Eadras
2. **Mirror Symmetry** — each progenitor race was sent to a reflection that **matched their corresponding hubris**: arcane-experimenting Velrathi to the negative-tainted Glimmerdark; magic-entangled Elfen to the wild-magic Wyldglimmer; arrogantly ordering Tarran to their original homeland
3. **Origin Echo** — the Tarran's banishment to their original creation site is a deliberate "return to first principles"

### Each Gaia reflection amplifies the exiles' civilizational tendencies

The choice of *which* Gaia reflection received which exiled people is not an arbitrary mirror-pairing — each Gaia **amplifies the civilizational tendencies of its exiles**, which is why the exiles' modern societies look the way they do (source: Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt):

- **Wyldglimmer Gaia** — *delightful instability* / *growth without restraint*. Bright, lush, and unstable; the exiled Elfen find their already-loose relationship to fixed form encouraged into riotous fae proliferation rather than tempered.
- **Glimmerdark Gaia** — *reluctant reality* / *oppressive coherence strain*. The plane resists ordinary persistence; the exiled Velrathi find their arcane experimentation pressed into ever more intricate working just to maintain stable existence in a place that does not want to be solid.
- **Prime-like Gaia** — *preserved ordered origin-state*. Closer to pre-catastrophe Eadras than anywhere on modern Eadras; the exiled Tarran find their civilizational inclination toward order and matriarchy unimpeded by external metaphysical strain — and intensified by the absence of competing peoples to balance against.

The amplification effect is part of why the Banishment was a permanent solution: each people's self-correction, if it were ever to come, would have to come from within — because the place they were sent does not push them back toward balance.

## Summary

> Gaia hangs above Eadras as both moon and prison — a three-faced mirror in which the makers of the world watch their children inherit what they themselves were forbidden to keep.
