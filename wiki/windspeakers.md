# Windspeakers

**Summary**: Windspeakers are **[[netharim|Netharim]] born with greater innate capacity than their peers** â€” members of [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] who carry the **heavy duty of anchoring the conduit junctions** of the [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]] and, with their lives, holding the defensive structure of the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] in place. The role is not a separate order; Windspeakers are Netharim. The role is, however, *distinct* â€” both in the magnitude of the innate gift and in the gravity of the duty laid upon those who carry it.

## What a Windspeaker is

A Windspeaker is a **[[netharim|Netharim]]** â€” a member of [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] â€” who is born with markedly greater capacity for Netharim practice than most of the order's members. The greater capacity is innate, not earned; a Windspeaker does not become a Windspeaker through training, study, or initiation. They are born into the role by virtue of what they are.

The greater capacity comes with a corresponding obligation. A Windspeaker carries the **heavy duty** of **anchoring a conduit junction** â€” one of the points at which the [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]]' geometry meets and the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]]'s defensive structure must be held coherent. Anchoring the junction is the load-bearing function the Weave depends on at its most strained points; without Windspeakers at the junctions, the Weave's defensive function would degrade.

The duty is heavy because **it is the Windspeaker's life that does the anchoring**. Per the Weave's broader mechanism (see [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave|The Weave]]), the defensive structure is sustained by the lives of every magic user in Eadras â€” and the Windspeakers, by virtue of their greater capacity, contribute correspondingly more. A Windspeaker at a conduit junction holds more of the Weave's defensive load than any ordinary magic user.

## Identification and selection

A Windspeaker's greater innate capacity manifests early â€” typically before training in any formal magical tradition has begun. [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] has long-standing protocols for identifying potential Windspeakers in the broader magic-using population, drawing them into the Order as quickly as their nascent capacity is recognised. The Order does not compete with other magical traditions for adult initiates; it claims the children whose capacity reveals them as Windspeakers before any other tradition has a chance.

The number of Windspeakers in the world is approximately constant â€” anchored to the **conduit junctions that exist**. When a Windspeaker dies, another Windspeaker comes of age to take the anchor; when a Windspeaker comes of age, an older Windspeaker is typically near the end of their service. The succession is not a coincidence of timing; the cosmology arranges its load-bearers when the Weave needs them. The exact mechanism by which this succession is coordinated (Lirra's direct intervention, the [[voroik|Voroik]]'s weaving, the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]]'s prophetic offices, the Weave-itself's self-maintenance) is open canon.

## The duty

A Windspeaker's life is not their own in the same way an ordinary mortal's is. The duty is **anchoring a conduit junction**, and the anchoring is **the daily work of the Windspeaker's existence** â€” not a once-in-a-lifetime ritual, not a part-time obligation, but the ongoing exertion of their capacity into the Weave's defensive load at the assigned junction.

The duty is heavy in several specific ways:

- **It is permanent.** Once a Windspeaker is anchoring a junction, they anchor that junction for the rest of their life. There is no retirement; there is no rotation; there is no relief. The junction needs an anchor; the anchor is the Windspeaker.
- **It is consuming.** The exertion required to hold a conduit junction shortens a Windspeaker's life relative to what their innate capacity would otherwise support. Windspeakers tend to die at ages that would be considered tragically young for someone of their power.
- **It is geographically binding.** A Windspeaker must remain near the junction they anchor. They can travel within a region, but cannot abandon the junction. Their lives are therefore tied to specific places â€” the [[conduit-cities|conduit cities]] and the regions surrounding the junction-points.
- **It is incompatible with ordinary life.** Marriage, family, scholarly pursuit, civic office â€” all of these are constrained by the duty. Most Windspeakers do not lead what mortals call full lives; the duty has been laid upon them and the duty is what they do.

## Relationship to the Weave

The Weave is the defensive structure that protects Eadras's magical substrate from outsider magic and from various forms of magical predation; it is **purely defensive in function** (see [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave|The Weave]]). It carries [[eldritch|Eldritch]] energy and is **supported by the lives of every magic user in Eadras** â€” each magic user's existence contributes to the Weave's coherence; each magic user's death weakens it by a small increment.

A Windspeaker's contribution is **categorically larger** than an ordinary magic user's. Where an ordinary magic user is a small constant load on the Weave's sustenance, a Windspeaker is a *load-bearing anchor at a specific structural point*. Loss of one ordinary magic user is a measurable but small weakening; loss of a Windspeaker is a structural failure that the Weave's anchoring mechanism has to compensate for until a successor takes the junction.

This makes Windspeakers **the single most strategically important class of mortal in Eadras** from the perspective of cosmic stability. Threats to a Windspeaker are threats to the Weave; threats to the Weave are threats to Eadras's defensive cosmology as a whole.

## Historical significance

During the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] (approximately 9,000â€“10,000 years ago, in the decades immediately preceding the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]), Windspeakers were targeted along with all other magic users. **Three Windspeakers died in that period before the Crusade was halted** â€” three conduit junctions lost their anchors simultaneously, and the resulting structural cascade is what produced the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]: the Weave's defensive function degraded across Eadras for a millennium until the succession could fully repair the anchoring at all seven junctions.

The three Windspeaker deaths are why the Dimming was as severe as it was. Other magic-user losses during the Crusade weakened the Weave by their accumulated small contributions; the Windspeaker losses weakened it structurally at specific points. The combination was almost too much.

## Open canon

- **The exact number of Windspeakers and conduit junctions.** The Seven Conduits have a junction geometry that is documented elsewhere ([[seven-conduits]], [[conduit-cities]]); whether there is one Windspeaker per junction, one per conduit, or some other count is not yet pinned.
- **The succession mechanism.** What coordinates the timing of Windspeaker generation, identification, and assignment to junctions is open. Candidates include direct [[lirra|Lirran]] selection, [[voroik|Voroik]] weaving, [[divine-bureaucracy|Bureaucracy]] prophetic-office coordination, or self-organising properties of the Weave-conduit system.
- **The Windspeaker's relationship to other Netharim.** Whether Windspeakers carry special institutional status within [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]], whether they have a separate internal hierarchy or rank-and-file role, whether other Netharim defer to them or treat them as peers, is undocumented.
- **Whether a Windspeaker can refuse the duty.** The role is laid upon them by birth; canonical mechanisms for refusal, escape, or transfer are not yet documented. The few historical attempts at refusal would be major plot material.
- **What happens to the Weave if a junction goes long-term unanchored.** Three Windspeaker deaths during the Anti-Magic Crusade produced the Thousand-Year Dimming; what would happen if all seven were lost is not documented and presumably is too catastrophic to want to canonise.
