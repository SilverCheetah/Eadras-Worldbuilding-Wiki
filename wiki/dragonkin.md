# Dragonkin

**Summary**: Dragonkin are Mendelian-bred descendants of [[dragons|dragons]] in humanoid form, inhabiting [[varkhul|Varkhul]]. They are **distinct from [[dragonborn|Dragonborn]] and the rest of the [[dragonfolk|dragonfolk]] subtypes** â€” dragonkin are not products of the dragon-created Lizard Peoples lineage but a separate, deliberately humanoid breeding line. Their innate territoriality is a structural feature of Varkhul's prison ecology.

## Origin

Dragonkin are **Mendelian-bred** â€” produced by a long, deliberate breeding program rather than by direct magical creation, divine fiat, or chaos infusion. The selective pressures of that program were oriented toward:

- **Humanoid form** â€” bodies usable in a humanoid-built world
- **Draconic territoriality** â€” the deep hostility toward intrusion that characterizes dragons in their own ranges
- **Combat capability** â€” the warrior-class dragon temperament refined down to the humanoid scale

The result is a humanoid people whose dispositions are recognizably draconic without their bodies being so. Open canon: who initiated the breeding program, when, and to what end. The fact that dragonkin are now part of Varkhul's population suggests at minimum that the program either originated outside of, or was repurposed to serve, the prison-continent regime.

## Distinction from dragonfolk

The [[dragonfolk]] umbrella covers all dragon-created sapients on Eadras: [[kobolds]], [[lizardfolk]], [[dragonborn]], and [[snakefolk]]. Those lineages are the Lizard Peoples and related dragon-craft work â€” products of magical creation by [[dragons|dragons]] themselves.

Dragonkin are **none of these**. They are not Lizard Peoples and not dragon-magical-creation. They are a humanoid breeding-program output. The two lineages share draconic ancestry in the most general sense (both ultimately trace back to dragons) but they are genealogically separate and culturally non-continuous.

A useful framing: dragonfolk are *what dragons made*; dragonkin are *what was bred from dragons*.

## Role on Varkhul

Dragonkin on Varkhul provide **natural territorial hostility**. Their very presence makes the continent's interior actively contested â€” any settlement, hideaway, or expedition within their range will be discovered, encroached upon, and eventually attacked. This is structural, not malicious; the territoriality is wired in by selection.

The combination of dragonkin territoriality, [[turkorcs|Turkorc]] amoral dangerousness, the lethal Scorched Waste, and the [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] forms the four-layered impossibility that makes the [[obsidian-sanctum|Obsidian Sanctum]] effectively unreachable.
