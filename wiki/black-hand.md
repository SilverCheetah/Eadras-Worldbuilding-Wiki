# The Black Hand

**Summary**: The Black Hand is a ten-thousand-year-old shadow conspiracy that pursues power through information, infiltration, blackmail, espionage, and worse â€” and whose top tier alone knows that everything they do is in service of freeing the imprisoned god [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] from his [[icons|Icon]] using the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]].

**Sources**: Playground generation 2nd part.txt, Black hand revised.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## What the public knows

To people outside the Hand entirely, the organization is a **boogeyman story** and an **uncertain rumor**. Either the Black Hand is everywhere â€” every misfortune, every missing person, every corrupt judge has its mark on them somewhere â€” or it does not exist, and the gullible scare each other with handprint sigils after dark. Educated skeptics treat it as folklore. Frightened commoners treat it as inevitable. Both responses are useful to the Hand. (source: Black hand revised.txt)

When the Hand grows too visible â€” when a city's coinage starts moving through Hand-controlled banks, when a temple's funerary records turn up missing, when a court's succession suddenly favors a previously irrelevant faction â€” the organization stops being a boogeyman in that region and becomes a confirmed enemy. This kicks off the next anti-Hand crusade (see **Cycles of Power**, below).

## What better-informed observers know

Those who have done the work â€” historians, intelligence officers of foreign powers, retired inquisitors, surviving anti-Hand veterans â€” know that the Black Hand is real, and that its surface goal is **power**.

Specifically: the Hand pursues power through **information gathering, blackmail, espionage, infiltration, and less savory means** (source: Black hand revised.txt). It places agents in courts, banks, temples, academies, militaries, criminal networks, and merchant guilds. It cultivates leverage rather than wielding force directly. When it does use force, it uses warlocks, summoners, and bought soldiers, not standing armies â€” except at the very rare peak of its cycles (see **Cycles of Power**).

This level of awareness does **not** include knowledge of [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] or the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]]. From the outside, even from informed positions, the Black Hand looks like an unusually persistent and unusually disciplined power-broker conspiracy with no metaphysical agenda.

## What members know

**Most people who join the Black Hand join because they want personal power**, and the Black Hand offers a ladder for it (source: Black hand revised.txt). The cover stories the cult presents in different settings are not just cover for outsiders â€” most members **believe** their cover story is the truth:

- In Xanar's successor states: *"We serve continuity."*
- In foreign courts: *"We offer alliance."*
- In temples: *"We preserve sacred death-knowledge."*
- In academies: *"We fund research."*
- In armies: *"We honor the fallen."*
- In criminal networks: *"We pay in untraceable coin."*
- In any city: *"We can make you matter."*

A temple infiltrator may genuinely believe they are preserving funerary mysteries. A court minister may genuinely believe the cult that pays their bribes is a benign continuity-cult. A magical academy researcher may genuinely believe the patron paying for their relic recovery is a private collector. **The Outer Hand has never been about resurrection or about freeing any god** (source: Black hand revised.txt). It has, for ten thousand years, been about exactly what it appears to be from the outside: information, infiltration, power.

This stability is the secret of the Hand's longevity. Most members are right about what they are doing. They simply do not know who, ultimately, benefits.

### Recruitment and control

The Hand operates a consistent loop on its own members:

1. **Tempt with the lure of power.** Initiates are recruited from people who already want a step up â€” ambitious clerks, bypassed third sons, talented Netharim from poor houses, court servants with grudges, criminals looking for a ladder. The Hand offers them what they want.
2. **Compromise them, then ensnare with blackmail.** Early "missions" are designed to produce evidence the recruit cannot afford to have surface â€” a forged signature, a poisoned cup, a body in a cellar, a heretical ritual. Once that evidence exists, the recruit is no longer choosing freely. Refusing further work means exposure.
3. **Use them to extend reach and gather information.** Compromised members are then deployed against their own institutions â€” courts, temples, banks, academies, militaries, guilds â€” to recruit further, harvest intelligence, and place new pieces.

(source: Black hand revised.txt)

The loop is self-replicating: each compromised member becomes a recruiter and a node in the information network, and each new recruit becomes new leverage. This is most of what the Outer Hand actually does, day to day.

## Grade structure

Five grades, matching the hand:

| Grade | Role | Awareness |
|---|---|---|
| **Nails** | Informants, sympathizers, message-carriers | Cell-local only |
| **Fingers** | Active agents embedded in institutions | Cell + handler |
| **Knuckles** | Handlers, cell leaders, logistical coordinators | Operational picture of their region |
| **Palm** | Strategists, ritualists, court infiltrators, senior operatives | Multi-region operational picture; aware that the Hand has older purposes than they are told |
| **Hidden Thumb** | Inner command | Knows everything below |

Even at the Palm tier, awareness of [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] is absent. Palms know the Hand is older than its current operations, that it has unspecified deeper aims, and that contradicting the Hidden Thumb means death. They do not know what the deeper aims are.

## Survival structure (the five enduring layers)

| Layer | Description |
|---|---|
| **The Open Shadow** | Criminals, smugglers, corrupt officials, and disposable agents. Visible to law enforcement; replaceable. |
| **The Mourning Societies** | Respectable fronts: grief orders, funerary charities, ancestor-preservation guilds, historical societies, hospitals, orphan funds, veteran memorial associations. Provide cover, recruitment pools, and laundered capital. |
| **The Hidden Palm** | True cult cells embedded in temples, courts, universities, banks, and mage circles. Members at this layer are operationally serious but still mostly believe the Hand pursues power for power's sake. |
| **The Bone Ledger** | Archival and genealogical apparatus preserving plans across generations; tracks bloodlines, debts, relics, blackmail, magical sites, and the conjunction calendar. Records survive personnel turnover. |
| **The Fivefold Inner Hand** | Supreme leadership holding five parts of the final plan: the Spear's trail, the prison's location, conjunction calculations, the rite of contact, and the means of awakening Xar'vion's Icon. |

Crucially, **knowledge does not flow downward** between these layers. A Hidden Palm operative knows their compartment and what they need to coordinate with adjacent compartments. They do not learn the Bone Ledger's contents or the Inner Hand's plan by promotion; promotion means doing more of the same kind of work with greater reach. Only invitation into the Inner Hand exposes the inner truth.

## Symbol

A **black handprint with one finger missing**.

Most members and most outsiders interpret the missing finger as a generic mark of incompleteness, secrecy, or the broken oath sworn against the Hand's enemies. A few esoteric traditions read it as submission â€” *the hand withholds, takes, and marks*. The original meaning â€” that the missing finger represents the family member stolen by death and the cult's promise that Xar'vion would "close the hand" when resurrection was perfected â€” has been forgotten outside the Inner Hand. References to it disappeared from the Hand's own records within two centuries of [[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] ascension.

## Methods and operational signatures

Across centuries, anti-Hand inquisitors have catalogued recurring tools the Hand uses. None of these conclusively identifies a Hand cell â€” many are independent practices the Hand has merely adopted â€” but a cluster of them is a common diagnostic:

- **Corpse-ravens** carrying encoded bone slips
- **Spirit-hounds** trained to track oathbreakers
- **Moth familiars** bred to consume ink after messages are read
- **Funerary beetles** hiding listening charms inside tombs
- **Black-veined horses** magically conditioned to never tire until death
- **Palm-spiders** whose webs form cult sigils under moonless light
- **Handless ravens** whose feet carry messages
- **Oath-moles** that chew through warded foundations
- **Sepulcher moths** that eat ink from forbidden records

The funerary theme is not coincidence â€” the Hand was founded under a death-cult cover and the apparatus persists â€” but a Hand cell in a banking district may use none of these and rely on perfectly ordinary clerks and ledgers.

### Demonic operations

In modern Eadras, demonic influence has been **almost entirely removed** from the world following the Demon War's resolution (source: Black hand revised.txt). The exceptions are deliberate. The Black Hand is one of those exceptions: it maintains a small cadre of **Warlocks** who hold pacts with demons, and **Magic Users who summon demons by true-name** when a problem requires it. This is current operational practice â€” not a faction-level corruption â€” deployed selectively by the Inner Hand and its senior handlers when the leverage is worth the metaphysical risk.

## What only the Inner Hand knows

Everything below this line is **Hidden Thumb / Fivefold Inner Hand knowledge only**. To everyone else in and around the organization, none of this exists.

### Origin

The Black Hand was founded by [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] before his ascension â€” originally as his hidden intelligence and research apparatus within the [[kingdom-of-xanar|Kingdom of Xanar]]. The Outer Hand was always built as a power-seeking conspiracy; the Inner Hand pursued the resurrection of Xar'vion's older sister [[velena|Velena]], using the apparatus of the Outer Hand to fund the search for resurrection knowledge, recovered relics, and ultimately the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] itself.

### Phases of the Inner Purpose

The Inner Hand's deepest goal has shifted three times in ten thousand years:

| Phase | Period | Inner goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Resurrection** | Pre-ascension to first ~2 centuries after Xar'vion's ascension | Resurrect [[velena|Velena]]. Records of this purpose vanished from the Hand's own archives within roughly two centuries â€” partly because [[dao|Dao's]] curse had stripped Xar'vion's motive, partly because the resurrection effort itself failed and was quietly buried. |
| **Phase 2: Free the Icon** | Mid-history (post-Demon War) until ~9,000â€“10,000 years ago (the Lost Zenith) | After Xar'vion was Icon-bound at the close of the Demon War, the Inner Hand's purpose shifted to **freeing him from his Icon**. The means were unclear; the goal drove research and relic-hunting across millennia. |
| **Phase 3: The Spear's Path** | ~9,000â€“10,000 years ago to present | The Inner Hand identified the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] as the means. The exact mechanism by which it frees Xar'vion is speculative â€” see *The two speculations* below. The [[lost-zenith|Lost Zenith]] â€” the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]] ~9,000â€“10,000 years ago â€” was the failed attempt to find and deliver the Spear during that window; the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] (preceded by the [[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]]) was the Spear-search's catastrophic diversion. |

### The two speculations

Even at the Inner Hand's deepest tier â€” and even within [[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] own thinking â€” the precise mechanism by which the Spear frees the Motionless King is **not known**. Two possibilities are entertained, both speculative:

**The freeing reading (modest).** The Spear, brought into Xar'vion's presence at the proper [[grand-conjunction|God's Day]] alignment, may simply break or unmake the Icon-binding without further apotheosis. He returns to the world as the Unrestricted Elder God he already was â€” free, dangerous, but not enlarged. Whether the Spear can do this at all is unknown.

**The second-ascension reading (ambitious).** Holding the Spear at God's Day opens the path from the [[eldritch|Source]] again (see [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|the Spear's mechanic]]). If Xar'vion can hold the Spear at that moment, the path may open to him a second time â€” granting a *second helping* of divine power on top of the first, raising him **above** the Elder Gods. A second ascension would almost assuredly free him from the Icon as a side effect: the prison cannot contain a doubly-ascended Unrestricted Elder God. Whether a second ascension is even mechanically possible is unknown.

The inner theology that wraps the second possibility is the **Second Crown Doctrine** â€” *First Crown: godhood (achieved); Second Crown: sovereignty over gods (the speculative goal); Third Crown: dominion over death (fantasy horizon)*. The Hand's inner liturgy still preserves the line *"The First Crown made him god. The gods made the Icon his coffin. The Second Crown will make the coffin a throne."*

Under either reading the operational target is identical: **deliver the Spear to Xar'vion at the proper conjunction**. Whether the result is freeing-only or a Second Crown is a question only the attempt itself will answer. (source: Black hand revised.txt â€” clarifying conversation 2026-04-30)

### Access to Xar'vion â€” and his mobility

The innermost Black Hand â€” **Those Who Stand in the Gaze** â€” can enter Xar'vion's presence and receive direct guidance. Xar'vion cannot leave his Icon or project power beyond line of sight, but those who come before him can receive commands, blessings, curses, and the **Gaze-Mark** â€” a divine imprint proving they stood in his presence. Black Hand authority at the Inner Hand level is authenticated by Gaze-Marks, which must be periodically renewed.

There is **no external prison and no guarding order** around the Icon. The line-of-sight restriction is a property of the Total Icon Binding, not a containment regime imposed from outside. (source: More corrections.txt)

This means the Icon is **mobile**, and the Inner Hand has moved it **repeatedly** across the ten thousand years since Xar'vion's binding. The [[kingdom-of-xanar|Black Throne of Xanareth]] is the Icon's most-frequently-occupied location and serves as the cult's nominal center, but at any given moment the Icon may sit elsewhere â€” under another roof, in another ruin, on a moving vessel, or hidden beneath another structure that the Hand has prepared. Where Xar'vion currently is is itself a Fivefold Inner Hand secret; even most Inner Hand members know only the Throne. (source: Black hand revised.txt â€” clarifying conversation 2026-04-30)

The mobility serves several purposes: it complicates any external attempt to locate or attack the Icon; it lets the Inner Hand maintain proximity to operations they consider critical at a given moment; and it allows them to position the Icon advantageously for the eventual conjunction at which they hope to deliver the Spear.

### The Lost Zenith

Five thousand years ago, during the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]], the Black Hand failed its central objective â€” they could not find the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]], which had remained in its original hiding place since Xar'vion's defeat. Their **diversion operation** â€” the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] â€” succeeded as cover, redirecting institutional and popular attention toward Netharim as a perceived internal threat for the duration of the Conjunction window. The Black Hand internally calls this failed conjunction the **[[lost-zenith|Lost Zenith]]**.

The Crusade also caused **catastrophic damage to the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]]** â€” the killing of three [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] through specialized Unspinning rites broke three Weave strands and triggered the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]. **This Weave damage was largely unintended.** The operational goal was diversion, not Weave sabotage:

- **The Outer Hand knew nothing of the Netharim-Weave connection.** They executed the diversion as ordered â€” promoting anti-Netharim doctrine through compromised churches and martial orders, providing cover for the Spear search. They did not know they were also cutting the Weave's living roots.
- **The Inner Hand suspected the connection** but did not fully know it. The suspicion was operationally relevant â€” they considered Weave damage a possible secondary consequence and were not opposed to it â€” but the Spear search was the goal. Weave damage was, at most, a possible bonus they could neither confirm nor predict.

(See [[anti-magic-crusade#Black Hand Intent â€” Diversion, Not Sabotage]].)

The Crusade was eventually halted by direct divine intervention â€” [[lirra|Lirra]] detected the Weave degradation and alerted the gods of the participating churches, who manifested **avatars** publicly to halt the persecution. By that point the three Windspeakers had already been killed and the Dimming had already begun. See [[anti-magic-crusade#Divine Intervention â€” How the Crusade Was Halted]].

## Cycles of Power

Across ten thousand years the Black Hand has not been one organization â€” it has been one organization passing through extreme cycles of expansion and collapse. The cycle, in stylized form: quiet recruitment â†’ growing institutional grip â†’ a precipitating exposure â†’ an anti-Hand crusade â†’ near-extinction â†’ quiet rebuilding.

| Extreme | Form |
|---|---|
| **Lowest troughs** | A handful of survivors. Sometimes the entire Hand has consisted of a single Inner Hand line and a few Bone Ledger archivists, hiding their existence completely from a hostile world. |
| **Highest peaks** | Hundreds of thousands of members. A standing army. Dozens of strongholds. Direct rule over three nations outright. At these peaks the Hand stops needing to hide; it openly governs. |

(source: Black hand revised.txt)

When the Hand grows large enough to be unmistakable â€” armies in the field, three nations under its banner, temples openly converted â€” the world coalitions that survive the rise mount **anti-Hand crusades**. These are distinct from operations the Hand itself has run (such as the Anti-Magic Crusade); they are external campaigns directed *against* the Hand by the rest of Eadras. Crusades typically reduce the Hand to one of its low troughs in a few decades, breaking its strongholds, scattering its membership, and forcing the Bone Ledger underground.

The Hand has never been wholly destroyed. The Inner Hand always survives in some form, and the Bone Ledger preserves the plan across the dark centuries. Specific named crusades and the precise three nations the Hand has ruled are still being established â€” see *Open canon* below.

## Modern remnants and offshoots

After ten thousand years and many cycles, "the Black Hand" can refer to several overlapping things:

| Type | Nature |
|---|---|
| **The Hand proper** | The true ongoing organization in its current cycle â€” currently rebuilding from the most recent crusade-driven trough; still oriented toward the Spear. |
| **False Black Hands** | Criminals using the symbol for fear and recruitment leverage. Many regional crime syndicates have nothing to do with the real Hand but find the brand profitable. |
| **Penitent Hands** | Descendants or successors of Inner Hand defectors who hunt the remnants. The [[white-palm|White Palm]] traces its lineage here. |
| **State Propaganda Labels** | Governments accuse enemies of Black Hand ties whether true or not. Especially common during anti-Hand crusades, when accusation is sufficient justification. |

The Outer Hand's purpose has always been power, so most members have nothing to be loyal-to or apostate-from. Inner Hand fragmentation has occurred but only on a small scale.
