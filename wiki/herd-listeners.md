# Herd-Listeners

**Summary**: **Herd-Listeners** are rare mortals permitted by [[unicorns|unicorn]] herds to learn recurring symbols, warnings, and memory-patterns from herd-deep memory. They mediate between unicorn warning and mortal institutions â€” particularly [[arathi-geo-substrate|Arathi archaeology]], [[vault-concordat|Vault Concordat]] proceedings, wound-site identification, and geo-substrate distress assessment. The role is **chosen by the herd, not taken by application**; mortals cannot train themselves to be Herd-Listeners. The work is **translation, not transcription** â€” Herd-Listeners convert pressure, aversion, symbol, and resonance into usable warning, knowing that unicorn memory is not citation-shaped.

## What the role is

A Herd-Listener is a **mortal who carries the herd's warnings into mortal institutional space**. The herd shows what it remembers; the Herd-Listener interprets and reports. The work has no equivalent in any other Eadran tradition â€” it is specifically the intersection between [[unicorns#The herd as trauma-healer|unicorn herd-memory]] and the mortal scholarly need for usable knowledge.

The role's necessity arises from the fundamental shape of [[unicorns#Layers of unicorn memory|unicorn memory]]. The herd may *know* that a place should not be awakened, but it does not necessarily know *the engineering reason*. The herd may *remember* a wound-pattern without remembering *which civilisation suffered it*. The herd may *recognise* a substrate's distress signature without being able to *describe* the distress in mortal categories.

> ***"A dragon may know how to open the vault. A unicorn may know whether opening it will make the land scream."***

The Herd-Listener takes the unicorn's *whether* and translates it into mortal language the [[vault-concordat|Concordat]] can use.

## How a Herd-Listener is chosen

Mortals **cannot apply**. The herd selects.

The selection criteria are not legible to mortals. The herd's reasons are not always communicable. What is observed:

- Selected mortals are typically people who have already had **non-aggressive contact** with the herd â€” usually wanderers, healers, [[druids|Druids]], frontier folk, or specific [[katharim|Katharim]] death-priests whose work brought them near herd territory
- The candidate is **usually unaware** that they are being considered
- The candidate often has **personal experience** of the kind of wound the herd intends them to recognise â€” a grief that calibrates them to grief-recognition, a fear that calibrates them to wound-recognition
- The candidate is **not always magically gifted** in any conventional sense; the role does not require Netharim, Lexanari, Druid, or priestly capability

Selection is **rare**. A single herd may select one Herd-Listener per several human generations. Sometimes a herd has no Herd-Listener for centuries.

## The first contact

A herd that has selected a candidate **arranges contact**. The contact is typically:

- A solitary or near-solitary encounter
- In territory the unicorn knows the candidate has reason to travel
- At a time when the candidate is alone, alert, and emotionally accessible
- Mediated by a single unicorn (typically a [[unicorns#Modern unicorn life patterns|Seasonal Strayer]] in scout role) acting as the herd's emissary

The first contact establishes a *channel* between the candidate's mind and the herd's outermost memory layers. The channel is not telepathy in the standard sense; it is a sustained alignment that lets the candidate receive symbol, pressure, and resonance from the herd.

The candidate is **always free to refuse**. Refusal is honoured; the unicorn withdraws. No herd has ever been recorded as retaliating for refusal.

If the candidate accepts, the role begins. There is no ceremony, no oath, no certificate. The candidate simply *now* hears the herd when the herd has something to say.

## What Herd-Listeners do

The role is **translation, not transcription**. Herd-Listeners do not record unicorn memory as if from dictation; they convert one register of knowing into another.

### Receive

A Herd-Listener perceives herd-warnings in registers that vary by individual and by herd. Common modes:

- **Aversive pressure** â€” a felt resistance to going somewhere, doing something, opening something
- **Symbolic dream** â€” visual or narrative content that carries the warning encoded
- **Resonance-feeling** â€” a substrate or place "tastes" wrong; the wrongness is informative
- **Pattern-recognition** â€” the Herd-Listener notices that several apparently unrelated phenomena form a pattern the herd has flagged
- **Direct memory-touch** â€” rarer; brief, controlled exposure to a piece of herd-deep memory that the herd judges the listener can hold

### Interpret

The Herd-Listener interprets the reception into mortal categories. This is where translation becomes craft. A felt aversion to a vault entrance is not yet a Concordat report; the Herd-Listener must decide whether it indicates [[arathi-geo-substrate#Five substrate clades|Oru-Sha War-Substrate]], a [[unreceived|demonic residue]] from the [[demon-war|Demon War]], a Context-Bound failure mode, or some other category â€” and must report accordingly.

### Report

Herd-Listeners testify in [[vault-concordat|Concordat]] hearings, advise [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] research programs, brief [[woundwatch|Woundwatch]] patrols, and occasionally appear in mortal courts where wound-recognition is at stake. Their testimony is recognised by all signatory Concordat institutions; it has formal weight.

The Herd-Listener's report is always **carefully bounded**. The Listener says what the herd shows. The Listener does not extrapolate. If the herd warns against opening a vault but does not show *why*, the Listener reports *the herd warns; the reason is not disclosed*. The Concordat institutions are accustomed to this register.

## What Herd-Listeners do NOT do

- **Channel decisions** â€” they do not vote in mortal institutions; they advise
- **Compel** â€” their warnings can be refused; many are
- **Translate technical specifications** â€” the herd does not have these to give
- **Speak for individual unicorns** â€” they speak for the herd's collective memory, not for any one unicorn's view
- **Reveal the unicorns' identities** â€” most herds remain hidden; the Listener does not name them, locate them, or expose them

## Recognition (FR 22)

The [[baldaranic-council-cycle#Phase II â€” The Arathi Council|Arathi Council]] formally recognised Herd-Listeners as legitimate testimony-givers in Concordat proceedings during FR 22 â€” the **Herd-Listener Recognition** is one of the Council's institutional outputs. Before recognition, Herd-Listeners' warnings carried only informal weight; some Concordat-precursor proceedings dismissed them. After recognition, their testimony has formal standing.

Recognition was contested. Some [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] traditionalists argued that herd-memory is too pattern-shaped to be admissible alongside Lirran scholarly assessment. The counter-argument prevailed: the herd's wound-recognition has been right enough times across enough centuries that institutional dismissal was producing predictable failure.

## Dangers to the Herd-Listener

The work is not safe:

- **Memory-weather addiction** â€” Herd-Listeners who linger in the herd's emotional field can become unable to function in ordinary mortal society
- **Pattern over-recognition** â€” without discipline, the Listener begins seeing herd-warnings in every shadow
- **Identity drift** â€” long contact with herd-mind can blur the Listener's individual self in directions that don't always heal
- **Bearing wound-kept memory** â€” Listeners are sometimes shown wound-kept memory the herd cannot fully hold; the burden settles into the Listener's life
- **Political exposure** â€” actors who don't want the herd's warnings to reach the Concordat may target the Listener directly (the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] is the obvious culprit)

The herd protects its Listeners where it can, but it cannot protect them everywhere. Several Listeners have died in service. Their replacements are not chosen for years afterward.

## Famous (and rumoured) Herd-Listeners

The specific names of active Herd-Listeners are typically not public â€” both for the Listener's safety and for the herd's privacy. Some historical figures have been retrospectively identified as Herd-Listeners by [[katharim|Katharim]] scholarship; their work is now studied as exemplary.

The specific catalog of historical and current Herd-Listeners is open canon.

## Open canon

- Whether **multiple Herd-Listeners** can serve a single herd simultaneously, or whether the herd typically maintains only one at a time
- Whether a Herd-Listener can be **dismissed by the herd** mid-service, and what that looks like
- The **first Herd-Listener killed in service** â€” institutional response, herd response, mortal consequence
- How Herd-Listeners interact with [[hearth-witness-mandate|Hearth-Witnesses]] in divine offices â€” overlapping or distinct roles?
- Whether **trollish geobiologists** are functionally similar Herd-Listeners for substrate-memory rather than unicorn-memory
- Can a Herd-Listener become *addicted* to entering herd-memory weather? (Working canon: yes, this is a documented failure mode.)
