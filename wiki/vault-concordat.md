## Why a Concordat

Without coordination, [[arathi-geo-substrate|Arathi geo-substrate]] handling becomes a race-to-the-bottom across kingdoms, treasure-hunting syndicates, mage academies, and priesthoods. Each actor has incentive to:

- Open vaults before rivals can
- Conceal substrate condition to avoid quarantine costs
- Activate Context-Bound systems to test capability
- Mine substrate as commodity
- Avoid sharing dangerous-find intelligence

The Council was explicit: the [[arathi|Arathi]] catastrophe is precisely the pattern of *individual ambition exceeding civilizational stewardship capacity*. Letting modern states repeat the pattern at vault-by-vault scale produces small Arathi-catastrophes at predictable intervals.

The Concordat exists to make coordinated, slow, ethical vault handling the institutional default and individual vault-rushing the deviation.

## Tensions in the negotiation

Several specific tensions slow the FR 18â€“25 negotiations:

- **Bold vs Orden** on whether Concordat standards apply equally to [[conduit-cities|conduit-tied]] substrate that Bold considers within its sovereign jurisdiction
- **Mage academies vs Order Sharanel** on which institutions get primary scholarly-access rights
- **Trollish polities vs human signatories** on the weight given to trollish geobiologist testimony when it conflicts with human scholarly assessment
- **Amunorian Empire vs everyone** on whether the Empire's pre-existing Arathi-relic claims grandfather into the new framework
- **Frontier kingdoms** that lack the institutional capacity to comply but have vaults under their jurisdiction

The Council anticipated these tensions but did not pre-resolve them. The reform's success depends on multi-decade institutional patience that the [[first-rebrightening|First Rebrightening]] may or may not sustain.
