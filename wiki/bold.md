# Bold

**Summary**: Bold (B-O-L-D) is **one of the oldest cities in the world** — and in the modern era, the **Bold Conduit Republic** (alternately *Spirehold* in older usage), an elected merchant-prince city-state and the most independent major polity of the modern conduit-trade world. Bold is dominated by the great Netharim fortress popularly known as the **Tower of Seven Spires** (which is the same building as, or stands directly atop, the **oldest [[order-sharanel|Tower of Lirra]]** — the founding chapter-house of the Order Sharanel, built atop one of the fourteen world-piercing [[magic-in-eadras|conduit]] ends). The Republic is a fierce commercial rival of the [[orden|Orden's Conduit Compact]], whose Compact merchants Bold derisively calls *the Gray Ledger* and who in turn call Bold *upstart disruptors*. Modern Bold is also famous for the waste-and-salvage empire of [[harvey-wash|Harvey Wash]], the goblin philanthropist who runs the *Golden Road* from a processing yard well outside the expanding city walls.

**Sources**: More corrections.txt; Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt; Grok Chat 5-8-26.txt

## Significance

Bold has two intertwined claims to importance:

1. **Magical** — it hosts the **first Tower of Lirra ever built**. The Order Sharanel was founded shortly after Netharim were created, and its founding chapter-house was raised in Bold — making the Tower of Bold the **oldest** of the seven surviving Towers, and a likely seat of seniority within the Order's federated structure (see [[order-sharanel]]). Whether Bold's Tower carries **formal** institutional authority over the Order's six other surviving Towers, or only ceremonial first-among-equals seniority, is open canon.
2. **Political** — Bold is an **elected republic** — a fiercely independent city-state that does not belong to the [[orden|Orden's Conduit Compact]] and has never accepted the Compact's tariff harmonization. (source: Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt; Grok Chat 5-8-26.txt)

Bold is one of the **oldest cities in the world** — a deep-time settlement continuously inhabited and rebuilt across multiple eras of civilization. It is also one of the major modern [[conduit-cities|Conduit Cities]] (see [[worldbuilding-needs#Modern present-day political geography|Worldbuilding Needs]] for the broader context).

## The Republic — modern political form

Bold's modern political identity is the **Bold Conduit Republic** (used interchangeably with the older designation *Spirehold*). The city is governed by an elected **Spirewarden** — a Doge-like office drawn from the merchant-prince families of the city for a strictly limited term — supported by a council of guild representatives and a permanent **Windspeaker advisor** seconded by the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] from the resident Tower of Lirra. The Republic deliberately fragments power: the Spirewarden cannot succeed themselves consecutively, no single merchant-prince family has ever held the Spirewardenship more than three times in five generations, and major decisions require concurrence between the Spirewarden, the guild council, and the Windspeaker advisor.

The republican form is a **deliberate and proud twist** on Bold's older history (the city was historically a kingdom, and traces of the royal era — the ceremonial castle compound, certain dynastic processions still observed at festivals, the language of Bold's older laws — are still visible). The transition to republic happened across the centuries following the [[demon-war|Demon War]], when the older royal line did not survive intact and the surviving merchant-prince families and conduit-city institutions cooperated to govern in its place. By the modern era, Bold is comfortably and confidently a republic and considers the kingdom-era a closed chapter.

### The merchant-prince economy

Bold's commercial character is **aggressive, opportunistic, and innovative**. The Republic is built on:

- **Sea-ley trade** — long-distance maritime commerce that uses the Tower of Seven Spires' conduit access for **Spire-Step jumps** (short magical-transport leaps that can move a ship between two paired conduit ends or between a ship and a Tower in moments). Bold privateer-merchant fleets are the only operation in the modern world that uses Spire-Step at commercial scale.
- **Relic auctions** — Bold's auction houses are the largest and most discreet in the modern world for pre-Cataclysm artifacts, magitek antiques, recovered crystalmancy components, and unique Arathi-era objects. The auctions are heavily attended, lightly regulated, and the source of more than one diplomatic incident.
- **Magitek innovation** — Bold workshops, in close cooperation with [[gnomes|gnome]] master-inventors and [[goblins|goblin]] networks, are at the front of modern magitek experimentation. Many of the prototype magitek items that later become standard Conduit Compact products are Bold inventions.
- **Black-market pre-Cataclysm artifacts** — open secret. Bold's official position is that it holds the strictest auction-floor regulations in the modern world; Bold's actual position is that whatever the Order Sharanel and the major civilizations are not currently willing to police, Bold's auction-adjacent merchants are willing to handle. Many of the more controversial Arathi-era objects in modern circulation passed through Bold at some point.

The Republic's standing motto, half-joking and half-not, is *"the Compact owns the land. We own the rest."* Their merchants are flashier, riskier, and more artistic than the Compact's stolid Hanseatic operators, and the rivalry produces both enormous commercial energy and constant low-level diplomatic friction.

Under the [[modernity|modernity framework]], Bold is a **Stage 3 mercantile-urban civilization approaching Stage 4 — magi-industrial**: it is the modern polity furthest along the magitek-and-conduit-infrastructure axis without yet being mechanized at state scale. Its conduit access, Spire-Step freight, magitek workshops, crystal communications, and dense urban administration are characteristic Stage 4 features grafted onto an unmistakably Stage 3 merchant-republic foundation. The aesthetic is **arcane urban modernization** rather than Victorian industrialization — enchanted cranes, conduit freight, magical refrigeration, civic glow-stones at dusk; no smokestacks. Bold is the closest modern Eadras comes to the [[arathi|lost Stage 5 ceiling]], and the gap remains immense.

### Culture

Bold's culture is **artistic, intrigue-soaked, and visually flashy** — closer in feel to a Renaissance Italian merchant republic than to anything else in the modern world. Public spectacle is a civic art form: **masked carnivals**, river-pageants on the city's canal-and-ley-water network, civic theater at extraordinarily high quality, duels of wit and contract that draw spectators, and festivals built around publicly resolving the year's most spectacular commercial disputes.

Notable cultural features:

- **Women hold significant merchant-prince seats.** Several of Bold's most famous Spirewardens of the past three centuries have been women, and at any given moment a substantial fraction of the guild council seats are held by women. This is genuinely meritocratic in the sense that the wealthier women's families historically were the ones who kept producing capable heirs across multiple generations, and is regarded by the rest of the modern world (with some envy) as a marker of how Bold's republican structure produces a different talent pool than older monarchies do.
- **Masked festivals.** The signature Bold celebration is the year-end masked carnival, in which identities are formally suspended for three nights and public performances of all kinds (including illicit ones — see [[house-sitharis|House Sitharis]] visiting delegations and [[black-hand|Black Hand]] cells alike) take advantage of the cover. Bold's ordinary policing rules are formally relaxed during the festival; the Tower of Seven Spires' ward-network is heightened to compensate.
- **Spire-Step.** Bold's residents are unusually comfortable with magical transport. Daily life in the inner city includes occasional Spire-Step transit in ways most other modern cities would consider grossly extravagant.
- **Public spectacle of wealth.** Bold merchant-princes display their wealth far more openly than Compact merchants do — gilded gondolas on the canals, heraldic illusion-displays on family palazzos, sponsored public art everywhere.

The Republic's relations with [[house-sitharis|House Sitharis]] are quietly cordial — both organizations operate outside the Conduit Compact's tariff structure, both are mocked by Compact merchants for the same reasons, and Sitharis products move along Bold's privateer routes with a steadiness that the Compact would prefer not to think about.

## The Tower of Bold (a.k.a. the Tower of Seven Spires)

Like all [[order-sharanel|Towers of Lirra]], the Tower of Bold is built **on top of a [[magic-in-eadras|conduit end]]** — a place where one of the seven world-piercing Weave conduits emerges to the surface. The conduit appears as a magic sink to ordinary perception and as a column of unbearable magical power to magical perception; the Tower is built to ring this column without occupying it. (source: More corrections.txt)

In Bold's civic and popular usage the same building is known as the **Tower of Seven Spires** — a "major Netharim fortress" in the city's skyline (source: Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt). The two names refer to the same institution: the Order Sharanel call it the *Tower of Lirra of Bold*; Bold's citizenry and the kingdom's court call it the *Tower of Seven Spires*. Whether the *seven spires* of the popular name reference the seven world-piercing conduits, the Order's seven surviving Towers worldwide, the literal architecture of the building, or something else entirely is undocumented.

## City structure

Bold has **expanding city walls** — meaning the city has grown across its long history and continues to grow, with successive walls drawing wider perimeters as the population has overspilled the previous ones. The ceremonial old castle compound (a holdover from Bold's pre-republican era, now used for civic ceremonies and as one of several Spirewarden audience venues) and the Tower sit within the inner walls; many older institutions are likewise gated within historical perimeters.

The expansion is the reason [[harvey-wash|Harvey Wash]] deliberately placed his main processing dump **well outside the current city walls** when he founded his operation: he calculated that even at Bold's growth rate, the dump would not be encroached on for centuries.

## Modern notable institutions and figures

- **The Spirewarden's residence** — the working seat of the elected head of the Republic. Located in the central merchant-prince district rather than in the older castle compound.
- **The ceremonial old castle compound** — preserved from Bold's pre-republican era; used for state occasions, civic ceremonies, and certain Spirewarden audiences. Not a royal residence and has not been one for generations.
- **The Tower of Seven Spires / Tower of Lirra of Bold** — see above.
- **The Bold Auction House** — the largest and most prestigious commercial auction venue in the modern world; a key node of pre-Cataclysm relic trade.
- **The Privateer-Merchant Fleet** — Bold's signature commercial-and-naval institution; combines mercantile and limited military authority under guild oversight.
- **[[harvey-wash|Harvey Wash]] and the Golden Road** — Bold's goblin-run waste-collection and salvage empire. Harvey holds the title *King of the Golden Road* among his workers and is a recognized civic philanthropist (soup kitchens, safe sleeping places, fair-wage employment) entirely funded out of fertilizer, urea, and salvage revenue. He has a standing compact with Bold's **Thieves' Guild** (they don't steal from his operation; he throws them a private *humdinger* once a year) and an ongoing — so far unsuccessful — bid to establish a formal **Scavengers' Guild** in the city.

## Open canon

- **Bold's pre-republican royal-era history** — Bold was a kingdom once, and the transition to republic happened across the centuries following the Demon War. The royal line's actual end and the specific transitional events are open canon.
- **The geography of Bold** (continent, climate, neighboring polities).
- **Whether Bold predates the Tower or grew up around it.** Given Bold's status as "one of the oldest cities in the world," this question now carries additional weight — Bold may predate the Order Sharanel and even the founding of the first Tower.
- **The current Spirewarden** — name, character, current public projects.
- **The full guild council composition.**
- **Bold's relationship with [[house-sitharis|House Sitharis]]** beyond the cordial-but-quiet documented baseline.
- **Specific named pre-Cataclysm relics that have passed through the Bold Auction House.**
- **The naming origin of "Tower of Seven Spires"** — whether the seven references the seven conduits, the Order's seven surviving Towers, the building's literal architecture, or something else.
- **Whether the Tower of Bold's Council of Seven holds any inter-Tower authority.**
- **The conduit's "Far-Kin" partner** — Bold's conduit pierces the planet and emerges at one other location; that location either hosts another of the seven surviving Towers, or hosts one of the six destroyed Crusade sites, or is the Verdant Scar.
- **Bold's pilgrim status.** [[worldbuilding-needs|Worldbuilding Needs]] lists Bold among speculative candidates for a major pilgrim route alongside Arathyn, the Verdant Scar, and Ratharil. Whether Bold is in fact a pilgrim destination, and for which faith(s), is undefined.
