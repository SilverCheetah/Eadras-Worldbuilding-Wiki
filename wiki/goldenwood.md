# Goldenwood

**Summary**: **Goldenwood** is an [[alien-objects|alien-origin]] tree species growing in scattered groves and individual trees across Eadras. Its substance was formed under the physical and metaphysical laws of *another Mythos* and only partially conforms to Eadras's own. Goldenwood is **extraordinarily durable** (dozens of resharpenings to cut a sapling), **biologically persistent after separation** (a stave-blank may remain alive for years on minimal water and indirect sunlight), and **propagable from cuttings** (severed portions can be planted and may eventually grow into new trees, in a process that behaves *more like continuation than reproduction*). Goldenwood is the clearest known canonical example of *alien life coexisting within Eadras without fully belonging to it* — and the canonical demonstration that alien material need not be hostile.

## What Goldenwood is

Goldenwood is not merely a magical tree. Its substance was formed under the laws of another Mythos and therefore exhibits material behaviors that native Eadras biology and forestry cannot fully explain.

Goldenwood is categorized alongside other [[alien-objects|alien objects]] in Eadras canon — but it is the only known **alien-origin living organism**. The Worldender, the [[worldender-crystal|Worldender Crystal]], and the Cauldron of the Unnamed Voice are inanimate; Goldenwood is alive, breeds, grows, and behaves as a self-perpetuating biological lineage. That difference is one of the structurally important things scholars and Druids continue to study.

## Appearance

Mature Goldenwood trees possess:

- **Greyish bark with a faint golden sheen** — dull at distance, warm-gold under angled light
- **Green-gold trefoil leaves** — observers commonly report the coloration appears *slightly more vivid than it should be*, without crossing into unnatural shimmer
- **Dense golden heartwood** — the prized core that gives the species its name
- **Extremely slow growth** — saplings to mature trees take generations
- **A subtle metallic warmth in direct sunlight**

The visual signature is recognisable to anyone who has seen one tree; the difficulty is *finding* them, since Goldenwood does not form continuous forests.

## Durability

Goldenwood possesses extraordinary structural durability far beyond native Eadras wood:

- **Extreme resistance to cutting** — a sharpened blade may require dozens of resharpenings merely to cut through a sapling or thick branch
- **Exceptional resistance to splitting**
- **High resilience under stress**
- **Very slow degradation**
- **Long-term structural stability** measured in centuries

Traditional axes dull rapidly against mature Goldenwood. Harvesting is therefore slow, specialized, and labor-intensive. A small Goldenwood grove may sustain a single crafter's work for an entire career without being fully harvested.

## Living after cutting

Goldenwood remains **biologically alive** after separation from the parent tree, for extraordinarily long periods. A cut branch, stave blank, or bow stave may remain viable for **years** on minimal care.

Requirements are minimal:

- Occasional water
- Indirect sunlight
- Protection from total desiccation

Even worked lumber — bow staves, ritual implements, structural beams — may continue exhibiting low-level living behavior long after the cutting. The phenomenon is well-attested enough to have produced a widespread folk proverb:

> *"Goldenwood never fully believes it has been cut."*

## Propagation

Severed portions of Goldenwood — including relatively small viable cuttings — **can be planted and may eventually grow into new trees**.

The process is extremely slow. Cuttings establish themselves over years rather than seasons; growth thereafter resembles the parent species' natural slow tempo. **The behavior is more like *continuation* than *reproduction*.** Some Druids and philosophers argue that Goldenwood does not conceptualize separation the way native Eadras biology does — that what mortals see as *cutting and replanting* may, in Goldenwood's native cosmological terms, be closer to *the tree extending itself*.

This interpretation is contested but persistent enough to enter craft tradition (see [[#Craft traditions]]).

## Uses

Goldenwood is highly prized for:

- **Bows** — Goldenwood bows maintain elasticity and structural memory over immense timescales; the most valuable Goldenwood objects in Eadras
- **Staves** — both martial and ritual
- **Ritual implements** — for traditions that prize the continuity-after-cutting property
- **Heirloom tools** — knives, hammers, drawknives whose Goldenwood handles outlast the steel
- **Reinforced handles** for weapons, tools, and ritual instruments
- **Long-duration structural pieces** — beams, ridge-poles, doorframes for buildings meant to outlast their builders by centuries
- **Rare magical applications** that benefit from alien-origin material's resistance to native decay assumptions

Many Goldenwood objects remain functional for centuries when maintained. Some old bows and staves are rumored to still be *technically alive* — a claim impossible to verify in practice but consistent with the species' documented behavior.

## Craft traditions

Working Goldenwood requires specialized techniques developed over generations:

- **Rotational cutting** — turning the blade through the cut rather than levering
- **Alternating blades** — using multiple blades in sequence as each dulls
- **Slow shaving** instead of deep chopping
- **Heat-softened scoring** before primary cutting
- **Long curing periods** measured in seasons or years

Some Druidic circles insist that **Goldenwood responds poorly to anger, haste, or violent harvesting** — that the wood's own resistance increases proportionately to the harvester's aggression. Whether this is superstition, observed empirical regularity, or a real interaction between the alien substance and local intentionality is debated.

Certain crafters **leave cut staves partially rooted during shaping** so the wood *"understands continuity"* — keeping the stave attached to the parent tree by a thin connecting band throughout the shaping process and only severing it at the final step. Whether this measurably improves the resulting object or is purely ritual remains contested.

## Relationship to alien objects

Goldenwood is categorized as [[alien-objects|alien-origin]] material. It originates outside the local Mythos and therefore does not fully obey Eadras's physical and metaphysical assumptions. Its unusual durability, persistence, and biological continuity are believed to derive from physical laws that differ subtly from those governing native Eadras life.

But Goldenwood **demonstrates that alien-origin matter is not automatically hostile**. The Worldender devastated [[arathi-cataclysm|a continent]]; the [[worldender-crystal|Worldender Crystal]] is hoarded under Dao's supervision because its alien properties make it both useful and dangerous; the Cauldron of the Unnamed Voice in the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy's]] Thorn Sanctum is treated with extreme caution. Goldenwood, by contrast, grows quietly in scattered groves, supports working crafters, and produces heirlooms that outlast generations.

It is, however, **ontologically non-native**. Whatever its peaceful coexistence, it does not belong to Eadras in the way an oak or a hearth-fire does. That distinction matters in some Druidic registers (see [[#Druidic interpretations]]).

## Druidic interpretations

Druidic opinion on Goldenwood varies across [[fae-da-nar|Fae-da'Nar]] and other Druidic traditions:

- **The tolerated immigrant reading** — Goldenwood is an adopted lifeform that has earned its place by behaving well; it is treated as a fellow plant-being with strange origins but compatible practice
- **The proof-of-coexistence reading** — Goldenwood is *evidence* that alien life can coexist peacefully with Eadras; some Druidic teachings cite it explicitly as the foundational example that *aliennes is not enmity*
- **The uneasy guests reading** — Goldenwood obeys ecological assumptions not negotiated into the living relationships of the world; it is permitted but watched
- **The dangerous-groves minority** — large unmanaged Goldenwood groves are metaphysically dangerous; alien-origin material at sufficient density and age might begin to behave according to its own cosmological rules rather than Eadras's. This minority view recommends Goldenwood groves be kept small, harvested actively, and never allowed to become genuinely wild

The four readings coexist within Druidic discourse. None has fully prevailed; particular Circles and Druidic individuals lean toward one or another depending on local Goldenwood-grove behavior and tradition.

## Scholarly theories

Common theories regarding Goldenwood's nature, none reaching consensus:

- **Different entropy conditions** — Goldenwood evolved in a Mythos where local entropy behaved differently, explaining its slow degradation
- **Distributed biological continuity** — Goldenwood treats its individual organisms as nodes of a larger continuity rather than discrete individuals, explaining the "continuation not reproduction" behavior
- **Different injury cognition** — Goldenwood experiences injury (cutting, breaking, dying) on a different time-scale or metaphysical register than native Eadras life
- **Magical or cellular partial resistance** — the cellular or magical structure of Goldenwood partially resists local decay assumptions, allowing the wood to maintain coherence longer than native physics should permit
- **Preserved foreign cosmological rules** — Goldenwood preserves foreign cosmological rules at microscopic scales, carrying tiny pockets of its native Mythos's physics inside its substance

No theory has been confirmed; the species is studied carefully because the answer would advance Eadran understanding of alien-life ecology in general.

## Common proverbs

- *"Goldenwood remembers the tree."*
- *"Never rush a Goldenwood cut."*
- *"A dead oak rots. A dead Goldenwood waits."*
- *"Strong as Goldenwood, patient as the hand shaping it."*

## Open canon

- **Which Mythos** did Goldenwood originate from? — open canon; no consensus exists, and no record of the species arriving on Eadras has survived
- **Truly harmless, or merely stable?** — the dangerous-groves minority view may eventually prove correct; insufficient observation
- **Can Goldenwood hybridize** with native Eadras trees? — open canon; no documented hybrid exists, but the question is occasionally tested
- Are **ancient Goldenwood groves partially interconnected** — a single distributed organism rather than a collection of trees? — speculative
- **Alien-entity interface** — can alien-origin entities (whatever those might be) interface with Goldenwood more easily than with native Eadras matter? — open canon
- **Exposure to [[arathi-geo-substrate|Arathi geo-substrate]]** — open canon; the two alien-influenced biological systems have not been documented in deliberate contact
- **Distributed alien organism** — could a sufficiently old Goldenwood forest become a single distributed alien organism, in the way the dangerous-groves Druids fear?
- **Why is post-cutting viability so long?** — the documented phenomenon is well-attested; the mechanism is not understood
- **Other alien-origin plant species** hidden in Eadras ecosystems — Goldenwood is the only documented species. Whether others exist is open canon
