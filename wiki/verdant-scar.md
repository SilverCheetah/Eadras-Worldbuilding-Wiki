# Verdant Scar

**Summary**: The Verdant Scar (Arath-Veyr, "the Green Wound") is a vast, magically charged forest that grew over the ruins of the [[arathi|Arathi]] land-based focusing array after the [[arathi-cataclysm]], serving as a living magical regulator over a broken ley-vault network.

**Sources**: Playground generation 1st part.txt, New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt, Crystalmancy.txt, EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt (2026-05-31, northwestern-continent placement + antipode coordinate)

**Last updated**: 2026-05-31

---

## Overview

The Verdant Scar grew over the ruins of the Arathi civilization's inland receiving/focusing array — the ground installation Kudur-eṭir used to receive and focus the Falling Star's energy during the [[arathi-cataclysm]]. It is not the impact site of the capital ([[altea|Altea]] was annihilated entirely and left no ruins); it is the overgrown remains of **Arath-Veyr**, a separate land-based catastrophe site on one of the surviving non-Altean continents.

### Origin: uncontrolled Verdantite overgrowth

The Scar's defining biology — its ability to fuse crystal with living matter, its self-regulating root-mind, its near-symbiotic relationship between rock and forest — is the legacy of **uncontrolled [[crystalmancy#4. Verdantite — Life Emeralds / Grove Beryls|Verdantite]] overgrowth** following the Cataclysm. Verdantite is the Arathi crystal lineage of growth acceleration and biological integration, used in life their lifetime for living architecture, regenerative armor, and the famous vitrified-wood technique. The Arath-Veyr ritual array contained substantial Verdantite infrastructure under continuous singer-regulation. When the singers died and the regulation collapsed, Verdantite did what Verdantite does without restraint: it ate outward through stone, soil, and life until it became its own ecology. The "Green Wound" is what Verdantite looks like when no one is alive to govern it. (source: Crystalmancy.txt)

### Antipodal location and the conduit end

The Verdant Scar / Arath-Veyr sits at the **antipodal point from [[altea|Altea]]** — exactly opposite on the planet — at *the opposite end of the conduit* (source: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt). This is the geographic relationship inherited from the pre-Cataclysm Arathi conduit system, when Altea and Arath-Veyr stood at the two ends of one of the seven Arathi Eldritch Conduits.

The Verdant Scar is also one of the **fourteen world-piercing [[magic-in-eadras|conduit ends]]** of the modern Weave that [[lirra|Lirra]] wove during the Demon War. Of the fourteen modern conduit ends, thirteen had Towers of Lirra built on them; **the Verdant Scar is the one site where no Tower was ever built.** The Arathi-era ruins of Arath-Veyr already stood at this site before Lirra created the Weave, and the Verdant Scar's distributed regulating mind grew over those ruins. When Lirra's new conduit infrastructure reached out to its fourteen surface points, the Verdant Scar was already serving as a living, ecological regulator — and she never raised an institutional Tower to replace what the forest was already doing. (source: More corrections.txt, clarifying conversation 2026-04-30 — *"there was never a true 'tower' at the Verdant Scar, just the arathi ruins"*)

### The shifted surface and the antipode

During the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]], extreme plate tectonics — newly initiated by the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]]'s impact — **shifted the surface underneath the conduits**. The conduits themselves are cosmologically anchored and did not move, but the planetary surface drifted relative to them. As a result, the surface points where the conduits emerge today are **not** the same surface points where they emerged pre-Cataclysm. (source: More corrections.txt — clarifying conversation 2026-04-30)

This is why pairing the Verdant Scar with [[altea|Altea]] as conduit Far-Kin is a *historical* relationship from the Arathi era; in the modern Weave, the Verdant Scar's conduit emerges at a different antipodal surface point than where Altea used to be. **The Verdant Scar's modern antipode is one of the six [[order-sharanel|Towers of Lirra]] ruined in the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]]** — meaning the conduit running through the Verdant Scar was one of the three conduits broken during the Crusade, and its other end was a Tower until the Crusade brought it down. On the modern map this ruined antipode is fixed at **(1175, 639)** — in the [[halaran|Halaran]] heartland, by the forest-river (the antipode of the Scar's own ~151, 385; see [[eadras-world-map#Marked coordinates and antipodes]]). The Verdant Scar's regulating-forest mind compensates for some of the loss; the ruined antipode does not.

The forest acts as a **distributed magical regulating mind** — every root, fungal web, creature herd, and whispering leaf participates in stabilizing the broken ley-vault network beneath. It is not sentient the way a person is; it is more like a dreaming immune system.

Warden wisdom: *"The forest does not forgive Arath. It remembers Arath so the world need not bleed that way again."*

## Geography

### Continental placement

On the modern world map, the Verdant Scar sits on the **northwestern continent** at map coordinate **~151, 385 (~22°N, temperate)** — the **same landmass as the thriving [[dragon-empire|Dragon Empire]].** The Green Wound is thus an ungovernable, semi-sentient Verdantite forest at the edge of (or within) the meritocratic dragon state; the [[#Veyran Wardens|Veyran Wardens]] hold that frontier. Whether the relationship is a revered no-go zone, a tense border, or a Warden-held march under imperial reach is open canon (see [[dragon-empire]] and [[eadras-world-map]]). (source: EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt, 2026-05-31)

The Scar lies in a basin where three mountain chains once met around an Arathi ritual site. After the shattering, the land collapsed into terraces — concentric rings of forest descending toward a hidden inner hollow. Rain flows inward but disappears into root-chasms and returns as mist.

### Regions

| Region | Description |
|---|---|
| **The Green Mantle** | Outer woodland; villages, Warden paths, Glasshart sightings. Navigable by hunters, pilgrims, scholars, and smugglers. |
| **The Tilted Miles** | Middle forest where distance warps; paths lengthen, hills appear where maps say none should be, ruins surface and sink. Spatial and perceptual instability caused by damaged Eldritch reflections and [[feywild]]/[[shadowdark]] resonance. |
| **The Road of Wanting** | Ancient imperial avenue haunted by Want-Wolves. Roads are metaphysically "worn" by purpose. |
| **The Sunken Forums** | Buried Arathi civic districts where Marble Mourners gather. |
| **The Root-Crucible** | The sealed inner hollow over the broken ley-vault network. Contains the *shape of the desire Kudur-eṭir lost* — not his soul, but a motivational absence given form. Referred to as "The Last Vault of Arath" (Remnant Houses), "The Place Under the First Root" (Wardens), and "The Shut Door" (Pilgrims). |

## Creatures

### Glassharts
Appear like tall, pale, antlered deer — but their bones are translucent and their antlers grow like branching crystal. At dawn, Sourceshine scatters colored light through their antlers. When threatened, they stamp and send fractures racing through stone, armor, and improperly warded spellwork. Their herds graze on **ley-moss**, preventing unstable Eldritch pools from bursting. Killing them for their antlers is illegal in Warden territories and causes local wild magic surges.

### Want-Wolves
Descendants of ordinary forest canids twisted by the Root-Crucible's emanations. Bark-dark, lean, split along the ribs with faint green light. They do not hunt flesh first — they hunt *fixation*. A traveler obsessed with revenge, lost love, forbidden knowledge, or ascension attracts them like blood in water. They circle silently until the victim speaks their desire aloud, then answer in the voices of people connected to that desire. A disciplined mind can evade them; a desperate one cannot.

### Marble Mourners
Arathi funerary statues animated by accumulated grief and spirit residue — not undead, but mobile echoes of mourning. They wander ruined plazas beneath the roots, cradling broken name-tablets. If approached respectfully, they guide travelers to burial sites. Their faces are smooth until they remember a name — then features briefly form. Stealing from the dead causes them to become relentless pursuers.

## Magic in the Scar

Three rules govern magical behavior here:
1. **Named magic is safer than improvised magic.** Established forms, gestures, songs, or written matrices are recognized by the forest's regulating mind.
2. **Emotional casting is amplified but unstable.** Netharim practice driven by fear or obsession risks attracting Want-Wolves or awakening Arathi ruin-engines.
3. **Necromancy works unusually well — but not cleanly.** Many bodies and animating spirits were trapped during the fall. Practitioners must distinguish between true spirits, memory imprints, and forest-generated mimics.

## Safe Havens: Hearth-Rings

Clearings where Nara's influence is strong. Fires lit inside burn smokeless and warm even in rain. Travelers who share food truthfully within a Hearth-Ring cannot be attacked until dawn — unless they lie, in which case the protection fails for them alone (truth law applies).

Common saying: *"Never lie in a Hearth-Ring, never whistle at a Glasshart, never answer a wolf that knows your mother's voice."*

## Factions

### Veyran Wardens
Mixed communities of elves, orcs, humans, goliaths, and beast-folk living along the forest edge. They keep bargains with the Scar. Children learn trail-songs in Elven modes (elven song-magic soothes minor Eldritch turbulence). Orcish leatherworkers craft warded boots. Gnomes maintain brass compasses pointing not north, but toward stable magical pressure.

### House Ilvane
Sympathetic Arathi Remnant House. Their family records contain proof that an ancestor broke one of the focusing pylons during Kudur-eṭir's ritual, lessening the disaster. They seek the Root-Crucible not for power, but to recover the names of those erased when the capital fell. They sponsor Pilgrim expeditions and Marble Mourner tablet-impressions.

### House Theryx
Arathi Remnant House that romanticizes Kudur-eṭir as the "Almost-Elder" — not quite worshipping him, but cultivating his image as a visionary betrayed by cowardly gods and lesser minds. They fund expeditions seeking the Root-Crucible's power, and have been known to capture Glassharts to use their crystal antlers as attempted keys to the Root-Crucible seal.

### Pilgrims of the Closed Wound
Devotees of Evye, Fryr, and Lirra who believe the Verdant Scar is holy evidence that the world protects itself. They enter barefoot, carry no iron weapons, and tie strips of undyed cloth around trees whose roots have swallowed ruins. Their doctrine: some doors should remain shut, even if treasure lies behind them. They carry blank clay tablets to take name-impressions from Marble Mourners for the edge-shrines called **The Unforgotten Kilns**.
