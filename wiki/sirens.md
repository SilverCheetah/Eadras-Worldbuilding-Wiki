## Origin

Sirens descend from the **Sea'tua**, the Elfen-engineered race created to oversee natural balance in the oceans. They are one of the five Sea'tua-descended modern oceanic peoples (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt; Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt).

## Anatomy

Sirens are **tall and willowy with subtle feather-like fins** and haunting, multi-tonal voices. The fins serve as resonance surfaces; an adult Siren's body is, in a real sense, an instrument that the Siren plays.

## Sonic architecture

A choir of Sirens can:

- **Collapse cliffs** by inducing resonant fracture.
- **Carve new trenches** by sustained directed vibration over years.
- **Open stable "song tunnels"** through hostile currents â€” channels of harmonized water that travelers can pass through if they hold the song's pattern; close behind the last passenger; reopen at the far end.

The work is geological in scale. A single Siren can shape a coral wall. A small choir can rearrange a reef. A Deep Conductor leading a great choir can move a whole submarine ridge â€” a project that takes decades. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

## Society

Sirens live in **floating, ever-shifting cities of giant hollow shells and bubble-glass domes suspended in the water column**. The city positions are tuned, not anchored â€” a Siren city is held in place by the same standing harmonics it uses to shape geology, and moves with its choirs.

**Social status is determined by vocal range and harmonic complexity.** The greatest Sirens are called **Deep Conductors** and lead the largest geological projects. A young Siren's status is set during the first few decades of life by demonstrated harmonic complexity in increasingly demanding choir work.

Sirens have a dry, cutting sense of humor and love **impossibly complex puns** that only other Sirens understand â€” multi-layered wordplay that depends on simultaneous interpretation across several harmonic registers. Cross-species translation usually loses the joke.
