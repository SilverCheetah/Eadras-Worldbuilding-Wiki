# Sirens

**Summary**: The Sirens — *the Echo Weavers* — are one of the **Five Modern Oceanic Races** descended from the **Sea'tua**, the [[elfen|Elfen]]-engineered overseers of oceanic balance. They are sonic architects who literally reshape underwater geography with complex layered song: a choir of Sirens can collapse cliffs, carve new trenches, or open stable "song tunnels" through dangerous currents.

**Sources**: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt; Peoples of Eadras.txt; EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt (2026-05-31, oceanic Netharim)

**Last updated**: 2026-05-31

---

## Origin

Sirens descend from the **Sea'tua**, the Elfen-engineered race created to oversee natural balance in the oceans. They are one of the five Sea'tua-descended modern oceanic peoples (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt; Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt).

## Anatomy

Sirens are **tall and willowy with subtle feather-like fins** and haunting, multi-tonal voices. The fins serve as resonance surfaces; an adult Siren's body is, in a real sense, an instrument that the Siren plays.

## Sonic architecture

A choir of Sirens can:

- **Collapse cliffs** by inducing resonant fracture.
- **Carve new trenches** by sustained directed vibration over years.
- **Open stable "song tunnels"** through hostile currents — channels of harmonized water that travelers can pass through if they hold the song's pattern; close behind the last passenger; reopen at the far end.

The work is geological in scale. A single Siren can shape a coral wall. A small choir can rearrange a reef. A Deep Conductor leading a great choir can move a whole submarine ridge — a project that takes decades. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

## Society

Sirens live in **floating, ever-shifting cities of giant hollow shells and bubble-glass domes suspended in the water column**. The city positions are tuned, not anchored — a Siren city is held in place by the same standing harmonics it uses to shape geology, and moves with its choirs.

**Social status is determined by vocal range and harmonic complexity.** The greatest Sirens are called **Deep Conductors** and lead the largest geological projects. A young Siren's status is set during the first few decades of life by demonstrated harmonic complexity in increasingly demanding choir work.

Sirens have a dry, cutting sense of humor and love **impossibly complex puns** that only other Sirens understand — multi-layered wordplay that depends on simultaneous interpretation across several harmonic registers. Cross-species translation usually loses the joke.

## Weave-magic and the oceanic Netharim

The Sirens are not a magically-blank song culture. The [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] is planetary, and three of the fourteen [[seven-conduits|conduit]] ends emerge underwater among the five oceanic peoples — so **[[netharim|Netharim]] arise among the Sirens** as naturally as on land. Oceanic Netharim are a branch of the same tradition (a deeply-studied internal model of reality imposed directly on the world); a Siren Netharim's model is most often one of **resonance, acoustics, and the geology that song already reshapes** — the sonic-architecture discipline carried all the way down to reality-shaping. A submarine [[order-sharanel#Oceanic Towers and chapters|Tower of Lirra]] in Siren keeping would be a **standing-harmonic structure of tuned shell and [[crystalmancy#3. Aqualith — Sea Sapphires / Tide Opals|Aqualith]]**, held in the water column the way a Siren city is. Because the surface [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] could not reach the seafloor, the oceanic peoples likely kept an **unbroken Netharim-and-Tower tradition** while the surface lost six Towers. Which of the five peoples hold which of the three submarine conduit-cities is open canon. (source: EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt, 2026-05-31)

## Relations with other oceanic peoples

- **[[merrow|Merrow]]** — natural collaborators on large architectural projects.
- **[[shimmercloaks|Shimmercloaks]]** — useful middlemen, but the Sirens consider Shimmercloak puns frivolous.
- **[[ink-lords|Ink Lords]]** — welcomed for their stories, especially long-form ones whose internal echoes can be folded into Siren composition.
- **[[tideclad|Tideclad]]** — frequent escorts for traveling Siren choirs through dangerous currents.
- **[[sahuagin|Sahuagin]]** and **[[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]]** — avoided. Kuo-toa "corrections" sometimes attempt to render Siren song into "true form," with horrifying results for any Siren who hears them.
