## Creation

At the beginning, the [[omniversal-creator]] birthed two creators for the Eadras Mythos: **Evye**, the Feminine, and **Fryr**, the Masculine. Together they decided every rule and layer of how the Mythos would work and what it would become.

For the full narrative account â€” including the dialogue between Evye and Fryr, Evye's admission of fear, and the naming of the ten Elder Gods â€” see [[creation-myth]].

**Evye** forged the physical world: land, seas, skies, mountains, stars â€” the solid frame of reality.

**Fryr** shaped the ethereal: the [[shadowdark]], the [[feywild]], [[limina]] (the misty threshold where worlds bleed together), every non-physical realm, and the source of all magic.

## Why they sacrificed

Evye and Fryr **had no desire to rule** what they made. The creator's role and the ruler's role are distinct, and the Primordial Duo wanted only the first. Their ambition was to **create the most bountiful, diverse, magically rich Mythos possible** â€” a world saturated with magic, populated with the conditions for endless variety of life and meaning â€” and they were willing to spend themselves entirely to achieve it.

Most creator deities elsewhere in the [[omniverse|Omniverse]] used their power *without* giving everything: their Wells stayed deep, their Mythoi came out less magically saturated, and they remained as rulers afterward. Evye and Fryr chose differently. They poured their essence into the substance of the world, accepted that this would mean they could not rule what they had built, and **entrusted rulership to the [[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]]** they themselves had created.

They trusted those children â€” the ten Elder Gods, made before the final exchange â€” to **shape creation alongside them during the creative act, and to rule after the Primordial Duo's departure**. The Elder Gods were not merely heirs by accident; they were **co-creators by design and successors by deliberate handoff**. Evye and Fryr made them, worked with them, and dissolved themselves into the resulting world knowing that what they left would be in tested hands.

This sacrificial pattern â€” creators who give themselves entirely and entrust rulership to their children â€” is **very, very rare** in the infinite Omniverse. Currently established are **the seven Mythoi closest to the [[bastion-of-light|Bastion of Light]]** (Eadras among them; see *Legacy* below), and possibly Mythoi of the second cosmographical ring outward (canonically uncertain). Across the vast majority of the Omniverse, the typical pattern holds: primordial creators retain their power and rule their Mythoi directly, never sacrificing.

## The Exchange

Before the final act of creation, Evye and Fryr exchanged a sliver of their essence with each other â€” Evye gave Fryr a thread of tangible weight to anchor his realms; Fryr gave Evye a spark of ethereal flow to let her world breathe. Then they poured out everything else, sacrificing themselves completely.

Evye dissolved into earth and storm. Fryr dissolved into shadows, dreams, and the core of every spell.

The method itself was extraordinarily unusual, even among the rare Mythoi (the [[#Why they sacrificed|seven closest to the Bastion]]) where primordial sacrifice happened at all. **Most Primordial creators across the Omniverse use only the energy in their Mythos's [[gods-of-eadras#Source of godly power|Well of Creation]]** â€” drawing on the Creative Power the [[omniversal-creator|OC]] put into the Mythos at its making, leaving their own essence intact. Evye and Fryr **did both**: they spent down the Well of Creation extensively *and* poured out their own essence on top of it. The result was a **severely depleted Well** and the complete dissolution of both creators into the world. The cost was extraordinary; so was the result.

This is why [[magic-in-eadras|magic in Eadras]] is so thick and alive: it is not borrowed, but their shared pulse woven into stone, veil, and whisper â€” pulled up *both* from the Mythos's Well-allotment and from the Primordial essence itself. This double-pour is also what placed Eadras among the seven Mythoi closest to the [[bastion-of-light|Bastion of Light]] (see *Legacy* below): pouring divine essence into a Mythos's substance is what draws that Mythos closer to the [[omniversal-creator|OC]]'s home.

## Ten Elder Gods

As their final act before this total sacrifice, Evye and Fryr jointly created **ten [[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]]** to guide what they poured out. The tenth split into two at the moment of creation â€” the twin death goddesses Shali and Shani.

## Legacy

Because of their total sacrifice, Eadras possesses orders of magnitude more magical energy than almost any other [[mythos]]. This also placed Eadras among the seven Mythoi closest to the core of the [[omniverse]].

The ground, wind, and the quiet between stars are Evye and Fryr. They did not die â€” they *became* the world.
