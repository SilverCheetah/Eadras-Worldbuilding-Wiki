# The Three Last Lights

**Summary**: The Three Last Lights are the three Arathi who ascended to godhood in the final age of their civilization — known by their post-ascension divine names as **Auralis, Thamior, and Ixthal**, and by their original Arathi mortal names as **Ra-shakir, Ilum-nāṣir, and Ašar-eṭir** respectively (User direction 2026-05-01). Each faced the [[arathi-cataclysm|Extinction-Level Event]] in a different way.

**Sources**: Playground generation 1st part.txt; Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-01

---

## Overview

In the final age of [[arathi|Arath]], three individuals among the Arathi ascended to godhood. Two spent all their divine essence attempting the impossible: saving their people. The third saw that they would fail — and survived. Their myth is told at funerals after disasters.

### A note on names

Each of the Three has both an **Arathi mortal name** (the name they bore in life, conforming to canonical [[naming-conventions#arathi-naming|Arathi naming conventions]]) and a **post-ascension divine name** (the name by which they are commonly known and worshipped today). The mapping:

| Arathi mortal name | Meaning | Post-ascension divine name |
|---|---|---|
| **Ra-shakir** | "Radiance-Tuner" / "Sun-Tuner" | Auralis |
| **Ilum-nāṣir** | "Light-Keeper" / "Sacred-Light-Guardian" | Thamior |
| **Ašar-eṭir** | "Order-Opener" / "Releaser of Order" | [[ixthal\|Ixthal]] |

The post-ascension names became dominant in modern usage as the Arathi language faded from common knowledge after the Cataclysm. Scholarly and theological works frequently still use the Arathi names — particularly when emphasizing the Three's mortal origins or contextualizing them within the broader Arathi pantheon.

*"One opened the doors. One held the stones. One carried the coal. The doors broke. The stones fell. The coal endured. Judge them gently, children of the later dawn, for you are warm by a fire none of them lived to see whole."*

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Ixthal the First and Last Arathi
*(Arathi mortal name: **Ašar-eṭir** — "Order-Opener")*

**Domain**: Foresight, renewal, last seeds, dragons, unicorns

The sole survivor of the Three Last Lights. See [[ixthal]] for full coverage.

Ixthal possessed probabilistic foresight and knew saving the Arathi was impossible. He chose not to expend himself in vain, instead carrying "one coal from the burning house." Millions of years later, he secretly created [[dragons]] and [[unicorns]] by amplifying natural Source surges. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)


---

## Thamior the Upholder
*(Arathi mortal name: **Ilum-nāṣir** — "Sacred-Light-Guardian")*

**Domain**: Endurance and burdens

Thamior ascended as a god of burdens and endurance. He attempted to hold the world's crust together. For seven days after the Falling Star, the first fractures slowed because Thamior set his divine shoulders beneath the plates of the world. Then the White Waters burst through, and his essence tore apart.

Some mountain roots still contain veins called **Thamior's Strain** — stone folded like muscle. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### Traces

- Mountain peaks where ash never settles because one dying goddess (Auralis) still exhales
- Veins of **Thamior's Strain** found in certain mountain roots — stone folded in shapes resembling muscle under strain

---

## Auralis of the Thousand Shelters
*(Arathi mortal name: **Ra-shakir** — "Radiance-Tuner")*

**Domain**: Refuge

Auralis was the last of the three to ascend, becoming a goddess of refuge in the final days. She poured her divine essence into sanctuaries across Arath — shielded schools, mountain vaults, seed-halls, temple basements, canal arks. Every one eventually failed.

Her final miracle was not survival, but gentleness: many inside her shelters died without terror. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### Traces

Failed Arathi sanctuaries may have left relic ecologies in places where Auralis's power lingered. None of these are at the [[altea|Altean]] impact site — Altea was annihilated entirely — but rather at the failed shelters of *other* Arathi cities:
- Valleys where fossils face upward, as if raised by hands
- Glass domes on the floor of the [[arathalassic-sea|Arathalassic Sea]] containing only clean air and dust, set above non-Altean ruins
- Reefs grown in the shape of sleeping children

No living Arathi survived. These are evidence of love losing to scale.

## Cultural Reception

Not well known outside of scholarly theology, the Three Last Lights are a complex mythic triad. Their myth forces the question: was Ixthal a coward, a realist, or the only sane mind who survived?

Some scholars judge all three as holy — the two who gave everything, and the one who gave what mattered most: continuance.

Some judge Ixthal harshly. Not because he caused the cataclysm, but because he survived it.

The best response, preserved in later scholarly theological traditions:

*"We guard what we do not fully understand."*

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

---

## The Two Spending Themselves

Why did Auralis and Thamior fail? Not because they were weak. The catastrophe was simultaneously planetary, crustal, atmospheric, oceanic, magical, and entropic. To save a people requires saving not only bodies, but air, water, soil, seed, memory, birth, disease, climate, and time enough for children's children. They had divine essence enough to attempt miracles — not enough to contradict a broken planet. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

Their failure became one of the theological reasons the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] was later codified: even compassionate direct intervention could not necessarily save mortals, and might draw divine beings into ruin.
