# Peoples of Eadras

**Summary**: A comprehensive overview of all sapient races in Eadras, from the first Arathi through the three progenitor races and their many descendant peoples.

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Trolls.txt, Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt, Notes for Changes for 5-10-26.txt, Mitans.txt, Halfling Origin & the Change 5-10-26.txt, draconic-servitor-races.txt + snake-folk_1.txt (2026-06-02 ingest — draconic servitor-race taxonomy); velrathi_consolidated.txt (2026-06-08 — the genetic-degradation law + divine-stability mechanism; the Velrathi greying as acquired-not-original)

## Order of Creation

Per established canon: **[[the-three|The Three]] (Mark, Rand, Melania) created only the Elfen, Velrathi, and Tarran directly.** All other "child races" were engineered by the progenitors themselves; "grandchild races" evolved from those over time.

**The Three did not direct, assist, or oversee any of the engineering work that followed.** Once the three eldest humanoid races existed, every subsequent engineered race was the progenitors' own initiative — the Elfen made their own child races, the Velrathi made theirs, the Tarran made theirs, and the Three did not participate in any of those decisions or workings. (Canon clarification: Notes for Changes for 5-10-26.txt; applied retroactively across the wiki on 2026-05-10.)

### How the engineering worked

There is a hard divide between what [[the-three|The Three]] could do and what their progenitor races could do. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

**The Three could create species from scratch.** Mark, Rand, and Melania learned the craft of creating life directly from [[dao|Dao]] himself, and they wielded their own deity-level power on top of that instruction. With those two ingredients combined, they made the [[elfen|Elfen]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], and [[tarans|Tarran]] as wholly new races — bodies, magic, sapience, and lineage shaped from nothing in advance.

**The three eldest humanoid races could not.** Elfen, Velrathi, and Tarran were powerful but not deity-level; they had no equivalent of Dao's tutelage and could not summon new species into being from nothing. What they *could* do was alter their own blood. The mechanism for every "engineered" child and grandchild race is therefore the same:

> **Magical modification of the progenitor's own babies in the womb, accumulated over a few generations**, until the offspring stabilized as a distinct race fit for whatever role the progenitors needed — *new servants and useful tools*. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

Practical consequences:

- **Every engineered race is literally descended from its progenitor by blood.** Sea'tua, El'mer, and Sidhe descend from Elfen wombs; Aetherin, Titans, and Mitans descend from Tarran wombs; Orug, Grug, and Rathar descend from Velrathi wombs. "Engineered by X" and "descended from X" are not in tension — the engineering *is* the descent.
- **The process is biological, not artificial.** No vat-grown bodies, no constructs, no clones. Each generation is born from the previous generation; the modifications accumulate gradually until a stable distinct race emerges.
- **Grandchild races are doubly modified.** Their parent generation was already a modified line; the grandchild lineage's modifications stack on top.
- **Modern devolved descendants** (Elves, Merfolk, Goblins, Humans, Dwarves, etc.) are therefore *triply* descended: from the progenitor, through the engineered child race, into the modern grandchild form. The blood chain is unbroken.
- **Purpose was utilitarian.** Engineered races were made as *servants and useful tools* — fitted to a role the progenitor wanted filled (mining, warfare, scholarship, oceanic stewardship, etc.). This is why every engineered race has a defining function on its origin page.
- **The grisliness is built in.** This was the progenitors deliberately reshaping their own pregnancies, magically, generation after generation, until the children were no longer their species. Modern in-universe scholars who know the mechanism treat it with appropriate weight.

This mechanism is implicit in every "Elfen-engineered" / "Velrathi-engineered" / "Tarran-engineered" line on the wiki. Pages don't repeat it individually; this section is the canonical reference.

### The genetic-degradation law and divine stability

A deep biological law underlies the wiki-wide pattern that descendants are "natural devolution" of their progenitors — **smaller, less capable** ([[#Notes on Devolution|see below]]). It is the biological face of the [[principles-of-eadras|lost-ceiling (I.D.19)]] and lossy-inheritance (I.D.20) principles. (Surfaced 2026-06-08, Velrathi reverse-culture run.)

- **The founders are the peak.** The Three agreed their progenitor races would be ~**3m** — a founding choice (parity among the eldest), not a derivation. Everything downstream is measurably diminished: shrinkage (most modern races now ≤2m) **and** loss of magical/cognitive capability, accumulating across generations.
- **Why the eldest races *don't* degrade, but their children *do*.** The eldest three races are **genetically stable** — their genetics were **perfected against degradation** at creation (built-in, not actively maintained). But when an eldest race engineers a child race by altering its own embryos, that engineering **disrupts the built-in perfection** — the eldest cannot pass the stability *through* the act of engineering. So child and grandchild races degrade across generations; the eldest races do not.
- **The three tiers (plus outsiders).** **Tier 1 — founders** ([[elfen|Elfen]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], [[tarans|Tarran]]; 3m; stable). **Tier 2 — child races** (womb-engineered from founder stock — e.g. from Velrathi: [[orugi|Orug]], [[grugi|Grug]], [[rathari|Rathar]]). **Tier 3 — grandchild races** (natural devolution — Gnomes/Goblins from Grug, Humans from Aetherin, etc.). Outside the genealogy entirely: the dragon-made [[draconic-servitor-races|draconic servitor races]].

*(The specific mechanism that perfects the eldest races' genetics is held as deeper canon; the public statement is simply that the eldest are stable and the engineered lines are not.)*

## Races Present in the Modern Era

**Common races**: Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Halflings, Gnomes, Goblins, Orcs, Katharim, Beast-folk; the Five Modern Oceanic Races ([[merrow|Merrow]], [[sirens|Sirens]], [[shimmercloaks|Shimmercloaks]], [[ink-lords|Ink Lords]], [[tideclad|Tideclad]]) collectively known on the surface as "merfolk"

**Less common**: [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]], [[sahuagin|Sahuagin]], Kobolds, Goliath, Centaurs, Fae (all descended from Sidhe), Golems/Warforged (playable), Trolls (Arathi-engineered, mostly hibernating)

**[[draconic-servitor-races|Draconic servitor races]]** (colloq. *dragonfolk*): [[kobolds|kobolds]] (mind/mouth), [[lizardfolk|lizardfolk]] (body), [[snake-folk|snake folk]] (magic), and the individually-forged [[dragonborn|dragonborn]] champions

**NPC races**: Dragons (rare individuals as PCs), Unicorns *(the [[snake-folk|snake folk]] death-cult is primarily antagonist/NPC material; RPG status of the loyal caste is open)*

## The closed-cycle rule

> **No entirely new sapient races have been created in the last 10,000 years** (except for golems, homunculi, and other artificial constructs).

(source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

All modern races descend from the ancient progenitors through:

- **Natural devolution** — Humans (from Aetherin), Elves (from El'mer), Dwarves (from Mitans), Gnomes & Goblins (from Grug), Halflings (from Mitans), Giants & Goliaths (from Titans), and the Five Modern Oceanic Races plus Kuo-toa and Sahuagin (all from Sea'tua), among others.
- **Cultural divergence** — modern Orcs from diluted Orug blood, etc.
- **The two special cases of the Demon War** — [[katharim|Katharim]] and [[beast-folk|Beast-folk]], both born of the Rathar in that 10,000-years-ago window.

This rule has the practical force of saying: any "new race" a modern campaign introduces must either be a construct (golem, homunculus, warforged) or, if biological, must trace to one of the existing progenitor lines. It also rules out divine creation events on the timeline since the Demon War.

## Notes on Devolution

Many modern races are described as "natural devolution" of their progenitors — smaller in size, less magically capable, less intelligent. This reflects the passage of thousands of years and the disruption caused by events like the [[demon-war]]. The progenitor races were 3 meters tall and possessed abilities their descendants lack.
