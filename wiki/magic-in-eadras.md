# Magic in Eadras

**Summary**: Magic is a pervasive and powerful force in Eadras — far more abundant than in almost any other [[mythos]] — with three sources and four major orders, plus a defensive cosmological structure (the **Weave**) sustained by the lives of every magic user.

**Sources**: Magic in Eadras.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Playground generation 3rd part.txt, Crystalmancy.txt, Mana.txt; user direction 2026-05-01 on wizard multi-source mechanics; user direction 2026-05-06 on three-state model; **user direction 2026-05-14 (make-it-mine pass on the magic system): Sorcerers retired in favor of [[netharim|Netharim]] (members of [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]); standalone Druid order folded into a [[druids|sub-priesthood]] of [[qoxli|Qoxli]] / [[sylph|Sylph]] / [[nox|Nox]]; fourth order [[lexanari|Lexanari]] established; Weavespinners replaced by [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]; the Weave is now a purely defensive structure that carries Eldritch and is sustained by the lives of every magic user.**

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14

## Why Eadras Has So Much Magic

Eadras is extraordinarily magic-rich. The [[primordial-duo|Creator Deities Evye and Fryr]] sacrificed themselves completely into the world — orders of magnitude more magical energy exist in Eadras than in almost any other [[mythos]]. Magic here is not borrowed; it is the shared pulse of its creators woven into stone, veil, and whisper.

**Every bit of matter on Eadras carries [[#Quintessence, Eldritch, Mana|mana]] — the question is only how much.** Everything in this Mythos is magical to some extent: that level of saturation is the canonical reason Eadras is **one of the seven Mythoi closest to the [[bastion-of-light|Bastion of Light]]** in the [[omniverse|Omniversal]] order. The seven nearest Mythoi are the most magic-dense in creation; Eadras's place among them is a structural consequence of Evye and Fryr's total self-emptying into it. (User direction 2026-05-06.)

## The Three Sources of Magic

### 1. The Source (The Sun)
Raw magical energy radiates from the sun like light. This raw form — **Sourceshine** — is, under normal solar conditions, **transparent to most living things**: it passes through flesh, scale, leaf, and spirit like clear sunlight through still water, felt only as faint warmth or vague vitality. Sourceshine becomes **active, transformative, and dangerous only during major [[source-surges|Source Flares]]**, when it is amplified beyond ordinary flux. (source: Crystalmancy.txt — refines earlier "dangerous in its raw state" framing)

The Source itself is the **surface representation of a deeper reservoir** — *The All*, the **leftover creation energy** of [[primordial-duo|Evye and Fryr]] left throughout the Mythos when they sacrificed themselves into it. Sourceshine is the visible radiating form of that deep reservoir reaching the world from above; what druids tap is the same reservoir reached from below. See [[#Quintessence, Eldritch, Mana|the three-state model]] and the Druidic tradition entry. (User direction 2026-05-06.)

**The Source is not a deity.** It is also **not an expression of [[lirra|Lirra]] nor of the [[#The Weave|Weave]]**: the Source predates both. Lirra is an Elder God created by the [[primordial-duo|Primordial Duo]]; the Weave is a structure she wove during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] *atop* the planetary magical infrastructure that already existed (the Source, the [[seven-conduits|conduits]], the [[eldritch-cycle|cycle]]). The Source is the reservoir from before any of that — **the leftover creation energy of Evye and Fryr**. Lirra has *governance* over magic as her divine portfolio; she is not its origin. The dragons of the [[religion-in-eadras#The Early Ages — all magic was divine|Early Ages]] who venerated the Source as the highest ultimate were not entirely wrong about its primordial standing — only about its identity as a deity. (User direction 2026-05-06.)

When Sourceshine interacts with **bedrock, sea, soil, or living matter**, it is transformed into the stable, mind-obeying form known as **reflected [[eldritch|Eldritch]]**. This is the workable magic that nearly every tradition on Eadras uses. The pre-Cataclysm Arathi were the supreme engineers of reflected Eldritch (see [[crystalmancy]] and [[magitek]]); modern wizards, [[netharim|Netharim]], and most clerics also work in reflected Eldritch even when they don't name it as such.

Eldritch naturally pools and flows. In the [[eldritch-cycle|pre-Weave Eldritch Cycle]] it gathered in rivulets, then streams, then rivers — a wild surface hydrology — before draining into the [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]] that anchor the cycle. Post-Weave, the same energy is carried within the Weave's defensive structure and channeled through managed flows to the conduit network.

The seven conduits are **eternal magical structures** anchored to the planetary core, not physical shafts piercing the planet. They produce fourteen surface termini ("the world-poles") that have served as Eldritch sinks across every era of Eadras history, including before and after the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] (which destroyed the surface infrastructure built around them but did not affect the conduits themselves). The Weave's defensive structure overlays the conduit geometry and requires **[[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]** ([[netharim|Netharim]] of exceptional innate capacity) to anchor each conduit junction. See [[seven-conduits]] for the full treatment.

#### The Dragon Truth

To **dragons**, the Sourceshine/Eldritch distinction does not exist. There is only one **Fire**. Raw Sourceshine and reflected Eldritch are not two different energies, and not two aspects of the same energy: to dragons they are the same thing. Dragon biology, forged in the **First Great Flare** (see [[dragons|Dragons]]), recognizes only *the Fire of the World* and draws upon it in whatever form or intensity their need demands. There is no internal switch, no instinctual sorting, no conscious or unconscious choice — whether the power originates as direct sunlight on their scales or has passed through a thousand miles of bedrock is irrelevant. (source: Crystalmancy.txt)

This makes dragons fundamentally alien in their relationship with magic. Every other race experiences a profound metaphysical divide between raw and reflected; dragons experience seamless oneness. To them, the Arathi obsession with crystal-array regulation of "reflected" flows must have looked like a strange mortal neurosis — trying to separate that which was never separate. It is also why dragons never built crystal conduits, ley-vaults, or complex crystalmancy lattices: they had no need. Even elder dragons who have lived among mortals for centuries sometimes forget that other races experience the Source as divided. The definitive entry in the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] preserves the dragon position: *"To the dragons there has never been Sourceshine and Eldritch. There is only the Fire."*

Note: **[[unicorns|Unicorns]]** are *not* part of the Dragon Truth. Unicorns remain attuned to **reflected Eldritch**, with the first light of dawn acting as their daily catalyst. The Arathi, the unicorns, and every later race work in the reflected/raw paradigm; only dragons stand outside it.

### 2. Extradimensional Magic
Magic drawn from outside Eadras — spirit magic, pact magic, alien magic, elemental plane magic.

- **Spirit magic**: powered by spirits (willing or unwilling)
- **Pact magic**: bargaining with powerful outsiders for powerful effects
- **Planar magic**: drawn from elemental planes
- **Alien magic**: Wizards sometimes call magic from entirely outside Eadras

### 3. Divine Magic
Flowing from the [[gods-of-eadras|Gods]]. Gods do not personally answer every follower's prayer — the **[[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]]** (located entirely within [[solarion|Solarion]]) draws on a pool created by all the gods to respond to the faithful, run by [[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods|Ascended Mortal Gods]] with a celestial-spirit workforce and a notorious dysfunction profile. See [[divine-bureaucracy]] for the institutional treatment and [[priests]] for the casting tradition.

## Quintessence, Eldritch, Mana

Magic on Eadras is **one substance with two states**, plus a general/umbrella term for the substance itself. The three terms are **technical** — each is a precise word in its own register, and the practitioners who work with each state most directly use the corresponding term.

| Term | What it names | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **Quintessence** | The general/umbrella term for magical energy as a substance, regardless of state | Velrathi scholars, Rathar archivists, high theology, metaphysical texts |
| **[[eldritch\|Eldritch]]** | The **free-flowing** state — magical energy in motion, active, shaped by a mind | Practicing mages, [[netharim\|Netharim]], [[windspeakers\|Windspeakers]], practical arcanists, adventurers |
| **Mana** | The **matrix-bound** state — magical energy held within the matrix of some matter, organic or inorganic | Miners, [[crystalmancy\|crystalmancers]], alchemists, [[magitek\|magitek]] engineers, [[dwarves\|Shada'din]] |

> *Mana is eldritch that has come to rest in physical matter.* — the canonical principle (source: Mana.txt)

The relationship is the water analogy: **Quintessence** is "H₂O" (the substance, generally named); **Eldritch** is "water flowing" (the free-flowing state); **Mana** is "ice" (the held-in-matter state). All three are the same substance; the term used names which state, or — for Quintessence — the substance generally.

The matrix-bound character of mana is precise: mana is held *within* the structure of matter, not adjacent to it. The matter can be **organic** (living tissue, plants, monster organs, dragon scales) or **inorganic** (ores, crystals, stone). The matrix that holds it shapes how it behaves and what it can be drawn out for.

**Sourceshine** fits inside this model as the incoming form: it is Quintessence radiating into the world from the Source (sun). When Sourceshine interacts with bedrock, sea, soil, or living matter it transitions into the free-flowing state — Eldritch. When that free-flowing Eldritch comes to rest in the matrix of some matter, it becomes the matrix-bound state — Mana.

**[[druids|Druids]]'** preferred name for what they touch directly is **The All** — the well of power deep within the Mythos itself (the [[mythos|defined reality]] of Eadras), of which the [[#1. The Source (The Sun)|Source]] is the surface representation. The All is **the leftover creation energy of [[primordial-duo|Evye and Fryr]]**, left throughout the Mythos when they sacrificed themselves into the world. Druids draw their magic from that deep reservoir directly rather than from the surface states (Sourceshine, Eldritch, Mana). Tapping The All in the deep is far more dangerous than working with the surface forms; the Druidic tradition is built around the discipline that makes it safe. (User direction 2026-05-06.)

### Why Eadras is unusually mana-rich

Because the [[primordial-duo|Primordial Duo, Evye and Fryr]], sacrificed themselves completely into the world. The substrate did not just *generate* magic on Eadras — they *became* the magic of Eadras. As a result:

- **All matter on the Prime Material Plane carries some mana**, in varying concentrations
- Some living tissue, ores, crystals, and other materials retain mana especially well
- This is the canonical reason behind:
  - **[[mana-metals|Mana-metals]]** — base metals exposed to sustained mana long enough to transform into mana-storing variants
  - **[[crystalmancy|Crystalmancy]]** — the seven Greater Lineages of cultivated crystal that retain mana with specific signatures
  - **Magical ecology** — ecosystems develop around mana saturation, producing magical hotspots, magical flora and fauna, dragon biology, and ley-line density
  - **Mana sediment in historic sites** — Arathi ruins, dragon lairs, ancient battlefields, [[windspeakers|Windspeaker]] sanctums all accumulate distinctive mana signatures over centuries

The pre-Cataclysm [[arathi|Arathi]] had their own term for the substance, but it was lost with their language; the modern wiki uses *Eldritch* (Dragon-coined) as the most widely understood common term, with *Quintessence* and *Mana* applied where their state-specific meanings are correct.

## The Four Major Traditions

The four orders are not equally common. The canonical population hierarchy is:

1. **Wizards** — by far the most numerous magical practitioners. Trained, book-based, multi-source. The common learned magical workforce of Eadras.
2. **Priests and Clerics** — close second. Magic via devotion, divine sanction, sacred office, vow, and institutional religion.
3. **Netharim** — distant third. Rare, powerful, feared. Specialists who reshape reality via internal models.
4. **Lexanari** — extremely rare; almost mythic. Innate-only mortal speakers of Primordial Language fragments.

Druids and [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] are specialized sacred or monastic traditions placed carefully by region and institution rather than counted in the general hierarchy.

(source: demon-war-01-inworld-facts.txt, 2026-05-15 ingest)


### Priests

*Full treatment at [[priests]]; the section below is the magic-system frame.*

Call upon Deities. Spells rely on divine bureaucracy drawing from the shared divine pool (**Godpower**). A priest's daily channeling capacity is set by their wisdom, their current Favor with the deity (which the [[priests#The Divine Bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]] continuously monitors and adjusts), and their experience. Priests do **not** memorize spells the way wizards do — they may cast any spell they know as long as they have remaining capacity. Daily capacity restores at the prayer hour set by the deity's temperament: **sunup** for most order-aligned gods, **sundown** for some twilight-aligned ones, **noon** for [[daj|Daj]] and a few warrior gods, **midnight** for [[nox|Nox]] and the secret-aligned. See [[priests]] for Favor states (Neutral / Positive / Negative / God's Favorite) and [[rpg/priest-mechanics]] for the splat-side formula.

### Druids
Ally with nature. Druidic spells draw on **[[#Quintessence, Eldritch, Mana|The All]]** — the **leftover creation energy** of [[primordial-duo|Evye and Fryr]] throughout the Mythos, of which the Source (sun) is the surface representation. Druids reach The All through nature, ecological attunement, and the wild; they tap the well from below rather than receiving its radiation from above. This is far more dangerous than working with the surface states (Sourceshine, Eldritch, Mana) but potentially very powerful, and the Druidic tradition is built around the discipline that makes the practice survivable.

### Wizards

*Full treatment at [[wizards]]; the section below is the magic-system frame.*

Trained, book-based practitioners. Wizards are **multi-source**: a single wizard's spell list may draw on **eldritch/quintessence** (reflected Sourceshine), **positive and negative energy**, and **alien mana** (magic from outside Eadras), with the energy used depending on the spell. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

Modern wizards work *within* the Weave's defensive overlay — their spells flow through the Weave-protected Eldritch environment, although they are not native to the Weave the way [[netharim|Netharim]] are. This distinguishes them sharply from Netharim (who work intuitively with reality-shaping using the Weave's Eldritch flow as their natural substrate) and from druids (who tap *The All*, the leftover creation energy of Evye and Fryr, directly).

**Pre-Weave wizards** (Velrathi mainline, Arathi crystal-mediated tradition, etc.) accessed the same energies — eldritch, positive/negative, alien mana — but **without the Weave's defensive overlay**, drawing on local magical fields, planar reach, ley-vault regulation, and similar more direct (and historically more dangerous or labor-intensive) methods. The Velrathi were "Masters of Magic" through the wizard tradition long before Lirra wove the Weave.

### Netharim

*Full treatment at [[netharim|Netharim]]; the section below is the magic-system frame.*

[[netharim|Netharim]] are intuitive reality-shapers — the members of [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] — whose practice operates by directly altering the local fabric of reality through trained internal models of how reality works. Their power scales with the depth and accuracy of the practitioner's personal model: a Netharim with a profound, well-built understanding of (say) thermodynamics can produce thermodynamic effects far beyond a Netharim whose model is shallower. The order's prestige tracks the depth of its members' internal models more than any other single measure.

The Netharim are **rare** — the third-most-common magical tradition in Eadras after Wizards and Priests/Clerics, but a *distant* third. They are dangerous specialists, not the magical workforce. Their work flows through the Weave's Eldritch substrate as their natural medium, and their lives sustain the Weave's defensive coherence along with the lives of every other magic user (see [[#The Weave|The Weave]] below).

A small subset of Netharim are born with markedly greater innate capacity than their peers, and these become the **[[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]** — the load-bearing anchors of the conduit junctions whose lives hold the Weave's defensive geometry at its most strained points. See [[windspeakers]] for the full treatment.

### Lexanari

*Full treatment at [[lexanari|Lexanari]]; the section below is the magic-system frame.*

The fourth order. **Innate-only mortal speakers of the [[true-language#The Generational Degradation Model|Primordial Language]]** — the speech that names reality directly and whose words rewrite what they describe. The capacity is hereditary and rare; most candidates die in training. The order is small, the discipline severe, and the consequences of every utterance load-bearing in a way no other order's work approaches.

## The Weave

The Weave is a **purely defensive cosmological structure** woven across Eadras by [[lirra|Lirra]] during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] to prevent outsiders (demons in particular, but any extra-Mythos predator more generally) from siphoning Eadras's magic. It is **not a creative or transmissive substrate**; it does not generate magic, does not transmit spells, does not channel energy for casting. What it does is *protect* — the Weave is the shield, not the sword.

The Weave's defensive function operates through three load-bearing properties:

- **It carries [[eldritch|Eldritch]].** The Weave is the medium through which the planetary Eldritch flow circulates in its post-Demon-War regulated form. By carrying the Eldritch within its defensive structure, the Weave makes the flow inaccessible to outsider magic that would otherwise siphon from the open [[eldritch-cycle|Eldritch Cycle]].
- **It is sustained by the lives of every magic user in Eadras.** Each practising magic user's life — wizard, [[netharim|Netharim]], druid, priest, [[lexanari|Lexanari]], or otherwise — contributes a small constant load to the Weave's coherence. The mechanism is not voluntary; it is automatic, a property of being a magic-using mortal in this Mythos. Mass death of magic users weakens the Weave by the cumulative loss of their contributions.
- **It is anchored at the conduit junctions by Windspeakers.** [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] are [[netharim|Netharim]] born with greater innate capacity than ordinary Netharim, who carry the heavy duty of anchoring the [[seven-conduits|conduit junctions]] — the load-bearing points where the Weave's defensive geometry is most strained. A Windspeaker's life is a structural anchor, not just an incremental contribution. See [[windspeakers]].

When three Windspeakers were killed during the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]], the corresponding three conduit junctions lost their anchors and the Weave's defensive structure suffered catastrophic damage at those points. The result was the **Thousand-Year Dimming** — a generation-spanning degradation of magical potency from which the world has only partially recovered. (See [[anti-magic-crusade]] and [[thousand-year-dimming]].)

### What the Weave Does and Does Not Do

The Weave is the **defensive overlay on the planetary Eldritch infrastructure**. It governs how energy already inside the [[eldritch-cycle|planetary cycle]] is shielded from outsider predation, distributed safely, and protected at its load-bearing junction points. It carries the Eldritch within its structure rather than transmitting it as a wild river-network would.

The Weave does **not** buffer external magical radiation from above. Most importantly, it does **not** filter or attenuate the Eldritch component of incoming [[source-surges|Sourceflares]]. That filtering is performed entirely by Eadras's atmosphere and magnetic field; the Weave has no purchase on energy that arrives from outside the planetary cycle. A high-intensity Sourceflare bypasses the Weave whether the Weave is healthy or shattered. (See [[source-surges#The Weave Does Not Buffer Sourceflares]].) (source: Refinement — Weave function.txt; user direction 2026-05-04)

The Weave also does **not** make individual magic users more powerful, give them new spells, or otherwise add to their capacity. The Weave is not a magical *system* that practitioners *use*; it is a defensive cosmological *condition* that they exist within. Practitioners' actual power comes from their own orders' substrates — divine grace for priests, the All for druids, scholarly multi-source mastery for wizards, intuitive reality-models for Netharim, the Primordial Language for Lexanari. The Weave protects the environment in which all of these can operate.

### The Secret

**Every living magic user sustains the Weave**, and most of them don't know it. The mechanism is automatic and life-tied; an ordinary practising wizard going about a quiet life is a small constant pillar of the world's defensive magic without ever noticing. The Windspeakers are the visible exception — they know, because their burden is consciously felt — but the rest of the magic-using population sustains the Weave invisibly.

The [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] keeps this fact closely held; mortal knowledge of the connection would make magic users targets for both predation and protection in ways no institution could safely manage. The Black Hand's discovery of part of the secret (via the Inner Hand's suspicions during the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] preparation) is the historical example of why the secret is kept. See [[anti-magic-crusade#The Hidden Truth|The Anti-Magic Crusade — The Hidden Truth]] and [[anti-magic-crusade#Tiers of Knowledge (Who Knows the Secret)|Tiers of Knowledge]].
