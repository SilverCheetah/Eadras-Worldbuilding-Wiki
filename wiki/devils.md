# Devils

**Summary**: The Devils are the common rank-and-file infernal spirits of [[nyxar|Nyxar]], primarily based in [[dreadharrow|Dreadharrow]]. They are the everyday staff of the [[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal Bureaucracy]] — the office workers of Hell, processing damned souls, managing torture quotas, and filing paperwork about paperwork. Their **true form is the universal spirit form** documented in [[spirits]] — a smooth, nearly featureless, androgynous humanoid distinguished as *infernal* by the **deep crimson-red to orange glow** that emanates from the body. What mortals perceive as wildly different "types" of devils (imps, barbed devils, horned devils, pit fiends, and so on) are the same species wearing different magically-reshaping uniforms issued by the Bureaucracy. Out of uniform, off-shift, in the locker rooms of Dreadharrow — the canonical example of the *back rooms* every spirit realm maintains — devils look surprisingly ordinary. **They are not [[demons|Demons]].**

## What they are

Devils are **native infernal spirits of [[nyxar|Nyxar]]** — beings of the lower plane that exist as part of the plane's structure, the way [[seraphim|Seraphim]] are native to [[celestia|Celestia]]. They are the ordinary population of Hell, who are also (and not coincidentally) the staff of Hell's institutions. Most are based in [[dreadharrow|Dreadharrow]], the capital city of Nyxar, where the [[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal Bureaucracy]] is headquartered.

The plural is **Devils**, the singular **Devil**.

## Base form

This is the canonical detail mortals almost never get right.

A devil out of uniform shares the **universal spirit true form** documented in [[spirits]]: a smooth, **nearly featureless, androgynous humanoid figure** of roughly mortal proportions, with minimal facial features and no hair. What identifies a devil as *infernal* rather than celestial, fey, or shadowdark is the **deep crimson-red to orange glow** that emanates from the body — the colour of molten metal or hellfire. The brightness of that glow indicates raw spiritual power: a junior clerical imp glows like the embers of a banked fire, while a senior infernal of pit-fiend rank carries a glow that scorches the air around them. The dramatic horns, wings, spikes, hide, and infernal armour of mortal iconography are **not part of the true form** — they are part of the *uniform* the devil dons for duty.

They are not, in their natural form, terrifying. They are not, in their natural form, even especially impressive. In the locker rooms of [[dreadharrow|Dreadharrow]] — the canonical example of the **back rooms** every spirit realm maintains — one can see groups of these crimson-glowing figures casually talking with one another between shifts, stripping off departmental uniforms, throwing them into lockers, and heading to the showers while complaining about their day, the routing of their cases, the corruption of the imps in Filing, and the new soul-intake quotas from upstairs.

This is the actual species. The drama happens with the uniforms.

## Uniforms — the source of the apparent variety

The dramatic forms mortals associate with devils — the imp, the barbed devil, the horned devil, the bone devil, the pit fiend, the ice devil, the spined or chained or fire-mantled forms catalogued in mortal demonological texts — **are not biological types**. They are **uniforms**, issued by the [[infernal-bureaucracy|Infernal Bureaucracy]] according to rank, department, and posting. (Strictly speaking these are *shells* — see [[spirits#Shells]] — physical and magical constructs that fundamentally change what the spirit *is* while worn; in the Bureaucratic context shells double as standardised rank markers, which is why *uniform* is the idiomatic term throughout this page.)

A devil's uniform is not cloth. It is a shaped magical apparatus that, when donned, **physically reshapes the wearer's body** — adding mass, growing additional horns, extending wings, sprouting spikes, encasing limbs in armor, layering scales or chitin or barbed hide as the office's specifications dictate. A senior pit-fiend uniform is enormous, fearsome, and laden with rank insignia of a kind only other devils know how to read. An imp uniform is small, quick, and disposable for fieldwork. A horned-devil uniform is the standard middle-management cut. The biology underneath is identical across all of them.

When a devil clocks out and removes the uniform, the magical reshaping reverses, and the same featureless crimson-glowing humanoid steps into the showers as everyone else. The next day they may put on a different uniform if their shift assignment has changed. Mortal demonologists who think they are studying a richly-speciated infernal taxonomy are studying a Bureaucracy uniform code.

(This is one of the canonical examples of the **horror of competence** that defines the Infernal Bureaucracy — the uniform code is brutally consistent, the rank insignia are unambiguous to the staff, and the system has been running this way for as long as the Bureaucracy has existed. There is no chaos in Hell. There is only the org chart.)

## Daily role: the Infernal Bureaucracy workforce

A devil's working life is paperwork. Endless paperwork.

They process **damned souls** at intake (similar in structure to [[divine-bureaucracy#Bureau of Souls|the Bureau of Souls]] but oriented to the other end of the post-mortem outcome). They manage **torture quotas** in the operational departments (the Bureaucracy treats this as a measurable institutional throughput question; "torture" in this context is the formal designation of post-mortem suffering, and the quotas are tracked and audited like any other deliverable). They handle **performance reviews**, **forms about forms**, **cross-departmental routing**, **archive maintenance**, **incident reports**, and the **vast bureaucratic machinery** that keeps the Infernal Bureaucracy operating.

The work is brutal in its monotony, exacting in its standards, and **continuous**. Unlike the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]], the Infernal Bureaucracy does not have sinecures. There is no equivalent of the Divine institution's overstaffing problem. Every devil has work, the work is real, the work is reviewed, and underperformance has consequences. (See [[infernal-bureaucracy#The horror of competence]] for why this is the load-bearing horror of the institution.)

A devil's career is structured. They begin somewhere, progress through visible ranks, and accumulate the magical-uniform changes as they go. Promotion is real. Demotion is real. The hierarchy is meticulously documented; everyone knows where everyone else stands.

## Field role: tempters and adversaries

Beyond their bureaucratic duties, devils also serve as **tempters and adversaries on the material plane**. This is simply another role within the system — assigned, scheduled, reviewed, and reported on like any other workload. A devil dispatched to tempt a mortal soul, to bind a mortal in pact, or to oppose a mortal hero is **on assignment** from the relevant infernal department, and will return to file the after-action paperwork upon completion.

In the field, devils wear the **traditional fiery infernal aesthetic** mortal religious tradition expects: brimstone, smoke, infernal radiance, the dramatic horns and wings and tails of the iconography. This is the field uniform. It is calibrated to mortal expectations because **mortal expectations make the encounter legible**, which makes the assignment efficient. A devil whose appearance contradicts what a mortal soul-broker expects to see has failed the encounter before it begins.

The horror of the field encounter is identical to the horror of the office: **the system works**. The devil that has come to bargain for a mortal soul has reviewed the case file, knows the mortal's history, knows the precedents, knows the relevant doctrines, and is competent at the task. The mortal who attempts to outwit a devil is attempting to outwit a long-tenured professional whose entire career is exactly this work.

## Mirror with the Seraphim

| | Devils | Seraphim |
|---|---|---|
| Plane | [[nyxar\|Nyxar]] | [[celestia\|Celestia]] |
| Institution | [[infernal-bureaucracy\|Infernal Bureaucracy]] | [[divine-bureaucracy\|Divine Bureaucracy]] |
| Institution's character | Terrifyingly efficient, competent, relentless | Sprawling, slow, sinecure-riddled, often dysfunctional |
| Base form | Universal spirit true form ([[spirits]]); crimson-orange glow; "type" variation comes from uniforms | Universal spirit true form ([[spirits]]); golden-white glow; same uniform mechanic |
| Field role | Tempters and adversaries | The setting's "angels" |
| Disposition | Bureaucratic, competent, infernal-aesthetic in uniform | Dedicated, dutiful, jaded |

Both peoples are **bureaucratic creatures first and foremost** — staff of an institution before they are creatures of any aesthetic. The Devil and the Seraph share more structural similarity than mortal religion usually wants to admit. The institutions they staff differ catastrophically in efficiency, and that difference is the load-bearing distinction between Hell and Heaven in Eadras: not flame and music, not torment and bliss, but **a Bureaucracy that works versus a Bureaucracy that doesn't**.

## Devils are not Demons

This distinction is canonical and load-bearing. **Devils are native bureaucratic spirits of [[nyxar|Nyxar]]. [[demons|Demons]] are something entirely different.**

- **[[demons|Demons]]** are the **chaos-bred war-forces of the [[demon-war|Demon War]]** — entities that broke into the Prime Material from outside the [[mythos|Mythos]] proper, hostile to the structure of reality, and the cause of the most destructive war in modern Eadran history. They are not natives of Nyxar. They are not staff of any institution. They do not file paperwork.
- **Devils** are **office workers**. They have always been on Nyxar. They had nothing to do with starting the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. They processed enormous amounts of paperwork *because* of the war (damned-soul intake spiked dramatically), and the Infernal Bureaucracy has continued to manage the war's ongoing legacy, but Devils-as-creatures and Demons-as-creatures are different categories of being.

A devil that meets a demon does not recognize them as kin. The Bureaucracy regards demons as **a workload management problem** more than as adversaries — the war-bred demons keep showing up uninvited, the Bureau of Demonic Containment keeps having to file paperwork on them, and everyone agrees it would be much easier if Chaos would stop.

If a mortal text uses "devil" and "demon" interchangeably, that text is wrong, and almost certainly wrong about other things too.

## Open canon

- **The senior tier of the Infernal Bureaucracy.** Whether the institution has Asmodeus-equivalent figures, archdevil rank-holders, a true upper echelon analogous to the Divine Bureaucracy's Ascended Mortal Gods — open canon. Current minimum is that *some* upper structure must exist to issue the uniforms, define the org chart, and authorize departmental policy; the specific identities and titles are not yet established.
- **Devil reproduction or generation.** Whether devil numbers are fixed, whether new devils are generated by the plane itself, whether they have any analogue of mortal reproduction — open canon.
- **The locker rooms of [[dreadharrow|Dreadharrow]].** The casual locker-room scene is canonical; the specific spatial layout of Dreadharrow's bureaucratic infrastructure (which floor of which building, who shares a locker bank with whom, where the off-shift cafeterias are) is open canon and ripe for splat-book-style elaboration.
- **The Infernal uniform code.** That uniforms shape the wearer is canonical. The complete catalogue of uniform forms — all the varieties mortal demonological tradition has named — and the rank-and-department logic mapping each form to its position in the org chart is open canon.
