# Firethorn Empire

**Summary**: The Firethorn Empire was the worldwide [[tarans|Tarran]] empire that dominated Eadras from approximately **18,000 years ago** (peaking around **15,000 ya**) until its catastrophic dissolution **10,000 years ago**, when [[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] ascension and the [[demon-war|Demon War]] tore through an empire that had already been rotting from within for nearly a millennium. Named after a legendary poisonous flower with a dual nature of destruction and healing.

## Origin and timeline

The Firethorn Empire was founded approximately **18,000 years ago** when the [[tarans|Taran]] declared dominion over all other races. In the centuries following [[the-three|The Three's]] withdrawal from the world, Taran arrogance grew unchecked until they concluded that their role as Keepers of Order entitled them to rule. The empire **peaked around 15,000 years ago** and ruled most of the world at its apex. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

The surviving Tarran (along with the Velrathi and Elfen) were subsequently [[great-banishment|exiled]] from the main Prime Material continents to [[gaia|Gaia]]; the Tarran specifically rebuilt themselves as the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] in [[firethorn-hold|Firethorn Hold]] on Prime-like Gaia. (sources: firethorn empire.txt, Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

## The Fall: Rome's final centuries

The collapse of the Firethorn Empire was **not** the result of gradual decline alone, **nor was it [[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] direct goal**. It was the catastrophic final blow â€” the equivalent of the barbarian invasions that ended Rome â€” delivered against an empire already rotting from within. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

For nearly a millennium before its dissolution, the Firethorn Empire had been sliding into decadence, debauchery, and profound ennui. Specifically:

- The once-disciplined Tarran martial culture had grown corrupt and entitled.
- Noble youths abused their power with impunity. (Among countless casualties of that abuse: the murder of [[velena|Velena]], a Velrathi orphan whose elder brother [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] would in time become the empire's destroyer.)
- The grand ideals of Order and Duty had become hollow slogans.
- The empire was collapsing under its own weight long before Xar'vion rose to prominence â€” much like Rome in its final centuries.

Xar'vion, a brilliant but broken Velrathi archmage, did not set out to destroy the empire. **His sole obsession was obtaining the power to resurrect Velena.** Through ruthless political maneuvering, revolutionary magical discoveries, and calculated military campaigns, he positioned himself to claim the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] on God's Day.

When he finally ascended as an Unrestricted Elder God, the resulting **War of Domination**, the desperate summoning of a Demon Prince, and the ensuing [[demon-war|Demon War]] tore through the already weakened empire **like a wildfire through dry grass**. What little structural integrity remained was obliterated.

The Firethorn Empire did not fall because Xar'vion willed its destruction. It fell because it was already dying â€” and Xar'vion was the final, devastating catalyst.

## The Firethorn

The empire was named after a legendary flower with a dual nature of destruction and healing:

**The flower's origin**: The very first Firethorn grew in the center of a blasted crater where a Tarran druid died in a violent magical explosion. When fellow druids arrived, they found it glowing untouched amid the devastation.

**Appearance**: Petals constantly shift between vivid red and bright yellow while glowing with soft inner light. Dark green leaves. Black thorns glowing with a dim, menacing red.

**The poison**: The thorns contain a fast-acting poison that burns the nerves like fire while destroying the blood from within. When the poison leaves the thorn â€” drawn out or injected â€” the empty thorn immediately self-combusts in a burst of magical flame. Hence: *Firethorn*.

*Even one thorn causes terrible agony. Two or three are usually enough to kill a human-sized creature. For a full-sized Tarran, four or more are almost always fatal.*

**The miracle**: The nectar from the petals is a powerful soothing agent bringing instant relief from even the worst pain or burns. Thorn poison mixed with this nectar creates the **Sovereign Antidote** â€” a universal cure that can neutralize any known poison.

Assassins favor stabilized thorn poison to guarantee death. Royal Tarran houses protect their greatest treasures with multi-thorn traps.
