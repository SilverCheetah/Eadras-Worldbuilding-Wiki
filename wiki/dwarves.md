# Shada'din

**Summary**: The Shada'din — commonly called **Dwarves** by the younger races — are a clan-based people of two extremes (Work Mode and Relaxation Mode), descended from the Mitans and serving Eadras as its master miners, smiths, magitek-factory operators, and disciplined underground warriors. They worship [[din-shadar|Din'shadar]] as their ascended mortal patron, partner closely with [[gnomes]] in industry and with [[beast-folk|Beast-folk]] in domestic life, and remain locked in an unresolved blood-debt with the [[trolls|trolls]] they have spent millennia mining for ore.

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Gnomish Prank v Dwarven Retort.txt, Trolls.txt, May 1 revisions.txt, Naming conventions.txt, notes on various topics 1.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

## Etymology

**Shada'din** is the in-universe canonical name. **Dwarves** is the term used by the younger races and most outsiders, and is fully recognized; either is acceptable in shared speech. The Shada'din themselves prefer their own name, especially in formal contexts and in religious settings dedicated to [[din-shadar|Din'shadar]]. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

The name is also the root of the patron god's title: *Din'shadar* — "of the Shada'din," in approximate translation. The exact etymology is preserved among Shada'din lore-keepers and not widely shared.

## Origin

The Shada'din are the natural devolution of the **Mitans**, the [[tarans|Tarran]]-engineered miners and tunnel warriors. Like all "natural devolution" races, they are smaller and less magically gifted than their progenitors, but they remain excellent at the disciplines the Mitans were created for: mining, craftwork, and warfare (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

The Mitans were Melania's third major engineering project after the Aetherin and the Titans, designed to extract the deep wealth of Eadras and to fight beneath it. The Shada'din continue both traditions in diminished but refined form.

## Anatomy

Short, stout, broad-shouldered, and built for endurance. Physically and culturally suited to underground life, though many surface settlements exist (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

## The two modes

The defining trait of Shada'din psychology is the absolute swing between **two distinct, all-consuming modes of being** (source: May 1 revisions.txt):

- **Work Mode** — completely serious, focused, and intense. When a Shada'din is working, there is **no humor, no wasted movement, no small talk.** They are all business. Outsiders frequently misread Work Mode as coldness or disrespect; it is neither. It is the trade itself, performed with full attention.
- **Relaxation Mode** — the moment work ends, they switch fully into **loud, boisterous, joyful, highly social** celebration: singing, drinking, joking, wrestling, feasting with wild abandon. In this mode the Shada'din wear **bright silks, satins, and brocades** — clothing that would seem positively un-dwarven to outsiders who have only seen them at the forge — and throw **massive, raucous parties** built on drinking, feasting, music, dancing, and games. Because [[#Multi-generational homes and creches|Shada'din factories run 24-hour shifts]], at any given hour somewhere in any major hold there is a party either ramping up (for those just off shift) or winding down (for those about to start). The Relaxation Mode wardrobe is one of the major economic anchors of the [[beast-folk|Beast-folk]]'s textile trade — see Relations below. (sources: May 1 revisions.txt; notes on various topics 1.txt)

The switch is fast and absolute. A Shada'din walking out of a forge into a hall does not warm up gradually. There is no transition state. The two modes are equally Shada'din, equally honored, and the cultural assumption is that **a person who cannot fully inhabit both is suspect** — overly grim or overly frivolous in equal measure.

Shada'din feel most free to express themselves around **close family**, where Relaxation Mode runs longest and most uninhibited.

## Home and lifestyle

Shada'din live primarily **underground**, in vast sprawling subterranean holds carved deep into the earth. These are their true homes and contain the heart of their civilization. They also maintain several types of surface outposts (source: May 1 revisions.txt):

- **Fortified trade posts** — gateway settlements where outsiders trade without entering the holds
- **Observatories** — surface astronomical and meteorological stations
- **Agricultural outposts in isolated hidden valleys** — the food supply, deliberately tucked away

They were **not the first race to live underground** — that honor belongs to the [[rathari|Rathari]]. The Shada'din studied Rathari techniques extensively, copied them, and then **significantly advanced and refined** them, making underground architecture distinctly their own. Modern Shada'din holds are no longer recognizably Rathar — they are Shada'din, in a tradition that began as borrowed and matured into a distinct civilization. See [[katharim]] for the Rathar branch that retained the older deep-stone idiom unchanged. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

### Multi-generational homes and creches

Shada'din families live in **multi-generational extended-family homes**. Because adults often work long or irregular hours (especially in active forges and factory cycles), most children spend their days and nights in **creches that operate 24 hours a day**. **An elder female from the family almost always accompanies the children in the creche** — both as guardian and as transmitter of clan-line memory. Creche-elder service is a respected and continuous role, frequently held until end of life. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

## Technology and craft

Shada'din make **extensive use of [[magitek]].** Magitek lights replace torches throughout most modern holds. **Powerful steamworks drive giant hammers** when forging especially large or magically significant pieces. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

Their **greatest industrial achievement is the gnome-Shada'din factory partnership**: **[[gnomes|gnomes]] invent the systems; Shada'din craft and operate the actual factories.** Gnomish-invented, Shada'din-built magitek factories can produce **almost anything in bulk** — tools, armor, structural components, magitek devices, household goods. The arrangement plays to each people's strengths: the gnomish bench-and-anvil instinct (the inherited Grug aptitude — see [[gnomes]]) supplies the engineering, and the Shada'din genius for disciplined large-scale fabrication supplies the production. The partnership is among the most economically consequential standing relationships in the modern world.

## Magic

The Shada'din are **as capable of magic as any other race** and can pursue any type of magic without cultural restriction. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

Their **most respected mages specialize in stabilization and structural magic**, used to reinforce vast underground chambers, contain pressure faults, set ley-stable foundations, and keep their holds safe and livable. A senior structural mage's word can determine whether a new gallery is excavated, sealed, or expanded — practical authority that often exceeds nominal clan rank.

### Rock Sense

A **rare few** Shada'din are born with **Rock Sense**, the ability to feel and "read" stone around them with great precision — fault lines, ore bodies, hollow chambers, magical resonance, slow movements of deep earth. **This gift is considered a direct blessing from [[din-shadar|Din'shadar]]**, the ascended mortal god of mining and the Shada'din people. Rock-sensed individuals are valuable beyond price: they identify safe excavation paths, locate veins of [[mana-metals|mana-metals]] and gem deposits, and serve as living instruments for senior structural mages. See [[din-shadar]]. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

## Weapon preference and the "make the other guy die" principle

Shada'din strongly prefer **axes and hammers over swords**. The reason is structural: in the tight, narrow tunnels of their underground holds, long blades are awkward and dangerous. Axes and hammers are compact, devastating, and equally effective whether wielded for craft or for combat — the same tool that splits ore splits a skull. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

This practical advantage matured into deep culture. To a Shada'din, **a sword is a weapon for someone who fights in open fields.** An axe or hammer is the **honest tool of someone who uses the same weapon for both their work and their killing.** A Shada'din warrior's only job is summarized in a single guiding phrase:

> ***"Make the other guy die."***

Everything else — honor, glory, technique — is secondary. There is honor in Shada'din warfare, and there is technique, but neither is the point of the engagement.

## Social structure

Shada'din society is **strictly clan-based**. Each major underground hold is ruled by a powerful clan; loyalty to one's clan is among the highest virtues. Cross-clan friendships and marriages are common and welcomed; cross-clan *betrayal* is the gravest crime in Shada'din life.

### The Overking

At the top of the inter-clan structure sits the **Overking**. The clan leaders gather and **elect one clan to become the Overclan**; the leader of that chosen clan **immediately becomes the Overking**. The election is procedural, not partisan — the Overking's primary responsibilities are narrow:

- **Dealing with outsiders** (treaties, foreign dignitaries, inter-race disputes that escalate beyond a single hold)
- **Serving as supreme war leader** if the entire race must unite

When neither concern is active, Overclan rule is largely ceremonial and most clan business proceeds at the hold level. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

### The Arena of Government

Every major clan maintains its own **Arena of Government** — a clan-level chamber for binding internal deliberation. The Arenas follow strict ancient rules (source: May 1 revisions.txt):

1. **The doors are locked from the outside.** No one leaves until the single issue is resolved.
2. **Only one issue may be discussed at a time.** Tabling, side-issues, and procedural amendments are out of order.
3. **Food is passed in through small passages.** Sessions can run for days.
4. **Most issues are resolved peacefully and quickly through speeches.**
5. **If words fail, ritual combat with axe or hammer is permitted.** A single combat does not necessarily settle the matter — the entire room may descend into a general melee.

A general Arena melee has happened **only two or three times in recorded history**. Each occasion is remembered by name and is spoken of with the seriousness reserved for foundational events.

## Religion

The Shada'din's primary patron is **[[din-shadar|Din'shadar]]**, the ascended mortal god of mining and the Shada'din people. Every hold maintains a foundry-altar or deep-shrine, and Din'shadar's faith is among the most fervent in Eadras. See [[din-shadar]] and [[religion-in-eadras|Religion in Eadras]] for the broader pattern of ascended-mortal-god worship.

Shada'din worship is not exclusive: many devotees of Din'shadar also keep observances to Vos, Raja, or [[baldaran|Baldaran]] (especially among lore-keepers), and worship of [[lirra|Lirra]] is common in stabilization-magic circles. The race has no taboo against cross-deity devotion.

## Brewing and Palebone Clay

Shada'din brewing is a proud, conservative tradition: malty, honest, and emphatic about not being interfered with. The signature defensive technique is **Palebone Clay** — a rare white clay so powerfully absorbent that even a modest sack can strip most of the flavor and color from an entire brewing vat. Palebone Clay is normally reserved for the **highest-quality metalwork** and is released for any other purpose only with a forge master's personal blessing. (source: Gnomish Prank v Dwarven Retort.txt)

The clay's brewing use is not routine — it is the dwarven retort to the centuries-old gnomish prank of secretly spicing dwarven vats (see [[gnomes#Brewing a hot batch — the gnomish prank|Gnomes — Brewing a hot batch]] and [[food-and-drink]]). The exchange has the rhythm of a long, dignified feud: gnomes spike a vat with cinnamon, cardamom, star anise, and a sliver of [[food-and-drink#Raven's eye|raven's eye]]; the Shada'din let the batch ferment, then drop in Palebone Clay with the forge master's blessing and serve the resulting watery shadow at the next clan feast. The standing Shada'din taunt to a smug gnome is *"We still have more clay."*

## Trolls — the wealth-in-stone dispute

Modern Shada'din view **[[trolls|trolls]]** not as people but as walking refineries. A wandering troll's body contains highly refined metals and gems that function as its muscles, vessels, and organs; killing the troll yields harvestable wealth. Shada'din mining concerns also **mine into dormant elder trolls** — treating their hibernating bodies as ore strata. The living trolls call the ancient dormant ones *"the Sleeping Ancestors"* and *"Grandfather Stone,"* and consider every such killing or mining operation a grave desecration. **Trolls have never forgiven these acts.** (source: Trolls.txt)

The conflict has been quiet only because no elder troll has woken in the 39,000 years since the younger civilizations arose; the oldest dragons remember the previous awakening with visible unease. See [[trolls]].

## Relations

- **[[beast-folk|Beast-folk]]** — long, respectful, mutually beneficial relationship dating to the centuries after the Demon War. The Shada'din openly acknowledge the Beast-folk as **superior craftsmen in textiles and "soft" arts**, and regularly purchase relaxation clothing, tapestries, banners, decorative hangings, and other soft goods from Beast-folk artisans. These pieces are highly prized for fitting Relaxation Mode perfectly: colorful, luxurious, comfortable, expressive in ways Shada'din practicality rarely achieves alone. In return, the Beast-folk value Shada'din reliability, protection, and access to rare underground materials. Many Laughing Circles maintain permanent quarters inside Shada'din holds — vibrant **"Veil Districts"** where Beast-folk culture blends with Shada'din discipline. The two peoples affectionately call each other **"Stone Cousins"** (Shada'din) and **"Laughing Cousins"** (Beast-folk). (source: May 1 revisions.txt)
- **[[halflings|Halflings]]** are kin: both descend from Mitan stock, with halflings preferring the surface and Shada'din the deep. The two share a quiet biological bond despite very different lifestyles.
- **[[gnomes|Gnomes]]** are not blood-kin to the Shada'din (gnomes descend purely from [[grugi|Grugi]] — the earlier "dual-progenitor" framing was retracted on 2026-05-10). What binds the two peoples is craft economy, not lineage: the **gnome-Shada'din magitek factory partnership** (see Technology and Craft above) is the most economically consequential standing alliance in modern Eadras, and the brewery feud is the warmest expression of how seriously each people takes the other's craft.
- **[[trolls|Trolls]]** are the Shada'din's unresolved blood-debt: dispassionate mining on one side, unforgotten grievance on the other.
- **[[katharim|Katharim]]** share architectural cousinship through the Rathar: both Shada'din (via Mitan-borrowed style) and Katharim (as the Weighted Third of the Rathar themselves) work in the deep-stone tradition. This sometimes makes Katharim necropoleis and Shada'din holds unsettlingly similar in their geometry.

## Naming

Shada'din naming descends from the **Mitan tradition** — heavy, resonant, and mineral, evoking metals, ores, gemstones, and the deep earth. Modern dwarven names have softened slightly from the Mitan original but still carry the same characteristic weight: hard consonants (K, D, R, Th, G, B, M, N, V), deep vowel sounds (long *o*, *u*, *ar*, *or*), 2–3 syllables ending in strong stops.

Examples (M): *Korthun, Durak, Tharok, Gromnir, Borak, Valdrak, Granthar, Adamar, Mithrak.* Examples (F): *Duraka, Tharona, Gromira, Valdris, Kortha, Granthara, Sapphira, Onyxia.* Clan names follow the same mineral logic: *Deepvein, Ironvein, Stoneforge, Obsidian, Granitehold, Mithralcore.*

Like halflings, Shada'din earn **cognomens** — always given by others, never self-chosen — through deeds in mining or battle. Common Shada'din cognomens are heavy and mineral-themed: *Deepvein, Stonebreaker, Ironfist, Riftcarver, Obsidion, Graniteheart, Mithralforge, Bloodvein, Underlord, Cavemaster.*

**Formal address examples:**

- *Clan phase*: *Korthun of Clan Deepvein.*
- *With cognomen* (especially honored warriors and miners): *Korthun Stonebreaker of Clan Deepvein* — or *Korthun Stonebreaker, Riftcarver of Clan Deepvein.*

A Shada'din with many cognomens is treated with great respect, as each title represents proven endurance in the deep places. See [[naming-conventions#mitan-naming-and-inherited-shadadin-dwarven-echoes|Racial Naming Conventions — Mitan and Shada'din]].
