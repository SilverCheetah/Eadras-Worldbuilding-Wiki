# El'mer

**Summary**: The El'mer were tall, graceful **proto-elves** engineered by the [[elfen|Elfen]] to oversee natural balance on land — one of the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]] who **went totally extinct** at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]], their diluted bloodlines surviving only in modern elves and fey.

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Elfen.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-29

---

## Origin

The El'mer were created by the [[elfen|Elfen]] — not directly by [[the-three|Mark]] — as one of three child races shaped to oversee specific aspects of natural balance:

- **El'mer** — overseeing natural balance on land
- **Sidhe** — regulating and ruling the [[feywild]]
- **Sea'tua** — overseeing natural balance in the oceans

Per established canon ([[the-three]] created only the three progenitor races; child races were created by their progenitor children), Mark created the Elfen alone. The Elfen themselves shaped the El'mer, Sidhe, and Sea'tua.

## Form and Role

The El'mer were tall, graceful beings — proto-elves in the truest sense. They tended forests, groves, and ley-touched wildlands across pre-Empire Eadras. Their connection to living growth was extensive enough that they could **weave song-magic from a grove the way an [[elfen|Elfen]] could from a city**, though at lesser scale.

They served the cooperative pre-Firethorn order alongside their [[velrathi|Velrathi]]-engineered [[orugi|Orug]] and [[tarans|Tarran]]-engineered [[aetherins|Aetherin]] cousins.

## Total Extinction

The El'mer **went extinct as a pure people** during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

They fell defending the wild groves and ley-groves. Their final, unified stand came at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] — the **Final Wall** — where they joined the [[orugi|Orug]] and [[aetherins|Aetherin]] in the legendary three-day defense.

### At the Final Wall

The El'mer were the **living song** of the defense. While the [[orugi|Orug]] formed the shield core and the [[aetherins|Aetherin]] launched counter-charges, the El'mer:
- Wove song-magic from the shattered groves
- Raised temporary barriers of **vitrified wood and thorn**
- **Healed the wounded with desperate beauty** even as demons tore through

Their final cry: *"We were made to preserve the living green."*

Then they charged with their cousins one last time.

**Not one El'mer survived.** The pure bloodline ended that day. See [[three-who-stood]] for the joint memorial.

## Modern Descendants

Diluted El'mer blood survives in:

- **Modern Elves** (all subtypes, descended through long centuries of dilution and cultural divergence)
- **Modern Fae** (where Sidhe and El'mer bloodlines mingled in the Feywild after the war)

Pure El'mer no longer exist. Modern elves are described as **natural devolution** of their progenitors — smaller, less magically capable, less imposing — but the **resilient song-touch instinct** that runs through all elven cultures traces to El'mer ancestry.

## Cultural Memory

The El'mer are remembered alongside the [[orugi|Orug]] and [[aetherins|Aetherin]] as the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]]. Their statue at the **Monument of the Threefold Oath** depicts a singer entwined with living vines, mid-song, arms spread.

[[beast-folk|Beast-folk]] (especially those carrying [[rathari|Rathar]] bloodlines saved by the Final Wall) honor the El'mer in their wildest [[flyting|flytings]]:

> *"The singers grew thorns from broken trees,*
> *Sang the wounded back from death,*
> *And made the demons hear their grief —*
> *Even the Wound paused at their last breath!"*

Modern elves trace songs of mourning and growing-magic back to the El'mer; many older elven liturgies are believed to be partial, blurred memories of El'mer healing-verses.

## El'mer in the Tomb of the Last Guardians

In the [[tomb-of-the-last-guardians|Tomb of the Last Guardians]], the El'mer plinth bears no bodies — only **empty stands** carved from petrified wood with thorn-vines silvered into the surface. Each stand bears a name and a single line from the song that El'mer are remembered to have sung.

Pilgrims often leave fresh-cut greens or living seedlings at the El'mer plinths.
