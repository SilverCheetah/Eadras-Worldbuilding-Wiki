# Omniversal Laws

**Summary**: Six universal, unbreakable laws installed by the [[omniversal-creator]] that govern every [[mythos]] in the [[omniverse]] without exception. They are not arbitrary divine decrees — they are **expressions of coherent existence itself**, the rules without which structured reality cannot be.

**Sources**: Omniversal Laws.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## The Six Laws

### 1. Truth is Truth
Truth is indivisible, interlocking, and unquestionable. It is not opinion — it is bedrock. Every fact links to every other fact like gears in a machine. If something *is*, it stays true forever. Lying causes reality to begin unraveling.

**Philosophical interpretation**: Truth is **ontological coherence**. The Law is not a moral injunction against deceit — it is a structural fact. Lies weaken structural consistency, and at sufficient scale they undermine the conditions under which coherent existence can persist locally. *Lies do not just deceive; they erode.*

### 2. Time Flows in One Direction
Time's arrow points forward only. No rewinding, no fast-forwarding, no loops. Even gods cannot stop or reverse the clock, though they may briefly slow a moment. Prophecies carry weight precisely because, once spoken, the future is locked — time is honest and gives only one shot.

### 3. Magic Must Be Ruled by a Mind
Magic is only safe when a sapient being is in the driver's seat. Unregulated magic — with no god or thinking being controlling it — becomes wild and unpredictable. In some Mythoi, wild zones cause spells to twist, portals to open randomly, and reality to glitch. Chaos realms represent the ultimate extreme: no mind, no rules, anything can happen.

### 4. Justice Seeks Balance
The cosmos does not ignore wrongs, but it does not always rush to correct them. Small wrongs correct quickly. World-shaking injustices may take generations or eons to balance — the greater the imbalance, the longer and harder the correction. The [[voroik|Three Loom Sisters]] step in for the worst imbalances, quietly snipping and reweaving fate.

### 5. Death is Sovereign
Death wins. Always. Mortals fade fast, gods linger, Mythoi crumble slower — but nothing escapes the final curtain. Immortality is only a delay. Even the [[omniverse]] itself will end. The law is not a threat; it is a fact.

**Philosophical interpretation**: Death is the **lawful transition of jurisdiction**. It is not an obstacle to be overcome but the structural mechanism by which a soul leaves one phase of existence and enters another under the appropriate authority. Resurrection is not impossible in principle, but it requires (i) consent of **both death goddesses** ([[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani]]), (ii) consent of the **returning soul**, and (iii) **appropriate metaphysical structure** for the return. *This applies specifically to Eadras*; other Mythoi may operate differently. [[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] tragedy was that he attempted **unilateral override** of the lawful death structure — true resurrection was difficult, not impossible, but the path he chose was not one that Death's jurisdiction would have authorized. See [[necromancy-and-undeath]].

### 6. Destruction is the Natural End
Everything erodes over time. Weak things break quickly. Strong things are hammered harder and tested longer, but the cracks eventually show. No one is truly unbreakable. Pressure builds, rust creeps, empires crack. The law says: hold on as long as you can, but the end is coming.

**Philosophical interpretation**: Entropy and dissolution are **structurally necessary**, not regrettable side effects. Permanence and stagnation are not the same thing; without the slow erosion of all things, the universe could not maintain the rhythm by which old structures yield room for new ones. **[[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir]]'s refusal to accept this Law is the philosophical root of the Cataclysm**: he believed destruction itself was morally illegitimate and tried to abolish entropy to "perfect" Arathi civilization forever. The attempt failed catastrophically, and the failure is taught in Vosian doctrine as the canonical example of compassion without humility.

## Significance in Eadras

These laws are why true resurrection requires explicit jurisdictional consent (the [[necromancy-and-undeath|conditions]] are very rarely satisfied; Xar'vion failed at this), why the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]] curse works the way it does, and why the [[voroik]] are necessary to maintain balance across the [[omniverse]].

## They are not arbitrary divine decrees

The Six Laws are best understood not as rules the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]] imposed on existence but as **the expressions of coherent existence itself**. Existence requires structure, truth, identity, continuity, memory, and lawful coherence; the Six Laws are the formalization of those requirements. To break a Law is not to break a rule — it is to undermine the conditions under which the universe can sustain ordered being. This is why Law-breaches always come at catastrophic cost. (source: Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt)
