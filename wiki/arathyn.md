# Arathyn

**Summary**: Arathyn — also called the **Ignic Continent** — is the continent at the center of the [[arathalassic-sea|Arathalassic Sea]], formed from a Large Igneous Province at the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] impact site where [[altea|Altea]] once stood; the continent broke the surface ~1 million years ago, and the **post-Demon-War impalement of the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] back into the original First Breach Wound 10,000 years ago triggered the renewed volcanism that forced the continent into its modern shape**. Its name preserves the *Arath* root, marking it as the geological scar of the lost civilization; *Ignic* is a descriptor of its overwhelmingly igneous origin. Arathyn is — in cosmological terms — **scar tissue grown over the gods' desperate fourth-attempt containment of the Spear**, and the impalement-prison at its conceptual center remains active and ongoing.

**Sources**: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt, New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt, Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt, spear-containment-diagnosis-and-the-alien-artifact-principle.md (2026-05-13 ingest, Arathyn-as-scar-tissue framing).

## Naming

**Arathyn** is the canonical name (source: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt). The name preserves the *Arath* stem and reads as "of-Arath" — a continent born of, and named for, the civilization the impact destroyed.

**Ignic Continent** is a common descriptor — not an alternate canonical name, but a recurring epithet — referring to the continent's overwhelmingly igneous origin. It is geologically literal: Arathyn is, more than any other continent on Eadras, made of fire-rock.

## Formation

When the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] struck [[altea|Altea]] in the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]], it punched through the planetary crust into the mantle. The canonical geological sequence (sources: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt, New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt):

1. **A gaping hole in the mantle**, briefly covered only by inrushing seawater as the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]] sundered around the wound.
2. **Lava extrusion**, beginning soon after and continuing for millions of years, building a vast Large Igneous Province on the sea floor.
3. **Surface emergence at ~1 million years before present.** Arathyn broke the ocean surface and became a true continental landmass only **about 1 mya** (Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt, 2026-05-05). For most of its existence the LIP was submarine; the continent we know today is geologically very young.
4. **The post-Demon-War impalement (~10,000 BP)** — the gods' [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#hiding-4-shove-it-back-down-the-hole-after-xarvion|fourth-attempt impalement of the Spear back into the original First Breach Wound]] reactivated and accelerated Arathyn's volcanic growth in a single catastrophic phase. The pre-existing LIP-continent had been quietly building since the Cataclysm; the impalement triggered the renewed mantle activity that forced the continent into its modern shape, expanded its surface, and reshaped its interior geology.
5. **Arathyn today** — the visible scar tissue grown over the impalement site, rising above the [[arathalassic-sea|Arathalassic Sea]] at the impact center, still growing slowly as the wound continues its very long not-quite-healing.

The Earth analogue cited in the original source was the **Ontong-Java Plateau** — a real-world LIP formed by sustained mantle plume volcanism, though the Ontong-Java Plateau is a submarine plateau, not a continent. Arathyn, on a longer timescale and with the post-Demon-War acceleration, achieved continental scale.

### Cause and mechanism

The geological mechanism — sustained mantle-plume volcanism building a Large Igneous Province — is the **mechanism**. The **cause**, in cosmological terms, is the Spear-prison at the continent's conceptual center: Arathyn's existence is **directly caused** by the gods' fourth-attempt impalement of the Spear back into the original Wound. The geology is the working-out of the cosmology. The continent's recent volcanic activity, the persistent magical disturbance in its deep interior, and the slow but ongoing surface growth all reflect the **active, ongoing nature of the impalement-prison**. The world is not stable around the Spear; Arathyn is what that instability looks like when it expresses itself as land.

This is `[CANONIZED]` as of the 2026-05-13 ingest. The pre-impalement existence of the LIP and the 1-mya surface emergence are preserved (the geology was already underway); the cause-statement and the modern-shape attribution are updated.

## Significance

Arathyn occupies the exact location of Altea's destruction. There are **no Altean ruins** on or beneath it — the impact and the subsequent magma flow erased everything, and Altea by canon left no remnants of any kind. Arathyn is geologically *new*: its basement rock is younger than the Cataclysm itself.

Implications worth flagging for future canon work:
- Arathyn's geology is overwhelmingly igneous; sedimentary and biological strata are thin and recent.
- Mantle-plume features (hotspots, persistent volcanism, unusual mineralogy) are likely concentrated here.
- Any settlement of Arathyn occurred long after the Cataclysm — by [[el-mer|Sea'tua]]-descended peoples (themselves an Elfen creation made roughly ten million years after the Cataclysm), by surface peoples reaching it during the post-Drowning recovery, or by other lineages whose claim to the land cannot be ancestral.
- Buried [[worldender|Worldender]] fragments are likely concentrated in Arathyn's deep crust — see [[worldender]] for the metaphysical hazards of such fragments.
- Arathyn is the youngest major continent on Eadras and the only one whose origin is directly tied to a deicidal event.
- The deep interior of Arathyn is, as a working assumption, **progressively reality-frayed** as one approaches the continent's conceptual center. The [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#reaching-the-spear|Spear's impalement site at the conceptual heart of the continent]] generates causality-fray, time-anomaly, and identity-slip in nested concentric rings. The coastal regions are habitable; the deep interior is dangerous to habit; the innermost approach is lethal even before any deliberate attempt to reach the artifact. (Working canon, 2026-05-13 ingest; surface biology and habitability rings remain open canon for development.)

## The Worldbreaker Scar connection

Arathyn's basement is the **[[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]]** itself — the persistent geological wound at the impact site that has endured across millions of years of plate tectonics. The Scar passes through phases (blazing wound → buried → deep rift → glowing rift-center within Eldravath) before sustained Large Igneous Province extrusion finally builds enough volume to breach the surface and form the Arathyn continent. Arathyn is therefore the *surface expression* of the Scar; the two are the same geological feature at different points in its evolution.

Under the canonical [[eadras-timeline|10my timeline]], the [[eadras-tectonic-history|tectonic snapshot series]] shows the Scar still glowing at 4 million years post-Cataclysm (~6 mybp); Arathyn must therefore have surfaced sometime between then and present, on the upper end of the original 2-8 million year LIP-buildup estimate.
