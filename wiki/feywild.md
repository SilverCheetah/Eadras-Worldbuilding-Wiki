# Feywild

**Summary**: The Feywild is the bright reflection of the Prime Material Plane, home to fey spirits, animal spirits, and positive energy beings — and twisted by the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]] into strange beauty.

**Sources**: Feywild.txt, Spear of Crystallized Entropy.txt, Internal Cosmology of the Eadras Mythos.txt, More corrections.txt, Extra large grok chat 5-9-2026.txt, spear-containment-diagnosis-and-the-alien-artifact-principle.md (2026-05-13 ingest, second-hiding time-displacement and reordered plane-corruption sequence).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-13

## Overview

The Feywild is a **complete reflection** of the entire Eadras Prime — including its planet, all four moons ([[gaia|Gaia]], [[daj|Daj]], [[shali|Shali]], [[shani|Shani]]), and every other Prime feature. It is shifted into a bright, positive-energy resonance influenced by [[celestia|Celestia]], and is home to many spirits filled with positive energy — including fey and animal spirits. [[gods-of-eadras|Sylph]], patron goddess of all Fae creatures, has a strong connection to this plane.

### The Deep Feywild — near-infinite extent beyond the reflection

Crucially, the Feywild is **not bounded by the reflected geography**. The reflection of the Prime Material occupies one region of the Feywild, but the plane extends **near-infinitely** in every direction beyond that reflection (source: More corrections.txt). Beyond the Prime-reflected territory lies vast, indeterminate Feywild expanse — bright wild-magic country, impossible terrain, regions of pure positive resonance, fey realms within fey realms, courts of beings whose existence has no Prime analogue.

The current working name for this near-infinite outer region is **the Deep Feywild** (provisional, captured 2026-05-09). The user has explicitly flagged the entire pair of plane names — *Feywild* and *Shadowdark* — as D&D-coded placeholders likely to be renamed before publication, so the Deep-Feywild label is held alongside them and may change.

This means the Feywild is **far larger than the Prime Material**, with the Prime-reflected portion serving as a kind of anchored shore from which the rest of the plane extends. The farther one travels from the Prime-reflected region, the stranger and more wondrous (and dangerous) things become.

## The Ways

The Feywild has a system of portals and paths called **Ways** that allow quick traversal of the nearly infinite plane. These Ways are corrupt and dangerous to use.

## History: Twisted by the Spear

The Feywild was once called **Brightmirror** — the bright reflection of the Prime Material. After Tarloth's wielding (sometime between 10 mybp and Darkhoof's much later retrieval), the Elder Gods imprisoned the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] in Brightmirror, using a **time-displacement** seal: the Spear was held in a state one minute ahead of present time, with the plane serving as the spatial anchor. Any attempt to reach the Spear always grasped at the empty space where the Spear *had been* one minute ago; the artifact was permanently just out of reach across a sliver of time that no ordinary effort could close.

Over the long centuries the Spear sat in Brightmirror, its time-displaced presence leaked into the plane around it. **Brightmirror gradually transformed into the Feywild** we know today — a plane whose hallmark is exactly the time-strange, half-out-of-step character that prolonged exposure to a displaced artifact produced. The Feywild's modern temporal anomalies — years passing in a night, an hour stretching to lifetimes, mortals returning from fae feasts to find the world centuries older — are the residue of the Spear's hiding shaping the plane around it.

The resulting plane, while still filled with positive energy, is warped into its characteristic uncanny strangeness by this history. In the [[verdant-scar]], where the Feywild presses particularly close, impossible-colored glades mark where Sylph's laughter fell during the aftermath of the Arathi Cataclysm.

The Spear is **no longer in the Feywild**. [[unicorns|Darkhoof]] retrieved it from the Feywild — by means not fully recorded; unicorn temporal sensitivity is the leading hypothesis — and the gods then placed the Spear in the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#hiding-3-reverse-time-bubble-over-a-god-prison-in-the-deepest-shadowdark-after-darkhoof|third hiding]] in the deepest [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]. But the Feywild's plane-character — the time-anomaly, the strangeness, the uncanny beauty — is the lingering residue of the centuries the Spear spent here.
