# Icons and Simulacra

**Summary**: Every god in the [[eadras-cosmology|Eadras Mythos]] — Elder or [[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods|Ascended]] — operates through the same two-part mediation framework. Each has exactly **one Icon on the Prime Material** (the god's only Prime-Material body and the required anchor for any direct interaction with the Mythos) and **one or more Simulacra in the spirit realms** ([[solarion|Solarion]], [[nyxar|Nyxar]], the [[feywild|Feywild]], the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]], the Elemental Planes, wherever the god's divine power reaches). **Avatars** — direct partial divine presences carrying full reality-weight, of the kind routine across many other Mythoi — **are impossible in Eadras**. Manifesting one would risk cracking the narrative foundation of the world. The Icon-and-Simulacrum framework is therefore not a design choice; it is what the Mythos's wounded condition can bear.

**Sources**: velveth-01/02/03/04 v2 (2026-05-15 ingest, Tier-1 framework); Playground generation 2nd part.txt; More corrections.txt.

**Last updated**: 2026-05-15.

## Why the framework exists

Eadras cannot bear **Avatars** — direct partial divine presences carrying full reality-weight. Manifesting one would risk **cracking the narrative foundation of the world**. The Mythos is too cosmologically wounded:

- Observer-hungry from the **Primordial Sacrifice** of [[primordial-duo|Evye and Fryr]] (see [[observer-and-naming]])
- Scarred by the **[[arathi-cataclysm|Worldbreaker]]** at the foundation of the Mythos
- Weakened by multiple **[[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]]** across the deep eras
- Damaged by the **[[demon-war|Demon War]]** and the divine compromises that ended it
- Degraded by the **[[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]]** in the post-war era

The accumulated wounds have left the Mythos too fragile to bear Avatar-class manifestation. The Icon-and-Simulacrum framework is what makes divine operation in Eadras possible at all. **The mediation is not chosen by any god; it is required by the Mythos's condition.**

This is the cosmological floor beneath the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]. The gods do not merely *choose* not to manifest fully in Eadras — they *cannot*, structurally.

## Icons

Each deity in Eadras has exactly **one true Icon**.

The Icon is the god's **only body in the Prime Material**. For an [[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods|Ascended Mortal God]], the Icon is the mortal body transformed at the moment of [[apotheosis|Apotheosis]]. For an Elder God, the Icon was constituted at the cosmological founding. In both cases, the Icon is the **load-bearing anchor required for any direct interaction with the Mythos**.

Temple maxim: *"Many stones bear the god's face. One stone bears the god's body. When the god enters, stone remembers how to walk."*

### Planar location

**All Icons are on the Prime Material, without exception.**

Never on the Mirror Planes ([[feywild|Feywild]], [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]], [[veil-gaia|Veil-Gaia]]), never on the Upper Planes ([[celestia|Celestia]] and its layers), never on the Lower Planes ([[nyxar|Nyxar]] and its layers), never on the Elemental Planes. The Prime Material is the load-bearing reality-substrate of the Mythos; Icons must anchor to it specifically.

This rule holds even for the gods of [[great-banishment|Banished]] peoples. [[velveth|Velveth's]] Icon manifested on the Prime Material of Eadras at the moment of her Apotheosis, although Kathira herself was in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] on Shadowdark [[gaia|Gaia]] at the time. The Banished progenitor pantheons' Icons remain on Eadras's Prime Material despite their peoples' exile; the mechanism by which they are maintained without their peoples' presence is **open canon**.

### Visibility

**Most mortals do not know that Icons exist.**

The concept is held by **inner clergy** of the gods, by **the gods themselves**, by **[[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]**, by the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran's]] curatorial scope, and by very few others. Most worshippers passing through a temple do not understand that their tradition's main devotional object might be the god's actual body or might be a sanctified replica; the question does not arise in ordinary practice.

**Most Icons are hidden** in protected sanctuaries, deep vaults, or sealed chambers known only to the highest priestly inner circles. **A few are hidden in plain sight** — embedded as the central altar-object in the nave of a main temple, where worshippers pass by daily without knowing what they are seeing. A famous temple may contain only a decoy; a humble roadside shrine may secretly guard a true Icon.

### Stillness and Habitation

The Icon has two operational states:

- **Stillness** — when the god's attention is elsewhere, the Icon resembles a statue (carved stone, metal, crystal, wood, bone, divine glass, or other sacred material aligned with the deity's nature). It may radiate holiness, but it does not move, speak, or act. It remains dangerous to attack because it remains the god's Prime anchor.
- **Habitation** — when the god turns full attention to the Prime Material, the Icon becomes a living divine body. In this state the god can speak directly, perceive through divine senses, move physically and fight, perform full divine spell usage, manifest portfolio power, bless or curse or bind with immediate force, and **divinely teleport anywhere in the Prime Material**.

A god using their Icon can choose how much divine aura to manifest: full suppression (appearing as nothing more than a moving sacred statue), partial radiance (a clear divine presence without overwhelming nearby mortals), or full glory.

Some gods use their Icon often; others rarely. Frequency of Icon use is a matter of divine temperament and political risk-tolerance.

### Materials

The material of a true Icon is aligned with the deity's nature:

- **Mountain marble** for [[ioa|Ioa]]
- **Hearthstone** for [[nara|Nara]]
- **Star-metal inlays** for [[lirra|Lirra]]
- **Wildroot petrified wood** for [[qoxli|Qoxli]]
- **Nightglass** for [[nox|Nox]]
- **Bone-white limestone** for [[shali|Shali]] or [[shani|Shani]]
- **Storm-fused stone** for [[daj|Daj]]
- **Laughing opal or Feywild materials** for [[sylph|Sylph]]
- **Midnight-blue Velrathi stone** with House Valtharion sigils for [[velveth|Velveth]] (her Icon retains [[velveth|Kathira's]] form: three metres tall, ashen-gray, red-eyed, with the long gray hair Kathira had combed on the [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]], wearing formal Velrathi robes in deep midnight-blue; partly-veiled face showing *resolve-grief-steadiness held as one feeling*)

Icon sculptors are theological engineers requiring sacred knowledge, not merely artistic skill. A flawed Icon can be dangerous — a malformed divine body might distort manifestation.

### The One-Icon Rule

Each deity has exactly one true Icon. Other statues, idols, shrine-images, and consecrated representations may:

- Receive prayers and focus worship
- Hold blessings and serve as ritual symbols
- Channel minor divine magic

But they cannot be inhabited as the god's full Prime body.

## Simulacra

Every god maintains **one or more Simulacra in the spirit realms** — wherever the god's divine power has reach. Simulacra are *not* Prime-Material bodies and never operate on the Prime Material. They are the institutional working-presences through which the god conducts the daily business of the divine bureaucracy.

The most common Simulacrum location is **[[solarion|Solarion]] (the Celestial main street)**, where almost every Ascended Mortal God and every Elder God maintains a working office. Other Simulacrum locations include:

- **[[nyxar|Nyxar]]** — for gods whose portfolios touch the infernal-bureaucratic register, or whose duty-bound cases require coordination with [[infernal-bureaucracy|the Infernal Bureaucracy]]
- **The [[feywild|Feywild]]** — for gods (notably [[sylph|Sylph]] and some Elderborn) whose domains reach the Mirror-Plane register
- **The [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]** — including the geode-city of [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]], for gods whose portfolios touch the exiled Velrathi
- **The Elemental Planes** — for elemental-portfolio gods
- **Multiple cross-spirit-realm offices** — for gods with broad portfolio reach

A Simulacrum is **fully conscious, fully the god in character, fully empowered** to act on the god's behalf in cross-pantheon institutional business. It is not a delegated lesser presence; it *is* the god, distributed across the cosmology. When [[velveth|Velveth's]] Solarion simulacrum signs a Velrathi vow-prayer at her desk, the goddess signs.

The Simulacrum framework is **how the divine bureaucracy functions**. Without it, every god would have to use their Icon for every interaction (Prime-Material-only) or work entirely through clergy. Simulacra are the institutional substrate that makes cross-pantheon coordination, prayer-signing, treaty-witnessing, and judgement-rendering possible at all.

## Divine realms

Many gods (especially Ascended Mortal Gods) also maintain a **personal divine realm** in the outer planes — a created cosmological region specific to the god. [[velveth|Velveth's]] is the [[hall-of-the-long-quiet|Hall of the Long Quiet]]; [[imma|Imma's]] is associated with her Solarion office and a wagon-style outer-plane structure. The divine realm is *not* a Simulacrum-location; it is the god's *primary cosmological presence*. The Simulacra handle the institutional working-day; the divine realm is where the god *is*.

Elder Gods inherited their cosmological positions at foundation; Ascended Mortal Gods have their divine realms created instantly at [[apotheosis|Apotheosis]]. The *form* the realm takes is shaped by the architecture of bearing that produced the god (see [[apotheosis#The portfolio inherits the bearing]]).

## Avatars

**There are no Avatars in Eadras.**

In other Mythoi across the [[omniverse|Omniverse]], gods routinely project Avatars — direct partial divine presences carrying full reality-weight, capable of acting in the world without risking the god's primary body. Such projections are common, mundane, and uncontroversial in those other Mythoi.

**In Eadras they are impossible.** The Mythos cannot bear them. Any prior canonical material describing an "Avatar" of an Elder God, Elderborn, or Ascended Mortal in Eadras is to be understood as either an Icon-manifestation (the body itself, present in the Prime Material) or a Simulacrum-presence (in a spirit realm), not as an Avatar.

This rule has no documented exception. [[dao|Dao]] is sometimes informally called *"the Avatar of the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]]"*; this is loose terminology and predates the framework's establishment. Dao operates in Eadras by a special mechanism outside the standard Icon-and-Simulacrum framework, hidden from the Elder Gods; the precise nature of Dao's presence is sealed canon and is *not* an Eadras-Mythos Avatar in the technical sense forbidden here. The only entity in Eadras's history known to have carried full divine reality-weight directly was the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] (an alien-Mythos object, not a deity) and [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] during his brief Unrestricted-Elder-God period — *which is precisely the kind of cosmic catastrophe the Avatar-impossibility rule exists to prevent.*

## Icon Destruction

Destroying a true Icon is extremely difficult. It is not enough to chip, topple, burn, or crack the body. True destruction typically requires:

- Divine-scale force
- Forbidden ritual knowledge
- An opposing god's aid
- An artifact of immense power
- A catastrophic magical event
- Corruption of the Icon's consecration matrix

### Consequences: ordinary gods

If a god's Icon is destroyed, the god **loses Prime-Material influence for approximately 100 years** — the time required to reform the Icon. During this interval:

- The god's domain becomes **unruled** — the portfolio continues to operate cosmologically, but its *governance* falls away
- *Magic without a mind becomes chaotic rule in effect* — the metaphysical structures of the portfolio still exist; what is missing is the deity's continuous arbitration over them
- The god continues to exist in the spirit realms, maintains Simulacra-presences, and conducts institutional work
- After approximately 100 years, the Icon reforms and Prime-Material influence is restored

The severity of *unruled-domain* consequences varies by portfolio:

- **Most cases**: bad but bearable. The portfolio continues to function chaotically while the god restores Prime presence
- **[[lirra|Lirra's]] case** — the canonical extreme: if Lirra's Icon were destroyed, **magic in Eadras would cease entirely for 100 years**, because magic's governance falls to her, and without her ruling presence the entire system loses its mind
- **[[ioa|Ioa]]**: sovereignty becomes spiritually brittle; succession crises and oath-breakage multiply
- **[[vos|Vos]]**: law, commerce, and institutional wisdom lose divine coherence
- **[[qoxli|Qoxli]]**: wild balance becomes harsher and less protected
- **[[shali|Shali]] or [[shani|Shani]]**: passage, rest, and necromantic reliability destabilise
- **[[nara|Nara]]**: domestic peace, childbirth blessings, family sanctity lose divine shelter
- **[[velveth|Velveth]]**: bargains-honoured-in-full would lose their governing presence; betrayals would happen without weight-bearing; cross-pantheon vow-signing would fail routing

No sane kingdom wants an enemy god's Icon destroyed, even one they hate — the portfolio vacuum may be worse than the deity's presence. **Icon-protection is one of the most serious institutional responsibilities in Eadras.**

### Consequences: Total Icon Binding (unique exception)

After [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] ascensions, the Elder Gods may combine their power to force a newly ascended Unrestricted Elder God fully into their Icon. This is called **Total Icon Binding**, and it converts the standard Icon-destruction consequences into terminal annihilation.

A Total-Bound god:

- Cannot leave the Icon
- Cannot teleport through it or manifest elsewhere
- Cannot project power beyond direct **line of sight** from the Icon
- Cannot act through distant worshipers normally
- Cannot freely touch their portfolio across the Prime
- Retains immense power — but only where the Icon can perceive

Because the god-spirit becomes fully localised within the Icon, **destroying the Icon becomes permanent divine annihilation**, not 100-year recovery. There is no banishment, no return after a century. The god is destroyed.

**Known Total-Bound gods**:

- **[[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]]** — bound after Spear ascension; the [[arathi-cataclysm|Worldender]] destroyed his Icon, annihilating him completely. *The unique recorded case of standard Icon-destruction-rule being terminal*.
- **[[xarvion|Xar'vion]]** — bound after the [[demon-war|Demon War]]; his Icon is intact but contained; he exists as the [[kingdom-of-xanar|Motionless King]]

Total Icon Binding is used to contain rogue Spear-gods who are too dangerous to fight openly but cannot easily be destroyed. It converts the Icon from a body into a prison.

## Why Gods Avoid Manifestation

An inhabited Icon exposes the god's only Prime Material anchor. Every reason for divine restraint:

- **Icon vulnerability**: Targets become clear when the god manifests
- **Hundred-year unruled-domain risk**: If the Icon is destroyed, the portfolio falls into chaos for a century
- **Doctrine of the Unheld Hand**: Direct action may violate divine noninterference (and is also a cosmological constraint — see [[apotheosis#Avatars are impossible in Eadras]])
- **Divine escalation**: One god's intervention may provoke responses from rivals
- **Icon-trapping**: Enemies may attempt to bind, corrupt, imprison, or misdirect the inhabited Icon
- **Consequential imbalance**: Even beneficial miracles can destabilize politics, ecosystems, or magical systems

A god who manifests too frequently creates a target. Gods therefore prefer working through clerics, champions, dreams, portents, prayer-routed Simulacrum signing, and limited miracles.

A Vosian maxim: *"A god who walks too often among kings teaches assassins where heaven keeps its throat."*

## Icon Warfare

Destroying a god's Icon is one of the highest forms of divine warfare. Enemies may attempt to:

- Locate and distinguish true Icons from ordinary statues
- Corrupt temple consecrations
- Sever the Icon's Prime linkage before physical destruction
- Trap a deity while it inhabits the Icon

Temple defenses include:

- Physical fortifications, priest-guards, and paladins
- False Icon-statues and decoy shrines
- Hidden sanctums and mobile Icons
- Divine seals and oath-locked chambers

Icon-guardians — beasts blessed or bred to protect specific Icons — are treated with immense reverence. The deepest schemes of [[black-hand|the Black Hand]] gain new dimensions when read against the unruled-domain framework: targeted Icon-destruction of high-leverage gods could cause civilization-class damage during the 100-year recovery interval, without requiring "killing gods" outright.

## Xar'vion's Total-Bound Icon

[[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] Icon is not destroyed — it is bound. He was forced into Total Icon Binding after his defeat in the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. His Icon, with him fully contained inside it, exists today at the [[kingdom-of-xanar|Black Throne of Xanareth]] — but it is not held in a prison and is not guarded by any external order. There is no order maintaining a "line-of-sight prison" around him. The line-of-sight restriction is a property of the Total Icon Binding itself; it does not require a watching order to enforce.

**The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] can move Xar'vion's Icon, and they have done so repeatedly across ten thousand years.** The Icon is mobile; it is not fixed at the Black Throne by any external constraint. Where the Icon currently sits is therefore a Black Hand operational secret, and "the Black Throne of Xanareth" should be understood as the most-frequently-occupied location, not as a guaranteed present location.

The Black Hand's plan requires bringing the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] into Xar'vion's presence at the proper [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]. The Inner Hand entertains two speculative outcomes — the Spear may simply free Xar'vion from the Icon, or it may trigger a second ascension that raises him above the Elder Gods (which would also free him as a side effect). Neither possibility is known to be mechanically possible. See [[black-hand#The two speculations|Black Hand: the two speculations]].

The Elder Gods did not destroy Xar'vion's Icon because destroying it risks unpredictable consequences and may require [[worldender|Worldender]]-scale force they are unwilling or unable to deploy.

## Ixthal's Icon

[[ixthal|Ixthal]] must have one true Icon, but its location is among the greatest hidden facts in Eadras. He does not communicate with mortals or gods, barely inhabits his Icon openly, and has shielded it from divine attention. It may stand in a sealed post-[[arathi|Arathi]] refuge, beneath the [[verdant-scar|Verdant Scar]], or in a cavern that receives reflected Source-light only during rare alignments.
