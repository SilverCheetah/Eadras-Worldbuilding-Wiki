# Tideclad

**Summary**: The Tideclad — *the Current Wardens* — are one of the **Five Modern Oceanic Races** descended from the **Sea'tua**, the [[elfen|Elfen]]-engineered overseers of oceanic balance. They are disciplined martial protectors who weaponize and endure extreme currents, riptides, and pressure — "cladding" themselves in living water forces to form temporary armor and blades from redirected currents and pressure waves.

**Sources**: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt; Peoples of Eadras.txt; EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt (2026-05-31, oceanic Netharim)

**Last updated**: 2026-05-31

---

## Origin

Tideclad descend from the **Sea'tua**, the Elfen-engineered race created to oversee natural balance in the oceans. They are one of the five Sea'tua-descended modern oceanic peoples — and the closest modern descendants to the classical "merman" form recognizable to surface dwellers (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt; Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt).

## Anatomy

Tideclad are **heavily armored, robust merman forms with natural bone plating and powerful fin structures**. Adult Tideclad are unmistakable in profile — broad, plated, and built for sustained pressure.

## Cladding

The discipline that gives the Tideclad their name is **cladding**: the active redirection of surrounding water forces — currents, pressure waves, riptides — into momentary armor or blades that wrap around the warden's body or extend from their hands. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

A clad Tideclad is not invulnerable; the cladding is alive, dynamic, and must be sustained. But a Tideclad in their natural element, properly clad, is dangerous on a scale that surface martial traditions struggle to translate.

## Combat philosophy

The Tideclad view **battle as "releasing the sea's tension."** They fight by turning an opponent's own momentum and the ocean's force against them — currents are routed back into the attacker, pressure waves are reflected at angles that crack what the attacker presents, and a Tideclad blade is often the same blade the attacker brought, redirected by water. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

This is a discipline more than a weapon. Tideclad martial training emphasizes patience, position, and the reading of currents. A Tideclad who has chosen the ground and timed the tide has, in their own teaching, already won.

## Society

Tideclad are **highly disciplined and honor-bound**. They guard:

- **Sacred boundaries** — markers placed by ancient Sea'tua oversight that are still maintained.
- **Dangerous rifts** — rift mouths where Limina bleed-through is possible (a discipline that puts them in adjacency to [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]] cathedrals more often than they would like).
- **Important migration routes** — the deep-current paths along which Merrow trade goods, Siren choirs, and Ink Lord chroniclers travel.

They live in **fortified trench-cities** and **roving warden schools** that patrol the most volatile waters. A warden school is a unit; a trench-city is a base; both are organized hierarchically and trained to move as a single body. Warden schools are the units typically encountered by surface mariners who have travel arrangements with Tideclad waters.

## Weave-magic and the oceanic Netharim

The Tideclad are not a magically-blank martial culture. The [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] is planetary, and three of the fourteen [[seven-conduits|conduit]] ends emerge underwater among the five oceanic peoples — so **[[netharim|Netharim]] arise among the Tideclad** as naturally as on land. Oceanic Netharim are a branch of the same tradition (a deeply-studied internal model of reality imposed directly on the world); a Tideclad Netharim's model most often runs toward **force, current, and pressure** — the same comprehension that underlies cladding, carried into reality-shaping. The Tideclad's institutional role makes them the natural **wardens of the submarine conduit-cities**: a [[order-sharanel#Oceanic Towers and chapters|submarine Tower of Lirra]] sits on the strongest Weave-source for leagues, and the warden schools that already guard rifts and migration routes are the obvious guardians of those Towers and their oceanic Netharim. Because the surface [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] could not reach the seafloor, the oceanic peoples likely kept an **unbroken Netharim-and-Tower tradition** while the surface lost six Towers. Which of the five peoples hold which of the three submarine conduit-cities is open canon. (source: EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt, 2026-05-31)

## Relations with other oceanic peoples

- **[[merrow|Merrow]]** — frequent contracted protection of reef-cities and Growth Circles.
- **[[sirens|Sirens]]** — escorts for traveling choirs through dangerous currents.
- **[[shimmercloaks|Shimmercloaks]]** — common employers for high-value memory-coin transit escorts.
- **[[ink-lords|Ink Lords]]** — embedded chroniclers; many warden schools have a long-resident Ink Lord as a memory-keeper.
- **[[sahuagin|Sahuagin]]** — adversarial. Tideclad warden schools guard the deep-current routes Blood Fleets target on raids, and the two peoples have several thousand years of running engagements.
- **[[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]]** — uneasy. The Tideclad guard the rifts; the Kuo-toa worship them. Open conflict is rare but rules of engagement around major Gaps are established.
