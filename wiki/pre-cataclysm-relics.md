# Pre-Cataclysm Relics

**Summary**: Relics, ecosystems, and creatures that survive in fragments from the world that existed before the [[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]] — including vitrified fossil reefs, mineral-preserved beasts, and petrified forests of the lost 70%-land world.

**Sources**: Playground generation 1st part.txt, Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-05

---

## Overview

Before the [[arathi-cataclysm|Falling Star]], Eadras was a land-heavy world. Roughly 70% of its surface was land: vast continental shields, inland salt basins, warm shallow seas, endless marshlands, broad savannahs under the [[eldritch|Source]]. The Cataclysm was an Extinction-Level Event killing approximately 60% of all life on Eadras. Recovery took millions of years (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt).

Yet not every trace of the old world vanished. Deep strata, sealed pressure pockets, mineral preservation, and rare magical revivals occasionally yield evidence — or living remnants — of Eadras as it existed before the world began to walk.

## Pre-Cataclysm Atmosphere & Fire Ecology

The pre-Cataclysm atmosphere of Eadras was substantially **oxygen-richer than modern air — approximately 35% O₂**, compared to the modern ~21–25%. This high-oxygen baseline is the load-bearing fact behind much of how Arathi civilization built and lived. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

### Why the high oxygen mattered

A 35% O₂ atmosphere makes **spontaneous combustion and rapid wildfire spread a constant, serious danger** across the entire surface. Dry plant matter ignites more readily, fires burn hotter and faster, and minor sparks become serious threats in a way they are not in the modern atmosphere. Every aspect of Arathi planet-spanning civilization — densely built cities, vast organic-fuel forests, magitek heat-engines, ritual flame-work, even cooking — had to operate against that baseline danger.

### How the Arathi managed it

The [[arathi|Arathi]] developed an integrated fire-management infrastructure that coupled their [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]] traditions to civic planning. The canonical control mechanisms are:

- **Crystal-tech fire-suppression fields** — [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]]-powered standing fields throughout cities and across vulnerable wilderness, capable of catching ignition events before propagation.
- **Active humidity control** — citywide and regional moisture management to reduce ignition probability in dry seasons.
- **Non-flammable construction materials** — preferential building in crystal, [[mana-metals#Orichalcum|orichalcum]], flame-veined marble, and other materials that simply do not burn.
- **Rapid-response Household Servitors** — see [[arathi-servitors|Household Servitors]]; one of their many duties was first-response to incipient fires in domestic and laboratory settings.
- **[[lirra|Lirra]]'s stabilizing lattices** — Lirra's own contribution to the pre-Weave magical infrastructure included stabilizing patterns over flammable regions, dampening fire propagation by holding ambient combustion below threshold.

The combination of these systems made high-oxygen Eadras navigable for civilization. Strip any of them away and the fire risk becomes intolerable.

### What the Cataclysm did to the atmosphere

The Cataclysm's planet-wide firestorms and re-entry ignition burned off **enormous amounts of oxygen** — in absolute terms, more atmospheric oxygen consumption than any other event in Eadras's history. The post-Cataclysm atmosphere then **gradually normalized over the following millions of years** as biological cycles, vegetation regrowth, and oceanic recovery rebalanced the system. The modern 21–25% range is the long-term equilibrium that emerged after the impact's combustion.

The implications are large and not yet fully integrated into the wiki:

- **Pre-Cataclysm forests, savannas, and atmosphere-dependent organisms** were adapted to 35% O₂ and would not survive the modern atmosphere even if revived. [[pre-cataclysm-relics#Ashseed Forests|Ashseed Forests]], [[pre-cataclysm-relics#Stillbeasts|Stillbeasts]], and similar revivals are not just out of their food web — they are out of their *air*.
- **Magitek heat-engines** designed for the dense pre-Cataclysm atmosphere may underperform on modern air. Recovered Arathi magitek that "doesn't quite work" may be calibrated to oxygen levels we no longer have.
- **The fire-management infrastructure described above** is no longer needed at modern oxygen levels, which is part of why later civilizations did not rediscover it; the problem went away with the air.

## Arathic Glass Reefs

Fossil reefs vitrified by the [[arathi-cataclysm|White Deluge]] when boiling subterranean waters met cooling impact glass. They contain impressions of extinct shallow-sea organisms preserved as ghost-prints in dark crystalline rock. They are valuable to magical archaeologists and to alchemists, who consider their crystalline matrix unique.

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

---

## Stillbeasts

Rare pre-Extinction organisms preserved in **mineral stasis** — not temporal stasis (the [[omniversal-laws|Omniversal Law of Time]] forbids that), but a stasis-like material enchantment in which biological processes have been suspended through unique mineral chemistry, ancient Arathi enchantment, or sealed environmental conditions.

Sometimes awakened by mining, deep excavation, or magical disturbance. They are not adapted to the modern world and usually die violently or tragically — their atmospheres, food webs, prey species, and climate niches no longer exist. A Stillbeast briefly walking the modern world is essentially a ghost in flesh.

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

---

## Ashseed Forests

Petrified forests from the old 70%-land world. Their seeds occasionally remain viable, particularly when reanimated by druidic or Qoxlian magic. The resulting trees cannot survive modern rainfall patterns, atmospheric chemistry, or temperature ranges without careful magical maintenance — they are botanical refugees from a dead climate.

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Failed Sanctuaries of the Three Last Lights

Some [[three-last-lights|Auralis and Thamior]]'s failed miracles left **relic ecologies** — not surviving Arathi, but evidence of love losing to scale:

- A valley where all fossils face upward, as if raised by hands
- A glass dome on the floor of the [[arathalassic-sea|Arathalassic Sea]] containing only clean air and dust — sited above the ruins of a non-Altean Arathi sanctuary, since Altea itself left no remnants
- A mountain peak where ash never settles because one dying goddess still exhales
- A reef grown in the shape of sleeping children
- Veins of **Thamior's Strain** found in mountain roots — stone folded like muscle under strain

These are not living relics but persistent magical anomalies — last gestures of essence-spent ascended deities who could not save their people (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt).

## Materials of the Cataclysm

The White Deluge deposited or exposed strange materials that have shaped post-Cataclysm civilization:

| Material | Description | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **Star-pumice** | Floating impact stone holding heat and enchantment | Magitek heat-engines; surface kingdoms fight over deposits |
| **White salts** | Mineral residues from superheated underwaters | Preservation, warding, [[necromancy-and-undeath|necromancy]] |
| **Abyssal glass** | Black-blue glass formed where boiling waters met starfire | Holds enchantment underwater; controlled by sea peoples |
| **Root-metal** | Deep metallic veins exposed by the unzipping | Mined by dwarves, gnomes, and giants |

Post-Drowning societies rebuilt around these new resources. Dwarves and gnomes prize root-metal. Sea peoples control abyssal glass. Surface kingdoms fight over star-pumice deposits. [[arathalassic-sea|Scaldwyrms]] nesting in star-pumice fields make mining dangerous.

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Calendar Markers

The Cataclysm created the central before-and-after of mortal history. Common scholarly and cultural markers:

- **Before the Falling Star** — the lost world
- **After the Drowning** — the post-Cataclysm world
- **After the Weave** — used in magical histories
- **After the Demon War** — used in later political histories

Common phrases:
- *"When the Star Fell"* — the ritual/impact moment
- *"When the White Waters Came"* — the steam and superheated flood
- *"When Arath Drowned"* — the civilization's fall
- *"When the World Began to Walk"* — the beginning of plate tectonics, used today to mean "earthquake"
- *"Older than the deep sea"*
- *"Built when roads crossed where ships now sail"*
- *"Proud as Arath before steam"*
- *"Don't drill where the world once screamed"*

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)
