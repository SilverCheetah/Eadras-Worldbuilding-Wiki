# Golems

**Summary**: Constructed sapient beings — including the **Warforged** variant — listed among the playable present-day races of Eadras. Modern golems are **creations of [[gnomes|gnomes]] using scavenged [[arathi|Arathi]] crystal minds**, making them the most direct living link between the modern world and the magitech of Eadras's first civilization.

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt, Arathis.txt, user clarification 2026-04-30

**Last updated**: 2026-04-30

---

## Origin

Modern golems and Warforged are **gnome creations** built around **scavenged crystal minds salvaged from [[arathi|Arathi]] ruins** (source: user clarification 2026-04-30).

This canon places golem-craft squarely in gnome hands and gives it a precise material genealogy:

1. The **[[arathi|Arathi]]** built and commanded golems on a vast scale during their pre-Cataclysm civilization. Their golem-mind technology — the crystalline cognition cores that gave their constructs sapience — was scattered across Eadras when the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] shattered their civilization.
2. The **[[grugi|Grug]]** were Velrathi-engineered as maintainers and inventors of imperial technological systems — small, smart, mechanically adept, clever, sneaky (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt). Their core competency was working with magitech.
3. **[[gnomes|Gnomes]]** inherited the Grug's mechanical aptitude. Across the long ages after the [[demon-war|Demon War]], they have applied that aptitude to **scavenging and rehousing** the Arathi crystal minds that survive in scattered ruins.
4. A modern golem or Warforged is the result: an Arathi crystal mind given a new gnome-built body, animated by the union of the crystal's salvaged sapience and the gnome's craftsmanship.

The supply of intact crystal minds is finite. There is no extant tradition of *making* new ones — only of finding, restoring, and rehousing the survivors. This makes every golem in the modern world, in a quiet way, a piece of the lost Arathi civilization still walking around.

## Sapience

Golems are sapient enough to be **playable** (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt, line 118). The sapience comes from the **Arathi crystal mind**, not from the gnome-built body. This distinguishes modern playable golems sharply from the simple non-sentient skeletons and constructs the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] used as labor in their imperial cities — those were mindless automatons with no comparable cognition.

Each Arathi crystal mind is, functionally, a single individual continuous cognition that was preserved when its original body failed and that has since been salvaged and rehoused. The mind itself remembers — sometimes vividly, sometimes only in fragments — what it was before. Modern golems carry the weight of that inheritance.

## Warforged

The user's canonical present-day races list specifies **"Golem (including Warforged)"** (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt). Warforged are a **subtype within the broader gnome golem-craft tradition** — a war-built or military-purpose lineage. They are still gnome creations using salvaged Arathi crystal minds, but built into bodies designed for combat rather than general utility.

The relationship between general-purpose golems and Warforged in Eadras is therefore one of **intent and chassis**, not of fundamental origin: the same gnome workshops produce both, drawing from the same finite stock of salvaged Arathi minds, but the body design and martial capability differs.

## Materials

Gnome golem-builders work primarily with the [[mana-metals|mana-metals]] tradition: **mithril**, **orichalcum**, **kylum**, and **valthyr**, with various alloys (valthrene, argentus, aurelius, elyrium) used for specialized capabilities. The most refined examples may incorporate **magisterium**, the apex tier — though magisterium is exceedingly rare and is more often found in genuine surviving Arathi pieces than in modern reconstructions.

The crystal minds themselves are not a mana-metal but a separate Arathi-developed substrate — apparently inseparable from the original Arathi magitech tradition.

## The Echo of the Arathi

Because every modern golem hosts a salvaged Arathi mind, the modern world's golem population is in a real sense an **echo of the lost first civilization**. This has unsettling implications:

- The crystal minds were created by the Arathi who lived under [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]]'s civilization — the same civilization that produced the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] and the [[worldender|Worldender]] catastrophe.
- Some minds remember pre-Cataclysm Eadras. They are the only first-person witnesses left.
- Whether they consider themselves to be the *same individuals* they once were is a question every restored mind must answer for itself.

The [[katharim|Katharim]], who refuse all arcane magic including **construct-binding** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, line 138), are unlikely to have any role in golem-craft and may regard sapient golems with theological complexity — preserving the **memory** of the dead Arathi is the Katharim's calling, but golems are something stranger: not memory, but resumed presence.

## Society

Whether golems form their own communities, integrate into gnome workshops as colleagues and apprentices, or live as solitary individuals shaped by their gnome restorers and their own salvaged memories — none of this is yet documented. The relationship between a gnome restorer and the mind they bring back is presumably intimate, but its norms and ethics remain to be defined.
