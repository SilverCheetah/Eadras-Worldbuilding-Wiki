# Spear of Crystallized Entropy

**Summary**: The Spear of Crystallized Entropy is the most dangerous artifact in Eadras — a weapon forged by [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] with the aid of the [[neverborn|Neverborn]] entity Cthosh, that transforms its wielder into an Unrestricted Elder God while stripping away their deepest desire. It has been **wielded three times and hidden four times**, and the gods' fourth hiding is a desperate cosmic violation whose visible scar is the modern continent of [[arathyn]]. The Spear is **alien to the Mythos** in a way that places aspects of it outside the gods' direct jurisdiction — they cannot destroy it, cannot eject it, and cannot hide it absolutely; they can only delay it, manage its consequences, and (in extremis) commit acts of cosmic violence against the body of the world itself.

**Sources**: Spear of Crystallized Entropy.txt, Arathis.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Playground generation 2nd part.txt, New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt, More Xarvion.txt, Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt, notes on various topics 1.txt, spear-containment-diagnosis-and-the-alien-artifact-principle.md (2026-05-13 ingest, alien-artifact principle and revised four-hidings sequence; user-integrated resolution preserves [[feywild|Feywild]] and [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] plane-corruption canon and reorders the prison sequence accordingly).

## Form

The Spear is a single **crystalline shaft** with a **dark stain running through its length** — the stain is the visible mark of Dao's curse, threaded through the crystal at the moment of forging. Most accounts that describe the Spear in detail emphasize the stain over the crystal: the eye is drawn to the wrongness inside the lattice rather than to the lattice itself.

The Spear is **sentient at a low level of awareness** — possessed of intent, but not of articulate thought. **It desires to be found.** Across the long centuries of each hiding it is understood to exert a faint but persistent pull on the world around it — strengthening dreams, biasing chance encounters, drawing the wrong kind of curious mortal toward whatever locale conceals it. Its caretakers across the four hidings have all reported the same intuition: the Spear is not inert. It waits, and it wants. (source: notes on various topics 1.txt)

## The alien-artifact principle

The load-bearing cosmological framing of the Spear — the framing that organizes every act of containment the gods have attempted against it — is that **the Spear is alien to the Mythos. It is not something under the gods' direct jurisdiction. They cannot destroy it, cannot eject it from the Mythos, and cannot hide it absolutely.** Everything else in creation answers to them; this one thing does not. (Source: 2026-05-13 ingest.)

The reason is in its forging. The Spear's body is Mythos crystal, but it was forged by [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] with the direct aid of the [[neverborn|Neverborn]] entity **Cthosh** — which means the Spear carries a **non-Mythos signature in its construction**. It is a hybrid: Mythos material bearing an outside-Mythos mark. [[dao|Dao]]'s curse, threaded through the crystal during forging, is the gods' partial answer; the curse is Mythos magic embedded in the alien-marked artifact and operates whenever the Spear is wielded. But the curse does not extend to *destruction* or *expulsion*; it only operates on the wielder's identity.

This is why the Spear can be **contained, delayed, and made to harm its wielder**, but cannot be **destroyed, ejected, or absolutely hidden**. The gods have managed the aspects of the Spear that intersect with Mythos rules; they have no leverage over the aspects that come from outside.

What the gods *can* do is three things:

- **Delay** the Spear's next emergence — often by very long spans of time.
- **Manage the consequences** of each emergence as it occurs.
- In extremis, commit **acts of cosmic violence against the Mythos itself** in order to constrain it.

What they *cannot* do is solve the problem. The Spear is permanent. The Spear is unsolvable. The Spear is, in a literal cosmological sense, *outside the rules*.

The Elder Gods are therefore not omnipotent within the Mythos. There is at least one thing inside their world that they cannot fully control — and they have been working around that limit ever since [[arathi-cataclysm|the day Kudur-eṭir called the Worldbreaker down]]. Folk theology generally elides this; the popular myths in [[mythic-recitations|the mythic recitations]] describe the gods as having *thrown the star at Ssar'veth in judgment*, which is the version of events that preserves divine authority. The actual cosmology is more uncomfortable: the gods could not destroy what Kudur-eṭir created with the aid of an outside-Mythos chaos entity. The [[unicorns|unicorns]] are the only mythic voice in Eadras that reads this correctly ([[mythic-recitations#1-4-the-unicorn-version-told-with-sorrow|*some wounds do not close — they only wait*]]); the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel's]] deepest theology and the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran's]] sealed chapters preserve the principle explicitly. The Tome's open-chapter scholarship acknowledges only that *the precise mechanism remains unknown*.

## Creation

In the final days of the [[arathi|Arathi civilization]], the archmage **Kudur-eṭir** sought to master decay — to reverse entropy itself. He reached beyond all [[mythos|Mythoi]] into raw [[chaos|Primordial Chaos]] and made contact with the unspeakable neverborn entity **Cthosh** — a being of pure chaos and madness. With Cthosh's impossible aid, Kudur-eṭir forged the Spear.

[[dao]] witnessed the creation and secretly laid a **terrible curse** upon it: the Spear would grant the wielder's deepest desire, but only by stripping that desire away and replacing it with its twisted opposite.

## How the Spear works

The Spear's mechanic is **not a ritual**. It is a **timed cosmic alignment**:

> On **God's Day**, every **five thousand years**, at **noon in a particular time zone**, the Spear opens a path for unlimited power to flow from the [[eldritch|Source]] to whoever is holding it. The flow utterly transforms the wielder: their **physical form becomes an [[icons|Icon]]**, and their **soul is transformed into the soul of a God**.

(source: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt)

The wielder becomes an **Unrestricted Elder God** — meaning specifically *not bound by any rules agreed to by other gods*. They have full power equal to other Elder Gods, but they sit outside the inter-divine compact that constrains the rest of the pantheon.

The Spear does not require a ritual; it requires being held at the right time in the right place. The 5000-year cadence is the alignment window itself.

## Dao's curse

Independent of the Spear's timing mechanic, [[dao]] secretly cursed the weapon at the moment of its forging. The curse strips away the wielder's deepest motivation — the central animating "why" that drove them to take up the Spear.

**The curse removes whatever makes the wielder desire the Spear** — more precisely, the most personal motivation that makes the wielder who they are. It identifies the motive most bound to the wielder's choice to take up the Spear and cuts it from their living identity.

### The Kudur-eṭir exception

The curse hit Kudur-eṭir differently. Because he was the *creator* of the Spear *and* maintained direct mental contact with a [[neverborn|Neverborn]] during its forging, the curse did not cleanly excise his motive. Instead it **shredded his consciousness**, rendering him completely insane while leaving his godly power and arcane knowledge intact (though only intermittently accessible). The result was an insane Unrestricted Elder God who could still call on what Kudur-eṭir had known. This is the only known case of the curse producing insanity rather than motive-stripping. (source: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt)

What the curse does NOT remove:
- Memory (the wielder may remember their old motive)
- Intelligence
- Power
- Historical awareness

What it does remove:
- The animating force of that motive — the central "why"

The wielder can describe their old self like a scholar describing a dead civilization. They cannot fully be that self anymore. Spear-made gods are best understood as **apotheoses of severed intent**. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

Dao's intent was to make the Spear self-betraying: *"Whatever you reach for it to accomplish, the act of gaining it removes the part of you that needed that accomplishment."* This does not prevent catastrophe — it ensures the catastrophe is caused by someone who no longer has reason to commit it.

A temple warning associated with the Spear:
*Touch not the Spear for love, for love shall leave you.*
*Touch not the Spear for justice, for justice shall become only judgment.*
*Touch not the Spear for grief, for grief shall become hunger.*
*Touch not the Spear for salvation, for salvation shall forget whom it meant to save.*

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Known wielders

The Spear has been **found three times and hidden four times**. The four wielders, in order, are Kudur-eṭir, Tarloth the Cruel, Darkhoof, and Xar'vion. The four hidings (the gods' four attempts at containment after each use) are documented in the next section.

### Kudur-eṭir (first use, 10 mybp)

[[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] desired to **end entropy entirely**, believing he could thereby make the Arathi civilization perfect and eternal. He held the Spear at the proper time on God's Day five thousand years into Arathi history, and the Source-flow transformed him into the first Unrestricted Elder God. Because he had created the Spear *and* had direct mental contact with the [[neverborn|Neverborn]] during its forging, [[dao|Dao's]] curse hit him in a unique mode: it shredded his consciousness, leaving him insane but still capable of using his power and knowledge. The insane god then called down an **alien [[worldender|Falling Star]]** onto the Arathi capital [[altea|Altea]] — *not* as part of any ritual, but as the act of a deranged Unrestricted Elder God. The Elder Gods combined their power to bind him into his own [[icons|Icon]] (**Total Icon Binding**, restricting him to line of sight); the Falling Star then struck and shattered the Icon, and because Kudur-eṭir's entire divine existence was concentrated in the Icon, its destruction extinguished him utterly. He perished with Altea. This was annihilation, not banishment. (sources: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt; Playground generation 2nd part.txt.)

### Tarloth the Cruel — a [[dragons|dragon]]

Tarloth was a dragon, wielding the Spear sometime between Kudur-eṭir's first use (10 mybp) and Darkhoof's much later retrieval. She was the **mate of [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Cindirith, the Magnificent]]** — the eldest of the Nine Embers of Renewal, First God of Dragons, the Platinum, and the most prestigious of the original nine progenitors. After Cindirith's ascension she sought to follow him into divinity, but the legitimate path was closed to her. Her grievance — recorded in Spear-lore as a *draconic complaint about the limits placed on dragonkind by the broader divine order* — was at root the denial of her own ascension despite her mate's success, and at a still deeper level the unbearable specific imbalance of being the unascended consort of the Eldest.

Tarloth was the **first being to discover that the gods had not actually destroyed the Spear**. The Elder Gods' [[#The four hidings|first hiding]] was an illusion of successful destruction layered over the actually-undestroyed artifact; whatever specific perception or knowledge let Tarloth see through the illusion (perhaps a draconic Fire-perception not subject to ordinary visual deception, perhaps a specific Cindirith-related insight) is not fully recorded, but she retrieved the Spear from its illusion-cover and used it to force the issue, becoming an **Unrestricted** Elder God rather than the legitimate Ascended Mortal Cindirith had become. Precisely because she stepped outside the divine compact, the Elder Gods eventually imprisoned her. (sources: Spear of Crystallized Entropy.txt; notes on various topics 1.txt; 2026-05-13 ingest.)

### Darkhoof — a [[unicorns|unicorn]]

A unicorn who retrieved the Spear from the [[feywild|Feywild]] — where it had been held in the gods' second hiding, [[#The four hidings|time-displaced into a state one minute in the future and surrounded by the Feywild's broader temporal anomaly]]. Darkhoof's history is notable; he was also the first to open one of the [[arathi|Arathi Deep Vaults]]. The mechanism by which he reached and seized the time-displaced Spear is not fully recorded — unicorn temporal sensitivity gives them some access to time-anomalous regions, but seizing the Spear *despite* the one-minute displacement was a feat with no replicable method. After his use, his consequences are recorded in [[unicorns|unicorn]] memory but not in mortal scholarly canon. (sources: Playground generation 2nd part.txt; 2026-05-13 ingest.)

### Xar'vion (most recent — 10,000 years ago)

[[xarvion|Xar'vion]] retrieved the Spear from the gods' [[#The four hidings|third hiding]] — the **god-prison in the deepest depths of the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]**, an Ascended Mortal Dragon God [[#The volunteer dragon god|volunteer]] sealed with the Spear inside a **reverse time bubble** that ran subjective time at vast acceleration relative to outside. Xar'vion broke the bubble's seal. The volunteer dragon god — by then subjectively older, more powerful, more experienced, and more isolated than any other being in the Mythos — was killed by Xar'vion in the act of taking the Spear. This act is among the heaviest specific moral injuries on Xar'vion's record: a god who had voluntarily sacrificed themselves to hold the line for the rest of the Mythos was slain by a Netharim-king in pursuit of resurrection magic. The voluntary martyr-god's sacrifice was both wasted and avenged on the wrong actor (Xar'vion, who at that point was acting under the Spear's pull and his own grief, not in pure malice).

Xar'vion used the Spear to become an Unrestricted Elder God. His deepest desire — to resurrect his murdered elder sister [[velena|Velena]] — was stripped away, leaving only ambition and the machinery of domination. His war eventually became the [[demon-war|Demon War]] when he made the catastrophic mistake of summoning a [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]]. (sources: Playground generation 2nd part.txt; More Xarvion.txt; 2026-05-13 ingest.)

## The four hidings

The Spear has been hidden four times — once after each wielder. Each hiding is the gods' best attempt at the time, given everything they have learned from the previous failures. The pattern is **progressively desperate**, not iteratively clever: each attempt represents what the gods reached for once the previous attempt had been broken, and the fourth attempt is a cosmic violation against the body of the world itself.

The hidings are summarized in the table below and described in detail in the subsections that follow. The arrangement preserves the [[feywild|Feywild]] and [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] plane-corruption canon (the Spear's presence in each plane drove its long-term transformation) while reordering the prison sequence per the 2026-05-13 ingest's revised cosmology.

| # | After | Mechanism | Plane / Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | [[kudur-etir\|Kudur-eṭir]] | Failed destruction attempts, covered by **illusions of destruction** | Hidden location, illusion-shrouded |
| **2** | Tarloth | **Time displacement** (held 1 minute in the future) | The [[feywild\|Feywild]] — its long-term corruption from Brightmirror is downstream of the displacement |
| **3** | Darkhoof | **Reverse time bubble** (vastly accelerated subjective time) over a [[#The volunteer dragon god\|volunteer dragon god's]] Total Icon Binding prison | Deepest depths of the [[shadowdark\|Shadowdark]] — its long-term corruption from Darkmirror is downstream of the Spear's presence |
| **4** | Xar'vion | Impalement back into the [[#The fourth hiding\|original First Breach Wound]] | [[arathyn\|Arathyn]] is the scar tissue grown over the impalement |

**Each use of the Spear also slowly corrupts the surrounding area or plane** — the corruption is gradual and cumulative, becoming visible only across long centuries, but every place the Spear sits for an extended time is changed by it. The plane-conversions of Darkmirror → [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] and Brightmirror → [[feywild|Feywild]] are the largest-scale demonstrations of this rule. Both took millions of years; both happened during the second and third hidings of the new sequence.

### Hiding 1 — destruction illusions (after Kudur-eṭir)

After Kudur-eṭir's use destroyed Altea and triggered the Cataclysm, the surviving Elder Gods **tried to destroy the Spear outright**. They tried three escalating methods:

- **Unmaking.** Direct ontological dissolution. Failed — the Spear's alien-marked construction was not subject to Mythos unmaking.
- **Expulsion from the Mythos.** The gods tried to eject the Spear back through whatever conceptual aperture had let its forging-mark in. Failed — the Spear refused to approach exit points, and the gods could not force it across.
- **Raw divine power.** The combined might of nearly all the Elder Gods, brought to bear directly on the artifact. Failed catastrophically: the Spear's alien properties **reflected** the power, and the gods' own combined attack was deflected directly into the original Wound in the Mythos Shell — the same wound Kudur-eṭir had drilled to call down the Worldbreaker.

**The deflected divine attack significantly worsened the First [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusade]].** The First Crusade would have happened in some form regardless (the Wound was already open and chaos was already flowing through it), but the gods' failed destruction attempt **poured their own power into the breach**, widening it and amplifying the invasion. The First Chaos Crusade as actually experienced by Eadras was specifically the version that the gods' own failure had made worse.

Accepting that destruction was not available to them, the gods then committed to a **cover-up**. They constructed an elaborate **illusion of successful destruction** — visible to every observer outside the divine community, sustained over millions of years — under which the actually-not-destroyed Spear was hidden. The illusion held until **Tarloth** saw through it, by means not fully recorded. From the perspective of every mortal observer (and most of the broader divine community) for those millions of years, the Spear had been destroyed and the matter was closed.

### Hiding 2 — time displacement in the Feywild (after Tarloth)

After Tarloth was contained, the gods placed the Spear under a new principle: **don't hide it in space; hide it in time.** They displaced the Spear into a state **one minute in the future** relative to ordinary present time, and located that displaced state within the plane then called **Brightmirror**.

The displacement was the key mechanism. The Spear was permanently slightly inaccessible — any attempt to grasp it always reached the empty space where the Spear *had been* one minute ago; the artifact was always just ahead of any approach, always just out of reach across a sliver of time that no ordinary effort could close. The plane itself served as the spatial anchor; the displacement was the lock.

Over the long centuries the Spear sat in Brightmirror, its presence — and especially its time-displaced presence — leaked into the plane around it. **Brightmirror gradually transformed into the [[feywild|Feywild]]**: a plane whose hallmark is exactly the time-strange, half-out-of-step character that prolonged exposure to the Spear's displacement produced. The Feywild's modern temporal anomalies (years passing in a night, an hour stretching to lifetimes, mortals returning from fae feasts to find the world centuries older) are the residue of the Spear's hiding shaping the plane around it.

**Darkhoof retrieved the Spear from the Feywild.** The exact mechanism is not in the open record. Unicorn temporal sensitivity gives unicorns some access to time-anomalous regions, but seizing a Spear that is permanently one minute ahead of any reach was a feat with no replicable method. Whatever Darkhoof did, it cost him; and the Spear came with him out of the plane.

### Hiding 3 — reverse time bubble over a god prison in the deepest Shadowdark (after Darkhoof)

After Darkhoof was contained, the gods accepted that containment-by-mechanism alone would not hold — the Spear's pull and the alien-artifact principle ensured that no purely impersonal seal could indefinitely resist a sufficiently motivated wielder. They needed a **living watcher** inside the prison to maintain the seal from within.

What they did was **ugly, costly, and theologically unprecedented**:

- **An ancient [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Ascended Mortal Dragon God]] voluntarily stepped forward.** A respected senior member of the divine community offered themselves for the role. *The gods did not demand a sacrifice. The dragon god offered themselves* — and the volunteer aspect is load-bearing. Without it, the act would have been cruelty; with it, it is martyrdom. See [[#The volunteer dragon god|below]].
- **The volunteer was placed into Total Icon Binding** — the same form the gods had used on Kudur-eṭir. The dragon god's entire divine essence was compressed into their Icon, restricting their existence and their power to the icon's footprint.
- **The Spear was sealed inside the Icon, with the volunteer god.** Icon and Spear became a single composite prison-object. The volunteer god maintained the seal from within for as long as the binding endured.
- **The composite prison was placed in the deepest depths of the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]** — the gods' chosen plane for the seal, both because of its depth and isolation and because they were willing to accept the corruption-cost of long-term Spear presence in the plane.
- **The entire prison was wrapped in a reverse time bubble** — a region where time inside ran *vastly faster* than time outside. The bubble was not a slow-down field; it was a runaway acceleration. From outside, ten thousand years passed in the time between Darkhoof's use and Xar'vion's retrieval; **inside the bubble, the volunteer god subjectively endured roughly 10^37 years** — a span so vast that the comparison to mortal duration loses all meaning.

Over the very long span the Spear sat in the Shadowdark, its presence corrupted the plane around it — **Darkmirror gradually transformed into the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]**. The plane's modern character (the cold, the lightlessness, the predatory wrongness of its deeper layers) is the residue of the Spear's hiding shaping the plane around it, the same way Brightmirror became the Feywild during the second hiding.

#### The volunteer dragon god

The volunteer was a senior **[[nine-embers-of-renewal|Ascended Mortal Dragon God]]** of the era — a respected elder of the dragon-god community. Their specific name, clan, and age are open canon; whoever they were, their identity is preserved in [[dragons|dragon]] esoteric tradition and in the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran's]] sealed chapters but is not in the open record.

What the reverse time bubble did to them, subjectively, is the heart of the third hiding's mythic weight. The volunteer entered the prison **knowing the cost**. They expected to maintain the seal from within for as long as the binding endured. They did not expect to enter what amounted to an **eternity of solitude**.

Inside the bubble, they:

- **Aged enormously.** A dragon god is long-lived even by divine standards; 10^37 subjective years places the volunteer's accumulated experience and power somewhere beyond any frame of reference the rest of the Mythos can supply. By the time Xar'vion broke the seal, the volunteer had subjectively existed for vastly longer than the Mythos itself.
- **Grew more powerful.** Compressed into the Icon with the Spear at their core, they had eternity to refine their relationship with the artifact, their understanding of the divine essence they had compressed into the seal, and the structure of the prison itself. By the end, they were the most experienced living seal-holder in the history of the world.
- **Grew more isolated.** No outside contact ever reached them. The gods could not check on them without risking the seal. They held the line alone, for what was, to them, longer than the Mythos itself had existed.
- **Likely fractured.** Ten-to-the-thirty-seventh years of unbroken solitude with only the Spear's low-grade sentient presence for company is beyond what any mind — including a divine mind — is built to endure. Working canon: the volunteer suffers from a form of **divine schizophrenia**, the late-stage state of a consciousness that has spent so long alone with itself that it has split, multiplied, forgotten, rebuilt, and renamed its own identity many times over. By the time Xar'vion arrived, the being inside the prison was no longer cleanly the dragon god who had volunteered. Multiple internal personas existed in tension; elaborate inner cosmologies had been constructed, forgotten, and reconstructed; the volunteer's relationship to the Spear had become its own private theology. (Working canon, 2026-05-13 ingest, time-mechanic clarification.)

When Xar'vion broke the bubble's seal and killed the volunteer to take the Spear, what he killed was a being of vast, alien, partially-shattered power. What he absorbed or learned in the act is open canon; what he carried out of the Shadowdark — a Spear that had been the singular companion of a 10^37-year solitude — was a substantially more *attuned* artifact than the one Darkhoof had taken from the Feywild.

The dragon god's death is **a major moral injury** to the gods. A volunteer-martyr who had given themselves to hold the line for the rest of the Mythos was killed in the act of doing so, by a mortal wielder pursuing resurrection magic, who afterward triggered the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. The gods experienced this as bitter, humiliating, and a refutation of even **willing god-sacrifice** as a containment strategy.

### Hiding 4 — shove it back down the hole (after Xar'vion)

After Xar'vion's [[demon-war|Demon War]] was contained through Xar'vion's own Total Icon Binding into the [[xarvion|Motionless King]], the gods inherited the Spear back for the **fourth** containment attempt. They were out of options.

What they did was an act of **divine panic and fury** — not careful planning, not iterative containment design, but a desperate decision to commit a cosmic violation in order to constrain something they had now failed to contain three times.

**They took the Spear and rammed it back into the original Wound** — Kudur-eṭir's First Breach, the same hole in reality where everything had started, the hole through which the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] had been pulled into Eadras. They impaled the Spear into the scar tissue of the world's most fundamental wound, using the lingering damage from the Worldbreaker impact as the prison itself.

The act has held. Ten thousand years on, the Spear has not escaped. It is still where they put it.

The act was also catastrophic in every other sense:

- **Global volcanic upheaval.** The impalement destabilized the planet's crust at the impact point and over a wide surrounding region. Massive volcanism erupted along what had been quiescent fault networks.
- **Arathyn was forced into its modern shape.** The pre-existing [[arathyn|Arathyn]] Large Igneous Province had been quietly building since the Cataclysm; the impalement reactivated and accelerated its growth in a single catastrophic phase. The modern continent of [[arathyn|Arathyn]] is **scar tissue grown over the gods' desperate fourth-attempt containment**. The continent's recent geology, persistent volcanism, and characteristic magical disturbance all reflect the active, ongoing nature of the impalement-prison. *Arathyn's existence is directly caused by the fourth-attempt impalement.* (See [[arathyn]].)
- **The impalement is permanent and ongoing.** The Spear is still impaled into the Wound. The [[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]] is still where the impalement happened. Arathyn is still growing — slowly, over geological time — as the scar continues to heal over the impalement.

This act differs from the previous three in a structural way. The first three hidings tried to contain the Spear in *something else* — an illusion of destruction, a time-displaced plane, a reverse-time-bubble god-prison. The fourth attempt **incorporated the Spear into the body of the world itself**. The Spear is no longer in a box; it is in the *fabric*.

#### Where the Spear is now, and who knows

The Spear is currently impaled into the original Wound, beneath the modern continent of [[arathyn|Arathyn]], at the conceptual center of the [[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]]. **No mortal has reached the location and returned.**

Public knowledge of the Spear's location is essentially nothing. The Tome of Baldaran's sealed chapters know. The deepest [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] theology can deduce it. The [[unicorns|unicorn]] voice gestures at it (*"the scar remains; some wounds do not close — they only wait"*). The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] has not yet succeeded in determining the location; the [[lost-zenith|Lost Zenith]] failed at the previous Grand Conjunction. The general public, and even most scholars, do not know. Sailor hearsay and Arathyn-traveler stories carry breadcrumbs — strange dreams in deep-interior Arathyn, reality fraying inward toward the continent's core, magical instruments behaving wrongly — but none is corroborated, and none appears in the open Tome chapters.

#### Reaching the Spear

The obstacles to reaching the Spear at its current impalement site are **not clever defenses the gods built**. They are the **consequences of the gods' desperation**. As any approach to the Worldbreaker Scar progresses inward toward the Spear's actual position:

- **Reality itself begins to come apart.** Causality frays. Time moves unevenly. Memory degrades. Identity slips. The closer one gets to the artifact, the more wrong every dimension of normal experience becomes.
- **The land is the guardian.** No constructed defense is needed; the continent of [[arathyn|Arathyn]] itself, the scar tissue of the world's wound, *actively rejects the existence of intruders the closer they come to the center.*
- **Any guardian-being encountered along the way is a casualty by accident, not a guardian by design.** Beings caught in the fraying region around the Spear persist there in distorted form — not as guardians, but as further evidence of the wrongness.

The land is dying inward. Any party approaching the Spear is crawling into a mutilation. That is the experience.

## Significance

The Spear is the single greatest engine of catastrophe in Eadras history. Each of its uses has reshaped the world:
- First use: [[arathi-cataclysm|destroyed the Arathi civilization and the planetary surface]]
- Tarloth's use: forced an Unrestricted Elder God ascension she had been denied; imprisoned
- Darkhoof's use: opened the first [[arathi|Arathi Deep Vault]]; further consequences preserved in [[unicorns|unicorn]] memory
- Xar'vion's use: triggered the [[demon-war|Demon War]], killed half of all living creatures

Each of the gods' four containment attempts has had its own world-visible cost:

- The first attempt's failed destruction worsened the [[chaos-crusades|First Chaos Crusade]] by amplifying the breach with deflected divine power.
- The second attempt's time-displacement seeded the long corruption of Brightmirror into the [[feywild|Feywild]].
- The third attempt cost the volunteer's life and seeded the long corruption of Darkmirror into the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] — and produced one of the most painful god-killings in Eadran cosmology when Xar'vion broke the seal.
- The fourth attempt incorporated the Spear into the body of the world itself, triggering the volcanism that forced [[arathyn|Arathyn]] into its modern shape; the continent is the visible scar over the impalement.

The loss of three **[[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]** during the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] contributed to the **[[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]** of the magical Weave.

The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] plans to give Xar'vion the Spear during the next [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]], enabling a second ascension — the **Second Crown Doctrine**. Since Xar'vion is Icon-bound, the Spear must enter his line of sight or be placed in his Icon's grasp for this to work. The Black Hand's present search for the Spear's location is constrained by what is publicly available; the alien-artifact principle means even if they reach [[arathyn|Arathyn]] and follow the right cosmological intuitions to the impalement site, the *extraction* problem may be as severe as the *location* problem — the Spear is woven into the fabric of the world, not stored in a chest. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt; 2026-05-13 ingest.)

## Open canon

- **The volunteer dragon god's identity** — name, clan, age, and place in the dragon-god tradition. Preserved in dragon esoteric tradition and the Tome's sealed chapters; not in the open record.
- **How Tarloth saw through the destruction illusion.** Draconic Fire-perception not subject to ordinary visual deception? A specific Cindirith-related insight? Some other knowledge unique to her?
- **How Darkhoof seized a time-displaced Spear.** Unicorn temporal sensitivity is the leading hypothesis; the exact mechanism is open.
- **The state of the volunteer god's mind by the end.** "Divine schizophrenia" is the working frame; the specific shape of the fragmentation, the identities the god split into, and whether any of those identities survived the killing is open canon. What did Xar'vion absorb in the act of killing them, and is any of it still in his Icon?
- **Whether Tower of Stars retains any role.** The pre-revision canon placed the Velrathi arch-wizard's Tower of Stars as the third Spear-prison; the new sequence retires it from prison status. Whether the Tower of Stars exists as a Velrathi arch-wizard's stronghold for other reasons is open canon.
