## When the era begins

The First Rebrightening is **not a single year**. It is a transitional period spanning several mortal decades, recognized when major magical institutions agree that the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]] is no longer the dominant magical condition â€” though the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] remains scarred and uneven.

The naming is retrospective and deliberately modest: *Rebrightening*, not *Restoration*. The Dimming is not over. Some regions remain Eldritch-dim. Some are dangerously overbright. Some are chaotic-flow zones where spells fail one day and magnify the next. What has changed is that the *trajectory* has reversed â€” the magical infrastructure is recovering rather than continuing to decay.

The era is called **First** because Eadran scholarship anticipates that other Rebrightenings may follow other Dimmings â€” the present recovery is one occurrence of a pattern that may recur over geological time.

## Characteristic features

The Rebrightening is shaped by the simultaneous pressure of several rebuilding currents reaching critical thresholds at roughly the same time:

- **The Weave/Ward is recovering unevenly** â€” defensive function is partially restored at most conduit junctions; the [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]] still bear scars where [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] were killed during the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]].
- **Eldritch flows are stabilising in some regions and not others** â€” the patchwork geography of the Dimming persists, and some regions experience real magical recovery while others worsen.
- **Magic is becoming industrialised again** â€” [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]], [[magitek|magitek]], conduit-tapping, and large-scale spellwork are returning to civilian commerce after long suppression.
- **[[bold|Bold]] and other [[conduit-cities|conduit-cities]] are rising politically** â€” their proximity to working conduits gives them magical and economic advantages no Dimming-era state could exploit.
- **Deep Vault archaeology is organised and politically competitive** â€” kingdoms, academies, and private syndicates race for [[arathi-geo-substrate|Arathi geo-substrate]] sites under the new [[vault-concordat|Vault Concordat]] negotiations.
- **Older divine doctrines are questioned** â€” the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] faces the [[doctrine-of-open-palm|Open Palm]] reform; institutional theology is in motion.
- **Prayer volume and miracle disputes increase** â€” the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]] is visibly straining; routing failures generate scandals.
- **Mortals become powerful enough to repeat Arathi-pattern mistakes** â€” the [[fallback-doctrine|Fallback Doctrine]] is articulated specifically in response.
- **Dragons and unicorns become relevant to ancient-history questions again** â€” their long memory matters now in ways it did not when Eadras was just trying to survive.
- **The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] uses renewed complexity as cover** â€” the institutional bandwidth absorbed by Rebrightening reforms creates exactly the operational shadows the Hand exploits.

## FR dating

The notation **FR** (First Rebrightening) marks years counted from the formal recognition of the era's beginning. Scholarly chronicles, sealed Tome chapters, and Order Sharanel records use FR dates. Common mortal speech does not: a farmer in a frontier village would say "in my grandfather's time" or "before the Bold Treaty," not "in 12 FR."

Two dating systems coexist within the formal register:

- **FR** â€” generic Rebrightening dating used by most scholars.
- **ORP** â€” *Open Palm Reckoning*, used in [[order-sharanel|Lirran]] reformist material; dates measured from the Open Palm Council itself. The systems are convertible (ORP 0 = 6 FR per the provisional chronology).

Both notations remain **semi-formal**. Outside of scholarly and clerical writing, mortals continue to use regional, dynastic, or event-anchored dating.

## What the Rebrightening is *not*

- **Not the end of the Dimming.** Patchwork magical geography persists; some regions may never fully recover.
- **Not a divine triumph.** The gods debate, reform, and admit failure across the era; the Rebrightening is not announced from heaven.
- **Not a guaranteed forward march.** A Second Falling remains possible (see the RPG-era splat material at [[eras#The Second Falling alternate dark future|the alternate-future framing]]); the recovery is real but contingent.
- **Not over.** The era is ongoing in the canonical present. Modern characters, events, and institutions exist *within* the Rebrightening, not after it.
