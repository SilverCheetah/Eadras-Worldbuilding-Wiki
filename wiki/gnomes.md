# Gnomes

**Summary**: Inventive, practical descendants of the [[grugi|Grugi]] â€” the [[velrathi|Velrathi]]-engineered maintainers and inventors. ## Origin

Gnomes are a "natural devolution" of the [[grugi|Grugi]] â€” the Velrathi-engineered race created to maintain imperial technological systems: small, smart, mechanically adept, clever, and sneaky (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

Gnomes descend **purely from Grugi stock**. The earlier-canon claim that gnomes carried partial [[mitans|Mitan]] ancestry alongside their Grugi heritage was retracted on 2026-05-10 (source: Notes for Changes for 5-10-26.txt). Gnomes are no longer "the only modern race with dual-progenitor lineage." Their craft tradition and resilience are now understood as developments along the Grugi line itself, not Mitan-derived.

The Grugi suffered catastrophic losses in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] but enough survived to diverge into goblins and gnomes, "preserving something of their clever spark" (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## The vault obsession â€” inherited cultural mission

The gnomish racial focus on locating, opening, and reverse-engineering [[deep-vaults|Arathi Deep Vaults]] is not generic inventor-curiosity. It is **inherited cultural mission**, traceable to a specific historical event their progenitor-line attended.

In the early [[firethorn-empire|High Firethorn Era]], a joint [[velrathi|Velrathi]]-[[rathari|Rathari]] expedition opened a Deep Vault and recovered a foundational Arathi instructional text: [[lost-primer|the Lost Primer]] on [[crystalmancy]] and [[magitek]]. Grug attendants on that expedition kept meticulous notes throughout the work. Those notes were preserved within Grug culture and passed forward to both child races â€” the gnomes and the [[goblins]] â€” when the Grug were shattered by the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. The vault itself was overrun and emptied by demons in the war's first wave; the Velrathi and Rathari lost the original to demonic consumption.

The Velrathi and Rathari **never thought to ask** the Grug whether they had taken notes. The Grug, and the gnomes after them, kept the record anyway.

The gnomish vault-obsession is therefore driven by two coupled forces:

- **Ancestral trauma** â€” the cultural memory of being present at the moment when the keys to a lost civilization were held and slipped away.
- **A near-religious desire to restore what was lost** â€” every gnomish trade (golem-craft, Arathi-derived cuisine, post-vault naming style, the catalog-crystal logistics empire) is at root an attempt to put pieces of the Arathi back into the world.

Each new vault opened is another shot at finishing the job their forebears watched fail. See [[lost-primer]] for the full event.

## Distinction from Goblins

Both gnomes and goblins descend from the Grug, but the two diverged sharply in temperament and culture after the Demon War:

- **Gnomes** kept the inventive, constructive side of the Grug â€” invention, fine craft, mechanical and alchemical experimentation.
- **[[goblins|Goblins]]** kept the scavenging, opportunistic, sneaky side.

Both are described as preserving "the clever spark" in different forms (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Anatomy and Society

Gnomes are small, inventive, and practical. They are present in modern settlements as both standalone communities and as integrated minorities within larger cities, particularly where workshops, tinkering, and mechanical crafts thrive.

Specific clan structures, settlement patterns, and notable gnomish institutions remain to be defined.

## Magical Inheritance

Like all Grugi descendants, gnomes carry an aptitude for working with magitech â€” the legacy of their progenitors maintaining Velrathi imperial systems. This sometimes manifests as a knack for enchanted items, clockwork, or alchemy. The craftsman's eye gnomes show in their bench-and-anvil work is a Grugi inheritance, refined along the gnomish line into their distinctive inventor's eye for finished mechanism.

Notably, the [[runeswords|Runeswords]] of [[dao|Dao]] have been wielded by **the occasional daring gnome** â€” a remarkable distinction given the blades' Tarran-royal origin (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## The Shada'din factory partnership

Beyond golem-craft, the most economically consequential standing alliance in modern Eadras is the **gnome-Shada'din factory partnership**: **gnomes invent the systems, [[dwarves|Shada'din]] craft and operate the actual factories.** Gnomish-invented, Shada'din-built [[magitek|magitek]] factories can **produce almost anything in bulk** â€” tools, armor, structural components, magitek devices, household goods. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

The arrangement plays to each people's strengths. The gnomish bench-and-anvil instinct (the inherited Grug aptitude) supplies the engineering. The Shada'din genius for **disciplined large-scale fabrication** â€” combined with their Work-Mode focus, their command of structural and stabilization magic, and their unmatched mining throughput â€” supplies the production. A gnome workshop produces the prototype; a Shada'din hold produces ten thousand. Many of the magitek lights, steam-driven hammers, and standard-issue magitek devices in modern Eadras are products of this partnership.

The partnership's **goblin-tested prototype loop** (see [[goblins#Invention and craft|Goblins â€” Invention and craft]]) supplies a third leg of the same economy: goblins source rare materials and abuse-test dangerous prototypes; gnomes refine; Shada'din mass-produce.

### Sales and distribution â€” gnomes don't travel for sales

Gnomes are **inventors and crafters, not salesmen**, and their cultural preference is to stay near the bench. The standing solution is to **hire other races as salespeople** â€” typically members of more naturally social and travel-friendly peoples ([[halflings|halflings]], gregarious humans, [[beast-folk|Beast-folk]], city [[orcs|orcs]] for caravan-route work, and the broader [[goblins|goblin]] network for street-level distribution). The gnomish workshop trains the salesperson on the goods; the salesperson does the talking and the road. (source: notes on various topics 1.txt)

Gnome-hired sales teams travel with two signature pieces of magitek:

- **Catalog crystals** â€” palm-sized magitek devices that, when activated, **project full-color holographic images** of every product in the workshop's catalogue. A customer can rotate, scale, and inspect a hologram of an item as if it were physically present. Catalog crystals are a Shada'din-built, gnome-designed product of the factory partnership and are themselves among the partnership's most-distributed items.
- **Teleportation boxes built into carts** â€” paired magitek shipping boxes, one in the cart and one at the dwarven factory. Orders and payment placed into the cart-box appear at the factory; finished goods placed into the factory-box appear in the cart almost instantly. The salesperson can walk a customer from "I'd like one of these" to "here it is" in minutes. The teleportation boxes are calibrated to specific factory pairs and are among the most logistically consequential magitek items in modern commerce.

The combined system means a single hired salesperson with a small cart can offer the entire bulk catalog of a major Shada'din hold to any village they reach. It is also the chief reason gnome-Shada'din goods are available essentially everywhere across Eadras' civilized regions: the salespeople go where the gnomes will not.

The relationship between gnomish workshops and their hired sales teams is generally a long-term partnership â€” many salespeople work the same workshop for decades, build personal customer relationships across the routes, and pass the position to apprentices when they retire. The salespeople are not employees in any servile sense; they are independent contractors with substantial autonomy over their territory.

## Golem-craft: the gnome inheritance of Arathi minds

The defining gnome trade in modern Eadras is **golem-craft**. Modern [[golems|golems]] and **Warforged** are gnome creations: the gnomes salvage **crystal minds from [[arathi|Arathi]] ruins** and build new bodies around them, restoring sapience that has waited since before the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] (source: user clarification 2026-04-30).

This is the most direct expression of the Grug â†’ gnome inheritance. The Grug were created to maintain Velrathi imperial systems; the gnomes carry that maintainer-and-tinkerer instinct outward in time, applying it to the **oldest** magitech of all â€” the work of the Arathi civilization that pre-dated even the Velrathi. Every modern golem is in some sense a gnome workshop's collaboration with a long-dead Arathi engineer, mediated by a salvaged crystalline mind.

Because the supply of intact Arathi crystal minds is finite, gnome golem-craft is an inherently **archival** trade: there is no canonical method of creating new minds, only of locating, restoring, and rehousing the survivors. Master gnome golem-restorers are correspondingly rare, well-respected, and often work closely with whatever mind they bring back â€” a relationship that ranges from collegial to filial to permanently intertwined.

## Cuisine

Gnomish food culture is **a gift of the [[arathi|Arathi]] vaults.** When [[inki|Inki]] (then a mortal craftsman, later the Ascended Mortal God of invention) became the first gnome ever to breach an Arathi vault, the prize the gnomes carried out was not a weapon but **a food fabricator** â€” a magitek device that, in low-power survival mode, dispensed **seeds**. It took decades of painstaking script-translation before the gnomes understood what they had. Once they did, generations of careful cultivation transformed gnomish cooking from basic medieval fare into a richly spiced, complex tradition that fuses ancient Arathi flavors with traditional gnomish technique. (source: Gnomish Food Culture.txt)

The original Arathi cuisine could never be fully replicated â€” it relied on the meat of giant insects that no longer exist â€” but every modern gnomish kitchen still works with crops, herbs, and spices descended from that single vault's seed stock.

### Raven's eye

The signature ingredient of gnomish cuisine is **raven's eye** â€” a small, glossy black pepper about the size of a fingernail. Inki himself was the first gnome to taste one (the Arathi cookbook described it only as *pleasantly warm*); it nearly blew his head off. To this day, tasting a raw raven's eye is the **traditional first test** for any young gnome who wants to be acknowledged as a master chef. Failing the test is not the inability to react â€” everyone reacts â€” but losing composure and disgracing the kitchen.

### Brewing a hot batch â€” the gnomish prank

A long-standing gnomish prank on dwarven brewers has run for centuries. Every few years a party of bold young gnomes will sneak into a dwarven brewery and quietly add **cinnamon, cardamom, star anise, and a paper-thin sliver of dried raven's eye** to a sealed fermenting vat. To a gnome, that is simply how beer is supposed to taste; to a dwarf, it is an outrage. Dwarves call the result *"gnome-tainted swill"*; gnomes call it *"improvement."* See [[food-and-drink]] for the full feud, including the dwarven retort with **Palebone Clay**. (source: Gnomish Prank v Dwarven Retort.txt)

## Relations

- **[[goblins|Goblins]]** are sibling-kin through shared Grug ancestry. The relationship is complicated: goblins and gnomes share fond and fraught memories of being created together. The view across the kinship is **mixed and unsentimental**: some gnomes treat goblins as **kin** and act on it; many others treat them primarily as **useful tools for dirty work** â€” the cousins who can find the rare part, abuse-test the dangerous prototype, and do the necessary work no respectable workshop wants to be seen doing. Goblins notice. The collaboration runs anyway because both sides need what the other provides, and the goblin network's cross-city reach makes it indispensable to gnome workshop supply chains. (source: Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt) See [[goblins#Hoarding and the goblin network|Goblins â€” Hoarding and the goblin network]] for the network reciprocity rule that quietly disciplines civilizations that mistreat goblins.
- **[[dwarves|Dwarves]]** and **[[halflings|halflings]]** are kin through the Mitan line. Gnomes occupy a peculiar middle ground between deep-stone dwarven craft and surface-loving halfling sociability. The brewery prank-and-retort feud (see above and [[food-and-drink]]) is one of the most visible recurring expressions of gnome-dwarf cousinship.
- **Velrathi descendants** (modern velrathi diaspora, where it exists) regard gnomes with a quiet, complicated pride: the Grug were Rand's hands, and the gnome's bench-and-anvil instinct is the last living remnant of that work.
