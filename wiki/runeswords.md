## Overview

[[dao]] created nine Runeswords outside of any Mythos, using **Starmetal** for the blades and **[[worldender-crystal|alien Worldender crystal]]** for the living hearts â€” both extracted from the [[arathi-cataclysm|Worldbreaker]] falling star. Two more were later forged by [[the-three|Melania]] for the [[tarans|Taran]] royal family using the same materials. All eleven are sentient blades with the willing-bound soul of an ancient warrior race from a Mythos that no longer exists â€” **extramythic warriors**, like [[the-three|the Three]] themselves.

In the Tome's philosophical framing, the Runeswords are **levers around which reality can be bent**: tools for restoring balance, **ontological force multipliers** that operate through structural leverage, referential precision, and lawful manipulation of coherent reality. They are not magical because they store magic; they are magical because their construction is closer to True Language than ordinary mortal artifice can reach. Each blade is therefore best understood as a *frozen referential working* anchored to material that itself resists dissolution. (See [[true-language|True Language and Referential Language]].)

Three are of Neutrality, three of Good, three of Evil, plus two Taran-specific swords (Brightthorn and Darkthorn).

**The eleven blades rest in the [[hall-of-kings|Hall of Kings]]** when not being wielded â€” an extradimensional sanctum [[dao|Dao]] forged at the same time as the swords themselves, with **Deathbringer alone at the apex** of a multi-tier dais and the other ten arranged on descending tiers by alignment.

## Forging â€” and why they have always existed

The nine original Runeswords were forged by Dao at approximately **50,000 BP** â€” soon after he rescued [[the-three|Mark, Rand, and Melania]] from the Witchfinder General Trials, with the children present as witnesses.

The two Taran swords (**Brightthorn** and **Darkthorn**) were forged by **Melania at approximately 39,000 BP, simultaneously with the creation of the [[tarans|Tarran]] race itself and the [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]]**. Melania crafted race, throne, and royal blades as one act of making. The two Tarran Runeswords therefore predate the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]'s founding by approximately 21,000 years â€” the Empire merely formalized a sovereignty whose emblems already existed. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

There is no contradiction in the simultaneous claim that the Runeswords have always existed in this Mythos. **[[dao|Dao]] is an avatar of the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]] â€” the very being who authored the rules of causality and time the Mythos runs on â€” and he can simply ignore those rules when he chooses.** When Dao forged the Runeswords at ~50,000 BP, he chose to insert them into Mythos history as eternally present. From any internal Eadras vantage they have always been here; from the external timeline they were forged at ~50,000 BP. Both are simultaneously true because Dao is sovereign over the rule that would otherwise force a contradiction. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

This is also the deepest reason **Deathbringer** can stand above the natural laws of space and time: the forging itself was unbound by those laws, and Deathbringer is the most direct heir to that out-of-time vantage.

## Sword Laws

All Runeswords are bound by the Runes of Power and the [[omniversal-laws|Omniversal Laws]]:

1. A sword cannot lie to its wielder, but there are things forbidden to reveal.
2. A sword cannot cause its wielder to lose their life through action or inaction, nor cause the wielder harm.
3. A sword may not reveal the location of another sword â€” except one that is diametrically opposed to it.
4. Swords cannot act on their own except in moments of extreme danger to their wielder.

All Runeswords (except **Deathbringer**) are only conscious when being actively wielded. When returned to their stone, they sleep, and memories of their previous master are purged â€” except the swords of Neutrality, which retain memory.

## Two Materials From the Worldender

The Runeswords are **hybrid weapons**, drawn from [[dao|Dao's]] harvest of the [[worldender|Worldender's]] remains. The blades themselves are **Starmetal**; the **living hearts, soul-vessels, pommel stones, harmonic focusing crystals, and rune-lenses** are made from **[[worldender-crystal|alien crystal]]** â€” the same material [[the-three|Melania]] later used for the [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

The crystal components serve several critical functions:
- **Soul-vessels** that bind the ancient warrior spirits within each sword
- Allow the blades to **partially resist or even counter [[omniversal-laws|Dao's own Omniversal Laws]]** under extreme conditions (the reason **Deathbringer** stands above time and space)
- Create the **same sovereignty-resonance** that once shielded the Firethorn Emperor â€” allowing each Runesword **limited autonomy and protection from direct divine meddling** when wielded by a worthy bearer
- During rare celestial alignments, the crystal veins can faintly *"remember"* their origin and **flare with Worldender-like fire**

This is why the Runeswords feel alive. The alien crystal is not native to the Mythos; it carries an echo of something **older, colder, and more absolute**.

## Starmetal (the blades)

All eleven swords' **blades** are forged from **Starmetal** â€” a material [[dao]] personally extracted from the remains of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Worldbreaker]]. Only he, as an avatar of the [[omniversal-creator]], could harvest, stabilize, and make it compatible with the [[eadras-cosmology|Mythos]].

Starmetal is foreign to Eadras. It is vastly harder and more resistant than any native metal, highly resistant to magic, and required god-level heat (Dao intensified volcanic fire tenfold) to forge.

Three types correspond to alignment:
- **White Starmetal** â€” Swords of Good; gleams with clean white luster.
- **Silver Starmetal** â€” Swords of Neutrality; highly reflective, almost mirror-like.
- **Black Starmetal** â€” Swords of Evil; deep, absolute black that swallows light.
