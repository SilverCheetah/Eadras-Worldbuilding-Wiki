# Elfen Blossom Wines

**Summary**: The signature alcoholic drink of the [[elfen|Elfen]] is **blossom wine** — a family of fermented wines made from a careful balance of berries, edible flowers, and herbs. Each Elfen clan keeps its own closely-guarded recipe, resulting in distinctly aromatic and flavored vintages. The wines are aged in **sung living-wood barrels** rather than glass bottles, and are gently de-alcoholized by magic for children to enjoy at celebrations.

## A family of wines, not a single wine

There is no single canonical "Elfen blossom wine." Every Elfen clan makes some variation of the basic concept (fermented berries + edible flowers + herbs), but each clan's recipe is its own. Different combinations of fruit, flower, and herb give each clan's vintage a unique aromatic profile and flavor character — some bright and crisp, some deep and aromatic, some sharply herbal, some softly floral.

A bottle (or rather a tapped barrel-section — see below) of one clan's vintage is therefore a **specific cultural artifact**, not a generic product. Offering a guest blossom wine from another clan's stock is a layered gesture: it is hospitality, it is a subtle status display, and it is a small act of acknowledgement of the producing clan's specific tradition.

## Quality and intoxication

Blossom wines are **light and only mildly intoxicating** by Elfen standards. Even substantial consumption rarely produces the heavy drunkenness that other races' stronger drinks induce. This is canonical to the wines themselves, not a comment on Elfen tolerance.

## Children and the de-alcoholization charm

For their children, the Elfen use **subtle magic to gently remove the alcohol content while preserving the flavor**, allowing even the very young to enjoy the traditional family wines during celebrations. The charm is part of standard Elfen domestic magic and is performed by any competent Elfen adult; it does not require a specialist.

## Sung living-wood barrels

The Elfen do not use glass bottles. Their entire vessel tradition is built around **sung living wood** — the broader Elfen practice of shaping wood through song, in which an Elfen sings to a tree (or to a separated piece of wood) and the wood follows the singer's intent.

For wine vessels, this produces a distinctive aging-and-storage system:

- A vessel can be **grown directly out of a living tree** and left attached for the entirety of the aging period. The wine ages within the tree itself, drawing on the slow exchange between the wine and the surrounding wood, which is itself alive and slowly responsive to the song the maker shaped it with.
- A vessel can be **separated from the tree once formed**, becoming a portable barrel. Even after separation it remains the result of a sung shaping; the wood retains a subtle song-character that affects the wine's maturation.

Both approaches are used. Major estate cellars are often complexes of carefully-sung trees, each tree carrying multiple vessels in its trunk and crown, with the wine harvested by tapping rather than by uprooting anything.

The tradition of sung wood is not unique to wine — Elfen also build houses, furniture, and most other objects of significance from sung wood. Wine vessels are simply the instance most relevant to this page. See [[elfen-architecture]] for the broader practice.

## Clan diversity in detail

The wines vary across clans both in ingredient selection and in herbal character. Every clan's vintage carries its own herbal signature drawn from the herbs that grow well in the clan's territory. Some clans are known for particularly prized variations.

Tradition holds that approximately ~25 named clan vintages have circulated widely enough beyond their producing clans to be referred to by drinkers from other peoples. Most blossom-wine production, however, never leaves the producing clan's home territory and is consumed in-house.

### Named clan vintages

The following clan vintages have circulated widely enough to be referred to by drinkers from other peoples; they are the standard entries in any modern catalogue of Elfen blossom-wines:

- **Starblossom Wine** — bright, floral, with a long aromatic finish
- **Lunapetal Wine** — softly floral, characteristically mellow
- **Aetherbloom** — high-magic variant; mana-rich ingredients give it a subtle magical resonance and a distinctive shimmer when poured under low light
- **Dawnpetal Reserve** — a long-aged variant; characteristically deep and herbal
- **Celestine Gold** — pale-gold, dry, prized for celebratory toasts

These five are the canonical core; the remaining ~20 widely-named vintages are documented across various clan records but have not been consolidated into a single catalogue at this wiki yet (open canon: the full ~25-name list).

## The high-magic variant

Some Elfen clans whose territories sit on or near high-magic zones produce a magically charged variant by including **mana-rich ingredients** — berries grown on ley lines, flowers from groves where Eldritch concentrations are unusually dense. The resulting wine carries a noticeable magical resonance and is correspondingly rarer and more valuable. These vintages are also the source ingredients for the most prized batches of [[aqua-vitae|Elven Aqua Vitae]].

## Distillation and Elven [[aqua-vitae|Aqua Vitae]]

The Elven contribution to alchemical foundation is [[aqua-vitae|Elven Aqua Vitae]] — the universal alchemical solvent distilled directly from blossom wines. The Aqua Vitae retains the source wine's light, bubbly, floral-herbal fingerprint, which is why it is preferred in alchemy for potions involving **nature, growth, healing, and life magic**. See [[aqua-vitae]] for the broader treatment.

## Open canon

- A definitive list of named clan blossom-wine vintages.
- Whether the **modern post-Banishment elves** ([[elves]]) still make blossom wines in roughly the same way, in a degraded form, or have diverged entirely. The implication is that the tradition survived in some form, but the modern recipe collection is not catalogued.
- Whether the **sung living-wood barrels** are exportable as artifacts (i.e., can a non-Elfen acquire one and use it for their own wine), or whether they only function within the song-relationship that grew them.
