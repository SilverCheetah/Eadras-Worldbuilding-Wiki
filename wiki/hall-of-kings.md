# Hall of Kings

**Summary**: The Hall of Kings is the extradimensional sanctum [[dao]] forged from leftover [[worldender-crystal|Worldender crystal]] alongside the [[runeswords|Runeswords]] themselves â€” a vast circular chamber housing all eleven blades on a multi-tier dais (with **Deathbringer alone at the apex**), surrounded by treasures that serve as both dragon-tempting trap for unworthy claimants and **the private royal treasury of the legitimate [[tarans|Tarran]] monarch**. The Door normally manifests in the Tarran Royal Citadel of [[firethorn-hold|Firethorn Hold]] but can appear before any worthy Swordwielder. The Hall also **secretly holds the [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]]** â€” fully cloaked and non-detectable until a worthy claimant appears â€” accessed by entering the Hall through the original royal palace on Eadras (not Gaia).

## What It Is

The Hall of Kings is an **extradimensional sanctum** â€” a private pocket folded outside normal space and time, anchored to the [[gaia|Prime-like reflection of Gaia]] yet reachable from the Prime under the right conditions.

It is not a Tarran creation, contrary to some scholarly assumption. It is **[[dao|Dao's]] own work**, forged in the same cataclysmic breath as the [[runeswords|eleven Runeswords]] (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

> In one continuous act of creation, [[dao|Dao]] folded the extradimensional Hall into existence â€” a private vault and testing ground anchored to the future exile of the Tarran people. The Hall was never an afterthought. It was the **twin to the blades themselves**: weapon and treasury, test and inheritance, forged together so that power and responsibility would remain bound.

## Architecture â€” The Multilevel Dais

At the center of the vast circular chamber rises a **multilevel dais** â€” a stepped ziggurat of living [[worldender-crystal|alien crystal]]:

- **Apex (highest pedestal, alone)**: **[[runeswords|Deathbringer]]**, the Hand of Death â€” the first and greatest blade, standing in solitary splendor, its black crystal core glowing like a dying star
- **Descending tiers, in proper order and alignment**:
  - Three blades of **Neutrality**
  - Three blades of **Good**
  - Three blades of **Evil**
  - The two Tarran blades: **Brightthorn** and **Darkthorn**, on the lowest visible level

Each sword rests in its own crystal pedestal when not being wielded. The entire structure **floats slightly above the floor, rotating imperceptibly** so that every blade is always visible from any angle. The air is cold, still, and heavy with the weight of oaths.

Deathbringer's solitary position at the apex reflects its unique status above the natural laws. The descending tiers mirror the moral alignments and relative power of the blades.

## The Firethorn Throne â€” secretly held

The lost **[[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]]** â€” vanished from its dais after the [[great-betrayal|Tarran Emperor's poisoning]] â€” is in the Hall of Kings, **fully cloaked and non-detectable** until a worthy claimant appears. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

The cloaking is structural, not symbolic. The Throne is not on the public dais among the Runeswords; it is in a separate chamber within the Hall that is invisible to ordinary perception, divine sight, scrying, and Tome inquiry alike. Even **[[she-who-shall-not-be-named|She Who Shall Not Be Named]]** â€” the multi-million-year-old dragon ruling the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] â€” does not know where the Throne is.

Access to the Throne's chamber requires entering the Hall **through the royal palace on Eadras itself**, *not* through the [[firethorn-hold|Firethorn Hold]] manifestation on [[gaia|Gaia]]. This is a significant distinction: most Swordwielder access to the Hall comes through the Gaia Door; the Throne's chamber is reachable only from the original imperial citadel on Eadras's Prime Material continent. The "Door normally manifests in Firethorn Hold" rule applies to Runesword-related access, not Throne access.

A worthy Tarran claimant who reaches the Throne's chamber will see the cloak fall and the Throne â€” alien crystal seat with its living thorns â€” as it has been since the empire's collapse. The Throne does not remain in this state forever; it answers only to legitimate sovereignty, and a true claimant's arrival is what re-anchors it to the world.

## The Door

The Door is a **thorn-framed arch of living [[worldender-crystal|alien crystal]]**. It normally manifests within the **Tarran Royal Citadel on [[gaia|Prime-like Gaia]]**, hidden behind layers of ritual wards and honor guards in [[firethorn-hold|Firethorn Hold]]. But it is **not bound to that location**.

When a potential worthy Swordwielder anywhere in the Mythos reaches a moment of true need and moral alignment with one of the swords, **the Door can appear before them**:

- A physical archway in a ruin
- A reflection in still water
- A sudden thorn-framed portal in a battlefield
- An archway in the air during desperate need

**Only those with legitimate potential** can pass. The Door **judges blood, intent, and worthiness**. Many have stood before it and seen **only blank stone**. A true potential Swordwielder sees the thorns bloom with inner fire and the archway open onto the Hall beyond.

When a worthy bearer crosses the threshold, the corresponding blade's crystal elements **flare to life even before the Door fully opens**:
- Brightthorn's white crystal thorns shimmer
- Darkthorn's black veins drink the light
- Deathbringer's core pulses like a dying star

The chosen sword **lifts from its plinth and drifts into the bearer's hand** as they step through.

## The Treasures â€” Trap and Treasury

Surrounding the dais are **vast side-vaults, alcoves, and seemingly endless galleries** filled with treasures that would ignite insatiable greed in the heart of the greediest dragon:

- Piles of ancient orichalcum coins bearing the flame-mark of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]
- Crates of flawless [[worldender-crystal|alien crystal shards]]
- Weapons and armor of pre-war masterwork
- Lost [[velrathi|Velrathi]] tomes of forbidden knowledge
- Heaps of gemstones the size of fists
- Living [[feywild|Feywild]] blossoms preserved in crystal
- Mundane wonders â€” perfect tapestries, flawless musical instruments, rare spices from extinct gardens

**Every item is real. Every item is valuable beyond measure. And every item is a trap â€” except for one specific person.**

### The Dual Function

The treasures serve **two purposes simultaneously**, by [[dao|Dao's]] design:

#### To Any Potential Swordwielder Not of the True Tarran Bloodline

The riches are a **deliberate and lethal trap**. The alien crystal lattice of the Hall **reads intent**. Greed, scholarly hunger, or even well-meaning curiosity triggers the test â€” illusions of glory, whispered promises, and **subtle compulsions that have broken stronger souls than most**.

Many have entered seeking a Runesword and **left empty-handed**, forever changed:
- Some wiser
- Some broken
- A few **driven mad** by visions of what they almost claimed

The treasures are arranged in **deliberate temptation zones** radiating outward from the central dais. The closer one gets to Deathbringer's apex, the more **subtle and insidious** the lures become â€” not crude gold, but artifacts that whisper of justice, redemption, or the restoration of lost loved ones.

The Hall does not punish greed with death. **It punishes it with self-knowledge.**

#### To the Rightful Tarran Monarch

To one who carries the **true Firethorn blood and legitimate claim**, the treasures are simply the **private royal treasury**. The Door opens without challenge. The riches part like obedient servants. The monarch may take what is needed to:
- Sustain the exiled royal house
- Fund righteous causes
- Prepare for the day the empire might rise again

The Hall recognizes its true owner and withholds nothing.

**No legitimate claimant has appeared since the [[great-banishment|Banishment]].** The treasury has waited untouched for ten thousand years.

## Anti-Theft

Any item removed from the Hall by an unauthorized hand **dissolves into harmless light the moment it crosses the threshold**, leaving only a mocking echo in the bearer's mind. The wealth literally **cannot be stolen**.

## Notable Failures and Honored Outcomes

To be invited by the Door is considered the **ultimate test of character**. To emerge with a Runesword is proof of worth. **To emerge without one â€” but alive and wiser â€” is still counted a rare honor.**

A famous raven-[[beast-folk|beastfolk]] scholar once entered seeking knowledge rather than a blade. He spent what felt like centuries cataloguing the tomes before remembering his purpose. When he finally approached the dais, the Hall offered him a **single perfect memory-crystal of the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Broken Conclaves]]** â€” unfiltered and raw. He took it, wept, and left without a sword. The crystal now rests in a [[katharim|Katharim]] vault, studied by those strong enough to bear its weight.

## The Empty Plinth â€” The Throne's Scar

In the [[dao|Dao's]] original design, a twelfth element was meant for the apex's neighbor: the [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] itself. After the Throne's disappearance, only a **faint circular scar** remains on the dais where it once stood â€” a slightly warm indentation in the alien crystal. Worthy bearers entering the Hall sometimes report an **echoing absence** at that spot, as though the Throne's silence is itself a kind of presence.

Some [[beast-folk|beast-folk]] stories claim that **if the rightful monarch ever does enter, the entire Hall will bloom with living Firethorn vines and the central scar will finally heal.**

## What Bearers Hear

Many who have been called describe the Hall as a place of **profound, crushing silence** â€” eleven empty plinths waiting for their siblings, the central dais bearing the absence-scar.

When a worthy Swordwielder enters, they sometimes hear faint echoes in **languages that do not exist in Eadras** â€” the **ancient warrior souls** offering counsel, or *"the distant laughter of three children who were once burned for being different and now help guard the world from those who would burn it again"* (a reference to [[the-three|Mark, Rand, and Melania's]] outsider origin).

In moments of supreme need, bearers have reported hearing fragments of the **Final Oath** sung by the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]] at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Broken Conclaves]] â€” carried through the crystal's memory of its violent birth.

## Cultural Significance

To the **outside world**, the Hall of Kings is the **last unbroken relic of the old empire's grandeur**, yet it belongs to no living monarch. Exiled [[tarans|Tarran]] royalty still guard the Door's usual location with **fanatical devotion**. They have not been granted entry in ten thousand years.

The blades have instead chosen their own wielders among the *"lesser"* peoples â€” orcs, humans, beast-folk, the occasional unlikely hero. This is seen by traditionalists as both insult and vindication: **the old empire's greatest weapons now serve the children of the world the progenitors helped break.**

To the [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]], the Hall is a place of awe and mischief: *"The big gray uncles built a fancy closet for eleven magic knives, and the knives still pick whoever they want."*

[[katharim|Katharim]] treat any rumor of the Door's appearance as a major archival event.

## Lesson Carved in Crystal

Around the multilevel dais, wealth enough to buy kingdoms lies untouched, a silent warning carved in crystal and gold:

> **Power is not for those who reach. It is for those who refuse everything else to grasp only what is needed.**

The [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] may have vanished, but its lesson remains â€” embodied in every glittering, deadly temptation that fills the Hall of Kings.
