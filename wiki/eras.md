# The Four Eras

**Summary**: Eadras officially supports **four playable historical eras** as full campaign settings. Inspired by the multi-era structure of Star Wars Saga Edition (Old Republic / Rebellion / New Republic / etc.), each era is a complete drop-in setting with its own tone, peoples, magic level, central conflicts, and signature aesthetic. The Modern Era is the default canonical present and the spine of the core book; the three earlier eras are intended as splatbook campaign settings that share the same continuity but feel like distinctly different games.

**Sources**: Grok Chat 5-8-26.txt; HighArathiCampaign_01_InWorld_Facts.txt (2026-05-12 ingest, "High Arathi Era" alias).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-29

---

## Why four

Eadras has roughly ten million years of canonical history, but the settings worth supporting as full campaign worlds cluster naturally into four high-density slices:

1. **The Crystalline Age** — peak [[arathi|Arathi]] civilization
2. **The High Firethorn Era** — middle [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] at its strongest and most stable
3. **The Demon War**
4. **The Modern Era** — the canonical present, ten thousand years after the Demon War

A fifth potential era — the lone post-Cataclysm [[dragons|Dragon]] / [[unicorns|Unicorn]] period before mortal civilization — was considered and rejected as too thin to support varied play. Dragons and unicorns are extraordinary scholar-predators and lifelong learners, not a civilization with the breadth of factions, races, and texture players need from a campaign world. They appear as major presences in three of the four eras instead, which is where they shine.

The four chosen eras all share the qualities that make a setting playable: enough distinct peoples doing big things, enough power gradient for a party to feel both consequential and at risk, and enough setting-specific signature material that a returning player feels they're somewhere new.

## The four eras

### 1. The Crystalline Age

> *Play in the height of [[arathi|Arathi]] civilization — a world of living crystal cities, song-powered [[magitek]], and flawless arcane engineering. Elegant, ancient, and unknowingly standing right at the edge of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]].*

> **Also called the High Arathi Era** — the names are interchangeable. *Crystalline Age* emphasizes the aesthetic (living crystal cities, song-powered magitek); *High Arathi Era* emphasizes the civilization. Both refer to the same playable period.

**Setting timeframe**: A few centuries before the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]]. Not the final ten minutes — full peak-civilization play, with the audience knowing it's all going to burn but the characters living inside the brightest moment a planet ever produced.

**Distinctive flavor**:
- The only era where mortal civilization has access to true [[crystalmancy|living crystalmancy]], untruncated. Modern characters cannot match it; this is the original.
- High-level play is built into the environment. A party with the right magitek and conduit access can step into the [[concordance|Concordance]], walk into the [[heart-of-the-goddess|Heart of the Goddess]], stroll through the [[nexus|Nexus]] like downtown, or drop into [[limina|Limina]] without leaving the city.
- The world is **tectonically still** — fixed, eternal, predictable continental layout. No plate tectonics, no continental drift. The [[seven-conduits|conduits]] sit exactly where the Arathi expect them to. (The world is not, however, geologically dead: earthquakes are a constant low-level background and the subterranean hydrological cycle is active. The Arathi simply have no concept that the continents themselves could move at scale.)
- The [[arathi|Arathi]] are the **only sapient race**, alongside their [[arathi-servitors|engineered servitor lineages]] (the [[trolls|trolls]] and the rest). [[dragons|Dragons]] and [[unicorns|unicorns]] **do not exist yet** — the first dragons wake ~8 mybp, roughly two million years *after* the Cataclysm — and none of the later peoples exist either. The world is Arathi, and Arathi-made. The Arathi are themselves [[planewalkers|worldwalkers]], so this golden age is cosmopolitan and multiversally connected rather than isolated.
- [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir]] is a child or unborn somewhere in the empire. The end is approaching that no character knows is coming.

**Central tension**: dramatic irony. The players know what's coming. The Arathi do not.

**Signature campaign hook**: Among other splat material, this era hosts the **Closed-Time-Bubble Chaos Crusade** — a campaign in which the players become the universe's designated observers for a war that gets surgically excised from history at its conclusion. They alone remember. The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] records it but seals the chapter. The full standalone treatment lives at [[rpg/closed-time-bubble|wiki/rpg/closed-time-bubble.md]] (per the 2026-05-09 RPG-subfolder organization); the [[#The Closed-Time-Bubble campaign|original inline summary]] below remains for context.

### 2. The High Firethorn Era

> *The [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] at its absolute peak. The three progenitor races rule in harmony, their engineered peoples thrive across the world, and young [[dragons|dragons]] and [[unicorns|unicorns]] serve proudly as mercenaries. A confident, golden age of grand ambition.*

**Setting timeframe**: The middle stable period of the Firethorn Empire, several thousand years after its founding (~18,000 BP) and several thousand years before its collapse in the Demon War. The Empire works. The progenitor races cooperate. [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] has not been born or has not yet become dangerous.

**Distinctive flavor**:
- The three progenitor races ([[velrathi|Velrathi]], [[tarans|Tarran]], [[elfen|Elfen]]) live and rule. Their three architectural traditions ([[velrathi-architecture|Velrathi Gothic]], [[taran-architecture|Tarran imperial]], [[elfen-architecture|Elfen song-shaped]]) are everywhere, freshly made and fully active.
- All the engineered child races ([[rathari|Rathari]], [[orugi|Orug]], [[grugi|Grug]], [[el-mer|El'mer]], [[sea-tua|Sea'tua]], [[aetherins|Aetherin]], Mitans, Sidhe) are alive and central to imperial life.
- **Dragons and unicorns serve as mercenaries.** Mostly young dragons, leaving their mother's caves and starting their hoards by signing imperial contracts; older dragons too, drawn down by curiosity, by a magical theory they want to test in the field, or simply by the grand-tour appeal of seeing mortal civilization up close. (See [[dragons]] and the [[#Dragon and unicorn mercenary culture|note below]].)
- [[crystalmancy]] and [[magitek]] are functional, sophisticated, but ultimately derivative — built on Velrathi and Rathari extrapolations from [[lost-primer|the Lost Primer]] recovered earlier in the era. The Empire is *good*, not Arathi.
- Politics, intrigue, decadence, and grand magical engineering all run high.

**Central tension**: confident imperial grandeur with the cracks not yet visible. Plenty of room for plots that might (or might not) seed the eventual collapse.

### 3. The Demon War

> *The world burns. [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] has ascended with the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]], unleashed the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince of the Open Wound]], and plunged everything into apocalyptic war. The last desperate alliance of mortals, dragons, unicorns, and gods fights to stop a fallen god-king and an endless demonic horde.*

**Setting timeframe**: The full sweep of the Demon War, from Xar'vion's first overt move through the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] and the [[great-banishment|Banishment]] of the progenitor races. Roughly 10,000 BP.

**Distinctive flavor**:
- Apocalyptic stakes. The gods walk the battlefield. The [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] is being founded by Lirra's hand even as people fight and die. The [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] are being made. The Weave does not yet exist as a stable structure.
- Dragons, unicorns, mortals, and gods stand together. Things that have never cooperated cooperate.
- Three engineered races die at the Final Wall ([[orugi|Orug]], [[el-mer|El'mer]], [[aetherins|Aetherin]] — the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]]) — heroic last stands are baked into the era.
- This is the era of [[great-betrayal|great betrayals]] (the Tarran Emperor poisoned by his Velrathi fiancée at the gods' demand), [[runeswords|Runesword wieldings]], and existential moral choices made under crushing pressure.

**Central tension**: outright war and survival. Hope as something fought for, not assumed.

### 4. The Modern Era

> *Ten thousand years after the Demon War. New kingdoms have risen from the ashes, the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] still hunts the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] in the shadows, and the next [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]] is approaching — the first real chance in millennia for the Spear to be found again.*

**Setting timeframe**: The canonical present. Default for the game's core book.

**Distinctive flavor**:
- All the modern peoples (humans, elves, dwarves, gnomes, goblins, beast-folk, orcs, halflings, Sea-tua descendants, etc.) are extant. The progenitor races are gone from the Prime, but their works litter the landscape.
- Crystalmancy is partially recovered (gnome vault-archaeology); magitek is a working technology integrated into commerce and daily life; the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] holds the seven surviving [[seven-conduits|Towers of Lirra]].
- Grand modern cultures (the [[orden|Orden]] trade compact, the [[bold|Bold Conduit Republic]], the [[azure-basin|Azure Basin]] poleis, the [[amunorians|Amunorian]] in faded glory, the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] on Gaia, [[varkhul|Varkhul]] as the prison continent, etc.) form a varied political and trading landscape.
- The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] is active, the [[white-palm|White Palm]] resists, and the [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]] is coming.

**Central tension**: shadow-war and mystery. The world looks stable; the inner truth is that it is not.

#### Two Modern Era variants

The Modern Era supports two officially-defined alternate framings. They are mutually exclusive. If a campaign uses one, it does not use the other.

**Variant A: The Spear Era** (default canonical present)
The Black Hand's hunt for the Spear ahead of the next Grand Conjunction is the central long-arc threat. Most of the population does not know the Spear still exists. The world is tense but stable. This is what the core book defaults to.

**Variant B: The Second Falling** (~8,000–12,000 years post-Demon War; alternate timeline)
A vengeful demon faction has spent millennia preparing their answer to the original [[worldender|Worldbreaker]]. They are about to hurl a **second falling star** at Eadras — not loaded with destruction alone, but with **Demon Lords** who will teleport free moments before impact and begin ravaging the planet. This is a darker, higher-stakes alternate present where the Spear is no longer the central question because something more immediate is incoming. Most of the canonical present-day institutions and peoples are still recognizable, but the Black Hand may be a footnote in the face of what the demons are doing.

The Second Falling is **alternate canon, not concurrent** with the Spear Era. A campaign uses one or the other.

## Cross-era constants

Several things are present in all four eras and serve as anchors for players moving between settings:

- **The [[seven-conduits|seven conduits]]** are eternal, magical, and unmoving relative to the planet's core. They predate even the Arathi. Across all four eras, the conduit termini are where civilizations cluster — though after the Cataclysm, the surface positions of the termini shifted relative to the land above (the conduits stayed where they were while the crust slid).
- **The [[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]]** exist throughout, though their level of direct intervention varies dramatically. They are most direct in the Crystalline Age and least direct in the Modern Era (per the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]).
- **[[dragons|Dragons]] and [[unicorns|unicorns]]** are present in three of the four eras — the High Firethorn Era, the Demon War, and the Modern Era — but **not the Crystalline Age**, which predates their existence entirely (the first dragons wake ~8 mybp, roughly two million years after the Cataclysm). They are sentient lifelong learners, not background creatures.
- **The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]]** has chronicled all four eras (and a fair amount of pre-Crystalline-Age history besides). Whatever the players do, it is, in some sense, in the book.

## Splatbook structure (forward-looking)

The intended publication shape is:

- **Core book** — Modern Era (Spear Era variant as default).
- **Splatbook 1** — The High Firethorn Era. Strongest splat for grand imperial campaigns with magitek and dragon mercenaries.
- **Splatbook 2** — The Demon War. Strongest splat for high-stakes apocalyptic-alliance play.
- **Splatbook 3** — The Crystalline Age. Strongest splat for high-magic doomed-golden-age campaigns. Hosts the Closed-Time-Bubble campaign as a flagship adventure.

The Second Falling variant of the Modern Era is most likely a Modern Era supplement rather than its own splat.

This is **forward-looking publication design**, not current canon scope. The current wiki's job is to make sure each era is internally coherent and has enough material that a future splat could be derived from it.

## The Closed-Time-Bubble campaign

A flagship adventure pitch for the Crystalline Age splat. Full standalone treatment lives at [[rpg/closed-time-bubble]]; this is the inline summary.

*(Working title is a placeholder slated for rename before publication — the current name spoils the climactic twist in its own marketing.)*

> The [[arathi|Arathi]] are at the height of their civilization. A [[chaos|Chaos]] incursion begins for which the world has no precedent and from which their cosmology cannot defend. The PCs are an elite troubleshooter team investigating early "minor anomalies." As the campaign progresses, the anomalies get worse. The Arathi response is calm, arrogant, and inadequate. By the end of the campaign, the players have fought heroically — and lost. The civilization is slipping. At the climax, **the actualized timeline of Eadras diverges away from the path the players have been walking**: the inflection point resolves the other way; the world's actual history takes a different branch; the path the players were on falls away as an unactualized potential.
>
> The PCs are **deliberately preserved as designated observers** of the unactualized branch — the universe and the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] cooperate to keep them in observer position so that the branch is preserved at all. The PCs wake at the inflection point, with all their memories, all their scars, all their knowledge of what was coming on the path that didn't actualize, and no one else who remembers anything.
>
> The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] records the unactualized branch (the PCs observed it, and the Tome's [[tome-of-baldaran#Potential events at inflection points|inflection-point recording mechanism]] preserves potentials of that scale), but seals the chapter and refuses to open it for anyone in this age. The Tome's stance is essentially: *this is in the records, but this is not history.*

The campaign pitch **does not break** the [[omniversal-laws|Time Is Absolute]] law and requires no fine-print loophole reading of it. Time was always absolute, the actualized timeline always proceeded forward without interruption, and cause-and-effect were never violated. An inflection point in the early Crystalline Age simply had multiple possible resolutions; the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] recorded the possibilities; only one resolution actualized; the others (including the one the players experienced) became sealed unactualized records. Branching potentials at inflection points are entirely compatible with absolute time.

This adventure exists as **splatbook campaign material**, not as canonical wiki history. The Crystalline Age era does not contain a remembered Chaos Crusade. The Tome contains the sealed chapter for the unactualized branch; the world does not. The campaign keeps its working title for tabletop branding; the in-universe metaphysics do not in fact require any kind of closed time bubble at all.

## Dragon and unicorn mercenary culture

Specific to the High Firethorn Era splat. Worth noting because it is canonical and it shapes what playable parties look like in that setting.

[[dragons|Dragons]] are fully sentient, with minds much like humans — curious, passionate, and naturally lifelong learners. Because they live for thousands of years (interrupted by hibernation cycles), elder dragons tend to become genuinely wise and extraordinarily knowledgeable, mastering complex fields like astronomy, geology, magic, biology, philosophy, or history over decades or centuries.

In the High Firethorn Era, **young** dragons make up the majority of dragons who come down to serve as mercenaries in the Empire — leaving their mother's cave, eager for grand adventures, and looking to start building their hoard. Imperial service is the perfect mix of excitement, combat, and very good pay. Dragons of all ages occasionally come down: some elders for fresh experiences, some to study mortal civilizations up close, some to test magical theories in the field, some simply because they feel like wandering for a few decades.

Mercenary work is not a coming-of-age rite in any sacred sense — there is no draconic ceremony attached. It is much simpler: *I'm old enough to make my own way now. Time to go have an adventure and start filling my hoard.* That said, leaving the mother's cave is itself a public declaration of adulthood among dragons, and signing imperial contracts is the most respected way to do it.

Unicorns occasionally serve in mercenary roles too, though they are more selective than dragons. The full unicorn mercenary tradition is undocumented in current canon.

For the cultural-vignette example of an elder dragon's interaction with the Empire, see the short story *The Well-Fed Experiment* in the High Firethorn campaign material (Thaloryx the Verdant, gut-flora researcher, proposes a year-long no-eat spell on the Imperium Palace; the Emperor diplomatically deflects to the empty Grand Granary Complex across the river).

## Open canon

- **Whether a fifth era — explicitly Crystalline-Age-pre-decadence ("Early Crystalline") or post-Cataclysm ("Recovery") — would benefit later development.** Current canon says four is enough.
- **The exact framing of how Modern-Era Variant B (the Second Falling) reconciles with Spear-Era Variant A** if both ever appear in the same publication. The current answer is "they are alternates, not concurrent."
- **Specific publishable splat structure** — running order, character class adjustments, magic-level rules, era-specific monsters. Out of scope for the wiki at the current development stage; left to the future game designers.
