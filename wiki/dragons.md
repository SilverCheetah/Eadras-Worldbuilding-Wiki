# Dragons

**Summary**: Dragons are one of the oldest sapient races on Eadras, created when [[ixthal]] secretly amplified a natural Source surge into an X-class flare, organized into nine color clans, and shaped by a hundred million years of war and cultural exchange with unicorns.

**Sources**: Dragons.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Chaos Crusades.txt, Crystalmancy.txt, map explanation.txt, Draconic language.txt, The Flesh-Eaters.txt, The Great Exchange.txt, notes on various topics 1.txt, dragon-empire-far-eastern-meritocracy.md (2026-05-13 ingest, [[dragon-empire|Dragon Empire]] modern-era polity), spear-containment-diagnosis-and-the-alien-artifact-principle.md (2026-05-13 ingest, volunteer dragon god of the Spear's third hiding).

## Origin

The first dragons were created **roughly 8 million years before present**, when the ascended mortal deity **[[ixthal|Ixthal]]** secretly amplified a natural **Source surge** into an **X-class Source Flare** — the **First Great Flare** — about 2 million years after the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]. The flare manifested as **nine distinct eggs**, each corresponding to a unique draconic archetype — not scattered randomly but emerging as a structured manifestation, suggesting a controlled rather than uncontrolled outcome. Each egg represented a different expression of draconic nature, establishing the foundational diversity of dragonkind from the moment of their emergence. These nine progenitors — the **[[nine-embers-of-renewal|Nine Embers of Renewal]]** — are the ancestors of every later dragon; the nine color clans descend from them. **All nine of them eventually ascended to godhood**, becoming the first generation of dragon gods (User direction 2026-05-06). Three are publicly named in the modern pantheon — Cindirith, Davian, and Koruss — and the other six remain present only in draconic esoteric tradition. See [[nine-embers-of-renewal]]. (See [[eadras-timeline]]; sources: Addition — Sourceflare manifestation (dragons).txt; notes on various topics 1.txt.)

The public myth is not wholly false: dragons truly were born during a natural Source event. The lie is that the event remained natural. Most dragons do not know Ixthal created them; common draconic doctrine holds that the Nine Progenitors hatched in a sacred natural mystery. The full forbidden truth is preserved only in the Sealed Hall of First Flame in [[ratharil]], and even there it is incomplete. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

Ixthal's dragons carried three inheritances: reptilian flesh from the renewed world, Source-born magical intensity from the flare, and Ixthalic regulation in the structure of their sapience — which may explain why dragons are intensely individualistic yet instinctively defer to age. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

From these nine progenitors, dragonkind spread across the still-fracturing post-Cataclysm world. Dragons dominated Eadras for the long stretch between their emergence and the rise of humanoid races (roughly 8 million years ago to roughly 39,000 years ago — the entirety of the dragon-unicorn era), in a destructive cycle: war → recovery → stability → overcrowding → war. The marks of those wars still shape modern geography: deserts that began as Red-Blue warfronts, glassed valleys where Gold and Purple dragons clashed, swamps acidified by Black clan battles, mountain peaks sheared flat by elder dragon duels.

The dragon-unicorn era unfolded across two major continental configurations: the original three-continent post-Cataclysm world ([[avalon|Avalon]], [[damedes|Damedes]], [[culethor|Culethor]] plus pre-Cataclysm survivors [[therion|Therion]] and [[aurettos|Aurettos]]), and the [[eldravath|Eldravath]] supercontinent that formed at ~6 mybp when those continents collided. Eldravath was the last single-landmass phase of the world before fracturing back toward the modern continents. See [[eadras-tectonic-history]].

During this era of draconic dominance, **the [[chaos-crusades|First Chaos Crusade]]** struck — approximately **6 million years before present**, around the same horizon as unicorn emergence. Raw [[chaos|Chaos]] flooded the Mythos through Kudur-eṭir's ancient aperture and took the form of first-order ideals, colors, and smells given temporary bodies. Dragons met it as a martial matter and fought alongside the Gods. Eventually the Gods determined dragonkind alone could not win and intervened directly, sealing the breach. Dragons remember the era as a calamity that affected one-third of the Prime Material and both the Bright and Dark Mirrors; the role they played is preserved in the Sealed Hall and in clan elder memory, not in popular doctrine.

## The Unicorns and Cultural Exchange

The dragon-unicorn relationship has three distinct historical layers: the **Unicorn Plains War**, the post-war **Compact of Horn and Flame**, and (much later) the **[[great-exchange|Great Exchange]]**. Modern dragon culture is shaped most strongly by the third.

### The Unicorn Plains War

Ixthal caused a second amplified Source surge — the **Silver Plains Flare** — that created the **[[unicorns]]** — the second sapient race. To dragons, they were initially just another type of prey. During one of the periodic draconic civilizational collapses, dragon clans discovered the [[unicorn-plains|Unicorn Plains]] and began hunting in earnest. The unicorns first hid; when extinction became a real threat, the unicorn **[[unicorns#The Chorus|Chorus]]** made a cold, calculated decision to strike the dragons while they were at their weakest. The brief but brutal **Unicorn Plains War** ended with the dragons decisively driven from the plains. No formal treaty was made; dragons simply stopped hunting the plains. (sources: Playground generation 1st part.txt; The Great Exchange.txt)

This altered predator patterns and opened grassland corridors that later humanoid peoples exploited unknowingly.

### The Compact of Horn and Flame (post-war norm)

The **Compact of Horn and Flame** is the post-war mythic accord between the first races: dragons would not hunt unicorns; unicorns would not cleanse dragon lairs without invitation (some dragon magic is fierce but not corrupt). It was not a written treaty but an understood norm — and it held for the long, distant coexistence between the war and the much later Exchange.

### The Great Exchange (much later mutual teaching)

Long after the war and the Compact had stabilized into background reality, **young dragons living at the edges of their fractured society** and **young unicorns experimenting with stepping outside the Chorus** began meeting at the borders of their territories. From those tentative conversations grew the **[[great-exchange|Great Exchange]]** — a centuries-long mutual teaching that fundamentally transformed both species. (source: The Great Exchange.txt)

What the unicorns taught the dragons:

- **The disciplined arts of psionics** — specifically **telepathy, telekinesis, psychic combat, and psychic shielding.** A four-discipline mental tradition the dragons had never developed on their own.
- **The true power of cooperation without domination or hierarchy.** A model of collective action that does not require an apex predator at the top — this is the cultural DNA from which [[ratharil|Ratharil]] and the [[ratharil#Council of 99 Eldest|Council of Ninety-Nine]] eventually grew.
- **The realization that other sapient species had existed on Eadras for approximately twenty thousand years.** A genuine shock — until the unicorns told them, the dragons did not know there were *other thinking beings* on the world beyond unicorns and themselves.

What the dragons taught the unicorns:

- **The power and burden of individuality** — the concept of a true, singular *"I"*, distinct from the [[unicorns#The Chorus|Chorus]].
- **New and aggressive applications of [[eldritch|Eldritch]] magic** previously unknown to the unicorns.

The Exchange's two cultural-legacy phrases are still in active use:

> **"Walking the Dragon Path"** — said of a modern unicorn who lives as an individual.
>
> **"Singing with the Unicorn Voice"** — said of a dragon who sets aside personal pride for the good of the whole. Among traditionalist dragons it can be an insult; among Council-aligned dragons it is among the highest possible compliments.

### The Treaty of Ratharil

Inspired most directly by the unicorn-given lesson of *cooperation without domination or hierarchy*, dragons created the **Treaty of Ratharil** in the city of [[ratharil]]: the total dragon population would be capped at exactly **999,999** — in honor of the original nine progenitors — to finally break the ancient cycle of overcrowding and war. This number may also serve as a metaphysical containment limit: too many Source-born dragons may disturb ley systems and the Weave. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

After the Compact and the Treaty, both races faced the **[[chaos-crusades|Second Chaos Crusade]]** together — approximately **2 million years before present**. Raw Chaos breached the Mythos a second time and took the form of dragon-and-unicorn body-horror, having observed the Mythos's dominant beings without understanding them. Dragons, Unicorns, and the Gods responded together; the Gods, having learned from the First Crusade, intervened faster, and damage was limited to a continental rather than worldwide scale.

## Ratharil: The City of Dragons

The **[[ratharil|City of Ratharil]]** is a genuine neutral city built by and for dragonkind — a real architectural achievement with quarried stone buildings, clan halls, the Ninety-Ninefold Hall, aerial terraces, landing courts, vaults, and archives. It is governed by the **Council of 99 Eldest** (eleven elder representatives from each of the nine color clans). The Treaty's population limit is maintained via the **Ratharil Stone** in the council chamber, magically tied to draconic bloodline. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## The Dragon Truth — one Fire, not two

Dragons are unique among the sapient races of Eadras in their relationship to magic. Where every other race — Arathi, unicorns, Velrathi, Tarran, Elfen, and all their descendants — experiences a metaphysical divide between **raw Sourceshine** and **reflected [[eldritch|Eldritch]]**, dragons experience only **one Fire**. To them, raw and reflected are not two different energies, and not two aspects of the same energy: they are *the same thing*.

Dragon biology, forged in the **First Great Flare**, recognizes only *the Fire of the World* and draws upon it in whatever form or intensity their need demands. There is no internal switch. There is no instinctual sorting. There is no conscious or unconscious choice. A dragon wants to breathe annihilation across a battlefield — the Fire answers. A dragon wants to weave a delicate ward or speak mind-to-mind with a unicorn — the Fire answers. Whether the power originates as direct sunlight on their scales or has passed through a thousand miles of bedrock is irrelevant. (source: Crystalmancy.txt)

The definitive [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] entry: *"To the dragons there has never been Sourceshine and Eldritch. There is only the Fire. What mortals call 'raw' and 'reflected' are illusions of limited perception. The dragon does not reach for one or the other — it simply is the Fire, and the Fire obeys."*

This singular perception is why dragons never built crystal conduits, ley-vaults, or [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]] lattices: they had no need. It is also why they regarded the [[arathi|Arathi]] with a mixture of respect and quiet pity — a race that spent ten thousand years perfecting elegant infrastructure to do, with regulation and ritual, what dragons do by breathing. Even elder dragons who have lived among mortals for centuries sometimes forget that other races experience the Source as divided. A dragon casually basking at noon on a mountain peak while simultaneously powering a complex ritual through distant ley lines is not performing two different acts — to the dragon it is one seamless expression of the Fire.

## Modern Dragons

**Elder dragons** are extremely reclusive. When they do act, the results are monumental and world-changing: an elder Red entering a war can end a nation; a Silver rising from the sea can close trade across an ocean.

**Young adult and older dragons** can **shapeshift into humanoid form** at will. Many work as highly sought-after elite mercenaries, building hoards and gaining status. A dragon mercenary may operate under a humanoid identity for decades. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### The Dragon Empire

Some millennia after the [[demon-war|Demon War]], a band of dragon mercenaries in the **far east of Eadras** — dissatisfied with the leadership they were serving — concluded that they themselves should be in charge and founded the [[dragon-empire|Dragon Empire]]. It is now one of three civilizations in the modern [[three-cultures-ethical-triangle|three-cultures ethical triangle]] (the authoritarian-meritocratic pole), ruled by a coalition of dragons who are treated as semi-divine by their populace, and organized around a comprehensive skill-rank exam state in which every adult citizen — *including the ruling dragons themselves* — carries a publicly visible pictogram-and-hashmark record of their tested competences. The Empire's relationship to the broader draconic civilization ([[ratharil|Ratharil]]-aligned dragons, the post-Banishment dragon clans, the [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Nine Embers]] and the broader dragon-god tradition) is presently open canon; the Empire's dragons are *some* modern dragons, not all of them, and the coalition's specific religious self-positioning within the broader pantheon is undeveloped. See [[dragon-empire]] for the full treatment.

## The Nine Color Clans

Each clan has distinct culture shaped by its eldest dragon's personality and traditions:

| Color | Habitat | Culture |
|---|---|---|
| **Blue** | Deserts | Highly clan-oriented |
| **Gold** | High mountains (some cities) | Most tightly knit; very communal |
| **Red** | Hot regions, volcanic, arid | Standoffish with each other; hands-off leadership. Fully **immune to fire and heat**; the most ancient settle permanently into active volcanic chambers and can swim through molten lava as easily as a young dragon swims through air |
| **Black** | Swamps, river deltas, forested wetlands | Less orderly |
| **Purple** | Any environment | Chaotic and unpredictable; heavily influenced by Elder Goddess Faye in early history |
| **Copper** | Devastated lands, volcanic, ruins | Prefer areas of destruction |
| **Silver** | Marine and oceanic | Staid and isolationist |
| **Green** | Forested regions | Highly individualistic |
| **White** | Cold, icy regions | Individualistic but fiercely maintain clan traditions; strict **hospitality customs** (any clan member arriving is welcomed, fed, and sheltered) |

## Social Customs

**Clan Hospitality**: All nine clans practice some form of hospitality toward same-color members, though the meaning varies dramatically. A Red may offer a heated cave and a carcass without conversation. A Blue requires formal recitation of lineage. A White must feed and shelter a clanmate even if an enemy, because the ice permits no lesser law. A Gold may host with ceremony so complex a young dragon needs coaching. A Black may quietly test whether the guest deserves to leave with all their possessions. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

**Respect for Elders**: Dragons automatically defer to any dragon older than themselves, regardless of clan. This deference is instinctual and nearly impossible to resist — but can be overridden to prevent catastrophe, defend hatchlings, uphold Ratharil, or resist a dangerously mad elder. The instinct may be a remnant of Ixthal's original regulation: born from only nine progenitors, early dragons may have annihilated themselves without age-deference. Ratharil transformed instinct into civilization. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

**Wyrm — the eldest's honorific**: The title **Wyrm** is reserved for the **oldest and most respected dragons across all nine clans** (User direction 2026-05-06). It is not a clan, a sub-type, or a separate species — it is a draconic honorific marking an individual whose age and standing place them above ordinary elder rank. A Gold Wyrm, a Black Wyrm, a Red Wyrm — each is an ancient member of that clan whom every younger dragon, including ordinary elders, would defer to without question. The honorific is awarded by general recognition rather than ceremony; a dragon does not become a Wyrm by claim but by being treated as one. Wyrms are exceedingly rare, and many clans have only one or none at any given time.

**Ixthal Taboo**: Dragons who have access to esoteric elder lore know not to call upon [[ixthal]] lightly — not because he will punish them, but because asking him to look directly upon the world is understood as cruelty. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Modern Dissidents — the Flesh-Eaters

Mainstream draconic society is governed by the [[ratharil#Council of 99 Eldest|Council of Ninety-Nine]] and shaped by the legacy of the Great Exchange with the [[unicorns]]. A small, secretive cult called the **[[flesh-eaters|Flesh-Eaters]]** rejects all of it. They hold that dragons are the rightful apex of all life, that civilization and restraint are corruptions of true dragon nature, and that all non-draconic sapient life is prey — preferred prey, because thinking creatures taste better. They reject treaties, coexistence, and any form of shared civilization, treating villages and travelers as feeding grounds and hunting in small mobile cells from concealed lairs.

Flesh-Eaters compound the offense by **raiding Council-aligned dragon clan nests, stealing eggs, and indoctrinating the stolen hatchlings** in total isolation. The Council has formally declared them **enemies of dragonkind**; they have proven hard to eradicate because they operate as small, mobile cells in remote wilderness or among the ruins of ancient draconic empires, and because the cult survives its own destruction through buried doctrinal caches that allow it to be reborn from a single new finder (*"Always reborn"*). Most dragons consider speaking the name *Flesh-Eaters* in polite company to be in extremely poor taste.

## Language

Dragons speak [[draconic-language|Draconic]] — a primordial, instinctual language they awaken already speaking. It is an agglutinative abjad built on triconsonantal roots, with meaning layered through a strict three-vowel template and stacked suffixes. Dragons consider Draconic sacred; spoken with true intent, it carries metaphysical weight and can subtly influence the [[eldritch|Fire]]. Their capital is officially **Rhtrakhadara** ("The Eternal Radiant Gathering") in Draconic, [[ratharil|Ratharil]] in Common. Mortals find correct pronunciation extremely difficult; even master [[windspeakers]] only approximate the deeper resonances. (source: Draconic language.txt)

## Dragon Gods

**All Nine Embers of Renewal** — every one of the original nine dragon progenitors — ascended to godhood in the long ages following their hatching. They are the foundation of the dragon-god strand of the pantheon. Three are publicly named in modern mortal religion; the other six are remembered only in draconic esoteric tradition and have withdrawn from active mortal divine life. See [[nine-embers-of-renewal]] for the full account.

### The volunteer of the Spear's third hiding

A specific [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Ascended Mortal Dragon God]] of the era — preserved in dragon esoteric tradition and the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran's]] sealed chapters but not in the open record — voluntarily entered **[[icons|Total Icon Binding]]** to seal the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] inside their own Icon for the gods' [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#hiding-3-reverse-time-bubble-over-a-god-prison-in-the-deepest-glimmerdark-after-darkhoof|third hiding]]. The volunteer's Icon, with the Spear at its core, was placed in the deepest depths of the [[glimmerdark|Glimmerdark]] and wrapped in a reverse time bubble that accelerated subjective time vastly relative to the world outside. The volunteer subjectively endured roughly 10^37 years of unbroken solitude — vastly older, more powerful, and more isolated than any other being in the Mythos, almost certainly fractured by what working canon calls **divine schizophrenia**.

[[xarvion|Xar'vion]] eventually broke into the prison and killed the volunteer in the act of taking the Spear. The killing is one of the heaviest specific moral injuries on the dragon-god record: a martyr who had voluntarily sacrificed themselves to hold the line for the rest of the Mythos was slain by a Netharim-king pursuing resurrection magic, who afterward triggered the [[demon-war|Demon War]].

The dragons remember the volunteer as a sacred martyr. Their specific identity (name, clan, age, place in the dragon-god tradition) is **open canon** — preserved in esoteric tradition and the Tome's sealed chapters but not yet locked at the curator level. Their loss is part of why the dragons' [[mythic-recitations#3-5-what-different-peoples-emphasize|grim-satisfaction mythic register]] applied to Xar'vion is complicated by deeper grief in dragon esoteric tradition.

Publicly named dragon gods (see [[gods-of-eadras]]):
- **Cindirith, the Magnificent** — First God of Dragons, the Eldest, Platinum Dragon, 8th original egg
- **Davian, Sky Sovereign** — Second God of Dragons, Tempest Lord, Blue Dragon
- **Koruss, of the Eternal Fire** — Third God of Dragons, God of Dragonfire, Red Dragon

A later dragon — **Tarloth the Cruel**, mate of one of the Nine Embers — sought to follow her ascended mate into divinity, was denied the legitimate path, and used the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] to ascend instead. She became an Unrestricted Elder God outside the divine compact and was imprisoned by the Elder Gods.
