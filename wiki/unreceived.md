# The Unreceived

**Summary**: **The Unreceived** are the souls of beings who were **consumed wholly by [[demons]] â€” body, spirit, and soul** â€” and therefore stolen from the known cycle of death, judgment, rest, and reincarnation. They are not dead in the ordinary sense. They do not pass through the afterlife. They do not return. They are a distinct cosmological category of *unrecoverable loss* â€” one of the three categories bound by [[baldaranic-council-cycle#The Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa|the Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa]]. The Unreceived emerge as a recognised theological category during the [[demon-war|Demon War]], and the modern rituals around them â€” the **Empty Cot Vigil**, the **Unreceived Ledger**, the **Black Banner Roll**, the **Laugh With No Echo**, and the **Ashless Shrines** â€” are the institutional response to a loss the cosmology itself cannot repair.

## What the Unreceived are

When a [[demons|demon]] kills, the demon usually kills as predators do â€” taking flesh, drinking energy, leaving a body. The dying soul enters the death-cycle normally: it is received by Shali or Shani, accounted, judged, rested, recalibrated, and eventually returned to the cycle as a new mortal life.

The Unreceived are the souls of those whom demons did *not* merely kill. They are the souls of those whom demons **consumed completely** â€” body, spirit, *and soul*. The demonic appetite did not leave anything for Shali or Shani to receive. The soul did not enter judgment. The soul did not pass through accounting. The soul did not return.

This is not annihilation in the philosophical sense; it is *theft* in the cosmological sense. The soul existed; the soul should have continued in the cycle; the soul does not. *Demons did not merely kill them. They interrupted death.*

The category is **unrecoverable**. The Unreceived cannot be retrieved by any known ritual, divine intervention, or magical work. Shali and Shani have both confirmed, in different registers, that the loss is final at the cycle level. What can be done is **memory** â€” the institutional and household work of refusing to let the theft become invisible.

## Why reincarnation makes the loss worse

In Eadras's cosmology, **death is part of the soul-cycle**, not its end. An ordinary death is grievous but is also passage â€” the soul carries on, eventually returning. A family that loses a child to ordinary causes mourns the child but knows that the child's soul continues its journey.

Demonic consumption denies the soul that continuation. The Unreceived's families mourn not only a person but **the road that person should have continued to walk**. Every future life that soul would have lived is also stolen. The grief is therefore *forward-extended* in a way ordinary grief is not.

The Tome's recommended phrase for official doctrine on this point is **"the known cycle"** â€” leaving room for mystery and divine action beyond mortal understanding without offering false comfort. Some theologians argue the Unreceived may have a fate the gods themselves cannot see; the Tome neither confirms nor denies. *The cosmology does not promise rescue. The cosmology promises only that the loss is named.*

## The ritual phrase

The anchoring phrase for the entire category of rituals is:

> ***May theft be remembered.***

This is recited at the Black Banner Roll, inscribed on Ashless Shrines, sung in the Empty Cot Vigil's seventh night, and held in the Unreceived Ledger's preamble. It is not a prayer for return. It is not a curse on demons. It is the institutional acknowledgement that the loss happened, that it cannot be repaired, and that the refusal to forget it is the only honour the lost can be given.

## Rituals

### The Empty Cot Vigil

A household rite. When a family confirms a member is Unreceived (typically through Katharim verification of demonic consumption or by absence-of-soul-passage detected by sympathetic divinatory means), a bed, cot, cloak, or blanket belonging to the lost member is laid out **for seven nights**.

The Vigil is **not** to summon the dead. The lost soul cannot return. The Vigil is **to give love somewhere to go**. The grief of the survivors has no destination â€” the soul is not waiting, not resting, not on a road â€” so the household arranges a physical place that holds the love that has no recipient. On the seventh night, the cloth is folded and stored. The vigil does not become an annual ritual; the bedding is folded once.

Some households integrate the Vigil with [[shali|Shali]]/[[shani|Shani]] death rites for ordinary deaths in the same lineage, but the Vigil is structurally distinct: ordinary deaths face *passage*; the Vigil faces *theft*.

### The Unreceived Ledger

A [[katharim|Katharim]] institutional record. The Ledger lists, by name where known, those stolen from judgment. They are *not* entered among the judged dead. The Katharim maintain the Ledger as a separate book; their death-counting work normally produces an accounting that balances. The Unreceived Ledger is the **deliberate non-balance** â€” the record of souls that should be in the books but aren't.

The Ledger has both institutional and ritual function. Institutionally, it lets the Katharim track patterns: where demonic consumption is rising, which regions are losing more souls than ordinary death would predict, which years bring more theft than others. Ritually, the Ledger is read aloud at appropriate ceremonies; the reading is part of *making theft remembered*.

A Ledger entry is permanent. Names enter; names do not leave. The Katharim are firm on this even when families request removal.

### The Black Banner Roll

A war-temple list, read aloud before anti-demonic campaigns. The Black Banner Roll names the Unreceived from prior demonic conflicts whose deaths the campaigners now honour or seek to forestall.

The response after each name is recited:

> ***"No grave. No surrender."***

The Roll is not a recruitment device. Soldiers are not asked to fight to avenge the Unreceived (avenging is structurally impossible â€” demons that consumed those souls may not exist by the time of any campaign). The Roll is recited as **acknowledgement of stakes**: campaigners are reminded what kind of war they may be entering, what kind of loss it can produce, and what kind of refusal is required.

War-temples that have rolled the Roll three times against the same Demonfall edge consider themselves to be in a sustained anti-demonic posture and adjust their operational structure accordingly.

### Laugh With No Echo

A [[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] rite. A joke is told for one of the consumed dead, and **never repeated**.

The Beast-Folk's broader cultural disposition is daily forgetting maintained through laughter (see [[sylph#The Veil|Sylph's Veil]] and [[beast-folk]]). The Laugh With No Echo is the rare deliberate exception: a joke is composed and told *once*, for a specifically Unreceived soul, in a Circle gathering. The joke is funny if at all possible. The Circle laughs. Then everyone present agrees never to retell it.

The function is paradoxical and exact. The Beast-Folk are committed to forgetting; the Laugh With No Echo says *this loss is so categorical that even our forgetting will not erase it â€” the silence afterward is the memorial*. The lost soul receives the joke as their last laugh in the world that contained them. The Circle carries the unrepeated joke as the shape of the loss, not as the words of it.

### Ashless Shrines

City-scale memorials. An Ashless Shrine is a memorial **without remains, ashes, relics, ghost, or soul-sign**. Only an empty bowl, often set on a stone plinth in a public square or beside a temple. The bowl is sometimes inscribed with *May theft be remembered*, sometimes left bare.

The Shrines are deliberately empty. A normal memorial contains *something* â€” bones, ash, a name plate, a depiction of the dead. The Ashless Shrine contains nothing because there is nothing to contain. The Unreceived are not in the bowl. The bowl is the visual instantiation of their absence.

Some cities maintain *one* Ashless Shrine for all their Unreceived; some maintain shrines per district, per ward, or per regional disaster. The Demonfall Marches towns nearly all have shrines proportional to the population they have lost.

A maintained Ashless Shrine receives a small offering of water on appropriate days â€” the water is poured into the bowl and allowed to evaporate, never replaced for any specific recipient. The water is not for the lost. The water is for the act of the giving.

## Frequency

The Unreceived are **rare** in the modern era. Demonic incursions large enough to consume souls wholly are now constrained by:

- [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave|The Weave]]'s defensive function (Lirra's Ward)
- The [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand's]] Crusade-class divine intervention threshold
- [[woundwatch|Woundwatch]] patrols on the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]]
- The [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]]'s long-tail demographic effect on demon-summoning practitioners

But the category remains active. New Unreceived are added to the Katharim Ledgers in most years. The Demonfall Marches towns are the most consistent contributors. The [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]] â€” when Chaos breached the Mythos through Kudur-eá¹­ir's old aperture â€” also produced Unreceived during the periods their incursions were active.

## What can be done for them

**Nothing in the cycle-restoration sense.** The Unreceived cannot be retrieved.

What *can* be done:

- **Maintain the rituals.** The five rituals above are the institutional memory architecture.
- **Track demonic incursions.** Reducing new Unreceived is the only forward-facing work; protecting the boundary protects future souls.
- **Refuse the demons' framing of consumption as inevitable.** *The Wound That Must Not Be Fed* (the doctrine emerging in the [[first-rebrightening|First Rebrightening]]) explicitly teaches that demonic hunger thrives where societies cultivate fear, secrecy, despair, uncontrolled appetite, depersoning, persecution, and metaphysical negligence â€” and that anti-demonic vigilance must protect the *dignity and continuity of persons*, because demons can consume body, spirit, and soul.
- **Carry love that has no destination.** This is the deeper Eadras-canon practice: when the cosmology cannot return the lost, the living can carry the love forward in their own lives. *Some grief does not heal. Some grief is what the survivors learn to walk with.*

## Theological status

The Unreceived are bound under [[baldaranic-council-cycle#The Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa|the Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa]] â€” one of three categories of unrecoverable cosmological ending the gods are bound to silence on. [[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani]] know more than they speak. [[ioa|Ioa]] holds the decreed line.

The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] records what is known; the open chapters acknowledge that the Unreceived exist as a category but do not speak the deepest cosmological details. The sealed chapters carry the full record.

The [[doctrine-of-open-palm|Open Palm reform]] generates theological pressure on the Decree â€” should the gods speak more openly to the families of the Unreceived? â€” but the reform does not break the Decree. The compromise is that the *category* may be openly named (and is), while the *full cosmological mechanism* remains sealed.

## Open canon

- The exact threshold at which a demonic kill becomes Unreceived consumption versus ordinary death-with-soul-passage. The Katharim verification protocols are documented at the institutional level but the metaphysical line is not.
- Whether any Unreceived soul has ever been demonstrably recovered. Mainstream theology says no; folk tradition includes accounts the Katharim catalogue as *folk-comfort fictions*; whether any of the accounts are real is canonically open.
- How the **Demon War Memory Crisis** (~25-35 FR) reshapes Unreceived rituals as living memory of the war fades.
- The **exact body of folk lore** that families develop to comfort themselves about the Unreceived: songs, false-prayers, name-passing rituals, the practice of giving a new child the name of a lost relative even when the relative was Unreceived (theologically problematic but emotionally common).
- Whether the [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]]' Unreceived (consumed by Chaos rather than by demons) are theologically the same category or a related-but-distinct sub-category.
