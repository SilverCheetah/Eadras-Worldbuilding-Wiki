## The general principle

Eadras (and every Mythos) operates under a coherent set of physical and metaphysical rules â€” Eldritch flow, ley structure, Omniversal Laws, the [[axes-of-creation|Axes of Creation]]. Native objects, by definition, conform to those rules. **Alien objects do not.**

An alien object originates outside the Mythos. Its substance was forged by a different physics. Inside Eadras it can manifest as (source: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt):
- **Harder** than anything native should be able to be
- **Stronger** under stress that should break native matter
- **More durable** against forces that erode the native world
- Or simply **weirder** â€” exhibiting properties that have no native analogue (geometries that don't close, surfaces that respond to attention, weights that don't match volume, behaviors that resist categorization)

Not every alien object is dangerous. Some are inert. But they are categorically *not* of Eadras, and that category is worth tracking.

## Why this matters

When evaluating an unfamiliar object in Eadras, the question "is this alien?" is structurally important. Alien material:
- May not conform to enchantment expectations
- May resist being broken, bound, or unmade by native means
- May affect surrounding native matter and creatures in unpredictable ways
- May carry metaphysical residue (see [[worldender]] on Worldender fragment hazards)
