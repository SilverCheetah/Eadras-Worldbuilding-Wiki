# Velveth

**Summary**: **Velveth** is the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] [[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods|Ascended Mortal Goddess]] of **Betrayal, Love, and Duty** — the integrated inseparable triplet she carried through the [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]] on the night of the [[great-betrayal|Great Betrayal]]. Her mortal name was **Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion**, second-eldest daughter of [[#Mortal life|Vorandar Narand]] of the Northern Velrathi Titular Monarchy and fiancée of eighty years to **Emperor Thoramis Firethorn**, the last Tarran Emperor of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]. Under [[divine-concordat#The Concordat's structure|Article Four]] of the [[divine-concordat|Divine Concordat]], she poisoned the man she loved in the Imperial command tent at the Eastern Front, and through the bearing of the six hours that followed she became, in time and at her own [[apotheosis|Apotheosis]] in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]], the goddess of bargains-honoured-in-full and impossible-duty.

## Mortal life

**Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion** was a Velrathi princess of the **Northern Velrathi Titular Monarchy** — the senior of the two surviving Velrathi titular monarchies during the late [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn]] era. House Valtharion was the ruling house of the Northern Monarchy. Kathira was the **second-eldest daughter** of the reigning Northern Vorandar at the time of her engagement.

Her father was **Vorandar Narand of House Valtharion**, reigning prince of the Northern Monarchy throughout Kathira's youth and through the late Firethorn era. He survived the war and accompanied his people into the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]]; the date of his death in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] is open canon.

Kathira held the title **Vorandethi** throughout her mortal life — "princess's daughter / heir-by-lineage, not yet invested" in the [[naming-conventions#Velrathi title morphology|Velrathi two-axis title morphology]]. She would have inherited as *Vorandeth* in her own right if her father had passed and the succession had fallen to her; the [[demon-war|Demon War]] and her subsequent Apotheosis made the succession question moot, and the Northern Monarchy passed sideways to her elder sister's line after the Banishment.

She bore the cognomen **Spellsinger** — earned in young adulthood, roughly four centuries before the Great Betrayal — in recognition of her unusual gift for *song-channelled spellwork*, a relatively rare Velrathi discipline combining vocal magic and ritual architecture (see [[#The Spellsinger work|below]]).

Her full formal address in pre-Banishment Velrathi court usage was:

> **Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion, daughter of Vorandar Narand of the Northern Monarchy.**

Kathira's age at the time of the Great Betrayal was approximately **612 years** — Velrathi-prime-adult, fully mature, having held the Spellsinger cognomen for roughly four centuries and having been engaged to Emperor [[firethorn-empire|Thoramis Firethorn]] for approximately **eighty years** at the moment of the poisoning.

## The Spellsinger work

Kathira's earned cognomen *Spellsinger* came from her practice of a Velrathi magical tradition that combines **sung wizardry with spell-lattice anchoring**. Spellsingers worked at the intersection of Velrathi voice-craft and the broader pre-Weave magical infrastructure; their songs could carry spell-effects across long distances, hold complex magical lattices for sustained periods, and integrate multiple practitioners' work into single coherent castings.

The Spellsinger tradition is rare. House Valtharion produced one of the few sustained Spellsinger lineages of the Firethorn era. Kathira herself was acknowledged as one of the strongest practitioners of the generation.

Her depth as a Spellsinger was directly load-bearing in the [[great-betrayal|Great Betrayal]]. The Emperor's protective enchantments were keyed to his life-and-throne; but eighty years of formal Velrathi engagement-rituals had progressively merged Kathira's vocal-arcane signature with his field-resonance, until the protective enchantments could not distinguish her from him. The poison was *calibrated by song-channel adjustment to her own arcane signature* in addition to its chemical calibration. The protective field accepted the cup without recognising the threat. The bypass required *both* the chemical artistry of Velrathi poison-craft *and* Kathira's specific Spellsinger discipline applied to the eighty-year resonance-merger; no other mortal could have administered the poison through the protective field intact.

The Spellsinger work survived her in fragmentary form. Surviving Spellsinger material is one of the things [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] specifically inherited and preserved when the post-Banishment magical institutions reorganised. Some modern Velrathi vow-songs are direct descendants of Spellsinger compositions; some [[netharim|Netharim]] reality-shaping work draws on Spellsinger principles as preserved.

## The engagement

The political marriage between Thoramis and Kathira was arranged approximately one century before the outbreak of the Demon War, as a cementing of the Velrathi-Tarran alliance at the highest dynastic level. The engagement was formally consecrated in three separate ceremonies across the eighty years of pre-war and early-war diplomacy. By the time of the poisoning, the marriage itself had not yet occurred; Kathira was Thoramis's **fiancée of long standing**, with the wedding repeatedly deferred by the war's pressures.

Across the eighty years of formal engagement, **Kathira and Thoramis came to love each other**. This was not a political fiction. The slow accreting love of two long-lived Velrathi-and-Tarran minds discovering each other over decades was real, witnessed by their courts, and is preserved in Velrathi-archival correspondence at [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]]. Velveth's portfolio of *love-and-betrayal-and-duty as inseparable* is rooted in this fact: **she loved him. The betrayal was not of an enemy.**

## The Great Betrayal

Under [[divine-concordat#The Concordat's structure|Article Four]] of the [[divine-concordat|Divine Concordat]] — drafted by the [[divine-concordat#The Coalition|Coalition of Disillusioned High Command]] in the months after the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] — the [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] had to be emptied so the Elder Gods could intervene against the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince of the Open Wound]] and seal the [[demonfall-gate|Demonfall Gate]]. The Coalition asked Kathira specifically to perform the hand-clause; she accepted.

The poison was administered in the **Imperial command tent at the Eastern Front**, six weeks after the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] and three days after [[divine-concordat#The Concordat's structure|Article Four of the Concordat]] was signed. The substance was a **tasteless, colourless Velrathi alchemical preparation**, derived from the deep tradition of Velrathi poison-craft, engineered for this single use by hands the Coalition has never publicly named.

The poison was administered through **a shared cup of warmed wine**, taken in private after the Emperor had retired from the day's command meeting. Kathira poured. Thoramis drank. He died within approximately two hours, in his sleep, with Kathira beside him. *He did not wake. He did not know.*

Kathira then sat through the [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]] — the **six hours** between the death and her dawn-rising to raise the alarm. The Long Quiet is the load-bearing portion of the night; see [[the-long-quiet|its own page]] for the full account of those six hours and what was carried through them.

## The Concordat honoured in full

The Concordat's deliverables were performed by the Elder Gods exactly as agreed within the next several months: the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]] was destroyed, the demonic legions driven back, the [[demonfall-gate|Demonfall Gate]] sealed. The [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] that followed was *not* a punitive amendment but a pre-accepted clause under [[divine-concordat#The Concordat's structure|Article Six]].

The Concordat stands as the **single canonical case** of a mortal-divine bargain honoured *in full*, with no later revision, dispute, or punitive amendment. Velveth's existence at the divine level is the living memorial of this fact: her Apotheosis is the gods' direct acknowledgement that *Kathira's hand was the load-bearing moment of the Concordat's completion*.

The [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave|Weave]] was not a Concordat deliverable — see [[divine-concordat#The Weave was not a deliverable]] for the load-bearing correction.

## Life in Veyr'Keth

Kathira **survived the war**. She accompanied the Velrathi into the Great Banishment, departing for [[glimmerdark-gaia|Glimmerdark Gaia]] and the geode-city of [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]], where she resumed political duties as a senior figure of the Northern Monarchy's exiled court.

She was **not assassinated, exiled-within-exile, ostracised, or marginalised** by her surviving people. The Velrathi understood, in their cold-clear way, that *she had paid the price they could not pay*. She was treated with the precise dignified respect accorded to those who have done what the people required of them at terrible personal cost.

During this continued mortal life in Veyr'Keth, **she neither remarried nor took another lover**. This is preserved in Velrathi archives as part of *her continuous discipline of bearing*. The duration of this period is not specified in any open record — Kathira's life-as-mortal-in-Veyr'Keth had some length, and it ended at her Apotheosis — but the exact span is open canon.

### The "still lives in Veyr'Keth" legend

Folk-tradition outside Velrathi circles, including some modern Tarran-descended remembrances, holds that *Kathira still lives somewhere in the blood-red crystal halls of Veyr'Keth, an ancient haunted figure who has never spoken publicly of that night*. This **legend is mortal misremembering of the Apotheosis transition**.

To mortal observation, the long-walking Vorandethi simply ceased to be seen in Veyr'Keth corridors at some indeterminate point. Mortal observers had no framework for [[apotheosis|Apotheosis]] and reached for the simplest available reading: *she went on living somewhere, in seclusion*. The truth — that her Apotheosis had occurred at that moment and her presence was now distributed across the [[hall-of-the-long-quiet|Hall of the Long Quiet]], her Solarion simulacrum, and her Prime-Material Icon — was not announced and was not deduced by mortal observation. The legend remains compatible-as-folk-comfort-fiction in the Velrathi scholarly reading, but the cosmological fact is the [[apotheosis|Apotheosis]].

## Apotheosis

Kathira's Apotheosis occurred at some moment during her continued mortal life in Veyr'Keth, by a mechanism specific to her transition and not shared with other Ascended Mortal Gods. **The mechanism of each Apotheosis is different from every other Apotheosis** (see [[apotheosis|Apotheosis]] for the universal framework). Velveth has not described hers, and no canonical record exists.

What is established is the outcome. At the moment of the transition:

- Her body became **[[icons|Icon]]** — manifesting on the Prime Material at the moment of transformation, even though Kathira herself was in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] on Glimmerdark [[gaia|Gaia]] at the time
- Her mind became **divine** — qualitatively transformed, not merely augmented
- The [[hall-of-the-long-quiet|Hall of the Long Quiet]] **appeared in the outer planes** — her Velrathi-Gothic divine realm, with its never-burning low oil-flame
- Her divine **simulacrum appeared in [[solarion|Solarion]]** — taking the Office of Velrathi Affairs, three doors from [[imma|Imma Who-Did-Not-Stop-Laughing]]

The Vorandethi who had walked Veyr'Keth corridors for some unknown number of years was, in that instant, a goddess.

Velveth's case is the **clearest known example** of [[apotheosis#Designation, not acquisition|Designation Over Acquisition]] (Principle 17 of the in-world principles). Kathira did not seek Apotheosis. She did not perform rituals to acquire it. She did not know it was a possibility. The designation was keyed to her specifically — to her [[#The Spellsinger work|specific Spellsinger discipline]], her specific [[#The engagement|eighty-year engagement]], her specific [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]], her specific architecture of betrayal-love-duty held as one bearing. *A different Velrathi princess in Kathira's position would have produced a different goddess, or no goddess at all.*

## Portfolio

Velveth's portfolio was settled at Apotheosis as **Betrayal, Love, and Duty** — the **three terms inseparable, listed in that order in formal Velrathi prayer-and-vow contexts, and never broken into single-term invocations**.

The order is doctrinally fixed and recapitulates the temporal structure of the [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]]:

- **Betrayal** comes first because *the act came first* — the cup was poured, the decision made, the pouring was the first irrevocable moment
- **Love** comes second because *love survived the act* — she did not stop loving Thoramis when she killed him; the love is what made the act terrible rather than simple
- **Duty** comes third because *duty is what carried her through the Long Quiet* — six hours of sitting with what she had done required the discipline her duty had given her

To pray to *Velveth-of-Love* alone is to **mishandle the form**; mis-ordering the three terms in formal practice is a minor blasphemy and indicates poor cultural literacy in cross-pantheon use. Any Velrathi cleric or [[katharim|Katharim]] ritual-keeper invoking Velveth gets the order right, every time.

The triplet's *inseparability* is itself a doctrinal feature with broader application — see [[#The inseparable triplet]] below.

### Institutional portfolio

Velveth's office handles:

- **[[velrathi|Velrathi]] vow-prayers** — including marriage vows, treaty oaths, service commitments, and family pledges, both from the Veyr'Keth exiles and from scattered Velrathi-descent populations on the Prime
- **Oath-arbitration** — where mortals find themselves bound by conflicting vows, Velveth's office mediates
- **Duty-against-love cases** — mortals who face the kind of choice she herself made come to her office for grace; she does not relieve them of the choice but **holds them as they make it**
- **Velrathi-Tarran intermarriage cases** — particularly fraught given her own history; she takes these personally
- **Cross-pantheon vow-witnessing** — when vows intersect non-Velrathi divine portfolios, Velveth co-signs
- **Bargain-honoured-in-full contracts** — the [[divine-concordat|Concordat]]-shape of treaty cross-signing has become a foundational form of serious commerce; Velveth's office handles the routing

She is not a goddess of mercy in the comforting sense. She is the goddess of **what it costs to remain in your duty when love is what you are betraying**.

### Prayer-traffic sources

Velveth's primary prayer-traffic comes from:

- **[[velrathi|Velrathi]] in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] and other exile-locations** making vows of duty-against-personal-cost
- **The [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]]** — descended from exiled Tarans who fled at the Banishment — addressing the lineage's collective shame about the Betrayal
- **Cross-cultural petitioners** facing the rare class of impossible-choice in which betrayal of a loved one is the only ethical path; these prayers cross from many peoples' offices to Velveth's, and she signs them with care
- **[[katharim|Katharim]] death-priests** at the deaths of those who died bearing impossible loyalty-conflicts
- **Modern Prime-Material peoples** — Tarran-descended humans, [[bold-republic|Bold]] merchants, [[orden|Orden]] trade-compact members, [[sarathim|Sarathim]] political figures — invoking her for bargain-honoured-in-full commercial and political agreements

## The Icon

Velveth's [[icons|Icon]] retained the appearance of Kathira: **three metres tall, ashen-gray, red-eyed, with the long gray hair Kathira had combed on the [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]]**, wearing formal Velrathi robes in **deep midnight-blue with House Valtharion sigils at the collar**. The partly-veiled face — the visible half showing *resolve-grief-steadiness held as one feeling* — is consistent across all known mortal accounts and temple-iconography.

The location of Velveth's Icon is a **Velrathi state secret**. Velveth's Icon manifested on the Prime Material at the moment of her Apotheosis, even though Kathira herself was in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] at the time. The Icon's specific location is known to the inner clergy of her cult, to whatever protective arrangement maintains the wider Velrathi pantheon's Icons on the Prime, and to Velveth herself; otherwise it is open canon. Plausible candidate locations include a sealed enclave-archive of House Valtharion, a hidden chamber in a deep-vault location maintained by remnant-Velrathi-keepers, or some other protected Prime-Material site established at the time of her Apotheosis.

**If Velveth's Icon were destroyed**: for the 100 years required to reform it, her domain of *betrayal-love-duty* would operate unruled. Bargains-honoured-in-full would lose their governing presence — *betrayals would happen without weight-bearing, loves would fracture without duty-anchoring, duties would compel without the bond-integrity that makes them bearable*. Velrathi vow-prayers would fail their cross-pantheon routing for the duration. Cross-cultural impossible-choice petitioners would face their decisions without any divine office accepting their case. The Mythos would survive this loss (Velveth's portfolio is significant but not load-bearing in the way Lirra's magic is), but the era of her absence would be characteristically chaotic in the relevant ways.

## The Office of Velrathi Affairs

Velveth's [[solarion|Solarion]] presence operates through her **simulacrum at the Office of Velrathi Affairs** on the divine main street. The simulacrum is fully conscious, fully Velveth-in-character, and fully empowered to act on her behalf in cross-pantheon institutional business. The full goddess remains in the [[hall-of-the-long-quiet|Hall of the Long Quiet]] in the outer planes; the simulacrum handles the Solarion working-day.

The building is in formal **Velrathi-Gothic style** — narrow, dark, with runes faintly glowing on the facade and the **House Valtharion sigil set above the lintel**. The office's distinctive interior chamber — a **vertical treaty-witnessing room**, stained with crimson morning light through tinted glass even when no actual morning exists in Solarion — retains the cosmological resonance of Kathira's mortal work. Modern Velrathi treaty-mages who have served at the office report that the room is partly *the night of the Concordat-drafting* still present in cosmological persistence.

The office is staffed by Velrathi clergy; Velveth's simulacrum is reachable when the matter is sufficient, but most prayer-traffic is processed by her senior staff.

### Iconography

Velveth's canonical iconography:

- **A sealed treaty-vial** in one hand — representing the architectural-discipline side of the [[divine-concordat|Concordat]] and of every bargain-honoured-in-full case she has signed since
- **A wine-cup** in the other hand — representing the instrument of the act itself, the cup of warmed wine she poured for Thoramis
- **The partly-veiled face** — representing the [[decree-of-shali-shani-ioa|Decreed silence]] about the Long Quiet and what passed through her in those six hours
- **House Valtharion sigils** at the collar — the family-and-line dimension; her duty was performed as a Vorandethi of her House and a princess of her people

The bone-handled comb of the [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]] does not appear in canonical iconography but is sometimes added in Velrathi devotional art.

### Tea: Clarity Thorn

Velveth's tea preference, maintained across her Apotheosis, is **Clarity Thorn** — the same memory-bright variety Kathira drank during her mortal life as an aid to political-architectural thinking. She drinks it daily, in the formal Velrathi tea-ceremony register, and **accepts visitors only when she has finished the day's first cup**. (Compare [[velrathi-tea-culture|the broader Velrathi tea tradition]] for the mental-acuity rationale across the people.)

## Relationship with Imma

[[imma|Imma Who-Did-Not-Stop-Laughing]] — the [[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods|Ascended Mortal]] [[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] Goddess — maintains a small office three doors down the same Solarion corridor. The two goddesses **cross-sign vow-prayers** regularly where Velrathi and Beast-Folk concerns intersect.

The two also share the **silent corridor nod** — a brief mutual acknowledgement when their paths cross. Imma's soul-thread passed through [[great-betrayal#The Coalition|Meleth Vaethari-of-the-Crimson-Spire]] — the architect who drafted the Concordat — and Imma was shown this in the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome's]] curation. She now carries the Velrathi-precision nod that Kathira would have recognised. Velveth returns the nod with a fraction of additional warmth that no spirit-clerk passing in the corridor would notice. *Neither goddess will ever speak of it. The [[decree-of-shali-shani-ioa|Decree of Shali-Shani-Ioa]] binds them both, and the silence is not a burden but a kind of intimate working language.*

The corridor nod is the canonical illustration of *Solarion thematic geography* (see [[#Thematic geography of the corridor]] below) and of *cross-pantheon working relationships that operate through silence rather than speech* (see [[baldaranic-council-cycle]]).

### Thematic geography of the corridor

The Velveth↔Imma corridor pairing is the canonical example of a recurring Solarion principle: **adjacent divine offices in Solarion often hold thematically-paired portfolios**. The corridor expresses the setting's deepest thematic tension between **memory** and **forgetting**:

- Velveth holds the Velrathi answer to catastrophic weight: *hold weight in architecture*. Treaty-vial and wine-cup, midnight-blue robes, formal Velrathi-Gothic office, the Long Quiet maintained as eternal practice
- Imma holds the Beast-Folk answer: *the daily forgetting, laughter renewing the Veil*. Wagon-wheel sigil, loaf-and-bowl on the porch, pale-wood structure, the daily refusal-to-teach-the-war-recitation

Two goddesses, three doors apart, performing the two opposite cultural answers to *how to carry catastrophic weight*. The corridor is not just bureaucratic geography; it is **thematic geography expressed as office adjacency**.

## The inseparable triplet

Velveth's portfolio is the clearest current example of an **inseparable-triplet portfolio** — a divine office that is formally inseparable, addressed always together and in a fixed order; breaking the form is to misuse the office.

This is a structural feature of certain divine offices that future canon should consider explicitly. Other likely cases include:

- **[[nox|Nox]]** — *Night, Secrecy, Hidden Truths, Loathsome Knowledge* — almost certainly an inseparable quartet
- **[[sylph|Sylph]]** — *Foolishness, Masks, Laughter, Fae, Merciful Absurdity* — possibly an inseparable five-term portfolio
- **[[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani]] as a pair** — the *two-faces-as-one-portfolio* structure operates on the same principle

When designing new divine establishment, the question is whether the portfolio is single-term, separable multi-term, or inseparable-triplet/quartet. The distinction affects liturgical practice, cross-pantheon protocol, and how the deity may be addressed in priestly work.

## Relationship with Xarvion

The political situation is complex. [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] was a member of the Velrathi nobility — though never a member of House Valtharion specifically — and Kathira knew him before his ascension and turn. Whether they were friends, rivals, or merely acquaintances in their mortal years is not recorded; Velveth does not discuss her pre-Demon-War Velrathi peers in her institutional capacity.

Xar'vion's sister [[velena|Velena]] was killed by Tarran noble youths during the Empire's degenerate decline — the catalysing event of Xar'vion's turn. Velena's death is one of the wounds in the Velrathi-Tarran relationship that the Great Betrayal was, in a deep sense, both *answer to* and *continuation of*: the Velrathi struck the Tarran imperial line at the apex partly in retribution for what Tarran nobility had done at the local scale.

Velveth presumably knew of Velena, possibly knew her personally, and certainly knew of the Tarran-noble-youth attack. The personal weight Kathira brought to the Concordat's hand-clause included this prior knowledge. The Great Betrayal was therefore *not* a purely procedural act for her — it was the specifically Velrathi response to specifically Velrathi-targeted Tarran violence, executed through specifically the right target. The Coalition asked her because *she could*. She could because *she carried*.

The relationship between Velveth's office and Xar'vion's now-Total-Bound state (see [[xarvion|Xar'vion]]) is institutional. She does not have a position on his rehabilitation or recovery; her work is too specific to that question for direct intervention to be appropriate. But she handles vow-prayers that touch his case, and the cases are not infrequent.

## Worship

Velveth has worshippers but does not actively cultivate them. Velrathi households facing duty-vs-love crises commonly petition her. Velrathi marriages are sometimes witnessed by her office (via cross-signing with the relevant local clergy). Some Velrathi names — especially given to daughters in difficult eras — explicitly honour her. The pre-Banishment Velrathi homeland traditions are largely lost, but the post-Banishment Velrathi diaspora carries continued Velveth-veneration in scattered forms.

She is also worshipped by some non-Velrathi mortals who face duty-vs-love crises — Tarran families with Velrathi marriage histories, [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] sworn members navigating the Oath's lifelong bind, Bold civic-magistrates whose work requires personal cost, and others. Cross-pantheon veneration is common enough that her office's cross-signing volume is substantial.

## Open canon

- Did **Kathira have surviving children** from any pre-engagement liaison, or did she enter the formal engagement to Thoramis childless? Are there surviving collateral Valtharion descendants in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] who carry portion of her bloodline?
- What was Velveth's **exact relationship with Meleth Vaethari-of-the-Crimson-Spire** across the years they overlapped in Veyr'Keth as mortal-and-goddess?
- Does Velveth ever **directly intervene in mortal Velrathi affairs**, or does she observe the strict [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]? Is there a documented case of her stepping outside that doctrine?
- Is **Thoramis Firethorn's soul** somewhere in the cycle now, having reincarnated through [[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani's]] offices, or did his death's circumstance produce some unusual disposition?
- The **relationship between Velveth's portfolio and [[daj|Daj's]]** (war) — both touch the domain of *cost-of-victory*. Do they cross-sign?
- The "Necessary Hand" / "Red-Eyed Widow" / "Betrayer Queen" tradition — does Velveth herself prefer a particular name?
- Are there **mortal Velrathi cults of Velveth** with formal priesthood, or does her worship remain entirely individual-vow-based?
- The **complete known catalogue** of Velrathi title-stems beyond Vorand- (see [[naming-conventions#Velrathi title morphology]])
- Did Vorandar Narand of House Valtharion **survive long enough** in Veyr'Keth to witness his daughter's Apotheosis (i.e. to notice she had ceased being seen in the corridors)?
- The **specific location** of Velveth's Icon on the Prime Material
- The location and maintenance arrangement for the **Velrathi pantheon's Icons** collectively, given the Banishment
- What **other Velrathi gods exist** alongside Velveth in Solarion (Vexara the Boneweaver is named; the rest is open)
- What **[[the-three|the Three]]** (Mark, Rand, Melania) think of her Ascension — she was their creation's most pivotal historical figure
