# Draconic Servitor Races

**Summary**: The reptilian humanoid peoples engineered by [[dragons|dragons]] — [[kobolds|kobolds]], [[lizardfolk|lizardfolk]], [[snake-folk|snake folk]], and the [[dragonborn|dragonborn]] champions. They are **not** descended from the three humanoid progenitors ([[elfen|Elfen]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], [[tarans|Tarran]]); they are a separate creation-tradition, reshaped from dragon-derived flesh and made ontologically real by bargain with the ascended dragon-gods (~30,000 BP), bound by an unbreakable **divine Mandate** to purpose and to a capped number. They are the only humanoid-shaped peoples in Eadras outside the progenitor family tree.

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt; Dragons.txt; draconic-servitor-races.txt (2026-06-02 ingest — creation genealogy, castes, and Mandate); the-three-amendment.txt.

## What they are

The draconic servitor races are present-day living peoples of the Prime Material. They were engineered by [[dragons|dragons]], **sanctioned by the ascended dragon-gods** (the line of the [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Nine Embers of Renewal]]), and they sit entirely outside the [[the-three|progenitor genealogy]]. Because they were dragon-business rather than progenitor-business, they were **not caught in the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]]** that removed the three progenitor races; they remained.

They are **latecomers** — made ~30,000 BP, younger than essentially the entire humanoid family tree. Among the ancient humanoid lineages they are "the young peoples," made during the long golden age, by dragon-gods, under divine contract.

## Creation — how dragons made them

Dragons **could not perform from-scratch creation.** That capacity belongs only to [[the-three|The Three That Are One]] (and to [[dao|Dao]] above them), who learned it directly from Dao and carry extramythic resonance. Dragons are themselves a Source-kindled species (see [[ixthal|Ixthal]] and the Forbidden Kindling), not a creator-lineage. What they did instead:

1. **They observed** The Three perform from-scratch creation — knowledge by witnessing, the seed of the idea, not the power to copy it.
2. **They reshaped their own flesh** through magic and alchemy, sculpting new reptilian humanoid body-types out of dragon-derived stock. (This differs from the progenitors' method — who blood-engineered their *own offspring* in the womb across generations — but sits at the same mortal-capable tier: form-shaping, not from-scratch creation.)
3. They could shape the **form** but not make it ontologically **real** — sapient, lineage-bearing, a true race rather than a magically altered dragon. Crossing that genesis threshold is god-gated (the Edict of Closed Genesis / [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]).
4. So dragons made a **bargain** with the ascended mortal dragon-gods to make the forms real — sanctioned creation, openly, by divine treaty.

This is the **opposite** of [[ixthal|Ixthal's]] concealed theft of genesis: two roads to the same forbidden act. Ixthal *stole* genesis and hides it forever; the dragons *bought* genesis by treaty and it is on the record. (A quiet irony: the dragon-gods who sanctioned this are themselves the product of Ixthal's illicit kindling — the illicitly-made became the legitimate makers.)

## The price of the genesis-bargain

The dragon-gods made the forms real on two conditions, both woven into what the races *are* and enforced by **divine Mandate** — the races cannot break them:

- **Bound to purpose.** Each race is divinely fixed to a designed function (see the castes, below).
- **Capped number.** The total real population of the draconic races is bounded.

## The four castes

The races were each engineered for a specific role in dragon-service — mind, body, will, and champion.

| Caste | Race | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Mind / mouth** | [[kobolds\|Kobolds]] | Interface, intellect, diplomacy, infrastructure — the voice through which elder dragons touch the humanoid world; constitutionally carry partial [[draconic-language\|Draconic]]-resonance compatibility. |
| **Body** | [[lizardfolk\|Lizardfolk]] | Labor, lair-defense, physical strength — the muscle of the order. |
| **Will / magic** | [[snake-folk\|Snake folk]] | Engineered as superior spellcasters — the arcane caste. The race that partially [[snake-folk#The schism|schismed]]. |
| **Champion** | [[dragonborn\|Dragonborn]] | Categorically different — not a population caste but individually-forged, soul-linked dragon-champions. See below. |

### Baseline social condition

The draconic servitor races mostly live **harmoniously** in their designed roles. Bound-to-purpose is experienced as belonging and place, not as a chain. They live in integrated communities around dragon lairs and at [[ratharil|Ratharil]]. Outliers exist — individuals living among other humanoid peoples or off on their own — but they are the exception; the baseline is contentment-in-purpose. This baseline is load-bearing: it is what makes the [[snake-folk|snake-folk]] schism an *aberration requiring explanation* rather than the natural response to a shared misery. The contentment of the many is what makes the breakaway few meaningful.

## The Mandate

The Mandate governs the three working castes (kobolds, lizardfolk, snake folk). It is **divine, not a treaty — unbreakable by the races themselves**, and distinct from the [[#The Treaty of Ratharil|Treaty of Ratharil]] that governs dragon numbers.

- **Pooled across all draconic races.** Each living dragon anchors up to **100** draconic-race beings *total*, shared across kobolds/lizardfolk/snake folk (not 100 of each). With the dragon soft-cap just under a million, the total draconic-race ceiling is therefore around **~100 million**, shared and (in practice) contested across the working castes.
- **The ceiling breathes** with dragon numbers: more living dragons = more anchored capacity; fewer dragons = lower ceiling.
- The races are **egg-layers**, and the Mandate gates egg-**quickening**: an egg may be laid, but whether it quickens into a real, sapient, lineage-bearing member of the race is gated against available dragon-anchored allotment.
- **Over-cap eggs wait.** When allotment is full, viable eggs enter a patient holding state — they do not spoil or die. They quicken later when allotment frees (a new dragon hatches and adds its 100; capacity otherwise opens). A population can rebound from a backlog of waiting clutches as dragon numbers recover, not only from fresh laying.
- **Death-tether.** When a dragon dies, its anchored allotment *stalls* — no further eggs quicken against that anchor — but already-living bound individuals are **unharmed** (the dragon's death does not kill them), and waiting eggs are unharmed (they simply wait against the general pool of remaining dragon-capacity).

## Dragonborn — the champions

[[dragonborn|Dragonborn]] are categorically different from the three working castes: not pooled, not mandate-bred, but **individually forged**, the most draconic of the dragon-made beings — half-dragon, half-humanoid, with shapeshifting wings; dragon-might made ambulatory in the humanoid world. Each is forged by an alchemical process from a single working-caste egg (kobold, lizardfolk, *or* snake folk) cloaked in dragon eggshell, into which the dragon binds a **sliver of its own soul**. They sit outside the Mandate entirely. Full mechanics — the lent-not-spent soul-link, the count-as-statement-of-might, and the captured-fragment vulnerability — are on the [[dragonborn|dragonborn page]].

## The Treaty of Ratharil

The **[[ratharil|Treaty of Ratharil]]** — the dragon population treaty — is a *separate instrument* from the Mandate, and must not be conflated with it.

|  | **Treaty of Ratharil** | **The Mandate** |
|---|---|---|
| **Type** | A *treaty* — dragons signed it with each other; a political instrument internal to dragonkind | A *divine mandate* — imposed by the dragon-gods as a condition of the races' existence |
| **Binds** | Dragon population only (soft cap **999,999**) | The reptilian working castes (pooled ~100M) |
| **Signed** | ~5 million years BP (deep dragon antiquity, roughly the unicorn-flare era — long before the reptilian races existed) | ~30,000 BP, with the genesis-bargain |
| **Breakable?** | **Yes** — dragons can violate their own treaty (an expansionist faction such as the [[flesh-eaters|Flesh-Eaters]] could repudiate it) | **No** — woven into what the races are; inescapable |

The structural contrast is the point: dragons bound *themselves* with a **breakable** treaty (their own choice, revocable); the reptilian races were bound by an **unbreakable** divine mandate they never chose. A people practiced for ~5 million years in the discipline of capped numbers built that same logic into their creations — but in the harder, unbreakable form. **They gave their creations the cage they themselves only ever agreed to wear.** Both the cap and Ratharil are voluntary corrections to the dragon instinct of *War → Recovery → Stability → Overcrowding → War* (cf. [[principles-of-eadras|the principle that institutions are corrections to instinct]]).
