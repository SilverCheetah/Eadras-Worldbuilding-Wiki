# Tomb of the Last Guardians

**Summary**: The Tomb of the Last Guardians is the [[katharim|Katharim]]-maintained memorial barrow at the edge of the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] battlefield within the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] — a vast empty barrow honoring the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]] (Orug, El'mer, and Aetherin), filled not with bodies (none remained) but with thousands of empty armor stands engraved with their names.

## Location

The Tomb stands at the edge of the **sacred scar** within the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] where the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] was fought. The battlefield itself is a vast field of fused black glass and shattered stone where no grass grows.

The Tomb is administratively part of [[kharathen|Kharathen]] — the [[katharim|Katharim]] necropolis at the western edge of the Marches — but it is treated as a separate sacred site.

## What It Contains

The Tomb is a **simple, massive barrow** containing **no bodies** — there were none left to bury after the demonic forces consumed the dead.

Instead, it contains:

- **Thousands of empty gray armor stands** for the [[orugi|Orug]] dead, each engraved with a name and the words *"We Guard Still"*
- **Thousands of empty wooden plinths** for the [[el-mer|El'mer]] dead, each carved from petrified wood with thorn-vines silvered into the surface, bearing a name and a single line of song
- **Thousands of empty steel-and-bronze armor stands** for the [[aetherins|Aetherin]] dead, each missing its spear (spears have been donated by visiting human warriors over ten thousand years), each bearing a name and the line *"Made to break the storm. Held when no one came."*

The shared epitaph carved on the Tomb wall reads:

> *"Reinforcements never came. We held anyway."*

## The Builders

The [[katharim|Katharim]] built the Tomb in the first centuries after the [[demon-war|Demon War]]'s end, as part of the founding effort that created [[kharathen|Kharathen]]. The barrow's architecture is austere even by Katharim standards — gray stone, no ornamentation, no figurative carving except the three statues of the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]] that flank the entrance.

The interior is **kept perfectly dry, cool, and still**. [[katharim|Katharim]] priests rotate through the Tomb on continuous vigil, naming the dead aloud in soft murmurs throughout the day and night.

## Pilgrimage

The Tomb is one of the most heavily visited pilgrimage sites in Eadras. Visitors include:

- **[[orcs|Orcs]]** — leaving offerings of broken weapons and oaths of protection at the [[orugi|Orug]] plinths; many orc clans send a delegation annually
- **Modern Elves** — leaving fresh-cut greens or living seedlings at the [[el-mer|El'mer]] plinths
- **Humans** — donating spears to the [[aetherins|Aetherin]] stands; many knightly orders make pilgrimage as part of their oath ceremonies
- **[[beast-folk|Beast-folk]]** — bringing colorful offerings, sometimes performing [[flyting|flytings]] outside the Tomb (never inside)
- **Hidden pure [[rathari|Rathar]]** — sending sealed missives to be read aloud at the site
- **Exiled [[tarans|Tarran]]** envoys — when rare [[grand-conjunction|conjunction-rifts]] open into [[gaia|Gaian]] reflections

## Joint Ceremonies

The Tomb is one of the few places where every descendant tradition gathers. **Beast-folk and Katharim hold joint ceremonies** at the threshold — gray-robed priests murmuring inside while colorful flyters dance and sing outside, neither tradition interrupting the other.

The annual [[orcs|Orc]] festival of the **Night of the Gray Wall** culminates at the Tomb, where the names of the [[orugi|Orug]] dead are recited collectively by ash-marked orcs and beast-folk together.

## Magical Resistance

Like the rest of [[kharathen|Kharathen]], the Tomb's defenses are **purely divine and architectural** — no arcane wards. Yet the Tomb resists demonic corruption more strongly than nearly any other site in Eadras. Some scholars believe the **metaphysical resonance** of the unified sacrifice itself protects the site, anchored by ten thousand years of remembered names.

[[lirra|Lirra]]'s priesthood quietly teaches that the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] itself is **slightly stronger** within the Tomb's walls — as though the **Three Who Stood are still, in some small way, holding the line**.

## A Common Phrase

The phrase from the Tomb's epitaph — *"Reinforcements never came. We held anyway."* — has entered general Eadras speech as an idiom meaning **stubborn, doomed loyalty in the face of betrayal**. It is one of the few pieces of Tomb-language that has spread well beyond pilgrimage culture.

## Summary

> The Tomb of the Last Guardians does not hold the dead. It holds the **fact of their dying**, made visible: thousands of empty stands waiting for the bodies that were already gone.

> In its echoing silence, the Three Who Stood are still standing — not in flesh, but in the unbroken memory of the people they died to save.
