# Goblin Ancestor Liquor

**Summary**: The traditional drink of the [[goblins|Goblins]] is a harsh, high-proof spirit with a pungent, acrid taste â€” unmistakably brimstone, tar, pitch, and scorched-earth notes â€” produced from a recipe inherited from their [[grugi|Grug]] ancestors who brewed it deep underground while maintaining vast magical machinery. Goblins know exactly how nasty outsiders find it, and embrace it. In their own language the drink is called something equivalent to *Nasty Ancestor Liquor*.

## Origin in the Grug

The recipe is older than goblin civilization. The [[grugi|Grug]] â€” created by the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] specifically to handle heavy physical labor and engineering on the Empire's vast magical infrastructure â€” lived and worked **deep underground**, maintaining the machinery and wards that kept Velrathi magitek running. They brewed their own alcohol down there, and the alcohol came out tasting like the environment it was made in: chemical, biting, brimstone-heavy, with the resinous tar-and-pitch character of the maintenance materials they worked with daily.

It was not designed to be unpleasant. It simply *developed* unpleasant â€” by the standards of any race that did not live underground in a magical machine-shop â€” because it absorbed the character of the place where it was brewed and the materials its makers handled. Over generations, the Grug's palate adapted to it and began to prefer it.

When the [[grugi|Grug]] line shattered in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] and gave rise to the [[goblins|Goblins]] (and the [[gnomes|Gnomes]] â€” see [[lost-primer]]), the goblins inherited both the recipe and the inherited taste-preference. They have made it more or less the same way ever since.

## Flavor profile (to outsiders)

To most other races, Goblin Ancestor Liquor tastes and smells like a **mixture of brimstone, tar, pitch, and scorched earth**. It has a sharp, biting, almost sulfurous quality that makes the eyes water on first encounter. The aftertaste lingers long enough that newcomers sometimes have difficulty eating immediately afterward.

It is **not** comparable to the industrial-fuel taste descriptions modern Earth might reach for; the magical-industrial-revolution character of Eadras's pre-Cataclysm civilization, and of the modern goblin recovery of magitek, means the closest reference points are **natural unpleasantness** (brimstone vents, tar pits, pitch-pine resin, scorched earth after a wildfire) rather than refined-petroleum products.

## Flavor profile (to goblins)

To goblins, it tastes like **home, heritage, and hard work**. The biting brimstone is the bite of a job well done; the tar-and-pitch is the smell of the workshop; the scorched-earth aftertaste is the satisfaction of having put in real labor. A goblin who has been working all day will sit down with a small cup of Ancestor Liquor and feel that the day's exertion has been properly closed off.

This is not goblin self-deception or contrarianism. They genuinely enjoy it. The Grug's underground palate is a real inherited preference, not a performance of toughness.

## Naming

In the goblin language, the drink's name translates to something equivalent to:

> *Nasty Ancestor Liquor* â€” or more loosely, *that nasty shit the ancestors drank.*

This is the name a goblin would use casually among other goblins. It is not insulting; it is **affectionate**. The drink's nasty character is part of what makes it the ancestral drink rather than some wishy-washy foreign substitute. Goblins are perfectly aware of how outsiders react to it and find that reaction faintly amusing.

When a goblin merchant sells the drink in non-goblin markets it is typically labelled in a more polite translation â€” variants on *Goblin Reserve*, *Underdeep Spirit*, or simply the producer's family name â€” but the casual goblin-internal name is "Nasty Ancestor Liquor" and that is what the drink is in goblin culture.

A formal Common-tongue cultural term has not been settled. Goblins are mildly amused at outside attempts to give it dignified branding.

## Production today

Modern goblin distilleries make the drink the way the Grug did, with the materials they have. The brewing environment is canonically deep, hot, and shared with magical-industrial activity â€” typically the same spaces in which goblin foundries, magitek workshops, and forge-machines operate. The ambient atmosphere of those places contributes to the spirit's character; a Goblin Ancestor Liquor brewed in a sterile clean-room would not, canonically, taste right.

This makes the drink **deeply place-rooted**. Goblin-run cities each have their own house variants of the spirit, depending on which workshops hosted the still and what materials were being worked nearby. Goblin connoisseurs (who exist, even if other races find it hard to imagine) can tell the producing city by the second sip.

## As [[aqua-vitae|Aqua Vitae]] base

Further distillation produces a goblin Aqua Vitae that retains the brimstone-and-tar fingerprint of the source. It is used in alchemy where a *biting* solvent serves the working â€” particularly forge-magic, smelting-related potions, and certain combat alchemy. (Detailed alchemical applications are open canon.) See [[aqua-vitae]].

## Cultural role

Ancestor Liquor is the goblin **after-work drink**. Goblins like being clean, fed, and surrounded by friends and family at the end of a working day, and a small cup of Ancestor Liquor is a standard part of that wind-down (see [[bathing-customs#Goblins]] for the bath that typically precedes it). It is also the drink of choice for **mourning** â€” goblins drink Ancestor Liquor at funerals because the drink itself is a connection to the ancestors being mourned.

It is **not** the drink of choice for **negotiations with outsiders**. Goblin merchants doing business with non-goblins typically substitute foreign alcohol for hospitality purposes, on the basis that subjecting a counterparty to Ancestor Liquor is more cruelty than business deserves. There are recorded cases of goblin negotiators winning unexpectedly favorable contract terms after a non-goblin counterparty insisted on the goblin drink and had to keep up appearances afterward.
