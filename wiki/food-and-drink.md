# Food and Drink

**Summary**: Cultural hub for the food and drink traditions of Eadras — a working index of cuisine, ingredients, brewing, table customs, and food-borne folklore across the races. Sections are added as canon develops; current focus is gnomish cuisine, dwarven brewing, and the long inter-race feud they share.

**Sources**: Gnomish Food Culture.txt, Gnomish Prank v Dwarven Retort.txt, Pre-empire foodways.txt, Inki.txt, Extra large grok chat 5-9-2026.txt, cheese-cultures-and-the-three-civilizational-ethics.md (2026-05-13 ingest — [[tchoukou]] and [[goblin-tunnel-cheese]]).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-13

## Scope

This page is a **hub**, not a closed reference. Race-specific food and drink lore lives on the race pages; this page collects the cross-cultural threads (shared dishes, trade in ingredients, ritual meals, prank-and-retort feuds) and points to the race-specific sections.

Planned race coverage as canon expands:
- [[elfen|Elfen]] foodways — seed in [[pre-empire-foodways]]; modern descendants pending; signature drink: [[elfen-blossom-wines|blossom wines]] aged in sung living-wood barrels
- [[tarans|Tarran]] foodways — seed in [[pre-empire-foodways]]; modern descendants pending; signature drink: [[taran-venom-aged-liquor|venom-aged liquor]]
- [[velrathi|Velrathi]] foodways — seed in [[pre-empire-foodways]]; modern descendants pending; **no alcohol** — Velrathi treat alcohol as cognitively detrimental; primary cultural beverage is [[velrathi-tea-culture|tea]]
- [[gnomes|Gnomish]] cuisine — see below and [[gnomes#Cuisine|Gnomes — Cuisine]]
- [[goblins|Goblin]] alcohol — [[goblin-ancestor-liquor|Ancestor Liquor]], the harsh brimstone-and-tar high-proof spirit inherited from [[grugi|Grug]] underground brewing
- [[orcs|Orcish]] drinks — [[orcish-drinks|Saddle Brew + Stonefire]], split between the nomadic and settled halves of orcish life
- [[orden|Orden]] mead — [[orden-whale-honey-mead|Whale-Honey Mead]], harvested cooperatively with the [[merrow|Marrow]] from northern-sea whales
- [[dwarves|Dwarven]] brewing — see below and [[dwarves#Brewing|Dwarves — Brewing]]
- Other races as written

**Universal alchemical foundation**: [[aqua-vitae]] is the universal alchemical solvent and catalyst that underlies most potion-craft on Eadras. It is distilled from each race's signature alcoholic drink (the Velrathi being the only major-race exception, since they do not drink), and each race's Aqua Vitae retains characteristics of its source.

## Cheese traditions

Cheese is one of the foodways that most clearly distinguishes civilizational ethics on Eadras. The two cheeses canonized so far come from opposite poles of the [[three-cultures-ethical-triangle|three-cultures ethical triangle]]:

- [[tchoukou|Tchoukou]] — the signature hard cheese of the [[amunorians|Amunorian]] caravan and pastoral hinterland; camel-and-goat blend, sun-dried, indefinitely shelf-stable, stamped with the imperial seal in basic / spiced / aged grades; the caloric backbone of every Amunorian garrison, caravan, and oasis-city tea-house.
- [[goblin-tunnel-cheese|Goblin tunnel cheese]] — a brie-blue hybrid aged in damp working tunnels with whatever native cave-bacteria happen to live there; inherently regional (each tunnel produces a distinguishable batch); prized by goblins as a delicacy, regarded by most surface-dwellers as cursed, with a small but reliable connoisseur market across the species gap.

The [[dragon-empire|Dragon Empire's]] own foodways — including whatever the Empire's own administered cheese tradition looks like under its skill-rank lattice — remain open canon.

## The Vault's Gift — origin of gnomish cuisine

The defining event of gnomish food culture was the recovery of an [[arathi|Arathi]] vault — a substantial complex including a **food fabricator**, a [[magitek|magitek]] / [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]] laboratory, living quarters, storerooms, and other features. (User direction 2026-05-01; see [[inki#Legend — the First Vault]].) After the gnomes finally translated the vault's script — a project of decades — they realized the food fabricator had been running in **low-power survival mode** ever since the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]], and that even in survival mode it could still **dispense seeds**.

Over the following generations, gnomes cultivated dozens of previously unknown crops, herbs, and spices from a single regional Arathi cuisine. They could never fully replicate the original cooking — that cuisine relied heavily on **the meat of giant insects that no longer exist** — but the new ingredients transformed gnomish food culture from basic medieval fare into the **richly spiced, complex** tradition it is today. (source: Gnomish Food Culture.txt)

The first gnome to breach the vault was, by tradition, **[[inki|Inki]]** — later the Ascended Mortal God of invention. See [[inki|Inki]].

### Raven's eye

Inki's first ingredient test from the recovered cookbook was a small, glossy black pepper about a fingernail in size, listed only as **"raven's eye."** The cookbook described it as *pleasantly warm*. Inki popped one in his mouth expecting something mild. **It nearly blew his head off.** That single pepper became the spark of the gnomish spice revolution.

To this day, tasting a raw raven's eye is the **traditional first test** given to any young gnome who wants to become a master chef. Successful candidates are not those who don't react — everyone reacts — but those who keep their composure and finish the bite without disgracing the kitchen. (source: Gnomish Food Culture.txt)

## Brewing a Hot Batch — the gnomish brewery prank

For centuries, bold young gnomes have run a recurring prank on dwarven brewers. Every few years a small party will sneak into a dwarven brewery and quietly empty a small bag of their own brewing spices — **cinnamon, cardamom, star anise, and (almost as an afterthought) a single paper-thin sliver of dried raven's eye** — into one of the sealed vats.

To a gnome, this is simply how beer is supposed to taste.
To a dwarf, it is an outrage.

When the batch is tapped weeks later, the dwarves discover their honest, malty stout transformed into something aromatic, complex, and dangerously warm. The first dwarf to drink it usually lets out a string of curses that echoes through the entire hall.

- **Dwarves call it**: *"gnome-tainted swill."*
- **Gnomes call it**: *"improvement."*

(source: Gnomish Prank v Dwarven Retort.txt)

## Palebone Clay — the dwarven retort

The dwarven counter is neither quick nor cheap. After one too many ruined batches, a forge master personally permitted a small amount of **Palebone Clay** — a rare white absorbent clay normally reserved for the **highest-quality metalwork** — to be used outside its proper trade. The brewers waited until a large vat of gnomish-spiked beer was sealed and fermenting, then added the clay with the forge master's blessing.

Palebone Clay is **so powerfully absorbent** that even a modest sack can strip most of the flavor and color from an entire brewing vat. When that vat was tapped, the vibrant spiced beer had been reduced to a **pale, watery shadow of itself** — mildly spiced, but so weak it was barely recognizable as beer.

The forge master considered the sacrifice of his rare clay a fair price for justice. The retort has remained on the table ever since. To this day, when a dwarf sees a gnome looking particularly pleased with himself, he will simply grin and say:

> *"We still have more clay."*

(source: Gnomish Prank v Dwarven Retort.txt)
