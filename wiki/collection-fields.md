# Collection Fields

**Summary**: Every living creature on Eadras has an unconscious **collection field** — a region around it that passively gathers ambient magical energy. Field size **scales with the creature's power**, producing a slow positive feedback: more power grows the field, a larger field gathers more, and the gathering feeds growth in both power and wisdom. At the far ceiling of that loop lies the **structural threshold of [[apotheosis|Ascension]]** — the capacity-side of a transition whose deciding act remains **designation**, not accumulation. The phenomenon is **not widely known**; no mortal — not even a dragon — has yet studied it methodically. Its most dramatic known instance is the [[unicorns|unicorn]] herd-mind, where the field-merge of a quorum of bodies appears to be what hosts the mind at all.

## The mechanism

Every living creature maintains, without awareness or effort, a **collection field**: a volume around the body within which ambient magical energy is slowly drawn in and metabolised. The field is not a spell, not a practice, and not a choice — it is as native to a living thing as breath, and as invisible to its owner.

Two facts govern the field's behaviour:

1. **Field size scales with power.** A field mouse's field barely exceeds its skin. A practiced [[netharim|Netharim]]'s field fills a room. An elder [[dragons|dragon]]'s field is measured in territory.
2. **The scaling runs both directions.** More power grows the field; a grown field gathers more; the gathering, integrated over a life, feeds growth in power — and, in beings that reflect on what they carry, in wisdom. More power → more wisdom → more power → more wisdom.

The loop is slow — decades and centuries, not days — and for nearly every being it stabilises long before its ceiling: bodies age, attention wanders, lives end. But the loop itself has no internal brake.

### Distinct from the mana toll

The collection field is not the same phenomenon as the [[eldritch#Mana — the toll matter takes|mana toll]]. The toll is **passive**: all matter, living or inert, absorbs a small residue of the Eldritch flowing through it, accumulating by size, complexity, and food-chain position. The collection field is a property of **living beings only**, scales with the being's *power* rather than its mass, and *gathers* energy from around the body rather than merely keeping a residue of what passes through. A great whale is mana-dense by the toll's laws; an archmage's field outgathers the whale's by orders of magnitude. The two mechanisms compound in powerful living creatures — which is part of why elder dragons read, to every form of magical perception at once, as small weather systems.

## The ceiling — collection fields and Apotheosis

At the loop's far ceiling, a soul's field has grown to the point where the being stands at the **structural threshold of [[apotheosis|Ascension]]**.

This must be stated precisely, because it is easy to misread as a recipe. **It is not one.** The collection-field ceiling is the *capacity side* of Apotheosis — the threshold a living soul must structurally stand at. But Apotheosis itself remains **designation over acquisition** (Principle 17 — see [[apotheosis#Designation, not acquisition]]): the cosmology recognises *a specific living soul whose specific life has produced a required portfolio*. The field-feedback loop explains why candidates are rare, why they are old in power and wisdom both, and why no amount of raw hoarded power has ever forced the legitimate path — capacity is necessary scaffolding and never sufficient cause. A being could, in principle, ride the loop to its ceiling and stand at the threshold forever, unacknowledged, because nothing in their life produced what designation selects for.

(The [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] is the known violent exception to the designation requirement, and its wielders' fates are the standing argument for why the requirement exists.)

## Obscurity

The phenomenon is **not widely known**. No mortal tradition — not the [[order-sharanel|Order]], not the [[katharim|Katharim]] archives, not even draconic scholarship, whose subjects live long enough to feel the loop personally — has yet studied collection fields methodically. Fragments of the truth survive in proverb form (*power gathers; the old grow strange and strong*) without anyone having named the mechanism.

The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] records it because the Tome observes what mortals have not yet measured.

## The unicorn instance — field-merge and the quorum

The most dramatic known expression of collection fields is the [[unicorns|unicorn]] herd-mind.

A herd-mind, though non-corporeal, **unconsciously increases the field size of its member bodies** — the collective is a more powerful being than any body, and the scaling law follows the collective. And the dependency runs the other way too: the **quorum floor** of unicorn biology (~8 bodies; see [[unicorn-biology#The quorum floor]]) appears to be the minimum number of bodies whose **overlapping fields can merge into one self-sustaining field large enough to host a mind**. Below the merge threshold, there is nothing for the herd-mind to live *in*; above it, the merged field and the mind it hosts sustain each other.

The unicorn case suggests the field is not merely a feeding structure but a **substrate** — something a mind can inhabit. What that implies for other collective or distributed beings on Eadras is unexplored.

## Open canon

- Whether collection fields interact with the [[seven-conduits|conduit-pools]] — does a standing pool of Eldritch feed resident creatures' fields faster, and is that part of why power concentrates in conduit cities?
- Whether any being has **noticed** the loop and deliberately cultivated it — and whether such cultivation is distinguishable from ordinary practice from the outside.
- Whether the **field-merge substrate** seen in unicorn herds has parallels in other collective beings (awakened animal circles, [[trolls|trollish]] geobiological networks, spirit assemblages).
- Whether field size can **shrink** — through trauma, severance, or deliberate ascetic refusal — and what that does to the being.
