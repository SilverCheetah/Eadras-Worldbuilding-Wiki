# Beast-Folk

**Summary**: The Beast-Folk are the **Laughing Third** — descendants of the [[rathari|Rathar]] who begged [[sylph|Sylph]] for the Veil of Forgetting during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] and were transmuted into a single unified people of anthropomorphic mammals, birds, and amphibians whose culture is built around deliberate joy, communal laughter, and the sacred refusal of perfect memory.

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Playground generation 2nd part.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt, May 1 revisions.txt, Commentary Ingest — Demon War, Chaos Crusades, Demons, and Aberrations.txt; user direction 2026-05-06 (Sylph primary, Qoxli assist); Extra large grok chat 5-9-2026.txt (lifestyle deepening, communal bath, generational acculturation)

## Origin

Beast-folk are not a divinely created race in the conventional sense, nor are they corrupted animals or war-shaped refugees. They are **one people** — *not scattered adaptations, but a single, deliberate act of divine mercy wrapped in laughter* (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

Ten thousand years ago, when one-third of the [[rathari|Rathar]] lay dead in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] and the survivors' perfect memories had become instruments of torture, [[sylph|Sylph]] answered the plea of another third: ***"Let us forget enough to live."***

The transmutation was not gentle scholarship refined into something new. It was **ecstatic, chaotic, and final**. The **Veil of Forgetting** settled over them like warm fog at dawn — dulling the endless replay of horror while sharpening every heartbeat of the present. They emerged as the Beast-Folk: unified in blood, origin, and the quiet knowledge that they chose joy over perfect recall.

[[sylph|Sylph]] was the **primary deity** of the transmutation; the Veil and the laughter were her hand. **[[qoxli|Qoxli]] assisted** — her wild magic protected the petitioners' biological coherence under the demonic ecological pressure of the war zones, keeping the canvas alive long enough for Sylph's working to land cleanly. The two are best friends among the Elder Gods; the Beast-Folk are their joint work, with Sylph as creator and Qoxli as enabler. (User direction 2026-05-06.)

They call themselves the **Laughing Third**, the **Veiled**, or simply **Sylph's Kin**.

See [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar]] for the full schism account.

### A consciously chosen lightness, then an inherited one

The Beast-Folk's emotional openness is **not** something done to them. The first generation **chose to give up** their [[demon-war|Demon War]] traumatic memories to [[sylph|Sylph]] — a willing, collective surrender, not a divine extraction. The first generation made the choice as an act of survival and healing: looking at the unbearable weight of perfect memory and saying *we will not carry this anymore*.

For the first generation, the resulting lightness was **a deliberate act**. Their laughter, their hugging, their constant expressiveness all carried the weight of a conscious decision made by people who remembered what they had given up.

Every generation since has been **born into the deliberately-joyful civilization that the first generation built in the war's aftermath**. They never had the lost memories themselves; they grew up with lightness as the default state of being. For modern Beast-Folk, joy and openness are simply *normal* — not a healing choice, not defiance, just the cultural water they swim in.

This produces a subtle but real layered character. The first generation chose lightness as survival. Their descendants live it as inheritance. Both are real Beast-Folk. The civilization is the result of the founders' decision; the citizens are the result of the civilization.

## Core Philosophy

Their cultures, while regionally flavored by ecology, share one **unbreakable core**:

> *Life is too short and too sweet to waste on what cannot be changed. Memory is a tool, not a chain. Laughter is sacred. Forgetting is holy.*

## Forms

The transmutation gave the Laughing Third forms drawn from animal kin: **mammalian** (wolf, cat, bear, fox, deer, bovine, rat, bat), **avian** (hawk, raven, owl, crane, songbird, vulture, parrot), and **amphibian** (frog, salamander, newt, toad). These forms are inherited through bloodline, not chosen — a mixed Laughing Circle naturally contains many kinds.

## Social Structure — Laughing Circles

Beast-folk organize around **Laughing Circles** — fluid, instinct-driven communities of 30 to 300 souls rather than rigid clans or scholarly conclaves. A Circle forms and dissolves organically: a bear-folk blacksmith, a raven storyteller, three frog-kin healers, and a dozen mixed-blood kits might share a hearth for a season because their instincts simply fit.

Leadership is **situational and joyous** — the one whose laugh rings clearest in crisis, or whose instinct proves sharpest that day, speaks for the Circle.

The Laughing Circles are the joyous inversion of the pre-war Rathar **[[rathari|Thought Conclaves]]**. Where Conclaves valued hierarchy, perfect memory, and ritual debate, Circles value instinct, present-focused joy, and play.

### Feast Debates

Decisions happen in **Feast Debates**: food is passed, stories told, arguments made through song, dance, or playful mock-combat. Grudges are rare — the Veil makes old slights feel distant and ridiculous.

### Families and The Change

Families are tight-knit but expansive — kits are raised by the whole Circle. **The Change** (a periodic restlessness every few years) is honored as sacred. When it strikes an adult, the Circle throws a farewell feast, gives them what they need, and welcomes them back changed or not at all. This mirrors halfling ways but feels wilder, more instinctive (see [[halflings]]).

### Inter-Circle Relations

Warm and competitive. **Great Moots** occur at solstices where Circles converge for weeks of feasting, games, and ritual forgetting. Outsiders are welcomed with boisterous hospitality — *"You carry old weights? Leave them at the fire; we have enough laughter for all."*

## Flyting — The Sacred Insult Duel

The Laughing Third's primary form of dispute resolution is [[flyting]] — ritualized, poetic insult contests sung or spoken in rhythmic verse. Few disputes reach blows or long silences; most dissolve in the shared hilarity of a well-fought verbal duel. Flyting also appears in courtship, trade, parenting, and even sacred matters affecting the whole Circle.

See [[flyting]] for the full tradition.

## Housing

Beast-folk settlements are **joyous hybrids** of lost Rathar precision and Sylph's wild exuberance. Underground halls carved by ancestors are kept but transformed: walls once lined with silent rune-shelves now blaze with **colorful murals, hanging lanterns shaped like laughing masks, and living vines that bloom only when songs are sung**.

Surface dwellings vary by region:
- **Marsh stilt-villages** with roofs of woven living reeds that whistle in the wind
- **Forest tree-nest clusters** where platforms connect by rope bridges hung with bells and colorful banners
- **Hillside burrow-cities** whose entrances are painted in riotous patterns so no two look alike

Every home has a **Forgetful Hearth** at its center — a firepit or glowing crystal where small personal relics of the "before" (a cracked scholar's lens, a singed page) are ritually burned or buried during private moments of grief.

Houses breathe: walls shift slightly with the seasons, roofs grow flowers that change color with the mood of the inhabitants. *They feel alive because the people who live in them refuse to be entombed by the past.*

## Clothing

Practical, expressive, and deliberately playful. Clothing celebrates body and movement rather than concealing it.

- **Base layers**: soft, breathable leathers, linens, or woven plant-fibers in bright clashing colors
- **Layers**: sashes, cloaks, and harnesses salvaged or remade from old [[rathari|Rathar]] scholarly robes — the heavy gray fabric **torn into colorful strips, embroidered with laughing runes or Sylph's fool motifs** (a grinning mask, a broken chain, a dancing fox)
- **Jewelry**: loud and kinetic — bells on tails or wingtips, beads carved from war-scar glass that chime, necklaces of tiny masks that flip between **stern Rathar faces and beast grins**
- **Warriors**: light, segmented armor that looks more like festive parade gear

The overall effect is vibrant, slightly chaotic, and impossible to ignore — exactly as Sylph prefers.

## Food

Food is **sacrament and celebration**. The [[rathari|Rathar]] once survived on preserved fungal stews and nutrient pastes; the Laughing Third turned necessity into **riotous indulgence**.

Signature dishes:
- **Veil Cakes** — thin, layered wafers of compressed fruits, nuts, and honey delivering complete nutrition yet tasting of pure joy. Carried on long journeys as both ration and reminder.
- **Laughing Broth** — a spicy, sour-sweet soup that clears the head and induces uncontrollable giggles.
- **Memory Burn desserts** — sweets wrapped around a single bitter herb eaten last, symbolizing the choice to swallow the past and taste the future.

Alcohol and mild hallucinogenic fungi are common but **never cruel** — indulgence is for connection, not escape. Children learn early: *"Eat with your whole heart; the past has no seat at this table."*

## Traditions

### Festival of the Laughing Choice

Held at the new moon after the autumn equinox. Every beast-folk dons a **two-faced mask** (scholar on one side, beast on the other), processes through the settlement telling exaggerated tales of the [[demon-war|Demon War]] that grow more ridiculous with each retelling, then **burns the scholar-face at a great bonfire while roaring with laughter**. The ashes are mixed into communal bread the next morning.

### Daily Reinforcements of the Veil

- **Forgetful Telling** — stories that deliberately leave out names or endings so listeners fill them with their own joy
- **Instinct Circles** — group meditations where participants act on the first impulse that feels right
- **Sylph's Masque Nights** — wild costumed revels where social roles are inverted and anyone may speak truth through foolishness

### Veil Walks

Periodic solo or small-group journeys to *"remember how to forget."* Walkers return changed, welcomed without question.

### Conflict and Grief

Conflict is rare and resolved through ritual combat-dance or [[flyting|Feast Debate]]. Grief is honored quickly and loudly, then released. *They are not naive; they simply refuse to let yesterday steal today.*

## Daily life and personality

Beyond the formal cultural structures (Laughing Circles, Feast Debates, the Festival of the Laughing Choice, Veil Walks), Beast-Folk daily life has a recognizable texture that holds across the various forms.

### Loud, expressive, physically affectionate

Beast-Folk **live out loud**. Where the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] are ascetically reserved and the [[rathari|Rathari]] are intellectually contained, the Beast-Folk are emotionally generous in nearly every domain. They laugh easily, hug freely, and physically pull people into things. A Beast-Folk who likes you is going to express it; the question of whether you want to be expressed-at is sometimes secondary in their reckoning.

The same texture extends to anger, grief, joy, and surprise. A Beast-Folk does not contain emotion well — and does not particularly try to. Outsiders sometimes find this exhausting; insiders find anything else slightly suffocating.

### Quick to forgive

Beast-Folk are **genuinely quick to forgive**. A sincere apology from someone who has insulted them is *actually forgotten* — not stored as a quiet resentment, not held as leverage, not raised again at convenient moments. Beast-Folk who hold grudges past apology are notable enough that other Beast-Folk comment on it.

This is part of the Veiled inheritance. A people who chose to surrender the worst of their pain do not, generally speaking, hoard small grievances either.

### Working mode and off-work mode

Beast-Folk have **two distinct modes** that shape their daily life:

- **At work** — focused, practical, fine being filthy, smelly, and physically rough. They get the job done. They do not, however, become quiet or grim while working. Even mid-task, Beast-Folk laugh and joke with the person next to them on the line, do small bits of physical theater with their hands, and break into hard laughter that they have to recover from before continuing. Their work-mode attentiveness is genuine; their work-mode humor is also genuine; the two coexist without contradiction.
- **Off work** — clean, fed, surrounded by friends and family. The end of a working day is a complete shift. They communal-bathe, eat together, dance, sing, hug strangers into the circle, and generally treat the off-work hours as **the whole point of the day**.

This produces caravan companies, work-crews, and mixed settlements where the Beast-Folk are the loudest and best-loved members of the group — the ones who pull their weight all day and then turn the camp into a party at night.

### Communal bath and shared food

Two of the strongest Beast-Folk daily-life cultural anchors are **the communal bath** (with [[bathing-customs#Beast-Folk|its open invitation to caravan companions]] and its conjured wagon-pool with the sealed [[eadras-cosmology|Plane of Water]] portal) and **shared food**. Beast-Folk consider eating alone faintly tragic and bathing alone faintly absurd. Both practices are venues for the broader emotional generosity — a meal becomes the shared anchor of the off-work hours; the bath becomes the day's reset and the place where small disputes get worked out by being carried into the same warm water as everything else.

See [[bathing-customs#Beast-Folk]] for the full bathing treatment, including the Beast-Folk wagon-pool — a magic-conjured pool with a sealed portal to a warm region of the [[eadras-cosmology|Plane of Water]] that fills and drains at one foot per minute, lives compact on every Beast-Folk wagon, and unfolds into a 3-foot-deep communal bath for the night's company.

## Preferred Professions

The Laughing Third gravitate toward roles that reward **instinct, presence, and joy**:

- **Scouts and Wayfinders** (ravens, cats, foxes) — instinctive pathfinders who read wind and land better than any map
- **Entertainers and Bards** — performances literally lighten the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] in their vicinity
- **Healers and Shamans** (frogs, bears, deer) — instinctive touch-healers who use laughter and touch as much as herbs
- **Artisans and Crafters** — playful workers who make beautiful, slightly ridiculous objects that function better because they are loved
- **Mercenaries and Guardians** — joyful warriors who fight with reckless delight and unbreakable loyalty
- **Farmers, Foragers, and Beast-Tenders** — coax abundance from the land through song and instinct

Few become pure scholars; those who feel the old pull often travel to **[[katharim|Katharim]] enclaves** as honored *"bridge-kin."* Many serve as diplomats or caravan masters precisely because **they forget old grudges fastest**.

## Magical Effect on the Weave

Their **instinctive pattern-breaking subtly strengthens the Weave**. [[lirra|Lirra]]'s threads respond to their present-focused joy; Sylph's influence keeps local magic flexible and alive. Some [[windspeakers]] quietly seek beast-folk assistants for their ability to improvise when rigid formulas fail.

## Relationship with the Shada'din

The Beast-folk and the **[[dwarves|Shada'din (Dwarves)]]** maintain a long, respectful, and mutually beneficial relationship dating to the centuries immediately following the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. The Shada'din are master craftsmen in stone, metal, and [[magitek|magitek]] — and proud of producing the vast majority of their own tools, weapons, armor, and architectural works — but they openly acknowledge that the **Beast-folk are superior craftsmen in textiles and "soft" arts.** (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

Shada'din therefore regularly purchase **high-quality relaxation clothing, tapestries, banners, decorative hangings, and other soft goods** from Beast-folk artisans. These pieces are highly prized because they perfectly suit the Shada'din's [[dwarves#The two modes|Relaxation Mode]]: colorful, luxurious, comfortable, and expressive in ways dwarven practicality rarely achieves alone. In exchange, Beast-folk value Shada'din **reliability, protection, and access to rare underground materials.**

Many **Laughing Circles maintain permanent quarters inside Shada'din holds**, creating vibrant **"Veil Districts"** — sectors where the Beast-folk's joyous culture blends with Shada'din discipline. A typical Veil District features painted entrances opening directly into a hold's stone arteries, Forgetful Hearths burning under monolithic dwarven vaulting, mixed feasts where flyting and Arena-rule debate happen in the same evening, and trade-stalls offering Beast-folk textiles in exchange for Shada'din-built tools and food.

The relationship is one of **genuine mutual respect**, not dependency. The Shada'din admire the Beast-folk's ability to embrace joy and beauty; the Beast-folk respect the Shada'din's strength, craftsmanship, and steadfastness. They affectionately refer to each other as **"Stone Cousins"** (the Shada'din) and **"Laughing Cousins"** (the Beast-folk).

## Relationship with the Katharim

The two peoples are **siblings** — both descended from the [[rathari|Rathar]], answering catastrophe in opposite ways. Beast-folk often bring their dead to [[katharim|Katharim]] crypts for final interment, then hold rowdy [[flyting|farewell flytings]] at the entrance while gray-robed priests stand in silent witness. *Neither interrupts the other. Both are necessary.*

Many Laughing Circles keep a **Katharim Grave-Watcher** in permanent residence — a gray-robed figure who tends the local dead and is gently teased, deeply loved, and fiercely protected by the entire community.

## The forgetting of the Demon War

Where the [[katharim|Katharim]] hold the names of the dead with institutional precision and the [[orcs|orcs]] hold the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Final Wall]] in ritual grief every year, the **beast-folk have tried to forget** the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. This is consistent with the broader [[mythic-recitations|mythic-recitations]] canon: where almost every other Eadran people preserves a mythic-recitation form of the war (the common *War of the Still King*, the orcish *Night of the Gray Wall*, the Katharim *Breaking of the Three*, the dragon contemptuous version, the unicorn darker version), beast-folk recitation of the war is sparse, evasive, or absent. They tend not to perform the recitations; they tend not to teach them to their young; they tend, when pressed, to redirect the conversation. (Source: [[mythic-recitations#3-5-what-different-peoples-emphasize|Mythic Recitations § 3.5]].)

Whether the forgetting is collective amnesia (the [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar|Veil of Forgetting]] choice their ancestors made, extended over ten thousand years), deliberate institutional silence (a Laughing Circle norm enforced by social pressure), the slow erosion of memory by [[sylph|Sylph]]'s ongoing Veil influence, or some combination is open canon. The category itself — **deliberate forgetting as a mode of mythic-relationship** — is one of the load-bearing pieces of canon the [[mythic-recitations|forthcoming mythic-recitations]] introduce, and beast-folk are the canonical example of it.

The forgetting is not universal. Beast-folk caretaker-priests of the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] (see below) preserve some memory by necessity; beast-folk who maintain shared ritual practice with the [[katharim|Katharim]] inherit some by proximity. But the dominant cultural posture is the choice their ancestors made, carried forward: *they have chosen what they refused to remember*.

## Modern Role

Some beast-folk communities guard lands surrounding the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] — the region where the first great demonic breach opened — because their ancestors fought and were transmuted in that war. The region remains unstable, heavily warded, and culturally diverse due to ancient refugee settlement.

## Summary

The Laughing Third are not what the Rathar were. They are **what the Rathar chose to become** when memory became too heavy to carry. And in their laughter the world still finds reasons to keep dancing.
