# The Divine Bureaucracy

**Summary**: The Divine Bureaucracy is a **vast, ancient, and notoriously dysfunctional** organization responsible for managing the day-to-day operations of divinity across the realms. **The entire Bureaucracy is located within [[solarion|Solarion]]**, the divine city in [[celestia|Celestia]]; in Bureaucratic and theological writing, Solarion is sometimes called *the Eternal City* in its administrative-capital aspect. The [[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]] stand at the very top of the hierarchy but are almost entirely hands-off; the Bureaucracy is primarily run by [[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods|Ascended Mortal Gods]], while the overwhelming majority of the actual workforce consists of celestial spirits from [[celestia|Celestia]]. After countless eons of service, these spirits have grown deeply bored, jaded, tired, overworked, and increasingly petty. The Bureaucracy is infamous for being massively inefficient, riddled with political maneuvering, turf wars, laziness, and corruption — and yet it is the institution through which **all** divine magic, prayer-routing, Favor adjustment, miracle approval, soul-judgment, oath-tracking, and most other actual divine operations actually happen.

## What the Bureaucracy is

The gods do not, themselves, run divinity. They embody it. The Bureaucracy *runs* it.

This distinction is important. An [[gods-of-eadras|Elder God]] is a being of cosmic scale whose nature is the domain they hold; their attention is enormous, their decisions consequential, and their direct involvement in any single mortal matter is rare by structural necessity (compounded by the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]). The actual day-to-day work of *being-a-god-in-the-world* — answering prayers, granting capacity to priests, judging the dead, approving miracles, regulating the divine courts, defending the celestial realms — has long since been delegated downward to an institution.

That institution is the Divine Bureaucracy.

In its idealized form, the Bureaucracy is what allows divinity to be *available*: the Elder Gods do not need to personally reach into every prayer because the Bureaucracy handles routing; they do not need to personally adjudicate every Favor adjustment because the Bureaucracy maintains the books; they do not need to personally answer every petition because the Bureaucracy filters which petitions warrant divine attention.

In its actual form, the Bureaucracy is **a mess**.

## The hierarchy

| Tier | Role | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **[[gods-of-eadras\|Elder Gods]]** | Top of the hierarchy | Almost entirely hands-off. They embody their domains; they rarely intervene in Bureaucratic operations. |
| **[[gods-of-eadras#Ascended Mortal Gods\|Ascended Mortal Gods]]** | Primary executive class | The actual day-to-day operation of the Bureaucracy is run by Ascended Mortals — gods like [[baldaran|Baldaran]], [[inki|Inki]], [[din-shadar|Din'shadar]], the [[three-last-lights|Three Last Lights]], and others. They have the practical experience of having once been mortals subject to the Bureaucracy themselves, and they bring that experience to running it. |
| **Celestial spirits** (workforce) | Vast majority of staff | Lower-tier celestial beings drawn primarily from [[celestia|Celestia]]. They handle the actual labor at scale. They have been doing it for eons. |

The structural logic is clean enough on paper. The Elder Gods cannot be dragged into operational details without violating the scale at which they exist. The Ascended Mortal Gods, having been mortals, can manage at the operational level without disrupting the cosmic structure. The celestial spirits, being numerous and durable, can do the bulk work.

In practice, however, the Bureaucracy has the failure modes you would expect of any institution that has been running this long with this little executive oversight.

## The workforce

The celestial spirits who do the actual work of the Bureaucracy have, **after countless eons of service**, grown:

- **Deeply bored.** The work is repetitive at cosmic scale — every prayer routed, every Favor calculation processed, every soul intake-interviewed has already been done a billion times before. The novelty is gone. The novelty has been gone for a long time.
- **Jaded.** Mortals are predictable. Mortals' prayers are predictable. The kinds of dilemmas mortals bring before the Bureaucracy are predictable. After enough cycles, even the most genuinely tragic petition starts to read as a category.
- **Tired.** A spirit who has been processing soul intake since before the [[arathi|Arathi]] civilization existed is deeply tired in a way that has no ordinary mortal analogue, even if their personal eons of service is shorter than the institution's age.
- **Overworked — in the operational bureaus.** The volume of work that the actually-functional bureaus must process is enormous and never lets up. Demand has scaled with the size of mortal civilization, and operational staff (the [[#Bureau of Prayer|Bureau of Prayer]] routing clerks, the [[#Bureau of Souls|Bureau of Souls]] intake spirits, the [[#Bureau of Favor|Bureau of Favor]] adjusters, and so on) are genuinely under-resourced even though the institution as a whole is overstaffed (see [[#Size and the sinecure problem|below]]).
- **Increasingly petty.** When you have been doing the same job for an eon — or watching colleagues in sinecure positions do effectively no work while you carry the actual load — the small slights and small satisfactions of office life become *very* important. A celestial bureaucrat slighted by a colleague three thousand years ago has not forgotten and is keeping a list.

These are not character flaws of poorly-chosen staff. They are the structural emergent properties of the Bureaucracy's design — what happens when you ask immortal beings to do paperwork forever in an institution simultaneously enormous in headcount and under-resourced where work actually happens. The Elder Gods know this is how the workforce ends up; the Ascended Mortal Gods know it; the workforce knows it about itself. Nothing in the system has yet found a fix.

### Service is not permanent

Individual spirits are **not permanently bound** to the Bureaucracy. They can be:

- **Promoted** — moved to a more prestigious or more senior position within their bureau, or up into supervisory work
- **Demoted** — moved to a less prestigious position, often as a soft punishment for serious misconduct or political loss
- **Transferred** between departments — sometimes voluntarily (a spirit growing too jaded for soul-intake might apply to move to prayer-routing), sometimes involuntarily (an Investigator-General-flagged spirit being quietly relocated to somewhere less sensitive)
- Or **leave entirely** — returning to ordinary celestial existence in [[celestia|Celestia]] or moving on to whatever post-Bureaucracy fate awaits them

What the Bureaucracy does *not* do is leave a position empty. **Every job position is always filled by someone.** The institution is structured around continuous coverage; whenever a spirit moves, leaves, or otherwise vacates, a replacement is always seated within whatever the Bureaucracy treats as the standard processing window. This is one of the Bureaucracy's few genuinely well-functioning features, and it explains why the institution has been able to run continuously for eons despite individual staff turnover.

The internal politics of who gets which position when one opens up are vicious in the way slow politics are vicious. Career-ending arguments play out across millennia. The "promotion track" of the Bureaucracy is real — *the same bureaucratic spirit might have started in a [[#Bureau of Souls|Bureau of Souls]] intake desk, transferred to [[#Bureau of Prayer|Prayer]] routing, been promoted into a sub-department head, lost a political fight and been demoted to archive maintenance, and finally settled into a sinecure she has held for the last twenty thousand years* — and the political maneuvering is largely about positioning oneself for the next move (or, in the bureaucracy-positive case, for a sinecure where one can finally rest).

### Size and the sinecure problem

The Bureaucracy is **massively overstaffed**. It employs far more spirits than it actually needs to do the work. The institution has accumulated headcount across eons without ever significantly trimming it, and the result is a workforce considerably larger than the operational requirement.

This is not, however, evenly distributed. The institution contains:

- **Genuinely overworked operational staff** in the bureaus where mortal civilization keeps generating new demand (the Bureau of Prayer especially, but also Souls and Favor and many of the regional-realm bureaus when their realms are active). These spirits are the ones the workforce description above ("overworked") is most true of.
- **Mid-staffed administrative spirits** doing real work at a sustainable but unremarkable pace.
- **Many sinecure positions** where spirits do very little actual work. These are the prized assignments — frequently held for thousands of years by the same spirit, fought over politically when they open, defended fiercely by the holders, and the source of much of the petty resentment the operational staff carry.

Sinecures are a structural feature, not an accident. The Bureaucracy has never significantly downsized; positions accumulate; the institution has more *titled positions* than functions to perform; the gap is filled by sinecures. Some sinecures are formally classified as sinecures within the institution's own internal documentation; others are operationally sinecures (the position has a real title and a real description, but the actual workload has dwindled to nothing over the eons). The [[#Office of the Investigator General|Office of the Investigator General]] occasionally proposes audits of sinecure positions; these proposals are uniformly buried by the very spirits the audits would target.

The sinecure problem is one of the load-bearing reasons the Bureaucracy is *both* infamously inefficient *and* extremely difficult to reform. Reform would require eliminating positions, and the spirits in those positions have eons of accumulated political skill to deploy against any reformer. Most reform attempts are themselves politely buried in inter-Bureau routing.

## Dysfunction

The Bureaucracy is infamous for:

- **Massive inefficiency.** Routine processes that should take a moment routinely take centuries. Petitions that should reach a god in a heartbeat sometimes get caught in inter-Bureau routing for so long that the petitioner has died of old age, and the prayer is processed against an empty file in the [[#Bureau of Records|Bureau of Records]].
- **Political maneuvering.** Bureaus compete for influence, budget (in whatever currency the Bureaucracy uses), staff allocation, and the favor of the Ascended Mortal Gods who run them. Internal politics are vicious in the way that slow politics are vicious — career-ending arguments that play out across millennia.
- **Turf wars.** Every Bureau's domain has edges that abut another Bureau's domain. The [[#Bureau of Souls|Bureau of Souls]] and the [[#Bureau of Justice|Bureau of Justice]] disagree about who handles murder cases. The [[#Bureau of Prayer|Bureau of Prayer]] and the [[#Bureau of Miracles|Bureau of Miracles]] disagree about when a prayer crosses into a miracle request. The [[#Bureau of Catastrophe|Bureau of Catastrophe]] and the [[#Bureau of Natural Order|Bureau of Natural Order]] disagree about whether a major hurricane is a natural-order item or a catastrophe item, and the disagreement has not been resolved in five thousand years.
- **Laziness.** Some bureaus simply do not work very hard. Spirits in those bureaus have learned which corners can be cut without anyone caring; the corners get cut; the Bureaucracy grinds onward inadequately.
- **Corruption.** Genuine corruption exists. Spirits whose Favor-tracking files happen to favor one priest over another with no actual basis in conduct; prayers that get routed to the wrong god by a clerk with a grudge; miracles approved or denied based on which Ascended Mortal owes which other Ascended Mortal a favor. The corruption is not universal but it is present, and the [[#Office of the Investigator General|Office of the Investigator General]] cannot keep up with all of it.

The Bureaucracy is, in short, **the worst institution in creation that almost works.** Nobody loves it. Nobody can replace it. Nobody can fix it. It limps onward, processing the world's divine business inadequately, eternally.

## Major Bureaus

The Bureaucracy is composed of many bureaus. The major ones are listed below in the canonical order; the boundaries between them are real but contested, and several have ongoing turf wars with each other (some noted; see [[#Dysfunction]]).

### Office of the Investigator General

The feared **internal audit office** that investigates corruption, inefficiency, and misconduct across all other bureaus. The Investigator General's spirits are the only Bureaucracy staff that other Bureaucracy spirits genuinely fear. Notoriously incorruptible (or at least, the most incorruptible the system has been able to manage). Disliked everywhere they investigate, which is everywhere.

### Bureau of Divine Affairs

Manages the **personal disputes, schedules, etiquette, and internal politics** between the gods themselves. When two Elder Gods need to coordinate without speaking directly, this is the bureau that handles it; when an Ascended Mortal God's elevation produces awkward seniority questions, this is the bureau that smooths them out. Considered prestigious to staff. Considered hellish to actually work in.

### Bureau of Mortal Affairs

Oversees the **relationship between mortals and the divine** — including worship patterns, religious culture, the broader interface between mortal society and divinity. Where the Bureau of Prayer handles individual prayers and the Bureau of Favor tracks individual priests, the Bureau of Mortal Affairs is the macro-level analog: how does this entire civilization relate to its gods, what trends are emerging in mortal religious practice, what reforms (if any) does the Bureaucracy need to consider in response.

### Bureau of Favor

**Tracks, calculates, and adjusts the Favor levels of all priests and divine spellcasters.** The bureau the [[priests|priesthood]] interacts with most directly, even though almost no priest knows it exists. Every Favor adjustment — Neutral to Positive, Positive to God's Favorite, Neutral to Negative — passes through this bureau. The bureau also handles the [[druids|druids']] unified [[druids#The nature of Druidic Favor|Druidic Favor]] tracking, which is canonically more administratively complex than ordinary single-deity Favor and which the bureau staff find genuinely tedious to maintain. See [[priests#Favor]] and [[priests#The Divine Bureaucracy]].

### Bureau of Prayer

**Receives, processes, sorts, and routes prayers to the appropriate deities.** The bureau with the highest sustained workload in the entire Bureaucracy. Every prayer ever uttered passes through here for routing. The bureau's routing tables are infamously baroque, infamously slow, and infamously inconsistent. Many prayers reach their destination perfectly; some are delayed by centuries; some, due to ongoing turf-war disagreements with the Bureau of Miracles, are simply left in indeterminate routing forever.

### Bureau of Miracles

Reviews and **approves (or denies) requests for divine miracles and major interventions.** The structural counterpart of the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] — the Doctrine sets the cosmic-level constraint that gods do not directly intervene; the Bureau of Miracles is the operational gatekeeper that enforces it case-by-case. Direct interventions happen rarely because this Bureau usually denies them. The denial is sometimes explicit; sometimes it is by routing-delay until the petition is no longer relevant.

### Bureau of Prediction

**Monitors possible futures, handles prophecy, and provides divination and foresight for the Bureaucracy.** Closely connected with the [[voroik|Voroik]] (the three Weavers of Fate). When a petitioner asks for divination, when an oracle's prophecy needs verifying, when the Bureaucracy itself needs to know what is coming, the Bureau of Prediction is the source. The Bureau is famously cagey about its outputs, both because the futures it sees are genuinely indeterminate and because foreknowledge in the wrong hands is institutionally hazardous.

### Bureau of Justice

Operates the **Divine Courts** and administers divine law and justice. The bureau most concerned with formal proceedings: heresy trials, oath-violation prosecutions, divine-claim adjudications, the processing of appeals from mortal religious courts up to the divine level. Long-running turf war with the [[#Bureau of Souls|Bureau of Souls]] over the boundary between justice-while-living and judgment-after-death.

### Bureau of Oaths & Pacts

Manages all **sacred oaths, contracts, and especially dangerous pacts made with outsiders.** Every truly binding promise made on a god's name is registered here. The bureau's records of dangerous pacts (with demons, with extraplanar entities, with [[neverborn|chaos]] and with worse) are some of the most carefully-guarded archives in the Bureaucracy. The bureau is theologically uncomfortable in its own self-image: managing pacts with outsiders is necessary work, but it puts the bureau in the position of formally tracking arrangements the Bureaucracy itself would prefer not existed.

### Bureau of Souls

Oversees the **judgment, transport, and afterlife placement of mortal souls after death.** Closely associated with [[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani]] (the gentle and stern halves of Death). The bureau is enormous because mortals never stop dying; the workload is constant; the work is structurally repetitive in a way that produces some of the most jaded staff in the entire Bureaucracy.

### Bureau of Natural Order

Maintains the functioning of **natural laws, seasons, weather, and ecological balance.** Closely associated with [[qoxli|Qoxli]] and (in some matters) with [[sylph|Sylph]] and [[nox|Nox]]. The bureau the [[druids|druids]] occasionally invoke when their mediation work requires authoritative ecological judgment. Long-running turf war with the [[#Bureau of Catastrophe|Bureau of Catastrophe]] over which side handles which kind of severe weather.

### Bureau of Arcane Regulation

Monitors the **use of arcane magic and prevents dangerous magical disruptions.** Watches for runaway spell-effects, unauthorized planar incursions, and the kind of magic-system damage that the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]]'s [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]] illustrated could happen. The bureau's relationship with the various magical orders ([[wizards]], [[netharim]], [[lexanari]], the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] in its still-extant form) is formal but distant. The bureau acts on dangers, not on practitioners.

### Bureau of Catastrophe

Manages, mitigates, or sometimes **authorizes** major disasters and apocalyptic events. The bureau with the most theologically uncomfortable mandate. It exists because some catastrophes are necessary corrections to a larger imbalance and the Bureaucracy needs an office to handle that authority; it is also the bureau that has the most to answer for, because the line between "necessary correction" and "horrific overreach" is exactly where the bureau works. The [[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]] is widely held to have been a Catastrophe-class event the Bureau **was caught flatfooted on** — the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] came through [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir's]] [[chaos-crusades|First Breach]] without any prior bureau coordination, and the failure was institutionally severe.

### Bureau of Records

Maintains **the infinite archives of every mortal deed, prayer, sin, and oath.** The Bureau's mandate is **mechanical precision** — raw data, recorded as it comes in, with no interpretive layer. Prayers in the prayers archive; oaths in the oaths archive; deeds in the deeds archive; everything filed and cross-indexed by the appropriate clerk-spirit, exhaustively, without judgment.

Not to be confused with the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] — and the relationship between the two is now canonical (see [[#Bureau of Records vs. the Tome of Baldaran|below]]). [[baldaran|Baldaran]] is the Ascended Mortal God most closely associated with the bureau in cultural perception, but the actual administrative arrangement is more nuanced: Baldaran built the Tome **as a personal alternative to** what the Bureau provides, not as an extension of it.

### Bureau of Heresy

Investigates **cults, false gods, and threats to the established divine order.** The bureau most concerned with maintaining the structural integrity of the pantheon as a recognized institution. Active against entities like the [[demons|demons]], the [[chaos|Chaos]] cults, the [[neverborn|Neverborn]]-touched, and the various doomed-attempt-at-godhood movements that arise across mortal history. Has a complicated relationship with the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]], whose [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine-of-Unheld-Hand]]-trained Netharim sometimes do bureau-aligned work without being formally affiliated.

### Bureau of the Heavenly Host

Commands the **divine armies** and manages the defense of the celestial realms. The bureau with the most direct combat capability, including the rare divine-army deployments that have happened across history. The bureau's general posture is defensive (the doctrine being that the divine realms must not be invaded, not that they should be projecting force outward), but during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] the Heavenly Host fought alongside mortals and dragons against the demons that had broken into the world.

### Bureau of Celestia

Oversees all matters related to **the celestial realms** — [[celestia|Celestia]] itself, the various lesser celestial regions, the divine cities ([[solarion|Solarion]], et cetera), the structural maintenance of the bright planar infrastructure. The bureau most of the workforce hails from, and (correspondingly) the bureau with the most internal politics about which celestial spirits get the better postings.

### Bureau of the Feywild

Manages affairs concerning **the [[feywild|Feywild]]** and its inhabitants. Closely associated with [[sylph|Sylph]] in particular. Handles the formal relationship with the [[fae|fae courts]], the long-running negotiations over the boundary between Prime and Feywild jurisdiction, and the [[#Bureau of Souls|Bureau of Souls]]'s occasional cross-jurisdictional cases involving fae deaths.

### Bureau of the Shadowdark

Handles matters related to **the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] and the realm of death.** Closely associated with [[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani]] (Death's two halves) and with [[nox|Nox]] (night and the loathsome). Handles the formal relationship with the dead, the regulation of the shadow infrastructure, and the long-standing question of how the [[veyr-keth|Velrathi exiles]] in Shadowdark Gaia interface with the rest of the Bureaucracy's structure.

### Bureau of Nyxar

Oversees all affairs concerning **[[nyxar|Nyxar]]**, the infernal realm. Includes the formal containment of and negotiation with the entities native to that plane, the management of the [[demons|demonic]] threat-vectors when they emerge through cosmological breaches, and the maintenance of the structural barriers between Nyxar and the other realms. The bureau most directly engaged with the [[demon-war|Demon War's]] ongoing legacy.

### Bureau of the Eternal City

Administers **the divine city where the Bureaucracy itself is headquartered**. The "Eternal City" is the Bureaucracy's own institutional name for **[[solarion|Solarion]]** in its administrative-capital aspect — the entire Divine Bureaucracy is located within Solarion, and this bureau handles the city's physical functioning, security, internal logistics, the maintenance of office space and archive infrastructure across all twenty-plus other bureaus, and the management of the city's other roles (notably its function as the gathering-place for the [[solarion#The Game of the Gods|Game of the Gods]], which the bureau coordinates around without interfering with).

This is the closest thing the Bureaucracy has to a domestic-services bureau. It is not glamorous, but its staff are widely respected — when the building works, every other bureau's work works, and when the building doesn't work, nothing else does either.

## Bureau of Records vs. the Tome of Baldaran

The [[#Bureau of Records|Bureau of Records]] and the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] are the two parallel record-keeping systems of divinity in Eadras. Their relationship has a definite institutional history.

The Bureau of Records maintains its archives as raw data — prayers, oaths, deeds — recorded with mechanical precision and no interpretive layer. The records are exhaustive and accurate but **dry**. They do not understand the events they describe; they enumerate them.

[[baldaran|Baldaran]], the Ascended Mortal God of knowledge and history, **wanted something more**. He wanted an active, intelligent observer of history — something that would not just record events but **understand them, interpret them, and apply judgment** to what was truly significant. The Bureau of Records is not, structurally, that kind of institution; the Bureau's clerks file what comes in, and asking them to evaluate which entries matter is asking them to do a different job from the one the Bureau is built around.

Baldaran could not get the Bureaucracy to provide what he wanted. Whether the Bureaucracy was unable or unwilling is open to interpretation; the result is the same. So Baldaran built the **[[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] as his personal solution**, working in his own godly realm with assistance from [[nox|Nox]], Erendor (Elderborn god of Time), and Handwright (Elderborn god of Craftwork).

This is **why the two systems exist in parallel**, and why they occasionally produce **conflicting records**. The Bureau records what is filed; the Tome records what is observed and understood — and the Tome can also record **potential events at inflection points** that may or may not actualize, sealing the unactualized ones (see [[tome-of-baldaran#Potential events at inflection points|Potential events at inflection points]]). The Bureau, having no such interpretive mandate, files only what actually occurred and was reported; it has no parallel record of timelines-not-taken. The two systems are consistent in ordinary cases and diverge in edge cases — typically when the Tome's curatorial judgment kicks in and seals a chapter because the event was non-canonical, was an unactualized potential, was undone by the universe, was too dangerous for the present age, or otherwise warranted withholding. In those cases the Bureau's mechanical record may reflect a different version of events than the Tome's interpretive one.

(The [[rpg/closed-time-bubble|Closed-Time-Bubble Chaos Crusade]] is the most-discussed splat illustration of how the divergence might play out — though it is splat-only material rather than canonical Eadras history. The two systems' divergence pattern is canonical; the specific Crusade campaign is not.)

The exact nature of their relationship — how much the two systems overlap, whether they share information at all, what happens when their versions of history diverge — remains a point of **quiet theological tension** in the broader divine institution. Neither side publicly contests the other; both are functioning components of how divinity records the world; but the underlying question of which is the *real* record is not a question anyone has the standing to answer.

(See [[tome-of-baldaran#Relationship with the Bureau of Records]] for the matched treatment from the Tome's side.)

## Connection to the rest of canon

- **[[priests|Priests]]** interact with the Bureaucracy primarily through the [[#Bureau of Favor|Bureau of Favor]] (Favor tracking), the [[#Bureau of Prayer|Bureau of Prayer]] (prayer routing), and occasionally the [[#Bureau of Miracles|Bureau of Miracles]] (extraordinary requests). The [[priests#The Divine Bureaucracy|priests page's Bureaucracy section]] sketches the surface; this page is the deep treatment.
- **[[druids|Druids]]** interact with all of the above plus the [[#Bureau of Natural Order|Bureau of Natural Order]] for ecological-balance matters, and have the additional bureaucratic complexity of the [[druids#The nature of Druidic Favor|unified Druidic Favor]] track that the Bureau of Favor finds genuinely tedious.
- **The [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]** is enforced operationally by the [[#Bureau of Miracles|Bureau of Miracles]]. The Doctrine is the cosmic-level rule; the Bureau is the office that says no when miracles are requested.
- **The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]]** coexists with the [[#Bureau of Records|Bureau of Records]]; their relationship is a small theological controversy and an open canon question.
- **[[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]** has a complicated relationship with the [[#Bureau of Heresy|Bureau of Heresy]] — Netharim of the Order sometimes do work that aligns with the Bureau's mandate without being formally affiliated. (Note: rework-pending status of the Order under the [[log|2026-05-09 magic-system rework]].)
- **The [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]** is widely held to have been a Catastrophe-class event the [[#Bureau of Catastrophe|Bureau of Catastrophe]] was institutionally unable to anticipate or prevent.
