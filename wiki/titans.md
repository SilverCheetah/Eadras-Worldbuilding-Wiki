# Titans

**Summary**: Titans are the largest of the [[tarans|Tarran]]-engineered war-stocks — towering living war machines twenty-five to thirty meters tall, bred to break chaotic incursions through sheer ruinous mass. **Most fell during the [[demon-war|Demon War]]**, but a feral remnant still walks Eadras today.

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt, Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, titans-canon-2026-05-10.txt, titans-canon-additions-2026-05-10.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-10

## Origin

The Titans were wrought by the [[tarans|Tarran]] through generations of spellcraft and selective breeding — the same ritual-and-lineage discipline that produced the [[aetherins|Aetherin]] and the [[mitans|Mitans]]. They were the heaviest piece of the Tarran martial doctrine: where the [[aetherins|Aetherin]] were swift shock troops and the [[mitans|Mitans]] were subterranean specialists, the Titans were *front-line obliteration* — moved into the path of demonic and chaotic incursions to crush them by mass and reach alone.

The shaping took centuries. Each generation was bred from the largest, most disciplined, and most magically resonant of the prior, with spells layered over each gestation and lineage rituals tracking every cross. Like all Tarran-engineered war-stock, the Titans emerged not as a single creation but as the slow distillation of a chosen trajectory across many generations of managed descent.

## The Tarran Martial Cluster

Titans belong to the Tarran martial cluster — the three engineered war-stocks the Tarran shaped to fulfill their progenitor role as Protectors:

- **[[aetherins|Aetherin]]** — shock troops against chaos and demonic incursions
- **Titans** — heavy front-line war engines against chaos
- **[[mitans|Mitans]]** — miners and subterranean / tunnel warriors

Together the three covered the doctrine the Tarran were responsible for: war against chaos in every register, from a strike-force at the front to a hammer at the breach to a defender beneath the earth. The Titans were the hammer.

## Anatomy

- **Adult height**: twenty-five to thirty meters — taller than the gateway towers of most modern cities, the height of an eight- to ten-storey tower.
- **Child height**: ten meters — already larger than most adult sapients of the modern races.
- **Build**: massive humanoid form scaled to siege-engine proportions; immensely thick bone and connective tissue, broad torso, columnar limbs.
- **Lifespan**: long; canon is silent on the precise figure, but a Titan grows for decades before reaching full adult height, and civilized lineages produced individuals who served across multiple generations of mortal warfare.

A Titan child stepping into an [[orcs|Orc]] clan-hold would tower over every warrior present. An adult Titan standing beside a city wall is roughly the height of the wall itself.

## The Calorie Problem

A body at twenty-five to thirty meters demands food in proportion. **Titans eat near-constantly.** The civilization that fielded them had to solve the calorie problem as part of fielding them at all — Tarran logistics included dedicated provisioning caravans, livestock herds bred for Titan diets, and grain-stores planned at scales most other peoples never need to plan at. A managed Titan was, among other things, a continuous logistical undertaking.

When civilization is removed, the calorie problem becomes the central fact of a Titan's existence. A feral Titan is a being whose hunger never stops, whose body burns at the rate its size demands, and whose mind is bent to one task: find food, eat food.

## The Civilized and the Uncivilized

The most consequential distinction among living Titans is between the **civilized** and the **uncivilized**.

**Civilized Titans** are full sapient beings, with names, language, oath-bonds, and culture, raised under [[tarans|Tarran]] discipline or its successor structures. Their cognition is fundamentally that of a person: a civilized Titan can be as quick or as dull as any sapient, with the full intelligence range one might find among humans or [[orcs|Orcs]]. Under Tarran direction they were the heaviest single asset of the martial cluster, drilled into formation tactics, given honors for valor, named in war-songs after long sieges. The Tarran fed them, taught them, and trusted them.

The qualifier is that **even attentive lineages cannot fully meet a Titan child's nutritional needs**. The calorie demand of a ten-meter child outstrips what any civilization can sustain consistently, and the result is that **developmental disabilities are common among Titans of every era**. A civilized Titan whose childhood was even briefly under-fed will often carry cognitive limitations into adulthood that no later abundance can repair. A fully-developed, fully-sapient adult Titan is consequently rare and precious — the survivor of a lineage whose stewards managed to keep its young fed all the way through growth.

**Uncivilized Titans** are sapient still — they did not lose their minds in becoming feral — but reduced to the predatory simplicity that their size enforces when no society stands behind them. **They eat what they catch, including sapient prey, alive.** A walking feral Titan in the wilderness is not figurative kaiju; it is literal kaiju, and the histories of any settlement near a feral Titan's path are histories of slow, helpless disappearances. For feral Titans the developmental picture is worse than for the civilized: chronic childhood under-feeding is nearly universal among the wild population, so even by feral-Titan standards a cognitively-intact adult is uncommon.

This is the cost the engineering carries. The Tarran built a weapon that requires civilization to humanize, and the post-collapse cost of that choice is borne by every people who must now share Eadras with the survivors.

## The Demon War

Titans fought in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] in great numbers, deployed against the most ruinous demonic charges — the only beings of mortal lineage who could meet such formations by physical mass alone. Their bodies were the bulwark thrown against breaches no smaller warrior could close.

**Most Titans did not survive the war.** The pure Titan population — already small relative to their scale and breeding cost — was decimated by the prolonged combat and by the catastrophic actions of the war's final years, including the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]]. The civilized lineages that had served the Tarran most directly were nearly extinguished.

But **not all Titans died.** A remnant survived — some hidden, some scattered, some simply elsewhere when the worst battles came. From this remnant descend both the feral pure-Titans of the present day and, through the slow scaling-down of natural devolution, the [[goliath|Giants and modern Goliath]].

The survivors were too few, however, to preserve the breeding diversity of the pre-war population. The bottleneck reduced the pure-Titan line to a narrow genetic base that has never recovered. The species is in slow-motion decline from two directions at once: devolution continues to drain individuals into the Giant line and onward to [[goliath|Goliath]], while the surviving pure-Titan stock — both feral and Gray-Matriarchy-managed — cannot replenish itself through natural variation alone.

## Modern Status

Pure Titans still walk Eadras. Most are **uncivilized** — feral survivors of a civilization that did not survive the war. They do not occupy fixed ranges; the appetite that drives them keeps them mobile, drifting through wildernesses wherever the calorie demand can be met and moving on once a region's accessible prey is exhausted. A kingdom whose frontier abuts a feral Titan's drift-path is a kingdom that buries its frontier villages every few years until the Titan moves on. The fully-fed wild Titan is a rare event; most feral Titans live in chronic insufficiency, hunting whatever moves and consuming whatever they kill alive.

**Civilized Titans persist within the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]].** The exiled [[tarans|Tarran]] kept Titan lineages with them after the [[great-banishment|Banishment]], and they maintain a small, stable breeding population of civilized Titans in their exile — though this remnant has itself lost most of its genetic diversity in the centuries since, sustained now through careful stewardship rather than natural variation. The Gray Matriarchy is widely held to use these civilized Titans as **instruments of terror**: paraded through villages, deployed against rebellious districts, held as a standing reminder to commoners of what the Tarran can still field. The exiled Tarran did not become kinder after their exile; they doubled down on instruments of dominance, and the civilized Titans of the Matriarchy are now as much a tool of internal political repression as a martial asset.

Whether any other civilized Titan tradition persists outside the Gray Matriarchy is unconfirmed; the [[katharim|Katharim]] hold, on tradition rather than evidence, that at least one free lineage somewhere still remembers its oaths and its songs.

The natural devolution line is unambiguous. **Titans → Giants → [[goliath|Goliath]].** Across two stages of gradual scaling-down, the lineage shed both size and the deeper Tarran martial spellcraft. Modern [[goliath|Goliath]], at roughly three meters tall, are the surviving downstream of the Titan project, carrying diluted Titan blood but no Titan culture, language, or scale.

## Cultural Inheritance

Civilized Titans, where they existed, are remembered in fragments:

- Tarran war-songs name individual Titans by their oath-cycles and their charge-records, though most such songs survive only in [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] traditions inaccessible to mainland scholarship.
- The handful of [[katharim|Katharim]] records that preserve Titan names — *Korvath-Who-Held-the-Eastern-Pass*, *Throgar-of-the-Six-Walls* — give no lineage information, only the deeds and the name and the date of falling.
- Modern [[orcs|Orc]] martial liturgies sometimes invoke *"the great ones who stood with us when the world bent"* as a collective reference to the civilized Titans of the war years.

## Relations to Other Peoples

- **[[orcs|Orcs]] and [[orugi|Orug]]** — the Orug ancestors of the Orcs fought alongside civilized Titans in the Demon War. The diplomatic shaping-of-non-aggression that defined Orug disposition was specifically calibrated against the Tarran martial cluster (of which Titans were the largest member); Orug were bred *not* to start fights with Titans, Aetherin, or Mitans. Modern Orc clans treat feral Titans as natural disasters to be avoided, but the civilized-Titan history is honored in their oldest liturgies.
- **[[goliath|Goliath]]** — direct descendants, two stages of devolution removed. Goliath carry diluted Titan blood but no Titan culture, language, or scale.
- **[[tarans|Tarran]]** — their makers. The relationship between the exiled Tarran of [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] and any surviving civilized Titan tradition is canon-unspecified.
- **Demon War survivors generally** — every people who survived the war owes something to the Titans who stood at the worst breaches. Most peoples have forgotten the debt; the [[katharim|Katharim]] have not.
