# Baldaran

**Summary**: Baldaran is the Ascended Mortal Human God of knowledge, history, books, and libraries — creator of the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]], the sentient self-transforming chronicle that records every important event and all collected knowledge in the [[eadras-cosmology|Eadras Mythos]].

**Sources**: May 1 revisions.txt, The Tome of Baldaran.txt; user direction 2026-05-09 evening (the Tome's origin as Baldaran's personal alternative to the Bureau of Records).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-09

---

## Domain

**Knowledge, history, books, and libraries.** Baldaran is the patron of every scholar, archivist, librarian, and chronicler in Eadras. His remit extends from the daily catalog-keeping of a parish library to the grand archival projects that span continents and ages, from the recovery of lost languages to the organization of living testimony before it is lost. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

He is also the patron of **truthful record** — the discipline of recording what happened, distinguished from what one wishes had happened. The distinction is theological: a Baldaran-trained chronicler is held accountable not to a patron, a faction, or a flattering narrative, but to the past itself.

## Origin

Baldaran is a **human Ascended Mortal**. He ascended in the **Third Age** (per [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] dating) and his first canonical act as a god was the creation of the **[[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]]** — accomplished with assistance from the Elder God [[gods-of-eadras|Nox]] (hidden knowledge), the Elderborn [[gods-of-eadras|Erendor]] (Time), and the Elderborn [[gods-of-eadras|Handwright]] (Craftwork).

The Tome exists in a **pocket dimension** within Baldaran's godly realm, partially outside time, which is how it can chronicle events that occurred before its creation.

## The Tome

The defining work of Baldaran's divinity. It is sentient, self-transforming, and culturally chameleonic — appearing as a psi crystal to dragons and unicorns, a book to humans, a rune-slate to dwarves, a bark scroll to elves. It records:

- Every important event in the Mythos — specifically the **decisions that lead to inflection points where timelines split**.
- All knowledge ever discovered in Eadras.
- Secrets that no living being knows (the [[neverborn|Neverborn]]'s role in the [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]], the [[dao|Dao]] avatar's hidden status from the Elder Gods, the [[black-hand|inner truth of the Black Hand]], and others).

The Tome chooses what to reveal and to whom. It often appears to aid heroes at unexpected times — a use Baldaran did not strictly design but which the artifact's intelligence appears to enjoy. See [[tome-of-baldaran]] for the full description.

### Why Baldaran built it himself

The [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]] already maintains a [[divine-bureaucracy#Bureau of Records|Bureau of Records]] — vast, dry, and bureaucratic, recording mortal deeds, prayers, and oaths with mechanical precision. Baldaran wanted something **fundamentally different**: an active, intelligent observer of history that would not just record events but **understand them, interpret them, and apply judgment** to what was truly significant. The Bureau, by its mandate and its institutional structure, is not built to do that.

Baldaran could not get the Bureaucracy to provide what he wanted — whether unable or unwilling, the institutional politics of getting the Bureau to acquire that capability were not worth fighting through. So he built **the Tome himself**, in his own godly realm, working with [[nox|Nox]], Erendor, and Handwright. The Tome became what the Bureau of Records was never going to be: an interpreter rather than a registrar, a judge rather than a clerk, a sentient archive with curatorial discretion rather than an exhaustive archive without it.

This is the origin of the Tome's most distinctive features. Its sentience, its willingness to seal chapters, its preference for understanding over enumeration — none of these are accidents. The Tome was specifically designed to do what the Bureaucracy *won't*. (See [[tome-of-baldaran#Why Baldaran created the Tome]] and [[divine-bureaucracy#Bureau of Records vs. the Tome of Baldaran]] for the matched canonical treatments.)

## Worship pattern

As an Ascended Mortal God, Baldaran **actively desires and encourages worship.** His faith is fervent, personal, and disproportionately concentrated among the literate, the curious, the academic, and the institutional. See [[religion-in-eadras|Religion in Eadras]] for the broader pattern.

Practical observances include:

- **Cataloging as devotion** — properly cataloging a book or scroll is itself a small act of worship.
- **The kept ledger** — Baldaran-faithful keep a personal chronicle of their own lives, however small. The act of keeping is what matters; the content is between the writer and the god.
- **Library and archive offerings** — donations to libraries, recovered texts presented at temple gates, manuscripts deposited with no expectation of return.
- **Truthful telling** — embellishment for narrative effect is common everywhere; in Baldaran's halls, it is a quiet sin. Skalds who serve Baldaran tell stories with a separate clear-record cadence for the parts they vouch for as factual.

## Cross-racial worship

Baldaran's clergy is among the **most racially diverse** in Eadras. Scholars, archivists, and librarians of every race serve at his altars. The famous [[gnomes|gnomish]] tradition of decades-long script translation and the [[dwarves|Shada'din]] lore-keeping practices are both consonant with — and frequently formally affiliated with — Baldaran's faith, even where the local clergy holds Inki or Din'shadar as primary patron.

## Open canon

- **Baldaran's mortal life and ascension narrative**. Canon establishes him as a human Ascended Mortal of the Third Age but does not yet name his mortal kin, his deeds, or the circumstances of his ascension. Whether his ascension was tied to writing or recovering some specific great chronicle is undefined.
- **The relationship between Baldaran-the-god and the Tome's growing intelligence**. The Tome is a sentient artifact with its own preferences (it appears to *enjoy* aiding heroes). Whether it has an independent will distinct from its creator's is open.
- **Whether Baldaran himself authors entries**, or whether the Tome's omniscience writes itself and Baldaran's role is curatorial.
- **His symbol and iconography**. Open book, quill, and key are obvious candidates; not yet canonical.
- **His standing relationships** with Nox, Erendor, and Handwright after the Tome's completion. He worked closely with all three; the Tome is a collaborative artifact even though it bears his name.
