# Eadras Core Canon & Thematic Principles (v2)

**Summary**: Foundational-context primer for Eadras, designed to be prepended to almost every major prompt. Establishes cosmology, tone, metaphysical assumptions, historical skeleton, divine structure, magical structure, thematic identity, and civilizational psychology in compact prompt-ready form.

**Sources**: `raw/eadras-core-canon & thematic principles.md` (v1 source). **2026-05-16 revision (v2)**: regenerated against current canon — magic-system (post-2026-05-09 four-order rework with Lexanari and reframed Netharim; Weave as purely-defensive structure sustained by every magic user's life and anchored by Windspeakers); Anti-Magic Crusade (2026-05-15 timeline shift to 9-10kya + Century of Sorrows + Silent Century + Knights of Lirra + Icon-Habitation halt); Sarathim → Amunorian Empire (2026-05-12 rename); Dragon Empire added to Modern Powers (2026-05-13).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-16.

---

## Usage

Copy the fenced block below verbatim into the system prompt or opening turn of any working session that needs to be grounded in Eadras canon. The primer is **interpretive and condensed** — it is not the canonical source of record. The authoritative source for the principles list is [[principles-of-eadras]]; the authoritative source for any specific cosmology/timeline/order claim is the relevant in-universe wiki page. When this primer and a wiki page disagree, the wiki page wins and this primer should be updated.

Treat the primer as a **derived export** that can drift if canon changes upstream. Back-propagate non-trivial canon updates here so the prompt-load doesn't carry stale claims into other sessions. When the primer is materially out of date, regenerate from the current state of [[principles-of-eadras]] + the relevant cosmology/timeline/order pages and version-promote (`v2` → `v3`, etc.).

## Protocol

```
eadras-core-canon & thematic principles (v2)
Purpose
This file is the permanent foundational context for Eadras. Load it into almost every major prompt. It establishes cosmology, tone, metaphysical assumptions, historical skeleton, divine structure, magical structure, thematic identity, and civilizational psychology. It is interpretive and incorporates all principles from Principles of Eadras.

Core Cosmology
Eadras exists within a larger Omniverse created by the Omniversal Creator. The Omniverse is surrounded by Chaos — not evil, but the absence of coherent reality. A Mythos is a bounded region of imposed structure floating within Chaos; Eadras is one such Mythos.
Reality in Eadras is observation-dependent: things that are not observed do not fully exist; things that are named are observed by their namer. The Mythos is slightly observer-hungry because the Primordial Duo (Evye & Fryr) sacrificed themselves completely into creation rather than ruling from above. There is no ultimate external observer; the Tome of Baldaran is the designated cosmic observer that partially compensates.
The Primordial Duo created the Elder Gods and then sacrificed themselves into creation itself. The Elder Gods are therefore inheritors and stewards of a reality built from sacrifice — they were born into responsibility.

The Elder Gods
Ancient beings embodying major conceptual domains. They are not perfectly moral, omniscient, or emotionally stable abstractions. They are:

ancient
exhausted administrators
political
wounded
prideful
affectionate
petty
deeply shaped by millions of years of history

They rarely intervene directly (Doctrine of the Unheld Hand + institutional restraint). They deceive one another through long cons rather than raw power (Divine Containment Protocols). Direct divine presence in Eadras requires mediation: every god has exactly one Icon on the Prime Material (the god's only Prime body) plus one or more Simulacra in the spirit realms. Avatars are impossible in Eadras for any god — the Mythos is too wounded to bear them; the strongest Prime-Material divine response available is full Icon-Habitation.

Major Elder Gods:

Ioa – Rulership, law, sovereignty, command. Speaks rarely. Authority through certainty.
Nara – Home, continuity, protection, femininity, generational care.
Raja – Masculinity, strength, craft, labor, direct action.
Vos – Civilization, institutions, roads, systems, administration.
Qoxli – Wildness, beasts, instinct, ecosystems.
Daj – War, weapons, armies, fortresses.
Nox – Night, secrecy, hidden truths, loathsome knowledge.
Sylph – Foolishness, masks, laughter, fae, merciful absurdity.
Lirra – Magic, the Weave, disciplined power.
Shali and Shani – Divided aspects of death.

Beyond the Elder Gods, Eadras has Elderborn (children of Elder Gods), The Three (Mark, Rand, Melania — adopted children of Dao who created the three progenitor races and are universally hands-off on engineered descendant races), and Ascended Mortal Gods (mortals who crossed an apotheosis threshold and were acknowledged by the gods; the mechanism is unique to each case, and only living mortals can ascend). The Divine Bureaucracy in Solarion is run by Ascended Mortal Gods with a celestial-spirit workforce; its terrifyingly efficient mirror on Nyxar is the Infernal Bureaucracy, staffed by Devils.

Magic and the Weave
Magic on Eadras is one substance with two states (Eldritch — free-flowing; Mana — matrix-bound) plus a general/umbrella term (Quintessence). Magic is unusually abundant on Eadras because the Primordial Duo sacrificed themselves completely into creation — every bit of matter on Eadras carries some mana, which is why Eadras is one of the seven Mythoi closest to the Bastion of Light.

Eldritch is reflected Sourceshine — raw solar magical energy transformed into the workable, mind-obeying form when it interacts with bedrock, sea, soil, or living matter. The Source itself is the surface representation of The All, the leftover creation energy of Evye and Fryr left throughout the Mythos. Druids tap The All directly from below; every other tradition works with the surface forms.

There are four major magical orders, plus a sub-priesthood:

Wizards (by far the most numerous) — trained, book-based, multi-source. Draw on Eldritch/Quintessence, positive/negative energy, and alien mana, with the energy used depending on the spell. The common learned magical workforce of Eadras.
Priests and Clerics (close second) — channel via Godpower drawn from the Divine Bureaucracy's pool. Daily capacity is set by Wisdom, current Favor with the deity (Neutral / Positive / Negative / God's Favorite), and experience. They do not memorize spells; restoration timing is set by the deity's temperament.
Netharim (distant third; rare, powerful, feared) — intuitive reality-shapers and members of Order Sharanel, whose power scales with the depth and accuracy of their personally-studied internal model of reality. A Netharim with a profound model of (say) thermodynamics produces thermodynamic effects far beyond a Netharim with a shallower one. The order's prestige tracks model-depth more than any other measure.
Lexanari (extremely rare; almost mythic) — innate-only mortal speakers of Primordial Language fragments. The capacity is hereditary; most candidates die in training. The consequences of every utterance are load-bearing in a way no other order's work approaches.

Druids are not a fourth standalone order. They are a specialized sub-priesthood of Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox — self-sufficient wilderness-skilled mediators between mortals and the fae, drawing on The All directly through nature.

Netharim and Lexanari share an underlying grammar called Pillar Magic — five Command Pillars (Control / Transform / Create / Destroy / Perceive) × five Substance Pillars (Essence / Matter / Flesh / Mind / Spirit) = twenty-five canonical combinations, with per-Pillar mastery progression.

The Weave is a purely defensive cosmological structure woven across Eadras by Lirra during the Demon War to prevent outsiders (demons in particular, but any extra-Mythos predator) from siphoning Eadras's magic. It is not a creative or transmissive substrate — it does not generate magic, does not transmit spells, does not channel energy for casting. It is the shield, not the sword. The Weave's defensive function operates through three load-bearing properties:

It carries Eldritch — the medium through which the post-Demon-War planetary Eldritch flow circulates in its regulated form, making the flow inaccessible to outsider magic that would otherwise siphon from the open cycle.
It is sustained by the lives of every magic user in Eadras — wizard, Netharim, druid, priest, Lexanari, or otherwise. Each practising magic user's life contributes a small constant load to the Weave's coherence. The mechanism is automatic and life-tied; most magic users do not know they are doing it. Mass death of magic users weakens the Weave cumulatively.
It is anchored at the conduit junctions by Windspeakers — Netharim born with markedly greater innate capacity than ordinary Netharim, whose lives are structural anchors for the Weave's defensive geometry at its most strained points.

The Weave does not buffer external magical radiation: it does not filter incoming Sourceflares (atmosphere and magnetic field do that). The Weave protects the environment within which the four orders operate; it does not make them more powerful.

Timeline Skeleton (high-level public framing)

Arathi Era – First true hypercivilization (≈10 million years). Mastered crystalmancy, magitek, conduit infrastructure. The lost civilizational ceiling.
Arathi Cataclysm – Kudur-eṭir forged/claimed the Spear of Crystallized Entropy → Worldbreaker entered via the First Breach. Shattered the world, created plate tectonics, killed most life, permanently traumatized history. Foundational geological and metaphysical trauma.
Chaos Crusades – Two breaches of the Mythos Shell through Kudur-eṭir's old aperture; introduced horrors and aberrations.
Demon War – Xar'vion summoned a Demon Prince → ontological warfare lasting roughly twenty years; half of all living creatures lost; Weave created; Beast-Folk born; Great Betrayal (Velveth, then Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion, poisons Emperor Thoramis Firethorn under Article Four of the Divine Concordat) and Banishment (all three progenitor races exiled to the three reflections of Gaia under Article Six). Foundational societal and existential trauma.
Anti-Magic Crusade – Roughly 9,000–10,000 years ago, in the decades preceding the previous Grand Conjunction. Engineered by the Black Hand as a Spear-search diversion, built atop the Century of Sorrows: a hundred-year deliberate ramp-up of magical disasters (poison clouds, plagues of undead, chaos pockets, screaming flesh-crops) that turned the populace against all magic. Doctrine: All magic is wicked. The Crusade swept up wizards, Netharim, druids, priests, Lexanari, hedge-witches, and psionic practitioners alike under the flat doctrine of the Cold Hearth. Halted when the gods inhabited their Icons and confronted the participating churches and martial orders directly. Three Windspeakers were killed before the halt; the resulting Weave damage at three conduit junctions caused the Thousand-Year Dimming. The Amunorian Empire was broken; the Silent Century stripped the participating priesthoods of divine power for one hundred years; the Knights of Lirra were founded as reparation, drawing power from both their original god and Lirra.
First Rebrightening (modern era) – The present era following the Thousand-Year Dimming. Civilization, magic, and trade are rising again, but every society carries inherited trauma. Progress exists; fear of progress also exists. The next Grand Conjunction approaches. FR dating is semi-formal (Sharanel / Tome / Lirran clergy use it; common folk often do not).

Major Modern Powers

Bold – Independent elected Conduit Republic (no longer a kingdom). Flashy, mercantile, magical, politically aggressive. Closest to Arathi-scale infrastructure. Anchored by the Tower of Seven Spires, the founding chapter-house and oldest Tower of Lirra of Order Sharanel.
Orden – Viking-and-Hanseatic Conduit Compact; disciplined seafaring trade league of conduit-anchored cities; symbiotic alliance with the Merrow (Deep Friends / Fish Friends).
Amunorians – Scholarly, elegant, politically dangerous diaspora across the Azure Basin (Renaissance Italy + Ancient Egypt + Islamic Golden Age fusion); self-image as true heirs of the Velrathi legacy; broken in the Anti-Magic Crusade; modern faded-glory remnant.
Azure Basin – Non-Roman Mediterranean fragmented patchwork of maritime poleis, lagoon republics (Venethar), Provençal principalities, Iberian Marches, North-African oasis-cities, Balkan highland clans, and Levantine ports. Southern corner of the modern trade-triangle with Orden and Bold.
Dragon Empire – Far-eastern modern-era polity ruled by a coalition of semi-divine dragons; comprehensive bureaucratic meritocracy with pictogram-and-hashmark skill ranks and free public education; Tang+Khmer aesthetic; authoritarian-meritocratic pole of the three-cultures ethical triangle; founded by a post-Demon-War dragon-mercenary coup.
Beast-Folk – The Laughing Third of the Rathar; deliberate joy and refusal of traumatic memory; maintain Fae-da'Nar (frontier compact of coexistence with humans) and patrol the Demonfall Marches.
Katharim – Death-priests; the Weighted Third of the Rathar; disciplined, solemn, duty-bound; necropolis of Kharathen at the western edge of the Demonfall Marches.

Tone Guidance
Eadras should feel: ancient • emotionally layered • intellectually serious • occasionally absurd • tragic without nihilism • hopeful without naivety • grand without losing ordinary life.
It is epic dark fantasy with earned hope, not grimdark. Light and darkness amplify each other. Humor, warmth, and beauty exist inside a world that remembers catastrophe. Deep time is core identity. Mystery is stronger than explanation. Humor prevents tonal collapse. Probability is more interesting than destiny. Prophecy creates administrative panic.


Thematic Spine (non-negotiable)
continuity · memory · catastrophe · identity · structure · survival · meaning
Every element of Eadras must touch at least one.
```

## Changes from v1

The v1 ingest (also 2026-05-16, morning) carried a number of pre-rework or stale canon claims that the v2 revision corrects:

- **The Weave** — was described in v1 in pre-2026-05-09 terms ("modern magical infrastructure", "Netharim biologically tied to the Weave", "Windtalkers stabilize it"). v2 reframes it as a **purely defensive cosmological structure** that *carries Eldritch*, is *sustained by the lives of every magic user*, and is *anchored at conduit junctions by Windspeakers* (Netharim with greater innate capacity). "Windtalkers" corrected to "Windspeakers" throughout.
- **The four magical orders** — v1 listed none explicitly; v2 introduces the canonical Wizards / Priests / Netharim / Lexanari hierarchy and notes Druids as a sub-priesthood of Qoxli / Sylph / Nox. Pillar Magic (5 × 5 = 25 combinations) added as the shared grammar of Netharim and Lexanari.
- **Anti-Magic Crusade** — v1 had a brief unspecified-date entry mentioning "killed Windtalkers" / "destroyed Sarathis". v2 places it 9–10kya, names the Century of Sorrows as its basis, identifies Icon-Habitation as the halting mechanism, names the Silent Century and Knights of Lirra as reparations, and identifies the Amunorian Empire (not "Sarathis") as the broken power.
- **Modern Powers list** — Dragon Empire added (2026-05-13 canon); Bold updated to **elected Conduit Republic** (not a kingdom); Orden updated to **Viking + Hanseatic Conduit Compact** (the modern trade-league framing); Amunorians named where v1 said "Sarathis"; Azure Basin given its non-Roman patchwork description.
- **Demon War entry** — Velveth named explicitly (full mortal title Vorandethi Kathira Spellsinger of House Valtharion); Articles Four and Six of the Divine Concordat named; twenty-year duration named.
- **Modern era** — labelled **First Rebrightening** with the canonical FR-dating-is-semi-formal qualifier.
- **Divine framing** — Icon-and-Simulacrum mediation made explicit; Avatars-impossible principle stated; Ascended Mortals / Divine Bureaucracy / Infernal Bureaucracy briefly framed.

The Part I (36 in-world principles) and Part II (8 author-side principles) lists were left structurally unchanged — they match the current [[principles-of-eadras]] page exactly. Principle 13 was clarified inline (one Icon per god; Avatars impossible in Eadras) to bring it forward as a load-bearing fact.

## Drift-watch going forward

The primer should be regenerated when any of the following shift materially upstream:

- New principles added to [[principles-of-eadras]] (currently 36 Part I + 8 Part II)
- Magic-system changes (especially anything touching the Weave's defensive function, Pillar Magic, or the four-order hierarchy)
- Modern Powers additions or restructuring (the three-cultures triangle is currently load-bearing; any fourth pole would shift the framing)
- New named-canon events at the scale of the Crusade or the Demon War
- Changes to the Tier-1 Icon-Simulacrum framework or the Avatars-impossible cosmological floor
