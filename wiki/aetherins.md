# Aetherins

**Summary**: The Aetherin were superior humans engineered by the [[tarans|Tarran]] as shock troops against chaos and demonic incursions â€” one of the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]] who **went totally extinct** at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]], their diluted bloodlines surviving as modern Humans.

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt, Taran.txt, Notes for Changes for 5-10-26.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-10

---

## Origin

The Aetherin were created by the [[tarans|Tarran]] as one of three child races shaped for specific martial purposes:

- **Titans** â€” warriors against chaotic incursions; ancestors of modern Giants
- **Aetherin** â€” shock troops against chaos and demonic incursions; ancestors of modern Humans
- **[[mitans|Mitans]]** â€” miners and tunnel warriors; ancestors of Dwarves and Halflings

Per established canon ([[the-three]] created only the three progenitor races directly; child races were shaped by the progenitors themselves), [[the-three|Melania]] created the [[tarans|Tarran]] alone. The Tarran themselves engineered the Aetherin; Melania did not direct, assist, or oversee the work. (Canon clarification 2026-05-10.)

> Note: "Aetherin" supersedes the earlier draft names "Extans/Spartans" used in some older notes. **Aetherin is the canonical name.**

## Form and Role

The Aetherin were **superior humans** â€” taller, stronger, swifter, and more durable than their modern descendants. They were bred specifically for:

- **Shock combat** against chaos incursions
- **Counter-attack tactics** against fast-moving threats
- **Chaos suppression** in the centuries when raw [[chaos|Chaos]] energies and proto-demonic incursions first began intruding from beyond the world (the term "demonic" properly belongs to the [[demon-war|Demon War]] era; pre-Demon-War the threat was Chaos and its native life)

They served the cooperative pre-Firethorn order alongside their [[velrathi|Velrathi]]-engineered [[orugi|Orug]] cousins and the [[elfen|Elfen]]-engineered [[el-mer|El'mer]].

## Total Extinction

The Aetherin **went extinct as a pure people** during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

They died **almost to the last as shock troops** against demonic incursions across many fronts. Their final, unified stand came at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] â€” the Final Wall â€” where they joined the [[orugi|Orug]] and [[el-mer|El'mer]] in the legendary three-day defense.

### At the Final Wall

The Aetherin were the **striking spear** of the defense. While the [[orugi|Orug]] formed the shield core and the [[el-mer|El'mer]] healed and barricaded, the Aetherin **launched ferocious counter-attacks into the demonic flanks**, buying minutes with their lives.

Their final cry: *"We were made to break the storm."*

Then they charged with their cousins one last time.

**Not one Aetherin survived.** The pure bloodline ended that day. See [[three-who-stood]] for the joint memorial.

## Modern Descendants â€” Humans

Diluted Aetherin blood survives in **modern Humans**.

Humans are described as **natural devolution** of the Aetherin â€” smaller, less capable, less long-lived. Modern human stature is roughly half what the Aetherin once stood; their endurance, strength, and martial instinct are pale echoes.

But the **resilient spark** that defines humanity â€” the willingness to push beyond exhaustion, the refusal to accept lost causes, the instinct to charge into impossible odds â€” traces directly to Aetherin ancestry. Some scholars argue that **every truly heroic moment in human history is a faint resonance of the Final Wall**.

## Cultural Memory

Humans (Aetherin descendants) speak of *"the uncles who bought our chance"* with quiet pride. The Aetherin are honored at the **Monument of the Threefold Oath**, where their statue depicts a spear-warrior in mid-charge, lance leveled, face calm.

Many modern human knightly orders trace symbolic ancestry to specific Aetherin units that fell at the Final Wall. Oaths of service and shield-rituals across human cultures often include obscure phrases that scholars believe are corrupted Aetherin war-cries.

The **Night of the Gray Wall** (annual) is mostly an [[orcs|Orc]] festival, but human knightly orders adjacent to orcish lands often participate, marking themselves with small symbols of all three races.

## Cultural Disagreement Among Exiles

Among the **exiled [[tarans|Tarran]] on Prime-like [[gaia|Gaia]]**, the Aetherin's sacrifice is remembered with complex emotion. The Tarran of the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] honor the Aetherin as ideals their own people **failed to fully embody** â€” particularly given that the [[great-betrayal|Tarran Emperor's failure to send reinforcements]] is what doomed them.

This is one of the few subjects on which the Gray Matriarchy expresses something resembling collective shame.

## Aetherin in the Tomb of the Last Guardians

In the [[tomb-of-the-last-guardians|Tomb of the Last Guardians]], the Aetherin plinth bears no bodies â€” only **empty armor stands** of polished steel and bronze, each missing a spear (the spears were given freely as offerings by visiting human knights and warriors over ten thousand years).

Each stand bears a name and the single shared line: *"Made to break the storm. Held when no one came."*
