# Mana Metals

**Summary**: Mana metals are common metals (silver, gold, copper, iron) naturally transformed by sustained mana exposure into stronger, lighter, mana-storing variants — and the alloys made from them, culminating in Magisterium, the apex tier capable of holding permanent self-sustaining magic.

**Sources**: New information about Arathi Cataclysm.txt; Mana.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## Overview

Four base metals, when exposed to sustained mana — typically deep underground or near mantle activity — undergo a natural transformation that changes their physical properties and gives them the capacity to **store mana within their structure**. From these four bases, all higher mana alloys derive.

Each transformed base metal can be alloyed with one or more of the others to produce magical alloys whose properties scale roughly with composition complexity. The four-component apex is **Magisterium**.

Mana metals are the canonical example of the principle from [[magic-in-eadras#Quintessence, Eldritch, Mana|the three-state model]]: **mana is magical energy held within the matrix of some matter.** All matter on Eadras carries some mana, but mana metals are inorganic matter whose mineral matrix retains and stabilizes mana especially well — well enough to be *built with* and *worked into devices*. The transformation that turns silver into mithril, gold into orichalcum, copper into kylum, or iron into valthyr is precisely this: ordinary matter becoming a matrix that holds substantially more mana than the un-transformed form, with that capacity now baked into the metal's working properties.

## Base Mana Metals

### Mithril
**Source**: Mana-transformed silver.
**Appearance**: Lighter than silver.
**Properties**: Lighter, stronger, capable of storing mana within its structure.
**Formation**: Forms under intense mana exposure, typically deep underground or near mantle activity.
**Use**: Highly prized by enchanters for its ability to hold complex spells.

### Orichalcum
**Source**: Mana-transformed gold.
**Appearance**: Distinctive reddish-gold color.
**Properties**: Heavier than normal gold, far stronger, stores mana.
**Use**: Structural and ornamental in high-end magical work; named in [[altea|Altea]]'s four signature building substances; substance of the **Orichalcum Ring of Command**'s golems (see [[arathi]]).
**Arathi body-integration**: Among Arathi practice, orichalcum was worked as **personal-scale magitech metalwork integrated with the body** — filaments traced along scales, accents woven into crest feathers, and fitted harness plates. The mana-storage property makes it a fitted personal infrastructure for the wearer's magical practice; the appearance is also why "gold" accents are visible on Arathi who would otherwise have no gold in their natural red-to-violet pigmentation. (The crystals and gems that orbit a working Arathi magus are *not* orichalcum metalwork — those are separate magical implements; see [[arathi]].)

### Kylum
**Source**: Mana-infused copper.
**Appearance**: Bright, almost glowing.
**Properties**: Excellent conductor of both electricity and magic.
**Use**: Mages use it in wands and magical circuitry.

### Valthyr
**Source**: Mana-infused iron.
**Appearance**: Iron-like but visibly changed.
**Properties**: Significantly stronger and more durable than normal iron while remaining workable.
**Use**: Preferred metal for high-quality weapons and armor that need to hold enchantments.

## Alloys

### Valthrene
**Composition**: Valthyr + carbon (mana-steel).
**Properties**: Exceptionally tough; holds a sharp edge extremely well.
**Use**: Standard material for masterwork weapons and heavy armor.

### Argentus
**Composition**: Mithril + Kylum.
**Appearance**: Beautiful silvery-white with a slight blue sheen.
**Properties**: Excellent at conducting and shaping magical energy.

### Aurelius
**Composition**: Orichalcum + Kylum.
**Appearance**: Rich golden-red.
**Properties**: Particularly good at storing large amounts of mana for long periods.

### Elyrium
**Composition**: Mithril + Orichalcum.
**Appearance**: Brilliant platinum-like.
**Properties**: Considered one of the most powerful magical metals; capable of holding extremely complex and long-lasting enchantments.

### Magisterium
**Composition**: Mithril + Orichalcum + Valthyr (the three-base apex).
**Properties**: Highest-tier mana alloy. Extremely rare and powerful. **Said to be able to hold permanent, self-sustaining magic.**
**Notable use**: One of the four signature building substances of [[altea|Altea]] (alongside crystal, flame-veined marble, and orichalcum). With Altea annihilated and *no remnants whatsoever*, any modern Magisterium must come from rare remnants of *other* Arathi cities.
