# Taran Venom-Aged Liquor

**Summary**: The signature alcoholic tradition of the [[tarans|Taran]] is liquor distilled from wine and **aged with carefully selected animal venom**. The venom largely denatures over the aging process, leaving behind a complex flavor profile that the Taran prize. Because the Taran possess [[tarans#Poison Immunity|near-total inherited immunity to poisons and toxins]], the drink is completely safe for them; for non-Taran races it remains mildly to moderately dangerous, which has made it a subtle test of who is and who is not truly Taran.

## How it is made

The base is wine â€” distilled to a strong spirit, then aged in vessels into which **carefully selected animal venom** has been introduced. Different Taran houses and cellars use different venom combinations, and the venom selection is the variable that differentiates one cellar's product from another.

The venom **denatures over the aging period** as the protein structure breaks down in the alcohol. What remains is the **flavor profile** the venom imparted before denaturing â€” a complex, layered character that ordinary aged spirits cannot reproduce. Different venoms leave different signatures: viper venoms produce one kind of complexity, scorpion venoms another, certain insect venoms a third.

The minimum aging period to achieve adequate denaturing is **six months**. This is the floor; nothing aged less than six months is offered for sale or service, because below that floor the active venom content remains genuinely dangerous to anyone (Taran or otherwise) and the drink is not yet a proper venom-aged liquor.

## Class structure of aging

By the [[firethorn-empire|Imperial era]], aging time had become a clear marker of class:

| Aging | Status | Drinker |
|---|---|---|
| **6 months** (the minimum) | *Common swill* | Commoners; everyday service in working-class taverns |
| **20+ years** | The standard for nobility | What a noble household serves at table; the floor below which serious nobles will not drink |
| **50+ years** | The finest | Reserve cellars; ceremonial occasions; gifts between great houses |

Vintages of 100+ years exist and are extraordinary; they are sometimes brought out of cellars for moments of imperial significance and otherwise rest undisturbed. The very oldest known venom-aged liquor in modern circulation is a small cellar reportedly dating to the late Imperial period â€” currently disputed by serious connoisseurs and not openly catalogued.

## Safety profile

For [[tarans|Taran]] drinkers â€” whose [[tarans#Poison Immunity|poison immunity]] has been a genetic trait for thousands of years following the inoculation ritual â€” the drink is **completely safe** at any age above the six-month minimum. The denatured venom has no effect on Taran biology beyond the flavor it imparts.

For **non-Taran** drinkers, the same drink is **mildly to moderately dangerous**. Some venoms denature more completely than others; six-month liquor still carries enough residual active material to produce sickness, hallucinations, or in cases of high-toxicity source venoms, more serious consequences. Aged 20+ years the residual is much smaller; a single noble-grade glass is rarely fatal to a healthy non-Taran but commonly produces a memorable evening of nausea, sweating, and heightened sensory disturbance.

This safety asymmetry has made venom-aged liquor a **subtle social test**. A host who serves a venom-aged drink to a guest is implicitly asking the guest to demonstrate Taran identity. A guest who drinks it without ill effect is confirming. A guest who refuses, asks for an alternative, or accepts and then visibly suffers is â€” in Taran social calculus â€” *not us*. Whether this is read as friendly clarification or as a hostile probe depends entirely on the social relationship at issue.

The convention is informally known as *the cup test* in modern Taran cultural commentary, though the practice predates the term.

## Cultural place

Venom-aged liquor is the drink for **important social moments** in Taran life â€” formal dinners, treaty negotiations, funeral feasts, the ceremonial marking of a new household head. Casual drinking is more often beer or unaged wine. The venom-aged variety is reserved.

It also functions as a **gift currency** between Taran houses. A bottle of properly-aged venom liquor is a recognized formal gift; vintages and house cellars are remembered for centuries.

The pairing of the drink's signature with [[tarans|Taran]] cultural attitudes runs deep: the Taran believe in toughening through deliberate exposure (see the [[tarans#Poison Immunity|inoculation ritual]]), they value pragmatism over squeamishness, and they understand the *with-us-or-against-us* polarity of their cultural worldview as a kind of practical realism rather than aggression. Venom-aged liquor concentrates all three attitudes into a drinkable form.

## As [[aqua-vitae|Aqua Vitae]] base

Distilling venom-aged liquor further produces a Taran [[aqua-vitae|Aqua Vitae]] that carries the *flavor-without-toxicity* signature of the source. Whether the magical signature carries the source venom's character even after the venom itself has fully denatured â€” and whether this matters for healing-vs-harm potion alignment â€” is open canon.
