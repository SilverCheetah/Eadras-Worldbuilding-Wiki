# Elves

**Summary**: The descendants of the [[el-mer|El'mer]], the [[elfen|Elfen]]-engineered race created to oversee natural balance on land. Modern elves are a rare, caste-structured, purpose-born, plant-linked, photosynthetic, magic-singing people who guard the natural world. They do not reproduce with one another conventionally; new elves are grown whole inside living **womb trees** at magical convergence points, formed over a year by the entire village. Their defining wisdom is **restraint** — they could feed the world to abundance, and refuse to, because unchecked abundance breaks the balance they exist to keep.

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt, Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Elfen.txt, elfin_culture_settled_ingest.txt (2026-06-02, culture and biology)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-02

---

## Origin and lineage

Elves descend from the [[el-mer|El'mer]], the [[elfen|Elfen]]-engineered race tasked with overseeing natural balance in every land environment. The El'mer themselves perished as a distinct people at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Final Wall]] during the [[demon-war|Demon War]], holding the line at the forest sanctuaries against the demonic flank (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt). The diluted El'mer bloodlines that survived became modern elves — a "natural devolution," smaller, less uniformly long-lived, and less magically capable than the towering El'mer before them (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt). Some El'mer blood also absorbed into beast-folk populations.

Modern elves nonetheless preserve the **whole living form** of the [[elfen|Elfen]] way — song-shaped architecture, plant-based foodways, communal gestation, and the guardianship of natural balance — wherever the bloodline has stayed thick. The culture described below is that inherited form as the elves live it today.

Elves are **the least numerous race by far.** Their scarcity is not accident but the product of their reproduction, their village structure, and a deliberate philosophical restraint about their own numbers as much as everyone else's.

## Subtypes

Elves come in **multiple types adapted to different environments** (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt). The full catalogue of subtypes is open canon; the [[varkhul-native-elves|native elf tribes of Varkhul]] are one documented branch, a full communal society sealed on the prison continent since before it was a prison.

## Biology

Elves are **mildly photosynthetic.** Their skin carries a green tinge, and standing in sunlight gives them energy. This does not replace eating, but it contributes to their vitality and reinforces their plant-linked nature.

Elves possess standard humanoid sexual organs — female elves have wombs — because their makers built them able to **breed with the other original races.** They **cannot, however, reproduce with one another** by conventional means. They reproduce with other races, and a female elf can **store the genetic material** from such a union for later incorporation into the womb-tree process. This is how the elven lineage keeps from narrowing inward: without outside genetic input and a sufficient village population, elven heredity would funnel too tight. Elves do not think of this in modern genetic terms, but they feel a real unease when a village grows too small and stops producing new elves. A village generally tries to hold a floor of roughly **30–50 elves**, the minimum that feels viable for producing healthy new ones.

Lifespan is **not universal long life** but a function of [[#Caste|caste]]: a lower-caste elf may live only around twenty years, while an upper-caste elf lives for centuries and a monarch may persist far longer. Longevity itself is part of design and social order.

## Reproduction — the womb trees

Elves are **born whole.** A new elf does not arrive as an infant and grow through childhood; it emerges from gestation already physically mature for its intended role and carrying the knowledge and cultural grounding to enter society.

A new elf is created through a communal ritual centered on a **womb tree** — a specialized [[#Spirit trees|spirit tree]], a conscious tree with a willing spirit bound into it, an aware and active participant in the making. The process begins when one elf decides to produce a new elf and **contributes part of their own flesh**, binding it to the spirit tree. This grows a **flesh womb** on the tree's side — a sacred, purposeful swelling, cancer-like in shape but holy in meaning — inside which the new elf grows for about **one year**, nourished by the donor's flesh, the spirit tree, the land at the convergence point, the village, and the turning seasons.

The defining feature is that **every member of the village becomes a secondary parent.** Nearly every day, villagers press a hand to the side of the womb and bless the growing elf — and the touch is not symbolic only: it **exchanges genetic material and memories.** Through this daily contact the growing elf absorbs language, culture, practical knowledge, the memories of village life, the emotional shape of the community, and the values of the people. Because gestation runs a full year, the elf learns the cycle of the seasons from within the tree; this seasonal education is foundational to elven consciousness. The spirit tree teaches too, through its own deep patience and rootedness. When the elf emerges it is not a blank child but **whole**: formed, educated, spiritually rooted, and already tied to the land and the village.

**Womb trees are always placed where two magical lines intersect** — convergence points, the places where the land's energies gather and cross, like two rivulets joining into a stream. This ties each elf to a specific landscape from before birth.

A womb tree with an elf growing in it radiates harmony and peace, and **forest animals are drawn to it.** They come to investigate where they sense no danger, familiarizing the growing elf with them before birth — and they remain, forming a **living guard-circle**: anyone approaching the tree with intent to harm is sensed and attacked. The womb tree is protected not by walls but by the wildlife that gathers around it.

## Caste

Elven society maintains a **rigid caste structure**, and it is not softened into meritocracy. Caste is not overridden by exceptional talent: if a low-caste elf proves a strong magic user, society does not elevate them — they may be trained only within strict limits, denied advanced instruction, or trained under control. Caste is tied to [[#Biology|lifespan]] (lower castes short-lived, upper castes long-lived) and to assigned [[#Purpose-born existence|purpose]]. This rigidity is part of what makes elven society alien and frequently uncomfortable to outsiders, who arrive expecting graceful egalitarian woodland folk and meet instead a stratified, purpose-bound order.

## Purpose-born existence

Most elves are **born for a purpose, and fulfilling it makes them happy**; failing it makes them sad, and other ambitions are often inconsequential to them. This makes elves psychologically alien to humans, goblins, and the like: a purpose-born elf usually simply wants to do what they were made to do, with little concern for personal ambition, social mobility, or self-invention. From the elf's own perspective this is not oppression — the role aligns with their created nature.

But **atavistic births** occur: elves born individualistic, with desires that do not match their assigned purpose. On the mainland an atavist is treated as a failure of the womb-tree process and is often cast out or leaves — which means **the wandering atavist is the elf outsiders meet most.** Other races thus build a false picture of elves as individualistic, mysterious travelers, and are shocked, on entering elven lands, by the rigid communal society they actually find. (The [[varkhul-native-elves|native elf tribes of Varkhul]] are the great exception: they deliberately *produce* atavists and treat the calling as an institution rather than a defect.)

## Magical singing

Elves **sing magic into existence** — their defining art — and it works most readily on living matter, especially plants and trees. With song they grow a tree into a home, grow a home into a tree's side without harming it, reinforce living wood, accelerate growth, guide many trees into one structure, coax plants to produce unusual substances, and breed entirely new varieties. A lone elf singing is **slow**; the miraculous feats require a **group** singing in harmony — dozens together can raise a giant tree from a sapling in less than a day. Every elf can sing to some degree and contribute to communal songs, but not every elf has the power to lead a major song or to work useful independent magic beyond minor effects; leadership takes stronger talent, training, and often caste permission. The art is tied somehow to sexual maturity, by a mechanism the elves themselves do not fully understand.

The same craft **transmutes wood** into materials with the properties of stone or metal while preserving its natural grain — a vitrified-wood fireplace inside a living house, or weapons and armor that keep organic curves and woodlike appearance while striking and turning blades like iron, steel, bronze, or brass. Even in war the elves do not abandon the aesthetic and metaphysical language of living nature.

## Memory tapestries and hair records

Elves keep woven records inspired by knotted-cord systems (quipu) but transformed into magic. **Elven hair retains magic exceptionally well**, so braided into cord and thread it becomes a recording medium that holds far more than symbols: spells, memories, instructions, history, even emotional impressions can be cast into it. Such records function as spell-books — knotted and enchanted rather than inked.

At the highest level the elves keep **living tapestries** that store memory directly. The great tapestry in the capital records the major events of elven history; during **festivals of remembrance** it is brought out so that any living elf may touch it and *feel* the history — not study it but experience it, sensation and perspective and all. This lets the whole people periodically reconnect with its collective past, and it expresses the central elven conviction: **memory is not information, memory is responsibility.** It is the standing reminder of why the elves protect nature and keep the balance.

## Villages and the capital

Elves live mostly in **small forest villages of fewer than a hundred**, scattered and deliberately modest because the people are few and their ecological philosophy discourages excessive growth. Settlements are not built in forests but **grown from** them: houses shaped into and from living trees, rising and changing over centuries as the trees grow, reinforced by song into stable living architecture rather than dead timber. Because the [[wyldglimmer|Wyldglimmer]] mirrors the natural world, every elven structure casts a corresponding reflection there; elven building shapes both the material forest and its spirit reflection.

There is **one great capital city** of roughly 100,000 elves (with perhaps a few regional cities, one per major region), built around an immense tree about **a kilometer tall**, with the council chamber at its crown. The capital holds the **only underground vault the elves have ever built** — keeping the great memory tapestry and the people's other treasures deep in the earth, and carrying the tapestry *up* to the high council chamber when the people need to remember and decide. Memory kept deep below, raised to the highest place of counsel: it is the culture's self-image in a single image.

## Spirit trees

Elves **awaken trees and bind willing spirits into them** — never against the spirit's will. These conscious spirit-bound trees (sometimes called treants, the term unsettled) serve as guardians, elders, teachers, [[#Reproduction — the womb trees|womb trees]], sacred landmarks, village anchors, and living memory-holders.

## Foodways, commerce, and companions

Elves **eat no meat** — a choice, not a strict biological impossibility — and their diet is vast because they can create and alter plants at will: cultivated and wild forest plants, magically altered fruits, leaves, roots, fungi, seeds, and herbs, much eaten raw, with a love of stews and soups. Their plant-shaping can coax wholly new sensory experiences from plants; the established example is the **roughly 500 unique tea varieties** the [[elfen|Elfen]] developed for the [[velrathi-tea-culture|Velrathi]] (teas that taste of glacier-meltwater, or of chai with no spice added), demonstrating that elves are less farmers than **life-artisans**. They prefer to **bathe in pools** fed by paired portals to the **Elemental Plane of Water** (an inflow and an outflow), the temperature set to taste.

Their external trade is **brisk but restrained**: rare foodstuffs, and **customized animals** sold for large fees. An **awakened animal** has had its brain and awareness magically enhanced to roughly the communicative capacity of a one-year-old child — it can express simple needs and bonds but remains fundamentally an animal, not a person. (This is distinct from a **familiar**, which is an animal *inhabited by a spirit*, making it far more personlike and spiritually bonded.) Custom commissions include psionically communicating pets, a tiger the size of a house cat, an elephant the size of a large dog.

The deepest expression of elven wisdom is **restraint in supply.** The elves could provide the world limitless food; they refuse, because abundance without foresight lets other races overbreed, overrun the land, and destroy the balance. Their guardianship is not generosity but stewardship — giving only what the world can survive. Younger, expansionist races read this as arrogance or cruelty; the elves read those races as dangerously short-sighted. The tension is permanent.

## Deities

The premier goddess of the elves is **[[qoxli|Qoxli]]**, Elder Goddess of the wild, animals, freedom, and the chase. Alongside her, **[[sylph|Sylph]]** — Elder Goddess of foolishness, indulgence, and independence, and patron of all Fae — is a patron of the elves in her fae, fate-touched, shenanigan-loving aspect. There are also lesser elven deities and **[[gods-of-eadras|Ascended Mortal]]** elven gods (such as [[aramet|Aramet]]), once elves who crossed the threshold into divinity. The elven gods are taken as expressions of the will of the wild itself: through them the land calls forth new elves, new villages, and expansion only when the wild requires its guardians — never by politics alone.

## Magical inheritance and relations

Modern elves retain only echoes of El'mer power, but elven mages still surface as **[[runeswords|Runesword]] wielders** alongside humans, orcs, and beast-folk — chosen by [[dao|Dao]]'s blades despite the original intent for Tarran royalty (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

- **Beast-folk** share genetic inheritance with elves through mixed El'mer blood.
- **[[fae|Fae]]** descend from a different Elfen child race — the **Sidhe** — but elves and fae share a deep genealogical kinship through common Elfen ancestry.
- The **exiled [[elfen|Elfen]]** in [[gaia|Wyldglimmer Gaia]] are the elves' true progenitors, two steps removed; faint contact during [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunctions]] sometimes lets the diluted children glimpse the originals.

## Pre-Cataclysm roots

Elves owe their cultural heritage — though most have forgotten its origin — to the [[elfen|Elfen]] way of life: profound connection to the land, building with living matter rather than dead, governance by Circles of Wardens, and song-shaped architecture. Modern elven cultures preserve these ideals most fully where the bloodline has stayed thickest (source: Elfen.txt).
