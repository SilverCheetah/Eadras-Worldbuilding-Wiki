# Elves

**Summary**: The descendants of the [[el-mer|El'mer]], who were themselves engineered by the [[elfen|Elfen]] to oversee natural balance on land. Modern elves carry only diluted El'mer blood, divided into multiple subtypes adapted to different environments.

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt, Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Elfen.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-30

---

## Origin

Elves descend from the [[el-mer|El'mer]], the Elfen-engineered race tasked with overseeing natural balance in every land environment. The El'mer themselves perished as a distinct people at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Final Wall]] during the [[demon-war|Demon War]], holding the line at the forest sanctuaries against the demonic flank (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

The diluted El'mer bloodlines that survived the Demon War became modern elves. Some are noted to have absorbed into beast-folk populations as well — Beast-folk are explicitly described as carrying mixed El'mer blood (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

Modern elves are a "natural devolution" — smaller, shorter-lived, and less magically capable than the towering El'mer who came before (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

## Subtypes

Elves are explicitly noted to come in **multiple types that live in different environments** (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt). Specific subtypes are not yet detailed in the available canon and remain to be defined.

## Magical Inheritance

The El'mer were singers and shapers of living matter, like the Elfen who made them. They could coax growth from forests and harmonize ecosystems through song. Modern elves retain only echoes of these gifts, but elven mages occasionally surface as Runesword wielders alongside humans, orcs, and beast-folk — chosen by [[dao|Dao]]'s blades despite the original intent for Tarran royalty (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Relations

- **Beast-folk** share genetic inheritance with elves through mixed El'mer blood (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).
- **[[fae|Fae]]** descend from a different Elfen child race — the Sidhe — but elves and fae share a deep genealogical kinship through common Elfen ancestry (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).
- The **exiled [[elfen|Elfen]]** in [[gaia|Feywild Gaia]] are the elves' true progenitors, two steps removed. Faint echoes of contact during [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunctions]] sometimes allow the diluted children a glimpse of the originals (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Pre-Cataclysm Roots

Elves owe their cultural heritage — though most have forgotten it — to the [[elfen|Elfen]] way of life: profound connection to the land, building with living matter rather than dead, governance by Circles of Wardens, and song-shaped architecture. Modern elven cultures preserve fragments of these ideals where the bloodline has stayed thickest (source: Elfen.txt).
