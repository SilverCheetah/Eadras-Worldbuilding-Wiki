# The Chaos Crusades

**Summary**: The Chaos Crusades are the two times in Eadras's history when raw [[chaos|Chaos]] has breached the [[mythos|Mythos]] â€” the first approximately **6 million years before present**, on the supercontinent [[eldravath|Eldravath]], ravaging a third of the Prime Material and both reflective planes; the second approximately **2 million years before present**, contained at continental scale by the combined response of Dragons, Unicorns, and the Gods.

**Sources**: Chaos Crusades.txt; user direction 2026-05-01; The Neverborn update.txt; Combined info dump 2 5-4-2026.txt; user direction 2026-05-04 (residual pockets and aberration origin).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-04

## The form-or-dissolve law

Chaos cannot persist as raw chaos inside a [[mythos|Mythos]]. When Chaos enters a Mythos it must take **temporary physical form**, or it dissolves â€” just as anything from a Mythos that steps out into raw Chaos likewise dissolves. The rule is symmetric. (source: Chaos Crusades.txt)

This is why the Crusades, despite being incursions of unbounded primordial chaos, look in their visible aftermath like wars between things-that-can-be-fought rather than like a region of the world simply ceasing to exist. Whatever Chaos becomes inside the Mythos has shape, location, vulnerability, and the possibility of being destroyed or expelled.

What shape Chaos chooses to take is a function of what concepts it has access to. In each Crusade the form was different â€” see below.

## The First Chaos Crusade's amplification

A specific cause of the First Chaos Crusade's severity, beyond the open Wound itself, was the **gods' failed first attempt to destroy the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]]**. After Kudur-eá¹­ir's annihilation, the surviving Elder Gods brought the combined might of nearly all the Elder Gods to bear directly on the Spear in an attempt to unmake it; the Spear's alien-marked construction **reflected the divine attack** and the deflected power was channeled directly into the original Wound â€” widening it and amplifying the chaos invasion that was already flowing through. The First Chaos Crusade as actually experienced by Eadras was specifically the version that the gods' own failure had made worse. The Crusade would have happened in some form regardless; what the gods' deflected attack added was severity, duration, and depth. (Source: 2026-05-13 ingest.)

The gods do not advertise this. The popular myths in [[mythic-recitations|the mythic recitations]] do not record it. The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran's]] sealed chapters do. The amplification is part of why the gods could not, in the longer historical record, simply close the Wound â€” they had poured part of their own divine fury through it, and the breach remembered.

## The First Chaos Crusade (~6 million years before present)

Approximately **6 million years before present** â€” on the supercontinent [[eldravath|Eldravath]], two million years before unicorn emergence (see [[eadras-timeline]]) â€” Chaos breached the Mythos through the **First Breach** [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir]] had drilled long before, in the course of his forbidden research into eliminating entropy. (The breach predated the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]]; it was the path he used to contact the [[neverborn|Neverborn]], the path through which the Spear was forged, and â€” on God's Day â€” the path through which the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] was pulled into Eadras. The Crusades use the same hole.) The aperture had been sealed by [[arathi-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] and the destruction of the [[arathi|Arathi]] sites at both ends of the conduit, but the seal was not absolute. The breach point at the planetary surface coincides with the [[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]]. As with all major Chaos-side intrusions, what entered through the breach passed through [[limina|Limina]] before it reached the Prime Material. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

### Forms taken

The Chaos that flooded in had no concept of complex bodies. It seized on **first-order ideals** â€” colors, smells, simple sensations, primitive concepts â€” and gave them temporary physical existence. The result was a region of the world in which colors hunted, smells consumed, and ideals ate stone. Mythos beings caught in the affected zones were unmade not by violence in any conventional sense but by exposure to concepts that were not supposed to be objects.

### Damage

- **One-third of the Prime Material** â€” a continuous swathe consumed before the breach was sealed.
- **The whole of [[shadowdark|Brightmirror]]** â€” the bright reflective plane, before its later corruption into the [[feywild|Feywild]] by the Spear's imprisonment.
- **The whole of [[shadowdark|Darkmirror]]** â€” the dark reflective plane, before its corruption into the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]].

(source: Chaos Crusades.txt)

### Defenders

[[dragons|Dragons]] and the Gods. Dragons, then the dominant sapient race of Eadras, met the Crusade as a martial matter. The Gods watched the engagement first, weighing whether dragonkind alone could end it. They came to the conclusion that **the Dragons could not win** â€” the Crusade was not a war that could be ended by killing every embodied piece of Chaos, because the breach itself kept producing more.

### Resolution

The Gods intervened directly. They **shoved the Chaos out** of the Mythos and **sealed the hole**. The seal at the end of the First Crusade was real, and held for approximately four million years.

## The Second Chaos Crusade (~2 million years before present)

Approximately **2 million years before present**, Chaos breached the same aperture again. By this horizon dragons and unicorns had passed through their own war and reached an uneasy coexistence under the [[unicorns#The Compact of Horn and Flame|Compact of Horn and Flame]]. The entering Chaos had access to richer concepts â€” it had observed, however imperfectly, the dominant beings of the Mythos â€” and it tried to take their forms.

### Forms taken

Chaos took the shapes of **Dragons** and **Unicorns**. But it did not understand the forms it was wearing. The result was **dragon and unicorn body-horror**: shapes recognizable in outline but wrong in every joint, every motion, every interior. They were neither Dragons nor Unicorns; they were Chaos *trying* to be those things and failing in the specific, alarming ways that come from imitating something whose internal logic the imitator does not grasp.

(source: Chaos Crusades.txt)

### Defenders

[[dragons|Dragons]], [[unicorns|Unicorns]], and the Gods. By this point Unicorns had existed for approximately four million years (Ixthal's [[unicorns|Silver Plains Flare]] at ~6 mybp on Eldravath), and dragons and unicorns had already passed through their own war and reached an uneasy coexistence under the Compact of Horn and Flame. They presented unified resistance.

### Resolution

The Gods responded **faster** than they had the first time, the lesson of the First Crusade evidently retained. Damage was contained at **continental** rather than worldwide scale â€” much less than the First Crusade's third-of-the-Prime devastation. The breach was sealed again. (source: Chaos Crusades.txt)

## Residual Chaos Pockets â€” the lasting aftermath

The Crusades did not leave the affected regions empty. Each Crusade left behind an irregular distribution of **residual chaos pockets** â€” sites where the failure of structure was incomplete and persistent, where ordinary causality, identity, and form do not quite hold even after the gross breach has been sealed and the visible Chaos expelled.

This is the deeper, longer-tailed consequence of both Crusades. The Gods sealed the breaches. The visible Chaos manifestations dissolved (per the form-or-dissolve law). But the **standing distortion in the affected ground** did not always heal completely â€” and where it did not, the result was a pocket of locally-failed reality that could persist for millions of years afterward.

**The First Crusade's pockets** are scattered across the modern continents (the post-Cataclysm fragments of what was once Eldravath and the surrounding Prime Material consumed by the Crusade) and across the [[feywild|Feywild]] and [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]] (the post-Crusade reflective planes). Some have dissolved over geological time; many have not.

**The Second Crusade's pockets** are younger, fewer, and continental-scale rather than worldwide; they may host more recent and more dragon/unicorn-themed distortions, reflecting the Chaos forms the Second Crusade was trying and failing to imitate.

These pockets are the canonical origin of **[[aberrations|aberrations]]** on Eadras â€” matter and life that develops within the locally-failed-reality conditions of a pocket grows up *wrong*, stabilizes the warped structure into heritable form across generations, and produces the alien, structurally-incorrect creatures that mortal scholars classify as aberrations. (source: Combined info dump 2 5-4-2026.txt; user direction 2026-05-04)

The pockets are **not actively Chaotic**. There is no Chaos in them in any persisting sense; the Crusades have ended. The pockets are *standing distortions* â€” like ponds left scummed and curdled after a flood receded. Life that develops in the curdled water grows up curdled.

## The seal between Crusades â€” and the secret behind both

> **What no living dragon, unicorn, or god knows.**

The cosmic aperture Kudur-eá¹­ir drilled was sealed by the Gods at the end of the First Crusade, and re-sealed at the end of the Second. **Both seals were perfect. They were absolute. They held.** What no surface record acknowledges, and what neither dragons, unicorns, nor the gods of either Crusade understood, is that the hole was not reopened by attrition, by the corruption of the seal, or by some accident of cosmic stress â€” and indeed, **the seal's perfection did not matter.**

**The [[neverborn|Neverborn]] opened the aperture both times.** (sources: Chaos Crusades.txt â€” clarifying conversation 2026-04-30; The Neverborn update.txt)

The deeper truth is sharper than the older "punched through the seal" framing implied: the Neverborn never needed the seal to be open. The First Breach gave it a foothold â€” the precise mathematical wound Kudur-eá¹­ir carved into reality â€” and once it had that outline against which to define its noticing, **the Neverborn could open the aperture by deciding to notice it again, no matter how perfect the seal.** The seals worked exactly as the gods designed them; the Neverborn worked around them by simply not requiring the hole to be open in the first place.

The second seal stands today. The Neverborn could open it again at any moment by becoming interested. Whether it will is not a question that has an answer, because the Neverborn does not have motives a structured mind could read; **it just acts, or doesn't.** See [[neverborn#The seals were real. The Neverborn never needed the hole.|Neverborn â€” The seals were real]] for the full account.

The Tome of Baldaran records this. The dragons, unicorns, and gods who fought both Crusades do not.
