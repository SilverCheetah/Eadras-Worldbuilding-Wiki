# Kharathen

**Summary**: Kharathen is the **City of Unforgotten Names** — the largest necropolis in Eadras, a 12-mile-circumference metropolis of the dead at the western edge of the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]], built and maintained by the [[katharim|Katharim]] across ten thousand years to record every soul lost in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] and every honored death since.

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-29

---

## Location

At the scarred western lip of the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]], where ash-choked soil finally yields to living grass and the last faint ichor-stains fade from the stone, stands Kharathen.

It is **not a city of the living**. It is a metropolis of the dead whose walls and avenues sprawl across the same footprint as the greatest living capitals (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt). From the air it resembles a vast gray labyrinth pressed against the Waste, its outer curtain of black marble and iron-laced granite forming a perfect, unbroken ring **twelve miles in circumference**. Inside, district after district of crypts, mausolea, and memorial halls stretch in perfect archival order, each era of the last ten thousand years preserved in its own architectural tongue.

## Founding

The city was founded in the **first century after the Demon War's end**, when the surviving [[rathari|Rathar]] third — the [[katharim|Katharim]] — chose to plant their **Hall of Unforgotten Names** on the very border of the wound they refused to let close. Over ten thousand years it has grown outward in concentric rings, each new ring built by the next generation of priests using only **hand tools, divine sanction, and the labor of the living who come to honor their dead** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Layered History — The Crypt Styles

The city's crypt architecture forms a **living timeline** of ten thousand years of grief and craft. Each ring records its own era:

| Era | Years (post-Demon War) | Style |
|---|---|---|
| **War-era Core** | 0–500 | Stark functional barrows of fused demon-glass and Rathar black stone. Low, bunker-like vaults half-sunk into earth. Iron doors stamped with names of the **Fallen Third**. No ornamentation — only casualty rolls carved deep enough to read by touch in total darkness. |
| **Rebuilding Age** | 500–2,000 | Austere colonnaded halls of charcoal granite with silver-inlaid friezes depicting battle sequences. Flat load-bearing roofs walkable for recitations. |
| **Beast-kin Influence Period** | 2,000–4,000 | First subtle softening — living-vine trellises trained by [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] visitors, small laughing-mask reliefs tucked into corners. Crypts feel more like **libraries with beds** than tombs. |
| **High Record Age** | 4,000–7,000 | Grand vaulted chambers with perfect geometric acoustics — a single priest's recitation can be heard throughout an entire district. Crystal lenses set in ceilings focus starlight onto name-plaques at night. |
| **Modern Outer Rings** | 7,000–present | Restrained elegance: clean lines, understated ironwork, occasional living trees grown from soil mixed with ashes of the recently interred. Still expanding. |

The central **Ledger Heart** is the original war-era crypt complex; its black marble is still faintly warm from the residual heat of the first pyres lit when the Demon Prince was driven back.

## The Ledger Heart and Eternal Archives

The Ledger Heart contains the **Eternal Archives of the Thirds** — sprawling underground galleries where the [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar|Fallen Third]] are interred in endless, perfectly ordered ranks. Each sarcophagus is carved with the deceased's full name, known deeds, and exact date and manner of death. Walls are inscribed floor-to-ceiling with the unbroken casualty rolls.

The **[[tomb-of-the-last-guardians|Tomb of the Last Guardians]]** — at the edge of the Broken Conclaves battlefield within the Marches — is administratively part of Kharathen.

## Daily Operation

Daily life inside Kharathen is a **slow, measured heartbeat of duty**.

- **Dawn**: priests perform the Recitation of the First Light, walking the outer avenues while naming every soul interred in the previous day's arrivals
- **Day**: Memory Circles move in silent processions, restoring faded inscriptions, resealing cracked sarcophagi, cataloguing new relics brought by grieving families
- **Continuous undertaking**: Bodies arrive by cart, by beast-folk litter, by dragon-wing, or by simple shoulder-carry from distant villages

Each is met at the **Gate of Names** by a priest who records the deceased's full known history before the body is prepared in the **Hall of Washing**. The rite is never rushed. **Even the poorest pauper receives the same meticulous wrapping** in undyed linen embroidered with their name and the date they were given to the earth.

## Beast-Folk Visits

[[beast-folk|Beast-folk]] visitors are common and welcomed. A [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] family will bring their dead in a riot of color and song, perform a final [[flyting|flyt]] at the graveside, then leave the Katharim to their quiet work. The gray priests never complain.

> Some of the most poignant moments in Kharathen occur when a raven-beastfolk bard perches atop a fresh crypt and sings a wild, ridiculous lament while a [[katharim|Katharim]] priest below carves the final line into stone with steady, tear-tracked hands.

## Defenses

Because the [[katharim|Katharim]] refuse all arcane magic, Kharathen's defenses are **purely divine and architectural**:

- Continuous prayer rotations
- Geometric alignments keyed to celestial events
- The sheer accumulated weight of ten thousand years of faithful record-keeping

Demons and [[black-hand|Black Hand]] necromancers have tried — and failed — to desecrate the outer districts on multiple occasions. **The stones themselves seem to remember every previous attempt and grow harder against the next.**

[[lirra|Lirra]]'s priesthood quietly approves: the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] around Kharathen is unusually stable, as though the city's refusal of personal arcane power creates a natural calm in the threads.

## Reputation

To the outside world, Kharathen is a place of **solemn pilgrimage and quiet awe**:
- Kings send their dead here
- Common folk save for decades so their loved ones may rest among the Weighted
- Even [[xarvion|Xar'vion]]'s cult gives the necropolis a wary berth — the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] has learned that the dead remembered here are never easily raised, and the priests who guard them remember every betrayal with perfect, unyielding clarity

To the [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] it is also home — a place where they may dance at the edge of memory without being swallowed by it. The two peoples complete one another here as nowhere else.

## Cultural Significance

> Kharathen does not merely hold the dead. It holds the choice the [[rathari|Rathar]] made ten thousand years ago. In its silent avenues and echoing halls, the [[katharim|Weighted Third]] still answer the Demon War the only way they know how: by refusing to let any of it be forgotten, so the rest of the world may choose — again and again — whether to remember or to laugh.
