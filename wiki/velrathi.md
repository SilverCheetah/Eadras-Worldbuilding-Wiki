# Velrathi

**Summary**: The Velrathi are the **second** progenitor humanoid race, created roughly 39,000 years ago by [[the-three|Rand]] (youngest of the Three) in the image of who he had become — the studious child who, unshielded, **interrogated the universe directly**. They are not power-hungry necromancers but **the world's scientists**: rigorous empiricists whose signature is the observe-understand-reproduce loop, who hold that the only real sin is *willful ignorance*, and whose status is earned by **published** original research. The iconic cadaverous grey-and-red look is **acquired**, not original. They are the deliberate dark mirror of the [[elfen|Elfen]].

## Overview

The Velrathi were the **second** race Rand made, in the image of who he had become. Across the 11,000-year childhood the three siblings' temperaments forked: Mark explored (→ [[elfen|Elfen]]), Melania stayed at [[dao|Dao's]] side (→ [[tarans|Tarran]]), and **Rand was in the library** — the studious child, read before his siblings. When Dao stopped shielding the Three late in childhood, Rand **interrogated the universe directly**, seeking knowledge from dragons, unicorns, and all the gods. He became an investigator of reality at its sources, with impeccable logic, eidetic memory, and a drive toward *full understanding of everything* — esoteric and mundane alike, "nothing less."

The Velrathi inherit that as an **appetite**. Where Mark loved a thing one can be *content* in (the wild), Rand loved a thing one can only ever *chase* — total understanding is an asymptote you never reach. The Velrathi are therefore a people of **unsatisfiable pursuit**: a people who can never be *done*.

**Physical description**: roughly 3 meters tall, surprisingly durable. The famous **ashen-grey skin and glowing red eyes are acquired, not original** — see [[#The greying|below]]. They wear dark, layered robes covered in flowing runes that glow faintly when magic is channeled.

## Empiricists, not transgressors

The Velrathi signature is the **observe → understand → reproduce** loop — the scientific method as a civilizational practice, Rand's appetite made culture. They observe what reality already does, ask *why*, and follow the answer until they can reproduce it. ("The Velrathi Method of inquiry.") In some marquee cases they *extend* another's discovery (psionics, the naturally-arising genius loci); in many areas they are the **first** to research a thing — Rand's "full understanding of everything" makes them frequent originators, not mere extenders.

This re-sources the old "no forbidden arts" reputation, more gently and more truly: if your method is *observe and reproduce*, then **forbidding an art is forbidding yourself from understanding something reality is already doing.** The only real sin is **willful ignorance** — looking away. [[necromancy-and-undeath|Necromancy]] carries no stigma not because the Velrathi are dark, but because squeamishness about where observation leads is, to them, the one disgrace. The pre-war Velrathi core was the world's scientists; their sinister reputation is mostly outsider projection.

## The brake: prestige requires publication

Status is earned **purely** by arcane achievement and **original research, claimed by publishing** — the social engine *requires* telling everyone what you work on, so **everyone knows what everyone else is doing.** (An institutional layer of Conclaves, and later Houses, controls funding; dangerous work draws extensive scrutiny before a funding vote — but that is secondary to the prestige/publication brake.)

This is the load-bearing constraint: *no forbidden arts, but also **no secret arts**.* Whatever may be studied may not be **hidden** — true secrecy costs you all your status, and is itself the loudest possible signal. The pre-Banishment Velrathi were therefore **radically transparent** — a glass-house culture of permanent peer review, nearly incapable of hiding anything among themselves.

The secretive, paranoid, stranger-arts character is a **post-exile development**. The [[great-banishment|Banishment]] shattered the prestige economy and dropped the exiles into the [[glimmerdark|Glimmerdark]]; without the publication brake, secrecy rose at the cultural scale. (The pre-war transparency is the baseline; the famous paranoia is the wound.)

## The greying

Rand made the Velrathi with **standard humanoid skin** (pink to brown) — **not** cadaverous, not grey, not red-eyed. The iconic look is **acquired**, by a precise and self-accelerating mechanism:

**Negative energy** (drawn from [[nyxar|Nyxar]], the infernal plane — not itself evil, a morally-neutral energy) used **by a pregnant Velrathi** alters the fetus three ways every time: skin shifts toward grey, glowing eyes develop, and the child's *propensity to use negative energy* increases. That third effect makes it a heritable **ratchet** — each generation is greyer **and** more disposed to do the thing that greys the next. Over millennia, a significant majority's use tipped the whole race to grey skin and stabilized red glowing eyes. The species became cadaverous **by collective habit, one pregnancy at a time.**

- **Throwbacks** are rare Velrathi born with other skin tones, differently-colored eyes, or non-glowing eyes — atavistic re-expressions of the original stock, and living proof the grey is acquired.
- The Velrathi **know this, and take pride in it.** They are archivists; the greying is recorded history. Grey skin and deep-red eyes are an **unfalsifiable, heritable credential** — proof of generations of deep magical engagement. The phenotype is the **prestige economy made flesh**: just as status is earned by published achievement, the body involuntarily *publishes* the depth of a lineage's magical work. You cannot fake grey; it is the one credential that cannot be plagiarized. (Conversely, a throwback's ordinary coloring legibly reads as "a lineage of shallow or timid engagement" — a brilliant throwback whose body misreports their lineage is a real character seam.)
- Outsider horror at "grey frog-licking necromancers" is the canonical **misprojection**. The grey is the visible **cost** of unflinching use, not corruption.

## Foodways

Most Velrathi treat food as **fuel optimized for cognition and function**, not pleasure: they **dose**, they do not dine; preparation is *processing*, not cooking. The base ration is a nutrient-dense **slurry** of carefully chosen ingredients (proportions vary by household), compressed and dried into **loaves sliced into bars**, reconstituted into soup when social. The ingredient palette is a complete system — organ meats (sometimes predator, for mana-density up the food chain), protein-rich meats, fully-macerated high-mana plants (technological extraction of otherwise-inedible cellulose), nutrient plants, **medicinal herbs** (preventative medicine built into the ration), fats as binder, and pure water (or [[velrathi-tea-culture|tea]] for cognition, or ale "as a purifier"). On top of the base bar sits a **dosing/supplement tier**: tea, [[velrathi-cognitive-frogs|cognitive frogs]], crystal-infused water. (The older "bone-meal bread / nutrient pastes / fermented broths" description is the same fuel-not-pleasure system seen from outside; the small "arcane cuisine" subgroup is the supplement tier taken to its dangerous extreme. See [[pre-empire-foodways]].)

The **hospitality keystone**: reconstituting a bar into **soup** for a guest deliberately reintroduces the inefficiency the Velrathi normally engineer out (time, water, warmth). For a fuel-not-pleasure people, **inefficiency-on-purpose is the signal of care.**

A revealing structural tell runs through all of it: the Velrathi **justify** rather than **admit**. Sugar cane enters the ration as "a glucose boost," ale as "a purifier," hospitality-soup as "digestion and warmth" — all valid functional rationales, sincerely held, that conveniently permit a preference or an affection. Because they can always justify, they never have to confront what they *want*. Pleasure, comfort, and love all travel disguised as function. **The tell for a Velrathi's heart is the place where the reasoning is suspiciously convenient.**

The *science* of food-as-fuel is openly published and abundant; the actual **household recipe** is held closely (shared versions usually omit or substitute a minor ingredient — craft-IP, not Nox-shame-secrecy, fully consistent with the publish-knowledge-guard-the-artifact pattern). The withheld ingredient is a household's identity-fingerprint; intermarriage merges formulas, a culinary parallel to "strengthened blood."

**Drink**: the Velrathi **do not consume alcohol for pleasure** (it clouds the mind), and their cultural beverage is **tea** — discovered by a Velrathi scholar, then elevated into an intellectual discipline once they found that rationed consumption during study measurably sharpens the mind. Tea plays three formal roles (the disciplined Tea Ceremony, lifelong Personal Preference, and Meditative Practice). Roughly **500 varieties** exist, almost all ancient creations of the [[elfen|Elfen]] (commissioned by the Velrathi, who asked for ~10–15 and were given two orders of magnitude more). See [[velrathi-tea-culture]]. The asymmetry between food-as-fuel and tea-as-art is not a contradiction — it is the same logic on two inputs: food does not improve cognition, so it is fuel; tea does, so it is art. The upper-class **[[velrathi-cognitive-frogs|cognitive frog]]** custom (licking poison-frog backs for a cognitive boost, with generational toxin-immunity) sits in the same supplement tier.

## Psionics, the open mind, and Mind Blank

Psionics is **available to all magic users**, not species-exclusive. **Unicorns developed it first** (their [[unicorn-biology|herd-field biology]] forced it early); the Velrathi **researched and extended it** far beyond the unicorn baseline — they are the only **textual, taught, theorized, published** psionic tradition. The same faculty is radically *private* among unicorns (the herd interior) and radically *published* among Velrathi — inverted by each culture's epistemics.

The mental realm is **loud**: non-psionics cannot shield, so their thoughts are involuntarily "spoken aloud" there. A new psion must first learn to **close the mind** — build the wall — or be driven mad by the ambient flood; mastery begins with **silence**, not reach. Two developmental clocks run in Velrathi children: **magical** power on a bell curve peaking at puberty (the rare gifted earlier; the very rarest, legend-shaping prodigies, *in the womb* — a magically volatile pregnancy that compounds the greying), and **psionic** ability **brain-gated** to roughly puberty. Childhood is therefore typically **open and magically dormant**, then a **dual emergence** at puberty makes a Velrathi at once a mage *and* a sovereign closeable mind.

This produces the **childhood-privacy ethic**, the hard kind: adults *hear* the open child unavoidably, and the discipline is in **not acting** on what they cannot help hearing — they know everything and behave as if blind. Privacy-of-consequence, not privacy-of-content, sustained as a loving illusion until the wall makes it real at puberty. (The one override is **harm-prevention**: if the child is planning something dangerous, they act.) The restraint proves the Velrathi are not merely their appetite — they subordinate the comprehension-drive to a value, drawing the line only at harm. [[lirra|Lirra]] governs [[nox|Nox]] here.

**Mind Blank** — a spell-plus-wearable that masks mental activity in impenetrable white noise — was developed in response to the discovery of unshielded minds. It works by **noise-masking** (jamming the broadcast), distinct from psionic shielding (closing the mind), which is why it can protect the **ungifted**, who have no wall to raise: it is **prosthetic privacy**. Pre-modern Mind Blank was individually crafted, rare, a luxury — so the ungifted masses stayed helplessly open across nearly all of history, protected only by the privacy-in-trust norm. **Industrial mass-production in the modern era** finally democratizes mental privacy: the end of the ancient shielded/open asymmetry is a distinctly **modern** rupture, achieved by manufacturing. (Whether the Velrathi invented Mind Blank or unknowingly reproduced an Arathi technology is open canon — they credit it to themselves.)

## Lifespan and legacy

The Velrathi share the eldest-race profile: biological **immortality** (no aging), bounded by the [[omniversal-laws|Fifth Law]] (Death is Sovereign) and the **Sixth Law** (Destruction is the Natural End); a bell-curve lifespan; the singular-apex funnel; and **ascension while living**.

But where the [[elfen|Elfen]] transfer knowledge communally at [[womb-trees|birth]], a Velrathi's sovereign private mind is **totally lost at death unless they published.** The archive is how a Velrathi mind survives its own death — so the prestige-publication economy is also their **funerary mechanism**: you publish so that when the Sixth Law takes you, your knowledge persists though your mind is gone. **Ascension** is revered both as worship and as the existence-proof that the ceiling is crossable — and, for a people whose understander is otherwise lost at death, it is the one way the *understander itself* survives whole. ([[xarvion|Xarvion]] is the forced-shortcut, Spear-driven dark version of that path.) This is also what the Amunorian dispossession of the archives truly steals — see [[#The Amunorian succession|below]].

## Belief

The Velrathi structure of worship runs **ground → practice → instrumental**:

- **Ground** — genuine **reverence grounded in demonstrated reality**. In Eadras the gods are *provably* real; the Velrathi do not believe on faith, they revere because the greatness is established fact. Worship is the awe of the proof. (This resolves the empiricist-worship knot: no faith required.)
- **Practice** — worship-as-**study**. The way to honor a god is to **comprehend its domain**: devotion is research, a temple is a laboratory, prayer looks like inquiry. **To understand is to worship**; their theology *is* their epistemology. (Dark mirror of [[elfen|Elfen]] worship-as-*remembrance*.)
- **Instrumental** — they also study and work the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]] as a real, optimizable system (expert petitioners). The lowest tier, not the heart.

Their scope is **universal**: they study and worship **all** the gods — Elder, Elderborn, and Ascended Mortals alike, and the Ascended *just as much*, with no origin-hierarchy. This is **rigor, not tolerance** — reverence keyed to demonstrated reality cannot play favorites by origin. *"Reality has no flag."* The objects of worship are **[[the-three|Rand]]** (the revered progenitor-maker, cooler than the Elfen's love of Mark), **[[lirra|Lirra]]** (the universal magic-patron), **[[nox|Nox]]** (whom *some* Velrathi worship and others merely **acknowledge** — one cannot deny a proven god), and Velrathi **Ascended Mortals**. The **Nox worship-vs-acknowledge split** is the countable measure of the cultural arc: pre-exile, mostly acknowledge (Lirra ascendant); post-exile in the Glimmerdark (literally Nox's domain), worship of Nox **rises**.

## Houses and the Conclaves

**Government**: no kings or emperors. Each major city or region was governed by a **Conclave** of the most accomplished mages, deciding by magical debate and contests of logic.

**Houses (Empire-era institution)**: in addition to Conclave governance, the Velrathi developed a parallel **House** system during the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] era (~18,000 to ~10,000 years ago) — a lineage/patronage structure to which the engineered servant races [[orugi|Orug]] and [[grugi|Grug]] were sworn under the **"ser"** particle. (User direction 2026-05-01: *"Velrathi Houses formed during the empire."*) **All Velrathi are associated with a House.** The House system is a Firethorn-era stratum layered onto the older Conclave model, not a replacement — every Velrathi belongs to a House, the Conclaves remained the public governance bodies, and Houses are the lineage/patronage layer.

**Velrathi Houses are NOT the same institutions as the [[rathari#The Ten Houses|Ten Ancient Rathar Houses]].** (User direction 2026-05-01: *"Rathar houses are not Velrathi houses."*) They are two parallel, distinct House systems. Some names match across the two — *Korthal*, *Narveth*, and *Valtharion* appear as both — but a Rathar House and a Velrathi House of the same name are *different organizations*. Additional Velrathi Houses (notably **Velroth** and **Kathor**) appear in canonical [[orugi|Orug]]/[[grugi|Grug]] servant-naming examples with no Rathar counterpart. The full roster is not yet documented.

The [[rathari|Rathar]] are a special case: Velrathi-engineered as a servant-scholar race, **and** developers of their own House system in parallel — analogous to **Oxford University colleges** within the Velrathi academic "university." Each Rathar served a particular Velrathi House *and* was a fellow of a particular Rathar House (College); their **formal address combines both** — `[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] of [Rathar House], [Hold]`. See [[rathari#Formal address]] and [[naming-conventions]].

## Cities and the cultivation mode

Towering gothic spires of black stone and crystallized magic, glowing red runes, floating platforms, needle-thin towers connected by slender high bridges. For the full architectural tradition — the spell-lattice principle, the willingly-bound **Stone-Bound** wardspirit (an *elevated*, pre-existing genius loci), the spell-plate-and-donation-box upkeep convention, and the twenty-thousand-year iterative arc — see [[velrathi-architecture]].

The Stone-Bound exemplifies the Velrathi's deepest social form, repeated at every scale: **cultivation of potential through patient, sustained investment in a willing partner.** They cannot *create* a spirit; they **invite** a minor pre-existing one into a prepared lattice and **grow** it into something far greater than it could become alone — patron-and-protégé, not maker-and-made. The same shape recurs in centuries-long master-apprentice mentorships and in household food-formulas refined across generations: [[lirra|Lirra's]] structure plus Rand's patience as a *relationship pattern*, generous rather than exploitative.

## Role Among the Three Races

For over twenty thousand years, the [[elfen|Elfen]], Velrathi, and [[tarans|Taran]] maintained a stable, cooperative civilization — the Velrathi as **magic** (Eadras's magic-R&D source: new spells, magic items, the world's scientists), respecting the Elfen's wardenship of balance and the Taran's keeping of order. The Velrathi homeland straddled the mountain spine of the large **eastern continent** — half arid southwestern lobe, half moist forest, founded on **two [[seven-conduits|conduit]] ends** (magically wealthy by geography; the dead-stone building was a *choice*, not a necessity). The cooperative age ended when [[tarans|Taran]] arrogance led to the founding of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]. (On the cradle map and why the three-hand interdependence is *post-colonization*, see [[elfen#Cradle, expansion, and the late triad|Elfen § Cradle, expansion, and the late triad]].)

## Engineered Servant-Scholar Races

The Velrathi engineered three crafted races from refined Velrathi stock — not as slaves but as **living tools, proud extensions of Velrathi will granted sapience, dignity, and the satisfaction of purpose**. All three share the Velrathi template (grey skin, red eyes, long-lived, precision minds — the greying inherited from their already-greyed makers) but were optimized for different roles. The founding need was, at root, **labor**: the early Velrathi first used actual [[elfen|Elfen]] labor (reluctantly — they disliked depending on a neighbor — exchanged for new spells and magic items), then their own growing population, and only later learned to engineer their own child races. The engineered races are partly a monument to **never depending on another people again.**

| Race | Role | Fate |
|---|---|---|
| **[[rathari\|Rathar]]** | Scholarly assistants, archivists, deep researchers — the intellectual thought-partners the Velrathi could get no other way | Fractured into [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar\|three branches]] during the Demon War: the Fallen, the [[beast-folk\|Laughing Third]] (Beast-Folk), and the [[katharim\|Weighted Third]] (Katharim). Pure enclaves still exist in deep vaults. |
| **[[orugi\|Orug]]** | Strong, honorable guardians and laborers | **Totally extinct** — died to the last individual at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves\|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]], singing their oaths. Diluted blood survives in modern [[orcs\|Orcs]]. |
| **[[grugi\|Grug]]** | Small, clever, mechanically adept maintainers and inventors | Catastrophically reduced; survivors diverged into modern Goblins and Gnomes; some accompanied the Velrathi into [[gaia\|Glimmerdark Gaia]] exile. |

These three races were **not directly created by [[the-three|Rand]]**. Rand created the Velrathi alone; the Velrathi themselves engineered Rathar, Orug, and Grug through generations of meticulous arcane modification of their own offspring in the womb (see [[peoples-of-eadras#How the engineering worked]]). Hierarchy was clear yet meritocratic; **intermarriage across all four groups was common and encouraged**, children celebrated as *"strengthened blood."*

## Pre-War Civilization

Velrathi society formed the apex, with the three crafted races as indispensable limbs. [[rathari|Thought Conclaves]] (mostly Rathar-led) formed the intellectual core; [[orugi|Orug]] companies provided martial protection; [[grugi|Grug]] work-teams maintained the vast cities and invented the small mechanisms that made high magic practical. The daily **Unified Recitation** brought all four together: Rathar reported discoveries, Orug reported the state of the realm, Grug demonstrated new mechanisms, Velrathi presided.

## The Amunorian succession

The ancient Velrathi homeland was eventually occupied by the [[amunorians|Amunorian Empire]] — a mixed-race, **human-dominant** polity whose ruling humans are **[[tarans|Tarran]]-line** ([[aetherins|Aetherin]] descendants). The sharpest irony in Velrathi history runs through this: the **destroyers' kin claim the legacy of the destroyed.** The Amunorian humans descend from the same Tarran line whose arrogance murdered [[velena|Velena]] and triggered the Demon War — and they now sit on the Velrathi homeland claiming **Velrathi heirship**, using the empire's Velrathi-blooded subject peoples ([[grugi|Gnomes and Goblins]] via Grug; [[katharim|Katharim]] via Rathar) as the blood-proof.

The claim is **true**, via the **archives**: the old Velrathi archives survived on the homeland (they were not destroyed in the Banishment that exiled the *people*), and whoever holds the surviving record inherits the knowledge. The Amunorians are not lying — though they reach for the **imperial** Velrathi specifically, the proudest and most *fallen* era, venerating Velrathi power, not Velrathi inquiry.

The exiles' response is **shame, not fury.** They know and cannot act (diminished, powerless), and the claim is *legitimate* — because the Amunorians hold the archives the Velrathi **lost**. Velrathi identity *is* knowledge-custody, so losing the archives is dispossession at the exact point of identity: the humans hold **what the Velrathi most are.** It is a standing humiliation they can only witness, compounding the [[velrathi-architecture|architectural]] and demographic losses of exile. (A bitter open thread: the Amunorians may now be *better* custodians than the Nox-drifting exiles — both partial, neither the imperial Velrathi restored. The contested claim *is* the canon.)

## Banishment

At the conclusion of the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the [[great-banishment|Elder Gods banished all three progenitor races]] from the Prime Material of Eadras. Of the three nature-keyed exiles, the Velrathi drew the **corrupting** mirror: the **[[gaia|Glimmerdark version of the moon Gaia]]** — [[nox|Nox's]] domain made into a world — where they rebuilt a fortress-archive capital, **[[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]]**, within a vast geode whose facets refract the few sources of light into blood-red patterns. This is the environmental cause of the cultural **Nox-rising**: the exile that *darkens* them.

Loyal [[grugi|Grug]] work-crews and a handful of [[orugi|Orug]] honor-guards accompanied them. The exiled Velrathi endure as a **diminished, proud, and dangerous** civilization — fewer, more paranoid, far more dangerous in isolation, turned to shadow-reflected rituals and negative-energy harmonics, still refusing to accept the justice of their banishment. Pure Velrathi no longer exist on the Prime Material — though hidden Velrathi archives remain on Eadras as part of the "enclaves remained behind" exception (see [[great-banishment]]).

## The vice — "Lost in the Weeds"

A Velrathi who has **lost sight of the big picture** by studying minutiae too long — a century spent monitoring one insect's lifecycle in one pond in one valley, and its effect on local Eldritch density. Real, rigorous, even published research that has **detached from any larger understanding.** It attacks the highest Velrathi value *invisibly*: the victim looks like the perfect comprehender (rigorous, publishing, refusing no question) while betraying comprehension's actual purpose — accumulating **knowledge** while losing **understanding** (the library without the librarian). It hides perfectly in the justify-don't-admit layer (every step *is* functionally justified), so the victim never asks the saving question — *does any of this matter? have I understood anything?* It is **theirs alone** — it counterfeits *understanding* specifically, requires their gifts to fall into (rigor, immortal lifespan for century-studies, publishing reward, eidetic ease with minutiae), and is made of their virtues; only a capable Velrathi can get this lost. Like the [[elfen#The vice — "Lost in Smoke"|Elfen "Lost in Smoke,"]] it is an **anti-ascension**: an immortal mind spent on the too-small never synthesizes into the wisdom that could cross the threshold — they study the pond until the Sixth Law cracks them.

## Naming

Velrathi names are arcane, scholarly, and slightly gothic — ancient and weighty. Common opening syllables: **Vel-, Roth-, Vex-, Nar-, Kath-, Zor-, Mel-, Vor-**; common endings: *-othi, -ar, -eth, -un, -or, -ith, -and, -ex.* Canonical examples include [[velena|Velena]] and [[xarvion|Xarvion]] (a great archmage of the late Firethorn era), plus Rothena, Narveth, Kathira, Vexara, Rothandar, Melaroth. See [[naming-conventions#velrathi-created-by-rand|Racial Naming Conventions — Velrathi]].
