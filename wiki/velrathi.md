## Overview

The Velrathi were created by [[the-three|Rand]] â€” the youngest of the Three â€” as the Masters of Magic. Their society revolved entirely around magical knowledge. Status was earned purely through arcane achievement and original research.

**Physical description**: 3 meters tall, cadaverous, with ashen gray skin and glowing red eyes. They wear dark, layered robes covered in flowing runes that glow faintly when magic is being channeled. Surprisingly durable despite their corpse-like appearance.

## Culture

**Knowledge as sacred**: All forms of magic were studied with equal passion â€” no forbidden arts. [[necromancy-and-undeath|Necromancy]] carried no moral stigma; it was a technical discipline. Non-sentient undead (skeletons, constructs) were commonly used for construction and maintenance.

**Status through merit**: The greatest scholars were treated like rock stars. Because they lived for thousands of years, knowledge was passed down through deep, long-term mentor-apprentice relationships lasting centuries.

**Government**: No kings or emperors. Each major city or region was governed by a **Conclave** of the most accomplished mages, with decisions made through magical debate and contests of logic.

**Houses (Empire-era institution)**: In addition to Conclave governance, the Velrathi developed a parallel **House** system during the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] era (~18,000 to ~10,000 years ago) â€” a lineage / patronage structure to which the engineered servant races [[orugi|Orug]] and [[grugi|Grug]] were sworn under the **"ser"** particle. (User direction 2026-05-01: *"Velrathi Houses formed during the empire."*) **All Velrathi are associated with a House.** (User direction 2026-05-01.) The House system is therefore a Firethorn-era institution layered onto the older Conclave model, not a replacement of it â€” every Velrathi belongs to a House, and the Conclaves remained the public governance bodies, with Houses as the lineage/patronage stratum.

**Velrathi Houses are NOT the same institutions as the [[rathari#The Ten Houses|Ten Ancient Rathar Houses]].** (User direction 2026-05-01: *"Rathar houses are not Velrathi houses."*) They are two parallel and distinct House systems. Some names may match across the two systems â€” *Korthal*, *Narveth*, and *Valtharion* appear as both Rathar Houses and Velrathi Houses in canon â€” but a Rathar House Korthal and a Velrathi House Korthal are *different organizations* with the same name, not the same House from two angles. Additional Velrathi Houses (notably **Velroth** and **Kathor**) appear in canonical [[orugi|Orug]] and [[grugi|Grug]] servant-naming examples but have no Rathar counterpart at all. The full roster of Velrathi Houses is not yet documented.

The [[rathari|Rathar]] are a special case: they were Velrathi-engineered as a servant-scholar race, **and** they also developed their own House system in parallel â€” analogous to **Oxford University colleges** within the Velrathi academic "university." (User direction 2026-05-01.) Each Rathar served a particular Velrathi House in their work AND was a fellow of a particular Rathar House (College). Their **formal address combines both** in a single string â€” `[Given Name] ser [Velrathi House] of [Rathar House], [Hold]` â€” displaying servant status and College membership simultaneously. See [[rathari#Formal address]] and [[naming-conventions]].

**Foodways**: Most Velrathi treat food as fuel for the mind and body â€” eating quickly, often while still working. The everyday diet is preserved foods (nutrient pastes, fermented broths, fungal stews) and **bone-meal bread**. A small subgroup pursues **arcane cuisine**, deliberately cooking with potion-grade ingredients (mana-infused organs, magically charged plants) to produce meals that grant temporary magical effects â€” sometimes dangerously. See [[pre-empire-foodways]].

**Drink**: The Velrathi **do not consume alcohol**, believing it clouds the mind. Their cultural beverage is **tea**, which they discovered (a Velrathi scholar dried particular leaves and steeped them in hot water as an experiment) and which they elevated into a sophisticated cultural and intellectual discipline after they realized that **rationed consumption during study produces a measurable improvement in mental acuity**. Tea plays three formal roles in their society â€” the highly disciplined Tea Ceremony, lifelong Personal Preference, and Meditative Practice as a [[velrathi#Cities|memory-palace]] focusing aid. Approximately 500 distinct varieties exist, almost all of them ancient creations of the [[elfen|Elfen]] (commissioned by the Velrathi over centuries; the Elfen famously overshot the original commission of ~10â€“15 varieties by two orders of magnitude). See [[velrathi-tea-culture]] for the full treatment.

The asymmetry between Velrathi food-as-fuel and Velrathi tea-as-art is not a contradiction â€” it is the same logic applied to two different inputs. Food is fuel because it does not improve cognition; tea is art because it does.

**Cognitive frogs**: A peculiar custom of the Velrathi upper class â€” particularly scholars, nobles, and mages â€” is to carry small, brilliantly multicolored frogs in elegant glass containers and **lick the frog's back directly** for cognitive enhancement. The Velrathi have generational immunity to the deadly fraction of the toxin; the dosing produces a measurable mental-acuity boost similar in character to (but distinct in mechanism from) measured tea consumption. See [[velrathi-cognitive-frogs]] â€” a deeply unsettling custom from any non-Velrathi vantage.

## Role Among the Three Races

For over twenty thousand years, the [[elfen|Elfen]], Velrathi, and [[tarans|Taran]] maintained a stable, cooperative civilization. The Velrathi served as the Masters of Magic â€” neutral scholars who advanced all forms of arcane knowledge. They respected the [[elfen|Elfen's]] wardenship of nature and the [[tarans|Taran's]] maintenance of order as complementary to their own role.

This ended when Taran arrogance led to the founding of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]].

## Engineered Servant-Scholar Races

The Velrathi engineered three crafted races from refined Velrathi stock â€” not as slaves but as **living tools, proud extensions of Velrathi will granted sapience, dignity, and the satisfaction of purpose**. All three shared the Velrathi template (gray skin, red eyes, long-lived, precision minds) but were optimized for different roles.

These three races were **not directly created by [[the-three|Rand]]**. Rand created the Velrathi alone; the Velrathi themselves engineered Rathar, Orug, and Grug through generations of meticulous arcane ritual.

## Pre-War Civilization

Velrathi society formed the apex, with the three crafted races as indispensable limbs. [[rathari|Thought Conclaves]] (mostly Rathar-led) formed the intellectual core. [[orugi|Orug]] companies provided martial protection. [[grugi|Grug]] work-teams maintained the vast underground cities and invented the small mechanisms that made high magic practical.

Hierarchy was clear yet meritocratic. **Intermarriage between the four groups was common and encouraged**; resulting children were celebrated as *"strengthened blood."*

The daily **Unified Recitation** brought all four together: Rathar reported discoveries, Orug reported on the state of the realm, Grug demonstrated new mechanisms, Velrathi presided.

## Banishment

At the conclusion of the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the [[great-banishment|Elder Gods banished all three progenitor races]] from the Prime Material of Eadras. The Velrathi were sent to the **[[gaia|Shadowdark version of the moon Gaia]]**, where they rebuilt a fortress-archive capital called **[[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]]** within a vast geode whose facets refract the few sources of light into blood-red patterns.

Loyal [[grugi|Grug]] work-crews and a handful of [[orugi|Orug]] honor-guards (those who survived the Broken Conclaves) accompanied them. Most of these bloodlines have since intermingled with Shadowdark-adapted life or faded into legend.

The exiled Velrathi endure as a **diminished, proud, and dangerous civilization** â€” fewer in number, more paranoid, and far more dangerous in their isolation. They have turned to stranger arts: shadow-reflected rituals, negative-energy harmonics, and the study of how the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear's]] lingering corruption warped the Shadowdark itself. They still refuse to accept the justice of their banishment and nurse quiet dreams of return.

Pure Velrathi no longer exist on the Prime Material â€” though hidden Velrathi archives remain on Eadras as part of the "enclaves remained behind" exception to the Banishment (see [[great-banishment]]).
