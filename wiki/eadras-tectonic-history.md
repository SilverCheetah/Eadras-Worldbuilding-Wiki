# Eadras Tectonic History

**Summary**: A reconstructive (out-of-universe) atlas of Eadras's surface from the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] forward through plate tectonic rebuilding, presented as a sequence of one-million-year snapshots produced via plate-tectonics simulation. This page is a **single rolling timeline** â€” new snapshots are appended as they are generated.

## Framing

These snapshots are **out-of-universe reconstructions** â€” geological models of how the planet's surface evolved after the Cataclysm. They are distinct from in-universe artifacts like the [[arathi-golden-age-map|Arathi Golden Age Map]] (an illuminated cartographic record made by the Arathi a generation before the Cataclysm).

Plate tectonics on Eadras was **ignited** by the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] impact (see [[arathi-cataclysm#Physical Consequences]]); before the Cataclysm the planet was tectonically still â€” **continental layout was fixed**, with no plate motion, no continental drift, and no large-scale crustal slippage. The pre-Cataclysm world was *not, however, geologically dead*: earthquakes were a constant low-level background, and an active subterranean hydrological cycle (massive underground oceanic reservoirs exchanging water with the surface) operated continuously. The Arathi therefore lived with everyday geological churn but had no concept that the continents themselves could move at scale. (Clarification 2026-05-09 ingest.) The world has had only one tectonic regime since, and it is young enough that direct reconstruction across each million-year boundary is meaningful.

Dating note: snapshots are labeled by **years post-Cataclysm**. Under the canonical [[eadras-timeline|10my timeline]], 1mya = 9mybp, 2mya = 8mybp, 3mya = 7mybp, 4mya = 6mybp.

The map explanation document carried two original dating lines (dragons at 1mya, unicorns at 2mya). Under the locked timeline these are corrected to **dragons at 2mya post-Cataclysm** (8mybp BP) and **unicorns at 4mya post-Cataclysm** (6mybp BP â€” on Eldravath at supercontinent maturity). Wiki captions below use the corrected dates. (User direction 2026-05-04 reverted an interim attempt to date unicorns at 4mybp BP back to the ~6mybp BP Eldravath placement; map remains in flux.)

## 1mya post-Cataclysm
*(9 million years before present)*

![[Eadras 1mya post-Cataclysm.jpg]]

One million years after the Cataclysm, the world is still **raw and unstable**. The **[[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]]** burns brightly at the center of what will become the **Central Rift Ocean**. Three major proto-continents have begun to stabilize from the sundered [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]]:

- **[[avalon|Avalon]]** in the north
- **[[damedes|Damedes]]** in the southwest
- **[[culethor|Culethor]]** in the southeast

Two additional continents are also visible on the map â€” **[[therion|Therion]]** and **[[aurettos|Aurettos]]** â€” both **pre-Cataclysm highlands** that survived the impact intact (rather than being fragments of the sundered Altean upland). They are the only major non-Altean landmasses to have endured the catastrophe in continent-scale form. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

Smaller island chains dot the young oceans. The planet's crust is fractured, its climate volatile.

This snapshot is **pre-dragons** under the canonical timeline â€” the First Great Flare arrives one million years later.

## 2mya post-Cataclysm
*(8 million years before present)*

![[Eadras 2mya post-Cataclysm.jpg]]

Two million years after the impact, the continents have **drifted further apart**. The Worldbreaker Scar has begun to cool and sink, now known as the **Buried Worldbreaker Scar**, though its influence still distorts ocean currents and ley lines.

The landmasses are taking on more recognizable shapes. Ocean currents are establishing distinct climate zones:

- **Northreach Ocean** north of Avalon
- **Vastwind Ocean** between Avalon and Damedes
- **Eonswell Ocean** between Avalon and Culethor
- **Southward Ocean** below the central rift

Smaller features visible on this snapshot: **Windward Isles**, **Shattered Isles**, and the early **Sundered Atoll** at the eastern margin.

This is the era in which **the first dragons awaken** â€” [[ixthal|Ixthal]]'s First Great Flare lands on a still-recovering world. See [[dragons]]. The long age of dragon dominance is beginning.

## 3mya post-Cataclysm
*(7 million years before present)*

![[Eadras 3mya post-Cataclysm.jpg]]

By three million years after the Cataclysm, Eadras has settled into a configuration **closer to its modern form**. The three major continents â€” Avalon, Damedes, and Culethor â€” are clearly distinct, each with its own mountain ranges, rivers, and coastal features.

The Worldbreaker Scar has become a **deep, stable oceanic rift** â€” still geologically active but no longer a blazing wound. New surface features include **fracture caps**, **stormbreak points**, and shifting current patterns. The named ocean currents that anchor modern circulation are taking shape: **Northreach Current**, **Southward Current**, **Atoll Drift**, **Elder Current**.

This is the era of dragon establishment. Unicorns have not yet emerged; the brief [[unicorns#The Unicorn-Dragon War and Peace|Dragon-Unicorn Wars]] are still in the future.

## 4mya post-Cataclysm â€” Eldravath
*(6 million years before present)*

![[Eadras 4mya post-Cataclysm.jpg]]

Four million years after the Cataclysm, the scattered continents have **collided once more** through the slow violence of plate tectonics, forming the great supercontinent **[[eldravath|Eldravath]] â€” The Forged Land**.

The world is in **violent geological rebirth**. The Worldbreaker Scar still glows at the heart of the Central Rift Ocean, though it has begun to cool and sink. The formation of Eldravath has triggered massive mountain-building events, new volcanic arcs, and extreme climate gradients across the single landmass.

**Key features**:
- Towering central mountain ranges dominate the interior.
- Vast interior basins, shifting sands, and newly formed inland seas create dramatic environmental contrasts.
- Active coastal rifting and volcanic activity mark the beginning of the forces that will eventually tear the supercontinent apart again in another million years.

**Significance**: This snapshot coincides with three of the most consequential events of the dragon-unicorn era:

- **Unicorn emergence** â€” the **Silver Plains Flare** manifests an entire herd at once on Eldravath. The supercontinent's broadly stable, interconnected ecosystems are what give the species its inherently herd-oriented and system-attuned character. (User direction 2026-05-04.)
- **First Chaos Crusade** â€” defended by dragons and Gods alone; unicorns are too newly-manifested to participate.
- **Founding of Ratharil** as the dragons' neutral capital, around the same horizon.

Eldravath is *"the last time the world existed as a single landmass before fracturing into the modern continents."*

The map explanation correctly identifies this snapshot as the **peak of the Dragon-Unicorn Wars**. Under the canonical [[eadras-timeline|10my timeline]] the Wars are **short** â€” they began roughly 5,000-10,000 years after unicorn emergence and lasted only a few thousand years total. At million-year geological resolution that entire war fits inside this single snapshot â€” there is no later snapshot in which the wars are "still going." After the Wars came the **Compact of Horn and Flame** and the long subsequent millions of years of dragon-unicorn coexistence, which lived on the post-Eldravath fractured continents. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

## Geological constants across the series

The single feature that persists through all four snapshots is the **[[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]]** â€” first as blazing wound, then as buried scar, then as deep oceanic rift, then as a faintly-glowing center of the Central Rift Ocean within Eldravath. The Scar's location in the deep crust is the **same point** as Kudur-eá¹­ir's old [[chaos-crusades|Chaos aperture]] from the Spear's forging, and is also the spot where the [[arathyn|Arathyn]] continent will eventually emerge from continued LIP extrusion. See [[worldbreaker-scar]] for the consolidated treatment.

## What comes next

The map series stops at 4mya post-Cataclysm. Subsequent snapshots are pending; the next major geological event is the **fracturing of Eldravath** back toward the modern continents (the supercontinent will not survive past roughly another million years; it is "the last time the world existed as a single landmass before fracturing into the modern continents"). Modern Eadras (39,000 BP onward) is therefore the product of post-Eldravath fracture, not the original Cataclysm-era continental drift alone.

The current-day map does not yet exist (memory: `project-map-development.md`).
