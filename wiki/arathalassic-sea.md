# Arathalassic Sea

**Summary**: The Arathalassic Sea is the great ocean that fills the cavity between the three continents sundered from the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]] during the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]; the [[arathyn]] formed by the Worldbreaker's Large Igneous Province sits at its center.

**Sources**: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, VerdantScarAndArathCataclysm_01_InWorld_Facts.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-13

---

## Formation

The Arathalassic Sea did not exist before the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]. When the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] struck [[altea|Altea]] and initiated plate tectonics, the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]] sundered into three continents that drifted apart. The cavity between them filled with:

1. **The White Deluge** â€” the immediate eruption of vast subterranean superheated oceans through the fractured crust (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt).
2. **Year-plus rainfall** â€” debris-darkened atmosphere produced a permanent rainstorm that lasted at least a year, condensing planetary moisture into the new basin (source: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt).
3. **Ongoing oceanic redistribution** â€” as the planet's hydrology rebalanced from a 70%-land world to a 70%-water world.

The Arathalassic Sea is therefore one of the youngest major ocean basins on Eadras, and the most directly tied to the catastrophe that defined the modern world.

## Geography

- **Bounding continents**: three landmasses, each a sundered fragment of the lost Altean Highlands â€” **[[avalon|Avalon]]** (north), **[[damedes|Damedes]]** (southwest), **[[culethor|Culethor]]** (southeast). See [[eadras-tectonic-history]].
- **Central feature**: the [[arathyn]] â€” a Large Igneous Province that grew over 2 to 8 million years of post-impact lava extrusion (Ontong-Java Plateau analogue, but on a different timescale and on a different world). It sits roughly where Altea once stood (source: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt).
- **Margin features**: hydrothermal vent fields, abyssal scarps where former Highland edges dropped into the new basin, and unstable shelf zones underlain by impact glass.
