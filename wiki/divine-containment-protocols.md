# Divine Containment Protocols

**Summary**: The four-phase procedure the Elder Gods have evolved for containing ascended [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]]-wielders — **Observation → Convergence → Pocket-universe imprisonment → Icon sealing** — built on the principle that containment in Eadras is *psychological and conceptual, not physical*. Gods defeat gods through deception, not force. Used twice in recorded history; both times under wartime pressure.

**Sources**: divine-containment-protocols-01-inworld-facts.txt (2026-05-14 ingest), divine-containment-protocols-02-meta-commentary.txt, divine-containment-protocols-03-principles-and-diagnosis.txt, divine-containment-protocols-04-narrative-scenes.txt.

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14.

---

## Why the protocols exist

A wielder of the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] who survives the activation window emerges as an **Unrestricted Elder God** — a being with the full power of an Elder God, but outside the inter-divine compact that constrains the rest of the pantheon. The Elder Gods cannot kill such a being through ordinary divine warfare; an Unrestricted's power equals theirs, and the alien-artifact principle (see [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#The alien-artifact principle|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]) prevents the Spear's transformation from being undone.

What the Elder Gods *can* do is **constrain**. The Divine Containment Protocol is the established procedure for doing so. It has been used twice — successfully against [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] (whose binding ended in annihilation when the [[worldender|Worldender]] subsequently destroyed his Icon) and against [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] (whose containment persists; he remains bound at the [[kingdom-of-xanar|Black Throne of Xanareth]] as the Motionless King). Both bindings were assembled in haste, under wartime pressure, against the first Unrestricted of their respective eras.

## The four phases

### Phase 1 — Observation

The first action is not engagement. It is **watching**. From the moment a Spear-wielder ascends, the Elder Gods refrain from direct combat and instead extend continuous metaphysical surveillance across every plane the Unrestricted is likely to enter. The purpose is not to gather information about the Unrestricted's powers — those are already known. The purpose is to identify **the moment of overextension**: the act, the location, or the emotional state in which the Unrestricted can be lured into a configuration the gods can convert into a trap. (source: divine-containment-protocols-01-inworld-facts.txt)

Observation has lasted for hours (Kudur-eá¹­ir's case, during the final hours of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]) and for decades (Xar'vion's case, during the long middle period of the [[demon-war|Demon War]] when his ascension had completed but the gods had no leverage to act). It does not have a fixed duration. It ends when the moment is found.

### Phase 2 — Convergence

Once a usable moment is identified, the Elder Gods **converge their attention and presence on the same location**. This is itself a major divine act — the pantheon does not usually act in unison — and the convergence proceeds under simultaneous license through the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand's]] Crusade-class exception. The convergence must arrive at the chosen moment without warning, which is difficult because the Unrestricted is themselves an Elder God and would normally sense the approach.

The Convergence phase therefore depends on **deception**. Multiple gods may appear to engage on a fictitious front while the actual binding work is staged elsewhere. The Unrestricted is induced to commit power to defending a battle that is not the real battle. By the time they realize the misdirection, the next phase has already begun. This is the moment the protocol earns its design principle: *gods deceive gods*.

### Phase 3 — Pocket-universe imprisonment

A **bounded reality** is constructed around the Unrestricted in the moment of overextension — a small, sealed metaphysical bubble in which the laws of the [[mythos|Mythos]] can be locally rewritten. Inside the pocket universe, the Unrestricted's powers operate, but their effects do not propagate outward; the walls absorb, redirect, or simply *do not transmit* what the Unrestricted does within them.

The pocket universe is **temporary by nature**. It is not a permanent prison. Its function is to hold the Unrestricted for the brief window in which the binding work of Phase 4 can be completed. If Phase 4 fails, the pocket universe eventually fails as well, and the Unrestricted escapes intact.

The construction of a pocket universe is a divine-scale magical operation that the Elder Gods can perform only by combining their power — and only against an opponent they have already maneuvered into the right configuration. It is not a tool of first resort.

### Phase 4 — Icon sealing

Inside the pocket universe, the Unrestricted is forced into their own [[icons|Icon]] — the singular divine construct that anchors them to the Prime Material — and the Icon is then **sealed shut by combined Elder God power**. This is the operation called **Total Icon Binding** on the [[icons#Total Icon Binding|Icons page]]; Divine Containment Protocol's Phase 4 *is* Total Icon Binding, viewed from the procedural side.

A Total-Bound god cannot leave their Icon; cannot teleport through it; cannot project power beyond the Icon's direct line of sight; cannot act through distant worshipers; and cannot freely touch their portfolio across the Prime. The Icon is converted from a divine body into a divine prison. Because the god-spirit is now fully localized within a destructible object, the Unrestricted is permanently vulnerable in a way no Elder God otherwise is — destroying the Icon annihilates the god rather than banishing them.

The pocket universe is dissolved once the Icon is sealed. The bound Unrestricted is then released into the Prime, immobile and contained.

## Why the protocol works

The protocol works because **gods are defeated through deception, not force**. The Elder Gods cannot defeat an Unrestricted in a direct power-on-power confrontation; the math does not work. What the Elder Gods *can* do is out-think an Unrestricted who is, by the nature of their ascension, focused on the wrong thing — on power, on portfolio, on the act they ascended to perform — while a coalition of older minds prepares the trap. (source: divine-containment-protocols-02-meta-commentary.txt)

This is one of the deepest principles of Eadran cosmology: **containment is psychological and conceptual, not physical**. The same principle organizes the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran's]] sealed-chapter discretion, the [[rpg/closed-time-bubble|closed time-bubble]] mechanism that excises uningestible truths, and the broader Eadran preference for hiding-as-mercy over destruction-as-solution.

Folk theology generally records the Elder Gods' victories over Spear-wielders as displays of overwhelming divine might. The actual mechanism is closer to a long con executed by exhausted administrators who could not have won any other way.

## Historical applications

### Kudur-eá¹­ir (Arathi Cataclysm, ~10 million years ago)

Phase 1 lasted hours, not years. [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir's]] ascension was the first the Elder Gods had ever encountered, and [[dao|Dao's]] curse had shredded his consciousness rather than excising a motive (see [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#The Kudur-eá¹­ir exception|the Kudur-eá¹­ir exception]]), leaving an insane Unrestricted whose decisions could not be predicted. Convergence and pocket-universe imprisonment occurred during the final hours of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]; the Icon was sealed in the moments before the [[worldender|Worldender]] arrived. The Worldender then destroyed the sealed Icon, annihilating Kudur-eá¹­ir entirely. This is the only known case of a Total-Bound god being subsequently destroyed.

### Xar'vion (Demon War, ~10,000 years ago)

Phase 1 lasted decades. Convergence required cooperation with [[lirra|Lirra's]] Weave-binding work and the actions of the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Final Wall]]; the pocket universe in which [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] was contained had to be larger and longer-lived than the Cataclysm-era version, because Xar'vion's ascension had not damaged him the way Kudur-eá¹­ir's had and his strategic awareness was intact. The Icon was sealed under combined divine pressure as the [[demon-war|Demon War]] closed. His Icon, with him fully contained within it, persists today; the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] now seeks to bring the Spear into the Icon's presence at the next [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]] in the hope of triggering an outcome the binding cannot survive.

## Limits of the protocol

The protocol has **never been tested against an Unrestricted forewarned of it**. Both Kudur-eá¹­ir and Xar'vion were the first of their kind; neither had any prior model of what containment would look like. A third Unrestricted who studied the historical record before ascending might recognize Phase 1 surveillance, refuse to overextend in Phase 2, and prevent the Convergence from finding its moment. Whether the protocol's deception layer can be reapplied in those conditions is unresolved.

The Elder Gods do not openly discuss this. The Tome notes their silence on the question.

<details class="ooc-block">
<summary>Author-side worldbuilding annotations (OOC)</summary>

The protocol works as worldbuilding because it preserves divine scale (the Elder Gods are still the most powerful beings in the setting), metaphysical seriousness (the procedure is real and costly), and mortal relevance (the protocol's gaps are where mortal action enters). Containment via deception keeps the gods *exhausted* rather than *infallible*; the gods in Eadras read as administrators and survivors, not as perfect abstractions. The Phase 1 / Phase 2 misdirection layer is also the structural reason the [[black-hand|Black Hand]]'s long con is a credible threat — they are not trying to overpower the gods; they are trying to engineer a configuration the gods' own protocol cannot trap.

The narrative-scene fragment from the source ingest opens with the Elder Gods' deliberation chamber: *"The marble table still held hairline fractures from arguments fought before humanity existed."* The fractures are the protocol's true history — every Containment that has ever been considered, fought over, and either enacted or declined.
