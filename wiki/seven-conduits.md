# The Seven Conduits

**Summary**: The Seven Conduits are eternal **magical** structures — not physical shafts — that pierce the body of Eadras along seven deep, stable axes anchored to the planetary core. Each conduit connects two antipodal surface endpoints, producing **fourteen termini** that have served as the world's primary Eldritch sinks across every era of Eadras history, including before and after both the Arathi Cataclysm and the creation of the Weave.

**Sources**: Refinement — Weave function.txt; Large page of notes 5-4-2026.txt; Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## What the Conduits Are

The Seven Conduits are the foundational magical infrastructure of Eadras. They are best understood as **fixed magical axes** through which surface [[eldritch|Eldritch]] energy is drawn back into the body of the planet, completing the great planetary cycle that begins with the [[eldritch|Source]] above and the bedrock below.

Each conduit:

- Connects **two antipodal surface points** (a paired sink, with linked behavior between the two ends)
- Is anchored to the **planetary core** (cosmologically, not physically)
- Is fixed across all geological timescales — including the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]
- Surfaces at termini surrounded by stable, abundant, predictable Eldritch density

Together the seven conduits produce **fourteen termini** at the surface. These are the same fourteen sites referenced as the [[conduit-cities|Conduit City]] termini in the modern Weave era; the conduits underlying them are older than the Weave by a vast margin.

## Origin — Evye and Fryr

The Seven Conduits were **created by [[primordial-duo|Evye and Fryr]]** when they sacrificed themselves into the world (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt).

The [[arathi|Arathi]] **built around the conduits' surface termini** but did not create them. The Arathi inherited the conduit network already in place when their civilization arose; their cities, crystalline control structures, and lattices were terminal infrastructure layered onto a pre-existing magical geometry.

## Geometric distribution

The fourteen termini are placed **roughly equidistantly across the globe**. The distribution is **not aligned to the rotational (north–south) axis** of the planet — it does not correspond to poles, equator, or rotational geometry in any neat way. The arrangement is its own geometry, internally consistent and antipodal-paired but unaligned to terrestrial cardinal frames. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

This is geographically load-bearing: a scholar mapping conduit termini against latitude or longitude finds **no axial pattern** — instead a roughly even spherical distribution that respects the antipodal-pairing constraint of the seven-conduit system but has no preferred orientation relative to Eadras's spin.

## Lirra has governed the conduits since their creation

[[lirra|Lirra]] has governed and stabilized the Seven Conduits **since their creation by Evye and Fryr** — both before and after the Demon War, both before and after the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]]'s creation. Conduit-governance is one of Lirra's oldest portfolios, predating the Weave by the entire span of pre-Weave history. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

The Weave is a *later* layer Lirra added on top of her existing conduit-stabilization role. It is not a replacement of her conduit governance; it is an additional managed layer for surface Eldritch flow. Her relationship to the conduits has been continuous from their first instantiation.

## They Are Magical, Not Physical

Common phrases describe the conduits as "world-poles" or "axes that pierce Eadras like spears" — these are useful metaphors, not physical descriptions.

The conduits are **not shafts of stone, not tunnels through rock, not ducts of any material substance**. A miner who drilled toward where a conduit terminus emerges would find no opening, no chamber, no walls. The conduit is a **magical pattern** — an enduring axis of Eldritch flow that the world has always carried in its body, the way a magnet carries its field.

This distinction is load-bearing. It is the reason the conduits **survived the Worldbreaker intact**. A physical structure penetrating crust, mantle, and core would have been destroyed by the impact, the steam-flash, and the runaway tectonics. A magical structure anchored at the cosmological level was untouched.

What *was* destroyed at the Cataclysm was everything mortal civilization had built around the conduits: the [[arathi|Arathi]] terminus cities, their crystalline control structures, their [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy lattices]] for regulating local flow, their towers, their archives. The mortal infrastructure was annihilated. The conduits themselves did not move and were not damaged.

## Antipodal Pairs and Linked Behavior

Each conduit's two endpoints are **paired** — they exhibit linked magical behavior across the shared axis. A surge at one terminus is registered at its partner. A breach at one is felt at the other. A regulating instrument tuned to one end can be calibrated against the other.

This linked-pair structure was the basis of the [[conduit-cities#The Age of the Spires (Pre-Crusade)|pre-Crusade Spire-Step network]]: the [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] who governed paired tower-domains travelled between them through the conduit's own paired axis ("Conduit Translation"). The cities at paired ends called each other **Far-Kin**.

The pairing is also what re-anchors settlement after disruption. When the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] shifted the surface relative to the conduit endpoints, the magical pull of each pair drew civilization back toward those points across millions of years. Settlements re-clustered on the same fourteen sites — see [[oldest-continuous-settlements]].

## Conduit Termini as Eldritch Sinks

A terminus is the surface point at which a conduit draws Eldritch back into the body of the planet. At each terminus, **Eldritch density is significantly higher than the surrounding land**, and the flow is **stable, continuous, and predictable** in ways that no other location matches.

Unlike rare and unstable [[eldritch-cycle#Eldritch Pools|Eldritch pools]] — the wandering localized accumulations that occasionally form spontaneous magical effects — termini are **permanent fixtures of the world's magical geography**. The pre-Cataclysm [[arathi|Arathi]] built their greatest cities on them. The post-Cataclysm civilizations rediscovered them and built again.

Properties of conduit terminus zones:

- Reliable, sustained magical effects
- Enhanced resonance for ritual work
- Persistent environmental anomalies (auroral phenomena, mana-luminescent flora, unusually fertile or unusually warped surrounding land)
- Suitable for sustained magical infrastructure (towers, academies, registries, ward arrays)
- Higher background risk during [[source-surges|Source surges]] and the [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]

## Relationship with the Weave

The creation of the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] by [[lirra|Lirra]] during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] **did not eliminate the conduits**. The Weave is a regulating layer overlaid on the surface flow of Eldritch — a managed substitute for the wild river-network of the [[eldritch-cycle|pre-Weave Eldritch cycle]] — but the underlying conduit structure is unchanged.

Specifically:

- The conduits remain at the same fourteen surface termini as before
- The conduits still draw Eldritch into the body of the planet
- The seven [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] stabilize the Weave's seven strands, *which terminate at the conduit endpoints* — but the strands are an addition, not a replacement
- Conduit terminus zones remain magically privileged sites independent of Weave regulation

In other words: the Weave changed *how* surface Eldritch flows toward the conduits, not *that* it does, and not *where*.

The conduits also are **not what the Weave protects against external magical input**. The Weave does not buffer external [[source-surges|Source surges]] or [[source-surges|Sourceflares]]; that filtering is performed by Eadras's atmosphere and magnetic field. (See [[source-surges#The Weave Does Not Buffer Sourceflares]] and [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave]].) Conduits and Weave are internal-Eldritch infrastructure; atmospheric filtering is the boundary against external Eldritch radiation.

## Symbolic and Religious Interpretation

The structure of seven conduits and fourteen paired termini has been mythologized by every major culture that recognizes it. Recurring interpretations include:

- **The Seven Pillars of the World** — the conduits as load-bearing supports of reality
- **The Paired Gates of Power** / **The Fourteen Wells** — the termini as openings between the world's surface and its hidden body
- **The Axes of Balance** — the conduits as the structural geometry of order
- **The Seven Roots of Lirra** — Lirran framing identifying the conduits with the goddess of magic

These interpretations vary widely in detail but converge on a recognition that the conduits are *foundational* — older than the gods who later regulated them, central to the world's magical character, and not optional features of the planet but structural ones.

## Hidden Layer (Tome of Baldaran)

A deeper question is still open within the Tome: whether the antipodal-paired sevenfold geometry was Evye and Fryr's free choice or whether it was an emergent constraint of the sacrifice itself. Did the Primordial Duo decide on seven? Did the Mythos's structure require seven? The Tome notes the question and lets it stand.
