# Dragonborn

**Summary**: The **champions** of the [[draconic-servitor-races|draconic servitor races]] — categorically unlike the three working castes. A dragonborn is not a population-bred people but an **individually-forged, soul-linked dragon-champion**: half-dragon, half-humanoid, with shapeshifting wings, carrying a sliver of its dragon's own soul. Dragon-might made ambulatory in the humanoid world.

**Sources**: Dragons.txt; Peoples of Eadras.txt; draconic-servitor-races.txt (2026-06-02 ingest — champion reconception).

## What a dragonborn is

Dragonborn are the most draconic of the dragon-made beings — half-dragon, half-humanoid in aspect, with shapeshifting wings. They are not a pooled, [[draconic-servitor-races#The Mandate|Mandate]]-bound, population-bred caste. They are **individually forged**, one at a time, and they exist *outside* the Mandate entirely. They are rare not because of a cap but because each is a deliberate, costly act of making.

## Forging

A dragonborn is made by an alchemical process from a **single working-caste egg** — kobold, lizardfolk, *or* snake folk — cloaked and modified by dragon **eggshell**. Into the result the dragon binds a **sliver of its own soul**, [[eldritch|Eldritch]] bound to the draconic soul: realistically a fragment of the dragon's own self.

- **Lent, not spent.** The dragon loses nothing in the long run, because the soul-fragment **returns** to the dragon upon the dragonborn's death. The investment is a loan, not a sacrifice.
- **Count as a statement.** Typically **one** dragonborn per dragon, but powerful or willing dragons field **two or three**. The number reflects how many soul-slivers a dragon chooses to extend — a visible measure of its power and commitment. A dragon attended by three dragonborn is making a statement of might.
- **Origin-caste flavour.** Source-caste traits show beneath the dragon-overlay: a snake-folk-sourced dragonborn inclines magical, a lizardfolk-sourced one mightier, a kobold-sourced one sharper-tongued. A single dragon's two or three dragonborn may span different source-castes — "soul-siblings" sharing one dragon's investment in a complementary champion-cohort.

## The soul-link

Because the dragonborn carries an actual fragment of its dragon's soul, the bond is a literal **soul-link** — not service but **extension**. The dragonborn is the dragon's own self, acting where the dragon will not go in person.

The exact reach of the link is **open canon**, but the likely shape: the dragon at minimum *feels* the dragonborn's death (as the fragment's return), and probably senses its general wellbeing and direction. Full remote control would undercut the dragonborn's agency and is probably **not** the case — sense more than control.

## The vulnerability

The soul-fragment returns on death **unless someone clever captures it.** A captured fragment is a piece of an actual dragon's soul, held by someone who should not have it: leverage over the dragon, exploitable [[eldritch|Eldritch]]-bound soul-stuff, a means of coercion or harm. This makes a dragonborn a strategic **liability** as well as an asset — an enemy who knows the mechanism might kill a dragonborn specifically to **harvest** the returning sliver. The domain of such treachery is squarely [[nox|Nox's]] (god of secrets and the corruption of death); his exact relationship to soul-fragment capture is a flagged open question.

## Two destinies for one race

The [[snake-folk|snake folk]] sharpen the point. **Loyal** (stayed) snake folk can be elevated to dragonborn — forged into a dragon's personal champion, becoming **more** (a dragon's living soul-extension). The [[snake-folk#The schism|breakaway cult]] descends toward becoming **nothing** (self-erasure). Same people, opposite vectors: the loyalists climb toward union; the cultists descend toward oblivion.
