# Dragonborn

**Summary**: The largest and most recognizably draconic of the three "Lizard People" subtypes within the [[dragonfolk|dragonfolk]] family. Dragonborn were created by [[dragons|dragons]] in response to the proliferation of humanoid races and stand as the most direct living link between dragonkind and the bipedal peoples of Eadras.

**Sources**: Dragons.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-30

---

## Origin

Dragonborn are part of the **Lizard People** branch of [[dragonfolk|dragonfolk]] — created by [[dragons|dragons]] in response to the appearance of the humanoid races (source: Dragons.txt, line 53; Peoples of Eadras.txt). They were not engineered as servants by any of [[the-three|the Three]]'s progenitor races; they are direct draconic creations.

Of the three Lizard People subtypes, dragonborn are the most recognizably "draconic" — closest to dragons in scale, presence, and bearing.

## Sibling Lines

The Lizard People family includes three subtypes, all of dragon origin:

- **Dragonborn** — largest, most directly draconic.
- **[[kobolds|Kobolds]]** — smallest.
- **[[lizardfolk|Lizardfolk]]** — middle-sized, more reptilian temperament.

The full [[dragonfolk|dragonfolk]] category also includes the [[snakefolk|snakefolk]] (Snake People — NPCs) and "Anthropomorphic Reptiles except Snakes" (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

## Anatomy and Society

Detailed canon for dragonborn anatomy and culture is not yet established. They are listed as a present-day race in modern Eadras and are intended to be playable. Their connection to the [[dragons|nine color clans]] gives them a distinctive cultural niche — closer to draconic dignity than the other dragonfolk subtypes.

## Relations to Dragons

Dragonborn occupy a particular position in draconic culture: they are kin to the dragons but not counted in the **[[runeswords|Treaty of Ratharil]] cap of 999,999 dragons**. Some dragonborn lineages claim affinity with specific dragon clans (Red, Blue, Green, etc.), and elder dragons occasionally take quiet interest in dragonborn descendants of "their" stock — though no formal clan structures connect the two populations.

In the modern era, where elder dragons are extremely reclusive and young dragons often work as humanoid-form mercenaries (source: Dragons.txt), dragonborn are sometimes the more visible face of draconic heritage in mortal society.
