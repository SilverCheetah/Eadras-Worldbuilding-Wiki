# Velrathi Cognitive Frogs

**Summary**: A peculiar custom of the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] upper class — particularly scholars, nobles, and mages — is to carry small, **brilliantly multicolored** frogs in elegant glass containers worn at the belt or kept in a pocket. The frogs secrete a toxin on their backs that is highly poisonous to most other races but, in carefully measured exposure, **enhances mental acuity** in the Velrathi, who have built generational immunity to the deadly fraction. The dosing method is direct: the Velrathi simply **lick the frog's back**. To outsiders, the sight of a dignified Velrathi scholar casually licking a frog is one of the most viscerally unsettling things about the race.

## The frogs

The frogs resemble Earth's poison-dart frogs in size, form, and the toxin-secreting biology of their backs — but where Earth's poison-dart frogs are typically a single brilliant hue per species, the Velrathi cognitive frogs are **multicolored**, with vivid intricate patterns running across their backs and limbs.

The patterning is not decorative — it is genuinely characteristic of the species, and different patterns correlate (loosely) with different toxin profiles and therefore with different cognitive effects when dosed. A Velrathi connoisseur of the practice can read a frog's pattern as a strong hint to the cognitive character it will provide. The actual matching of pattern-to-effect is empirical, has been catalogued by generations of Velrathi physiologists, and is reliable enough that buying a frog from a reputable dealer is essentially buying a known cognitive tool, not a guess.

The frogs themselves are kept and bred in Velrathi lands. Wild populations exist in certain forested regions historically associated with [[velrathi|Velrathi]] settlement; the cultivated population (much larger and more reliably-marked) lives in specialist Velrathi enclosures.

## The toxin

The frogs secrete a **potent toxin** on their skin. To most other races, ingesting the toxin in any but the most trace amount produces severe sickness; in heavier doses it is **fatal**. The toxin is real, dangerous, and well-known.

The Velrathi are different. Through **generations of careful exposure** combined with their own deliberate cultivation of immunity (in a parallel to the [[tarans|Taran]] poison-immunity tradition, though developed independently and through a different mechanism), they have developed **a limited immunity** to the deadly component of the toxin. They can absorb doses that would kill a non-Velrathi outright with no ill effect.

The cognitive-enhancement component of the toxin operates separately from the deadly component. In small Velrathi-tolerable doses, the toxin produces a measurable, perceptible improvement in **mental acuity and focus**, similar in character (though distinct in mechanism) to the effect of [[velrathi-tea-culture|measured tea consumption]] in Velrathi cognitive practice.

The improvement is strongest in the immediate hour or two after a dose, then tapers over the following several hours. Velrathi who use the frogs typically dose throughout the day on a self-developed personal schedule, the way some scholars elsewhere might use small cups of strong coffee.

## The carrying custom

It is **common practice** among upper-class Velrathi — particularly **scholars, nobles, and mages** — to carry one or more frogs on their person at all times. The frogs are kept in:

- **Elegant small glass containers**, typically with mesh ventilation, sized to slip into a pocket or hang from a belt-clip
- **Decorative belt-pouches** built around the same kind of glass interior, with an outer leather or brocade covering for protection and aesthetic display
- Occasionally **small chest-worn lockets**, though these are considered ostentatious

The container is itself a small piece of Velrathi craft. Wealthy Velrathi commission custom containers from specialist artisans; the artistry of the container is a small but real status marker, parallel to the way other cultures might invest in ornamental sword-sheaths or jewel-set wand-holders.

The carrying practice means that at a Velrathi formal gathering, a substantial fraction of the attendees will have a frog visible on their person. Newcomers to Velrathi society sometimes mistake the containers for elaborate jewelry until they look more closely.

## The dosing method

This is the part outsiders find genuinely disturbing.

When a Velrathi wants a dose, they take the frog out of its container and **lick its back directly**. There is no preparation, no distillation, no carefully measured pipette. They simply pick up the frog and lick it.

The Velrathi do not consider this remarkable. From their perspective, this is simply the most efficient and reliable dosing method — the toxin's bioavailability is highest from direct mucosal contact, the dose is naturally regulated by how long the lick lasts and how deeply they pressed, and the practice can be done quickly and without paraphernalia in the middle of any daily activity. A Velrathi scholar deep in research will pick up the frog, lick it, return the frog to its container, and resume reading without breaking concentration.

To non-Velrathi witnesses, the sight is **deeply unsettling**. The combination of:

- A dignified, formally-dressed Velrathi scholar
- An elegant glass container
- The brilliantly-colored small live amphibian inside
- And then the calm, casual licking of the frog's back

…is one of the most viscerally weird things outsiders encounter in Velrathi cultural life. It does not become less weird with familiarity. It contributes substantially to the broader unsettling reputation the Velrathi carry — *cold, brilliant, slightly inhuman, and oh, yes, they lick frogs in public*.

## Cultural meaning

For the Velrathi who practice it, the carrying-and-licking is an extension of the same logic that produces [[velrathi-tea-culture|tea ceremony]]: a measurable cognitive benefit is worth the investment to maintain, and any cultural elaboration that supports the practice (beautiful containers, careful frog-care, social conventions about dosing in company) is rational. Tea and frog work side-by-side as parallel cognitive maintenance tools.

A Velrathi who does both is not seen as either redundant or excessive — the two methods produce subtly different cognitive effects (tea is calmer, frog is sharper) and many practitioners use them at different times of day or for different kinds of work.

The class restriction is real but soft. Frogs are expensive, the containers cost more, and the practice is associated with the leisure to do scholarly work. **Working-class Velrathi typically do not carry frogs.** This is not a formal exclusion; it is a function of the practice's cost. Working-class Velrathi who develop a serious scholarly career and acquire the means usually adopt the practice in due course.

## Sourcing and trade

The frogs are bred in Velrathi enclosures and traded inside Velrathi territory and, in limited quantities, to Velrathi diaspora elsewhere. They are **not** sold to non-Velrathi outside of unusual diplomatic contexts — the Velrathi consider it irresponsible to put a deadly-toxic creature into the hands of someone who lacks the immunity to handle it safely. There is a small underground market for Velrathi frogs in certain circles among non-Velrathi who want the cognitive enhancement and are willing to risk death for it; the death rate among such buyers is high enough to keep the market small.

## Open canon

- The full **catalogue of frog-pattern-to-effect** correspondences. The user has indicated this is real but not yet detailed.
- Whether the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] (the post-Banishment exile in [[glimmerdark|Glimmerdark Gaia]]) maintain the practice. The frogs are presumably native to the Prime Material; whether they have analogues in Glimmerdark biology, or whether the exiles import them from Eadras through whatever residual trade exists, is open.
- Whether the **immunity** carries through Velrathi descendants who have produced children with non-Velrathi partners. The Velrathi-engineered races (the [[rathari|Rathari]], the [[grugi|Grug]], the others) all came from Velrathi stock; whether their descendants retain any frog-toxin immunity is open.
- The **biological mechanism** by which the cognitive enhancement separates from the deadly toxicity. The current canon treats it as empirical fact; whether anyone has ever produced a refined cognitive-only extract that non-Velrathi could safely use is open. (If they have, presumably the extract would be a Velrathi state secret.)
- The relationship between the cognitive-frog tradition and the [[tarans|Taran]] poison-immunity tradition. Both are immunity-cultivation practices; the [[tarans|Taran]] one is the older and more rigorously-proceduralized of the two, but the Velrathi worked in parallel rather than from the Taran model. Whether the two traditions have ever exchanged technique is open.
