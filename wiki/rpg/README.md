## Why a separate subfolder

The main wiki is **the in-universe knowledge base** â€” what the world contains, what the people of Eadras know about it, and (in [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome-of-Baldaran]] framing) what reality there is to record. RPG material is a different category: it is **design notes for tabletop play in the setting**, including campaign concepts that are deliberately *not* canonical history.

The flagship example is the [[#Closed-Time-Bubble Chaos Crusade|Closed-Time-Bubble Chaos Crusade]] campaign concept â€” a Crystalline Age splat campaign whose entire premise is that the events get scrubbed from canonical history at the campaign's end and only the player characters remember. This is **brilliant tabletop design** and **specifically not in-universe canon** (the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] holds the sealed chapter, but the chapter does not open in this age and the world does not contain the memory of the war).

Mixing material like this into the main wiki risks contaminating the in-universe canon with splatbook design that is supposed to remain potential rather than actual. The subfolder keeps the two concerns cleanly separated.

## What does not belong here

- **In-universe canonical history.** That goes on the main wiki pages.
- **Race profiles, cosmology, magical system descriptions.** Same.
- **Real-world inspirations.** Those go on [[inspirations]] in the main wiki.

The test is: *if this material were taken at face value as something true about the world, would it fit the in-universe wiki?* If yes, it goes on the main wiki. If no â€” if it is a play-design construct, an alternate-canon variant, an adventure-seed-not-meant-as-real, or a system-mechanics treatment for tabletop use â€” it goes here.
