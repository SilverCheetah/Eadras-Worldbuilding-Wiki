# Sahuagin

**Summary**: The Sahuagin — *the Blood Navigators* — are aquatic descendants of the **Sea'tua** who diverged dramatically in body and culture from their progenitors and from the closer-form modern oceanic races. They are deep-current voyagers who travel in **Blood Fleets**: massive, living, submarine war-constructs grown from coral, bone, and the shells of ancient sea beasts. Surface dwellers describe a Blood Fleet on its rare appearance as "an island that hunts."

**Sources**: Peoples of Eadras.txt; Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-01

---

## Origin

Sahuagin descend from the **Sea'tua**, the [[elfen|Elfen]]-engineered race created to oversee natural balance in the oceans (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt). The Sea'tua themselves were a comparatively late creation — produced roughly ten million years after the [[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]] — which makes the Sahuagin a relatively young descendant lineage in cosmic terms.

Sahuagin are noted as a "natural devolution" — smaller and less capable than the Sea'tua — but with a **completely different body shape and cultural shift** from their progenitors (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

## Sibling lines

The Sea'tua descendant tree split into two groups:

- **The Five Modern Oceanic Races** — [[merrow|Merrow]] (Tideweavers), [[sirens|Sirens]] (Echo Weavers), [[shimmercloaks|Shimmercloaks]] (Echo Traders), [[ink-lords|Ink Lords]] (Story Eaters), [[tideclad|Tideclad]] (Current Wardens). The five modern Sea'tua-descended peoples that compose ocean society.
- **The Alien Branches** — [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]] (the Fracture Cult of the Gaps) and Sahuagin. Both diverged dramatically in body plan and culture from the rest of the Sea'tua line.

Sahuagin and Kuo-toa are sibling-strangers: both are "alien" Sea'tua descendants, neither resembling each other or their common progenitor. The Sahuagin's blood-prophecy mysticism and the Kuo-toa's Gap-worship do not mix.

## Blood Fleets

The most distinctive Sahuagin institution is the **Blood Fleet** — and the most consistent surface misconception about Sahuagin is that they sail surface ships.

They do not. **Blood Fleets are submarine.** (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

Each fleet is a collection of **massive, living war-constructs grown from coral, bone, and the shells of ancient sea beasts**. They move through the water column like predatory schools — sleek, silent, and terrifyingly fast. Some of the largest Blood Fleets carry "sail" structures made of enormous living manta rays or jellyfish colonies that catch deep currents.

They rarely surface. When they do, it is usually to:

- **Raid coastal settlements** for materials, slaves, or intelligence.
- **Fulfill a particularly strong "blood prophecy"** — a directional vision shared across a fleet that compels surfacing for a specific purpose.

Surface dwellers describe a fleet that has surfaced as **"an island that hunts."** Several recorded sightings list Blood Fleet hulls being mistaken for natural island chains until the chain begins to move.

## Sahuagin worldview

The Sahuagin see the open surface ocean as a **shallow, noisy, and dishonorable place**. True navigation and worthy combat happen in the deep currents, where the great living vessels glide between thermoclines and the deep-water predators with which they have long-standing accommodations. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

Surfacing is therefore something between a chore and a humiliation. A Sahuagin captain who must surface to honor a prophecy will do so quickly, raid hard, and dive again. Surface nations that mistake brevity for weakness have learned the correction by being raided again the following season.

## Relations

- **The Five Modern Oceanic Races** ([[merrow]], [[sirens]], [[shimmercloaks]], [[ink-lords]], [[tideclad]]) — recognizable cousins via the common Sea'tua progenitor; the gulf in body plan and culture is wide. The [[tideclad|Tideclad]] in particular guard against Sahuagin incursions where Sahuagin fleets approach inhabited deep-current routes.
- **[[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]]** — fellow alien Sea'tua branch; sibling-strangers with incompatible mysticisms.
- **[[fae|Fae]]** and **[[elves|elves]]** — extremely distant kin via the common Elfen progenitor; the link is real but the cultural distance is total.
- **Surface nations** — wary. Coastal nations that share waters with Sahuagin migration routes maintain anti-raid garrisons and pay close attention to recorded "hunting island" sightings.

## Open canon

- The mechanism of "blood prophecy" — whether it is a magical discipline, a hereditary divinatory sense, or both.
- Whether any Sahuagin fleets ever cooperate with surface powers, on any terms.
- Specific famous Blood Fleets and their flagship constructs.
