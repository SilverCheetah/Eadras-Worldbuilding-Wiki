# Source Surges

**Summary**: Source surges (also called Source flares or **Sourceflares**) are natural stellar events that produce bursts of high-intensity radiation across the entire spectrum — from above radio through visible light to x-rays — including a magical component within the Eldritch spectrum. Classified by intensity from harmless C-class to mythic-scale X-class events capable of species-level transformation — the latter of which [[ixthal]] secretly amplified to create [[dragons]] and [[unicorns]]. Most surges produce no significant magical effect at the surface because the planet's atmosphere and magnetic field filter the Eldritch component before it reaches the ground; the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] does not perform this filtering.

## What Source Surges Are

The [[eldritch|Source]] is the sun and primary origin of most magic in Eadras. Like any star, it produces ordinary solar flares — bursts of high-intensity radiation across the entire electromagnetic spectrum. **What makes Source flares distinctive is not that they are a different kind of phenomenon — they are not — but that the Source's full radiative spectrum includes the Eldritch band.** A Source flare is therefore an ordinary stellar flare *plus* an Eldritch component, all in one event.

These events are called **Source surges** (older term), **Source flares** (more common technical usage), or **Sourceflares** (single-word form, increasingly standard). All three names refer to the same phenomenon.

Under most circumstances, a Source flare's effect on Eadras is negligible. The Eldritch component is filtered or absorbed before it reaches the surface in significant intensity (see [[#Atmospheric and Magnetic Filtering]] below), and the rest of the flare is just the ordinary solar weather any planet experiences.

Higher-intensity flares can produce stronger surface effects: strengthening ley lines, awakening dormant seeds, intensifying Netharim practice, agitating magical beasts, causing minor Eldritch blooms where reflected Sourceshine pools, and other localized magical disturbances. The most extreme — the X-class events — can influence planetary ecology, alter the magnetic field, trigger species-level manifestations, and reshape regional magical conditions. (sources: Playground generation 1st part.txt; Refinement — Sourceflare mechanism.txt)

## Classification Scale

Lirran astronomer-mages classify Source surges by intensity:

| Class | Description |
|---|---|
| **C-class** | Noticeable to instruments; minor ley brightening |
| **B-class** | Affects sensitive creatures, spellcasting, and Eldritch pools |
| **A-class** | Regional magical effects; possible mutations in plants or beasts |
| **S-class** | Rare and dangerous; continent-scale magical weather |
| **X-class** | Extreme, mythic-scale; capable of altering species, awakening dormant magic, or destabilizing ley systems |

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## X-Class Flare Effects

X-class flares can cause:
- Sudden magical speciation
- Awakening of latent sapience in suitable vessels
- Ley-line overbrightening
- Eldritch storms
- Mass Netharim awakening
- Mutation of eggs, seeds, embryos, or dormant spores
- Temporary thinning between Prime Material and Wyldglimmer or Glimmerdark reflections
- Increased risk of wild magic where no regulating mind is present

Under [[omniversal-laws|Omniversal Law]], magic must be ruled by a mind. A natural X-class flare would cause broad wild effects. **Regulated** X-class flares — guided by a divine or powerful mind — can produce precise, stable results. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Ixthal's Amplification Method

[[ixthal|Ixthal]] discovered a method to secretly amplify natural Source surges into X-class flares. His method:

1. **Detect** an incoming natural Source surge through pattern-sight/foresight
2. **Secretly amplify** it — not by fabricating a flare, but by widening what was already opening ("He did not cut a new door in the wall. He waited for a window to open and forced it wide enough for giants to pass through.")
3. **Regulate** the amplified surge toward specific biological vessels
4. Allow the natural carrier event to provide cover for the forbidden act

The amplification left no obvious external divine signature because it worked within the surge's own lawful magical parameters. The selectivity of the result — nine eggs transformed across separate locations without mass mutation in surrounding life — is the most telling evidence that a mind regulated the event. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## The Shard of First Shell

The **Shard of First Shell**, preserved in [[ratharil|Ratharil's]] Sealed Hall of First Flame, records the magical signature of the First Great Flare. When studied under Lirran wards, it shows the Source's natural signature overlaid by suspicious smoothness: the flare's intensity increased along a curve too precise to be natural. A divine seal prevents reverse-engineering of the amplification method.

The Shard proves something unusual happened. It does not name [[ixthal]]. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Atmospheric and Magnetic Filtering

The reason most Source flares produce no significant magical effect at the surface is **atmospheric and magnetic-field filtering**, not Weave regulation.

Eadras's atmosphere, magnetic field, and (in the modern era) the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave's]] surface-flow regulation each interact with incoming radiation in different ways:

- The **atmosphere** absorbs and disperses much of the conventional electromagnetic component (heat, x-rays, ionizing radiation in the ordinary sense)
- The **magnetic field** deflects much of the charged particle component
- Both layers also significantly attenuate the **Eldritch component** of the flare, the only filtering layers that act on it before it reaches the surface

Only **exceptionally high-intensity flares (X-class events)** carry enough Eldritch energy to penetrate these layers in usable quantity. This is why such flares are rare in their effects even when more modest flares are common in their occurrence. (source: Refinement — Sourceflare definition.txt)

## The Weave Does Not Buffer Sourceflares

The [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] is sometimes loosely described as "buffering" Source surges. **It does not.** This is a common misunderstanding worth correcting explicitly. (source: Refinement — Weave function.txt; user direction 2026-05-04)

The Weave's function is the regulation of **internal Eldritch energy** — the energy already inside the [[eldritch-cycle|planetary cycle]], being processed from Sourceshine through bedrock, distributed across the surface in managed channels, and reintegrated through the [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]]. The Weave is the regulating layer for *that* internal flow.

A Sourceflare's Eldritch component is **external magical radiation** — energy that arrives at Eadras already in usable Eldritch form, from outside the planetary cycle entirely. It does not enter the system through bedrock-transformation; it strikes the surface directly. The Weave's regulating machinery has no purchase on it.

The only filters that act on incoming external Eldritch radiation are:

- **The atmosphere** (partial absorption and dispersion)
- **The magnetic field** (partial deflection)

That is the entire stack. A high-intensity flare that exceeds atmospheric and magnetic capacity reaches the surface intact, regardless of whether the Weave exists or how well it is functioning.

This has practical implications for understanding the Cataclysm-era and modern magical environments:

- **Pre-Weave conditions** allowed greater penetration overall, because the planet's atmosphere and magnetic field were slightly thinner before the Cataclysm thickened both. High-impact Sourceflares were correspondingly more likely.
- **Post-Weave conditions** further restrict surface-level magical effects from *internal* Eldritch — the Weave keeps wild magic in check — but **do not provide additional protection against external Sourceflares**.
- **Another [[ixthal|Ixthal]]-style amplified Sourceflare event remains physically possible in the modern era**, since the Weave does not stand between the Source and the surface for this kind of input. The threshold conditions (rare flare intensity coinciding with the right environmental and systemic state) are what make it rare, not Weave protection.

A would-be [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Genesis Seeker]] who believes the Weave must be defeated before they can repeat the Forbidden Kindling has misdiagnosed the obstacle. The Weave was never the obstacle.

## Threshold Conditions for Significant Effects

For a Sourceflare to produce significant surface effects, multiple conditions must align simultaneously:

- **Flare intensity** must be sufficient to penetrate atmospheric and magnetic filtering — typically X-class
- **Atmospheric state** must allow the Eldritch component to reach the surface (heavy cloud cover, dense ionospheric activity, or magnetic-field disturbance can all alter outcomes)
- **Systemic state** of the planet must be receptive — pre-Weave instability and recovering ecosystems made the dragon and unicorn manifestations possible; the same flare landing on a stable, dense, well-regulated modern world might do nothing visible

This **threshold requirement** explains the long intervals between major manifestation events — millions of years between [[dragons]] and [[unicorns]] is not because Sourceflares are rare in occurrence (they are not) but because flares that meet all the threshold conditions for surface manifestation are. (source: Refinement — Sourceflare definition.txt)
