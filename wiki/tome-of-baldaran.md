# Tome of Baldaran

**Summary**: A sentient, self-transforming chronicle created by the Ascended Mortal God Baldaran that records every important event and all collected knowledge in the [[eadras-cosmology|Eadras Mythos]].

**Sources**: The Tome of Baldaran.txt; Grok Chat 5-8-26.txt; user direction 2026-05-09 evening (relationship with the Bureau of Records); Cosmology_01_InWorld_Facts.txt (2026-05-12 ingest, scope-and-curatorial clarification).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-12

---

## Overview

The Tome of Baldaran exists to chronicle every important event in this [[eadras-cosmology|Mythos]] — specifically decisions that lead to inflection points where timelines split — and to record all knowledge ever discovered in Eadras.

## Form

The Tome appears as a **culturally relevant document** to whoever encounters it:
- A **psi crystal** to dragons and unicorns
- A **book** to humans
- A **rune-slate** to dwarves
- A **bark scroll** to elves
- And so on for every culture

## Nature

The Tome is a **sentient object**, created for its singular purpose. It has been present in society since the Second Age, possessing the magical ability to conceal and transform itself. It often appears to aid heroes at unexpected times — a purpose it was not designed for, but one its innate intelligence seems to enjoy.

## Creation

Created by the Ascended Mortal Human God **[[baldaran|Baldaran]]** in the Third Age, with assistance from:
- The Elder God **[[gods-of-eadras|Nox]]**
- The Elderborn **[[gods-of-eadras|Erendor]]** (God of Time)
- The Elderborn **[[gods-of-eadras|Handwright]]** (God of Craftwork)

The Tome exists in a **pocket dimension** outside of Eadras (within Baldaran's godly realm). This is why it can accurately chronicle events that occurred before it was created — it exists partially outside time and can access records of past events.

### Why Baldaran created the Tome

The Tome is **Baldaran's personal solution** to a gap he could not get the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]] to fill.

The [[divine-bureaucracy#Bureau of Records|Bureau of Records]] maintains vast, dry, bureaucratic archives of mortal history — raw data, prayers, oaths, and deeds, recorded with mechanical precision. Baldaran wanted something more. He wanted **an active, intelligent observer of history** — something that would not just record events, but **understand them, interpret them, and apply judgment** to what was truly significant.

The Bureaucracy was unable (or unwilling) to provide this. The Bureau of Records' mandate is mechanical-precision archive-keeping; an interpretive, judgmental, sentient chronicler is not what the Bureau is structured to produce, and the institutional politics of getting the Bureaucracy to acquire that capability were apparently more than even an Ascended Mortal God of knowledge wanted to fight through.

So Baldaran built the Tome himself, as his own godly-realm artifact, and the Tome became what the Bureau of Records was never going to be: an interpreter rather than a registrar, a judge rather than a clerk, a living archive with discretion rather than a dead archive with completeness.

This is the structural origin of the Tome's most distinctive features — its sentience, its curatorial discretion, its willingness to seal chapters, its preference for understanding over enumeration. The Tome was designed to do what the Bureaucracy *won't*.

## Naming conventions in the Tome

Some events are remembered by Eadras's various peoples under their own names; the Tome records the event-as-such alongside those local names. For instance, dragons and unicorns may have private names for the [[chaos-crusades|two times raw Chaos breached the Mythos]], but the Tome lists them simply as **the First Chaos Crusade** and **the Second Chaos Crusade**. Where the Tome's neutral name is used in this wiki, that is the canonical chronicle entry; where alternate cultural names exist, they are noted on the relevant page.

The Tome's **scholarly voice** — measured, factually careful, and explicit about what is not known — is one of the six canonical [[mythic-recitations#4-the-institutional-mythic-voices|institutional mythic voices]] of Eadras. The Tome's open-chapter recitation forms of the [[mythic-recitations#1-6-the-scholarly-version-tome-of-baldaran-fragmentary-history-of-the-first-age|Fall of Ssar'veth (Fragmentary History of the First Age)]] and [[mythic-recitations#2-4-the-scholarly-lirran-version-the-first-and-second-breaches|the First and Second Breaches]] are preserved alongside the bardic, [[dragons|dragon]], [[unicorns|unicorn]], humanoid, [[orcs|orcish]], and [[katharim|Katharim]] forms at [[mythic-recitations|Mythic Recitations of the Foundational Catastrophes]]. The Tome's voice is the canonical record-of-the-uncertain-but-rigorously-recorded; *the precise mechanism remains unknown* is its signature phrase. The Tome admits the existence of a name, a day, an artifact, and an alien Falling Star at the Cataclysm, but the [[worldender-crystal|Worldender Crystal]] mechanism, the First Breach geometry, the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear's]] full role, and the post-event Spear-prison sequence remain in the closed chapters.

## Knowledge held in the Tome

The Tome is omniscient with respect to Eadras. It records secrets that no living being knows — including the [[neverborn|Neverborn]]'s role in the Chaos Crusades, the [[dao|Dao]] avatar's hidden status from the Elder Gods, the [[black-hand|inner truth of the Black Hand]], and others. Reading the Tome does not automatically expose those secrets — the Tome chooses what to reveal and to whom — but the chronicle itself contains them.

## Recording mechanism: after-the-fact, with branching

The Tome records reality **after** events occur, not as they happen. It is a chronicle of the past as the past *settled into being*, not a live feed of the present. Combined with its existence in a pocket dimension partially outside time (see [[#Creation|Creation]] above), this means the Tome can document the history of any era — including eras before its own creation — without ever pre-empting the unfolding of events that have not yet finished resolving.

The recording requires an **observer** to have witnessed the event for it to be eligible for entry. The Tome is itself a designated cosmic observer (see [[observer-and-naming]]), but it is only *one* such observer; events that no mind ever witnessed remain genuinely unknowable, even to the Tome.

### Potential events at inflection points

The Tome's mandate explicitly includes **decisions that lead to inflection points where timelines split**. At such moments, the Tome can record **not only the path the timeline takes but the paths that were possible but not taken** — potential events that might have occurred had the inflection point gone differently.

When the timeline subsequently shifts and those potential events do not actualize — when the path the world ends up walking is one path and the others fall away — the recorded potentials remain in the Tome's archive but are **sealed**. They are real on the page (the Tome saw the inflection point and recorded what could have followed) but they are not part of the actualized history of the world.

This is one of the canonical reasons the Tome contains **chapters that depict events which did not happen in canonical Eadras history**. The Tome is not lying; the events were genuinely possible at the moment of recording. The timeline simply went elsewhere, and the Tome's record of the road-not-taken is preserved-but-sealed.

This mechanism is complementary to (but distinct from) the [[divine-bureaucracy#Bureau of Prediction|Bureau of Prediction]]'s forward-looking work: the Bureau monitors possible futures *before* inflection points; the Tome records the potentials *at and after* inflection points. The two systems triangulate on the branching structure of time without doing the same job.

## The Tome as curator of acceptable reality

The Tome is not a neutral archive. It actively decides which of its records to display to readers and which to keep sealed.

The Tome **seals chapters for several reasons**, and the canonical ones include:

- **Non-canonicity.** Some material the Tome holds is, in the Tome's judgment, **not part of the actual canonical history of Eadras** — recorded because some observer remembered it, but not part of what really happened in the world's authoritative timeline. The Tome marks this status by sealing the chapter. The seal is, in effect, the Tome's way of saying *"this is in the records, but this is not history."*
- **Potential events that did not actualize.** Closely related to the above: at inflection points where the timeline could go several ways, the Tome can record the potentials. When the timeline shifts and the recorded potential never actualizes, the chapter remains in the archive but is sealed. The events were genuinely possible when recorded; they simply did not become real. (See [[#Potential events at inflection points|Potential events at inflection points]] above.)
- **Events the universe itself chose to undo.** A specific subset of the above: when reality has used a temporal closed-loop, an excised pocket, or a similarly surgical correction to remove something that "shouldn't have happened," the resulting unactualized timeline is, in the Tome's view, simply another non-realized potential. The Tome's posture in such cases is: *this technically happened in the unactualized branch, but the actualized timeline went elsewhere, and the record will not be shown to anyone who does not already know.*
- **Material too dangerous or too premature for the current age.** Some truths the Tome holds are real, canonical, and consequential — but their disclosure in this era would cause more harm than benefit. The Tome seals these and waits, often for thousands of years, before opening them.
- **Material the Tome simply judges not worth showing the current age.** The Tome's curatorial discretion is not exhaustively rule-governed; sometimes a chapter is sealed because the Tome itself has decided that, on balance, this should not be open to the present.

This makes the Tome feel less like a passive ledger and more like an active participant in maintaining the world's working version of itself. The implications are deliberately unsettling:

- Sealed chapters exist. The Tome holds them.
- Their number is unknown.
- The Tome's criteria for sealing are its own.
- A sealed chapter may correspond to a real event, a never-actually-canonical event, an event the universe itself unmade, or an event the Tome alone judges should not be shown — and the reader has no way of telling which from the outside.

It is consistent with the Tome's broader pattern of **choosing what to reveal and to whom**: the curator-of-acceptable-reality role is not a separate function but the same function applied across multiple categories of judgment.

The [[rpg/closed-time-bubble|Closed-Time-Bubble Chaos Crusade]] is the most-discussed example of a Tome-sealed chapter — but it is **not canonical Eadras history**. It is splat material for the [[rpg/closed-time-bubble|Crystalline Age RPG splat]], a campaign concept depicting exactly the kind of unactualized-potential timeline the Tome would seal: an inflection point at the height of [[arathi|Arathi]] civilization where Chaos *could* have broken in catastrophically, recorded by the Tome as a potential, then sealed when the actualized timeline went elsewhere. The Crusade itself is non-canonical; the Tome's *capacity to seal unactualized potentials* is canonical. The campaign and the canonical sealing-mechanism complement each other without the campaign itself being part of Eadras's history.

In-universe scholars who notice the broader sealing pattern (mostly Katharim memory-priests and a handful of dragon archivists) generally treat the Tome's sealed chapters with respect rather than suspicion — the Tome is widely understood to have judgment, and its judgments have so far been borne out. But the same scholars also acknowledge that whatever has been sealed, by definition, cannot be cross-checked.

The Tome's scope is therefore broader than a strict actualized-history archive: it covers **actualized events, unactualized inflection-point potentials, and curatorial-judgment sealings**, all under the same set of mechanisms. **Most unactualized potentials are never revealed.** They sit sealed in the Tome's archive indefinitely, often forever — the timeline did not walk that path, the world's working history is the path it did walk, and there is no era in which the unactualized version would be more useful to display than to hold. This is the structural reason the Tome can hold an enormous archive of road-not-taken material without that archive contaminating the canonical history Eadras's scholars and priests work with: the canonical history is the *unsealed* layer, and the *sealed* layer is the rest. The proportion is heavily skewed toward sealed — the Tome curates a small revealed surface over a vast hidden depth, and most of what is hidden will stay hidden because revealing it would do more harm than good or simply has no living relevance.

## Relationship with the Bureau of Records

The Tome and the [[divine-bureaucracy#Bureau of Records|Bureau of Records]] are the **two parallel record-keeping systems** of divinity in Eadras, with fundamentally different mandates.

| | **Bureau of Records** | **Tome of Baldaran** |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Bureaucratic institution | Sentient artifact |
| **Style** | Vast, dry, mechanical | Active, intelligent, interpretive |
| **Records** | Raw data — prayers, oaths, deeds, with mechanical precision | Events — with understanding, interpretation, and judgment of significance |
| **Discretion** | None (or close to none) — the registrar files what comes in | Curatorial — chooses what to reveal, when, and to whom; can seal chapters |
| **Origin** | Built by the Bureaucracy as one of its core institutional functions | Built by [[baldaran|Baldaran]] personally, as the solution to a gap the Bureaucracy refused to fill |
| **Scope** | Mortal-deed-and-prayer archive | Every important event in the Mythos plus all collected knowledge |

The two systems exist in parallel and **occasionally produce conflicting records**. The exact nature of their relationship — how much they overlap, whether they share information, what happens when their versions of history diverge — remains a point of **quiet theological tension** in the broader divine institution. Neither institution publicly contests the other; both are functioning components of how divinity records the world; but the specifics of *how exactly they relate* are unsettled.

In ordinary cases the two are consistent: the Bureau files what happened, the Tome interprets what happened, both agree on *that it happened*. The divergence emerges when the Tome's curatorial judgment kicks in — when the Tome decides a chapter should be sealed because the event was non-canonical, was undone by the universe, was too dangerous for the current age, or otherwise warranted withholding. In such cases the Bureau's mechanical record may reflect a different version of events from the Tome's interpretive one. The Bureau's record is *accurate* by its own standards; the Tome's record is *true* by its own standards; the two are not the same thing.

(The [[rpg/closed-time-bubble|Closed-Time-Bubble Chaos Crusade]] is the most-discussed splat illustration of this kind of divergence — though as splat material rather than canonical history; see [[#The Tome as curator of acceptable reality]] for the canonical sealing principles the campaign concept depends on.)

Most working priests, scholars, and Ascended Mortals encountering the divergence in any specific case treat it the way most theological tensions in Eadras are treated: respectfully, professionally, and without forcing a public reconciliation. The two systems do their respective jobs; the divergence is acknowledged when it has to be acknowledged; the underlying question of which is the *real* record is not a question anyone has the standing to answer.
