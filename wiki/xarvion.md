# Xar'vion

**Summary**: Xar'vion is an Icon-bound Unrestricted Elder God â€” a brilliant and tragic [[velrathi|Velrathi]] archmage of the final centuries of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]], a sickly orphan whose elder sister [[velena|Velena]] was raped and murdered by a gang of Tarran noble youths. He swore he would never be powerless again and would find a way to bring her back; centuries of ruthless political, magical, and military maneuvering positioned him to claim the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]] on God's Day. He lost his motive to Dao's curse, triggered the War of Domination and the [[demon-war|Demon War]] (the empire's collapse a side effect, not his goal), and now exists as the Motionless King: an unmoving divine statue who still holds court and receives his cult.

## Early Life

Xar'vion was born a sickly [[velrathi|Velrathi]] orphan in the brutal final days of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]. His older sister [[velena|Velena]] â€” strong-willed and fiercely protective â€” served as his only shield and family. She protected the weak Xar'vion from predators on the streets of the imperial capital, and he loved her for it. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

One night, a gang of young Tarran noblemen from a sadistic club caught her. They raped and murdered her for sport while Xar'vion could only watch, too weak to stop them. She died trying to shield him one final time.

That night broke something in Xar'vion permanently. He swore two vows: **he would never be powerless again**, and **he would find a way to bring Velena back.** (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

Three years later, now a teenager, Xar'vion took a job as a waiter in the very tavern those noblemen frequented. He spent weeks studying their habits. On the exact anniversary of his sister's death, he served them all drinks laced with massive doses of poison. He set the cups on their table, turned around, walked out the back door, and never looked back. He didn't need to watch them die. He already knew they were dead.

## Rise to Power

That night became the seed of a man who would spend centuries building the **[[kingdom-of-xanar|Kingdom of Xanar]]** and the **[[black-hand|Black Hand]] spy network** â€” all for one purpose: to claim the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]] and truly resurrect his sister.

Not to speak with her shade. Not to animate her body. To truly restore the dead.

This desire already pressed against the [[omniversal-laws|Fifth Omniversal Law]]: *Death is sovereign in the end.*

Xanar was a real kingdom with laws, courts, temples, scholars, and ordinary citizens. Its public ideology centered on remembrance, continuity, and the conquest of grief. The slogan *"No Name Forgotten"* may have originated with Velena's funeral. The Black Hand infiltrated temples, courts, academies, guilds, and governments across Eadras, using Xanar's wealth, roads, and necromantic expertise as cover. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

His ruthless political, magical, and military maneuvering destabilized the already-decaying Firethorn Empire â€” but **the empire's collapse was a side effect of his obsession, not his primary intent.** The Firethorn Empire had been sliding into decadence and ennui for nearly a millennium before Xar'vion came of age; he was the final, devastating catalyst that toppled what was already rotting. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

## The Ascension

Xar'vion overcame every protection around a major **Velrathi arcane stronghold** â€” a city held by a Velrathi arch-wizard with large numbers of pre-Weave wizards, priests, and troops at his command, and the site at which the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] was then concealed under that arch-wizard's keeping. He destroyed the city, killed the defenders and the arch-wizard, and then claimed the Spear and activated it. (source: More Xarvion.txt; the specific name *Tower of Stars* attached to the stronghold in earlier drafts was retired from canon during the 2026-05-13 spear-prison-sequence revision; the stronghold itself remains canonical, the name does not.)

He became an **Unrestricted Elder God.**

[[dao|Dao's]] curse triggered: his love for his sister â€” the one thing that still made him human â€” was ripped away in a single instant. What remained was the machinery of ambition without the heart that had once justified it: power, domination, possession, control.

He became terrifying not because he was evil for wanting his sister back â€” many mortals would understand that desire â€” but because grief was *amputated*, not healed.

(Note that the curse interacts with Xar'vion in its standard mode: it strips the deepest desire, leaving him intact but without the motive that drove him. Only [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir]], because he had previously contacted the [[neverborn|Neverborn]] through the First Breach **and** was the Spear's creator, was struck by the curse in its consciousness-shattering mode.)

### Recurring tragedy â€” Xar'vion and Kudur-eá¹­ir

Xar'vion's arc structurally parallels [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir's]]. Both are **driven by grief**, both **refuse to accept lawful limitation**, both **attempt to override existential structure**, and both are **catastrophic despite compassionate origins**. Where Kudur-eá¹­ir rejects **entropy** (the Sixth Omniversal Law), Xar'vion rejects **death** (the Fifth). Both represent **compassion without sufficient humility before cosmological law** â€” and the recurrence of the pattern across the millions of years between them is one of the setting's central philosophical themes. The [[omniversal-laws|Omniversal Laws]] are not arbitrary decrees; they are *the conditions of coherent existence*. To refuse them on behalf of those one loves is the canonical Eadran failure mode of the gifted and the merciful. (source: Commentary Ingest â€” Demon War, Chaos Crusades, Demons, and Aberrations.txt)

## The War and the Demon Catastrophe

Every god encouraged the world to resist Xar'vion. All peoples fought him â€” not from love of one another, but because every temple, druid circle, dragon elder, and unicorn herd-sense pointed to the same truth: if Xar'vion won, all peoples would become possessions.

For years, the war raged globally. When Xar'vion was losing, he made a desperate and catastrophically stupid decision: **he summoned a Demon Prince to Eadras.** This tore open a portal to the Demon Mythos and caused the [[demon-war|Demon War]].

It took Xar'vion five full years to realize the Demon Prince had no intention of honoring any promises. The demon simply wanted to consume everything in this [[mythos]].

Only then did Xar'vion turn to the Elder Gods and say: *"I have made a terrible mistake. Let me join you."*

The gods accepted. Xar'vion turned all his forces and divine power against the demon lord, forcing it back through the portal. The moment the portal was sealed, the Elder Gods betrayed Xar'vion and trapped his entire essence inside his own **Icon**.

## Current State: The Motionless King

Xar'vion is not imprisoned in a dungeon or sealed away. He is **Icon-bound** â€” forced by the combined power of the Elder Gods into his [[icons|Icon]], which now exists as an unmoving statue. He is the Motionless King.

**What he cannot do:**
- Leave the Icon or teleport
- Project divine power beyond his line of sight
- Act freely through distant worshipers

**What he can still do:**
- Speak, perceive, and judge
- Command, bless, and curse those before him
- Dominate weak wills within his line of sight
- Brand followers with divine marks (the **Gaze-Mark**)
- Empower objects placed before him

His Icon's most-frequently-occupied location is the **Black Throne of Xanareth**, in the ruins of his former capital â€” but the Icon is **mobile**, and the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] has moved it repeatedly across ten thousand years (see [[black-hand]]). Where the Icon currently sits is therefore a Black Hand operational secret, not a fixed point. The innermost Black Hand â€” called **Those Who Stand in the Gaze** â€” comes before him wherever he is, receives direct guidance, and bears the **Gaze-Mark** as authentication of having stood in his presence. He holds court. He is present. He is simply still.

If the Icon is destroyed, Xar'vion is permanently and completely gone â€” his god-spirit destroyed with it (since he is Icon-bound rather than merely linked through the Icon). (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

### The Black Hand's Present Plan

The Black Hand's **Inner Hand** has survived ten thousand years and still serves Xar'vion â€” though most of the larger Outer Hand membership knows nothing of him. The Inner Hand's operational goal is to deliver the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] to him during the next **[[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]** (an imminent celestial alignment). The exact mechanism by which the Spear frees the Motionless King is **speculative** â€” even Xar'vion himself does not know which of two possibilities, if either, will actually work:

- **Freeing only**: the Spear simply unmakes the Icon-binding when brought into his presence at the proper conjunction.
- **Second ascension**: holding the Spear at God's Day opens the path from the Source a second time, granting Xar'vion a *second helping* of divine power that raises him **above** the Elder Gods. A second ascension would almost certainly free him from the Icon as a side effect, but no one knows whether a second ascension is mechanically possible at all.

The Inner Hand's theology â€” the **Second Crown Doctrine** â€” wraps the ambitious second-ascension reading. See [[black-hand]] for the full discussion and for the phase history of the Inner Hand's purpose. The original pre-ascension purpose of resurrecting [[velena|Velena]] vanished from the cult's own records within roughly two centuries after Xar'vion's ascension and was replaced first by a generic free-from-Icon goal, then refined into the present Spear-focused plan.

A previous attempt at the [[lost-zenith|Lost Zenith]] (the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]], roughly **9,000â€“10,000 years ago**) failed to find the Spear. The Black Hand instead engineered the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] â€” preceded by the hundred-year [[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]] ramp-up â€” breaking the Weave for a thousand years (the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]) as the diversion's catastrophic side-effect. The Spear has remained in its original hiding place across the full 10,000-year span since Xar'vion's ascension. (sources: Playground generation 2nd part.txt; Black hand revised.txt)

### No external prison or guard order

There is **no external order guarding Xar'vion's Icon**, and there is no prison around it. The line-of-sight restriction on Xar'vion's power is a property of his Total Icon Binding (see [[icons#Total Icon Binding|Icons]]), not a containment regime maintained by anyone outside.

The only question for any party seeking him is where the Icon currently sits â€” and the Black Hand has moved it repeatedly across ten thousand years. The Black Throne of Xanareth is the Icon's most-frequently-occupied location, not its only one.

## The Spear, the volunteer god, and the broken seal

What is not generally remembered in the mortal mythic record â€” but is preserved in [[katharim|Katharim]] memory, [[dragons|dragon]] esoteric tradition, and the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran's]] sealed chapters â€” is the specific atrocity by which Xar'vion took the Spear. The gods' third hiding of the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] was a [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#hiding-3-reverse-time-bubble-over-a-god-prison-in-the-deepest-shadowdark-after-darkhoof|reverse-time-bubble god-prison]] in the deepest depths of the [[shadowdark|Shadowdark]]: a volunteer [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Ascended Mortal Dragon God]] had voluntarily entered Total Icon Binding, sealed inside an Icon with the Spear, the prison wrapped in a reverse time bubble that ran subjective time at vast acceleration. By the time Xar'vion broke into the prison, the volunteer god had subjectively endured roughly **10^37 years** of unbroken solitude â€” vastly older, more powerful, and more isolated than any other being in the Mythos, almost certainly fractured by what the working canon calls **divine schizophrenia**.

Xar'vion **killed the volunteer dragon god** in the act of taking the Spear. What he absorbed in the killing â€” power, knowledge, perhaps fragments of the volunteer's fractured personas now lodged inside his own Icon â€” is open canon and load-bearing for any reading of the Motionless King's later operations. The killing itself is among the heaviest specific moral injuries in Eadran cosmology: a god who had voluntarily sacrificed themselves to hold the line for the rest of the Mythos was slain by a Netharim-king pursuing resurrection magic, who immediately afterward triggered the [[demon-war|Demon War]].

The dragons remember the volunteer as a sacred martyr. The fact of their death â€” and Xar'vion's role in it â€” is part of why the dragons' [[mythic-recitations#3-5-what-different-peoples-emphasize|grim-satisfaction mythic register]] applied to Xar'vion is *complicated* by deeper grief in their esoteric tradition: the Demon War cost them a martyr-god as well as a war.

## Mythic memory â€” the Still King

In living cultural memory across most Eadran peoples, Xar'vion is remembered as the **Still King** or **Motionless King** of the [[demon-war|Demon War]] â€” the Netharim-king who opened a gate to Hell in mad grief and was eventually bound in eternal stone by the gods. The canonical recitation forms in the common, longer-bardic (*The War of the Still King*), [[orcs|orcish]] (*[[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves#Cultural Memory|Night of the Gray Wall]]*), and [[katharim|Katharim]] (*The Breaking of the Three*) voices are preserved at [[mythic-recitations#3-the-demon-war-myths-of-the-still-king|Mythic Recitations Â§ 3]]. The recitations produce two proverbs in active modern use: *some doors should never be opened* and *grief unchecked invites ruin*. Canonical constraint on every common form: **the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] is not commonly known to be associated with Xar'vion**; cult-of-the-Still-King rumors exist, but the connection to the modern criminal-political organization is held by the Inner Hand and the deepest scholarship only.

The [[dragons|dragon]] version is delivered with characteristic contempt â€” *"A mortal who thought he could control what he could not understand"* â€” applying the dragons' grim-satisfaction register; the [[unicorns|unicorn]] version is darker, claiming the Demon Prince is not truly gone but only waiting *for the next heartbroken fool to open the door again*. The [[beast-folk|beast-folk]] are an exception: they have *tried to forget* the Demon War, and Xar'vion's name with it. (Why is open canon.)

## His Legacy on Magic

Xar'vion weaponized grief-magic and soul-theory â€” techniques originally developed to restore the dead â€” and repurposed them into domination:
- Soul-chains instead of restoration
- Memory-breaking instead of remembrance
- Corpse-legions instead of mourning rites

He is one reason certain cultures distrust [[necromancy-and-undeath|necromancy]] despite its technical neutrality. Not because necromancy is inherently evil, but because history's greatest tyrant stained the discipline.
