# Arathi

**Summary**: The Arathi were an ancient pre-Cataclysm civilization of humanoid insect-reptile hybrids who dominated Eadras for ten million years, mastering magitech and crystalmancy before being annihilated in the [[arathi-cataclysm]].

## Overview

The Arathi were both a race and a civilization â€” humanoid reptile-insect hybrids who built the **first true civilization on Eadras** â€” the First Civilization, before dragons, before unicorns, before any later peoples. They existed in the First Age and dominated the world for at least ten million years. Their culture emphasized mastery, order, and the harmonious blending of magic and technology. *Arathi* serves as both singular and plural â€” a single Arathi, many Arathi.

**In their own tongue they called themselves the Nerathi.** *Nerathi* is a word from their own naming conventions â€” see [[naming-conventions#arathi-naming|the Arathi naming pattern]] â€” and is the self-name preserved in their language and in surviving Arathi-era inscriptions. *Arathi* is how the modern Eadran world refers to them: a post-catastrophe outsider name worn smooth by ten million years of foreign tongues. Both forms are correct in their respective registers; the Tome and most modern scholarship use *Arathi*. (source: notes on various topics 2.txt)

**The Arathi left no descendants.** Every last Arathi perished in the cataclysm or its aftermath. Their language became a fossil. Their cities became pressure-crushed labyrinths. Any later culture claiming Arathi descent is mistaken, fraudulent, religiously symbolic, or politically opportunistic. Two proverbs preserve the cultural memory: *"Every tyrant eventually discovers he is descended from Arath"* (used of any ruler whose ambition is felt to be unearned) and *"Proud as Arath before steam"* (used of complacency about permanence â€” of a person, faction, or civilization that has become certain its current order will last). The second proverb has effectively domesticated the Arathi catastrophe into everyday warning speech: cultures that say *"proud as Arath"* are using the Arathi as a teaching tool, and the trauma has accumulated into common wisdom. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt; Arathi_01_InWorld_Facts.txt)

### Arathi color palette

Arathi **natural** pigmentation â€” across scales, crests, and eyes â€” draws from **anything in the visible spectrum from red to violet, with two omissions: never yellow and never orange.** Allowed colors include reds, magentas, pinks, greens, cyans, blues, indigos, and violets, in any combination and saturation. The two warm-yellow bands are absent from natural scale and crest pigmentation across the entire species. (source: clarifying conversation 2026-04-30)

**Eye exception â€” rare golden eyes.** The eyes can carry an exception the rest of the body cannot: **golden eyes do occur, but they are rare**, and they are not merely cosmetic. **Golden eyes indicate exceptional strength in [[eldritch|Eldritch]].** A gold-eyed Arathi is recognized at a glance as a being of unusual magical power â€” disproportionately likely to become a crystalmancer, an archmage, a ley engineer, or a member of the ruling council. Most Arathi have eyes within the standard red-to-violet palette; the gold-eyed minority dominates the upper tiers of Arathi magical society.

### Applied orichalcum

What looks like gold on Arathi scales and crests is not natural pigmentation but **applied [[mana-metals#Orichalcum|orichalcum]]** â€” the mana-transformed reddish-gold metal that Altea was partly built of. Arathi craftspeople work orichalcum into intricate metalwork that integrates with the body: filaments traced along scales, accents woven into crest feathers, and harness plates fitted to the chest. The orichalcum is decorative and functional simultaneously: as a mana-storing alloy, it serves as a fitted personal magitech infrastructure for whatever the wearer practices.

(The orbiting items are different â€” those are working magical crystals, magic items, and spelled gems, not orichalcum metalwork. See *Personal magical equipment* above.)

The pictured crystalmancer demonstrates: her gold-streaked crest, gold-traced scales, and gold harness are all applied orichalcum, not pigment. Her gold eyes are natural â€” the rare Eldritch-strong eye trait. The floating items around her are her active magical toolkit. Each element has its own in-world explanation; together they read as one coherent rendering.

**Personal magical equipment**: They wore intricate orichalcum harness armor embedded with pulsing mana-gems, and were typically surrounded by **levitating magical crystals, magic items, and spelled gems orbiting their bodies** â€” working implements, not jewelry. These were a magus's deployed toolkit kept in personal orbit so they could be reached, activated, redirected, or recombined without taking up hand-grip during a working. A crystalmancer might keep storage crystals, focusing gems, channeling rods, ward-shards, and active spell-anchors all simultaneously aloft around them. The "jewelry" appearance was secondary; the function was operational.

**Nature**: Organized, hierarchical, entirely devoted to mastery of their arts. Their orientation toward perfection was **patient and methodical** â€” Arathi society as a whole was willing to wait centuries or millennia to refine a working, build a better city, or solve a metaphysical problem rigorously. The pinnacle of perfection, in the consensus Arathi view, was *near but not yet*. **[[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir]] (later corrupted to Ssar'veth) was a hidden societal outlier**: he secretly believed the Arathi era was already the pinnacle and sought a shortcut to lock it in forever. That outlier conviction is the seed from which the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] eventually grew. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

## The Civilization

### The Twin Pillars: Crystalmancy and Magitek

Arathi supremacy rested on the perfect marriage of two arts (source: Crystalmancy.txt):

- **[[crystalmancy|Crystalmancy]]** â€” the living, sung, harmonic manipulation of crystal to capture, regulate, and transform transparent Sourceshine into stable [[eldritch|Eldritch]]. Provides the **power source and the regulatory mind**.
- **[[magitek|Magitek]]** â€” the engineering application of regulated Eldritch through machines, constructs, networks, and everyday devices. Provides the **delivery systems, automation, and daily convenience**.

Because the Arathi were the only sapient race alive for their entire history, they had millennia with no external competition or cultural borrowing â€” every breakthrough in one field fed the other in a self-reinforcing golden loop. To an Arathi citizen, there was no meaningful separation between *magic* and *technology*: a train was a living crystal organism guided by song, a communicator both a machine and a piece of attuned personal crystalmancy.

Crystalmancy provided seven major lineages of cultivated crystal â€” Celestite, Emberite, Aqualith, Verdantite, Voidglass, Solariite, Prismite (see [[crystalmancy|the lineages]]) â€” used in everything from heart-stones to personal toolkits. Magitek built on those lineages to produce the Crystal Rail Network, the Skyfleet, Whisper Bands, Echo Orbs, Servitor Constructs, Comfort Engines, war constructs, floating siege platforms, and the Crystalline Forge artifacts (see [[magitek|magitek systems]]).

### The Eldritch Conduits

Raw Sourceshine radiated from the sun, struck the planet's bedrock, reflected into stable [[eldritch|Eldritch]], and flowed through ley lines into **seven massive Eldritch Conduits** â€” magical poles that **pierced the world like axes**, with **fourteen surface end points** in total. The Conduits returned energy to the planet's core to be recycled and provided the planet-spanning power grid the Arathi built around.

### Leadership

Ruled by a powerful council of archmages and crystalmancers, centered at **[[altea|Altea]]** â€” the capital, and the heart of the entire world's magical network.

### Cities

Built atop highland plateaus directly over the Eldritch Conduits. Construction substances included **crystal**, **flame-veined marble**, **[[mana-metals#Orichalcum|orichalcum]]**, and **[[mana-metals#Magisterium|magisterium]]** â€” see [[mana-metals]] for the full mana-metal system (source: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt).

A surviving illuminated cartographic record from the late Golden Age depicts the world of Eadras roughly a generation before the Cataclysm, with Altea at the center as *"Heart of the Conduits"* and named Termini and regions arrayed across the surface â€” see [[arathi-golden-age-map]]. Eleven Termini are named on the map: **Northwatch, Frosthold, Skyreach, Sunspire, Dawnward, Emergate, Starfall, Southwatch, Stoneward, Westwatch, Stormpear**. Major regions include the Haelings and Cloudhelm Mountains, the Veridian Wilds and Evergreen Vale, the Eldrim Forest, the Golden Savannas and Aurelian Plains, the Sea of Glass, Sea of Mirrors, and Sea of Origins, the Kharosian Waste, the Crystal Wastes, the Obsidian Highlands, the Thalorian Range, and the Sea of Giants.

A typical Arathi cityscape, observable in the embedded reference image's background, included (source: arathi image.txt â€” *"the background is a typical [Arathi] cityscape"*):

- **Towering crystal spires** of staggering height, often capped by crystalline lattice-work and embedded mana-gems
- **Glowing arcane circuitry** traced along the structures themselves â€” cyan and electric-blue lines of active spellwork visible on the surface of every major building, reading as both ornament and live magical infrastructure
- **Rainbow bridges of solidified light** spanning between spires and between floating platforms â€” pedestrian and transit-grade light-bridges held in place by the underlying conduit pressure
- **Floating crystal platforms** suspended at multiple altitudes, often used as workspaces, observation decks, ritual surfaces, and civic plazas
- **Bioluminescent vegetation** â€” lush gardens of glowing alien plants, crystal-flowering shrubs, and flora that absorbed ambient Eldritch through their leaves
- **Volumetric Eldritch lighting** â€” visible god-rays piercing through atmospheric layers, especially at twilight, where the conduit's stored magic scattered into the air

Every working surface, every transit path, and every architectural element doubled as living magical infrastructure. Walking through an Arathi city was walking through an active, mind-regulated machine.

### Transit Network

Every Arathi city was connected to every other by an integrated transit network: **teleportation circles**, **magitek underground trains**, **airships**, and **portals** (source: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt). [[altea|Altea]] was the nexus of this network â€” its destruction severed the entire system in a single instant, which is part of why the Cataclysm collapsed Arathi civilization planet-wide rather than merely killing the capital.

### World Scale

Before the Cataclysm, the Arathi civilization was world-spanning: continental uplands and the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]] in particular, jeweled canals, continent-spanning causeways, floating observatories, reef-cities, and ley-vaults rooted into the planetary crust. The capital, **[[altea|Altea]]**, sat at the center of the Altean Highlands on a TenochtitlÃ¡n-like inland lake, in three concentric rings cut by canals. (source: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt)

### Civilizational stage

Under the [[modernity|modernity framework]], the Arathi at peak operated **fully at Stage 5 â€” conduit-centered hypercivilization**: continent-scale magical systems, civilization-scale coordination, true globalized infrastructure, advanced magical administration. They are **the canonical Stage 5 reference** for modern Eadras â€” the **lost ceiling** against which every later civilization measures itself. No modern polity has recovered Stage 5; only fragments survive (the unbroken [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]], the [[deep-vaults|Deep Vaults]], a handful of artifact-grade survivals). Modern Stage-4-approaching cities like [[bold|Bold]] are working in the **ruins of something more whole** â€” by setting law, the peak of Eadras civilization is behind, not ahead.

## The conduit-city dislocation

A consequence of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] worth surfacing explicitly: the [[seven-conduits|conduits]] are pinned in absolute space relative to the planet's core, and **the crust slid above them** in the post-impact reshaping. The Arathi had built their greatest cities directly on the conduit termini, and those cities' surface locations physically walked away from the conduits after the Arathi were already dead. The conduit termini we know today are where the conduits surfaced after the slide, **not** where the Arathi cities stood.

The figurative weight of this is heavier than the literal: the Arathi spent ten million years building **the wrong map on top of the right power**. They were not wrong about where the power was. They were wrong about whether the surface they built on would stay loyal to it. Modern conduit cities (Bold, the Verdant Scar, and the rest of the fourteen termini) sit on the post-slide positions; the lost cities sit on dead ground somewhere underneath the modern continents, no longer aligned with anything magical at all. (source: Arathi_01_InWorld_Facts.txt)

## Inherited trauma

The Arathi themselves carried no scar â€” they never survived long enough to develop one. The catastrophe was instant. The slow grinding horror of continental drift, ocean formation, and crustal reshaping all happened to a world without Arathi in it. The first-day death meant **no Arathi diary, no last words, no surviving testimony, no warning the precursor culture left for those who would come after**. The Arathi died inside their own moment of perfect certainty. The warning is the absence itself.

The trauma was inherited by every civilization that came after. Modern Eadras is, in a real sense, a setting of second-generation survivors carrying someone else's pain. Every later culture is, somewhere beneath its surface, organized around the question *"how do we avoid becoming Arathi?"* â€” and around the contradictory drive that follows: an unconscious cultural ambition to recover Arathi-tier capacity while also unconsciously trying to prove the recovering civilization is not Arathi.

The unconscious goal is **exoneration through achievement**. If a later civilization can hold Arathi-tier power without falling, it will have demonstrated that the problem was Arathi character, not the power itself. The danger is that the conditions for achievement and the conditions for Arathi-style fall are the same conditions â€” modern civilizations that climb toward the lost ceiling are necessarily the most exposed to repeating the catastrophe they are unconsciously trying to disprove. (source: Arathi_01_InWorld_Facts.txt; Arathi_02_MetaCommentary.txt)

## Remnants

**Absolute rule**: *no remnants of [[altea|Altea]] whatsoever survive*. The Worldbreaker struck the capital directly, and impact plus subsequent Large Igneous Province extrusion erased every vault, archive, and ley-spire. Remnants of *other* Arathi cities are rare-but-existent. Anything below applies to non-Altean ruins. (source: Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt)

### Deep Vaults

The current canonical inventory is **twelve known [[deep-vaults|Deep Vaults]] on Eadras**: five opened, seven sealed. **One additional vault sits on the red moon, [[daj|Daj]]**, off Eadras and counted separately.

- **5 opened**: 1 by Darkhoof (the unicorn); 3 by gnomes; 1 undersea (opener not specified).
- **7 sealed**: 6 underwater; 1 on dry land â€” the **[[obsidian-sanctum|Obsidian Sanctum]]** in the Scorched Waste of [[varkhul|Varkhul]].

(source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt) See [[deep-vaults]] for the full inventory and discussion.

None of the vaults are Altean â€” the Worldbreaker erased everything at the impact site. Vault contents (where opened) and presumed contents (where sealed) include pristine magitek, stored Eldritch crystals, and lost knowledge.

### Crystal Conduit Ruins
The seven Eldritch Conduits were shattered. Only fragments â€” called **Conduit Ends** â€” remain, jutting from mountainsides or sea floors. They leak dangerous amounts of raw Eldritch, creating wild magic zones.

### Floating Crystal Shards
A handful of city districts and crystal towers survived the Cataclysm and still drift in the sky. Some have crashed into land or ocean.

### Major Artifacts
- **Orichalcum Ring of Command** â€” Summons and commands up to five thousand orichalcum golems from anywhere on the Prime Material Plane.
- **Rod of Transmutation** â€” Crystal-powered artifact that transmutes one substance into another, with no known limits.
- **Crystalline Forge** â€” Appears as a necklace with a crystal pendant. Thrown to the ground with a command word, it expands into a fully functional magitech factory. No known limits on size or complexity.

## Engineered Servitors

The Arathi engineered **dozens of servant lineages** â€” the [[arathi-servitors|Arathi Servitors]] â€” across their long tenure. Of all of them, **only [[trolls|Trolls]] survived** the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]], because trolls were living mineral constructs whose hibernating elders endured the impact in a way no organic body could. Every other Servitor lineage was organic or semi-organic and **died out completely** when the world broke.

Two lineages are named in canon. The named extinct lineage is the **Household Servitors** â€” engineered from insectoid stock with mantis-like limbs, beetle carapaces, and iridescent wings; they handled domestic duties, personal assistance, laboratory work, and fine craftsmanship across Arathi civilization. Their absence is one of the more visceral measures of what the Cataclysm erased: at the height of the civilization, Household Servitors were everywhere; today, none survive. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

For the full Servitor system â€” Trolls, Household Servitors, the dozens of other extinct lineages, and the relationship to surviving golem-craft â€” see [[arathi-servitors]] and [[trolls]]. For golem-craft preserved through later gnome traditions, see [[golems]].

## Fall

The Arathi civilization was annihilated in the [[arathi-cataclysm]] when the archmage **[[kudur-etir|Kudur-eá¹­ir]]** ascended via the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]] on God's Day, was driven insane by Dao's curse, and his uncontrolled deific reach pulled the **[[worldender|Worldbreaker]]** â€” a ten-mile alien Falling Star â€” through the First Breach he had drilled, onto [[altea|Altea]] directly. The impact shattered the crust, ignited plate tectonics, sundered the [[altean-highlands|Altean Highlands]] into three continents, triggered the White Deluge, ignited planet-wide firestorms, and produced a year-plus permanent rainstorm under oven-temperature debris re-entry. Approximately 60% of all life on Eadras died. The world changed from 70% land to 70% water in the process. **[[arathyn]]**, formed by 2-8 million years of Large Igneous Province extrusion at the impact center, occupies the place where Altea once stood.

<summary>Author's craft notes (OOC)</summary>

The load-bearing craft choices behind the Arathi, preserved from the 2026-05-09 expert-council analysis:

- **Inverted trauma structure.** Most fantasy precursor civilizations are remembered through their own surviving lamentation â€” ruins inscribed with elegies, last-survivor diaries, prophetic warnings. The Arathi invert this completely: they left **no scar tissue** because they had **no time to develop any**. They died inside the moment of realization. The warning is the absence itself.
- **Post-scarcity as the perfect trauma setup.** A civilization with nothing to be traumatized by, when finally traumatized, has nothing it is equipped to face. Their entire worldview assumed mastery, order, and abundance were permanent. The Worldbreaker did not just kill them â€” it proved their worldview was fragile.
- **Inheritance, not creation.** The Arathi inherited the conduits rather than building them. This deepens their guilt: their fall is the story of taking something eternal that was given to them and breaking the world on top of it. Same pattern as the True Language being passed down through corrupted tiers â€” in Eadras, **nothing fundamental is invented from scratch**; everything important is received, often in degraded form, from something older.
- **Scarring the eternal.** The conduits cannot be destroyed. And the Arathi still managed to scar them. Eadras takes seriously a category of *things that cannot be destroyed but can be scarred* â€” the conduits, the language hierarchy, the cosmology of naming. Mortal arrogance can leave permanent marks on the eternal.
- **The ground walking away.** The image of the crust sliding while the conduits stayed fixed produces one of the setting's strongest figures: the Arathi spent ten million years building **the wrong map on top of the right power**. The map and the territory came apart. This kind of tragedy structure is so deeply ironic that most fantasy avoids it.
- **Complacency about permanence is the deepest sin.** The Arathi had ten million years of stability â€” every reasonable basis for treating their world as permanent â€” and they were still wrong. The proverb *"proud as Arath before steam"* domesticates this into everyday warning.
- **The precursors left no diary.** The Arathi cannot be solved. Stories engaging with them must work in incomplete recovery, never revelation. They are **an absence with a shape**, not a mystery with an answer. Modern Eadras has access only to fragments: conduit endpoints, crystal-mind shards, a proverb, the Tome's silence on certain questions. Everything else is silence.
