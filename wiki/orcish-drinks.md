# Orcish Drinks — Saddle Brew and Stonefire

**Summary**: The [[orcs|Orcs]] make and drink **two distinct alcohols** that match the two halves of their nomadic-and-settled life: **Saddle Brew**, a low-alcohol fermented drink brewed communally in saddle-bags while clans are on the move, that primarily serves to make sketchy plains water safe to drink; and **Stonefire**, a high-proof distilled spirit produced only in clan holds and cities, used both for celebration and as a wound-and-instrument sterilizer.

**Sources**: Extra large grok chat 5-9-2026.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-09

## The two-drinks structure

[[orcs|Orc]] life splits between two modes that need different things from alcohol:

- **On the move**: The clans travel the plains for long stretches, and the water available along the way is often not safe to drink straight. They need a beverage they can make under saddle-and-bag conditions, that protects them from waterborne illness, and that they can drink daily without any of them becoming useless to ride.
- **In clan holds and cities**: When the clans settle for an extended stay, they have access to stable, equipment-heavy facilities and can do real distillation. Then they want a strong drink for celebration, ritual, and practical sterilizing purposes that they cannot manufacture on the move.

This produces the two-drink tradition: Saddle Brew as the on-the-move daily, and Stonefire as the at-home strong.

## Saddle Brew

### How it's made

Each rider on a long march carries a **leather brewing bag** on the side of their horse. Into the bag they put **ground grain seed-heads, wild honey or other natural sweetener, and water** in roughly the proportions a generations-old recipe specifies. As the clan rides, the constant motion of the horse keeps the contents sloshing, agitating, and aerating, which kicks off and sustains fermentation faster than a still vessel would.

A full ferment takes the order of a week — far longer than a single day. The riders therefore carry a brewing bag that is *days into its current ferment*, not a fresh start every morning. When the clan camps for the night, ideally near a stream, they **toss the bag into the cool stream water** to slow the ferment and bring the temperature down; in the morning, they pull it out and ride on.

### The communal pool

Saddle Brew is **communal**. When a batch is ready, every rider's contribution is poured into a larger common batch, and everybody draws their daily ration from that pool. Different individuals' batches will be at slightly different stages and slightly different qualities; pooling smooths out the variance and ensures that no rider is stuck with a particularly bad batch while others have good ones. The clans then **rotate through batches** — the current pooled batch is the day's drinking supply, while individual riders' new bags continue their ferment for the next pool.

This makes Saddle Brew a daily ritual of the clan's mutual interdependence, not just a beverage. A rider who refused to contribute their bag to the pool would be making a strong social statement.

### Why they drink it daily

The alcohol content of Saddle Brew is low — comparable to a weak beer — and is incidental to the actual reason it exists. The point of Saddle Brew is that **the fermentation makes plains water safe to drink**. Untreated plains water from streams and pools across the orcish range frequently produces severe digestive trouble (called *the squirts* in the riders' own blunt phrasing); fermented Saddle Brew does not.

In other words, Saddle Brew is the orcish equivalent of weak beer as drinking water — a bacterial-elimination drink that happens to be mildly alcoholic, not a recreational drink that happens to keep you healthy. It is consumed daily, by everyone who is old enough, in modest quantities throughout the riding day.

### Variation in flavor

Different recipes across different clans use different sweeteners (wild honey is most common; some clans use fermented fruit additions; some mix in tree-saps from the regions they regularly cross). The flavor consequence is that Saddle Brew from a southern plains clan tastes recognizably different from Saddle Brew from a northern clan, even when both follow the same basic structure.

## Stonefire

### How it's made

Stonefire is produced by **proper distillation** in clan holds and cities, beginning from a stronger fermented mash than Saddle Brew (a winter-stock fermented in stable conditions, not under saddle). The distillation requires equipment that is impractical to move with a clan on the march, which is why Stonefire is a **settled-life drink**, not a road drink.

The result is a **strong, high-proof spirit** with a clean, fierce profile. Compared to Saddle Brew's mild, sour-earthy character, Stonefire is sharp, hot, and direct.

### Uses

Stonefire serves **three primary roles** in clan life:

1. **Celebration and ritual.** Major clan events — successful hunts, victories, funerals, marriages, the formal welcoming of guests — are marked with Stonefire. The drink's strength makes a small pour into a guest's cup a significant gesture.
2. **Wound and instrument sterilizer.** Stonefire is high-proof enough to **sterilize** wounds and surgical instruments, and clan medicine relies on it for that purpose. The same bottle that pours a celebration drink doubles as the field-medic's primary disinfectant. This is one of the canonical reasons every clan hold has a working still — without Stonefire, orcish field medicine becomes substantially more dangerous.
3. **[[aqua-vitae|Aqua Vitae]] base.** Stonefire is the source spirit for orcish [[aqua-vitae|Aqua Vitae]] — the further-refined alchemical-base spirit. Orcish Aqua Vitae's specific alchemical character is open canon, but the dual sterilizer-and-celebration role of the source spirit suggests an alchemical temperament leaning toward purification, sealing, and pain-numbing.

### Storage

Stonefire is stored in **clay bottles** rather than wooden casks (the clays of the orcish range are excellent vessels, and the orcs have not historically had a tree-cooper culture comparable to the [[orden|Orden]]'s or the [[elfen|Elfen]]'s). Older bottles develop a distinctive surface character that connoisseurs read as a quality marker.

## How the two drinks interact

A long-traveled clan returning to its hold will typically perform the **arrival ritual** of the eldest cup-keeper opening a fresh bottle of Stonefire and pouring small measures for everyone in the welcoming line. The Saddle Brew that survived the trip is poured out and the bags retired for cleaning; from the next day, the riders are on Stonefire-and-fresh-water (the hold's water is treated by other means and is safe).

When the clan rides out again, the arrival ritual is reversed: a **departure cup** of Stonefire passed around, the brewing bags packed fresh, and the ride begins.

## Naming

Both drink names are working-canonical Common-tongue translations. The orcs do not yet have a developed orcish-language name for either drink in the wiki. (Open canon: the orcs' own internal names.)

## Open canon

- The orcish-language names for both drinks.
- Whether all orcish clans use the same Saddle Brew base recipe or whether there are major regional structural variants (versus the canonical minor flavor variants).
- Whether **older clans** preserve a third drink — a ritual drink for high holy days, distinct from both Saddle Brew and Stonefire — that has not yet been documented.
- The role of orcish [[aqua-vitae|Aqua Vitae]] specifically, beyond the implied purification-sealing-pain-numbing temperament.
