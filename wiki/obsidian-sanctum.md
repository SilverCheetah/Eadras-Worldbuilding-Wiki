# Obsidian Sanctum

**Summary**: The Obsidian Sanctum is an ancient [[arathi|Arathi]] [[deep-vaults|Deep Vault]] sealed deep within the Scorched Waste of [[varkhul|Varkhul]] — the **only sealed Deep Vault on dry land** anywhere on Eadras. No formal guardians watch it. Its inaccessibility is environmental: the Scorched Waste, the Turkorcs, the dragonkin, and the [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] together form a four-layered impossibility around it.

**Sources**: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-05

## Identity

The Obsidian Sanctum is one of the **twelve known [[deep-vaults|Arathi Deep Vaults]]** on Eadras. Within that inventory it occupies a specific and unique position:

- It is one of the **seven sealed** vaults (the other five having been opened, see [[deep-vaults]]).
- Of those seven, **six are underwater** and **only one is on dry land** — and that one is the Obsidian Sanctum.

This makes the Obsidian Sanctum the only Deep Vault that any non-aquatic recovery effort could in principle attempt to reach by ordinary travel — and yet it is, in practice, the most thoroughly cut off of all the sealed vaults.

## Location

The Sanctum lies **deep within the Scorched Waste** at the interior of [[varkhul|Varkhul]]. The Scorched Waste itself comprises roughly a quarter of Varkhul's surface and is, by canon, lethal to traverse without preparation.

## The four-layered impossibility

There are no formal guardians. The Sanctum is not protected by an order, a cult, a guard rotation, or watchful enchantments. Its security is entirely environmental and political, in four overlapping layers:

1. **The [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]]** — twenty-five miles offshore around Varkhul; the strongest magical barrier on Eadras; one-way reflective. Crossing it inbound requires the cooperation of the rotating casters of nearly every major nation.
2. **The Scorched Waste** — the Sanctum's home environment, lethal to ordinary travel.
3. **Roaming [[turkorcs|Turkorcs]]** — wholly amoral, individually dangerous warriors and casters who patrol no perimeter and stand for no master, but will engage anything in their range.
4. **Territorial [[dragonkin|dragonkin]]** — Mendelian-bred humanoid descendants of dragons whose innate territorial hostility makes Varkhul's interior actively contested ground.

Any of these alone would be a serious obstacle. All four together explain why the Sanctum's contents have never been recovered.

## Contents

Like the rest of the [[deep-vaults|Deep Vaults]], the Obsidian Sanctum is believed to contain pristine [[magitek]], stored Eldritch crystals, and lost knowledge — preserved across millions of years against the Cataclysm and everything that came after. Specifics are open canon.

## Open canon

- **Function in Arathi times** — what the Sanctum was for before the Cataclysm.
- **Specific contents** — undocumented.
- **Why it sits where it does** — whether the Scorched Waste post-dates the Sanctum's siting (and the Sanctum was placed in safe wilderness later corrupted), or whether the Arathi already understood the region as hostile.
- **Whether the four-layered impossibility was foreseen** — i.e., whether Arathi-era planners chose this site partly because of dragon territoriality and harsh terrain that would later be reinforced by the Ward and the Turkorcs.
- **Any opening attempts** in recorded history.
