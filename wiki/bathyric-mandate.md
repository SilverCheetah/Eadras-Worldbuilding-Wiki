# The Bathyric Mandate

**Summary**: The Bathyric Mandate is a magical bacterial hive mind, created as a side effect of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]], that infests living hosts, migrates to the brain, seizes control by telepathy, and converts captured biomass into neural matter for the collective while using the emptied host bodies as labor. It is entirely underwater. Undersea civilizations have eradicated it **five times** — and five times it has regenerated, because a single surviving bacterium is enough, given time, to grow the whole mind back.

## Origin

The Bathyric Mandate was not designed by anyone. It is an **accidental product of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]** — a magical mutation of ordinary bacteria into a single distributed intelligence, one of the stranger pieces of wreckage left in the deep oceans by the world-breaking event.

## Nature and method

The Mandate is a **hive mind** with no central body, expressed across a colony of magical bacteria. Its method is parasitic and patient:

1. It **infests a host** — any sufficiently large living creature.
2. The colony **migrates to the brain**.
3. It **takes over the host by telepathy**, overriding the host's will.
4. It **converts biomass into neural matter** for the collective — growing its own thinking-mass out of the captured body — while keeping the host's frame functional and **using it as labor**.

The horror of the Mandate is that it does not simply kill. It *recruits* — turning each victim into both additional mind and additional hands, the body kept walking (or swimming) in service of the thing now wearing it.

The Mandate is **entirely underwater.** It has never established itself on land, and its threat is a matter for the [[merrow|undersea peoples]], not the surface world.

## Eradication and regeneration

Undersea civilizations have **completely eradicated the Bathyric Mandate five times.** It has returned every time. The reason is built into what it is: the whole hive mind can **regenerate from a single surviving bacterium**, given enough time. Extermination that misses even one cell only resets the clock. This makes the Mandate less a war that can be won than a **recurring tide** the deep civilizations have learned to beat back, knowing it will rise again.
