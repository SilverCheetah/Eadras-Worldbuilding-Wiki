# Velena

**Summary**: Velena was a [[velrathi|Velrathi]] woman, elder sister of [[xarvion|Xar'vion]], strong-willed and fiercely protective. She was raped and murdered by a gang of arrogant [[tarans|Tarran]] noble youths during the degenerate decline of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]. Her death became the central tragedy that drove Xar'vion's entire life — and the desire he forgot the moment he ascended to the godhood he sought to restore her. She currently resides in the Verdant Fields of [[solarion|Solarion]].

**Sources**: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt; Playground generation 2nd part.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## Overview

Velena was the elder sister of the young Velrathi orphan who would one day become [[xarvion|Xar'vion]]. In the brutal final days of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]], with both children abandoned to a decadent and dangerous capital, she was strong-willed, fiercely protective, and served as her sickly younger brother's only shield and family. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

She was raped and murdered by a gang of arrogant Tarran noble youths during the empire's degenerate decline. The Firethorn Empire was sliding into corruption, debauchery, and ennui in its final centuries; noble youths abused their power with impunity, and the Tarran martial culture of Order and Duty had hollowed into entitlement. Velena was one of many casualties of that decay — but it is her death, more than any other, that gave shape to the centuries of catastrophe that followed.

## Her role in Xar'vion's history

The night of Velena's death broke something in Xar'vion permanently. He swore two vows: **he would never be powerless again, and he would find a way to bring her back.** (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

Centuries of ruthless political, magical, and military maneuvering were all in service to one goal — claiming the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] on God's Day so he could ascend to Unrestricted Elder Godhood and resurrect Velena. He did not create the Spear (it was forged long before by [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]]). He simply positioned himself to wield its ultimate power.

This desire pressed against the [[omniversal-laws|Fifth Omniversal Law]]: *Death is sovereign in the end.* He wanted not communion with her shade, not animation of her body, not a memory-echo bound to a relic — true reversal of the finality of death after Death's sovereignty had fully settled.

## After Dao's curse

When Xar'vion finally claimed the Spear and ascended, [[dao|Dao]]'s curse stripped the love-driven motive that had defined him. He still remembered Velena. He still remembered wanting to resurrect her. He may have remembered her name, her face, her death, and every step he took toward the Spear.

But the need was gone.

She became only a fact. He could say *"I did this for my sister"* — but it was only historically true, no longer spiritually true. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

Two postwar laments preserve the tragedy:

*He climbed to godhood carrying her name.*
*At the summit, the name had weight but no warmth.*

*Xar'vion broke the world for Velena,*
*then forgot her before the first crown fell.*

## Velena now

Velena currently resides in the **Verdant Fields of [[solarion|Solarion]]** — a peaceful pastoral afterlife of gentle golden light and quiet happiness. She is content there. **She does not (yet) wish to return to the living world.** Like all souls in the Mythos's natural cycle, she knows she will eventually reincarnate when the time is right. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

This is one of the harshest ironies of Xar'vion's tragedy. The sister he broke the world to retrieve was, by the time he had the power to retrieve her, already at peace and uninterested in being retrieved.

## The Sister's Sepulcher

Xar'vion built the **Sister's Sepulcher** in his pre-ascension capital Xanareth as a public monument to her memory. Before his ascension, people viewed it sympathetically — a grieving ruler honoring a beloved sibling.

After Dao's curse, Xar'vion stopped visiting. Ceremonies continued by inertia. Priests noticed. [[black-hand|Black Hand]] inner members noticed. That absence may have been one of the first signs that something fundamental in him had been amputated. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Her remains and modern interest

Her body or remains were likely preserved with extraordinary care. In the modern era, they may be targets for:

- [[black-hand|Black Hand]] remnants
- Resurrection Heretics who believe she is the key to restoring Xar'vion's original motive
- Anti-Xar'vion wardens who seek to control access
- Scholars of Dao's curse
- Demon-corrupted cults seeking symbolic leverage

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Theological significance

Velena is a symbol of grief exploited by power. She was the reason. Then she became only a fact. Her memory survives in postwar culture as a reminder of how far the tragedy of the Spear extends: Xar'vion broke the world to save her, and by the time he had the power to do so, he no longer cared.

A Resurrection Heretic sect believes that if Velena is truly resurrected, Xar'vion's severed motive could return — a near-impossible hope that drives dangerous cult activity. The inner [[black-hand|Black Hand]] considers this view naive and has reinterpreted Dao's curse as purification. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)
