# Worldbreaker Mechanism

**Summary**: The detailed geophysical mechanism by which the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]]'s impact at [[altea|Altea]] produced the planet-scale restructuring of the [[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]]: a multi-stage cascade in which kinetic shock destabilized crust and mantle, fractured high-pressure subsurface water reservoirs that flash-vaporized into superheated steam, decoupled the crust from the mantle, allowed the entire crust to slide as a semi-fluid layer over the planetary interior, and ignited runaway tectonics — while the rotational axis, magnetic poles, and core-anchored [[seven-conduits|conduits]] all remained fixed.

**Sources**: Large page of notes 5-4-2026.txt; Refinement — biological survival.txt; Addition — catastrophe sequence.txt; user direction 2026-05-04. Companion page to [[arathi-cataclysm]], which carries the historical, narrative, and political treatment of the same event.

## Why a Separate Page

The [[arathi-cataclysm]] page treats the Cataclysm as a *historical event* — Kudur-eṭir's role, the destruction of Arathi civilization, the political and religious consequences, the full canon of names by which the event is remembered.

This page treats the same event as a *geophysical mechanism*. It is the deep-mechanism reference for how the impact at [[altea|Altea]] produced a planet-restructuring outcome rather than a localized one, and is the canonical source for crustal displacement, polar stability, the steam-flash cascade, and the surface-vs-conduit drift relationship that explains why [[oldest-continuous-settlements|oldest-continuous-settlement sites]] re-cluster on the same fourteen [[seven-conduits|conduit termini]] across every era.

All conclusions on this page are **post-hoc reconstructions**. No sapient observer survived the Cataclysm with the partial exception of [[trolls|trolls in deep hibernation]]; everything below is inferred from later geological, magical, and stellar evidence. (See [[#Knowledge Provenance]].)

## The Cascade

The Worldbreaker did not cause the Cataclysm in a single mechanical event. It triggered a **multi-stage cascade** in which several distinct physical processes interacted, each amplifying the others.

```
Impact (Worldbreaker → Altea)
   ↓
Kinetic shock + extreme heat propagated through crust and upper mantle
   ↓
Subsurface high-pressure water reservoirs fractured and exposed
   ↓
Reservoirs flash to steam — pressure release + thermal expansion
   ↓
Crust temporarily decoupled from mantle (becomes semi-fluid relative to deep interior)
   ↓
Crust slides over mantle ("butter on a hot skillet")
   ↓
Runaway tectonics initiated — entire surface in horizontal motion
   ↓
Multi-year surface chaos: oven-temperatures, windstorms, deluge
   ↓
Gradual stabilization at modern tectonic rates
   ↓
New world (modern continental geography over millions of years)
```

### Stage 1 — Impact and Kinetic Shock

The Worldbreaker — a ten-mile object foreign to the Mythos, pulled through [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir's]] [[arathi-cataclysm#Kudur-eṭir's Goal, the First Breach, and the Spear|First Breach]] — struck [[altea|Altea]] directly. The impact:

- Annihilated Altea (no remnants survive)
- Drove the Worldbreaker through the crust into the upper mantle
- Propagated extreme kinetic energy through both crust and mantle in concentric shock waves
- Released sufficient heat to vaporize bedrock at the impact point and superheat surrounding rock to plastic states

Conventional impact mechanics on a tectonically still planet would have produced a major crater, a regional firestorm, an ejecta blanket, and a centuries-long climate disturbance. None of those alone would have restructured the surface of the planet. The Cataclysm-scale outcome required the **next stage**.

(Clarification 2026-05-09: pre-Cataclysm Eadras was tectonically still — fixed continental layout, no plate motion — but **not geologically dead**. The planet had constant low-level earthquake activity and an active subterranean hydrological cycle, with massive underground oceanic reservoirs exchanging water with the surface. The reservoirs that flash-vaporized in Stage 2 were not anomalous geological structures; they were a normal part of pre-Cataclysm Eadras's hydrology, and the impact's catastrophic effect on them required the kinetic-and-thermal scale only the Worldbreaker could deliver.)

### Stage 2 — Subsurface Reservoir Exposure

Pre-Cataclysm Eadras carried **massive subsurface reservoirs of liquid water** held at extreme pressure within and below the crust. These reservoirs were a normal feature of the planet's hydrology — the source for [[arathi-cataclysm#Physical Consequences|the White Deluge]] when they later vented at the surface — and were unremarkable so long as their containment held.

The impact's shock waves and crust-fracturing propagation **breached the containment** across a wide region around the impact site. Once breached, the reservoirs were exposed to the lower-pressure conditions of newly-fractured crust.

### Stage 3 — Steam Flash and Pressure Release

When the breached reservoirs depressurized, they did not simply leak. They **flash-vaporized** — the liquid water suddenly converting to superheated steam in massive volumes. Two effects compounded:

- **Pressure release**: vast volumes of high-pressure water suddenly available as low-pressure gas, expanding violently outward through the fractured crust
- **Thermal expansion**: the heat of the vaporization (and the residual heat of the impact) transferred into the surrounding rock and crust, raising temperatures further

The combined pressure-and-thermal release was a far larger energy event than the impact's kinetic shock alone. The planet's outer layers — crust and uppermost mantle — were now subjected to forces capable of restructuring them mechanically.

### Stage 4 — Crustal Decoupling

Under the combined pressure release, thermal expansion, and ongoing kinetic shock, the **crust temporarily decoupled from the mantle**. The interface between crust and mantle, normally rigid on geological timescales, briefly behaved as a **lubricating semi-fluid layer**.

The standard analogy used by post-Cataclysm scholars: **butter sliding across a hot skillet**. The crust did not melt. It simply lost its mechanical grip on the underlying mantle and became free to move horizontally as a coherent layer.

This is the **load-bearing event** of the Cataclysm. Everything that follows — the continental displacement, the crustal slide, the rapid plate motion, the climate inversions — depends on the moment of decoupling.

### Stage 5 — Crustal Displacement (Slide)

Once decoupled, the crust **slid horizontally over the mantle** as a single layer, driven by the residual kinetic energy of the impact and the asymmetric pressure release of the steam-flash region. This is the **catastrophic crustal displacement** that defines the Cataclysm geophysically.

The slide moved the entire surface of the planet — every continent, every ocean basin, every settlement — relative to the deep interior. Surface locations that had been at one latitude were transported to a different latitude over a geologically instantaneous timescale.

The crust did not slide indefinitely. As the energy of the steam-flash dissipated and the crust-mantle interface re-rigidified, the slide slowed and eventually halted, leaving the crust in a new orientation relative to the mantle.

### Stage 6 — Runaway Tectonics

The decoupling did not fully reverse. Even after the initial slide stopped, the crust-mantle interface remained more mechanically active than before, **igniting plate tectonics on a planet that had previously been tectonically still** (fixed continental layout, no plate motion — though it had carried ordinary earthquake activity and a substantial subterranean hydrological cycle throughout the pre-Cataclysm era; see [[#Stage 1 — Impact and Kinetic Shock]] above).

Plates that had not existed as independent units now existed. They moved at extreme velocities for the first millions of years post-Cataclysm — far faster than modern tectonic rates — gradually slowing as the system damped. By the modern era, plate motion has reached the slower steady-state rates that produced the [[eadras-tectonic-history|four-snapshot tectonic history]].

The post-Cataclysm continental geography is the product of this runaway tectonic phase, not of the original Cataclysm impact alone.

## Polar Stability

A critical and counterintuitive feature of the Cataclysm: **the rotational axis and magnetic poles remained fixed**. Only the crust moved.

The crust slid *relative to* the rotational axis, which means surface locations changed their latitude — but the axis itself, as a property of the planet's angular momentum and deep mass distribution, was unchanged. Similarly, the planet's magnetic field, generated in the deep interior, retained the same orientation.

Evidence supporting polar stability includes:

- **Stellar reference patterns** — alignment of ancient crystalline systems and surviving observatories with present-day stellar positions, consistent across the post-Cataclysm period
- **Magnetic signatures** — preserved magnetic alignment in arcane metals and geological formations laid down before, during, and after the Cataclysm shows continuous polar orientation
- **Fossil and sediment distribution inconsistencies** — fossil and sedimentary records of pre-Cataclysm life show climates that do not match their current latitudes, but in a pattern consistent with surface displacement rather than axis rotation

The polar-stability conclusion is one of the deepest pieces of post-hoc Cataclysm reconstruction; it is not directly observed but is inferred from these convergent lines of evidence. (See [[#Knowledge Provenance]].)

## Climatic Displacement

Because the crust shifted under stable poles, **regions changed their latitude essentially overnight in geological terms**. Climate zones that were stable for the pre-Cataclysm world became wildly mismatched with their new positions:

- Equatorial regions transported to high latitudes — sudden polar climate
- Polar regions transported to lower latitudes — sudden warming
- Temperate zones flung to extreme positions in either direction
- Ecosystems adapted for one climate dropped into a fundamentally incompatible one

This climate displacement contributed massively to the post-impact biological collapse. Even ecosystems that survived the immediate impact, firestorms, deluge, and atmospheric disruption then faced sudden climate change of a magnitude no organism could adapt to. Most could not survive. (See [[#Biological Survival]] below.)

The displaced settlements of the [[arathi|Arathi]] suffered the same problem, in a different way. Cities built for specific climates were abruptly transported into incompatible environments — desert outposts now in tundra, polar observatories now in temperate forest, lowland river deltas now elevated by tectonic upwelling. Cities that survived the impact and deluge often failed in the climate-incompatibility phase that followed. This is part of why some surviving non-Altean Arathi ruins are found today in locations that appear geographically or climatically inconsistent with their original design.

## The Conduit Stability Anomaly

The [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]] are the great exception to the Cataclysm's surface chaos.

The conduits are not physical structures. They are eternal magical axes anchored at the cosmological level to the planetary core (see [[seven-conduits#They Are Magical, Not Physical]]). They were therefore **not subject to crustal displacement**. The crust shifted; the conduit endpoints did not. The conduits remained anchored where they had always been.

The consequence: in the immediate post-Cataclysm period, **surface geography and conduit endpoints were misaligned**. Pre-Cataclysm Arathi cities that had been built directly on conduit termini were no longer at those terminus points; they had been carried elsewhere by the slide. The actual conduit terminus was now somewhere different — at whatever surface point now sat above the unchanged anchor.

Over the following millions of years, as plate tectonics gradually re-stabilized and post-Cataclysm civilizations emerged, **settlement re-centered on the conduits**. New populations independently converged on the new surface points where the conduits emerged, without knowing they were re-establishing the same pattern. This is the mechanism behind [[oldest-continuous-settlements]]: every era's civilization gravitates back to the conduits, regardless of which civilization came before.

## Geological Indicators of Crustal Displacement

Modern Eadras still carries the geological signatures of the displacement. Recognized indicators include:

- **Mismatched geological strata across regions** — strata that should be continuous show abrupt offsets where the crust slid as separate sections
- **Discontinuities in mountain formation patterns** — mountain ranges that begin in one orientation and abruptly continue in another
- **Fossil and sediment distributions inconsistent with current latitude** — tropical fossils in present-day cold zones; cold-water sediments in present-day equatorial bands
- **Anomalous geological strata orientation** — bedding planes laid down horizontally in one era now tilted or rotated relative to neighbors that should match

These anomalies are recognized by **advanced scholars** — geologists, [[crystalmancy|crystalmancers]], certain Lirran arcanums, the deepest Tome of Baldaran archives — but are **not widely understood within most cultures**. The mainstream interpretation in most kingdoms remains that "the world was different before the Falling Star" without specific mechanism.

## The Catastrophe Sequence (Surface Phenomena)

Once the crustal cascade was underway, the surface experienced a multi-phase catastrophe over months to years. The phases overlapped and varied by region:

1. **Impact and immediate ejecta**. Worldbreaker strikes; vaporized rock and debris launched into atmosphere and beyond.
2. **Sustained oven-like surface temperatures**. Two compounding heat sources: ejecta re-entering the atmosphere over weeks to months, and steam vented from the breached subsurface reservoirs. Surface temperatures stayed elevated globally for an extended period.
3. **Global windstorms**. Atmospheric instability driven by the temperature differentials and pressure releases produced sustained worldwide windstorms that redistributed heat, debris, and biological material.
4. **The White Deluge / hydrological reset**. As temperatures eventually declined, atmospheric moisture (much of it the vaporized reservoir water) condensed into prolonged global rainfall lasting at least a year. Oceans formed in the new basins. Surface geography was reshaped by erosion at unprecedented scale.
5. **Persistent tectonic instability**. Quakes, volcanism, mountain-building, and rifting continued at extreme rates for the first million years and at gradually slowing rates afterward.

These surface phenomena are the manifestation of the deeper cascade described above. The historical record on [[arathi-cataclysm#Physical Consequences|the Cataclysm page]] preserves the same sequence in narrative form.

## Biological Survival

The Cataclysm did **not produce complete extinction**. Approximately **60% of biological life on Eadras was destroyed**, with the greatest losses among complex organisms and surface ecosystems. (source: Refinement — biological survival.txt)

Survival was concentrated in the most extreme resilient forms:

- **Dormant plant seeds** — particularly hard-shelled species that survived in soil
- **Subsurface organisms** — deep-rock microbiota, cave-dwelling fauna, root-zone fungi
- **Insulated and protected life** — organisms in deep caves, hot springs, high-altitude refugia, or other naturally insulated environments
- **Sea creatures and plants** — much of the marine biosphere, particularly deep-water species, survived where surface ecosystems collapsed

These survivors formed the **basis for ecological recovery** during the long post-Cataclysm reset. The 40% that survived was not a representative sample of pre-Cataclysm life; it was a heavily filtered subset, and modern Eadras's biosphere is descended from it.

Sapient survival was much narrower. Of the sapient races, **only the [[trolls|trolls in deep hibernation]] survived**. All [[arathi|Arathi]] perished, including those in supposed refuges. (See [[arathi-cataclysm#Survivors]].)

## Knowledge Provenance

Everything on this page is **post-hoc reconstruction**, not direct observation.

No sapient observer survived the Cataclysm to record what happened. The hibernating trolls were not conscious during the event. The [[ixthal|surviving ascended Arathi]] (who experienced the event from godhood) have been almost entirely silent on its mechanics, and what little is known of their accounts focuses on the Arathi people's death rather than the planetary mechanism. The [[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]] presumably observed but have not shared a comprehensive account.

The mechanism described above is therefore reconstructed by **later civilizations** through indirect evidence:

- Alignment of ancient crystalline systems with current stellar positions
- Magnetic signatures preserved in arcane metals and geological formations
- Inconsistencies between fossil distribution and present-day latitude
- Geological strata patterns showing discontinuity at the Cataclysm horizon
- Settlement-pattern data showing re-centering on conduit termini regardless of cultural continuity
- Persistent localized concentrations of stable Eldritch energy at fixed (conduit) points
- Continued operation of the [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]] from their pre-Cataclysm positions

Each piece is independently weak. Together they converge on the cascade described here. The reconstruction is the consensus of [[crystalmancy|crystalmancers]], geologists, the deeper Lirran arcanums, and the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]]'s scholar-tier entries — but it is consensus, not certainty. Some details (especially the polar-stability conclusion and the specifics of the steam-flash) remain debated in scholarly circles.

What is *not* debated is the basic shape: surface displaced, deep interior stable, conduits unmoved. That much is overdetermined by the evidence.
