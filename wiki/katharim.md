# Katharim

**Summary**: The Katharim are the **Weighted Third** of the [[rathari|Rathar]] — the gray-robed undertakers, crypt keepers, and divine-only priests who refused [[sylph|Sylph]]'s [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar|Veil of Forgetting]] and chose instead to remember the dead with unflinching precision, becoming Eadras' foremost archivists of mortality.

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt, Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt, Grok Chat 5-8-26.txt, mythic-retellings-of-the-cataclysm-and-demon-war.md (2026-05-13 ingest, Katharim mythic voice).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-13

## Origin

The Katharim are descended directly from the [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar|third of the Rathar]] who refused the Veil of Forgetting during the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. While the [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] chose to forget so they might live brightly, the Rememberers took upon themselves the sacred, crushing duty of **never letting the dead be forgotten** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

Across ten thousand years they have become the world's preeminent undertakers, crypt keepers, and guardians of the silent places. Where others turn away from graves, the Katharim walk in.

They are **not priests of death** — that domain belongs to [[shali|Shali]] and [[shani|Shani]]. They are **its archivists**. They do not judge souls. They record them.

## The Refusal of Arcane Magic

The Katharim's rejection of magic is not total. It is **surgical**, born of ten thousand years of unflinching memory.

They refuse **all arcane magic** — the raw manipulation of [[eldritch|Eldritch]] through personal will, [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]]-threads, Netharim practice, wizardry, or scholarly formula. No spellbooks, no innate Netharim practice, no ley-line tapping, no construct-binding, no transmutation of matter by mortal mind alone. They witnessed too many Rathar colleagues torn apart by their own perfect calculations during the Demon War. **Arcane hubris helped open the wound. They turned their backs on it forever** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

What they embrace instead is **divine magic channeled through priesthood**. Quiet, gray-robed servants of the Elder Gods (especially [[ioa|Ioa]], [[nara|Nara]], [[shali|Shali]] & [[shani|Shani]], and [[vos|Vos]]) act as intermediaries, never as masters. Their power flows only through prayer, ritual obedience, and historical precedent. It is not theirs. It is borrowed, recorded, and offered back in service.

### The Priestly Oath

> *"I will not bend the world with my own will. I will only remember, record, and beseech. Let the gods judge. Let the dead rest. Let the living choose."*

### Enforcement

Arcane attempts are met with **ritual ostracism**. Any Katharim who experiments with Netharim practice or Weave-work is gently but permanently exiled to live among the [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] or in remote hermitages. Their name is still recorded, but marked with the phrase *"One Who Chose Forgetting."* The community mourns them as one already dead to duty.

## Memory Circles

Katharim society is organized into **Memory Circles** — direct descendants of the pre-war Rathar **[[rathari|Thought Conclaves]]**. Each Circle includes at least one ordained priest who leads recitations, sanctifies graves, and maintains divine wards.

Priests dress identically to other Katharim — gray robes heavy with embroidered names — save for a single silver thread at the hem representing the divine chain they willingly wear, plus a small crystal vial of sanctified ash worn at the throat.

## Role in the World

Every major city, every ancient battlefield, every forgotten war-barrow eventually finds a Katharim presence. They maintain:

- The great necropoleis (above all, [[kharathen]])
- Sealed family crypts
- Memorial vaults beneath ruined Rathar halls
- Quiet roadside shrines where travelers leave names of the lost
- Sealed war-crypts in the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] (where demonic taint is still held at bay)

In mixed communities they are the ones called when a body must be prepared with dignity, when a grave must be dug to exact historical specifications, or when an entire lineage's resting place needs restoration after centuries of neglect.

### Wandering caretakers

While [[kharathen|Kharathen]] is their spiritual heart, the Katharim **do not stay there**. They tend graves and maintain memorial sites all over Eadras, and **small groups travel constantly as wandering caretakers** to reach every grave that needs them. The wandering caretakers are not pilgrims, and not exiled — they are an organized and continuous part of Katharim practice. Their explicit duty is *that no fallen soul be forgotten*, including the souls of those buried far from any necropolis. They walk circuits, visit barrows, restore weathered name-stelae, conduct overdue rites, and update local death-ledgers; they then move on. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

This is the practical mechanism behind the rule that *every major city, every ancient battlefield, every forgotten war-barrow eventually finds a Katharim presence* — much of that presence is itinerant rather than stationed.

**No one pays them in mere coin.** Payment is memory: a story, a name, a relic, a sworn oath to remember. Their ledgers — vast tomes bound in dark leather and iron — contain the death-records of kings, commoners, beast-folk heroes, and even the occasional dragon who requested honorable interment.

They also serve as **neutral arbiters** in mixed communities, precisely because everyone trusts their refusal of arcane shortcuts.

## Crypts and Architecture

Katharim architecture is unmistakable: somber, perfectly proportioned halls of gray stone and black marble, lit by **cold crystal lanterns that never flicker**. Their greatest works are the **Eternal Archives of the Thirds** — sprawling underground complexes where the Fallen Third (those who died in the Demon War) are interred in endless, perfectly ordered galleries. Each sarcophagus is carved with the deceased's full name, known deeds, and the exact date and manner of death. Walls are inscribed floor-to-ceiling with the unbroken casualty rolls.

Smaller crypts dot the landscape: humble barrow-shrines for local dead, fortified family vaults beneath cities, sealed war-crypts in the Demonfall Marches.

### Magical Defense Without Arcane Magic

Because the Katharim refuse arcane magic, their wards are purely **divine, geometric, and historical** — powered by centuries of consistent prayer and perfect stonework rather than fluctuating Eldritch. Demons and [[black-hand|Black Hand]] necromancers find Katharim vaults extraordinarily difficult to breach — not because of flashy power, but because **the very stones remember every previous violation**.

[[lirra|Lirra]]'s priesthood quietly approves: the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] around Katharim sites is unusually stable, as though refusal of personal arcane power creates a natural calm in the threads.

## Undertaking Rites

Preparation of the dead is slow, reverent, and communal within the Memory Circle:

1. The body is washed with ash-infused waters
2. Wrapped in undyed linen embroidered with its life-record
3. Laid in a stone sarcophagus or simple barrow
4. The deceased's name and deeds are recited aloud
5. Families speak **one true, unsoftened memory** — joyful or terrible — so the dead are not reduced to polite fiction

Beast-folk kin often attend uninvited but welcomed. A [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] will dance or [[flyting|flyt]] a farewell verse at the graveside, bringing color and noise to the solemn gray. The Katharim never stop them.

## Daily Life

Lifestyle is **ascetic and rhythmic**:
- Dawn Recitation
- Day's labors among the dead or in the archives
- Evening Recitation of newly recorded names
- Silent study
- Sleep in sparse alcoves within the crypt-complexes themselves — the living literally dwelling among the honored dead

Food is simple and symbolic: **Ash Tea**, **Remembrance Loaf**, clear broths, preserved roots. During major funeral rites they fast until interment is complete.

## Major Traditions

### Night of All Names

Held on the anniversary of the [[demon-war|Demon War]]'s end. Every Katharim community reads aloud the entire known list of their Fallen Third — a recitation that can last from dusk until dawn. [[beast-folk|Beast-folk]] are invited and may respond with music or [[flyting|flyting]] once the names are spoken, turning grief into shared catharsis.

### Passing of the Ledger

When an elder undertaker feels their own death approaching, they personally hand their current death-ledger to a chosen successor during a private vigil, reciting the final entries together. One of the highest honors and deepest sorrows in their culture.

### The Silent Fast

During major funeral cycles: no food or speech from dusk until the body is sealed.

### The Exchange of Burdens

A new tradition emerged in the last two thousand years. Once per generation, a young Katharim priest may spend one year living among a [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]] Circle (under strict vow of silence regarding arcane matters). They return quieter, heavier, and more certain of their calling. Beast-folk in turn sometimes send particularly troubled youths to serve briefly in the crypts, learning the weight of memory before choosing the Veil again.

## Relationship with the Laughing Third

This is the most poignant relationship in modern Eadras. The two peoples are **siblings**, both descended from the Rathar, who answered catastrophe in opposite ways.

Beast-folk often bring their dead to Katharim crypts for final interment, then hold rowdy farewell flytings at the entrance while gray-robed priests stand in silent witness. *"One dances the dead into forgetting, the other records them into eternity. Neither interrupts the other. Both are necessary."*

Many [[beast-folk|Laughing Circles]] keep a Katharim **Grave-Watcher** in permanent residence — a gray-robed figure who tends the local dead and is gently teased, deeply loved, and fiercely protected by the entire community.

## Sacred Architecture

Many crypt complexes contain small temples to [[shali|Shali]] (gentle passage) and [[shani|Shani]] (final judgment) where the twins are honored side-by-side in balanced iconography — **one of the rare places where even the quarreling goddesses maintain decorum.**

## The Katharim mythic voice — the keeping of true names

The Katharim's institutional function as the Rememberers of the Dead extends to the **mythic memory of foundational catastrophes**. Where other peoples soften painful events into didactic proverbs or grim-satisfaction lessons over generations, the Katharim hold the precise versions. The canonical [[mythic-recitations#3-4-the-katharim-version-the-breaking-of-the-three|Katharim recitation of the Demon War — *The Breaking of the Three*]] holds the three fallen races at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Final Wall]] as **"the last true children of the Three Progenitor Gods,"** names the failure that the orcs call betrayal in institutional terms (*others held back their strength*), and refuses the softening that other peoples apply over centuries. *They do not forgive, and they do not forget* is the load-bearing line of the Katharim mythic register — and the broader pattern (the Katharim mythic voice as **institutional precision, the keeping of true names**) is established at [[mythic-recitations#4-5-the-katharim-voice-institutional-precision-the-keeping-of-true-names|Mythic Recitations § 4.5]] as load-bearing canon for any future Eadran mythic-recitation material.

The Katharim are not vengeful in the sense of plotting revenge; they are vengeful in the sense of refusing to let the memory dim. Their institutional function is to *remember*, and they apply that function to every named death — including the failures that surround a death — as steadily as they apply it to honoring the dead themselves.

## The Lost Primer bitterness

The Katharim still speak with **quiet bitterness** about [[lost-primer|the Lost Primer]] — the [[arathi|Arathi]] crystalmancy and magitek primer recovered by a joint [[velrathi|Velrathi]]-[[rathari|Rathari]] expedition early in the [[firethorn-empire|High Firethorn Era]] and lost forever when demons consumed the source vault during the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. Their stock phrase for the loss is *"the knowledge we were allowed to taste, but never permitted to keep."* It is the cleanest example, in their telling, of the arcane hubris they have spent ten thousand years repudiating: a civilization that held the keys to a lost golden age, used them for centuries, and could not protect them when the wound came.

The Katharim do not, as of canonical present, know that the [[grugi|Grug]] who attended the original expedition kept meticulous notes — and that those notes were inherited by the [[gnomes]] and [[goblins]]. The Velrathi-Rathari side of the original team never thought to ask the Grug. The Katharim, who inherited the bitterness, have similarly never thought to ask the gnomes. Whether the Katharim and gnomish workshops will ever bridge that gap and cross-reference what each side preserves is open canon. (See [[lost-primer]].)
