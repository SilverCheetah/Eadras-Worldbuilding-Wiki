# The Unicorn Plains

**Summary**: The **Unicorn Plains** are the ancestral homeland of the [[unicorns|unicorns]] â€” vast grasslands shared with wild horses, antelopes, and countless other plains megafauna, where for untold millennia the unicorns lived deliberately hidden among ordinary herd life as nothing more than *unusually beautiful and magical animals*. The Plains are the site of the **Unicorn Plains War** that followed dragon discovery, and they remain dragon-restricted under the post-war norm that became draconic instinct: **Do not hunt the plains.**

**Sources**: The Great Exchange.txt; Playground generation 1st part.txt; map explanation.txt (cross-references)

**Last updated**: 2026-05-02

---

## Geography and ecology

The Unicorn Plains are **vast grasslands** of the kind that support large mobile herds. The native fauna is dominated by **wild horses, antelopes, and countless other plains megafauna**; the unicorns share the Plains with these herds and historically blended into them. (source: The Great Exchange.txt)

Specific continental placement (which post-Cataclysm continent, what climate band, neighboring landmasses, named features) is not yet documented in canon. The [[eadras-tectonic-history|tectonic snapshot series]] places unicorn emergence on the **[[eldravath|Eldravath]] supercontinent** at ~6 mybp; the Plains exist on whichever sector of Eldravath hosted that emergence and on whichever modern continental fragment that sector eventually became after the Breaking at ~5 mybp.

## The hidden millennia

For **untold millennia** before contact, the unicorns lived in the Plains in a state of deliberate concealment, **appearing to outsiders as nothing more than unusually beautiful and magical animals**. Their illusion-mastery and command of the land let them hide among the ordinary herds without drawing attention. During this period they **endured two full cycles of draconic civilization** â€” observing the dragons rise to dominance, build empires, and collapse back into barbarism, twice over. (source: The Great Exchange.txt)

Throughout these millennia the Plains functioned as a **deep-time observatory of the world** â€” the unicorns watched the rise and fall of dragonkind, and (per the lessons later shared in the [[great-exchange|Great Exchange]]) they also noticed the existence of other sapient species long before the dragons did.

## The Unicorn Plains War

During one of the periodic draconic collapses, **dragon clans discovered the Plains and the creatures that lived there**. To the dragons, the graceful horned beings were simply another species of exceptionally fine prey, and they began hunting in earnest. (source: The Great Exchange.txt)

The unicorns first attempted to **hide and flee**, using their mastery of illusion and the land to remain unseen among the ordinary herds. When it became clear the dragons would not stop and **extinction became a real threat**, the [[unicorns#The Chorus|Chorus]] â€” the unicorn collective decision-making body â€” made a **cold, calculated decision**. Knowing the dragons were at their weakest, the unicorns struck.

The **brief but brutal Unicorn Plains War** ended with the dragons being **decisively driven from the plains**. No formal treaty was signed; the dragons simply stopped hunting the Plains. The lesson passed into draconic instinct and law: **"Do not hunt the plains."**

## Post-war status

Since the Plains War, the Unicorn Plains have been a **dragon-restricted** zone â€” not by treaty, but by hard-won instinct. The territorial shift this produced opened broad **dragon-free corridors** through the world's grasslands, which later humanoid peoples (emerging tens of millions of years afterward) **exploited unknowingly**, expanding through plains that had been quietly cleared of large draconic predators. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt; cross-referenced in [[unicorns#The Unicorn-Dragon War and Peace]])

The Plains are also the silent prerequisite to the [[great-exchange|Great Exchange]] that followed millions of years later: the post-war Plains-edge borderlands were where young unicorns experimenting with individuality and young dragons living at the edges of their fractured society first met.

## The Plains today

Modern unicorn herds still inhabit and govern the Plains, and the dragon-restriction norm still holds. Outsiders find the Plains **difficult to map and difficult to cross safely** â€” partly the unicorns' historical illusion practice, partly the simple fact that mainstream draconic culture refuses to overfly them, partly the herd-magic of the modern Chorus.

The [[flesh-eaters|Flesh-Eaters]] â€” the rejectionist dragon cult â€” explicitly do not respect the dragon-restriction norm and continue to hunt the Plains opportunistically; they are one of the principal modern threats unicorn herds protect themselves against.
