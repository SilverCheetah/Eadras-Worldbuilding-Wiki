# House Sitharis

**Summary**: The wealthiest of the [[amunorians|Amunorian]] noble houses by a massive margin, sustained by their **complete control of the international drug trade** â€” dream-wines, lotus extracts, memory dust, pleasure elixirs, and a hundred other mind-altering substances. House Sitharis is decadent, hedonistic, hospitality-obsessed, and **uniquely dangerous because they do not play the political game** â€” they are interested in pleasure, beauty, sensation, and their own private pursuits, and **they will end you outside the rules** if you cross them. The house operates a famous free-public zoo, the strongest illusion magic in the Amunorian world, a major bardic patronage, criminal-guild ties across multiple civilizations, a long-standing alliance with the elven [[clan-nightshade|Clan Nightshade]] (their master horticulturalists and quiet poison-makers), and a charity program targeting orphans and ex-prisoners that doubles as a long-term recruitment pipeline. The head of the house always takes the symbolic name **Arissa** (female) or **Arian** (male). Reputation: *if your heart desires it, House Sitharis can procure it â€” for a price.*

## Place within the Amunorians

The Amunorians are a league of competing merchant-prince houses bound under a ceremonial Emperor (see [[amunorians]] for the full civilization). Most houses play **the game** â€” the formal and informal political competition for status, council influence, and imperial favour, with rules that all serious players respect.

House Sitharis does not play the game.

This is the central thing to understand about them. They are not too weak to play. They are not unambitious in any small-scale sense. They are simply **interested in something else**: pleasure, beauty, sensation, art, the pursuit of new substances and new sensations, the cultivation of beautiful environments and beautiful experiences. The political game does not reward what they value, so they ignore it.

The other houses **cannot remove them**. House Sitharis is obscenely wealthy â€” wealthier, by a wide margin, than any other Amunorian house â€” and the Amunorian economy is materially dependent on the drug trade Sitharis controls. Cut them out and the rest of the houses bleed gold. So House Sitharis is tolerated.

The other houses **cannot afford to cross them**. Sitharis does not retaliate inside the political game; they have no chips to spend in it. They retaliate **outside** the rules. If you cross Sitharis, you do not lose status. You lose your life, elegantly and untraceably, sometimes weeks or months later, and no one will ever prove it was them.

The other houses' standing wisdom on Sitharis is therefore terse: *Do not fuck with the people who are not playing.*

## The underlying thesis

Everything about House Sitharis â€” the zoo, the bardic patronage, the orphan charity, the indifference to the imperial council, the willingness to retaliate outside the rules, the alliance with [[clan-nightshade|Clan Nightshade]], the obsession with mind-altering substances â€” flows from one load-bearing organizing principle the house has held for centuries:

> **Consciousness alteration is social power.**

Sitharis does not pursue thrones, treaties, or armies. It pursues *the conditions of perception in the minds of the people who hold thrones, sign treaties, and command armies*. Every Sitharis instrument â€” hospitality, dream-wine, illusion magic, perfumed atmosphere, lotus-room conversation, memory dust, the slow cultivation of a poet's gratitude, the lifelong friendship offered to an heir at fourteen â€” is an instrument of consciousness alteration aimed at the people who do the work of running the world.

The house's compressed self-description, traded internally and quoted occasionally by enemies who understand them best:

> *"Why sit on the throne when you can own the man who sits on it?"*

This is not metaphor. House Sitharis does not want the throne. They want, and frequently have, *the man who sits on it* â€” not as a slave, not as a hostage, but as a man whose pleasures are shaped by their hospitality, whose dreams are seeded by their wines, whose closest friend is a Sitharis cousin he has known since they were both children, and whose deepest opinions have been forming, since long before he held the throne, in rooms whose incense, music, lighting, and conversational tempo were chosen by people who knew exactly what they were doing.

This is why the rest of the Amunorian houses cannot remove them and cannot afford to cross them. Sitharis is not playing the game on the same axis. The game is *who holds power*; Sitharis is on the axis *what the holders of power experience while they hold it*. The game-players cannot defeat Sitharis on Sitharis's axis without becoming Sitharis themselves; and Sitharis, having no interest in the throne, cannot be defeated on the game-players' axis by ordinary means.

The thesis is the reason the house's apparent decadence is misread by outsiders as weakness. The pleasure-cultivation is not the *symptom* of a wealthy house gone soft. It is the *method* by which the house exercises the only kind of power it has ever cared about.

## Source of wealth: the drug trade

House Sitharis controls the international **drug trade** â€” specifically, mind-altering substances, **not** spices or general luxury goods. Their inventory includes (but is far from limited to):

- **Dream-wines** â€” fermented vintages laced with subtle dream-inducing compounds
- **Lotus extracts** â€” distilled relaxants and reverie-inducers in many strengths
- **Memory dust** â€” fine powders that selectively soften, sharpen, or rearrange recent memory
- **Pleasure elixirs** â€” cocktails of stimulants and disinhibitors
- **Incenses and atmosphere-modifiers** â€” aerosol substances used to alter mood, suggestibility, or perception in a closed environment
- **Pharmacological refinements** â€” purified versions of common analgesics, soporifics, antidepressants, and stimulants, sold at the upper end of their respective markets

Spices and general luxury goods (silks, jewellery, exotic woods) are handled by other Amunorian houses; Sitharis specializes. **Altered states** are their domain. They are extremely good at it.

Production runs through the elven [[clan-nightshade|Clan Nightshade]], Sitharis's centuries-long horticultural partner. (See [[clan-nightshade]] and [[#The Clan Nightshade alliance|below]].)

## Internal culture: the three groups

House Sitharis is internally stratified into **three distinct groups**, each playing a different role in the house's overall functioning. The stratification is not formal rank â€” every member of the house can move between groups across their life, and many do â€” but at any given moment most members fall clearly into one of the three.

### The Clear Minds

The **small core** of family members who deliberately remain mostly sober. These are the political animals of the family â€” sharp, charismatic, ruthless, and personally interested in the work of running the house's business and protecting its position.

Clear Minds run:

- Major trade negotiations and contracts
- Relationships with other Amunorian houses (formal courtesies, treaty-level dealings)
- Diplomatic representation to non-Amunorian powers
- The house's criminal-guild ties (see [[#External alliances and information|below]])
- Long-term strategic planning
- Internal discipline and family governance

Clear Minds do not regard their relative sobriety as a sacrifice; they enjoy the political game, the intrigue, the maneuvering. In any large family there are a few people who are natural political junkies, and Sitharis's Clear Minds are those people. They genuinely prefer being sharp to being blissed-out, and they take genuine pleasure in being the family members who actually matter in the wider world.

The number of Clear Minds at any one time is small â€” typically a few dozen across the entire extended house â€” and the role tends to run in informal sub-lineages within the broader Sitharis family tree.

### The Socialites

The **flashy middle class** of the house. Half-lucid most of the time â€” heavy users at parties and social events, but not so deeply submerged in the lifestyle that they cannot function in public. They dominate the empire's high society and serve as the public face of the house.

Socialites do:

- Throw the most legendary parties and festivals in the Amunorian world
- Set fashion trends with **ultra-rare luxury goods that only Sitharis can supply** â€” diaphanous spider-silk gowns from sources only Sitharis controls, fur stoles from absurdly rare animals, exotic perfumes, never-before-seen cosmetic compounds, illusion-enhanced jewellery
- Run the **intelligence side** of the house â€” at a Sitharis party, eventually, every important person in the city will pass through, and what they say in their cups is heard by people who remember
- Patron the arts (especially bards â€” see [[#Patronage|below]])
- Maintain cultural relevance and visibility in a way that backstops Sitharis's political untouchability with constant cultural prestige

Socialites are not the family's brain. But they are its public face and its sensory organs, and their work is what makes Sitharis matter culturally as well as economically.

### The Blissed

The **majority of the house**. Members who are deep in the lifestyle â€” long-term users, cultivators of beautiful environments, connoisseurs of sensation. They live in perfumed gardens and silk-draped halls. They sample everything. They go in and out of states of awareness across their days. When clear-headed, they are genuinely **grateful** to the Clear Minds â€” they understand that someone has to do the political work, and they pity the Clear Minds for not being able to enjoy what the rest of the family enjoys.

The Blissed are not useless. They include the family's master cultivators, alchemists, perfumers, gardeners, illusion-mages, sensory artists, and many of its most distinguished horticultural collaborators with Clan Nightshade. Their work is what produces the rare and exquisite goods that the Socialites then trade on. They are simply the part of the family that does not run the house and does not particularly want to.

### Movement between groups

Members move between groups across their lives. Many young Sitharis start as Socialites, drift toward the Blissed in middle age as their tastes deepen, then drift back toward the Socialites or the Clear Minds in later life as they take on responsibilities. A few remain Blissed their entire adult lives. A few find at adulthood that they are temperamentally Clear Minds and stay there. Inheritance of the **Arissa / Arian** title (see [[#Leadership|below]]) almost always goes to a Clear Mind.

## Hospitality is the threat

The defining external behaviour of House Sitharis is their **hospitality culture**.

A guest at a Sitharis palace, a Sitharis dinner, or a Sitharis reception will be **drugged**. This is not malice and not (in their view) deception. It is **how Sitharis entertains**. The wine is laced. The food carries something. The incense in the receiving room was already burning when you walked in. The flowers in the hall were chosen for a reason. **You consented to all of this when you accepted the invitation.**

Sitharis's view of this is sincere. They drug themselves the same way. Their internal culture is built around the position that an interesting world deserves to be experienced in interesting ways. Sober interaction with a Sitharis is, in their estimation, a slightly impoverished version of the same conversation. They are not trying to compromise you; they are trying to **share the world they live in**. The fact that they are simultaneously gathering intelligence on you and softening your defenses isâ€¦ not so much intentional manipulation as a fortunate side-effect of how they prefer to live anyway.

This is what makes them dangerous in a uniquely Sitharis way. They drug their enemies as a courtesy. They drug their friends as a courtesy. They do not differentiate. The only people who walk out of a Sitharis encounter unaltered are people who were already alert enough to refuse the wine, decline the incense, eat only food they brought themselves, and even then never quite know what was in the air.

For political opponents, the experience is harrowing. For long-term allies, it is simply how the relationship works.

## Leadership: Arissa and Arian

The head of House Sitharis always takes the **symbolic name Arissa** (if female) or **Arian** (if male) upon assuming the position. The name is **adopted as a regnal title**, regardless of the holder's birth name. When one Arissa or Arian dies or steps down, the successor takes the same name; the previous holder, if living, returns to a private name and retires.

The convention has held without exception since the founding of the house in the late post-Demon-War centuries. **The current head is therefore always referred to publicly as Arissa or Arian** â€” never by their birth name, which is treated as a private family matter. (Other Amunorian houses know perfectly well that the current Arissa was born, say, *Velara of the Eastern Garden*; nobody uses that name in public. This is the form, and the form matters.)

The name itself is a deliberate echo of older naming traditions and is sometimes interpreted as a quiet nod to certain decadent serpent-cult civilizations that informed Amunorian cultural inspiration. Sitharis members will not confirm this and consider the question gauche.

The Arissa or Arian is **virtually always a Clear Mind** â€” sharp, sober, politically capable, and deeply committed to running the house. Selection of the next Arissa/Arian is a private internal matter handled by the senior Clear Minds in conclave; the choice is announced to the wider Sitharis family and to the Amunorian political world simultaneously, and the new holder takes the name in a brief, dignified ceremony.

## Patronage and arts

House Sitharis is one of the great patrons of the arts in the Amunorian world, and the **single greatest patron of bards** in the modern era. The combination of obscene wealth, taste, and a culture obsessed with sensory beauty produces a steady stream of patronized musicians, poets, performers, and theatrical companies â€” many of them famous well beyond Amunorian borders.

The house possesses **by far the strongest illusion magic in the Amunorian world**. Their Socialites use illusion-magic to construct breathtaking party environments â€” galleries that change rooms as you walk through them, gardens that bloom only at midnight, ballrooms with three different ceilings, performances in which the line between performer and audience deliberately blurs. The bardic patronage and the illusion mastery feed each other; the illusion-mages stage the bards, and the bards' performances inspire new illusion techniques.

The combination of mind-altering substances + elite illusion magic + first-rank bardic performance makes a Sitharis high-society event feel almost like stepping into another world. The other Amunorian houses can match Sitharis on wealth or political weight, but **nobody else can match the spectacle**.

## The Sitharis Zoo

House Sitharis maintains the **most magnificent zoo in the world**. It is **free to the public**. It sits prominently in the major Sitharis-affiliated city (open canon â€” likely a southern Azure Basin port or river-city) and is a major civic attraction.

The zoo is a triple-purpose installation:

- A **public face of generosity and culture** â€” visible, tangible proof that House Sitharis is a benevolent and sophisticated patron of the city. The civic goodwill is enormous.
- A **research facility for [[clan-nightshade|Clan Nightshade]]** â€” every exotic creature Sitharis traders bring back from across the world (especially venomous ones â€” snakes, spiders, scorpions, certain frogs, certain insects) is studied by Nightshade horticulturalists for biologically useful compounds. Venom extraction is routine. New drug and medicinal compounds are developed from animal-derived inputs.
- A **subtle reminder** â€” the diversity and exoticism of the collection serves as a quiet flex of Sitharis's reach. The animals come from places nobody else trades with.

The zoo's veterinary science is genuinely first-rate, partly because Clan Nightshade's interest in the animals' health is sincere (a healthy specimen yields better compounds) and partly because Sitharis hires the best modern beast-handlers regardless of cost. Modern visitors describe the Sitharis Zoo as one of the most visually spectacular places in Amunorian territory.

## The Imperial Charity

The Amunorian ceremonial Emperor's standing decree on noble charity (see [[amunorians#Government and the houses]]) requires every great house to engage in tangible support of the poor. Different houses target different beneficiaries.

House Sitharis's charity heavily targets two specific populations:

- **Orphaned and indigent children**
- **Recently released prisoners**

They give them food, clothing, shelter, education, and â€” most importantly â€” **job offers**. Low-level work at first: runners, observers, lookouts, message-carriers, courtyard attendants. Harmless-seeming entry positions.

By the time those children grow up and those ex-prisoners have settled into their post-prison lives, a substantial number of them are **quietly working for House Sitharis** in the criminal underworld â€” feeding information, moving product, maintaining the network of eyes and ears across the city. The Sitharis charitable philanthropy is one of the most efficient long-term recruitment pipelines in the modern world, **disguised as genuine generosity** because, on the surface and in many individual cases, it is.

The other Amunorian houses know what this is. They cannot complain â€” the Emperor commanded all houses to help the poor, and on paper Sitharis is doing exactly that. The cleanness with which Sitharis converts a public obligation into a private intelligence and personnel operation is a textbook example of why the other houses fear them.

## External alliances and information

House Sitharis maintains discreet, long-standing ties to:

- **Thieves' guilds** in major cities across Amunorian and adjacent territories
- **Assassins' guilds** (rarer but documented)
- **Smuggling rings** working both Amunorian and Azure Basin trade routes
- **Black markets** in pre-Cataclysm relics, exotic biologicals, illegal magitek, and forbidden substances
- **Selective relationships with criminal organizations of other modern civilizations** â€” especially [[orden|Orden]] trade-route fences and certain Azure Basin lagoon-republic underworlds

These relationships are managed by the Clear Minds. The Blissed and the Socialites usually do not know the full extent of them. The relationships are old, layered, and discreet; they are also the reason House Sitharis can credibly claim, *if your heart desires it, we can procure it.*

The reputation is **earned**. Whether a customer wants a forbidden substance, a rare artifact, an impossible luxury, an unusual service, or something far darker, the Sitharis network can obtain it. The price varies; the answer is essentially never *no*.

## "If your heart desires it..."

The standing public saying attached to House Sitharis, known across Amunorian territory and beyond:

> *"If your heart desires it, House Sitharis can procure itâ€¦ for a price."*

This is not marketing. The other Amunorian houses understand it as a literal description of Sitharis's reach and willingness. The "for a price" is critical â€” Sitharis is not a charity (the imperial-decree charity work aside), and serious requests come with serious payments â€” but the procurement capability is real, and Sitharis enjoys their reputation for it.
