# Goblins

**Summary**: Goblins are small, scavenging, opportunistic descendants of the [[grugi|Grug]]. While their cousins the [[gnomes|gnomes]] inherited the inventive and constructive spark, goblins retained the cleverness, sneakiness, and adaptability â€” turning it toward scavenging, improvisation, and survival on the edges of civilization, expressed culturally in the famous **"goblin solution"**: a clever, extremely unorthodox, and potentially explosive fix to a problem. In the Tome's framing, modern goblin society is **a distributed salvage civilization** â€” descendants of Grug maintenance instincts who quietly form the **decentralized continuity infrastructure** of every settled world they live near, whether or not their hosts notice.

## Origin

Goblins descend from the [[grugi|Grug]], the Velrathi-engineered race created to maintain imperial technological systems. The original Grug were small, smart, mechanically adept, clever, and sneaky (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

The Grug suffered catastrophic losses in the [[demon-war|Demon War]], but enough survived to diverge into two descendant lineages: the **inventive [[gnomes|gnomes]]** and the **scavenging goblins** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Distinction from Gnomes

Of the two Grug descendant lines:

- **[[gnomes|Gnomes]]** retained the inventive, constructive instinct.
- **Goblins** retained the scavenging, opportunistic, sneaky instinct.

Both branches are described as preserving "the clever spark" in different forms (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Anatomy

Smaller than goblins of the Grug peak, with rough features adapted to opportunistic survival. Diminished from their progenitors in size, intellect, and magical aptitude â€” a recurring pattern in all "natural devolution" races (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

## Invention and craft

Like [[gnomes|gnomes]], goblins are **natural inventors** â€” but their approach is far less refined. Goblin inventions are classic **Rube Goldberg machines**: wildly creative, overly complicated, often held together with scavenged parts, string, and sheer stubbornness. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

Where a [[gnomes|gnome]] device is **elegant and carefully engineered**, a goblin device is likely to involve **seventeen unnecessary moving parts and at least one explosive component "just in case."** Goblin creations are brilliant in concept but **notoriously prone to catastrophic failures** â€” sometimes spectacularly so. The phrase **"a goblin solution"** is widely understood across Eadras as shorthand for a clever but extremely unorthodox â€” and potentially explosive â€” fix to a problem.

The differing approaches make the gnome-goblin partnership unexpectedly productive: goblins **provide rare materials and test dangerous prototypes**, while gnomes **refine** what survives the testing.

## Society

Goblins thrive on the **fringes of civilization**. Many work as **scavengers, tinkers, information brokers, waste collectors, or "acquisition specialists."** Some have formed successful (if chaotic) merchant networks or urban underclasses. They are **remarkably good at finding value in what others discard** â€” a skill descended from Grug system-maintenance instincts, redirected from imperial machinery to the leavings of every later civilization. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

Goblins **do not have their own homeland.** Some live integrated into other races' cities, providing salvage, sanitation, and acquisition services from within; others live **outside civilization as feral scavengers** subsisting on what the cities throw out. (source: Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt)

A common rude nickname for them â€” used by surface-races who don't think about where their refuse goes â€” is **"shit-pickers."** The slur is descriptive of the trade rather than fanciful, and goblins know it perfectly well; the most successful operators of the trade (see [[harvey-wash|Harvey Wash]] of [[bold|Bold]]) have made it a point of fierce civic pride.

### Hoarding and the goblin network

Goblins are **hoarders**. They maintain vast stocks of items pulled from trash and ruins on the principle that anything **"might be useful someday."** A given goblin's hoard might contain the only surviving example of a half-millennium-old part. (source: Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt)

The hoards are connected through **extensive social networks that span multiple cities and civilizations.** When a [[gnomes|gnome]] workshop needs a specific ancient part or artifact, gnomes routinely **ask goblins to find it**, and the network often already has the item somewhere â€” possibly in another city. The Grug â†’ goblin maintenance instinct survives intact: rather than maintaining a single empire's machinery, goblins now maintain a multi-city distributed inventory of *what might one day need replacing*.

The goblin network is **not unconditionally open**. Networks **close themselves to civilizations that look down on or mistreat goblins.** A city that treats its goblins as vermin gradually finds that its salvage flow dries up, that its rare-parts requests stop being filled, and that its sanitation problems compound â€” usually without anyone in the establishment realizing why. The compact is implicit but real: respect buys access; contempt buys silence. (source: Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt)

Specific tribal structures, leadership patterns, and settlement archetypes remain to be defined in detail. What is canonical is the niche: **edge-of-civilization, salvage-driven, network-based, mercurially organized.**

### Radical egalitarianism â€” the work-gang rotation

Goblin society is the most extreme expression of the [[philosophy-of-contribution|Velrathi â†’ Grug â†’ Goblin philosophy of contribution]]. **No goblin is exempt from manual labor.** When the labor roster is published â€” typically every week or so â€” *everyone* is on it. Kings, archmages, high priests, and master merchants alike show up for their work-gang shifts and do real engineering, magitek maintenance, hauling, mining, and infrastructure work alongside ordinary laborers.

This is not ceremonial: the king does the same shift as the goblin standing next to him. The cultural reasoning is direct. **Everyone is a goblin first**; different members just have different responsibilities; **a king is a goblin who has been entrusted with the job of making decisions**, not a person who is intrinsically better than other goblins. Status is **earned, demonstrable, and provisional** â€” a goblin who stops earning their position is replaced.

Goblin culture is therefore a strict **meritocracy** that finds the rigid hereditary hierarchies of other races mildly ridiculous. The pragmatic exceptions for the injured, ill, elderly, and disabled apply as in all three cultures of the descent line. See [[philosophy-of-contribution]] for the full Velrathi-Grug-Goblin treatment.

### Drink, work, and rest

The goblin daily-life pattern is shaped by the work-gang structure: focused dirty effort during the working day (goblins do not waste energy on cleanliness during work that will dirty them again immediately) and a clean, fed, communal off-work period in the evening â€” typically including the post-work bath (see [[bathing-customs#Goblins]]) and a small cup of [[goblin-ancestor-liquor|Ancestor Liquor]], the harsh brimstone-and-tar spirit goblins inherited from the [[grugi|Grug]] underground brewing tradition.

### Tunnel cheese

The signature *food* of goblin tunnel culture â€” alongside Ancestor Liquor as the signature drink â€” is [[goblin-tunnel-cheese|tunnel cheese]]: a **brie-blue hybrid** aged in damp working tunnels with whatever cave-bacteria and mould happen to live there. Each tunnel produces a distinguishable batch reflecting its specific microclimate, and goblins celebrate the variation in ways outsiders find disorienting. The cheese is prized internally as a delicacy on the order of any other culture's prestige food; most surface-dwellers regard it as cursed. A small surface market exists for connoisseurs, adventurous gourmands, and cultures with their own strong-cheese traditions; the mass surface-population reception is hostile or fearful. See [[goblin-tunnel-cheese]] for the full treatment.

### The "live and let live" ethic

Goblin civilization sits at the **maximally permissive** pole of what is sometimes called the [[three-cultures-ethical-triangle|three-cultures ethical triangle]] â€” the philosophical contrast among goblin tunnel cultures, the [[amunorians|Amunorian]] caravan-and-pastoral hinterland, and the far-eastern [[dragon-empire|Dragon Empire]]. The governing logic â€” *if it tastes good and doesn't kill too many people, it's fine* â€” extends well beyond cheese: goblin culture tolerates **black markets and informal economies**, **risky experiments** in craft and magic, **crude personal freedoms** that more rigid cultures would never permit, and **occasional tragedy** as the unavoidable cost of the freedoms goblins actually want. The pole is **anarchic in the technical sense** â€” not lawless, but minimally centrally-regulated, with order produced bottom-up through custom and local consensus rather than top-down through edict and inspector. From the [[dragon-empire|Dragon Empire's]] perspective the goblins are an existential threat to be regulated for the greater harmony; from the goblins' perspective, Dragon Empire authoritarianism is the active enemy. The Amunorian caravan culture sits between the two and trades with both.

## Notable goblins

- **[[harvey-wash|Harvey Wash]]** â€” The "King of the Golden Road" of [[bold|Bold]]. Operates the city's dawn-to-dusk waste-collection-and-processing empire under a human alias; reinvests the entirety of his profits into Bold's poor (soup kitchens, safe sleeping places, fair-wage employment); maintains a standing compact with Bold's Thieves' Guild; has been trying to win formal civic recognition for a **Scavengers' Guild**. The most visible canonical example of the goblin-scavenger archetype taken to its civic-positive extreme.

## Magical Inheritance

Goblins, like all Grug descendants, carry residual aptitude for working with leftover magitech and salvaged enchantments â€” a fitting echo of their progenitors' role as system-maintainers, applied now to the rusted-out scraps of older civilizations rather than to active imperial machinery.

## Relations

- **[[gnomes|Gnomes]]** are sibling-kin through shared Grug ancestry. The relationship is generally positive but marked by contrast â€” gnomes are the *"respectable"* inventive branch, goblins the wilder, more unhinged cousins. The two **frequently collaborate**: goblins provide rare materials and test dangerous prototypes; gnomes refine them. Many of the most striking gnomish workshop breakthroughs have a quiet goblin testing-cycle behind them. (source: May 1 revisions.txt) The view across the kinship is **mixed and unsentimental on both sides**: some gnomes treat goblins as **kin** and act on it; many others treat them as **useful tools for dirty work** and don't pretend otherwise. Goblins notice. The collaboration runs anyway because both sides need what the other provides. (source: Compiled Lore - Velrathi and Their Descendant Races.txt)
- **[[orcs|Orcs]]** are often neighbors. Goblins sometimes live near or beneath orc settlements, trading scavenged goods and deep-tunnel knowledge. Orc clan settlements were historically built above Rathari cities, and goblins inherit the Grug deep-tunnel familiarity beneath those same urban centers. (sources: May 1 revisions.txt; Peoples of Eadras.txt)
- **[[dwarves|Shada'din (Dwarves)]]** are valuable partners in scavenging and in testing experimental devices. The Shada'din-built [[magitek]] factory output (gnome-invented, Shada'din-built â€” see [[gnomes#Magical Inheritance|gnome-Shada'din partnership]]) creates a steady volume of test-stage equipment that Shada'din shops trust goblin testers to abuse, break, or accidentally improve. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)
- **Surviving pure Grug bloodlines** are now extremely rare and mostly absorbed into goblin and gnome populations or living in secluded Velrathi-descended enclaves (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Naming â€” the rough sibling branch of Grug

Goblin names are **Grug names made shorter, meaner, funnier, and more accident-prone** â€” clipped, dented, and turned into names that sound like they were *shouted during a fire*. (source: Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt)

### Sound and form

Hard consonants, doubled letters (Grizz, Tikk, Vexx), clipped endings, comic violence, harsh nicknames, earned **insult-cognomens**. Common endings: *-ix, -ik, -ikk, -zak, -zog, -ruk, -tik, -nix, -grix, -x.*

The same Grug roots produce many distinct goblin variants â€” *Grix* becomes *Grik / Grizz / Grixit*; *Tikzar* becomes *Tik / Tikk / Tikzak / Tizzar*; *Phrikon* becomes *Frik / Phrik / Frikk*. See [[naming-conventions#goblin-naming-the-rough-sibling-branch|Racial Naming Conventions â€” Goblin]] for the full transformation table.

**Example given names**: *Grik, Grizz, Vex, Vexx, Nark, Tik, Tikk, Tizzar, Rix, Rux, Frik, Zik, Krizz, Skiv, Snik, Vezrik, Grazik, Kazzik* (and feminine forms like *Grizza, Vexa, Tikka, Frika, Snikka, Kazzara*).

### Cognomens â€” rough and physical

Goblin cognomens descend from Grug craft-cognomens but become **rougher and more physical**. Where a Grug earns *Gearwhisper*, a goblin earns *Gearbiter*. Where a Grug earns *Quickfix*, a goblin earns *Wronglever*. Examples: *Gearbiter, Fusegnaw, Sparkspit, Trapfinger, Locksnip, Quickstab, Wirechewer, Boltlick, Ratchetjaw, Kettleblast, Snaregrin, Cranktooth, Wronglever, Boomhand, Switchsnatcher, Gearscream, Nailtongue, Fusesniffer, Knifefix, Rustlaugh, Badwrench, Tripwire, Latchrat, Smoke-Eye.*

### Den, crew, and boss affiliations

Goblins often append a den, crew, or boss affiliation to their address:

- *Grizz Gearbiter of Red Wrench*
- *Tikka Fusegnaw of the Bent Nail Crew*
- *Frik Boomhand under Boss Vexx*
- *Rixa Snaregrin from Smokehole*

These affiliations are **fluid** â€” goblins switch crews far more than gnomes switch benches, and a goblin may carry several historical "of" or "under" affiliations as part of their reputation.

### What they don't use

Modern goblins do **not** use the imperial-era "ser [Velrathi House]" servant construction â€” that form was dropped after the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] and would in any case have been offensively formal for goblin culture. Goblin names are deliberately the chaotic side of the Grug heritage; the Velrathi House structure that gave servant naming its gravity is gone, and goblins are not the type to mourn it.
