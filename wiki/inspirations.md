# Inspirations

**Summary**: Bibliography of external works and frameworks — novels, short stories, internet pages, scientific theories, mythological traditions, and other influences — that shaped concepts which were later developed into Eadras canon. **Nothing here is canon.** These sources document the conceptual lineage of Eadras ideas; they are never ingested into wiki pages as factual sources and are never cited on canonical claims.

**Sources**: Files placed in `raw/inspiration/`. Inline disclosures recorded in conversation; updated 2026-05-09 to reflect the partial reversal of the 2026-05-08 *"document the influences, no rework"* decision (see [[#Note: 2026-05-09 partial reversal on the magic-system Galloway borrowings|note below]]). 2026-05-14 (later): the setting-wide design-principle catalog formerly anchored on this page was elevated to its own dedicated home at [[principles-of-eadras|Principles of Eadras]] — this page returns to its original purpose of cataloguing external influences.

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14.

## Note: 2026-05-09 partial reversal on the magic-system Galloway borrowings

The 2026-05-08 ingest concluded with the user's choice to **document the influences rather than restructure the magic system**. That decision was **partially reversed on 2026-05-09**: the Sorcerer and Druid orders specifically were too distinctive in their original form to be kept comfortably even with documentation, and have been replaced by new original orders ([[netharim|Netharim]] and [[lexanari|Lexanari]]) plus a redefined [[druids|Druid sub-priesthood]] of [[qoxli|Qoxli]], [[sylph|Sylph]], and [[nox|Nox]]. The Weave's status is **under review** in a planned make-it-mine pass before publication. Other documented borrowings (goddess Lirra, the Firestaff/Spear lineage, the Source/Eldritch/Conduits triad, dragons-and-unicorns, the Three / Dao / Runeswords / Mythos / Progenitor pattern, and the Background fictional influences) remain in their previously-documented status. See [[log]] for the full ingest record and [[#Tales of the Firestaff — novel series (James Galloway / "Fel")|the Galloway entry below]] for what was specifically reversed.

## What this is for

Eadras is a deliberately built world, and many of its concepts have roots in things the user has read or encountered. This page tracks those roots. It answers questions like:

- "Where did the idea for X come from?"
- "What real-world work shaped this concept?"
- "What did this canonical idea evolve out of?"

It does **not** make those external works part of the world. The relationship is **inspiration → development → canon**, with the development step (conversations, refinements, ingest of canon sources) being where the actual canon is decided.

## Rules for Claude

- Treat `raw/inspiration/` as **influence record**, not canon source.
- Do **not** create per-source summary pages (the way canon ingest does).
- Do **not** cite an inspiration as the source on any factual claim about Eadras. Citations belong to canonical raw sources only.
- Do reference an inspiration when explaining the *origin* or *lineage* of a concept (e.g., *"this concept developed out of [the influence in raw/inspiration/X]"*).
- When the user adds a file to `raw/inspiration/`, ask them which Eadras concept(s) it influenced and add a catalog entry pointing both directions: the work, and the wiki pages whose concepts it shaped.

The catalog has **three tiers**:

- **Direct fictional concept influences** — fiction works that contributed specific named concepts (a god, a magical structure, an artifact, a race-conception) lifted from another author's invented world. Each entry lists the wiki pages whose concepts trace back to it, with the original-vs-borrowed boundary noted. These are the concepts worth tracking for derivative-work awareness.
- **Real-world references** — scientific theories, mythological traditions, historical events, and other real-world inputs that informed specific Eadras concepts. **These are not fictional borrowings.** Concepts informed by real-world references remain the author's original work in the standard authorial sense — every fantasy author draws on real-world knowledge, and no other author invented K–T impact physics or the global flood myth. Recorded here for documentation and lineage purposes only.
- **Background / atmospheric fictional influences** — the **complete fiction reading-list** of works that shaped Eadras's general register and feel. Every Direct-fictional work appears here as well, because no author who contributed a specific named concept did so without also shaping the general register; works appearing *only* in Background did not contribute specific named concepts but shaped Eadras through small background details that diffuse throughout the setting. **Real-world references are not in this tier** — they have their own.

## Direct fictional concept influences

Fiction works that contributed specific named concepts lifted from another author's invented world. Entries below catalog the structural seeds of specific Eadras concepts. Each entry names the external work, the medium and creator, and the wiki pages whose concepts trace back to it. Catalog entries are author-disclosed and may exist without a corresponding file in `raw/inspiration/`; when the entry was disclosed in conversation rather than ingested as a file, that is noted.

### Tales of the Firestaff — novel series (James Galloway / "Fel")

**File**: *(disclosed by author 2026-05-08; no source files in `raw/inspiration/`)*

**Influenced**: [[lirra]], [[magic-in-eadras]], [[order-sharanel]], [[order-sworn-netharim]], [[weavespinners]], [[dashar]], [[heart-of-the-goddess]], [[sorcerer-crusade]], [[thousand-year-dimming]], [[lost-zenith]], [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]] — Galloway's goddess **Niami** is the original of [[lirra|Lirra]]; the broader **Sorcerer apparatus**, the **Weave**, and the **Four Major Traditions / Orders** framing descend from this series, as does most of the rest of Eadras's magic-system mechanics. The **Firestaff** itself is the conceptual ancestor of the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]; the Eadras Spear's specific mechanics (Crystallized Entropy, Grand Conjunction activation, Unrestricted Elder God transformation, Dao's curse, the four hiding places, the Final Ward composition) are original developments on top of that seed.

**2026-05-09 update**: The user reversed the 2026-05-08 *"document the influences, no rework"* decision **for the Sorcerers and Druids specifically**. Sorcerers are now replaced by the new **[[netharim|Netharim]]** order (intuitive reality-shapers built directly on Eadras's [[observer-and-naming|observer-and-naming]] cosmology); the standalone Druid order is folded into a [[druids|sub-priesthood]] of [[qoxli|Qoxli]], [[sylph|Sylph]], and [[nox|Nox]]; a fully new fourth order — the **[[lexanari|Lexanari]]** (innate-only mortal speakers of the [[true-language#The Generational Degradation Model|Primordial Language]]) — fills the "extreme power, severe consequences" slot the borrowed Druid concept used to occupy. The **Weave** is candidate-for-removal in the user's planned make-it-mine pass, with the possible exception of its defensive function as a Lirra ward against outsider magic theft. **Note-and-defer**: existing Sorcerer/Weave/Weavespinner/Sorcerer-Crusade narrative is preserved unchanged on its own pages with canon-status banners pointing to the new orders; the broader rework happens in a later pass before publication. Goddess Lirra and the Spear-of-Crystallized-Entropy / Firestaff lineage are **not** affected by the reversal — those remain in the same documented-and-kept status they held before. See [[log]].

### Nexus of Kelleran — novel series (Wayne Edward Clarke)

**File**: *(disclosed by author 2026-05-08; no source files in `raw/inspiration/`)*

**Influenced**: [[magic-in-eadras]], [[eldritch]], [[eldritch-cycle]], [[seven-conduits]], [[source-surges]], [[dragons]], [[unicorns]], [[ratharil]], [[great-exchange]], [[unicorn-plains]], [[ixthal]] — Source of two distinct seed-clusters in Eadras canon: (1) the **Source / Eldritch / Conduits** triad that anchors the magical substrate; and (2) the basic conception of [[dragons|Dragons]] and [[unicorns|Unicorns]] as Eadras sapients. **Heavily extended on both sides** — the Eadras-specific elaborations are original developments grown from these seeds: on the magic side, the **Three-State Model** (Quintessence / Eldritch / Mana), the **Sourceshine → Eldritch** state transition, the full **Eldritch Cycle** with its rivulets-streams-rivers-conduits hydrology, the conduit-as-eternal-magical-structure framing (older than the Weave, anchored to the planetary core, indestructible, surface-array destroyed in the Cataclysm but the conduits themselves untouched), and the **Sourceflare / atmosphere-and-magnetic-field filtering** model; on the dragon/unicorn side, secret creation by [[ixthal|Ixthal]], the **Nine Embers of Renewal** and Cindirith / Davian / Koruss as the three publicly named progenitors, the **Treaty of Ratharil** (999,999 cap, Council of 99), the [[great-exchange|Great Exchange]] and its derived phrases (*Walking the Dragon Path*, *Singing with the Unicorn Voice*), the **First Great Flare** as dragon biological origin, the dawn-catalyst for unicorns, the [[unicorn-plains|Unicorn Plains War]] and the post-war "Do not hunt the plains" norm, the [[flesh-eaters|Flesh-Eaters]] cult, the Compact of Horn and Flame, and unicorn psionics-as-distinct-paradigm.

### Bard's Tales (Rose series) — web fiction series (R22CoolGuy, storiesonline.com)

**File**: *(disclosed by author 2026-05-08; no source files in `raw/inspiration/`)*

**Influenced**: [[the-three]], [[dao]], [[runeswords]], [[mythos]], [[peoples-of-eadras]], [[great-banishment]], [[elfen]], [[velrathi]], [[tarans]] — A very obscure web-fiction series; the source of [[the-three|The Three]] (Mark, Rand, Melania), [[dao|Dao]] as an individual avatar character, the [[runeswords|Runeswords]] concept, the [[mythos|Mythos]] as a cosmological unit, the **Progenitor Races** structural pattern (three races created by three deity-figures), and the **Exile** concept that became [[great-banishment|the Great Banishment]]. ### Discworld — novel series (Terry Pratchett)

**File**: *(disclosed by author 2026-05-08; no source files in `raw/inspiration/`)*

## Real-world references

Scientific theories, mythological traditions, and historical frames that informed specific Eadras concepts. **These are not fictional borrowings.** Concepts informed by real-world references remain the author's original work in the standard authorial sense — every fantasy author draws on real-world knowledge, and no other author *invented* the K–T impact theory or the global flood archetype. They are recorded here for documentation and lineage purposes (so future readers can trace what informed which Eadras concept), not as derivative-work attributions.

### K–T boundary impact theory — scientific framework (Alvarez et al., 1980 onward; current paleontological consensus)

**File**: *(disclosed by author 2026-05-08; recorded here as a scientific-theory reference, not a fictional borrowing)*

**Informed**: [[arathi-cataclysm]], [[worldender|Worldbreaker]], [[worldbreaker-mechanism]] — Informed the **physical mechanism** of the Cataclysm. The Eadras Cataclysm draws on the impact-event hypothesis for the Cretaceous–Paleogene mass extinction — a falling-star strike, immediate global devastation, atmospheric and oceanic upheaval, and a long-term ecological reset that resets the dominant biome. **The Cataclysm itself remains the author's original work**; K–T informs its physics, but no other author invented this event. Eadras-specific elements: [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]]'s First Breach as the proximate cause, the Worldender's alien crystal as the physical artifact, the Worldbreaker's steam-flash cascade and crustal decoupling as the canonical geophysical detail, the polar stability and conduit anchoring that preserved the seven termini through the impact, and the **70%-land → 30%-land geographic inversion** — a specific synthesis the author considers original to Eadras (no fictional precedent identified for the combination of astrophysical impact + this scale of land/water inversion + millions-of-years-ago framing).

### Global flood mythology — cross-cultural tradition (Mesopotamian, Hebrew, Greek, Hindu, Mesoamerican, Chinese, and other deluge myths)

**File**: *(disclosed by author 2026-05-08; recorded here as a cross-cultural mythological reference, not a fictional borrowing)*

**Informed**: [[arathi-cataclysm]], [[pre-cataclysm-relics]] — Informed the **cultural and narrative frame** of the Cataclysm — a world-changing event that transitions a remembered earlier age into a diminished present, with surviving fragments, folk memory, and a remnant population carrying forward what was lost. The 70%-land → 30%-land inversion brings the global-flood archetype into Eadras's geophysical canon. **The narrative frame remains the author's original work**; the deluge archetype is everyone's mythological heritage, not any single author's invention. Eadras-specific elements: [[pre-cataclysm-relics|Stillbeasts and Ashseed Forests]] as concrete pre-Cataclysm relics, the [[arathi-golden-age-map|Arathi Golden Age Map]] as a rare cartographic survival, and the three sundered continents ([[avalon|Avalon]] / [[damedes|Damedes]] / [[culethor|Culethor]]) as the post-Cataclysm geography.

## Setting-wide design principles

**Moved.** The setting-wide design principles catalog — surfaced incrementally from the 2026-05-11 Draconic + Modernity expert-council sessions and expanded across the 2026-05-12, 2026-05-13, and 2026-05-14 ingests — now lives on its own dedicated page at **[[principles-of-eadras|Principles of Eadras]]**.

That page is the canonical home for the principles going forward. It splits the material into:

- **Part I — In-world principles** (canon facts about how Eadras works: cosmological, theological, civilisational, magical, anthropological, tonal)
- **Part II — Author-side craft principles** (design discipline that emerged from building the setting)

The seven-word thematic spine — *continuity · memory · catastrophe · identity · structure · survival · meaning* — and the *light and darkness need each other* anti-grimdark tonal frame both live on that page now.

This page ([[inspirations|Inspirations]]) returns to its original purpose: cataloguing the external works (novels, web fiction, real-world references) that influenced concepts later developed into Eadras canon.
