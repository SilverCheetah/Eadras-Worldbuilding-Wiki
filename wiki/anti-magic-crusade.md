# The Anti-Magic Crusade

**Summary**: A violent persecution of **all magic users** that occurred **roughly 9,000–10,000 years ago**, in the decades immediately preceding the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]. It was **covertly engineered by the [[black-hand|Black Hand]]** as a strategic diversion to enable their search for the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]. Over the preceding century the Black Hand orchestrated the **[[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]]** — hundreds of major magical disasters and thousands of smaller ones — to turn the world against all magic. The Crusade catastrophically damaged the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] (every magic user's life sustains it), but this damage was **largely unintended**. It was halted when **the gods inhabited their [[icons|Icons]]** and confronted the participating priesthoods and martial orders directly. Three [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] were killed before the halt, triggering the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]].

## Overview

Roughly 9,000–10,000 years ago, in the decades immediately preceding the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]], a violent persecution of **all magic users** erupted across much of Eadras under the doctrine that *all magic is wicked*. The Black Hand spent the preceding century engineering ever-worsening magical disasters — the **[[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]]** — to create the conditions for the Crusade. The persecution swept up [[netharim|Netharim]], wizards, druids, priests, [[lexanari|Lexanari]], psionic practitioners, hedge-witches, and more before it was halted by direct divine confrontation.

The Crusade catastrophically damaged the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] and triggered the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]] — the millennium-long collapse of magical potency from which Eadras has only partially recovered.

## The Century of Sorrows

The Black Hand's campaign was a deliberate, long-term operation that slowly ramped up over approximately **one hundred years**, reaching a crescendo in the final 15–20 years. There were **hundreds of major incidents** and **thousands of smaller ones**, each intended to provoke horror, revulsion, and ultimately anger at magic itself.

Representative major disasters of the crescendo period:

- A **magical poison cloud over the city of Vaelis** killed 18,000 in a single night
- A **plague of hungry undead** ravaged the trading town of Dun Lira
- A **chaos pocket ruptured at the caravan village of Threeforks**, dissolving reality before hundreds of witnesses
- Rivers transformed into liquid shadow; fields grew screaming flesh-crops; wild magic surges warped entire districts

Once the Crusade became self-sustaining, the Black Hand largely stopped manufacturing new disasters and focused on directing the resulting violence. See [[century-of-sorrows]] for the full operational account.

## The Core Crusader Claim

> ***"All magic is wicked. Recent events have proven it beyond doubt."***

The argument did not require sophistication. The hundreds of catastrophes the Century of Sorrows had inflicted were the proof; the magic users were the culprits. The Demon War's living memory amplified the fear, but the doctrine's load-bearing claim was *recent* — the disasters had been within witnessed lifetimes, and they continued nightly until the populace turned.

## The Doctrine of the Cold Hearth

**Public name**: The Doctrine of the Cold Hearth
**Critics' name**: *Wickedness to Combat Wickedness*

Core tenets:

- All magic is inherently corrupt and dangerous
- The Century of Sorrows proved that any magic inevitably leads to horror
- No exceptions — every magic user must be purified, bound, exiled, or slain
- *A cold hearth is safest*

The doctrine was deliberately flat: it refused to make distinctions between practitioner-classes. A wizard with a lifetime of scholarly study was no different, under the Cold Hearth framing, from a [[netharim|Netharim]] child who had just manifested her first reality-shaping. Both wielded magic; both were targets. The doctrine's *flatness* was its political strength — it could not be refuted by appeal to any specific magical tradition's lawful practice.

## Magic-User Slurs and Reclaimed Names

Modern magic users remember the insults of the era through preserved slurs. Most do not distinguish among practitioner-classes — they treat magic users as a single category, which was the Crusade's framing:

- **Demon-seeds**
- **Hearth-killers**
- **Breach-born**
- **Spellblights**
- **Shadow-hands**
- **The Bringing-Down**

Some have been reclaimed by surviving traditions; others remain unforgivable. Counter-mottos preserved by modern magical orders:

- *"Not every fire burns the world."*
- *"The hand that lights the candle is not the hand that opened the Breach."*
- *"We are not what they feared. We are what survived their fear."*

## The Hidden Truth

Every living magic user sustains the [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave|Weave]]. Mass death weakens it cumulatively. The death of a [[windspeakers|Windspeaker]] without a ready successor causes **structural damage to a conduit junction** — not a small increment but a load-bearing failure.

The crusaders believed magic was wicked. What they did not know was that they were cutting the living anchors of the world's primary defensive magic. The doctrinal argument — *"all magic is wicked because of the Century of Sorrows"* — was, ironically, almost the opposite of the truth: the lives of the magic users were the principal mechanism by which the next demonic incursion was being held back.

## Black Hand Intent — Diversion, Not Sabotage

The Crusade was engineered purely as a **distraction for the Spear search**. As the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]] approached, the Black Hand needed reduced scrutiny to conduct a coordinated search for the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]. Weave damage was an unintended consequence.

- **The Outer Hand knew nothing** of the magic-Weave connection. They executed the diversion as ordered, treating it as straightforward institutional manipulation.
- **The Inner Hand only suspected** a possible connection and could never prove causation amid the chaos. They considered Weave damage a possible secondary consequence — not opposed to it but not the operational goal. The Spear search was the goal.

### Plausible deniability

The Black Hand maintained deniability throughout by acting through existing religious institutions and pre-existing social tensions rather than through visible Hand cells. The resulting events are widely recorded as religious conflict rather than coordinated manipulation — *the Crusade* in popular history is the church-and-mob persecution, with no surface trace of Hand orchestration. Even modern anti-Hand investigators struggle to reconstruct the manipulation from available records.

## How the Crusade Was Halted

The Crusade was halted by what mortals perceived as **direct divine confrontation**: the gods of the participating churches **fully inhabited their [[icons|Icons]]**, making the Icons appear and act as living deities before the participating priesthoods and martial orders. This is the strongest form of direct Prime-Material divine presence available in Eadras — the gods cannot manifest [[icons#Avatars|Avatars]] (the Mythos cannot bear them), so full Icon-Habitation was the strongest possible response.

[[lirra|Lirra]] detected the Weave degradation first and recognised it as systemic rather than localised. She alerted the gods of the participating churches; once informed, they understood immediately what had been done in their names. The Icon-confrontation was decisive and left no ambiguity regarding divine disapproval.

### The bureaucratic oversight gap

The Crusade exposed a structural feature of the divine system: **gods are not continuously aware of all actions taken in their name**. Divine power is distributed through the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]], and routine prayers, rituals, and divine-magic requests are delegated through that system rather than processed by direct divine attention. Actions framed within accepted doctrine and routed through legitimate institutional authority can therefore escalate substantially before reaching the relevant deity's direct awareness.

The militant martial orders that conducted the persecution operated within their *interpreted* doctrine, channeled their requests through normal pipelines, and presented their actions as orthodox enforcement of theological purity. Their patron gods received the prayers framed as *"strengthen us against the world-ending power"*, not *"sanction the killing of every magic user in our reach"*. The bureaucratic delegation made the difference invisible — and it was this delay the Black Hand's operation exploited.

### Punishment and Reparations

In the aftermath, the gods imposed direct institutional consequences on the participating churches and martial orders:

- **The Silent Century** — participating priesthoods and martial orders were **stripped of divine power for one hundred years**. The Silent Century is the canonical reference case of mass divine sanction in Eadras. Modern theology treats it as the load-bearing precedent for the principle that *institutional authority does not guarantee alignment with divine intent*.
- **The [[knights-of-lirra|Knights of Lirra]]** — each participating order was required to donate **one-third of its membership** to form a new order dedicated to the protection of magic users as bodyguards, companions, and a unified martial force. The Knights of Lirra draw power from their original gods *plus* [[lirra|Lirra]]. The Order persists into the modern era as a small but doctrinally load-bearing reminder of the Crusade's lesson.

The *Cold Hearth* doctrine was formally anathematised by the participating churches. Modern theology treats the Demon-War-equates-all-magic argument and the Century-of-Sorrows-equates-all-magic argument as the canonical examples of *theology made fear's argument*.

## A Political Revolution Against Tower Rule

The Anti-Magic Crusade was not merely religious persecution. It was also a **political uprising against [[windspeakers|Windspeaker]] tower rule** — long-simmering resentments that the Black Hand weaponised alongside the religious framing.

Before the Crusade, during the **High Weave Age** (the **Age of the Spires**), the [[conduit-cities|Conduit Towers]] were ruled by Windspeakers — the [[netharim|Netharim]] anchors of the conduit junctions, who in that era exercised inter-junction political authority through their command of the Spire-Step network. Seven Windspeakers each oversaw a paired tower-domain, traveling between conduit termini through Spire-Step teleportation without abandoning their anchoring duty.

Resentments accumulated across the High Weave Age:

- Kingdoms chafed under tower independence and the Windspeakers' transcontinental authority
- Mage guilds excluded from tower privilege resented magical monopolies
- Temples of other gods worried about Lirra's parallel power
- Common folk feared rulers who never aged in ordinary ways, who spoke with a goddess, and who could vanish in light
- Because the life-force sustaining the Weave was secret, [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] could not publicly justify why Windspeakers specifically deserved protection; secrecy became evidence of conspiracy

The Black Hand fused these political resentments with the anti-magic panic. Anti-tower nobles could publicly cry *"Are we to be ruled by demon-handed magicians in shining towers?"* — turning institutional grievance into holy violence. The result was the **Fall of the Spirelords** and the **Shattering of the Tower Thrones**.

## The Three Cut Anchors

Three [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] were killed during the Crusade — but these three could not have been killed by ordinary fire, weapons, or magical force. Their innate capacity and their direct binding to the conduit junctions made them extraordinarily difficult to harm by mundane means.

Each assassination required an **enormous coordinated operation**:

- Approximately 100 coerced magic users (wizards, priests, Netharim, Lexanari) channelled into the killing-ritual
- Dozens of assassins and guards
- Poisons and curses prepared specifically for Windspeaker resonance signatures
- A massive frontal assault serving as distraction for the precision strike

The three slain Windspeakers are remembered in [[lirra|Lirran]] secret records as the **Three Cut Anchors**. Their public memorial names — the **Bell Martyr**, the **Burned Anchor**, and the **Silent Drowned** — describe the simplified or symbolic appearance of their deaths; the true severing happened at the binding level, where the specialised **Unanchoring** rite cut the Windspeaker's connection to the Weave at the structural level.

A hidden Lirran record from the era: *"The western stars vanished one by one, though their bodies still breathed. Then we knew the towers were worse than prisons."*

## The Thousand-Year Dimming

The three vacant anchors caused catastrophic damage. **Six [[conduit-cities|Conduit Towers]] were destroyed** in total — the two termini of each of the three broken junctions — and magical potency across Eadras collapsed for a thousand years. No mortal spell above the **Fifth Circle** could be reliably cast.

The Spire-Step network shattered. Tower-to-tower governance collapsed. Cities formerly connected instantly were isolated by months of sea and road travel. Political alliances failed; trade collapsed; magical supplies could not move; refugees were trapped.

See [[thousand-year-dimming|the Thousand-Year Dimming]] for the full account of the millennium-long magical dark age that followed.

## The Ethical Dilemma

Secrecy did not fail at the level of revelation — the Black Hand never *knew* the truth, only a very few suspected, and even they could not establish a positive causal link amid the chaos. The uncertainty (*"Was it the magic users? The Windspeakers? The general slaughter?"*) continues to protect the Weave today.

But the secret nearly killed when only the enemies of the Weave knew enough to suspect. A modern [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] reform vow attempts to resolve this dilemma:

> *"Never again shall secrecy feed the pyre."*

The core debate among Order Sharanel theologians:

- *"The secret must remain sealed. Knowledge kills."*
- *"The secret killed when only our enemies knew it."*
- *"It is our life. We have the right to know what it feeds."* (the rank-and-file magic-user faction)

## Aftermath: The End of Open Windspeaker Rule

The three slain Windspeakers have **never been reborn**. Their anchors remain vacant. The damaged conduits have regenerated over the millennia but are **still not at full power** without living Windspeakers to regulate them. The world's magical defenses remain permanently diminished at those three junctions.

After the Breaking, the Elder Gods permitted [[lirra|Lirra]] to identify and bind new Windspeakers as the conduit-junction succession required — but the old open political rule of Windspeakers over the towers was not restored. Modern Windspeakers are typically hidden, advisory, or partially restored rather than openly ruling.

A modern lament:

> *Once the Spirelords crossed the world between heartbeats.*
> *Now we send ships and pray the storms remember mercy.*

The Crusade marked the permanent end of open Windspeaker political rule and left deep cultural, political, and metaphysical scars that still shape Eadras today.
