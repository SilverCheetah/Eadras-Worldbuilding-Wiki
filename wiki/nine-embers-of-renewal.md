# The Nine Embers of Renewal

**Summary**: The Nine Embers of Renewal — the original nine dragon progenitors created by [[ixthal|Ixthal]] in the First Great Flare — **all ascended to godhood** in time, becoming the first generation of dragon gods. Three are publicly named in the modern pantheon: Cindirith, Davian, and Koruss. The other six are remembered only in draconic esoteric tradition.

**Sources**: notes on various topics 1.txt; user direction 2026-05-06; Playground generation 1st part.txt; Dragons.txt; Addition — Sourceflare manifestation (dragons).txt; VerdantScarAndArathCataclysm_01_InWorld_Facts.txt.

## The canon

The First Great Flare manifested as **nine distinct eggs**, each corresponding to a unique draconic archetype. From these hatched the **Nine Embers of Renewal** — also called the **Nine Progenitors** — ancestors of every later dragon and founders of the nine color clans. (See [[dragons]] and [[ixthal]] for the creation event.)

In the long ages that followed, **all nine of the original dragons ascended to godhood**. Their ascensions were spread across the deep dragon era, before the rise of humanoids and well before the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. They are the eldest dragon gods and the foundation of the [[gods-of-eadras#Dragon Gods|dragon-god strand of the Eadras pantheon]].

Their ascensions were **legitimate** — through means consonant with normal Ascended Mortal pathways (sustained Source-only worship, divine notice, demonstrated cosmic significance) rather than through the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]]. They are full Ascended Mortal Gods, not Unrestricted Elder Gods, and they hold their places within the divine compact.

## Why are six unnamed?

The three publicly named dragon gods (Cindirith, Davian, Koruss) are those whose mortal followings persisted into the modern era — they are **actively worshipped**, have iconography and clergy, and appear in the standard pantheon listings used by Eadras' temple traditions.

The other six progenitors **withdrew** — into the deep places of the world, into the Source itself, or into draconic-only spheres of divine activity. They are present in **draconic esoteric lore** (in Ratharil, in clan elder memory, and in the Sealed Hall of First Flame) but absent from public mortal religion. To most mortals they are simply not part of the pantheon. To dragons they are foundational.

The reasons for their withdrawal are not consistently recorded across the clans. Different draconic traditions give different accounts:

- Some hold that they were disturbed by the rise of humanoid sapience and chose to retreat from a world they did not recognize as theirs
- Some hold that they regard divine portfolios in the humanoid pantheon as beneath them
- Some hold that their nature is so purely draconic that mortal worship cannot reach them
- Some hold that they continue to act on Eadras through the Source itself, never mediated through a worshipping mortal

None of these accounts is definitively confirmed.

## Tarloth and Cindirith

The dragon **Tarloth the Cruel**, who used the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] to ascend, was specifically the **mate of Cindirith the Magnificent** — the eldest of the Nine, First God of Dragons, the Platinum, the highest-standing of the original progenitors (User direction 2026-05-06). The detail makes her grievance bite differently than a generic denied-ascension story: she was the unascended consort of *the Eldest*. After Cindirith's ascension she sought to follow him into divinity, was denied the legitimate path, and used the Spear to force the issue. The Elder Gods imprisoned her precisely because she became an Unrestricted Elder God outside the compact — a status the Nine had not taken. (See [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#Tarloth the Cruel — a dragon|Spear → Tarloth]].)

The legitimate / illegitimate distinction matters. The Nine ascended through divine notice and proper consensus; Tarloth attempted the forced shortcut. The pantheon's recognition is correspondingly different: the Nine are honored, Tarloth is jailed.

How Cindirith himself feels about his imprisoned consort is **open canon**, and is the kind of question that has not been recorded in any Tome chapter — the Eldest does not speak on the matter, and dragon historians have learned not to ask.

## Dragon scripture of the first hatching

A short hymn preserved in draconic religious tradition recounts the first flare and the nine eggs. Its language reflects the older draconic register — sparse parallel construction, Source-and-shell imagery, no claim of dragon agency in the kindling itself:

> *After the long hunger of the world, when fern and scale had returned beneath the Source,*
> *Ithren climbed the ashless height and opened his remaining eye.*
> *He did not command the sun, for the Source is no lantern in a servant's hand.*
> *He pleaded in the language of endings endured.*
> *The Source flared.*
> *Nine eggs burned and did not break.*
> *Nine shells sang and did not melt.*
> *From them came claw, wing, horn, and word.*

*Ithren* is the elder-draconic name for [[ixthal|Ixthal]], preserved in this scripture as the Arathi spoke it before the post-Cataclysm folk-corruption to *Ixthal* took hold. Most modern dragons reciting this hymn do not know that the figure named is Ixthal; in mainstream draconic religion the line is read as a sacred mystery about *the brightness before dragonkind*, with the agent unspecified. Among the very few esoteric initiates — [[ratharil#The Sealed Hall of First Flame|Sealed Hall]] custodians, certain Gold archivists, the deepest Silver memory-keepers — the identification is explicit.

The line *"he pleaded in the language of endings endured"* is doctrinally important: it preserves, even in the public version of the hymn, the truth that the kindler had to ask. The Source was not his to command. He asked, in a language only the survivor of an extinction would know how to speak, and the Source answered. (sources: Playground generation 1st part.txt; VerdantScarAndArathCataclysm_01_InWorld_Facts.txt)
