# Worldbuilding Needs

**Summary**: A forward-looking development punch list — areas of the setting that are **thin, undefined, or missing** and would benefit from a future ingest, raw source, or dedicated session. Sister page to [[open-questions]] (which catalogs open canon already flagged on existing pages); this page covers what we **haven't written yet** rather than what we wrote and left open.

**Sources**: Audit of all wiki pages, 2026-05-01; refresh 2026-05-09 (folding in 2026-05-08 Sarathim/architectures/eras ingest, 2026-05-09 magic-system rework + cultural-drinks tree + Divine Bureaucracy + Pillar Magic).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-09.

---

## How to use this page

- Each section names a **gap** the setting could fill.
- Items are **forward-looking** — they describe what *would benefit from canon*, not what *is broken in canon*.
- Where a sister page exists, the bullet links to it; otherwise the entry stands alone.
- Items move from this page to specific wiki pages as canon is generated.
- Entries are roughly grouped from most-likely-needed-soon (race detail, present-day political geography) toward bigger speculative arcs (cosmological mysteries, deep-time secrets).

## ~~Magic-system rework — make-it-mine pass (planned)~~ **COMPLETED 2026-05-14**

The 2026-05-09 ingest replaced the Galloway-derived Sorcerer order with the [[netharim|Netharim]] and the standalone Druid order with a [[druids|sub-priesthood]] of [[qoxli|Qoxli]] / [[sylph|Sylph]] / [[nox|Nox]]. The downstream make-it-mine pass was deferred at that time and **completed on 2026-05-14** in response to the *fully expunge all deprecated sorcerer/Weavespinner references* directive.

Resolutions:

- **The Weave's fate** — Resolved. The Weave is **a purely defensive cosmological structure** that carries Eldritch and is **sustained by the lives of every magic user**. See [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave]].
- **Sorcerer order** — Resolved. Retired; replaced by [[netharim|Netharim]], who are the members of [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]. *Sorcerer* is no longer canon as a practitioner-class name.
- **Weavespinners** — Resolved. Renamed to **[[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]** and reframed as Netharim of exceptional innate capacity who anchor the conduit junctions. The full Weavespinner-specific mechanics (Burning Passage, transmuted-mortal selection by Voroik, etc.) are retired.
- **[[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]** — Resolved. Persists as the institution; the members are now Netharim, not Sorcerers. The Order's founding, Tower structure, and historical role are all preserved with terminology updated.
- **The Sorcerer Crusade** — Resolved. Renamed to **[[anti-magic-crusade|the Anti-Magic Crusade]]** and reframed as a crusade against all magic as wicked (not just one practitioner-class).
- **Lirra's role** — Resolved. She remains the goddess of magic; her direct involvement now generalises across all magical orders rather than tying specifically to the (retired) Sorcerer class.
- **[[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] Crusade exception** — Preserved as canon under the new (Anti-Magic Crusade) name.
- **The four deprecated pages** ([[dashar]], [[weavespinners]], [[heart-of-the-goddess]], and the historical *order-sworn-sorcerers* stub) — Resolved as deprecation stubs pointing to the new canon, with the *order-sworn-sorcerers* concept elevated to a live page at [[order-sworn-netharim|Order-Sworn Netharim]].

No outstanding magic-system rework remains. Future canon develops on top of the new framework rather than rebuilding it.

## Modern present-day political geography

The Eadras setting has rich pre-Cataclysm history but the **modern political map remains thinly populated** despite the 2026-05-08 expansion (Amunorian, Bold Conduit Republic, Orden Conduit Compact, Azure Basin's six sub-zones, Tariq al-Honor):

- **Modern nations and kingdoms beyond the named ones** — many continents and regions still lack any named polities. The plate-tectonics map work in progress (memory: `project-map-development.md`) will eventually anchor this; until then much of the modern political world is still a blank canvas.
- **Conduit cities** — [[conduit-cities]] documents the typology and now names [[bold|Bold]] as one terminus, but **the other thirteen** modern conduit cities remain undefined. The Far-Kin partner of Bold's conduit is also undefined.
- **Trade routes, leagues, alliances beyond the trade-triangle** — the [[orden|Orden Conduit Compact]] / [[bold|Bold Conduit Republic]] / [[azure-basin|Azure Basin]] trade-triangle is now defined; what other large trade structures exist (overland silk-road analogues, sea routes, magical cargo systems) is open.
- **Borders, rivalries, current wars or cold conflicts** — only sketched.
- **Population centers in modern Arathyn / the Arathalassic Sea / the surviving continents** — none currently described.
- **The kingdom whose capital is Bold** is named (Bold Conduit Republic) and the city-state structure is documented; what borders the Republic, what its trade partners on land are, and what the kingdom's territorial extent actually is remain open.

The 2026-05-09 ingest deepened Beast-Folk substantially with the lifestyle / personality / off-work mode treatment, the wagon-pool with sealed Plane-of-Water portal, and the generational acculturation framing. **Remaining gaps**:

## Cultural drinks (largely defined 2026-05-09; open extensions)

The 2026-05-09 ingest produced [[aqua-vitae]], [[elfen-blossom-wines]], [[goblin-ancestor-liquor]], [[orcish-drinks]], [[taran-venom-aged-liquor]], [[orden-whale-honey-mead]], and [[velrathi-tea-culture]]. **What remains thin**:

- **Drinks of the [[katharim|Katharim]]**, the Rathar-descended undertaker priesthood. Currently undocumented — possibly cultural fasting traditions instead?
- **Drinks of [[halflings|halflings]]** — currently undocumented. Could be a homebrew tradition fitting the "everyone's an inventor" gnome cousin.
- **Drinks of [[dragons|dragons]] and [[unicorns|unicorns]]** — biological-and-cultural: do they drink alcohol at all? Dragons might brew something draconic; unicorns probably do not.
- **Drinks of the Five Modern Oceanic Races** — only the Marrow's whale-honey-mead-share is documented; the other four oceanic races' drink cultures are open.
- **Drinks of the modern human kingdoms** ([[amunorians|Amunorian]], [[bold|Bold]], [[orden|Orden]] beyond the mead, the [[azure-basin|Azure Basin]] sub-zones).
- **Drinks of the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]]** — Gaia-local drink traditions of exiled Tarans, possibly preserving venom-aged tradition with local fauna venoms.

## Deities — Ascended Mortal slots still open

Per [[religion-in-eadras]], Ascended Mortal gods actively desire worship and have the most fervent faiths. The current named roster:

- **Dragon**: Cindirith, Davian, Koruss
- **Unicorn**: Starwyn, Faylune, Valenor
- **Elfen**: Nirya, Lirael, Aramet
- **Velrathi**: Vexara
- **Tarran**: Korvath
- **Human**: Duana, Elaran, [[baldaran|Baldaran]]
- **Gnomish**: [[inki|Inki]]
- **Shada'din**: [[din-shadar|Din'shadar]]
- **Three Last Lights** (Arathi): Auralis, Thamior, [[ixthal|Ixthal]]

User has flagged that **the full roster has not yet been defined**. Likely additions, by lineage:

- **More Velrathi ascended** (currently only Vexara) — gods of memory, of secrets, of magical theory
- **More Tarran ascended** (currently only Korvath) — gods of governance, of formal ritual, of the imperial style
- **More Human ascended beyond knowledge / hope / spellcasting** — patrons of agriculture, of seafaring, of love, of mercy, of revolution
- **Goblin / orc / halfling / merfolk / dragonfolk / fae / etc. ascended** — currently none named for any of these races
- **Beast-folk ascended** — likely Sylph-aligned given the chaos-infusion lineage, but no specific Beast-folk ascended-mortal god is named

For each new ascended mortal god, canon should also specify: pre-ascension mortal life, the deed that triggered ascension, domain, symbol, and standing relationships with the older gods.

**Also new (2026-05-09)**: which Ascended Mortal Gods run which **Bureaus** of the [[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]]. [[baldaran|Baldaran]] is canonically *not* the executive of the [[divine-bureaucracy#Bureau of Records|Bureau of Records]] (the Tome was built as his alternative to it). The actual executive assignments across the 21 named bureaus are open.

## Religion — institutional detail

[[religion-in-eadras]] sets the high-level pattern. Specific institutions are mostly undefined:

- **Major temples** of each Elder God — none named (besides allusions to Lirran towers and the Ratharil Temple)
- **Major orders, monastic traditions, missionary practices** — undefined
- **Pilgrim routes** — undefined; potential candidates include Arathyn, the Verdant Scar, Ratharil, Bold
- **Religious calendar** — undefined; festival days, fasting cycles, sabbaths
- **Theological schools and heresies** — undefined; the post-Weave divine-vs-arcane split alone implies several centuries of doctrinal development
- **Priestly hierarchies, sacraments, vestments, holy texts** — none specified
- **Druidic order training institutions** — newly relevant after 2026-05-09 sub-priesthood reframe; whether druids of Qoxli, Sylph, and Nox train together or separately is open. → [[druids#Open canon]]

## Cuisine and culture (forward-looking sister to food-and-drink)

The [[food-and-drink]] hub is positioned for race-specific expansion. Drinks are now well-developed (above); food-side gaps:

- **Elfen-line food culture** (modern, post-Banishment) — Sea'tua-descendant cuisine, modern elf cooking, fae meal-rules
- **Tarran-line food culture** (modern) beyond Shada'din brewing — Gray Matriarchy table customs, human regional cuisines, halfling cooking
- **Velrathi-line food culture** (modern) — gnome and goblin Grug-inheritance, modern orc Orug-practice survivals (if any)

Beyond food: clothing traditions, music, dance, courtship, funerary practice are uneven across the wiki and would benefit from per-race development. **Architecture** is now well-developed for the three progenitor races (Velrathi spell-lattice, Taran three-tradition, Elfen song-shaped) per the 2026-05-08 ingest.

## Languages

No canon currently exists for:

- The **Arathi language** (script reportedly preserved on the Golden Age Map cartouche; otherwise undefined)
- **Draconic** has gained some detail (triconsonantal-root agglutinative abjad; Rhtrakhadara as Draconic for Ratharil; the 2026-05-02 transcription rule for multi-letter consonants), but full vocabulary and grammar are undeveloped
- **Mortal trade tongues**, **Common**, regional dialects
- **Religious / liturgical languages** — likely several
- **Velrathi, Tarran, Elfen progenitor languages** — referenced but undocumented
- **Goblin language** — referenced (the in-language name for [[goblin-ancestor-liquor|Ancestor Liquor]]) but undeveloped
- **Names and naming conventions** for each race — partially documented (see [[naming-conventions]] for the progenitor races)

The new **[[true-language|Language Hierarchy]]** (True / Primordial / Divine / Arcane) per the 2026-05-09 magic-system rework provides metaphysical grounding for the Arcane Language used by [[wizards|wizards]], but the in-fiction working details of the Arcane Language (specific incantation patterns, runic notation, common formulas) remain open.

## Plot threads positioned for play

If the setting is run as an RPG, several threads are explicitly *active and unresolved* at the modern moment, and would benefit from canon scaffolding for table use:

- **The next [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]** — approaches; the Spear's window is open. Specific dates in-setting; what activates first; who is positioned to act.
- **Black Hand modern operations** — currently unsited; specific cells, blackmail networks, infiltrated institutions.
- **Sleeping Ancestors waking** — no elder troll has woken in 39,000 years; the dragons are uneasy. The premise alone is a potential campaign frame.
- **Verdant Scar's distributed regulating mind** — the only conduit-end without a Tower of Lirra. Anything that disturbs it has world-scale consequences.
- **Recovery of additional Arathi vaults** — the gnomish food-fabricator vault and the Lost Primer vault are the most prominent recovered examples; the ones that remain sealed are setting-defining.
- **The Closed-Time-Bubble Chaos Crusade campaign** (working title — slated for rename before publication) — flagship Crystalline Age splat campaign. → [[rpg/closed-time-bubble]]
