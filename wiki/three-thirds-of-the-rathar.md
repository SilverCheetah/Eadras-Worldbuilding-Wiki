# Three Thirds of the Rathar

**Summary**: The [[demon-war|Demon War]] shattered the [[rathari|Rathar]] people into three answers to catastrophe — one third died, one third chose the Veil of Forgetting and became the [[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]], one third refused forgetting and became the [[katharim|Katharim]] — with a fourth thread of pure survivors who refused both extremes.

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt; Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## The Breaking

The [[rathari|Rathar]] were engineered by the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] for **perfect, unflinching memory**. When the Demon Prince's invasion came, that perfection became their torment. One-third died screaming in breached libraries. One-third begged [[sylph|Sylph]] for relief and was transmuted. One-third refused both death and forgetting. The Rathar as a single people **ceased to exist on that day** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

What remains are their two divergent children, the ghost of what they once were, and the few pure bloodlines that hid in the deepest vaults.

## The Three Answers

### The Fallen Third

The Fallen Third died defending their libraries and Conclaves during the war. Many fell at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] alongside the [[orugi|Orug]], [[el-mer|El'mer]], and [[aetherins|Aetherin]]. Their casualty rolls are recorded in obsessive detail by their Katharim descendants in the **Eternal Archives of the Thirds** at [[kharathen|Kharathen]].

### The Laughing Third

When demonic horror became unbearable replay in minds engineered to remember everything, one-third of the Rathar **knelt before [[sylph|Sylph]]** and begged: *"Let us forget enough to live."*

> The transmutation was not gentle scholarship refined into something new. It was ecstatic, chaotic, and final. The **Veil of Forgetting** settled over them like warm fog at dawn — dulling the endless replay of horror while sharpening every heartbeat of the present.

They emerged as the [[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] — unified in blood, origin, and the quiet knowledge that they chose joy over perfect recall. They call themselves the **Laughing Third**, the **Veiled**, or simply **Sylph's Kin** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

Their core philosophy: *"Life is too short and too sweet to waste on what cannot be changed. Memory is a tool, not a chain. Laughter is sacred. Forgetting is holy."*

### The Weighted Third

The remaining third refused the Veil entirely. They took upon themselves the sacred, crushing duty of **never letting the dead be forgotten**. Across ten thousand years they became the world's preeminent undertakers, crypt keepers, and guardians of the silent places. They are the [[katharim|Katharim]] — the **Rememberers**, the **Weighted Third**.

Their oath: *"I will not bend the world with my own will. I will only remember, record, and beseech. Let the gods judge. Let the dead rest. Let the living choose."*

They also abandoned all arcane magic — having watched too many colleagues torn apart by their own perfect calculations during the war. They retain only divine magic channeled through priestly supplication. (See [[katharim]].)

### Pure Rathar Survivors

A small fourth thread: pure [[rathari|Rathar]] bloodlines that refused all three answers. They sealed themselves in the deepest underground vaults and continued the old ways in isolation. They are reclusive, fiercely traditional, and rare — living archives of the pre-war scholarly tradition.

## Why Three Answers Were Needed

The schism is now understood across Eadras as the Rathar's collective answer to catastrophe — **three responses to unbearable memory** in a people engineered for perfect recall. (source: Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt) The three answers are not contradictions but complements:

- The Fallen demonstrated **the cost** — that some prices cannot be negotiated.
- The Laughing demonstrated **deliberate forgetting** — joyful forgetting that *transformed trauma into communal laughter*. Their flyting tradition is the ritualized form of this transformation: emotional pressure release, [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] stabilization, and *anti-rigid memory processing* in a single cultural mechanism. (See [[flyting]].)
- The Weighted demonstrated **deliberate remembering** — *burdened remembrance*, becoming archivists of mortality and rejecting personal arcane will so the records remain unwarped by ambition.

Modern Eadras philosophy (especially among the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]], Katharim, and reflective Beast-Folk circles) treats the Three Thirds as the canonical model for how a people can metabolize trauma without losing itself entirely. The Laughing Third's thesis — *"Steel breaks. Memory breaks. Only laughter mends."* — is held up alongside the Katharim's *"Let no name be forgotten"* as **two equally necessary postures**, neither sufficient alone.

## Cultural Echoes

The schism is felt throughout modern Eadras:

- **Beast-folk Laughing Circles** are the joyous inversion of pre-war Rathar **Thought Conclaves**.
- **Katharim Memory Circles** are the direct, austere descendants of the same Conclaves.
- **Pure Rathar enclaves** still hold Conclaves in the original form, in sealed vaults.

A common saying captures the relationship: *"Some chose to remember the dead. Others chose to give the living reasons to keep laughing. Both are necessary. Neither is enough alone."*

The most poignant moments in modern Eadras occur when these branches meet — a raven-beastfolk bard's wild lament echoing through a silent Katharim crypt while gray-robed Rememberers stand unmoving, tears tracking silently down their faces.
