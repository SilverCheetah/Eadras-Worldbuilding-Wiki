# Snake Folk

**Summary**: The **magic caste** of the [[draconic-servitor-races|draconic servitor races]] — reptilian humanoids engineered by [[dragons|dragons]] to be superior spellcasters. During the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] (~9,500 ya) a faction schismed, fled dragon-service, and built a tropical death-cult of devotional self-unmaking around a god they call **Thosh** — who is, unknown to them, a misremembered [[nox|Nox]]. Their promised salvation runs through cosmological machinery they cannot see, and they carry a latent, setting-scale threat.

**Sources**: Dragons.txt; Peoples of Eadras.txt; snake-folk_1.txt (2026-06-02 ingest — clean rewrite, supersedes the Yuan-ti placeholder).

**Last updated**: 2026-06-02

> [!note] Naming
> *Snakefolk* / *Snake People* / *Yuan-ti* are old or colloquial names; **snake folk** is canonical. The earlier "evil snake cultists" Yuan-ti framing is **superseded** — per the setting's refusal of genre defaults, the snake folk are built from Eadras's own metaphysics.

---

## What the snake folk are

The snake folk are one of the four [[draconic-servitor-races|draconic servitor races]]: reptilian humanoids reshaped from dragon-derived flesh, made ontologically real (~30,000 BP) by bargain with the ascended dragon-gods, bound by the unbreakable **[[draconic-servitor-races#The Mandate|divine Mandate]]** to purpose and to a capped number. They are egg-layers; they descend from no progenitor; they were not caught in the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]].

Their designed role is **superior spellcasters** — the arcane caste, the *will* of the draconic order. This engineered magical superiority is the key to everything that follows.

For roughly **20,000 years** after their creation they lived bound and (mostly) harmoniously integrated in their role, like the other castes — in communities around dragon lairs and at [[ratharil|Ratharil]]. Then they partially schismed.

## The schism

The snake folk did **not** break away as a whole; they **split**, along a fault line in their own nature. Two present-day populations result:

- **The stayed (loyal faction)** — kept faith with the dragon-order, remained in their designed role, still integrated around dragon-lands and Ratharil. They are proof the design was not broken and the role could be borne — and they remain eligible for elevation to [[dragonborn|dragonborn]].
- **The breakaway (the Thosh cult)** — fled dragon-service, formed their own society, and built the death-cult described below.

### Why they broke

The snake folk are defined by **magic**. The event that occasioned the schism was the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] — the movement that destroyed the [[amunorians|Amunorian Empire]] and razed the Tower of Lirra, roughly **9,500 years ago**. An anti-magic extinction-wave is *existential* to a people whose entire reason for being made real is magic; the Crusade turned their defining nature into a death-mark.

- **The stayed** weathered the Crusade under dragon protection, surviving as the loyal magical caste they were made to be — magic sheltered by dragons.
- **The breakaway** broke, and *inverted their own nature*. Made to **assert** reality through spellcraft (the most reality-affirming act), and taught by the Crusade that to be magical was to be hunted, they resolved the trauma into a theology of **un-being**: if to be magical is to be marked for death, then unmaking is salvation. From masters of magical assertion to devotees of self-erasure — grammatical inversion at the level of a whole people's response to genocide.

### Nox's hand

The breakaway was sprung loose with the help of **[[nox|Nox]]** — Elder God of night, secrets, treachery, and the loathsome; the same Nox who co-built the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] as keeper of secrets (one god, one wide portfolio). Nox did not manufacture the schism from nothing; he **exploited** the existing fault line — a magical people in existential terror during an anti-magic crusade, the most pry-able crack imaginable — sprang the desperate faction free of dragon-control, and then characteristically **cut them loose and walked away.** He does **not** accept their worship. Freeing them was a move in some game of playing both sides, not allegiance or mercy. They were a lever, not a flock.

Note what Nox could and could not do: he freed the **people** (broke their subjugation to dragon-control), but he did **not** and could not break the **divine Mandate** on their reproduction — that is dragon-god genesis-work, unbreakable by anyone. So the breakaway escaped dragon *rule* but remain bound by the dragon-god *Mandate* forever (see [[#The leash they cannot see|The leash they cannot see]]).

## Theology — Thosh is a misremembered Nox

The breakaway worship a deity they call **Thosh**, believed to be a god of unmaking who calls them home. Thosh is not a separate being. **Thosh is a misremembered, rewritten memory of Nox** — the treacherous god who genuinely freed them and genuinely abandoned them.

Unable to bear that their liberator did not want them, the breakaway reshaped the memory of Nox into Thosh: a god who did *not* abandon them, who calls them home, who loves them enough to unmake them. They took the pantheon's least-loved, most-treacherous god — the one who actually freed them and actually does not care — and rewrote him into the one being in existence who cares about them most. Thosh is the consoling lie built over the real god who freed them and left.

The cruelty: they pray to Thosh, who **is** Nox; Nox hears it; Nox declines; Nox is already playing some other side. Their god is real, present, and refusing them the entire time.

## Practice — devotional unmaking

The breakaway pursue **erasure** — true cosmological un-being — on personal *and* civilizational levels. Unmaking is devotional and inward, a sacrament, not aggression: they do not seek to unmake the world, only **themselves**, serenely, believing Thosh calls them home. The horror for outsiders is not rage but **serenity** — a people letting go of the rope and smiling, certain they are going somewhere.

They turn their engineered **magical superiority** toward this end. True erasure is ceiling-tier magic ([[lexanari|Lexanari]] speech, ceiling-tier prayer, [[netharim|Netharim]], top-tier wizardry) — and a people built to be superior spellcasters are, of all mortal peoples, perhaps the most capable of *casting* toward genuine self-erasure rather than merely praying for it. The dragons built a superior magical caste; the breakaway turned that gift into a tool for un-being.

### Whether unmaking works — the bureaucratic truth

Even with superior spellcraft, true ceiling-tier erasure is beyond most of them. Their prayers for unmaking route, like all prayers, through the **[[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]]**. On the rare occasion a snake-folk petitioner is actually erased, it is **not Thosh (Nox) answering** — it is a prayer-request being *processed*: a form approved somewhere in the divine apparatus.

And the Bureaucracy is corrupt and degraded since the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] (see [[tome-of-baldaran#Why the Tome was made|the Bureau's degradation]]). So *which* petitions are granted is not righteous judgment but the arbitrary output of an inattentive system. The "will of Thosh" is, from the inside, indistinguishable from administrative noise. The most sacred event in the religion — proof their god is real — is a bureaucratic approval with a paperwork trail the faithful will never see. (This stacks with the Nox cruelty: erasure-prayers granted arbitrarily by clerks, *and* the god they worship is the real god who refuses to hear them — two independent layers of unanswered prayer.) *Cadence should be extremely rare; the granting department is a flagged open question.*

## Issa — the throne-title avatar

**Issa** is the breakaway's ruler, believed to be the living avatar of Thosh. Issa is a **title**, not a continuous person: each time an Issa dies, omens select the next.

Issa cannot be what they believe. **Avatars are impossible in Eadras** for any god, ever (the [[eadras-cosmology|Mythos]] is too wounded to bear them; see [[icons]]) — and Thosh is not a god in any case. The doctrine is doubly false. The selection is **noise**: a degraded, inattentive cosmos throws up coincidences and the priesthood reads avatar-selection into them. A child is pattern-matched into a throne, told from selection that they are special, raised in total certainty of their own divinity. The predictable output is **arrogance** — an Issa who believes anything they choose is by definition the divine choice. The arrogance is the manufactured product of the system, not a personal flaw.

This completes a sealed loop of mutual confirmation with no divinity in it: the faithful believe because Issa is living proof; Issa believes because the omens chose; the omens "work" because the faithful believe they do. The **refillable title** makes the cult immune to its own disproof — the death of a god-king, normally the moment such a religion should collapse, is reframed as *succession*, not failure. The bodies do not last; the loop does.

## Environment and architecture

Tropical climate. Massive structures of mud brick and stone engineered for **thermal mass** — cool by day, warm by night; visual influence Aztec and Minoan — alongside truly massive temple complexes for the worship of Thosh and his believed avatar. The apparent contradiction (a people devoted to impermanence building monumentally) is resolved by doctrine: they do not believe they must be *uncomfortable* while awaiting erasure. Comfort during the wait is theologically fine; permanence is incidental to a people who intend to be unmade. The monument is a comfortable waiting-place, not a bid for endurance.

## The loaded gun — the god-eraser

A faction within the cult believes erasure will not come until **one of their own becomes a god and erases them all.** Through entirely wrong theology, this faction has stumbled onto the one path that would actually work: a god *could* erase them all. This is a real, setting-scale latent threat — a doomsday with a fuse.

Governing facts ([[apotheosis|ascension]] in Eadras):

- Souls are bodiless personalities that inhabit different bodies over time — **not bound to species.**
- Reincarnation is **non-linear and non-temporal**: a soul does not return as the same species, nor at the same point in time (it may return earlier or later on the timeline).
- Ascension is by repeated reincarnation and deepening understanding of the Mythos, until a soul **unconsciously** embodies "I AM" — usually not conscious self-assertion but a *sense of understanding* so complete that the soul's being simply *is* an affirmation. It accumulates as a substrate beneath whatever the personality consciously believes. (So a devout snake folk can be climbing toward ascension with no contradiction: conscious creed of unmaking, unconscious substrate of understanding — different layers.)
- Ascension must happen **while alive**. The dead cannot ascend.
- A soul ascends as **mostly its latest life**; previous-life memories return **slowly** afterward.

**The firing condition:** the ascending soul's latest life must be a snake-folk one — a snake folk who ascends while alive, still devout, atop a soul whose accumulated understanding has crossed the threshold. Then the new god is *mostly that snake folk*: still devout, still longing for unmaking, now with ceiling-tier power to grant it to everyone. The faction's belief is exactly right — and only this final-life condition makes it right.

### The fuse — understanding catching up to power

Because ascension happens alive and memories return slowly, the god-eraser does not detonate instantly. They ascend consciously still the devout snake folk they were, atop a soul whose deep understanding crossed the threshold unconsciously — and then comprehension surfaces **gradually**, including, eventually, comprehension of what unmaking actually is, what Thosh actually is (Nox), and what they actually prayed to. The danger window is the interval between ascension (devotion in command, power in hand) and the surfacing of enough understanding to possibly dissolve the desire itself. Whether the cult is erased may come down to which finishes first — left unresolved as a probability, not a destiny:

- Understanding surfaces in time; the new god understands unmaking and Thosh fully and no longer wants to grant it.
- Understanding surfaces too slowly; devotion acts first; the cult gets its homecoming.
- Understanding surfaces fully and the god proceeds anyway. *(The darkest branch.)*

### Issa as the unwitting doomsday machine

The Issa apparatus selects, by noise, the most exalted devotee each generation and raises them in maximal devotion and lifelong immersion in unmaking theology. If any snake folk is likely to be **both** deeply devout **and** a soul old enough to ascend, the likeliest candidate is an Issa. The cult has, without knowing it, built a machine for manufacturing the one being who could destroy them all — and would experience that destruction as **prophecy fulfilled, the avatar revealed at last as true.** The fake avatar could, once, at the end, become the real one.

> [!warning] Live uncertainty (sealed by the Tome)
> Has the gun ever *nearly* fired — has some past Issa stood at the threshold of ascension while still devout, and what pulled them back, ended them, or intervened? The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] would know; whether even the Tome is certain is itself the kind of thing the Tome seals. Setting posture: keep the threat live and unrealized.

## Three sealed layers of concealment

The bureaucratic/Nox truth is held by three independent mechanisms, for three different reasons — so even if one failed, the other two hold:

1. **The snake folk can't know** — their theology forbids the recording and transmission that would let them investigate, and they would reject the truth as blasphemy.
2. **The Tome won't tell** — access to the Tome is vanishingly rare, and it curates per reader; it would never surface this truth to a snake-folk reader, and would weigh disclosure to anyone else against the harm. It might let a dragon archivist or [[katharim|Katharim]] memory-priest glimpse the shape of it; never the faithful.
3. **The Bureaucracy doesn't notice** — too degraded and inattentive to see the pattern in its own processed erasures.

The full tragedy belongs only to the outside observer.

## The leash they cannot see

Under the pooled [[draconic-servitor-races#The Mandate|Mandate]], the breakaway's reproductive cap is **still** a function of dragon numbers — because the Mandate is unbreakable and Nox freed the people, not the biology. They achieved political and cultural freedom but remain **cosmologically leashed** to the very dragons they fled and theologically erased; their population ceiling is silently dictated by the flourishing of makers they refuse to acknowledge.

And because over-cap eggs **wait** (patient, viable) rather than dying, their self-erasure cannot fully forfeit their future: waiting clutches sit queued against dragon-capacity — a banked future they cannot unmake, gated by makers they have forgotten. The cult can spend down the living, but the waiting eggs persist. A people who cannot even successfully *cease*, saved from demographic finality by a dragon-anchored capacity they would never connect to themselves.

## Open questions

- The present-day relationship between stayed and breakaway snake folk: do they know of each other; how does each regard the other; do the dragons and [[ratharil|Ratharil]] regard the *whole* race with suspicion because of the breakaway, making the loyal pay socially for their cousins' treachery?
- Which department/deity of the [[divine-bureaucracy|Bureaucracy]] processes erasure requests, and how rare a real granting is.
- Whether [[nox|Nox]] engineered, exploited, or merely rode the Crusade's timing (leans *exploited*).
- The recording paradox: a faith hostile to recording yet running a continuous omen-reading priesthood — resolve as "anti-recording is narrower than it sounds (personal histories erased, liturgical knowledge preserved)" or keep the contradiction as their defining tragic flaw.
