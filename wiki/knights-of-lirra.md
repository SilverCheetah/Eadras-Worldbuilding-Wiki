# The Knights of Lirra

**Summary**: The **Knights of Lirra** are a martial order founded as **reparation for the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]]**. After the gods inhabited their [[icons|Icons]] to halt the persecution, each participating martial order was required to **donate one-third of its membership** to form the Knights — a new order whose explicit purpose is the **protection of magic users** as bodyguards, companions, and a unified martial force. They draw power from **their original gods plus [[lirra|Lirra]]**: a dual-source covenant unique among Eadran orders, marking them as the institutional reminder that the orders which once persecuted magic users now exist, in part, to defend them.

## Origin

The Knights were established as part of the divine reparations imposed at the close of the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] (~9,000–10,000 years ago). The gods, after halting the Crusade through direct [[icons|Icon]]-Habitation confrontation with the participating priesthoods and martial orders, imposed three specific consequences:

- The **[[anti-magic-crusade#Punishment and Reparations|Silent Century]]** — participating priesthoods stripped of divine power for one hundred years
- **Doctrinal anathematisation** of the *Cold Hearth* framing
- **The donation rite** — each participating martial order was required to give one-third of its membership to form the new Knights of Lirra

The donation was non-negotiable. Orders that resisted were extinguished outright. The resulting Knights drew their initial membership from across every martial order that had participated in the persecution — a deliberately mixed body whose internal culture had to integrate the doctrines of many different gods who had until very recently sanctioned, or failed to prevent, the killing of magic users.

## Dual-source covenant

The Knights are **unique among Eadran orders** in drawing divine power from **two sources at once**:

- **Each knight's original god** — the deity their parent order had served, with that order's traditional sacraments and prayer-forms preserved
- **[[lirra|Lirra]]** — the goddess of magic and the Weave, added at the moment of the order's founding by direct divine decree

This is not the multi-faith tolerance of a secular guild. It is a structurally enforced **theological hybrid**: a knight of the Order who has lost touch with either source loses the ability to draw the full power of either. A Knight cannot serve only their original god and ignore Lirra; cannot serve only Lirra and ignore their original god. The dual-source structure is itself the reparation — *the gods who failed to stop their orders' Crusade work now share their orders' membership with the goddess whose Weave was wounded*.

In practice, the dual-source covenant has produced an unusual order culture. Knights tend toward the philosophical rather than the simply martial; theological reconciliation is woven into their training; cross-faith dialogue is a daily working practice rather than a special occasion. Knights are sometimes called *the only order that prays in two voices at once*.

## Function

The Knights' explicit purpose is the **protection of magic users**. In practice this has taken several forms across the millennia since the founding:

- **Personal bodyguards** assigned to high-value or high-risk magic users — particularly [[netharim|Netharim]] who have shown unusual reality-shaping range, [[lexanari|Lexanari]] candidates during their dangerous training period, [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] (when their identities are even partially known), and senior [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] figures whose roles cannot be safely concealed
- **Companions** of magic users on dangerous travel — particularly in regions where anti-magic sentiment still persists locally or where the Knights' presence has measurable deterrent effect
- **A unified martial force** that can be deployed to halt incipient mob violence against magic users before it reaches Crusade-class scale, and that maintains direct institutional channels back to the gods who imposed the reparations
- **Cross-pantheon liaison** with [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] on operational matters involving magic-user protection — a relationship that is functional, careful, and predates many other modern institutional alliances

The Knights' very existence operates as **deterrence**. Mob action against magic users today triggers Knights-of-Lirra mobilisation faster than it triggers any other institutional response, and the Knights' divine backing makes them very hard to obstruct.

## Membership today

The Order persists into the modern era. It has remained relatively small — the dual-source covenant is structurally demanding, and the donation rite has not been repeated since the founding (no further reparation-class divine sanctions of the Crusade-scale have occurred). New members are recruited from across the martial orders whose lineage traces back to the founding donation.

A knight's full title reflects the dual-source covenant:

> *Knight of Lirra and [original-order's god], Order of [original-order]*

This is sometimes shortened in everyday use to *Knight of Lirra* when the dual-source detail is not relevant to the immediate context.

## Doctrinal significance

The Knights are the **load-bearing institutional reminder** that the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]'s cosmological floor (the Mythos cannot bear Avatars) does not absolve gods of responsibility for actions taken in their names. The Crusade was halted by the strongest direct intervention the Mythos can bear (Icon-Habitation); the Knights are what the gods built so the failure of bureaucratic oversight could be partially addressed at the institutional level.

A Knight-of-Lirra training-text:

> *We are what they should have been. We do what they failed to do. We carry the names of the gods who failed and the goddess who was wounded. Both, together, every day, for the protection of those who could have been killed by our predecessors.*

- The Knights' **internal structure** (ranks, training stages, oath-form) — open canon
- Whether [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] and the Knights of Lirra maintain a formal joint command structure or operate as separate-but-coordinating institutions
