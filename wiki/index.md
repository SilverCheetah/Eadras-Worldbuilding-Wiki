## Welcome to Eadras

> *In this world, light and darkness cannot exist without each other.*
> *The presence of one makes the other bigger, deeper, and brighter.*
>
> Great civilizations have fallen. Terrible wars have scarred the land. Ancient evils still stir in the shadows.
>
> And yet, hope is not foolish here.
>
> It is precisely because the darkness is so real that every act of courage, every act of mercy, every choice to build something beautiful matters all the more.
>
> This is Eadras.

Eadras is **epic dark fantasy with earned hope**, not grimdark. The darkness is real. So is the light. Each makes the other matter.

## Cosmology & Structure

### The Omniverse
- [[omniverse]] — The totality of the Omniversal Creator's creation; all Mythoi and the central Heart
- [[mythos]] — What a Mythos is; how each closed plane-bubble is structured
- [[concordance]] — The vast outer plane at the center of the Omniverse
- [[nexus]] — The megacity at the center of the Omniverse; governed by the One Law
- [[bastion-of-light]] — The absolute center of the Omniverse; home of the Omniversal Creator
- [[omniversal-laws]] — Six universal, unbreakable laws governing all Mythoi
- [[axes-of-creation]] — Four opposing principles that govern the alignment of all created things
- [[chaos]] — The absence of reality; co-eternal with the Creator; surrounds and fills the gaps between every Mythos
- [[aberrations]] — Native Eadras life that grew up in residual chaos pockets where reality's structure failed locally; not Chaos with bodies, but reality grown wrong
- [[neverborn]] — Pre-ontological Chaos entity that aided Kudur-eṭir's forging of the Spear
- [[observer-and-naming]] — Reality requires an observer; naming requires a namer who is also an observer. Eadras runs slightly observer-hungry because the Primordial Duo sacrificed themselves into the Mythos and left no ultimate observer behind
- [[night-sky-cosmology]] — The stars visible from Eadras are not distant suns but other [[mythos|Mythoi]] seen through [[limina|Limina's]] distortion; the slow precessional cycle (5,000 / 10,000 / 26,000-year figures all extant) functions as a **cosmic lock**, not a recharge mechanism, gating the eras in which the Spear can fire
- [[declaration-principle]] — *Proposed.* Cosmological hypothesis that existence rests on the Creator's primordial declaration "I AM"; each lower tier of being a fainter echo, and Ascension the point where reality accepts a new declaration as true

### Eadras Internal Planes
- [[eadras-cosmology]] — Full internal structure of the Eadras Mythos and its nine planes
- [[spirits]] — Universal canon for the four non-mortal realms' native populations: shared featureless-androgynous true form, glow colour indicating realm and brightness indicating power, **shells** (uniforms in bureaucratic shorthand) as the source of all "type" variation, and [[back-rooms|Backrooms]] across every realm
- [[back-rooms]] — Network of interconnected institutional rest-spaces across all four non-mortal realms: lounges, locker rooms, dining areas serving mana-as-food, serene parks; styled after [[arathi|Arathi]] [[crystalmancy]] and [[magitek]]; spirits shed shells and roles here and interact as equals; mortal-civilization-inspired, divine-assisted creation
- [[planewalkers]] — Beings capable of traveling between [[mythos|Mythoi]]; Eadras-origin planewalkers are fundamentally 2–4× stronger than planewalkers from other Mythoi, by virtue of Eadras's intrinsic magical richness
- [[celestia]] — The highest plane; analogue to Heaven
- [[seraphim]] — Native rank-and-file celestial spirits of Celestia; workforce of the Divine Bureaucracy; the setting's "angels" when dispatched to the mortal world
- [[nyxar]] — The lowest plane; analogue to Hell
- [[devils]] — Native rank-and-file infernal spirits of Nyxar; office workers of the Infernal Bureaucracy; ordinary 5-foot red-skinned humanoids whose dramatic mortal-iconography forms (imps, pit fiends, etc.) are magical Bureaucracy uniforms; **explicitly not Demons**
- [[wyldglimmer]] — Bright reflection of the Prime Material; home of fey and positive spirits
- [[glimmerdark]] — Dark reflection of the Prime Material; home of darkness-tainted spirits
- [[limina]] — The connecting plane; the "air" of the Mythos through which all planes are linked

### Moons and Exile Realms
- [[four-moons-of-eadras]] — The four moons of Eadras: Gaia, Daj, Shali, Shani
- [[gaia]] — The Green Moon; largest of Eadras's four moons; lush and temperate; reflected across all three planes; place of exile for the three progenitor races
- [[daj]] — The Red Moon; second-largest; named for the Elder God of war

### Divine Constructs & Doctrine
- [[icons]] — Every god in Eadras operates through the Icon-and-Simulacrum mediation: one Icon on the Prime Material (the god's only Prime body and direct-Mythos-interaction anchor) plus one or more Simulacra in the spirit realms (institutional working presences). **Avatars are impossible in Eadras** — the Mythos is too wounded; this is the cosmological floor beneath the Doctrine of the Unheld Hand
- [[divine-containment-protocols]] — The Elder Gods' four-phase procedure for containing ascended Spear-wielders (Observation → Convergence → Pocket-universe imprisonment → Icon sealing); used twice (Kudur-eṭir, Xar'vion); core principle: *gods deceive gods*, containment is psychological and conceptual, not physical
- [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand]] — Foundational principle of divine noninterference
- [[divine-bureaucracy]] — The vast, ancient, notoriously dysfunctional institution that handles the day-to-day operations of divinity; entirely located within [[solarion|Solarion]] (which it calls *the Eternal City*); run by Ascended Mortal Gods with a bored-and-jaded celestial-spirit workforce; 21 Major Bureaus including Favor, Prayer, Miracles, Souls, Records, Catastrophe, and the Heavenly Host
- [[infernal-bureaucracy]] — The structural counterpart to the Divine Bureaucracy on [[nyxar|Nyxar]], headquartered in [[dreadharrow|Dreadharrow]] and staffed by [[devils|Devils]]; **terrifyingly efficient** where the Divine institution is dysfunctional; the horror of Hell in Eadras is precisely that the system works
- [[religion-in-eadras]] — Religious history and practice: dragon Source-only origins, the post-Weave theological turn, temple traditions, and how each tier of god receives worship
- [[baldaranic-council-cycle]] — The cosmic deliberation framework — the *Convocation of the Reopened Ledger* — through which the Elder Gods deliberate setting-shaping decisions; three named phases (Open Palm, Arathi, Demon War councils); institutional caution and doctrinal restraint as native register
- [[doctrine-of-open-palm]] — First-Rebrightening reformist correction to the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Unheld Hand]]: *the Hand remains unheld; it shall not be absent*; five mortal theological responses (Orthodox Unheld / Empty Palm Lament / Stewardship Compact / Wild Refusal / Bold Revision)
- [[three-bells-of-intervention]] — The threshold structure governing divine action under the Open Palm: First Bell (mortals act), Second Bell (institutions fail), Third Bell (continuity threatened); *permitted does not mean guaranteed*
- [[hearth-witness-mandate]] — Mortal and recently-mortal auditors permanently embedded in divine offices; observe *consequence*, not procedure; the recently-dead-service controversy
- [[petition-of-abandoned-circumstance]] — Formal afterlife procedure by which a soul may accuse the divine offices of negligence; high denial rate is structural rather than dismissive
- [[decree-of-shali-shani-ioa]] — Cosmic decree binding the gods to silence on unrecoverable cosmological endings (Chaos-unmaking, demonic consumption, spirit-destruction); the Curator-Not-Speaker Loophole permits the Tome to *show* what the gods may not *speak*; load-bearing kindness preserving mortal unselfconsciousness

## History & Events

- [[eadras-timeline]] — Canonical chronology under the locked 10my system; anchors all dated events from Cataclysm to present
- [[eadras-tectonic-history]] — Atlas of post-Cataclysm continental drift across one-million-year snapshots

- [[worldbreaker-mechanism]] — The deep geophysical mechanism of the Cataclysm: steam-flash cascade, crustal decoupling, polar stability, conduit anchoring
- [[pre-cataclysm-relics]] — Fossils, Stillbeasts, Ashseed Forests, and other fragments of the lost 70%-land world
- [[post-cataclysm-fauna]] — Abyssal Crownwhales, Vent Seraphs, Scaldwyrms, Crown-Eels, Lantern Dead, Pressure Echoes — the new fauna of the world the Drowning made
- [[great-northern-whales]] — Oceanic ecological engineers whose whale honey triggers drifting Life Blooms; the Merrow's "Gardens of the Sea," seeding reefs thousands of miles south
- [[bathyric-mandate]] — A magical bacterial hive mind, a Cataclysm side-effect; infests hosts, takes over by telepathy, regrows from a single cell; eradicated five times and returned five times
- [[arathi-golden-age-map]] — Illuminated Arathi cartographic record of Eadras a generation before the Cataclysm; Altea as Heart of the Conduits, named Termini and regions
- [[chaos-crusades]] — The two times raw Chaos breached the Mythos through Kudur-eṭir's old aperture (~6 mybp, ~2 mybp)
- [[great-exchange]] — Centuries-long mutual teaching between young dragons and young unicorns; foundation of the Council of 99 and the Treaty of Ratharil; source of the phrases *Walking the Dragon Path* and *Singing with the Unicorn Voice*
- [[firethorn-empire]] — The worldwide Tarran empire; ended in the Demon War
- [[demon-war]] — Triggered by Xar'vion summoning a Demon Prince; killed half of all living creatures; created the Weave
- [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves]] — The Final Wall / Threefold Oath; the united last stand of the Three Who Stood (Orug, El'mer, Aetherin) — total extinction in three days
- [[great-betrayal]] — The Tarran Emperor poisoned in bed by his Velrathi fiancée at the gods' demand; emptied the Firethorn Throne and turned the war
- [[great-banishment]] — All three progenitor races sent into exile in the three reflections of Gaia
- [[anti-magic-crusade]] — The persecution of all magic users ~9,000–10,000 years ago, in the decades immediately preceding the previous Grand Conjunction; engineered by the Black Hand as Spear-search diversion via the [[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]]; halted by direct divine Icon-Habitation; the Silent Century and the founding of the [[knights-of-lirra|Knights of Lirra]] followed; caused the Thousand-Year Dimming
- [[century-of-sorrows]] — The Black Hand's hundred-year ramp-up to the Crusade; engineered magical disasters (Vaelis poison cloud, Dun Lira undead, Threeforks chaos pocket, etc.) that turned the populace against magic
- [[knights-of-lirra]] — Martial order founded as reparation for the Crusade; each participating order donated one-third of its membership; dual-source covenant (original god + Lirra); the only Eadran order drawing power from two divine sources
- [[lost-zenith]] — The Black Hand's failed Spear-retrieval at the previous Grand Conjunction (5,000 ya)
- [[thousand-year-dimming]] — Millennium-long magical dark age following the Crusade; no spell above 5th Circle for 1,000 years
- [[grand-conjunction]] — The rare celestial alignment that activates the Spear; next window approaches
- [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar]] — How the Demon War shattered the Rathar into Fallen, Laughing (Beast-folk), and Weighted (Katharim) branches
- [[lost-primer]] — Joint Velrathi-Rathari expedition's early-High-Firethorn Arathi Deep Vault opening that recovered a foundational crystalmancy/magitek primer; vault later overrun by demons; **Grug attendants kept records** that passed to gnomes and goblins, canonical reason for gnomish vault-obsession
- [[eras]] — The four officially-supported playable RPG eras (Crystalline Age / High Firethorn / Demon War / Modern), with the Modern Era's two variants (Spear Era default; Second Falling alternate dark future)
- [[mythic-recitations]] — Canonical compendium of the recitation texts in which the major peoples of Eadras remember the three foundational catastrophes (Cataclysm / Chaos Incursions / Demon War); dragon, unicorn, humanoid, orcish, Katharim, and Tome scholarly voices; load-bearing canon for any future Eadran mythic-recitation material
- [[first-rebrightening]] — The present era following the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]; **FR dating** is semi-formal (Sharanel, Tome, Lirran clergy use it; common folk often do not); five characteristic features (post-Weave recovery, institutional doctrine of restraint, demon-trauma absorbed but not closed, magic-political reform, the Council Cycle as functioning framework)
- [[unreceived]] — Souls consumed body-spirit-AND-soul by demons; stolen from the cycle entirely; subject of five Eadran rituals (Empty Cot Vigil, Unreceived Ledger, Black Banner Roll, Laugh With No Echo, Ashless Shrines); anchored by *may theft be remembered*
- [[woundwatch]] — Proposed institutional patrol of the Demonfall Marches under the doctrine *The Wound That Must Not Be Fed*; beast-folk laughter-houses recognised as defensive infrastructure

## Races & Peoples

- [[peoples-of-eadras]] — Complete chronological listing of all sapient races and their relationships
- [[arathi]] — Ancient insect-reptile civilization; first masters of magitech; left no descendants
- [[arathi-servitors]] — Dozens of Arathi engineered servant lineages; only [[trolls|Trolls]] survived; named extinct lineage is the insectoid Household Servitors
- [[dragons]] — Nine color clans; secretly created by Ixthal; Treaty of Ratharil limits population to 999,999
- [[unicorns]] — Second sapient race; secretly created by Ixthal; defeated dragons; introduced psionics; **individuality is temporary, herd is permanent** — herd-deep memory predates and survives the Great Exchange's gift of personal selfhood
- [[herd-listeners]] — Rare mortals permitted by herds to translate unicorn wound-memory into mortal institutional language; recognised by the [[order-sharanel]] under the [[doctrine-of-open-palm|Open Palm reform]]
- [[unicorn-wound-keeping]] — Herd discipline that treats unrecoverable-knowledge as hazard rather than asset; *we remember the wound, not the weapon*
- [[circling-of-the-returned]] — Quarantine-and-reintegration rite for unicorns rejoining a herd after long straying; outcomes include sleep, exile, death, and long-wandering
- [[demons]] — Alien-Mythos energivores; existential threat to the omniverse; arrive only by summoning or breach

### Progenitor Races (the only three created by The Three; all banished after Demon War)
- [[elfen]] — First progenitor; wardens of wildlands; created by Mark; banished to Wyldglimmer Gaia
- [[velrathi]] — Second progenitor; masters of magic; created by Rand; banished to Glimmerdark Gaia (Veyr'Keth)
- [[tarans]] — Third progenitor; keepers of order; created by Melania; banished to Prime-like Gaia (Gray Matriarchy)

### Engineered and Descendant Peoples
- [[rathari]] — Velrathi-engineered scholars; fractured into three branches in Demon War
- [[orugi]] — Velrathi-engineered guardians; **extinct** at Final Wall; diluted blood in Orcs
- [[grugi]] — Velrathi-engineered maintainers/inventors; ancestors of Goblins and Gnomes
- [[el-mer]] — Elfen-engineered proto-elves; **extinct** at Final Wall; diluted blood in Elves & Fae
- [[sea-tua]] — Elfen-engineered oceanic wardens; created ~10M years after the Cataclysm; common progenitor of all seven modern sea peoples (Five Modern Oceanic Races + Kuo-toa + Sahuagin)
- [[aetherins]] — Tarran-engineered superior humans; **extinct** at Final Wall; ancestors of modern Humans
- [[mitans]] — Tarran-engineered tunnel-fighters and miners; common ancestor of Dwarves and Halflings; lost too many in the Demon War, in slow genetic-bottleneck decline ever since with deep-hold remnants the only living trunk
- [[titans]] — Tarran-engineered living war machines (25–30m); heaviest of the martial cluster; most fell in the Demon War, in slow-motion decline from bottleneck + devolution; feral remnant still roams Eadras, Gray Matriarchy keeps a small civilized breeding population
- [[katharim]] — Weighted Third of the Rathar; undertakers and divine-only priests
- [[beast-folk]] — Laughing Third of the Rathar; transmuted by Sylph's Veil; one unified people in many forms
- [[halflings]] — Surface-divergent Mitan subrace; defined by "The Change" (post-Demon-War racial wanderlust adaptation, *not* an ancient cycle)
- [[orcs]] — Honor-bound warriors; descended from Orug bloodlines

### Culture
- [[food-and-drink]] — Cultural hub for cuisine, brewing, and food-borne folklore across the races
- [[pre-empire-foodways]] — Distinct food cultures of the Elfen, Velrathi, and Tarran before the Firethorn Empire
- [[aqua-vitae]] — Universal alchemical solvent / catalyst, distilled from each race's signature drink; each race's variety retains the source drink's character
- [[elfen-blossom-wines]] — Family of fermented Elfen wines (clan-specific recipes); aged in sung living-wood barrels; magic de-alcoholization for children
- [[velrathi-tea-culture]] — Velrathi-discovered, Elfen-cultivated; ~500 ancient varieties; tea as cognitive-acuity tool; Tea Ceremony, Personal Preference, Meditative Practice
- [[velrathi-cognitive-frogs]] — Upper-class Velrathi custom of carrying brilliantly-colored frogs in glass containers and licking their backs for cognitive enhancement; deeply unsettling to outsiders
- [[goblin-ancestor-liquor]] — Goblin signature drink, harsh brimstone-and-tar high-proof spirit inherited from [[grugi|Grug]] underground brewing
- [[orcish-drinks]] — Saddle Brew (low-alcohol, communal-rotating, road-brewed for plains-water safety) and Stonefire (high-proof clanhold-distilled, wound sterilizer + [[aqua-vitae]] base)
- [[taran-venom-aged-liquor]] — Taran-distilled spirit aged with denatured animal venom; class-marked aging (6mo swill / 20yr noble / 50yr+ finest); subtle social test of true Taran identity
- [[orden-whale-honey-mead]] — Orden signature mead from northern-sea whale honey; Marrow-cooperated harvest with ivory-and-teak syringes; rite of passage for every young Ordon; shared with the Marrow
- [[tchoukou]] — Amunorian caravan-ration cheese (camel-and-goat blend, sun-dried, imperial-sealed); caloric backbone of every desert garrison and oasis-city tea-house; aged grades reserved for the imperial table
- [[goblin-tunnel-cheese]] — Goblin signature foodway; brie-blue hybrid aged in damp working tunnels; inherently regional (each tunnel's microclimate); prized internally, regarded as cursed by most surface-dwellers
- [[three-cultures-ethical-triangle]] — Philosophical configuration positioning [[goblins|goblin tunnels]] (live-and-let-live), [[amunorians|Amunorian caravan culture]] (pragmatic middle), and [[dragon-empire|Dragon Empire]] (authoritarian-virtuous) as three mutually-incompatible poles on the axis of liberty vs centralized harm-minimization
- [[bathing-customs]] — Cross-cultural compendium of communal-bathhouse traditions ([[velrathi|Velrathi]] origin → [[rathari|Rathari]]/[[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] propagation), [[orden|Orden]] cast-iron pots that double as kid-shelters, [[gnomes|gnome]]/[[goblins|goblin]] practice
- [[philosophy-of-contribution]] — Shared cultural value across the Velrathi → Grug → Goblin descent line; "every capable person contributes; freeloading is not tolerated"; Goblin work-gang rotation puts kings on shifts
- [[flyting]] — Beast-folk poetic insult duels as primary dispute resolution; descended from Sylph's transmutation
- [[orden]] — Modern seafaring clan-culture (Viking analogue); Jarls, High King, the Tale-Earning rite of passage; symbiotic alliance with the Merrow (Deep Friends / Fish Friends); **modern Conduit Compact** is their Hanseatic-style trade league of conduit-anchored cities
- [[amunorians]] — Modern human-led civilization (Renaissance-Italy + Ancient-Egypt + Islamic-Golden-Age fusion); self-image as true heirs of the Velrathi legacy; broken in the Anti-Magic Crusade; modern faded-glory diaspora across the Azure Basin
- [[house-sitharis]] — Wealthiest Amunorian noble house, sustained by the international drug trade; three internal castes (Clear Minds / Socialites / Blissed); always-Arissa/Arian-titled; doesn't play the political game; hospitality drugs guests as courtesy; "if your heart desires it…"
- [[clan-nightshade]] — Elven horticulturalist clan allied to House Sitharis for centuries; master drug-crop growers and quiet poison-makers; major bridge between elven and human society
- [[tariq-al-honor]] — Syncretic post-Firethorn Catholic-Islamic-machismo cultural-religious tradition; **La Fe de la Espina Ardiente** (Faith of the Burning Thorn); strongest in southern Azure Basin
- [[velrathi-architecture]] — The Velrathi spell-lattice tradition: dark Gothic cathedrals, every detail a magical focus, willingly-bound wardspirit (Stone-Bound, akin to a *genius loci*), spell-plate + donation box at every entrance
- [[taran-architecture]] — Three strict Tarran traditions: austere Roman military (army-controlled), Roman+Bagan+Javanese sacred temples (variable by religious order), Roman+Ottoman imperial buildings with **spell-crystal minaret towers** (signature feature)
- [[elfen-architecture]] — Song-shaped living-tree-and-fungi architecture: heart-groves, canopy walks, root-vaults, song-runes; NO crystalmancy (that was Arathi-only)

### The Four Magical Orders (current canon; 2026-05-09 rework)
- [[wizards]] — Scholarly arcane casters; mental focus + physical grimoires; Arcane Language tier; multi-source spell lists; personal-shorthand-encoded grimoires as prized treasure; mana-flavored spell ingredients shape and enhance workings
- [[priests]] — Divine practitioners who call upon their gods; daily capacity is granted by the **Divine Bureaucracy** based on the priest's current **Favor** (Neutral / Positive / Negative / God's Favorite); priests do not memorize spells; restoration timing is set by the deity's temperament (sunup / sundown / noon / midnight)
- [[lexanari]] — The Name-Bound. Innate-only mortal speakers of the Primordial Language. Enormous power, catastrophic failure mode (mortal minds can't hold deific complexity, so vague invocation kills); fills the niche the borrowed Druid concept used to occupy. Uses [[pillar-magic|Pillar Magic]].
- [[netharim]] — The "Little Gods." Intuitive reality-shapers whose power scales with the accuracy of their personally-studied internal model of reality; worldwide order, numerous, politically and scholastically active; fills the niche the borrowed Netharim concept used to occupy. Uses [[pillar-magic|Pillar Magic]].
- [[druids]] — Now a specialized sub-priesthood of [[qoxli|Qoxli]], [[sylph|Sylph]], and [[nox|Nox]] (no longer a standalone fourth order); self-sufficient wilderness-skilled mediators between mortals and the [[fae|fae]]
- [[pillar-magic]] — The shared underlying grammar of [[lexanari|Lexanari]] and [[netharim|Netharim]] magic. Five **Command Pillars** (Control / Transform / Create / Destroy / Perceive) + five **Substance Pillars** (Essence / Matter / Flesh / Mind / Spirit) = 25 canonical combinations. Per-Pillar mastery progression in place of spell points
- See [[true-language]] for the **Language Hierarchy** (True / Primordial / Divine / Arcane) underlying all four orders

## Factions & Institutions

- [[black-hand]] — Ten-thousand-year-old conspiracy serving Xar'vion's Inner Hand goal; Outer Hand pursues power via information, infiltration, and blackmail
- [[white-palm]] — Penitential order descended from Inner Hand defectors; protects Netharim and hunts the Black Hand
- [[order-sharanel]] — The singular ancient and most prestigious order of Netharim; sworn directly to Lirra; based in the Towers of Lirra; founded during the Demon War as Lirra's Weave-creation act transmuted 10,000 mortals (no progenitors) into the first Netharim
- [[kingdom-of-xanar]] — Xar'vion's pre-ascension realm; foundation of the Black Hand
- [[dragon-empire]] — Far-eastern modern-era polity ruled by a coalition of semi-divine [[dragons]]; comprehensive bureaucratic meritocracy with pictogram-and-hashmark skill ranks; free public education; Tang+Khmer aesthetic; authoritarian-meritocratic pole of the [[three-cultures-ethical-triangle|three-cultures triangle]]; founded by a post-Demon-War dragon-mercenary coup
- [[fae-da-nar]] — Hidden frontier society of [[beast-folk|Woodkin]] survivors who maintain coexistence with humans rather than confrontation; *Fae-da'Nar does not ask whether the individual is precious; it knows the individual is precious — then it asks whether keeping them alive will kill everyone else*
- [[four-laws-of-fae-da-nar]] — The four behavioural thresholds (don't frighten humans / don't interfere / oppose Shunned threats / obey Druids)
- [[the-shunned]] — Predatory races and groupings that opted out of the frontier compact; functional category (those whose practices have crossed the line), not a racial classification
- [[frontier-hospitality]] — Human-side of the coexistence compact (First Bowl / No Iron on the Table / Fair Measure Under Witness / No Mocking the Mask / Guest-Fire Peace); Peace-Brass and Guest-Bronze as marker metals
- [[threshold-cities]] — Five-stage growth pattern from Guest-Bowl Settlement to Severed City; five long-term endpoints — Faithful, Ceremonial, Severed Expansion, Ruined Caution, Relocated

## Artifacts & Objects

- [[runeswords]] — Eleven sentient Starmetal blades forged by Dao to maintain balance; alien-crystal hearts
- [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]] — The most dangerous artifact in Eadras; turns wielders into Unrestricted Elder Gods. **Alien to the Mythos** and outside the gods' direct jurisdiction (they cannot destroy or expel it). Wielded 3 times, hidden 4: destruction-illusions / time-displacement in the [[wyldglimmer|Wyldglimmer]] / reverse-time-bubble god-prison in the deepest [[glimmerdark|Glimmerdark]] / impaled back into the original First Breach Wound under [[arathyn|Arathyn]]
- [[worldender]] — The apocalyptic falling star pulled through Kudur-eṭir's First Breach when his shattered post-ascension mind reached uncontrollably back into Chaos; destroyed his Icon and shattered the planet
- [[worldender-crystal]] — Alien crystal Dao harvested from the Worldender; foreign to the Mythos; all fragments hoarded by Dao alone
- [[firethorn-throne]] — Melania's masterpiece from leftover alien crystal; vanished after the Emperor's poisoning; not in any known location
- [[hall-of-kings]] — Dao's extradimensional sanctum; houses all eleven Runeswords on a multi-tier dais; Door manifests in Firethorn Hold
- [[tome-of-baldaran]] — Sentient chronicle of all Eadras history and knowledge
- [[mana-metals]] — Mithril, Orichalcum, Kylum, Valthyr and the alloys built from them; Magisterium is the apex tier
- [[alien-objects]] — General principle: objects not native to the Mythos have peculiar properties (harder, stronger, weirder)
- [[goldenwood]] — Alien-origin tree species growing in scattered groves; the only documented alien-origin *living* organism in canon; extraordinarily durable, remains biologically alive after cutting for years, propagates from cuttings in a process closer to *continuation* than *reproduction*; the canonical example that alien-origin matter is not automatically hostile

- [[fallback-doctrine]] — Setting-wide principle: *no life-supporting system without mortal-scale fallback*; produces the Entropy Taboo and the [[fallback-doctrine#Festival of Broken Certainties|Festival of Broken Certainties]] and the [[vault-concordat]] itself
- [[unsealing-oath]] — Three-witnesses standard for opening a sealed vault: knowledge, consequence, the unborn
- [[absence-traditions]] — Formal honest-no-memory theology: *we do not remember, and this too is true*; refuses invented continuity where the record genuinely failed
- [[obsidian-sanctum]] — Sealed Arathi Deep Vault deep in Varkhul's Scorched Waste; the only sealed vault on dry land
- [[continental-ward]] — Strongest magical barrier on Eadras; encircles Varkhul 25mi offshore; multi-layer, frequency-shifting, inward-reflective; fed continuously by hundreds of casters from nearly every nation
