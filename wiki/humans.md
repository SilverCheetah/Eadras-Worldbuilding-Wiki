# Humans

**Summary**: The diminished, scattered descendants of the [[aetherins|Aetherin]] â€” Melania's superior shock troops who died almost to the last in the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. ## Origin

Humans are the natural devolution of the [[aetherins|Aetherin]], the [[tarans|Tarran]]-engineered superior humans Melania herself shaped as shock troops against chaos and demonic incursions (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt). After the Aetherin spent themselves at the Final Wall during the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the surviving diluted bloodlines became the scattered humans of today (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

The Aetherin were 3 meters tall, hale, and "perfect-human" in form. Modern humans are smaller, less long-lived, and lack their progenitors' martial gifts â€” but the basic body plan and the resilient spark remain (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

## Anatomy

Humans average roughly five and a half to six feet tall, far short of the three-meter Aetherin frame. They are biologically the most varied of the descendant races, ranging widely in build, coloration, and constitution depending on region (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt).

## Society

Humans are the most numerous and widely distributed of the descendant races in the modern era. Lacking the focused cultural inheritance of orcs (who kept the Orug guardian tradition) or beast-folk (who carry Sylph's Veil), human cultures are diffuse and vary wildly between regions (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

Human knightly orders speak of the Aetherin as "the uncles who bought our chance," with quiet pride. Pilgrimages to the [[tomb-of-the-last-guardians|Tomb of the Last Guardians]] at [[kharathen|Kharathen]] are common, especially among martial orders (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Magical Inheritance

Some scholars believe the resilient spark in modern humans traces faint power to the unified deaths of the Aetherin, El'mer, and Orug at the Broken Conclaves â€” that the metaphysical resonance of three races' selfless sacrifice helped anchor [[lirra|Lirra]]'s early [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] threads, and that humans who descend from those bloodlines carry a trace of that anchoring vitality (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

Notably, the [[runeswords|Runeswords]] of [[dao|Dao]] have repeatedly chosen human wielders in the modern era â€” alongside orcs, beast-folk, and the occasional gnome or elf â€” despite the blades originally being intended for the Tarran royal line (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

## Relations

- Humans share a quiet kinship with **orcs** and **beast-folk** as fellow descendants of the Three Who Stood (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).
- Humans share courteous and reliable trade relationships with **[[halflings|halflings]]**, who pass through human lands during their cycles of "The Change" (source: Halflings.txt).
- The exiled **[[tarans|Tarran]]** of the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] view humans as faded children of their lost masterpiece, the Aetherin â€” a perception that ranges from pity to contempt depending on faction (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).
