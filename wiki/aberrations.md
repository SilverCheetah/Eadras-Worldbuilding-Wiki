# Aberrations

**Summary**: Aberrations are creatures whose flesh, mind, or both formed under conditions in which the structure of reality could not be fully maintained — typically in the **residual chaos pockets** left behind by the [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]]. They are not beings *of* Chaos (no such being can persist inside a Mythos under the form-or-dissolve law) but **products of the interaction** between reality and residual chaos: matter and life that emerged where the imposed rules of the Mythos held only partially, and that therefore exhibit inconsistent, alien, and structurally incorrect properties relative to the world's normal rules.

**Sources**: Combined info dump 2 5-4-2026.txt; user direction 2026-05-04.

**Last updated**: 2026-05-04

## What Aberrations Are

An aberration is **not** a being of pure [[chaos|Chaos]]. Pure Chaos cannot persist as itself inside a [[mythos|Mythos]] — the form-or-dissolve law (see [[chaos#The Form-or-Dissolve Law]]) requires Chaos that enters a Mythos to either take temporary physical form or dissolve. Aberrations are not that temporary form either; they are not Chaos with a body, and killing them does not "release" anything Chaotic.

Aberrations are something stranger and more durable: **matter and life that originated under conditions where reality's structure was failing**, and that consequently grew up *wrong* — wrong in ways that go beyond ordinary deformity, mutation, or magical corruption. An aberration's flesh, mind, sensory apparatus, biological logic, behavioral patterns, or even relationship to time and space may exhibit **inconsistent, alien, or structurally incorrect properties** relative to the rest of the Mythos. (source: Combined info dump 2 5-4-2026.txt)

The defining feature is that aberration biology and behavior do not quite obey the rules the rest of Eadras's biology obeys. Cause and effect may be slightly off. Sensory organs may register dimensions that aren't supposed to exist. Internal anatomy may not be mappable to any organism's body plan. Reproduction, communication, motivation may all be conducted on principles that mortal scholars find hard or impossible to fully describe.

This is the in-universe origin of every D&D-style aberration creature on Eadras: mind-bending eyestalk-tyrants, brain-eating tentacled telepaths, gibbering many-mouthed swarms, tide-mind tyrants of deep water, dream-eating things from beneath the world. They are not foreign invaders. They are **native life that grew up where the world's rules were broken**.

## How Aberrations Form

Aberrations arise in regions where reality's structure has been **locally disrupted** — most commonly in the **residual chaos pockets** left behind by the [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]]. These pockets are remnants of the Crusades' incursions: places where the failure of imposed structure has been incomplete and persistent, where ordinary causality, identity, and form do not quite hold, even after the gross breach has been sealed and the visible Chaos expelled.

In a residual chaos pocket:

- Matter exposed to the local conditions develops in ways the rest of the world does not allow
- Living organisms that arise within or migrate into the pocket may incorporate the local incoherence into their own structure
- Eggs, seeds, embryos, and larval forms are particularly susceptible — early development is the most rule-governed phase of biology, and the most vulnerable to disruption
- Subsequent generations descended from such originals can stabilize the warped structure into a heritable form, producing an aberration species rather than a one-off horror

The process does **not** require active Chaos. The Crusades have ended; the breaches are sealed. What remains is the **standing distortion** in the pockets themselves — like a pond left scummed and curdled after the flood receded. Life that develops in the curdled water grows up curdled.

## Sources of Residual Chaos

The known and suspected sources of aberration-generating residual chaos on Eadras:

### The First Chaos Crusade pockets

The principal canon source. The First Crusade (~6 mybp on [[eldravath|Eldravath]]) consumed one-third of the Prime Material plus the entirety of both reflective planes before the Gods sealed the breach. Across that vast affected region, the Crusade left an irregular distribution of **residual chaos pockets** where the failure of structure persisted past the sealing. Some pockets dissolved over geological time; many did not. (source: Combined info dump 2 5-4-2026.txt)

These pockets are scattered across the modern continents (the post-Cataclysm fragments of what was once Eldravath and the surrounding Prime Material) and across the [[wyldglimmer|Wyldglimmer]] and [[glimmerdark|Glimmerdark]] (the post-Crusade reflective planes). Aberrations native to Eadras are concentrated wherever those pockets persist.

### The Second Chaos Crusade pockets

By analogy, the Second Crusade (~2 mybp) presumably also left residual pockets, though contained at continental rather than worldwide scale. These pockets are younger, fewer, and (depending on geography) may host more recent and more dragon/unicorn-themed aberrations — distortions of forms the Second Crusade's Chaos was trying and failing to imitate.

### Other potential sources

- **The First Breach itself** — [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir's]] aperture in the [[mythos|Mythos]] Shell. The Breach is sealed but the surrounding region of the shell is still scarred; whether that scarring qualifies as a residual pocket is undefined.
- **The microfracture network** across the Prime Material Plane's exterior shell (the [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa's]] *Gaps* — see [[arathi-cataclysm#The Healed Shell and the Microfracture Network]]). These are too small for entities of any meaningful size to traverse, but whether they produce localized aberration-spawning conditions on either side of the membrane is undefined and a likely Kuo-toa theological assertion.
- **The Cataclysm impact site** itself — the [[worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]]. Not a Chaos breach per se, but the place where the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] (a foreign-Mythos object pulled through Chaos) struck. Whether the impact zone produces aberrations or only ordinary post-impact monsters is undefined.

## What Aberrations Are Not

To be canon-precise:

- Aberrations are **not demons**. [[demons|Demons]] are alien-Mythos energivores from another Mythos entirely; they enter Eadras only by summoning or breach. Aberrations are *native* — they originated on Eadras, in regions where the world's rules failed locally.
- Aberrations are **not fae**. [[fae|Fae]] are descended from chaos-infused Sidhe, but the chaos-infusion was channeled through Sylph and produced a coherent lineage with its own divine patron. Aberrations have no divine patron and no coherent lineage; they are the products of structure failing, not of structure being patroned.
- Aberrations are **not undead**. [[necromancy-and-undeath|Undead]] are post-mortem reanimations of beings that lived under normal conditions. Aberrations developed wrongly from the start.
- Aberrations are **not mutations**. Ordinary mutations and magical corruptions occur within the rules of biology. Aberration "incorrectness" is at the level of the rules themselves — the organism is not breaking biological rules so much as obeying *different ones*.
- Aberrations are **not Chaos given a body**. The form-or-dissolve law's "temporary physical form" is what the Crusade-era visible-Chaos manifestations were (colors that hunted, dragon-and-unicorn body-horror that fought) — and those dissolved when the Crusades ended. Aberrations persist; the temporary forms did not. Aberrations are reality grown wrong, not Chaos wearing reality.

## Behavior and Encounters

Because aberrations grew up under different rules, they are difficult to predict, negotiate with, or fight using ordinary tactics. Common patterns observed in surviving accounts:

- **Communication failure** — many aberrations use signaling systems that don't map to spoken or telepathic languages used by Mythos-native sapients. A "conversation" with one may consist of disorienting impressions, fragmentary geometric intrusions into the listener's mind, or no perceptible communication at all.
- **Sensory mismatch** — aberrations may perceive and target dimensions, frequencies, or ontological categories that ordinary creatures don't experience. Hiding from them by ordinary means may not work.
- **Anomalous combat behavior** — a wound that should kill an ordinary creature may not affect an aberration, because the wounded "organ" wasn't doing what it appeared to be doing. Conversely, trivial-looking injuries may be lethal.
- **Reality-local effects** — an aberration's mere presence in a region may produce minor distortions: time slipping, geometry tilting, observers losing track of how long they have been there, persistent unease that resists naming.
- **Regional concentration** — aberrations rarely wander far from the residual chaos pockets that produced them. The pockets are their habitat in a literal ontological sense; outside them, an aberration's local rule-set may not be sustained, and the creature may weaken, behave erratically, or in some cases die.

## Theological and Scholarly Treatment

Aberrations are studied chiefly by:

- **Lirran arcanums** in regions near known residual pockets — particularly any Tower of Lirra near a First Crusade scar
- **Followers of [[nox]]** — Nox-aligned scholars track aberrations as part of the broader "loathsome" portfolio
- **The Order Sharanel** at the highest levels — pocket containment and aberration management is a Weave-adjacent specialty
- **The deeper [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] archives** — collected aberration taxonomy, encounter records, containment doctrine
- **The [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]]** — informally, in their fixation on the *Gaps*; the Kuo-toa's theological investment in the broken shell may include aberration phenomena though their interpretive framework is heterodox

## Open canon

- **Specific named aberration species** on Eadras — not yet defined in canon
- **The geographic distribution** of First Crusade residual pockets — not yet mapped
- **Whether aberrations can reproduce outside a residual pocket** — undefined
- **Whether the Cataclysm's crustal displacement** moved any residual pockets to new surface locations along with the rest of the crust, or whether the pockets are anchored to the deep interior like the [[seven-conduits|conduits]] — undefined
- **Whether divine intervention can stabilize a residual pocket** back into ordinary reality — undefined; may relate to [[ixthal|Ixthal's]] portfolio of "renewal of life after catastrophe"
