# Glimmerdark

**Summary**: The Glimmerdark is the dark reflection of the Prime Material Plane within the [[eadras-cosmology|Eadras Mythos]], home to darkness-tainted spirits and the underdark of Eadras.

**Sources**: Shadowdark.txt, Spear of Crystallized Entropy.txt, Internal Cosmology of the Eadras Mythos.txt, More corrections.txt, Extra large grok chat 5-9-2026.txt, spear-containment-diagnosis-and-the-alien-artifact-principle.md (2026-05-13 ingest, third-hiding god-prison and reordered plane-corruption sequence); Grok Chat Results 6-2-26.txt (2026-06-02 rename from Shadowdark).

**Last updated**: 2026-06-02

## Overview

The Glimmerdark is a **complete reflection** of the entire Eadras Prime — including its planet, all four moons ([[gaia|Gaia]], [[daj|Daj]], [[shali|Shali]], [[shani|Shani]]), and every other Prime feature. It is shifted into a dark, negative-energy resonance influenced by [[nyxar|Nyxar]], and serves as the home of spirits tainted by darkness — including the restless dead. It contains what would be called the Underdark in other Mythoi.

### The Deep Glimmerdark — near-infinite extent beyond the reflection

Like the [[wyldglimmer|Wyldglimmer]], the Glimmerdark is **not bounded by the reflected geography**. The reflection of the Prime Material occupies one region of the Glimmerdark, but the plane extends **near-infinitely** in every direction beyond that reflection (source: More corrections.txt). Beyond the Prime-reflected territory lies vast, indeterminate Glimmerdark expanse — endless tunnels and labyrinths, regions of pure negative resonance, dark realms within dark realms, jurisdictions of beings whose existence has no Prime analogue.

The near-infinite outer region beyond the reflection is called **the Deep Glimmerdark** (the dark-plane counterpart of the [[wyldglimmer#The Deep Wyldglimmer — near-infinite extent beyond the reflection|Deep Wyldglimmer]]). The plane names *Wyldglimmer* and *Glimmerdark* replaced the earlier D&D-coded placeholders *Feywild* and *Shadowdark* in the 2026-06-02 publication-prep rename; the older names are retained as aliases.

This means the Glimmerdark is **far larger than the Prime Material**, with the Prime-reflected portion serving as a kind of anchored shore from which the rest of the plane extends. The farther one travels from the Prime, the stranger and more dangerous things become.

## Dark Paths

The Glimmerdark has a system of portals called **Dark Paths** that allow quick traversal of the nearly infinite plane. These paths are corrupt and dangerous to use.

## History: Twisted by the Spear

The [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy]] was once imprisoned in the Glimmerdark (then called **Darkmirror**, the original dark reflection of the Prime Material). The Elder Gods used the deepest depths of the plane for the **third hiding** of the Spear — the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#hiding-3-reverse-time-bubble-over-a-god-prison-in-the-deepest-glimmerdark-after-darkhoof|reverse-time-bubble god-prison]] in which a volunteer [[dragons|dragon god]] was sealed with the Spear inside a single composite Icon, the prison wrapped in a reverse time bubble that accelerated subjective time vastly relative to the world outside.

Over the long centuries the Spear sat in the deepest Darkmirror, its presence corrupted the plane around it — **Darkmirror gradually transformed into the Glimmerdark**, tunneling deep into the ground and creating the twisted mazes and chambers of dark energy that mark the plane today. This transformation was unintended in scope but anticipated in kind: the gods knew the Spear's prolonged presence corrupts whatever plane houses it (the same mechanism by which Brightmirror became the [[wyldglimmer|Wyldglimmer]] during the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#hiding-2-time-displacement-in-the-wyldglimmer-after-tarloth|second hiding]]), and they accepted the corruption-cost as the price of containment. The corruption took millions of years; the plane we know today is the result.

The Spear is **no longer in the Glimmerdark.** [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] broke into the deepest depths and killed the volunteer dragon god to take the Spear; in the wake of the [[demon-war|Demon War]] the gods committed the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy#hiding-4-shove-it-back-down-the-hole-after-xarvion|fourth-and-final hiding]] elsewhere. But the Glimmerdark's plane-character — the cold, the lightlessness, the predatory wrongness of its deeper layers — is the lingering residue of the millennia the Spear spent here.
