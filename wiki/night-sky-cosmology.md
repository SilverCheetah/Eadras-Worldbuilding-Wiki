# Night-Sky Cosmology

**Summary**: The stars visible from Eadras are not distant suns â€” they are other [[mythos|Mythoi]] seen through [[limina|Limina's]] distortion. The night sky's slow precession through a cycle measured in thousands of years is the outer lock that gates the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear of Crystallized Entropy's]] activation windows. Most Eadrans know neither truth.

**Sources**: stars-spear-sithis-topic-01-inworld-facts.txt (2026-05-14 ingest), stars-spear-sithis-topic-02-meta-commentary.txt, stars-spear-sithis-topic-03-principles-and-diagnosis.txt.

## What the stars are

The stars visible from Eadras are **complete, self-contained realities elsewhere in the [[omniverse|Omniverse]]** â€” other [[mythos|Mythoi]] viewed through the intervening medium of [[limina|Limina]]. Limina functions as a metaphysical transition layer and a distortion medium between realities; what reaches an Eadran observer is not light in any ordinary sense but the *attenuated impression* of a foreign Mythos seen through that distortion. Each star is a different reality. None are stars in the modern astrophysical sense.

The reason they twinkle is the reason: Limina's distortion is not stable, and the foreign Mythos is not stationary relative to the observer. Each star is a smeared, flickering imprint of an actual world â€” too distant, too distorted, and too foreign to resolve into anything more.

Most Eadrans do not know this. Folk astronomy reads stars variously as distant fires, divine eyes, ancestral souls, or fixed lanterns of the gods; each civilization has its own answer, and most of those answers are wrong in the same way the modern Earth answer would be. The truth is recorded in [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel's]] deeper scholarship, in [[ratharil|Ratharil's]] dragon star-charts, in the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] (open chapters acknowledge the principle without naming the mechanism), and in fragmentary [[arathi|Arathi]] cosmological records. It is not common knowledge anywhere on Eadras. (source: stars-spear-sithis-topic-01-inworld-facts.txt)

## The Great Precessional Cycle

Eadras experiences a slow astronomical precession â€” a gradual rotation of the night sky that, over the span of a full cycle, returns the heavens to a previous configuration. Scholarly estimates of the cycle's length vary across the institutions that track it:

- Modern [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] working figure: **approximately ten thousand years**.
- Older [[lirra|Lirran]] observatory records preserve a **five-thousand-year alignment** of unclear relation â€” possibly a half-cycle, possibly a sub-window, possibly a separate phenomenon entirely. This is the figure that historically anchored the [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]] calculations and is preserved in older Tome chapters.
- [[ratharil|Ratharil]] dragon star-charts preserve calculations consistent with a longer **twenty-six-thousand-year master cycle** whose subdivisions are not understood outside the dragon archives.

The three figures are not yet reconciled. The Tome notes the discrepancy and does not pick a winner.

What is agreed on is what the cycle *does*: it functions as **a cosmic lock**, not a recharge mechanism. The Spear of Crystallized Entropy does not accumulate power between windows and discharge it at the conjunction. The Spear is always loaded; what the precession does is *open a permitted era* during which the Spear's pathway to the [[eldritch|Source]] can be made to resonate. Outside a permitted era, no Grand Conjunction can fire it. Inside one, the conjunction's exact timing is the next layer of the lock. (source: stars-spear-sithis-topic-01-inworld-facts.txt, "the cycle is a lock, not a recharge mechanism")

This is a load-bearing correction to older cosmological readings that framed the cycle as a *recharge*. The Spear is permanent and ready; the universe is what has to align for it to be used. The framing matters because it is the reason the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] cannot simply wait out the gods' containment â€” they must catch a permitted era *and* a Grand Conjunction *and* deliver the Spear. Three locks, not one.

## What the cycle is the activation key for

Only the Spear. No other Eadran artifact, ritual, or magical operation depends on the precessional cycle. The cycle exists because the Spear exists â€” or, equivalently, the Spear's activation rules are written against the cycle's clockwork â€” and the rest of the world's magic is indifferent to which configuration of stars is currently visible.

This is the cosmological reason the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] feels mythic in a way other dangerous artifacts do not. Most artifacts are constrained by their wielder, their location, or some ritual condition the wielder can meet through diligence. The Spear is constrained by *the sky itself*. No mortal effort closes the lock; no mortal effort opens it.

## The Return / God's Day

The full alignment that returns the sky to its original Spear-forging configuration is known in scholarly circles as **The Return** or **God's Day**. The name *God's Day* is older and ambiguous in folk tradition â€” many mortal calendars use the term loosely for any cosmologically significant day â€” but in [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] and dragon astronomical scholarship the term is specifically reserved for the precessional Return alignment. *The Return* is the unambiguous scholarly term; *God's Day* is the folk-traditional term reserved by some lineages for the specific event the scholars mean by Return.

The naming carries weight. The Spear's activation window is not merely a celestial event; it is the *return of the world to a prior configuration*, and in that configuration the cosmological geometry permits the Spear to function. The Return-framing also captures the long-cycle pessimism implicit in the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] canon: the activation is not an exception but a *recurrence*. The world that produced the Spear returns; the conditions repeat; the question is only who is holding it when the cycle closes.

## Layered star-lore across the eras

Different peoples have interpreted the same sky in radically different ways â€” and the interpretations themselves are a layer of canon worth preserving. Each layer corresponds to a major civilizational era:

- **Arathi Era â€” Crystal Lattices.** The pre-Cataclysm [[arathi|Arathi]] read the stars as vast geometric networks of light â€” interlinked lattice-structures whose geometry mirrored the Arathi's own [[arathi-geo-substrate|geo-substrate]] engineering. The reading was technically incorrect in detail but structurally insightful at the level of intuition: the Arathi understood that the night sky carried a meaning beyond the individual lights, and their geometric framing prefigured the Mythoi-as-other-realities reading in shape if not in content. Surviving Arathi astronomical material (in [[deep-vaults|Deep Vaults]] and [[verdant-scar|Verdant Scar]] fragments) preserves the Lattice diagrams.
- **Dragons â€” The Great Hoard of Souls.** The [[dragons|dragons]]' tradition reads the stars as draconic shapes and patterns â€” a great hoard of souls scattered across the sky, organised into clan-significant constellations. Each clan has its own preferred patterns; the [[ratharil|Ratharil]] dragon star-charts catalogue them. The "hoard" framing is characteristically draconic: stars as possessions of cosmic value, named and held. The dragons' framing has no direct mortal equivalent and is largely opaque to outsiders.
- **Unicorns â€” Wounds in the Veil.** The [[unicorns|unicorns']] tradition reads the stars as **wounds in the Veil** â€” organic, scar-like glowing patterns that mark places where reality once tore open and never fully healed. Some unicorns alone among Eadran sapients are sometimes credited with reading the night sky correctly: that the lights are other worlds, that some of those worlds are *also* wounded, that the wait between is long. The wound-framing matches the unicorn species' broader [[unicorn-wound-keeping|Wound-Keeping]] discipline: knowledge treated as hazard, scars remembered so they are not repeated.
- **Modern Humanoid â€” multiple competing systems.** The post-Banishment humanoid era developed several parallel astronomical traditions: **[[lirra|Lirran]] Weave Constellations** (stars organised by [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] scholarly convention as nodal points in the Weave-defence geometry); **folk animal/hero patterns** (constellations named for local mythological figures, varying by region and culture); **[[katharim|Katharim]] soul-lights** (stars read as cosmologically-displaced soul-traces, including theological inferences about [[unreceived|Unreceived]] cases too deep to discuss openly). Modern humanoid astronomy is not unified; the multiple systems coexist with limited cross-reconciliation.

The persistence of multiple readings across eras is **load-bearing** for the setting's epistemology: the night sky is one of the clearest illustrations of [[principles-of-eadras|Mystery is stronger than explanation]] â€” civilizations *should* disagree on what the stars are, because the truth is structurally beyond what any single civilization's framing can carry.

## Civilizational interpretations

Different cultures read the same phenomenon differently:

- The [[unicorns|unicorns]] alone among Eadran sapients are sometimes credited with reading the night sky correctly â€” that the lights are other worlds, that the wait between worlds is long, and that some of those worlds are also waiting. The unicorn voice on this is sparse and not widely consulted outside the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel's]] deepest theology.
- The [[ratharil|Ratharil]] treat the stars as a navigational and mathematical resource without much speculation on what they *are*; their star-charts are the most accurate in the world and they have no theological commitment to any particular interpretation.
- Most human civilizations treat the stars as divine â€” eyes, fires, lanterns, witnesses. The [[amunorians|Amunorians]] alone among human powers maintain an observatory tradition with serious astronomical mathematics; their results are theologically uncomfortable and are not advertised.
- The Order Sharanel's deeper theology accepts the stars-as-Mythoi reading and treats it as confidential scholarship. Why the truth is held back is itself a matter of internal Order debate; the public-facing answer is that the answer is too unsettling for common consumption.
- The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] preserves the principle in open chapters but treats the precise mechanism as sealed.

## The gradient of knowledge

The pattern of what Eadras's institutions know about the night sky is uneven and revealing:

| Knower | Star-truth | Precessional cycle | Lock-vs-recharge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Common folk | unknown | unknown | unknown |
| Amunorian astronomers | suspected | tracked | unknown |
| Order Sharanel scholarship | known | tracked | known |
| Ratharil archives | partial | tracked precisely | known |
| Unicorn herd-voice | known | sensed | sensed |
| Tome of Baldaran (open) | acknowledged | acknowledged | not openly named |
| Tome of Baldaran (sealed) | full | full | full |

The [[black-hand|Black Hand's]] relationship to this material is operational rather than scholarly â€” they have the numbers they need to plan, kept secret by hereditary archive (the **Bone Ledger**), and care nothing for the cosmological framing beyond what affects their timing.
