# The Demonfall Marches

**Summary**: The Demonfall Marches are a region where the first great demonic breach opened during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] — heavily warded, culturally diverse, unstable to this day, and guarded in part by [[beast-folk|beast-folk]] communities whose ancestors were born from that war.

**Sources**: Playground generation 2nd part.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-29

---

## Overview

The Demonfall Marches are the region where the first great demonic breach opened when [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] summoned the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]] during the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. The breach was the entry point through which demonic forces poured into Eadras, and the Marches bear the deepest scars from that incursion.

The region remains:

- Unstable
- Heavily warded
- Culturally diverse due to ancient refugee settlement

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## The First Breach

When Xar'vion was losing his war, he performed the desperate true-name summoning that opened the Demonfall breach. The act was a severe metaphysical breach: [[demons]] are alien-Mythos energivores whose substance is poisonous to this Mythos and whose presence destabilizes magic, spirit, flesh, and ecology. The rupture opened a direct wound between the demon Mythos and Eadras's Prime Material at this site, introducing a nonnative magical metabolism that would, on a long enough timeline, have eaten the world's magic to depletion.

The breach was eventually closed — sealed by the combined work of dragons, mortals, [[lirra|Lirra's]] nascent Weave, and the Elder Gods. But the metaphysical damage to the local territory remains.

## Beast-Folk Guardianship

[[beast-folk|Beast-folk]] communities founded around the Marches because their ancestors emerged from the demonic corruption, divine intervention, and mortal ritual that defined the war near the breach. These communities preserve founding myths about:

- *"The Wound That Howled"*
- *"The Red Door"*
- *"The Place Where the Wild Stood Up"*

These mythic memories refer directly to the Demonfall breach. Some beast-folk communities still guard the lands surrounding the Marches because their ancestors were born from that war. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Refugee Cultures

The Marches' cultural diversity is a legacy of wartime displacement. War-scarred refuge zones became the first beast-folk communities, and the Marches host:

- Forests resisting demonic infestation
- Marshes poisoned by abyssal ichor
- Mountain refugee camps
- Ruined farmlands where animals and people sheltered together
- Qoxlian wild sanctuaries
- Weave-storm zones near the original demonic breach

This patchwork of survivor traditions explains why the modern Marches contain communities with very different languages, rites, and divine alignments — all descended from those who survived the breach.

## Lingering Threats

The Demonfall Marches remain dangerous because demon-spawn from the war persisted there:

- **Bloodhorns**
- **Ichor hounds**
- **Ashwings**
- **Maw-root trees**
- **Hollow-eyed carrion giants**
- **Mirror parasites**
- **Nightglass locusts**

Some are demonic remnants. Others are native species permanently altered during the war. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Modern Strategic Importance

If the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]] remains sealed somewhere within or beneath the Marches, the region is one of the greatest magical danger sites in modern Eadras:

- The Weave may be thickest there
- Demonic cults may seek to weaken the seal
- Demon-scarred [[black-hand|Black Hand]] offshoots may target the breach to reopen it

Modern beast-folk wardens, [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] maintainers, and [[white-palm|White Palm]] agents may all maintain presence in the region to prevent the breach from reopening or to intercept cult activity.
