# Tarans

**Summary**: The Taran are the third progenitor humanoid race, created by [[the-three|Melania]] as Keepers of Order — disciplined, honor-bound warriors who eventually grew arrogant and founded the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]].

**Sources**: Taran.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Pre-empire foodways.txt, Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Open Questions Answers May 1 2026.txt, Naming conventions.txt, Grok Chat 5-8-26.txt, Extra large grok chat 5-9-2026.txt; eadras_principle_dao_pendulum.txt (2026-06-08 — the seeker-nature departure framing; the full Tarran reverse-culture run remains to come)

## Overview

The Taran (also called Tarran) were created by [[the-three|Melania]] — the middle sibling of the Three — approximately **39,000 years ago** as the Keepers of Order. For the first 21,000 years they performed their duty honorably and were respected by the [[elfen|Elfen]] and [[velrathi|Velrathi]].

**Physical description**: 3 meters tall, hale, with a "perfect-human" appearance. Wore heavy lead-gray cloaks made from the fur of a now-extinct animal that was impossible to dye — the fur perfectly matched the color of their stonework, making them nearly invisible against their own walls.

## Culture

**Values**: Duty, Honor, and Order. Self-control was valued as much as strength. Justice was seen in stark black-and-white terms. Children were raised with rigorous martial training and discipline from a young age.

**Public life**: Trials and oath ceremonies were important public events.

**Appearance**: Plain, almost undecorated armor forged from a rare, hard-to-work magical metal — **mithril**. Tarran mithril armor is structural, not ornamental: practical lines, minimal decoration, the metal allowed to show its own muted luster. See [[mana-metals]].

**Foodways**: Pre-Empire Taran viewed food as fuel for martial duty. Daily meals were simple roasted or grilled meat seasoned only with salt and basic herbs — sauces and elaborate preparations were dismissed as **"foreign frippery."** Major celebrations (victories, oath ceremonies) were the exception: at feasts, Taran enthusiastically borrowed elaborate dishes from both [[elfen|Elfen]] and [[velrathi|Velrathi]] cuisine. See [[pre-empire-foodways]].

## Cities

Grim, layered fortresses built entirely around defense. Streets were narrow and twisting to create choke points. Every building and street was designed as part of the defensive plan. Tall, uniform gray stone structures loomed over the streets.

At the heart of their capital stood an enormous domed hall inside the central citadel, where the **[[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Throne]]** sat on full public display.

For the full architectural tradition during the High Firethorn Era — the strict three-way functional split between austere Roman military fortresses (army-controlled, no ornament), Roman-Bagan-Javanese sacred temples (varying by religious order, every detail a magical focus), and Roman-Ottoman imperial buildings (with the signature spell-crystal minaret towers projecting wards), plus the Angkor-Wat-scale Imperial Palace layout — see [[taran-architecture]].

## Origin: peacekeepers, not conquerors

When the three progenitor races were first created, the Tarran were given a **specifically protective role**. There were warriors among all three races — Velrathi battle-mages, Elfen warden-defenders, and Tarran martial professionals — but the Tarran took on the primary duty of **shielding the non-warrior populations of all three peoples** from external threats: dangerous beasts, rogue dragons, rogue unicorns, and the other natural perils of an early world. The earliest Tarran settlements were practical defensive shelters and frontier strongholds built around protecting Velrathi scholars, Elfen song-tenders, and their own non-combatant kin.

This was honourable, communally-respected work. For the first many millennia of the cooperative age, the Tarran were the **shield** of the three races, and were treated by the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] and [[elfen|Elfen]] as honoured peers in that role.

## Becoming the law

As populations grew across the cooperative age, **internal crime emerged** — and the Tarran, already the protectors against external threats, naturally took on internal law-enforcement duties as well. They became the police of the cooperative age: dispute resolvers, criminal investigators, judicial enforcers, peace-keepers in the literal civic sense.

This expansion of role was not sought aggressively. It was accepted because there was no other natural candidate — the Velrathi were absorbed in scholarship, the Elfen in wardenship of the wildlands, and the Tarran already had the institutional infrastructure (martial training, command structures, defensive strongholds) that the new function required. The Tarran did the work because they were good at it and because the others were grateful.

Their architecture during this transition shifted accordingly: practical frontier shelters gave way to larger, more institutional strongholds, courthouses, and garrison towns. The seeds of the later three-tradition split (military, sacred, imperial — see [[taran-architecture]]) were sown during this period.

## The Fall into Arrogance

Between **20,000 and 19,000 years ago**, [[the-three|The Three That Are One]] **stepped away to explore other Mythoi** — an expression of the seeker-nature their long childhood instilled (see [[the-three#Withdrawal From the World|The Three § Withdrawal]]); Melania left believing, reasonably at the time, that the Tarran were mature enough to stand alone. The Tarran in particular felt the absence of [[the-three|Melania]]'s direct guidance, and the question they had never quite asked themselves while she watched over them began to surface:

> *We protect the people from outside threats. We keep criminals off the streets. We put our lives on the line every day to maintain order. **Why are we not the ones actually ruling?***

This was the thought that birthed the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]. It was not born of conquest-lust or open villainy. It grew from a sense of **earned authority** — the natural arrogance of a race that had spent tens of thousands of years carrying the heaviest civic burden and, with their goddess no longer present to remind them of the larger order they served, came to feel that the burden ought to confer command.

This belief led them to declare the **[[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]** approximately **18,000 years ago**, ending the long cooperative age between the three progenitor races. The transition from peacekeeper to police to imperial ruler took roughly 21,000 years; the final step took only one generation. The Velrathi and Elfen accepted the new political order rather than fighting it, and the Empire became the dominant political reality of the world for the next eight thousand years until the [[demon-war|Demon War]].

## Poison Immunity

One of the most distinctive Taran traits is their **near-total immunity to poisons and toxins**. The trait is now **inherited genetically** — it has been so for generations — but it was originally cultivated through a deliberate cultural practice that lasted **roughly ten thousand years**.

### The original ritual

When the Taran assumed the [[#Origin: peacekeepers, not conquerors|protective role for the three races]], they reasoned that their work would routinely bring them into contact with poisonous plants and venomous animals — across an enormous variety of ecosystems and species. Rather than rely on luck, they engineered the response: **starting in early childhood**, Taran parents began administering **gradually increasing doses of various poisons** to their children, working up over years from minute traces to substantial amounts. The protocol's stated cultural purposes were twofold: **build up resistance** to known toxins, and **toughen the children**.

By the time a Taran reached adulthood under the ritual, they were resistant to nearly every common poison and most rare ones. The ritual was applied across the population for thousands of years.

### Genetic now, ritual still observed

After roughly ten thousand years of population-wide application, the resistance became an **inherited trait** — encoded in Taran biology. Modern Taran (and the Imperial-era Taran the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] absorbed) **no longer require** the ritual to have the immunity.

But the ritual is **still performed**. It survives in the [[firethorn-empire|Imperial era]] as a **cultural rite of passage** — every Taran child still goes through some form of the protocol, and most modern Taran **do not realize the trait is genetic**. To them, the ritual is what makes them poison-resistant. The cultural memory of the original purpose has decoupled from the biological reality, and the ritual continues on its own momentum as a marker of Taran identity rather than a functional necessity.

There remain extremely rare individuals whose bodies, for whatever reason, fail to develop the full resistance even with the ritual. They are uncommon enough that the Taran often regard them with suspicion or pity — a Taran-without-the-immunity is perceived as both a freak occurrence and a small but real liability.

### The cultural consequences

Two of the most distinctive Taran cultural traditions descend directly from the poison-immunity baseline:

- **[[taran-venom-aged-liquor|Venom-aged liquor]]** — the Taran age their distilled spirits with carefully selected animal venom. The venom denatures over time, leaving a complex flavor profile that Taran prize. The drink is completely safe for Taran but mildly to moderately dangerous for non-Taran races, and serves as a subtle social test of who is and is not truly Taran. By the Imperial era, aging time had become a class marker (six months = swill, twenty years = noble standard, fifty years and up = the finest).
- **[[#Venomous Pets|Venomous pet culture]]** (below) — the Taran value venomous animals as status symbols and have, for centuries, commissioned the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] to magically engineer venomous breeds for them.

## Venomous Pets

Taran cultural appreciation for venom extends from the bottle to the kennel. The **more dangerous** the creature, the **higher the status** of owning one. Because the Taran themselves do not have the magical expertise to engineer new venomous animals from scratch, they have for centuries **commissioned the [[velrathi|Velrathi]]** — their progenitor sibling-people — to do the magical work for them.

The Velrathi accept these commissions willingly, treating them as a sibling-people favor and (privately) as interesting biological design problems. Several recognized engineered breeds have been created over the centuries:

| Breed | Description |
|---|---|
| **Venomfang Warhounds** | Large, powerful war-dogs based on traditional Taran burly warhound stock, magically altered to produce a potent neurotoxin in their saliva. The most iconic and widely-used venomous pet among Taran nobility and military officers. Lethal to non-Taran on a serious bite. |
| **Blood Parrots** | Striking crimson parrots with toxic bites and claws. Known for their intelligence and aggressive personality. Favored by nobles and mages who want a venomous pet that doubles as a conversational companion. |
| **Ironscale Monitors** | Large, heavily armored monitor lizards delivering a fast-acting hemotoxin through their claws. Status symbols of the very wealthiest Taran houses; less common than the other two breeds. |

Owning a well-bred animal of any of these lines is a substantial status marker. A Venomfang Warhound from a recognized bloodline costs more than most ordinary horses; a documented Ironscale Monitor can be a major estate's prized possession.

### The flea question — pragmatism over morality

Taran philosophy on weaponized venom is ruthlessly practical, **not** moral. They are perfectly comfortable with the principle of using venomous creatures against enemies; the [[#Origin: peacekeepers, not conquerors|peacekeeper-to-police-to-imperial]] arc made *with us or against us, and the enemy is there to be killed* a load-bearing Taran cultural axiom centuries ago.

What stopped the Taran from one specific direction of biological warfare — a [[velrathi|Velrathi]] proposal to engineer **venomous fleas** for use against enemy populations — was **not** ethical objection. It was **control**. Fleas cannot be aimed; their propagation cannot be contained; the risk of releasing them and watching them spread back across the Taran's own population is too high. The proposal was rejected outright on those grounds, with no real moral debate. *We do not deploy weapons we cannot recall.*

The principle still governs Taran venom-pet engineering today. Every recognized breed is a creature that can be **owned, kept, restrained, and killed if necessary**. Anything that would self-propagate beyond the owner's control is not on the menu.

## Engineered Child Races

The Tarran themselves engineered three child races. Per established canon, [[the-three|The Three]] created only the [[elfen|Elfen]], Tarran, and [[velrathi|Velrathi]] directly; child races were shaped by their progenitor parents, and the Three did not direct, assist, or oversee that work (canon clarification 2026-05-10).

| Race | Description | Modern Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Titans** | Created as warriors against chaotic incursions | Ancestors of modern Giants (and Goliath descend further) |
| **[[aetherins\|Aetherin]]** | Created as shock troops against chaos and demonic incursions | **Totally extinct** — died at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves\|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] as one of the [[three-who-stood\|Three Who Stood]]. Diluted bloodline survives as modern Humans. |
| **[[mitans\|Mitans]]** | Created as miners and tunnel warriors | Ancestors of Dwarves and Halflings |

## Banishment and the Gray Matriarchy

At the conclusion of the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the [[great-banishment|Elder Gods banished all three progenitor races]] from the Prime Material. The Tarran were sent to the **Prime-like reflection of the moon [[gaia|Gaia]]** — the very moon where [[the-three|Melania]] had originally placed their progenitor stock during the early ages of creation. There they rebuilt fortified gray cities under harsher skies, their **[[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] vanished**, and over millennia transformed into the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy of Gaia]] — a strict matriarchy with male enslavement, ruled secretly by an ancient dragon they call [[she-who-shall-not-be-named|She Who Shall Not Be Named]].

Pure Tarran no longer exist on the Prime Material. See [[gray-matriarchy]] for the modern post-exile society.

## Naming

Taran names are strong, martial, disciplined, and authoritative — built from hard consonants (t, k, r, th, d, v) in short, powerful syllables. Common opening syllables: **Tar-, Kor-, Val-, Thor-, Dar-, Mar-**; common endings: *-an, -ath, -or, -ak, -un.* Examples: *Korvath, Valdrak, Tharun, Daramis, Markor, Tarveth, Tarran.* See [[naming-conventions#taran-created-by-melania|Racial Naming Conventions — Taran]].
