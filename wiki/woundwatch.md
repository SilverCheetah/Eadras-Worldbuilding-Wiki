# The Woundwatch

**Summary**: The **Woundwatch** is the proposed (and slowly emerging) institutional framework for sustained anti-demonic vigilance along the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] â€” the regions where the [[demon-war|Demon War's]] boundary trauma is still active and demonic incursion remains a continuing risk. The Watch is not a single army, order, or temple. It is a deliberately distributed network: military wardens, [[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] laughter-houses, [[katharim|Katharim]] cairn-lines, [[lirra|Lirran]] Weave pylons, armed monasteries, local scouts, healers, teachers, cooks, midwives, and grief-keepers â€” each institution doing its own work, with the Watch as the connective tissue. It is one of the first canonical applications of *the Wound That Must Not Be Fed* doctrine: demonic hunger thrives on despair, so anti-demonic defence is not only soldiers â€” it is everything that keeps a community whole.

## Status

**Proposed and slowly emerging.** As of the [[first-rebrightening|First Rebrightening's]] early decades, the Woundwatch is not yet a single chartered institution. Different regions have implemented different pieces of it â€” some have formalised charters, some have informal cooperative arrangements, some have proposed but not yet adopted the framework. The Watch's coherent existence is partly aspirational and partly already operational.

A formal **First Woundwatch Charter** is anticipated within the FR decades, drawing on the [[doctrine-of-open-palm|Open Palm]] reform's institutional momentum, but its exact issuing authority, jurisdiction, and operational details are open canon.

## Why a distributed network

The Demon War's lesson is that demons feed on more than energy. They feed on **fear, despair, identity-instability, secrecy, depersoning, and the collapse of meaning**. Anti-demonic defence that focuses only on the sharp end â€” soldiers, wards, killing demons when they cross the boundary â€” misses the half of the threat that operates below the level of incursion.

A distributed network addresses both layers simultaneously:

- **Soldiers and wardens** address active incursion.
- **Laughter-houses, healers, cooks, teachers, midwives** address the conditions that *prevent demonic appetite from finding purchase* â€” sustained community wholeness, joy, dignity, continuity of care.

The doctrinal frame for this is *The Wound That Must Not Be Fed* â€” see below.

## Composition

The Watch's component institutions, as currently proposed or operating in various Demonfall Marches towns:

### Military layer

- **Military wardens** â€” standing armed forces, often locally raised, patrolling the boundary. May be national, regional, or town levies; the Watch does not require any single command structure.
- **Armed monasteries** â€” fortified religious institutions where militant orders combine prayer, vigilance, and direct defence. Common in regions where the boundary is sufficiently active to require religious-military fusion.
- **Local scouts** â€” irregular, knowledge-of-terrain-based vigilance. Often the first line; almost always the first to notice an incursion.

### Magical layer

- **[[lirra|Lirran]] Weave pylons** â€” installed [[seven-conduits|conduit]]-anchored magical structures that reinforce the Weave's defensive function locally. The pylons are not full conduits themselves; they are amplifier-stabilisers maintained by [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] technicians.
- **[[katharim|Katharim]] cairn-lines** â€” sacred markers placed along the boundary that catalogue the [[unreceived|Unreceived]] who fell in that region. The cairns are simultaneously memorial and warning: the Katharim's accounting work means a town with a long cairn-line knows exactly how much demonic appetite has fed there before.

### Community-wholeness layer

- **[[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] laughter-houses** â€” Circle-level institutions deliberately maintained as defensive infrastructure. Their function is to *sustain communal joy* against demonic despair. Laughter-houses are not merely social spaces; they are recognised by the Watch as anti-demonic assets.
- **Healers** â€” community-level medical work that prevents the despair of unattended sickness, unmanaged grief, untreated trauma.
- **Teachers** â€” institutional and informal pedagogy that maintains the continuity of meaning across generations. A community that cannot teach its children why life matters cannot resist demonic appetite at the community-wholeness layer.
- **Cooks** â€” sustained, communal, dignified eating practice. The opposite of demonic consumption-without-return is *life that eats and returns*. Communal cooks-and-feasts maintain that pattern as lived experience.
- **Midwives** â€” the continuity-of-life institution at the moment of greatest cosmological vulnerability. The Watch recognises midwifery as load-bearing.
- **Grief-keepers** â€” people who carry the work of acknowledged mourning, including the rituals for [[unreceived|the Unreceived]], so that grief does not become buried, contaminated, or weaponisable by demonic suggestion.

## The Beast-Folk Laughter Doctrine

A specific load-bearing element of the Watch is the formal recognition that **[[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] laughter-houses count as defensive infrastructure**.

The doctrinal claim is direct: demonic hunger feeds on despair, fear, isolation, and the collapse of communal meaning. Communities where laughter is structurally maintained â€” through Circle gatherings, daily forgetting practice, [[flyting|flyting]], shared meals, and the daily refusal to teach the inherited war-grief to children â€” are communities where demonic appetite finds less purchase.

This is not metaphor. The Watch treats the laughter-houses as **functional military assets in the same operational sense** as a Weave pylon or a watchtower â€” different modalities, complementary effects, all measured in the same outcome: reduced demonic activity in the surrounding region.

Sceptics within the Watch (and within mainstream military thought outside it) have argued that laughter-as-defence is too soft a framing. The response within the Watch is that the Beast-Folk communities adjacent to the boundary have measurably lower demonic incursion rates per resident than equivalent human communities, and the laughter-house framing is the most parsimonious explanation. The mechanism is debated; the correlation is documented.

## The Wound That Must Not Be Fed

The Watch's anchoring doctrine is **The Wound That Must Not Be Fed**:

> *Demonic hunger thrives where societies cultivate fear, secrecy, despair, uncontrolled appetite, depersoning, persecution, and metaphysical negligence. Anti-demonic vigilance must protect the dignity and continuity of persons â€” because demons can consume body, spirit, and soul.*

The doctrine's core teachings, frequently quoted in Watch material:

- **Vigilance without cruelty.** A Watch that becomes cruel feeds the wound. Cruel vigilance produces the fear and depersoning demons consume; the Watch must guard against itself.
- **Judgment without hysteria.** Threat assessment must remain calibrated; panic feeds the wound.
- **Mourning without false comfort.** The [[unreceived|Unreceived]] are not coming back; pretending otherwise either deceives the families or generates the false-prophet movements demons can corrupt.
- **Laughter without denial.** Communal joy that ignores the reality of the boundary is brittle and ineffective; laughter must coexist with awareness of what the laughter is protecting against.
- **Hunger with return.** *Life eats and returns;* demons consume without return. The Watch's economic and ecological practices favour cycles of return over hoarding extraction.
- **Secrecy with accountability.** Some information must be held back (the full mechanism of demonic consumption, the Unreceived theology, specific operational details), but the secrecy must remain accountable to mortal time and mortal consequence â€” the [[hearth-witness-mandate|Hearth-Witness Mandate]] applies.

The anchoring phrase:

> ***Demonic hunger is not defeated by making society hungrier for purity.***

## Relationship to other institutions

- **[[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]** â€” provides the Weave pylons, the [[netharim|Netharim]] consultants, and the technical understanding of the Ward's regional state. Order Sharanel does not command the Watch but is its principal magical partner.
- **[[katharim|Katharim]]** â€” maintain the cairn-lines, the [[unreceived|Unreceived]] Ledgers, and the death-priest rituals that the Watch's community-wholeness layer cannot perform itself. Often the moral conscience of the Watch.
- **[[druids|Druids]]** â€” partial overlap, particularly where the Watch borders [[fae-da-nar|Fae-da'Nar]] territory. Druids may judge Watch disputes involving Woodkin allies; the Watch must defer to Druid grounds.
- **[[doctrine-of-open-palm|The Open Palm reform]]** â€” provides the doctrinal authority for the community-wholeness layer's legitimacy. Without Open Palm doctrine, the Watch's non-military components would be harder to legitimise institutionally.
- **[[divine-bureaucracy|Divine Bureaucracy]]** â€” processes the prayer traffic from Watch institutions; the Watch's specific category of requests is one of the rare areas where the Bureaucracy is reportedly responsive rather than evasive.
- **[[orden|Orden]]** â€” has been particularly active in proposing formal Watch charters; Orden's audit-friendly theology suits the Watch's institutional needs well.
- **[[bold|Bold]]** â€” provides conduit-power for some pylons; Bold has resisted being drawn into Watch command structure, preferring to retain its political independence.

## Open canon

- Whether the **Demon War Memory Crisis** (~25-35 FR) accelerates Watch formalisation or fractures it.
- The specific **regional Watch chapters** â€” which towns have which institutions, which alliances, which gaps.
- Whether **[[woundwatch]] members are recognised across kingdom borders** â€” does a Watch member from one polity have authority or hospitality rights in another?
- The Watch's relationship to the **[[black-hand|Black Hand]]** â€” has the Hand attempted to infiltrate, redirect, or co-opt the Watch? (Almost certainly yes; specific operations open canon.)
- The first major test of *The Wound That Must Not Be Fed* doctrine â€” under what crisis does the doctrine come under serious pressure?
