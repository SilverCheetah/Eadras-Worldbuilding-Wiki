# Order-Sworn Netharim

**Summary**: **Order-Sworn Netharim** are [[netharim|Netharim]] who have completed the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel's]] training program *and* taken the **Oath of Service**, formally binding themselves to [[lirra|Lirra]]'s service for life. The Oath distinguishes them from Order-trained graduates who declined the bond: most Netharim trained by the Order are *not* Sworn — many enter civilian practice — but those who do take the Oath carry corresponding institutional standing, narrow priestly access, and recognisable regalia (the [[order-sharanel#The Sworn Eye amulet|Sworn Eye amulet]]).

## What the Oath does

The Oath of Service is the institutional act that converts a trained Netharim graduate into a Sworn member of [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]. Three things happen at the Oath-taking, in canonical order:

1. **The Netharim binds themselves to [[lirra|Lirra]]'s service for life.** The bond is permanent. Sworn Netharim cannot un-swear; the Oath is not a contract terminable by either party. (A Sworn Netharim who proves to be working against Lirra's purposes is removed by other means — exile, censure, or in extreme cases full Unmaking by the senior Order — but not by their own choice.)
2. **They receive the [[order-sharanel#The Sworn Eye amulet|Sworn Eye amulet]].** The amulet is the visible signal of Sworn status. Priests, civil authorities, fellow Netharim, and well-informed laypeople recognise the Eye on sight.
3. **They gain narrow access to priestly magic.** Sworn Netharim may receive priestly magic from Lirra *for divine functions that ordinary Netharim practice cannot accomplish* — blessings, sanctifications, certain consecrations, and other functions that intersect Lirra's divine portfolio rather than the underlying Netharim reality-shaping. The access is real but narrow: an Order-Sworn Netharim cannot simply double-dip the priestly and Netharim pipelines for ordinary work; the priestly channel only opens for properly divine functions.

## What the Oath does not do

The Oath does **not** make a Netharim more powerful. A Sworn Netharim and an unsworn Netharim of equal training, equal innate capacity, and equal internal-reality-model depth perform Netharim practice at the same level. The Oath is institutional and theological, not magical-substrate-altering.

The Oath also does **not** make a Netharim a priest. Priestly magic and Netharim practice remain distinct, and an Order-Sworn Netharim is still primarily a Netharim. The narrow priestly access exists for functions only Lirra can authorise; everything else remains the practitioner's own work.

## Why most Netharim do not take the Oath

The Order Sharanel deliberately produces more trained graduates than it Swears. Several reasons:

- **The Order benefits from a wide diaspora of trained Netharim.** Graduates who decline the Oath go on to careers in courts, academies, militaries, trade, scholarly research, and civilian magical practice. Their training is the Order's contribution to the world; their independence is part of what makes that contribution welcome rather than feared.
- **The Oath is permanent and selective.** The Order does not press graduates to take an Oath they cannot fulfill. A Netharim who is not ready for a lifelong bond to Lirra's service should decline.
- **Some graduates are temperamentally unsuited to Order discipline.** Netharim practice scales with the depth and accuracy of the practitioner's internal model of reality; some of the strongest reality-models develop in solitude, dissent, or pursuits the Order itself would not undertake.

The result is an institutional pattern recognisable elsewhere in Eadras: a strong training pipeline that produces a small core of bound institutional members and a much larger diaspora of unbound alumni, with both halves recognising each other as kin even when they disagree.

## Distinguishing markers

A Sworn Netharim is distinguished from an unsworn Order-trained Netharim by:

- **The [[order-sharanel#The Sworn Eye amulet|Sworn Eye amulet]]**, worn visibly
- **Institutional standing** — promotion eligibility, voting in Tower-level Councils, participation in Order operational deployments
- **Access to the [[order-sharanel#The Towers of Lirra|Towers of Lirra]]** at full institutional rank, including sealed research and ritual chambers that civilian Order-trained Netharim do not enter
- **The narrow priestly-magic channel** (functional, not symbolic)

The Sworn Eye is the binary external signal; everything else follows from it.

## Open canon

- Whether the Oath can be administered outside a Tower of Lirra
- The exact divine functions the priestly-magic channel permits, and the limits on its frequency / scope
- Whether an Oath-bound Netharim's Sworn status survives them after death — whether their afterlife relationship to Lirra differs from an unsworn Netharim's
- The specific consequences of an Oath-breach that does not rise to the level of Order removal (does the amulet dim? Does Lirra withhold the priestly access? Do other Sworn members feel it?)
