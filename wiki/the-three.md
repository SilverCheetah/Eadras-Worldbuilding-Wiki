# The Three

**Summary**: Mark, Rand, and Melania — "The Three That Are One" — are mortal children rescued by [[dao]] from the Witchfinder General Trials on a distant world, raised in the spaces between mythoi, and granted divine status. Each created one of the three progenitor humanoid races of Eadras: [[elfen|Elfen]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], and [[tarans|Tarran]] — and **only those three races**. All other **humanoid** races of the progenitor lineage descended from those three. (The dragon-made [[draconic-servitor-races|draconic servitor races]] are the sole humanoid-shaped peoples that stand *outside* this genealogy — a separate creation-tradition; see below.)

## Outsider Origin — Witchfinder General Trials

The Three were **not born of Eadras**. They were **mortal children from a distant world**, a place of iron, fire, and fearful superstition. **Their mother was accused of witchcraft during the Witchfinder General Trials and burned at the stake** (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt; user direction 2026-05-06).

As the flames rose, [[dao]] stepped through a momentary tear in the fabric of realities, gathered the three terrified orphans, and carried them away. He **erased their existence from the memories of that world** and raised them as his own in the quiet spaces between mythoi.

He brought them **across both space and time**, depositing them in Eadras at **approximately 50,000 years before the present**. The timeline they were rescued from was the **mid-1640s of their own world** — the height of the Witchfinder General persecutions in East Anglia; Eadras-time at the moment of their arrival was 50,000 BP, placing them in Eadras's deep pre-humanoid era, about **11,000 years before the modern progenitor races would emerge**.

This extramythic origin is significant — they carry resonances from outside Eadras's laws, allowing them (and their works) to partially transcend local constraints. The Three are themselves outsiders, like the [[runeswords|Runesword]] warrior souls and the [[worldender-crystal|Worldender crystal]] itself.

## Upbringing

They grew under [[dao|Dao's]] **patient guidance** over the **~11,000 years between rescue (~50,000 BP) and the creation of the progenitor races (~39,000 BP)**, learning the shape of power, the weight of responsibility, and the necessity of balance. He taught them many things, including the use of [[eldritch|Eldritch]]. **Soon after the rescue, Dao forged the eleven [[runeswords|Runeswords]] and the Runes of Power; the children were present as witnesses** — their introduction to god-tier creative work.

Only when they were ready did Dao reveal his great work to them in full and permit them to undertake their own creations.

## The Three and Their Races

When they reached maturity, each of The Three wanted to create on their own. Each chose to create **a single progenitor race based on their own humanoid form**. Per established canon: **The Three created only the Elfen, Tarran, and Velrathi directly**. All child and grandchild races were engineered by the progenitors themselves, not by the Three.

### What only The Three could do

The Three could create **whole new species from scratch** — bodies, magic, sapience, and lineage shaped from nothing in advance. They were able to do this for two reasons (user direction 2026-05-01):

1. **They learned the craft directly from [[dao|Dao]].** Dao is the most senior actor in Eadras's history; his teaching is unique. The Elder Gods do not even know he exists (see [[dao]]). The Three are the only beings in the Mythos who learned creation under his patient guidance.
2. **They had deity-level power of their own.** Their extramythic origin (see [[#Outsider Origin — Witchfinder General Trials|Outsider Origin]]) gave them resonance the local pantheon does not match; their divine status gave them sustained creative force.

The progenitor races they made — [[elfen|Elfen]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], [[tarans|Tarran]] — were powerful, intelligent, and arguably the peers of mortals anywhere in the Omniverse. But they were **not** at deity level, and they had **not** received Dao's tutelage. They could not create new species from scratch.

What the progenitors could do — and did — was alter their own blood, magically modifying their own babies in the womb across a few generations until the offspring stabilized as a distinct race fit for whatever servant or tool function they needed. This is the canonical mechanism for every "engineered" child and grandchild race. See [[peoples-of-eadras#How the engineering worked]].

> **Important doctrinal point.** None of the Three directed their creations to do this engineering work, nor did any of the Three help or oversee the engineering of any child race. Once the Elfen, Velrathi, and Tarran existed, every subsequent engineered race was the progenitor race's own initiative. The Three made the eldest three; the eldest three engineered every subsequent race **within that lineage**. This descent rule governs the **humanoid progenitor family tree**. (Canon clarification: Notes for Changes for 5-10-26.txt; applied retroactively.)

### The draconic exception

The progenitor-descent doctrine governs the humanoid lineage **only**. It does **not** govern the [[draconic-servitor-races|draconic servitor races]] — [[kobolds|kobolds]], [[lizardfolk|lizardfolk]], [[snake-folk|snake folk]], and the [[dragonborn|dragonborn]] champions — which are a separate creation-tradition: reshaped by [[dragons|dragons]] from dragon-derived flesh and made ontologically real by bargain with the ascended dragon-gods (~30,000 BP), descended from **no** progenitor. They are the only humanoid-shaped peoples in Eadras outside the progenitor genealogy.

This is not a second from-scratch creator-lineage. The capability that makes the Three special — creating a body, its magic, its sapience, and its lineage *from nothing* — remains unique to the Three (and to [[dao|Dao]] above them). Dragons did **not** do this: they self-sculpted their own flesh by magic and alchemy (form only) and required the dragon-gods' bargain to make those forms *real*. Same mortal-capable tier as the progenitors' womb-engineering, a different mechanism — and still not from-scratch creation. See [[draconic-servitor-races]] and [[ixthal]] for the two roads to the genesis-threshold (sanctioned bargain versus concealed theft).

### Mark (Eldest)

Created the **[[elfen|Elfen]]** — proto-elves, wardens of wildlands. Mark appears in the [[gods-of-eadras|pantheon]] as God of the Elfen.

The Elfen subsequently engineered three child races (the Elfen did this themselves; Mark did not direct, assist, or oversee the work):
- **[[el-mer|El'mer]]** — ancestors of all modern Elves; **totally extinct** as a pure people after the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]]
- **Sidhe** — ancestors of all modern sapient Fae
- **Sea'tua** — ancestors of all modern sapient sea peoples (Merfolk, Kuo-toa, Sahuagin)

### Melania (Middle)

Created the **[[tarans|Tarran]]** — keepers of order. Melania did this as a **single act of making**: she crafted the Tarran race, the two Tarran [[runeswords|Runeswords]] (**Brightthorn** and **Darkthorn**), and the **[[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]]** **simultaneously**, at approximately 39,000 BP, using [[worldender-crystal|alien crystal]] [[dao]] had given her. The Tarran existed from their first generation with their throne and royal blades already in place — the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]]'s founding ~21,000 years later merely formalized the sovereignty those emblems already represented. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

The Tarran subsequently engineered three child races (the Tarran did this themselves; Melania did not direct, assist, or oversee the work):
- **Titans** — ancestors of modern Giants (and further down, Goliath)
- **[[aetherins|Aetherin]]** — superior humans bred for war; **totally extinct** as a pure people after the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]]; ancestors of modern Humans
- **[[mitans|Mitans]]** — ancestors of Dwarves and Halflings

### Rand (Youngest)

Created the **[[velrathi|Velrathi]]** — masters of magic.

The Velrathi subsequently engineered three servant-scholar races (the Velrathi did this themselves; Rand did not direct, assist, or oversee the work):
- **[[rathari|Rathar]]** — scholarly assistants and pinnacle archivists; fractured into [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar|three branches]] in the Demon War (Fallen, [[beast-folk|Laughing Third]], [[katharim|Weighted Third]])
- **[[orugi|Orug]]** — strong, honorable guardians; **totally extinct** at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]]; diluted blood survives in modern [[orcs|Orcs]]
- **[[grugi|Grug]]** — clever maintenance/inventors; ancestors of modern Goblins and Gnomes

## Withdrawal From the World

The Three That Are One withdrew from the world sometime between **20,000 and 19,000 years ago**. Their departure removed direct divine oversight over the [[tarans|Tarran]], who over the following centuries grew arrogant and power-hungry. This directly enabled the Tarran to declare the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]] approximately **18,000 years ago**.

## Their Status as Outsiders Matters

Because the Three originated **outside the Eadras Mythos**, several things follow:

- Their creations (the [[elfen|Elfen]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], [[tarans|Tarran]]) carry faint extramythic resonance — which is part of why pre-war progenitors were so much more powerful than their modern descendants
- The [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] (Melania's work using extramythic crystal) was effective precisely because Melania herself carried outsider resonance and could shape extramythic material
- The [[runeswords|Runeswords]] they witnessed forged carry the same outsider quality
- Modern Eadras has no concept of where the Three came from; even the Elder Gods do not know

## Knowledge of [[dao|Dao]]

[[the-three|The Three]] are among the **only beings in the Mythos who know [[dao]] is an avatar of the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]]**. Even the **Elder Gods do not know Dao exists** — Dao is concealed from them by the architecture of the Mythos itself. (See [[dao]].)

Mark, Rand, and Melania know the long game in part — they know they are extramythic, that they were rescued, that Dao gathered the [[worldender-crystal|alien crystal]] for specific purposes. But they may not know the **full** scope of his stewardship across the Omniverse.

## What the Three represent

In the broader cosmological framework, the Three are distinct from every other category of higher being on Eadras:

- They are not **Elder Gods** — Elder Gods are second-derivative referential speakers (see [[true-language]]); the Three are not.
- They are not **ordinary ascended mortals** — those ascended within Eadras's own metaphysics; the Three were rescued from outside it.
- They are not **primordial beings** — the [[primordial-duo|Primordial Duo]] sacrificed themselves into Eadras at its founding; the Three arrived already adult of mortal origin from elsewhere.

What the Three represent, in the Tome's framing, is **educated transcendence, learned creation, and reality literacy**. They are the only beings in the Mythos who learned the full arts of magic, truenaming, reality shaping, rune-sword smithing, and structural cosmology directly under [[dao|Dao]]'s patient guidance — and who therefore inherit not just power, but the discipline that knows what to do with it. They are *what mortals become when they are taught creation by the Creator's own avatar*. (source: Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt)

### Triadic thematic structure

The Three are not three duplicates of the same office. Each carries a distinct thematic weight, and the three weights together cover the structural domains of created existence:

| Sibling | Theme | Created |
|---|---|---|
| **Mark** | **Stewardship / wildness** — the warden's responsibility to wild and growing things, and the discipline to tend them without domesticating their soul | [[elfen\|Elfen]] |
| **Rand** | **Knowledge / magic** — the mage's relationship to power, the cost of understanding, and the obligation to study reality patiently | [[velrathi\|Velrathi]] |
| **Melania** | **Order / sovereignty** — the ruler's responsibility to structure, law, and the inheritance of authority across generations | [[tarans\|Tarran]] |

Each created the progenitor race that best embodies their sibling's theme; each progenitor race's eventual trajectory — wild Elfen wardens, magic-mastering Velrathi, order-bearing Tarran — is the natural extension of the maker's defining concern.

### Names as literary homage

**Their names are deliberate literary homage** by the curator. Mark's name is a homage to *Blessings of a Curse*. (User direction 2026-05-06.) The literary referents for Rand and Melania are open canon and may be elaborated by the curator at any time.
