# Chaos

**Summary**: Chaos is the **absence of reality** — an unreality in which the structures that define existence (time, space, identity, causality) do not exist in any stable or meaningful form. Chaos and the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]] are co-eternal; each [[mythos|Mythos]] is a bounded region of imposed structure floating within the surrounding unreality. When Chaos intrudes into a Mythos it does not replace reality — it undermines the conditions that allow reality to exist.

**Sources**: Chaos.txt, Omniversal Laws.txt, Spear of Crystallized Entropy.txt, Chaos Crusades.txt, Combined info dump 2 5-4-2026.txt; user direction 2026-05-04 (Chaos = absence of reality, co-eternal with the Creator).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## What Chaos Is

Chaos is **not disorder**, and it is **not contradiction**. It is the **absence of reality** — an unreality. The structures that ordinary minds take for granted as the floor of being itself — time, space, identity, causality — **do not exist in Chaos in any stable or meaningful form**. There is no "thing" that is one thing and then becomes another, because there is no "thing" at all. (source: Combined info dump 2 5-4-2026.txt)

Chaos is what's there when reality isn't. The "shifting" appearance Mythos beings perceive when they look toward Chaos is an artifact of trying to perceive unreality with senses calibrated for reality. There is nothing actually shifting, because there is nothing.

Sanity breaks upon entry to Chaos because the conditions for sanity — for thought, for self, for any persistent state of mind — are precisely the conditions that don't exist there. From the outside (from within a Mythos), Chaos appears hypnotic. From the inside, it dissolves.

## Co-Eternal with the Creator

**Chaos and the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]] have always existed.** Both are eternal; neither created the other. Chaos is the standing background against which the Creator imposes structure. (User direction 2026-05-04.)

Each [[mythos|Mythos]] is therefore a **bounded region of imposed structure within the surrounding unreality**. The Mythos bubble is not a region carved out of chaos by removal; it is a region of *positive imposition* — rules where there were no rules, persistence where there was no persistence, identity where there was no identity. Chaos surrounds every Mythos, fills the gaps between every Mythos, and would be everywhere if not for the Creator's continuous imposition of structure.

This is the deep cosmological reason the [[omniversal-laws]] are unbreakable: they are not arbitrary rules added to existence. They are some of the rules that *constitute* existence. To break them would be to remove the imposition of structure — and what is there without the imposition is Chaos.

## Intrusion: Undermining, Not Replacement

When Chaos intrudes into a Mythos — through a breach in the shell, through a wound like the one [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] drilled, or through the [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]] — it **does not replace reality**. It cannot. There is nothing of Chaos to substitute *for* reality, because Chaos is not a thing.

What happens instead: the **conditions that allow reality to exist locally are undermined**. Time becomes unreliable. Space becomes incoherent. Identity becomes unstable. Causality breaks. The result is **instability and incoherence** in the affected region — not a region replaced by something else, but a region where the imposed structure is failing.

This is why Chaos intrusions look the way they do in the historical record. The First and Second [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]] did not consume regions of Eadras into nonexistence; they produced regions where the rules of existence held only partially. Things in those regions could still exist — barely, briefly, badly. Hence the form-or-dissolve law, and hence the formation of [[aberrations]] in residual chaos pockets where the failure of structure has been incomplete and persistent.

## The Form-or-Dissolve Law

**Chaos cannot persist as raw chaos inside a [[mythos|Mythos]].** When Chaos enters a Mythos it must take **temporary physical form**, or it dissolves — just as anything from a Mythos that steps out into raw Chaos likewise dissolves. The rule is symmetric: Mythos and Chaos are mutually corrosive, and only by adopting the local rules of either side can the other persist there at all. (source: Chaos Crusades.txt)

This is why the [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]], when Chaos breached Eadras through Kudur-eṭir's old aperture, manifested as fightable phenomena — colors, smells, and ideals given temporary bodies the first time; dragon-and-unicorn body-horror the second — rather than as nihilistic erasure of the regions affected.

What shape Chaos takes inside a Mythos is constrained by what concepts it has access to. Raw Chaos with no template seizes on whatever is simplest — first-order ideals — and grows more recognizable (and more grotesque) as it observes the local Mythos's beings.

## Role in Eadras History

The Arathi archmage **[[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]]** reached into Primordial Chaos when forging the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]], making contact with an unspeakable entity called **Cthosh** — a [[neverborn|Neverborn]] primordial being of pure chaos and madness. This act is considered one of the maddest things ever done by a mortal.

The cosmic aperture Kudur-eṭir drilled to make that contact has had two further uses: it was the entry point of both [[chaos-crusades|Chaos Crusades]], approximately **6 million years before present** (First Crusade, on Eldravath) and **2 million years before present** (Second Crusade). Both Crusades left **residual chaos pockets** in the affected regions — sites where the failure of structure was incomplete and persistent — which are the ongoing sources of [[aberrations]].

## The Presence of the Creator

In the [[omniverse]], the presence of the [[omniversal-creator]] — or at least his [[bastion-of-light]] — keeps Chaos back far enough from [[concordance]] that no enclosing shell is needed. This illustrates the power differential between Chaos and ordered creation.

## The Third Omniversal Law

The [[omniversal-laws|Third Omniversal Law]] — "Magic must be ruled by a mind" — exists precisely because unregulated magic tends toward Chaos. Chaos represents what happens when magic has no governing intelligence at all.
