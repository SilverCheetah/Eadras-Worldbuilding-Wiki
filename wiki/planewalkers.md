# Planewalkers

**Summary**: *Planewalkers* are beings capable of traveling between [[mythos|Mythoi]] of the [[omniverse|Omniverse]]. They are rare across creation, and almost always distinct individuals — beings whose nature, training, or artifacts grant them the capacity to cross the boundaries between Mythoi. **Planewalkers originating from Eadras are fundamentally 2–4 times stronger than planewalkers originating from other Mythoi** — a consequence of Eadras's intrinsic magical richness, its [[bastion-of-light|Bastion]]-fed [[gods-of-eadras#Source of godly power|Well of Creation]], and the saturated cosmological substrate that infuses everything born or made within the Mythos.

## What a planewalker is

A planewalker is any being capable of moving between [[mythos|Mythoi]] — crossing the [[concordance|Concordance]] or otherwise traversing the boundaries that separate one cosmological bubble from another. This is a category of *capacity*, not a species: planewalkers can be mortals trained in specific cross-Mythos arts, divine beings dispatched on cross-Mythos errands, artifact-bearers carrying the right kind of instrument, ascended souls whose nature has been adjusted to permit the crossing, or entities native to the [[concordance|Concordance]] itself.

The capacity is rare in any Mythos. Crossing the boundary between Mythoi is not a casual act; it requires either intrinsic ontological flexibility (as with [[dao|Dao]], who walks between Mythoi as part of his role as the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]]'s monitor), specific magical preparation, alignment of cosmographical circumstances that mortal arts have never reliably reproduced, or a few rare artifacts that grant the capacity to their bearer.

## The Eadras advantage

**Planewalkers originating from Eadras are fundamentally 2–4 times stronger than planewalkers originating from other Mythoi.** The multiplier is broad — physical strength, magical capacity, raw endurance, the depth and quality of whatever powers the planewalker exercises. A planewalker from Eadras stepping into another Mythos finds themselves operating significantly above the baseline of any planewalker native to that Mythos.

The mechanism is structural. Eadras's intrinsic magical richness — saturated into the substance of the Mythos when the [[primordial-duo|Primordial Duo]] poured out both Well-allotment and their own essence to create it — infuses every being born or made within Eadras. They carry that saturation with them when they cross Mythos boundaries. Combined with the Eadras Well's [[bastion-of-light|Bastion]]-fed continuous replenishment, an Eadras-origin planewalker exists at a higher native power level than their cross-Mythos counterparts, and *retains much of that advantage outside Eadras*.

The 2–4x range reflects variation by individual. Different planewalkers from Eadras bring different mixes of intrinsic-magic-saturation, training, divine endorsement, and personal aptitude. None of them, however, are merely at parity with planewalkers from typical Mythoi.

## Persistence and asymmetry

The advantage is **enduring rather than transient**. An Eadras-born planewalker does not lose their saturation by leaving Eadras; the Mythos's richness was woven into them at the substrate level when they came into being. They carry it across boundaries, indefinitely.

This produces a structural asymmetry that mortal scholarship has largely not parsed. **Cross-Mythos contact involving Eadras is not symmetric.** If a being from Eadras visits another Mythos, they arrive overpowered relative to the locals. If a being from another Mythos visits Eadras, the same effect does not run in reverse — the visitor is not boosted by being in Eadras; they simply visit. The advantage is *bound to origin*, not *bound to location*.

The advantage applies regardless of whether the Eadras-origin being is divine, mortal, ascended, or artifact-bound. A merchant who somehow finds their way into another Mythos via planar accident is, in that other Mythos, several times more durable and resourceful than they would have been in their native Eadras context. The merchant does not know this; the locals usually do not know it either. But it is canonically true.

## What this means in practice

Most Eadrasi never encounter the multiplier because most Eadrasi never planewalk. The capacity is too rare. But for the very small number who do — for the dispatched [[seraphim|Seraphim]] sent on cross-Mythos errands, the rare mortal scholars whose study or artifacts permit a crossing, the artifact-bearers who acquire one of the seldom-discovered cross-Mythos instruments, the Tarran exiles whose ancestors crossed into [[gaia|Gaian]] reflections — the 2–4x amplification is a real and recurring feature of their experience outside Eadras.

The great historical exception to Eadrasi planewalking rarity was the ancient [[arathi|Arathi]]. The First Civilization crossed between [[mythos|Mythoi]] as a matter of course, traded across the Omniverse, and kept a presence in [[nexus|Nexus]] — and because a scattered few of them happened to be off-world when the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] struck, those individuals survived it. They persist as a **world-less Arathi diaspora**, still carrying the full 2–4x amplification wherever they wander, and may be encountered even in the modern era. See [[arathi#Worldwalkers and the off-world diaspora]].

In other Mythoi, the experience of meeting a planewalker who came from Eadras is recognisably different from meeting any other kind of planewalker. The Eadrasi are *larger* in the cosmic sense — they push harder against the world around them, their spells work more readily, their physical persons hold up under pressures that would break locals of their tier. Cosmologically-curious scholars in those Mythoi sometimes notice the pattern and theorise about its origin; in most Mythoi, they have no way to verify the theory because they themselves cannot reach Eadras to look.

## Open canon

- **Specific named planewalkers** in Eadras-canon — open. The category is established; the named individuals are not yet documented.
- **Mortal arts that produce planewalkers** in Eadras — what training, what artifacts, what alignments — open.
- **Whether the 2–4x advantage is constant or varies with time spent away from Eadras** — current canon says *fundamentally*, suggesting persistent rather than situational. Whether sufficiently long absence eventually erodes the saturation is open.
- **Whether the same advantage applies to planewalkers from the other six [[bastion-of-light|Bastion]]-closest Mythoi** — plausibly yes if those Mythoi share the sacrifice-derived intrinsic richness, but specific cross-Mythos data is open.
- **What planewalkers from typical (non-close-to-Bastion) Mythoi look like** — the comparison case — open.
- **Whether [[dao|Dao]] counts as a planewalker** in this taxonomy. Dao walks between Mythoi but is an Avatar of the [[omniversal-creator|Omniversal Creator]] rather than a Mythos-native; he is more *cosmically prior* to the planewalker category than a member of it.
