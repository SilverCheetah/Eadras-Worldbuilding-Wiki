# Inki

**Summary**: Inki is the Ascended Mortal God of invention, craftsmanship, and discovery — the divine patron of [[gnomes]], famous in legend as the first gnome ever to breach an [[arathi|Arathi]] vault.

**Sources**: Inki.txt, Gnomish Food Culture.txt, Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## Domain

Invention, mechanical genius, the unlocking of secrets, and the pursuit of knowledge through cleverness. Inki is the patron of every gnome who solves a difficult problem, every tinker who builds a thing that has not been built before, and every scholar who deciphers a script no one else can read.

He stands apart from the older Ascended Mortal gods of Eadras: Inki is not extramythic in origin, not pre-Banishment, and not connected to the [[the-three|Three]] or the early progenitor pantheons. He is a **modern** ascended mortal — a gnome who became a god of gnomes, his ascension entirely an event of post-Cataclysm Eadras.

### Inki as the philosophical opposite of Kudur-eṭir

Inki represents what [[kudur-etir|Kudur-eṭir]] refused to be. Where Kudur-eṭir was *compassion without humility*, Inki is **curiosity with humility**. Where Kudur-eṭir was driven by **catastrophic overreach** — abolish entropy, perfect civilization forever — Inki is the patron of **rediscovery without catastrophic overreach**: a god of *finding what has been lost and using it gratefully*, not of *imposing perfection on what exists*.

Where Kudur-eṭir thought *understanding grants jurisdiction*, Inki's character is that of an old working gnome who never claims jurisdiction, only the right to keep tinkering. The two figures are read together in modern Vosian and gnomish theology as the two possible postures a brilliant mortal can take toward the world: *fix everything by force* or *find what's there and make it work*. The first ended Arath. The second made modern gnomish civilization. (source: Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt)

The affectionate common title **"Old Quickfix"** survives even after Inki's ascension precisely because it preserves this contrast: a god who is still, at heart, a working gnome with smudged hands solving the next problem in front of him.

## Appearance

Usually depicted as a small, wiry figure with wild, **snow-white hair** and **ink-stained fingers**, surrounded by floating tools, half-built mechanisms, and glowing runes. The depiction is consciously a divine flattery of a venerable working gnome — apron, smudged hands, hair gone white from age and from his blessing — rather than a stylized celestial.

## Personality

Curious, restless, and endlessly tinkering. Little patience for tradition that stands in the way of progress, but a deep respect for well-made things and for the satisfaction of a hard problem solved.

## Blessings — Inki-blessed white-haired gnomes

Gnomes born with **naturally white hair** are considered **especially blessed by Inki**. These individuals are almost always gifted with exceptional intelligence and inventive talent — true prodigies who seem to have received a direct spark of his divine genius. White-haired gnome children are watched carefully from infancy by clan elders and frequently apprenticed early to whichever master craftsman or scholar best matches the spark they show. The trait is treated as both an honor and a responsibility; an Inki-blessed gnome who wastes the gift is regarded as a serious failure of vocation. (source: Inki.txt)

## Legend — the First Vault

Inki is famous for being **the first gnome to successfully breach an [[arathi|Arathi]] vault** — an achievement still regarded as the ultimate act of cleverness and daring in gnomish culture. Every gnomish workshop tradition that engages with Arathi salvage descends symbolically from his first breach. (source: Inki.txt)

The vault Inki opened was a substantial complex — a self-contained Arathi facility, not a single chamber. Confirmed contents (user direction 2026-05-01):

- **A food fabricator** that, in low-power survival mode, dispensed **seeds** — the foundation of modern gnomish cuisine (see below).
- **A laboratory for [[magitek]] and [[crystalmancy]]** — the working space that gave the gnomes their first sustained access to Arathi engineering. Modern gnomish golem-craft, magitek factories, and crystal practice descend from study of this lab.
- **Living quarters** — preserved Arathi domestic spaces.
- **Storerooms** — and a great many other features that have been documented by the gnomes over the generations since.

This single recovery is the foundation event of post-Cataclysm gnomish technical civilization. From it descend the gnome-Shada'din magitek factory partnership, gnomish cuisine, and the symbolic chain of every modern gnomish workshop tradition.

Inki personally tested an unlabelled spice from the vault's recovered cookbook — the small black fingernail-sized pepper called **raven's eye** — expecting something pleasantly warm. It nearly blew his head off. To this day, tasting a raven's eye is the traditional first test given to any young gnome who wants to become a master chef. See [[food-and-drink]] and [[gnomes#Cuisine|Gnomish Cuisine]]. (source: Gnomish Food Culture.txt)

## Symbol

A **six-pointed gear with a single glowing rune in the center**, or alternately a **hammer striking an anvil that produces sparks shaped like runes**. Both are widely used; the six-gear is favored by builders and engineers, the hammer-and-anvil by craft guilds and forge-side temples.

## Inki's evolving names

Inki's name changed dramatically over the millennia, in lockstep with the cultural transformation his First Vault triggered. The full evolution is preserved in gnomish liturgy and tablet inscriptions:

### Pre-vault — *Inki Quickfix*

Before the First Vault, Inki was just a clever gnome with a bench-craft cognomen — perhaps an unusually inventive one, but not yet holy. **Quickfix** is a classic Pre-Vault Gnomish craft cognomen: practical, modest, exactly the kind of name a working gnome earned for solving small mechanical problems with admirable speed (see [[naming-conventions#pre-vault-gnomish|Pre-Vault Gnomish Naming]]).

### Early historical style — first formal Arathi-imitation names

After the First Vault opened, gnomes began giving Inki increasingly formal names in the post-vault Arathi-imitation style:

- ***Inki Bāb-Eṭir*** — "Inki, Gate-Opener"
- ***Inki Eš-Kalib*** — "Inki, Measurer of the Vault"
- ***Inki Zēr-Iddin*** — "Inki, to whom the Seed was Given"

### Later religious style — divine epithets

As gnomish religious tradition deepened, the names lengthened and became more sacred:

- ***Inkīlu-Bāb-Eṭir*** — "Inki of the Opened Gate"
- ***Inki-Ilum-Iddin*** — "Inki, Given by the Light"
- ***Inki-Zēr-Nāṣir*** — "Inki, Keeper of the Seed"
- ***Inki-Rabu-Eš*** — "Inki of the Great Vault"
- ***Inki-Bel-Qiru*** — "Inki, Master of Crystal"

### Most sacred title

The full ceremonial form, used in the most reverent gnomish religious contexts:

> **Inki-Bāb-Eṭir, First Opener of the Sealed World**

### And yet — among common gnomes

Among everyday gnomes, the god is still affectionately called:

> **Old Quickfix**

This contrast — between the most sacred title and the affectionate workshop nickname — is doctrinally embraced rather than corrected. Inki is **both divine and still recognizably one of them**: a god whose holy name commemorates the act that transformed civilization, and whose familiar name remembers the bench-craft prodigy he was before. Few other Eadran gods carry this kind of dual register so visibly. (source: Grug, Goblin, Arathi and Gnome naming conventions.txt)

## Open canon

- The exact circumstances and date of Inki's ascension. Canon establishes him as a modern ascended mortal post-Banishment but does not place his death and ascension within Eadras's recorded chronology.
- His relationship to the gnome-led [[golems|gnome golem-craft]] tradition. The thematic alignment is obvious; the doctrinal connection is undefined.
