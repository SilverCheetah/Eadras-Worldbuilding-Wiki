# Post-Cataclysm Fauna

**Summary**: The new creatures and pressure-echo phenomena born from the [[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]] — abyssal giants, hydrothermal-vent grazers, mineral-steam serpents, ley-feeding eels, pressure-bound death-imprints — the wildlife of the world the Drowning made. Paired with [[pre-cataclysm-relics]], which catalogs what survives from the old world. Together they bracket Eadras's most consequential ecological reset.

**Sources**: Playground generation 1st part.txt, VerdantScarAndArathCataclysm_01_InWorld_Facts.txt, Major changes to Arathi Capitol.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-13

## Overview

The [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]] killed approximately 60% of all life on Eadras. The recovery that followed, across millions of years, did not merely restore what was lost — it produced entirely new lineages adapted to habitats the Cataclysm had created. Deep ocean basins where shallow seas had stood. Hydrothermal vent fields where the crust had been unzipped. Boiling fissures along volcanic coasts. Pressure-crushed civic chambers where the surface had once been.

The fauna catalogued here are the visible flowering of that adaptation. They are real animals in the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome's]] biological register — not echoes of the lost world, not relics of the Arathi, not Sea'tua-line peoples. They are *new* life, of post-Cataclysm origin, whose habitats only exist because of the Worldbreaker.

For surviving fragments of the *old* (pre-Cataclysm) world — Stillbeasts in mineral stasis, Ashseed Forests, Arathic Glass Reefs — see [[pre-cataclysm-relics]].

For the **Bathyric Mandate** — a proposed deep-sea collective intelligence whose status (creature, polity, or both) has not been resolved — see [[open-questions]].

## Abyssal Crownwhales

Descendants of shallow-sea leviathans that fled into the newborn ocean trenches as the planet flooded. Their bony **crown-like lattice atop the skull** is a ley-pressure sensing organ, evolved to read the slow magical currents that travel through deep water. The crown reads pressure, salinity, and Eldritch flow simultaneously, giving Crownwhales an oceanographic awareness no surface beast possesses.

Sailors believe Crownwhales remember the old land-roads now drowned beneath them. The belief is poetic but not entirely wrong — Crownwhale migration paths follow the [[arathi-cataclysm#Geological Features Created|Crown Trenches]] (deep fractures radiating from the impact zone), which are also the buried courses of pre-Cataclysm river valleys and trade routes. A Crownwhale travels the geological ghosts of Arathi roads without knowing what it traces.

They are among the first true abyssal giants of Eadras, and the most reliably encountered: a Crownwhale spout reading at dawn is interpretable by trained navigators as a chart of the immediate ley-pressure environment.

## Vent Seraphs

Long, translucent, ray-like creatures born around the hydrothermal vents the unzipping created. They feed on **mineral-rich heat and Eldritch seepage** — a metabolism with no pre-Cataclysm analogue. Their wing-fins glow gold and blue along the trailing edges, visible kilometers away in clear deep water.

The name is misleading: Vent Seraphs are not celestial in any sense, not spirits, not divine. They are simply beautiful, fragile, and tied to a habitat that did not exist before the Cataclysm. [[merrow|Merrow]] and other Sea'tua-descended peoples treat them as omens of safe deep currents — a Vent Seraph holding station near a vent means the current is stable and the Eldritch seepage is within tolerable bounds. A nervous or absent Seraph means the opposite.

They congregate in the [[arathi-cataclysm#Geological Features Created|Boiling Rifts]], the active hydrothermal scars from the Cataclysm.

## Scaldwyrms

Serpentine, armored survivors that nest in boiling fissures, volcanic coasts, and unstable sea-caves. They **exhale clouds of mineral steam** that can cook flesh inside armor — the steam carries dissolved minerals at temperatures far above ordinary boiling, and condenses on cooler surfaces with corrosive precipitation.

Scaldwyrms may descend from dragon-shaped beings or from reptilian creations later altered by hydrothermal magic; **they are not true [[dragons]]**, and dragons treat them as a kind of insult by resemblance. A Scaldwyrm has no shapeshifting, no Source-borne magic, no instinct toward [[ratharil]], and no shared culture with dragonkind.

They nest along the [[arathi-cataclysm#Geological Features Created|Steamfall Cliffs]] and within [[pre-cataclysm-relics#Materials of the Cataclysm|star-pumice]] fields, which is one of the major reasons star-pumice mining is dangerous: a Scaldwyrm nest hidden in a pumice deposit may be discovered only by losing the first mining crew.

## Crown-Eels

Enormous silver-black eels that coil around drowned towers and ley-spires — particularly any surviving Arathi conduit-array infrastructure on the sea floor (outside the Altea impact, where nothing survived). They feed on **residual Eldritch leaking from broken Arathi conduits**, which gives them an unusually long lifespan and unusually high resistance to ordinary deep-sea predators.

Their spines hold enchantment underwater — a rare property in modern thaumaturgy — and are prized illegally because the legitimate market for Crown-Eel spine cannot keep pace with demand. The Council of [[lirra|Lirra's]] Magic and modern conduit cities both forbid trade in Crown-Eel parts on conservation grounds; the Black Hand is reputed to handle the underground supply.

A Crown-Eel coiled around a drowned spire is a sign that the spire's Eldritch infrastructure is still partially active — useful intelligence for archaeologists, dangerous intelligence for anyone trying to extract the spire intact.

## The Lantern Dead

Pressure-bound death-echoes trapped in diving bells, crystal corridors, and sealed civic chambers from drowned Arathi cities (again, not Altea, which left no remnants). They appear as **drifting lights inside old helmets, glass masks, or sealed armor** — the lights are not flames; they are the magical residue of a final moment of consciousness, held in place by pressure differential and Arathi crystal integrity.

The Lantern Dead are not undead. They are not spirits. They are **imprints** — pressure echoes that have retained enough coherence to be visually and ritually distinguishable, but not enough to be persons.

Necromancers and Sea'tua spirit-guides can speak with them, but **only by equalizing pressure both physically and ritually**. A diver who brings a Lantern Dead too rapidly toward the surface causes the echo to disperse — sometimes silently, sometimes with a long thin cry that is not a voice but the sound of pressure leaving a container. The dispersal is irreversible.

The Lantern Dead are the closest the modern world comes to interviewing Arathi citizens. They are not Arathi citizens. The distinction is one of the standing lessons of Arathic necromancy: most "Arathi ghosts" recovered from the sea floor are imprints, not spirits. The Arathi as persons did not survive. (See [[arathi-cataclysm#All Arathi died — the extinction was absolute|Arathi Cataclysm — All Arathi died]].)

## Pressure Echoes

A more general category beneath the [[#The Lantern Dead|Lantern Dead]]: **residual patterns trapped in abyssal glass**, repeating final moments without full personhood. After millions of years, bodies are gone unless mineral-preserved, sealed, or magically maintained — but patterns can persist in the crystalline matrix of [[pre-cataclysm-relics#Arathic Glass Reefs|Arathic Glass]] and in deep sealed chambers.

A Pressure Echo is not a creature in the ordinary sense; it has no metabolism, no agency, and no choice. It is closer to a **looped magical recording** — the last second of a person's life replayed in slow time at deep ocean pressure, visible to anyone who passes the glass at the right angle.

Necromancers studying the Arathic Glass Reefs treat Pressure Echoes as **archaeology with moral consequences**: the recordings are real, the people are not, but watching a person's last second over and over is not without weight. The doctrine that has emerged: *witness once, then move on*. To replay an Echo deliberately, more than once, for amusement or curiosity, is considered theologically unsound by every magical tradition that has examined the question.

## What is conspicuously absent

The fauna above are real. The following are *not* real, despite what folk tradition sometimes claims:

- **Arathi ghosts in the modern sense** — no Arathi person survives as spirit, shade, or revenant. The Lantern Dead and Pressure Echoes are imprints, not spirits. (See [[arathi-cataclysm#All Arathi died — the extinction was absolute]] and [[ixthal]] for the one ascended survivor.)
- **Surviving Altean wildlife** — Altea was annihilated. Nothing biological from the impact city survived.
- **Submerged Arathi as a people** — the Drowned Continuities (sealed undersea vault-cities preserving Arathi populations) were briefly proposed by historians and conclusively rejected. Every last Arathi *on Eadras* perished; no submerged population survived. (Off-world Arathi survivors, abroad in the Omniverse when the world broke, are a separate matter — see [[arathi#Worldwalkers and the off-world diaspora]].)
- **Bathyric Mandate as currently-confirmed canon** — proposed as creature, polity, or both; status deferred. See [[open-questions]].

The post-Cataclysm fauna are the world's *new* answer to the Drowning, not its memory of what drowned.
