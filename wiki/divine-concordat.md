# The Divine Concordat

**Summary**: The **Divine Concordat** — full ceremonial title *"the Divine Concordat of Joint Agreement between the Peoples and Gods of Eadras"* — was the multi-article treaty drafted by the **Coalition of Disillusioned High Command** during the late [[demon-war|Demon War]] and sealed by the Elder Gods. The Concordat is the load-bearing canonical instrument of the [[great-betrayal|Great Betrayal]]: the mortal Coalition delivered the [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] empty (per Article Four), the gods destroyed the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]], drove back the demonic legions, and sealed the Demonfall Gate, and the Coalition pre-accepted the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] (Article Six). It is the **single canonical reference case** of a mortal-divine bargain honored *in full*, with no later revision, dispute, or punitive amendment.

## The Coalition

The **Coalition of Disillusioned High Command** opened secret channels to the Elder Gods within weeks of the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]]. The membership was the same coalition that has long appeared in [[great-betrayal#The Coalition|Great Betrayal canon]]:

- Disillusioned [[tarans|Tarran]] generals — those whose veterans had been bled at the Final Wall
- [[velrathi|Velrathi]] archmagi who had watched their people consumed
- Surviving [[rathari|Rathari]] Conclave leaders, before the [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar|three-way schism]] solidified
- Key priests and oracles who relayed the gods' responses

The Coalition's name — *Disillusioned High Command* — is preserved in [[katharim|Katharim]] historical ledgers and in surviving [[velrathi|Velrathi]] archival correspondence at [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]]. It captures the Coalition's defining feature: they were the empire's own command structure turned against the empire's own emperor, in the name of the alliance the empire was refusing to serve.

## The Concordat's structure

The Concordat is a **multi-article instrument**, drafted across several months of secret negotiation. The articles known by name and number in surviving record:

- **Article Four** — the operative clause obliging the [[firethorn-throne|Firethorn Throne]] to be **emptied**. Article Four was signed by the Coalition three days before the poisoning at the Eastern Front. Its signing is the canonical legal-cosmological boundary between *preparation* and *act*: once Article Four was signed, [[velveth|Kathira's]] participation in the poisoning was the fulfillment of a sealed clause rather than the initiation of an act. In Velrathi archival language, **"Article Four was signed"** is the formal indicator that the [[the-long-quiet|Long Quiet]] was already determined.
- **Article Six** — the **pre-accepted clause** under which the Coalition agreed, in advance, that the Elder Gods would render *judgment on the age* alongside the bargain's fulfillment. The gods' rendering took the form of the [[great-banishment|Great Banishment]] of the three progenitor races to the three reflections of [[gaia|Gaia]]. Because the Coalition pre-accepted Article Six, the Banishment **does not count as a punitive amendment** to the bargain. It is part of the original instrument.

Articles One, Two, Three, Five, and any after Six are open canon. Surviving fragments suggest Articles One and Two named the active-intervention deliverables (kill the Demon Prince, drive back the legions, seal the [[demonfall-gate|Demonfall Gate]]); Articles Three and Five concerned procedural matters (oracle-channels, witness-arrangements, the Coalition's authority to speak for the empire). The complete instrument has not been recovered in any single canonical record.

## The bargain's deliverables

The Concordat obligated the Elder Gods, after the Throne was emptied, to perform the following within the next several months:

- **Destroy the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince of the Open Wound]]** through coordinated divine action — the Demon Prince was reduced, banished, and its anchor in Eadras unmade
- **Drive back the demonic legions** that had penetrated the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] — most were destroyed, the remainder fled back through the gate
- **Seal the [[demonfall-gate|Demonfall Gate]]** — the breach through which [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] had originally summoned the Demon Prince, closing the major demonic incursion route into Eadras for the duration of the present age

The deliverables were **performed in full**. There was no later revision, no diplomatic recalibration, no dispute over what had been promised. The gods received the empty Throne and they did what they had committed to do.

## The Weave was not a deliverable

A common modern misconception is that [[lirra|Lirra's]] [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave|Weave]] was part of the Concordat's deliverables. This is **incorrect**. Lirra wove the Weave early in the Demon War as a defensive structure specifically to prevent demonic energivore-siphoning of Eadras's magical infrastructure. By the time the Concordat was sealed, the Weave was already operational.

What the [[firethorn-throne|Throne's]] protective field had been blocking was the Elder Gods' *direct intervention against the Demon Prince and its legions* — not the structural-defensive magic Lirra was already maintaining. The Concordat's deliverables concerned the **active-intervention** clauses (kill the Prince, drive back the legions, seal the Gate). All of them were performed exactly as agreed.

## The Coalition asked Kathira; the gods received the result

A subtle but important distinction in cosmological agency:

The **Coalition** drafted the Concordat, sealed it, and asked [[velveth|Kathira Spellsinger]] specifically to perform the hand-clause of Article Four. Kathira accepted the Coalition's request. The Elder Gods did not address her directly with the request; they received what the Coalition delivered, under the terms the Coalition had signed.

This matters because it preserves the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] in its proper shape: the gods *did not direct a mortal to kill another mortal*. The mortals organized themselves under terrible constraint and *asked the gods for help* on terms the mortals had drafted. The gods accepted the offered terms. The hand was always mortal.

This shape is what makes the Concordat such a load-bearing case for the broader theological coherence of Eadras: it shows that the gods can be *bargained with* — not commanded, but bargained with, by mortal coalitions who understand the constraints under which the gods operate.

## The reference case for bargains honored in full

The Concordat is the **single canonical case** of a mortal-divine bargain honored exactly as sealed, with no later amendment. Most divine-mortal bargains in Eadras are partial, revised, or disputed. The Concordat is not.

[[velveth|Velveth's]] existence at the divine level is the living memorial of this fact. Her Apotheosis is the gods' acknowledgment that *Kathira's hand was the load-bearing moment of the Concordat's completion*. Any future mortal-divine bargain in Eadras can be measured against the Concordat as the historical baseline: *if the bargain is properly framed and the mortal side delivers, the gods deliver in full*.

The gods of Eadras are [[divine-bureaucracy|exhausted administrators]], but **they are not faithless**. The Concordat proves it.

## How the Concordat is remembered

- **[[katharim|Katharim]]** preserve the full text of Articles Four and Six, with marginalia and the named-witnesses of each signing. The other articles survive only in fragments.
- **[[velrathi|Velrathi]] archives at [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]]** preserve Article Four's signing-witness ledger, including the names of Coalition members who countersigned alongside Kathira's House Valtharion.
- **[[tarans|Tarran]] exilic tradition** records the Concordat with bitter precision — it is the legal instrument under which their empire was, by their own hand, ended.
- **[[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]] doctrine** treats the Concordat as the foundational case of *the price collected for our continued existence*.
- **Modern [[bold-republic|Bold]], [[orden|Orden]], and other [[merchant-traditions|merchant traditions]]** invoke Concordat-form treaty language in major commercial contracts — *"under the Concordat-shape"* — meaning *a bargain whose mortal side delivers and whose divine countersign is final*.

## Open canon

- The **full text** of all the Concordat's articles — only Four and Six are securely named; Articles One, Two, Three, Five, and any beyond Six are open
- **Where the Concordat instrument is physically held** today — the original document, with all signatures and divine countersigns, is open canon
- Whether **further articles** governed the post-war disposition of the [[arathi|Arathi]] crystal infrastructure, the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] founding, or the Weave's post-war role — possible, not confirmed
- Whether the **Coalition itself outlived the Concordat's enactment**, or dissolved with the empire it had betrayed — open
