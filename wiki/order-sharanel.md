# The Order Sharanel

**Summary**: The Order Sharanel is the singular ancient and most prestigious order of [[netharim|Netharim]] in Eadras, founded during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] in the same act of divine making by which [[lirra|Lirra]] created the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] and conferred the capacity for Netharim practice on approximately ten thousand mortals. **Netharim are the members of Order Sharanel**; the Order serves simultaneously as the foremost academy of magical instruction and as the primary enforcers of Lirra's will in the mortal world.

**Sources**: More corrections.txt; More info 5-4-26.txt; Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt; **user direction 2026-05-14 (make-it-mine pass on the magic system): Sorcerers retired in favor of Netharim, who are the members of Order Sharanel; Weavespinners replaced by [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]; the Weave is now a purely defensive structure sustained by the lives of every magic user).**

## Name

The Order's canonical name is **Order Sharanel** — often shortened to **Sharanel** or simply **the Order** in context.

*Sharanel* is a blended linguistic construction drawn from the three [[peoples-of-eadras|progenitor humanoid race]] tongues without belonging to any single tradition. It denotes those aligned with order, structure, and the maintenance of the Weave. The blended construction is itself meaningful — the Order serves all sapient peoples of Eadras and is anchored to none of them in particular. (source: More info 5-4-26.txt)

## Founding

The Order Sharanel was founded **during the height of the [[demon-war|Demon War]]**, in the same act of divine making by which [[lirra|Lirra]] created the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] and **conferred the capacity for [[netharim|Netharim]] practice on approximately ten thousand mortals**. The Weave, the first Netharim, and the Order Sharanel as their organizing institution all came into being together — a single integrated act answering the catastrophic instability and mass loss of life of the Demon War, when demonic forces were consuming both life and Eldritch energy at scale. (source: More info 5-4-26.txt; user direction 2026-05-14)

This makes the Order among the oldest standing institutions on Eadras. It is older than every modern nation, every modern religion's hierarchy, and most surviving cities.

The Order Sharanel is the singular canonical Lirran order — there has only ever been one.

### The First Ten Thousand

The first ten thousand whose capacity Lirra opened were not assembled from a single profession or social class. The composition was deliberately mixed: (source: More info 5-4-26.txt)

- **Trained practitioners** — wizards already studying magic in Velrathi, Arathi-derived, or hybrid traditions. A minority of the founding cohort.
- **Some priests** — a smaller group of clergy from various pantheons, transmuted alongside the wizards.
- **A majority of ordinary individuals** — the bulk of the cohort came from people with **no prior magical training**: farmers, artisans, soldiers, scholars, sailors, merchants, parents, children. Lirra did not select for prior aptitude. She selected for whatever inner resonance her transmutation required, and most of the resonant were ordinary.

This is a load-bearing fact about the Order's character. The Order Sharanel was not founded as an elite academy of wizard-survivors. It was founded as a mass body of newly-capable commoners, bound to the Weave by their lives themselves, and trained into Netharim discipline only afterward — the Order's founding training program was developed *during the war itself*, to teach the new initiates how to use what Lirra had given them.

### No Progenitors Among the First Netharim

**No member of the three progenitor humanoid races — [[elfen|Elfen]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], or [[tarans|Taran]] — was among the founding ten thousand.** The founding membership was drawn entirely from other populations present at the time: the engineered/descendant races (Aetherin, Orug, Grug, El'mer, Sea'tua, Rathar, Mitan, and others), and presumably any other non-progenitor mortal peoples within reach of Lirra's transmutation. (source: More info 5-4-26.txt)

The reason for this exclusion is not stated in the source. Plausible readings:

- The progenitor races already had their patron Three and their established magical traditions; transmuting them would have crossed Mandate-of-Genesis lines or been seen as competing for souls Lirra did not have claim to.
- The progenitors' magical biology — particularly the Velrathi as Masters of Magic — may have been incompatible with the transmutation itself, or may have produced unstable Weave-anchors.
- Lirra may have deliberately built the Order on populations *outside* the three Mandate races to ensure the Weave's mortal foundation was not aligned to any one progenitor's interests.
- The progenitors' impending [[great-banishment|Banishment to Gaia]] at the close of the Demon War may have made them unsuitable as load-bearing Weave anchors that needed to remain on Eadras.

Whichever the reason, the consequence is canonical: **the Weave's mortal foundation has never included a member of the three progenitor races.** Any Netharim of Elfen, Velrathi, or Taran lineage who has appeared in the millennia since must therefore have arisen through ordinary post-founding transmission rather than through the founding act — and remains an unusual case.

### Netharim as Living Sustainers of the Weave

The first ten thousand Netharim were not merely transformed; they became **integral sustainers of the Weave itself**. The mechanism generalises: **every living magic user — Netharim and otherwise — contributes to the Weave's defensive coherence with their own life**. This is the load-bearing relationship between mortal magical practice and the Weave that makes the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade's]] long-tail damage to the Weave possible. The Crusade's mass killings of *all* magic users (not just Order members) weakened the Weave's general sustenance; the killings of three [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] in particular caused structural damage at the conduit junctions those Windspeakers had been anchoring.

This relationship is the Order's deepest practical secret. See [[anti-magic-crusade#The Hidden Truth]] for how knowledge of it is controlled.

## The Order as Lirra's face — why no priesthood

Lirra is **not permitted a conventional priesthood**, and the reason is structural: in Eadras, **every act of spellcasting is, technically, an act of worship of Lirra.** Every magic-user is therefore already, functionally, her worshipper — so a dedicated priesthood would be redundant. She already has every mage. (See [[lirra#Her Institutions: No Priesthood]].)

In a priesthood's place stands the Order. The Order is **Lirra's face in the world** — the institution through which her will is carried, her Towers maintained, and her presence held at the cosmologically significant conduit-ends. It is a **humanoid** institution, distinct from the draconic servitor castes: [[kobolds]] and the other [[draconic-servitor-races|draconic servitor races]] have nothing to do with Tower upkeep (correcting an earlier loose association) — Tower maintenance is the Order's work alone. The Order is also **recent** in the long view, postdating [[ratharil|Ratharil]] and the dragons' own relationship with the gods by **millions of years**, which is why a dragon shrine — not a Tower — marks Ratharil's conduit-end (see [[#Fourteen conduit ends, seven Towers remain]]). (source: conduits-towers-order.txt, 2026-06-03)

## The Towers of Lirra

The Order is not a single campus but a **federation of Towers** distributed across Eadras. Each Tower of Lirra is a self-contained chapter-house: a magical academy, a research and ritual sanctum, an archive, and a regional seat of Lirra's mortal-world authority. Towers train Novices and Initiates locally, run their own research programs, and maintain their own libraries and laboratories.

Each Tower is governed by its own Council of seven — see the grade structure below. The Voices of Lirra / [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] constitute the Order's unifying apex above all Towers.

### Built on conduit ends

A **Tower of Lirra is built directly on top of a conduit end** — one of the fourteen places where the seven planet-piercing [[seven-conduits|Seven Conduits]] emerge to the surface. Without the conduit, there is no Tower; with it, the Tower is the **institutional seat and physical home of the assembly of magic-users** that gathers at the conduit-end to exploit its extraordinary natural concentration of magic. A Tower does **not regulate the conduit** — the conduit is a non-physical, core-anchored **drain** that needs no regulation and cannot be damaged from the surface (see [[seven-conduits]]); people simply gather where the magic pools deepest. A Tower organizes the *community and its work*, not the conduit, and a fallen Tower is a scattered mage-community, not a ruptured engine.

To ordinary perception, a conduit end appears as a **magic sink** — a place where ambient magic flows naturally down into the earth. To **magical perception**, the same place appears as a **column of unbelievable magical power** rising vertically from the ground. The flow at a conduit end is so concentrated that **any magician who steps inside the column is dissolved** — flooded with so much power their body simply unmakes itself. Towers are therefore built *around* conduit ends, not over them: their inner sanctums (Spindle Chamber, Poleheart, Conduit Mouth — see [[conduit-cities]]) ring the column of power without intruding on it. The Windspeaker who anchors that junction is the sole exception — their innate capacity, and the binding that anchors them to the junction, permits them to exist within the column the way no other magic user can. (source: More corrections.txt — clarifying conversation 2026-04-30; user direction 2026-05-14)

### Fourteen conduit ends, seven Towers remain

There are **fourteen conduit ends** — the seven Weave conduits each with two antipodal surface termini (see [[seven-conduits]]). Of the fourteen sites, **seven still have a standing Tower** of Lirra today. The breakdown:

- **Seven Towers stand.** These form the Order's federated backbone. **Three of the seven are submarine** — oceanic Towers grown by oceanic Netharim on the three [[conduit-cities|conduit ends that emerge underwater]] — and four are dry-land surface Towers (including the oldest, the [[bold|Tower of Bold]]). The three submarine Towers' inaccessibility to the surface [[anti-magic-crusade|Crusade]] is a principal reason a full seven endured; surface records, written by surface scholars, often counted only the four they could see (see [[#Oceanic Towers and chapters]] below).
- **Six Towers were destroyed — most, but not all, in the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]]** roughly 9,000–10,000 years ago (in the decades immediately preceding the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]]), when the killing of [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] caused structural damage at the conduit junctions they anchored and the termini at those junctions suffered catastrophe so total that no Tower has been rebuilt. **At least one of the six fell far earlier, in the [[demon-war|Demon War]]:** a conduit-city at the edge of the [[demonfall-marches|Demon Wastes]], devastated in the war and never reclaimed, because the blight made resettlement impossible. Its Tower is rubble — but the conduit beneath it still pools, unwatched, at the edge of the blight, a rich and dangerous loose thread. ([[amunorians|Sarathis]] is a Crusade loss; the Demon-Wastes ruin a Demon-War loss; the full distribution of fall-causes across the six is open canon.) (user direction 2026-06-03, spreading the fall-causes across more than one catastrophe)
- **One conduit end never had a Tower at all** — **[[ratharil|Ratharil]]**, the City of Dragons. Ratharil's end is ancient sacred dragon-ground, and dragons keep their own older, direct relationship with the gods (the [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Nine Embers]] are themselves ascended dragons); they neither use nor permit a Tower of Lirra, marking the conduit instead with a primordial **dragon shrine** that predates the Order by millions of years. The Order has no presence at Ratharil. (See [[ratharil#The conduit shrine]].) (user direction 2026-06-03)

The arithmetic closes on the fourteen canonical ends: **7 standing (3 submarine + 4 surface) + 6 destroyed + 1 Ratharil (shrine, never a Tower) = 14.** The lost six sites are dangerous, abandoned, or sealed-off. The [[verdant-scar|Verdant Scar]] is **not** among the fourteen modern ends — its forest grew over the ruined Arathi array at Arath-Veyr, which stood at an *Arathi-era* conduit end (paired with [[altea|Altea]]) but is not one of Lirra's fourteen modern ends. (user direction 2026-06-03, replacing the earlier reading that seated the Verdant Scar as the never-Tower end; [[amunorians|Sarathis]]'s modern conduit Far-Kin is now open canon.)

### Oceanic Towers and chapters

The [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] is planetary; it does not stop at the waterline. Three of the fourteen [[seven-conduits|conduit]] ends **emerge underwater**, and conduit ends are the strongest Weave-sources on the planet — so [[netharim|Netharim]] arise among the [[merrow|oceanic peoples]] as naturally as anywhere else, indeed nearly of necessity, since the [[merrow|Merrow]], [[sirens|Sirens]], [[shimmercloaks|Shimmercloaks]], [[ink-lords|Ink Lords]], and [[tideclad|Tideclad]] live atop three of the fourteen conduit ends. **Oceanic Netharim are a branch of the same tradition as surface Netharim, not a separate magic.** (source: EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt, 2026-05-31)

Towers of Lirra were — and may still be — built on the submarine conduit-ends by these oceanic Netharim, in **oceanic form**: grown coral fused with **[[crystalmancy#3. Aqualith — Sea Sapphires / Tide Opals|Aqualith]]** (the canonical sea-crystal lineage) rather than dry-land masonry. The Order Sharanel is therefore best understood as a **planetary (surface-and-sea) order with oceanic chapters**, crewed by oceanic Netharim and [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] who may be Merrow, Tideclad, or any of the five peoples. The surface canon's "seven surviving Towers" should be read with this in mind: the surface world records what surface civilization built and can see, and so under-documents the oceanic chapters — consistent with the world's surface-biased epistemology.

Because the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] was a surface political-religious movement that could not reach the seafloor, the oceanic Towers and their Netharim **likely survived the Crusade intact** — making the oceanic peoples the probable keepers of an unbroken Netharim-and-Tower tradition while the surface lost six Towers and suffered the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]. How many of the oceanic towers are intact versus ruined, whether the surface world even knows of them, and which peoples hold which submarine conduit-cities are open canon.

### The Tower of Bold — the oldest

The **oldest Tower of Lirra is in the city of [[bold|Bold]]**. As the seat of the founding chapter, the Tower of Bold has special seniority within the Order's federated structure. Whether that seniority is formal (a "Mother Tower" with veto authority) or merely traditional (first among equals) is one of the open questions of the Order's modern governance. (source: More corrections.txt)

## Duties

The Order exists for the following ongoing functions (source: More corrections.txt):

1. **Training new [[netharim|Netharim]].** Most trained Netharim in the world either belong to the Order or received their training from it. The Order's program is rigorous and selective, and a Netharim's institutional standing is bound up with the depth of their internal reality-model — which the Order's training is built to develop.
2. **Identifying and binding [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]].** Order agents recognise the markedly greater innate capacity of nascent Windspeakers, often in childhood, and bring them into the Order before any other magical tradition can claim them.
3. **Magical research.** The Order maintains the most advanced standing magical research apparatus in Eadras.
4. **Lending power to the border guard during times of crisis.** The Order is a strategic reserve of magical force, deployable when civilizational threats emerge.
5. **Various other tasks** — operations, missions, and assignments as Lirra directs through her clergy and priestly channels.

## Membership and the Oath of Service

The Order's pipeline runs in two stages:

- **Trainee / graduate.** Most students who complete the Order's program graduate without taking permanent vows. Many of them go on to careers in courts, academies, militaries, and trade — the Order's training is valuable in itself, and the Order benefits from a wide diaspora of well-trained alumni.
- **Sworn member.** Graduates may be offered the chance to swear the **Oath of Service** and formally join the Order, **binding themselves to Lirra's service for life**. Sworn membership entails commitments and protections that distinguish them sharply from civilian alumni.

(source: More corrections.txt)

### The Sworn Eye amulet

Every sworn member of the Order Sharanel wears the **Sworn Eye amulet** — the formal regalia of Order membership. It is a small, consecrated amulet that visibly marks the wearer as Lirra-sworn and serves as the Order's primary identifying token: priests, civil authorities, fellow Netharim, and well-informed laypeople recognize the Sworn Eye on sight as evidence of full Order membership. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

The Sworn Eye is not worn by trainees, by graduates who declined the Oath of Service, or by Netharim trained outside the Order. It is the binary external signal of *sworn vs. not sworn* status within the Order's two-stage pipeline.

Specifics — the amulet's material composition, the exact rite by which it is consecrated, what magical functions (if any) it performs beyond identification, and how it is replaced if lost or destroyed — are open canon.

## Grade structure

Sworn members of the Order ascend through a six-grade hierarchy. Below the Adept tier, members are still in training; from Adept up, they are full members of the Order with corresponding authority and responsibility (source: More corrections.txt — clarifying conversation 2026-04-30).

| Grade | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Novice** | In training | Earliest stage of formal Order education. |
| **Initiate** | In training | Second stage; deeper instruction in spellcraft and Order doctrine. |
| **Lirrian Adept** | Sworn (graduate) | Graduation tier. A student who has completed the Order's rigorous program and taken the Oath of Service. Most Order-sworn Netharim in active practice are Adepts. |
| **Lirrian Master** | Sworn | Senior practitioners who teach Adepts, lead chapter-houses, run research programs, and command field operations. |
| **Lirrian Councilor** | Sworn | The governing body of an individual Tower. **Each Tower of Lirra has exactly seven Lirrian Councilors**, mirroring the sevenfold conduit geometry of the Weave. Each Councilor holds institutional authority over a designated domain of Tower affairs — doctrine, research, operational deployment, training, archive, civil liaison, and conduit-junction oversight, in the most common (though not the only) division. Councilors collectively direct their Tower's affairs. With seven Councilors per Tower across the seven surviving Towers, the Order has 49 Councilors total. |
| **Voice of Lirra / Windspeaker** | Sworn (apex) | The Order's apex tier and the goddess's direct mortal voices, sitting above all Towers. **Voice of Lirra** and **[[windspeakers\|Windspeaker]]** are closely-related offices: a Windspeaker anchors a specific conduit junction by virtue of their innate capacity and the heavy duty laid upon them; *Voice of Lirra* is the leadership-coordination title that the Order's most senior Windspeakers carry when they speak for the Order as a whole. There are as many Windspeakers as there are anchored junctions; the Voice of Lirra title rotates or distributes among them by Order custom. (See [[windspeakers]].) |

The Voices of Lirra (drawn from the Windspeaker ranks) form the Order's unifying apex. Below them, each Tower is governed by its own seven Councilors. Below the Councilors at each Tower, Lirrian Masters carry day-to-day institutional authority; Adepts staff the bulk of operations; Initiates and Novices fill the training ranks.

## Relationship to other institutions

The Order is the **single largest concentration of arcane practice in Eadras**. This gives it natural friction with:

- **The [[black-hand|Black Hand]]**, which actively cultivates compromised Netharim (see *Recruitment and control* in [[black-hand]]) and which engineered the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] ~9,000–10,000 years ago as a Spear-search diversion (preceded by the hundred-year [[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]] ramp-up). The Order suffered catastrophically during the Crusade and has never fully forgiven the institutions that participated in its scapegoating — even though it now understands that the Black Hand's primary intent was diversion, not Weave sabotage.
- **Civil authorities** that distrust concentrated magical power. Some kingdoms try to tax, license, or restrict Order activity; the Order's response varies by goddess-mood and political moment.
- **[[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]** — the Order treats this specialised role with respect bordering on reverence; the Windspeakers are the Order's load-bearing apex and the Order's institutional life is organised around protecting and preparing them.

The [[white-palm|White Palm]] coordinates with the Order on counter-Black-Hand work, sharing intelligence on cells that target magic users.

## What's been resolved (user direction 2026-05-01 and 2026-05-14)

- **Lirran Tower-craft is a combination of magical traditions** — not a preserved Arathi lineage and not a single inherited art. Lirra drew from multiple living magical practices and synthesized them into the Tower craft that anchors the Weave conduits.
- **Voice of Lirra and Windspeaker are closely related but not identical.** *Windspeaker* is the cosmological role (anchor of a conduit junction by innate capacity); *Voice of Lirra* is the institutional leadership-coordination title that the most senior Windspeakers carry when speaking for the Order. The two are linked but conceptually distinct.

## Open canon

- The **locations** of the other six surviving Towers (the seventh, in [[bold|Bold]], is the oldest and is named) — now known to include **three submarine oceanic Towers** (see [[#Oceanic Towers and chapters]]); which of the five oceanic peoples hold which submarine conduit-cities is open.
- Whether the Tower of Bold has formal authority over the other six Towers, or only ceremonial seniority.
- The **specific identities** of the six destroyed Tower sites — which conduit junctions broke, where their termini stood, and the full distribution of fall-causes (most Crusade, at least one a [[demon-war|Demon War]] loss; the Demon-Wastes-edge ruin and the unnamed fifth eastern site still need names — see [[open-questions]]).
- Specific Tower layout, rituals, and inter-Tower coordination protocols.
- Notable historical Sharaneli / Archmages / saints.
- The Order's role during the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]], the [[demon-war|Demon War]], and the [[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]] — all need elaboration.
- The specific magical traditions Lirra synthesized when crafting Tower-craft.
- The exact division of domains across the seven Lirrian Councilors at each Tower (the "seven domains" listed above are the most common rendering, not the only one).
- How the Voice-of-Lirra title circulates among the Windspeaker ranks.
