# Nox

**Summary**: Nox is the Elder God of night, hidden wisdom, secrets, treachery, darkness, the undead, and the loathsome — the smooth, sarcastic, dangerously charming knower of everyone's secrets, disliked by almost the entire pantheon except [[sylph|Sylph]].

**Sources**: More Info on the Elder Gods.txt, Creation Myth.txt, Gods of Eadras.txt, snake-folk_1.txt (2026-06-02 ingest — the Thosh role)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-02

---

## Domain

Shadows, deception, forbidden knowledge, and corruption of death (source: More Info on the Elder Gods.txt). Formal pantheon listing covers night, hidden wisdom, secrets, treachery, darkness, undead, and the loathsome (source: Gods of Eadras.txt).

He was the seventh of the [[creation-myth|ten Elder Gods]] to step forward in the first naming, declaring: *"I am Nox — god of night."* In the great work, **he hid the secrets**. In the [[lirra|Lirran]] cosmological summary, *"Nox whispers."*

## Personality

Smooth, sarcastic, cynical, and dangerously charming. **He knows everyone's secrets and enjoys playing both sides** (source: More Info on the Elder Gods.txt).

## Relationships

- **Disliked by almost everyone** in the pantheon
- **Except** [[sylph|Sylph]], who finds him entertaining (and he loves to tease her)
- **Strong rivalry with** [[shani|Shani]] — born of his liberal use of [[necromancy-and-undeath|undeath and necromancy]], which Shani sees as meddling with what should be final
- **Tension with** [[shali|Shali]] — for the same reason; Shali views necromancy as distasteful and a mockery of death
- **Grudging respect for** [[lirra|Lirra]] — though Lirra dislikes him for corrupting magic
- **Watched closely by** [[ioa|Ioa]]
- **Strongly disliked by** [[qoxli|Qoxli]] and [[raja|Raja]] (the latter for his treachery)

## Theological Note

The death twins do **not** view necromancy and undeath as inherently evil — only as a violation of their domain. This nuance distinguishes Nox's transgression from a moral judgment. (See [[necromancy-and-undeath]].)

## The breakaway snake folk — Thosh

Nox is the unacknowledged origin of the [[snake-folk|snake folk]] death-cult. During the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] (~9,500 ya), he found the dragon-made snake folk — a magical people in existential terror during an anti-magic genocide — and **sprang the desperate faction free of dragon-control.** He did not manufacture their plight; he *exploited* the existing fault line, characteristically playing both sides, and then **cut them loose and walked away.** He could free the people but could not break the dragon-gods' [[draconic-servitor-races#The Mandate|divine Mandate]] on their reproduction — that was never his to touch.

Unable to bear that their liberator did not want them, the breakaway rewrote their memory of Nox into **Thosh** — a god who *does* want them, who calls them home, who loves them enough to unmake them. They worship the pantheon's least-loved, most-treacherous god as the one being who cares about them most. He hears the prayers; he declines; he **does not accept their worship.** It is a fitting cruelty for the knower of secrets who plays every side: the god who freed them is real, present, and refusing them the entire time, while they pray to a name they invented for him. (Full treatment: [[snake-folk#Theology — Thosh is a misremembered Nox|Snake Folk]].)

The corruption-of-death thread in his domain rhymes with this: a people he freed turned death-obsessed, longing for un-being, never knowing the god behind their god.
