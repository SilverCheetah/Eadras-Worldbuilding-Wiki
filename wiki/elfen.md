# Elfen

**Summary**: The Elfen are the **first** progenitor humanoid race, created roughly 40,000 years ago by [[the-three|Mark]] (eldest of the Three) — not as a tool but as a **love letter**: made in the image of who Mark had become across his 11,000-year childhood, an immortal in love with the wild. Tall, song-shaping, biologically immortal beings born adult from [[womb-trees|womb trees]], the Elfen are **equilibrium-keepers** who hold that nature *and* civilization are both necessary and that neither may consume the other. They make only by singing over living matter, cannot digest flesh, and tend the world's balance because, at root, they *are* a balance.

## Overview

The Elfen were the first race [[the-three|Mark]] made — and he did **not** build them as a fearful counterforce against civilization. He built them **out of love**: the expression of an immortal who had spent eleven thousand years falling in love with the wild, the intricate web of life, exploration, and freedom. They are his beloved, made in the image of who he became. And he **walked among them for many millennia** before he left — so the Elfen inherited a *parent who lived with them and taught by living*, not a distant blueprint. They know what Mark was and is. (See [[the-three]] for the maker; the love-letter framing reframes, but does not contradict, the older "wardens of the wildlands" description.)

**Physical description**: roughly 3 meters tall, slender, strong, and durable, with an almost ethereal presence. They have no reproductive equipment and do not reproduce sexually (see [[#Reproduction — born adult from womb trees|below]]). Their metabolism runs on [[eldritch|Eldritch]] and plant processes — partly **autotrophic** on ambient mana — bonded to the elemental planes of earth and water.

## Equilibrium-keepers, not anti-civilization

The Elfen and their descendants **do not hate civilization.** They see it as **necessary** — part of the balance. They guard against **imbalance in either direction**: not the wild encroaching on a world emptied of civilization, and not civilization consuming all of the wild. Selling heartwood to the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] and [[tarans|Taran]], and watching cities rise from it, are **not** compromises with a feared enemy — civilization existing *is* part of the balance they tend. They know exactly what they do and why.

This reframes their old "warden" reputation without discarding it: they are wardens of **balance**, not partisans of the wild. Their distrust falls on **all** zealotry, including pro-nature zealotry. The [[demon-war|Demon War]] and the [[great-banishment|Banishment]] are wounds *to the balance* (and to a keeper-race), not proof of a flawed design.

## Making, materials, and cost

The Elfen make **only by song over living matter** — additive, grown work, never subtractive, smelted, cast, or finely geared. This is a **capability**, not a preference (see [[elfen-architecture]] for the full architectural tradition).

- **Non-consumptive making.** Singing does not harvest or kill the plant; the plant is infused with [[eldritch|Eldritch]] and induced to grow. New material is grown, or older (more mana-dense) material is expelled. The tree lives — the process may even enrich it.
- **A lopsided tech ceiling.** The Elfen overshoot wildly on anything grown, shaped, or alive, and hit a hard floor on anything cast, drawn, smelted, or geared. **Vitrified living wood** reaches steel-equivalent strength (blades, armor, vessels, structure) while preserving the grain — abundant, available to anyone with the song. But fine gear and clockwork are **impossible** for them; sustained metallurgy-grade heat is above their floor (their hearth cookware is petrified wood, below smelting). The fine, conductive tier is gated: native [[goldenwood|goldenwood]] (alien-origin, naturally conductive, grove-grown, very slow) is the prestige route; ordinary heartwood reaches that tier only when **another race finishes it in metal**.
- **The three-hand artifact.** The Elfen never handle or smelt metal. The Velrathi and Taran acquire Elfen heartwood as a tough substrate, then dip, coat, and alloy it. An alloy-coated, Elfen-enchanted heartwood blade **cannot exist without all three races** — Elfen grow and enchant it; Velrathi or Taran do the downstream metallurgy. The triad is a literal production line (Elfen = living world / Velrathi = magic / Taran = order). (This full interdependence is **post-colonization**, not founding — see [[#Cradle, expansion, and the late triad|below]].)
- **The cost of a great work** has two non-substitutable axes: **time** (mana-density accrues over centuries to millennia and cannot be rushed; newly-grown material is blank) and **the singer's body** (channeling Eldritch through the body is acutely exhausting — a great work can leave an Elfen bedridden for days). The two do not trade against each other.
- **Why the Elfen cannot ratchet.** [[eldritch|Eldritch]] can be neither created nor destroyed, and making only *borrows* it (mana in an imbued item is a loan, returned to the cycle when the item is unmade — perhaps after millennia). The Elfen are therefore **physically incapable of the irreversible ratchet**: there is no point-of-no-return inside Elfen making. A people made of love for the wild simply make the way the wild makes — growth, not extraction. Conservation is the wild's own logic in them.

**The strategic seam:** because a great imbued export *relocates* centuries of banked density out of its grove (which rebuilds only at the slow flat global rate), whoever the Elfen sing for, they **arm**. Every great export is a grove agreeing to run shallow for a millennium. The [[#Wardens and the Council of the Eldest|wardens']] real authority is exactly this husbandry decision.

## Reproduction — born adult from womb trees

New Elfen are born **whole and adult** from **[[womb-trees|womb trees]]** — a willing nature-spirit infused into a living tree, joined with Elfen tissue, sung into a gestation chamber. Over a one-year gestation, **every villager** lays hands on the womb daily to transfer memory and knowledge, so the newborn emerges already knowing who they are, their role, and the community's whole library. But **wisdom does not transfer** — only the library, never the librarian; wisdom is earned only by living. This is why Elfen society is **choral at the root**: an Elfen is made by the whole village and is constitutively everyone's. See [[womb-trees]] for the full mechanism and its consequences.

## Lifespan, the apex, and ennui

None of the three eldest races die of old age. Immortality means **no aging, not invulnerability** — death comes only by violence, accident, or exhaustion, bounded by the [[omniversal-laws|Fifth Law]] (Death is Sovereign) and the **Sixth Law** (Destruction is the Natural End). The Sixth Law is a **stress model**, so Elfen lifespan is a **bell curve peaking near 5,000 years**; the far-right tail is rare, and a literal handful of present-exile Elfen have seen **20,000 years** — anomalies, not "the old guard," and walking primary sources who personally lived the Demon War and the world before the Banishment.

Growth **slows but never stops, and changes shape**: raw magnitude tapers (unbounded growth would make a god, which the Fifth Law forbids) while **wisdom broadens**. The surviving population sorts, and the far tail funnels toward a **singular apex** — functionally *one* Elfen who is at once most-skilled, strongest in Eldritch, and wisest, who "stands alone." This is **not** a caste or a birthright; it is a funnel earned by surviving and never ceasing to grow.

The real enemy of an immortal is not death but **ennui** — "the same day repeated ten thousand times." What keeps the elders alive in mind is **novelty as a discipline**: facing each day as new. (Wardenship is ennui-proof — the living world is never twice the same; exploration is the purest renewable new-day; the ~500-tea overshoot is the same defense in miniature.) A mind flattened into repetition is under *more* Sixth-Law wear; the far tail are the ones who never stopped beginning.

**The scheduled tragedy.** Two thieves take Elfen wisdom: violence (early) and the Law-cap (eventual, unappealable). The apex death is the worst instance — losing the singular stands-alone elder means losing *the* mind, perhaps 20,000 years deep, and the funnel refills only over millennia. The Elfen live under a scheduled **decapitation of their own summit, and they know it.** ([[aramet|Aramet]] is an **Ascended Mortal**, a separate mechanism — ascension is the one path off the wheel; ordinary Elfen do not transition at the cap.)

## Wardens and the Council of the Eldest

Authority among the Elfen is **capability-assignment** — who can best do the thing — continuous with the way a song-circle works. A circle's capacity is capped by its leader, and circles take **fluid mid-song leadership**: *stepping down is the skill* (a status-jealous people could not do it). Magnitude (the young) and wisdom / land-listening (the elder) are the same person across a lifetime — the prodigy **becomes** the warden. Raw power is the **junior** virtue, ranked below knowing what is worth doing.

A settlement has a chief or leader; above that, the **Council of the Eldest** is the true governing authority. The "monarchy" outsiders see is **hollow-outward** — a diplomatic interface presented to races who expect a monarch to treat with, not the real seat of power. Crucially, the singular **apex Eldest is kept *out* of operational rule**: the wisest is a resource to consult — oracle and arbiter — not a throne to occupy. (This is the same instinct as the ennui-defense and an anti-autocracy firewall, and it resolves how an egalitarian womb-born people can also venerate a singular apex: by making the apex a counselor, not a king.)

## Diet and the funerary ethic

The Elfen eat **no meat** — but for a **metabolic**, not a moral, reason: they run on Eldritch and plant metabolism and **cannot digest flesh**; the machinery isn't there. (An Eldritch-thin region is therefore *metabolically dangerous* to Elfen specifically.) Their cuisine is extraordinarily diverse, because nature-magic can grow plants to any desired flavor — see [[pre-empire-foodways]].

They are **not** "killers of nothing." Eating is a daily act of taking **bonded plant life**, and they feel it. The Elfen moral universe is not life-versus-non-life; it is **which** lives are taken and **how** the death is honored. From this comes a single **funerary ethic for all bonded death** — plant, animal, and their own dead alike: **a death must be utilized to the fullest to honor it.** Waste is obscene — a death that bought nothing. (Greed is firewalled mostly by *need*: mana-and-plant metabolism plus non-consumptive making leave almost nothing that profit could buy. The residual corruptibility is rare but real — hence a taboo against letting trade-value sway a cull, and a quiet category of disgraced wardens. The taboo's existence proves the sin occasionally happens.)

## Relationship with animals and nature

The Elfen **do not keep traditional pets**. Wild animals nonetheless move freely through Elfen villages because the Elfen do not hunt for sport or food and present no ordinary threat. **Herbivores in particular** grow very comfortable — deer, rabbits, songbirds, small grazers approach openly and beg for food the way a town squirrel might, and the Elfen feed them by hand. One of the textures that most surprises visitors is the dignified warden walking through a settlement with a stag's nose dropped into her hand for a piece of fruit.

**Carnivores are not welcome** in settlements. A predator that enters a populated village is killed — but with **regret**, performed with ritualistic respect, never for food, trophy, or skin-trade. The dead predator is given a proper return to the wild. (That an obligate-vegetarian people can nonetheless *kill* is exactly the point: they are guardians of **balance**, not protectors of all life.)

### Guardians of natural balance

Elfen are not pacifists toward animals. When the local ecosystem requires it they hunt **both herbivores and carnivores** — to thin an overgrown population, remove a diseased animal, or relocate a displaced species — with the same reluctance and ritual respect. This produces a recognizable field-craft: the warden who moves silently, draws a bow with a master's accuracy, and uses the kill to **mend the web** rather than tear at it. The same warden, in a different ecological condition, would feed that species by hand.

### Agriculture without clear-cutting

Elfen agriculture follows the same balance-maintenance principle. **They do not clear-cut.** They plant cultivated crops **between existing trees** in harrow-style integration with the forest, using nature-magic to ensure the crops get sunlight and nutrients without damaging the surrounding ecosystem. The result rarely looks agricultural at all — no open fields, no monocultures, no obvious orchards, just food woven into the living forest. Yields per area are smaller than industrial farming, but the practice is sustainable across millennia and produces no ecological debt.

## The water-vulnerability — their true existential threat

The Elfen need **uninterrupted river flow.** All forests are ultimately bound to their watersheds, so the survival engine under everything else is **hydraulic**: dam the rivers upstream and the forests wither — and with them the groves, the [[womb-trees|womb trees]], and the living basis of the race. A civilization need not clear-cut the Elfen; it need only **dam a river** — ordinary, deniable, devastating. (Spreading across the world did not escape this; it only multiplied the number of places the Elfen are vulnerable.)

This is why the Elfen's defining treaty is **materials-for-watershed**: *we arm your civilization; you do not dam our rivers.* When the [[#Wardens and the Council of the Eldest|Council]] speaks of "acceptable terms," it is, at bottom, about the water. Equilibrium-keeping is not just a philosophy for the Elfen — a tipped balance kills *them* first.

## Psionics and intimacy

The Elfen are **voice/song-primary** in their magic, but also **psionically capable** — psionics (a mind-channel faculty: attack, defend, conceal, seek) and song-magic are **different, non-interchangeable** faculties. Their **intimacy** is private singing that carries a psionic **mind-link**, and consent in it is **mechanical** — the link cannot be forced open in ordinary intimacy; it is *offered*. A retention rule shapes Elfen depth and marriage: after intimacy one **keeps a single thing** and the rest generalizes (you remember the shape, not the whole interior), and exclusivity accretes by repetition — a long-bonded pair has accumulated many "kept things," which is what depth means for them. **Forced merging** — overriding the consent-mechanism to plunder a mind — is possible only for the very powerful and is among the worst crimes in Elfen moral life, a rare apex-atrocity.

## Belief

The Elfen worship along the whole ladder:

- **[[the-three|Mark]]**, their maker, who *walked among them* — worship-as-**remembrance** of a known parent.
- **[[qoxli|Qoxli]]**, goddess of the wild they tend — worshipped with a devotion outsiders sometimes find disproportionate for "a people merely made by Mark."
- **Ascended Elfen mortals** — gods risen from within their own kind, such as [[aramet|Aramet]] (Lesser Goddess of Hidden Things), who crossed the threshold *while living*.

The pantheon thus spans maker-above, wild-around, and their-own-kind-risen-from-within. One quiet Elfen mystery is sometimes reported by the very oldest in deep attunement: the sense of *something in their own people too vast and too changeable to be wholly their maker's hand* — felt, but never named.

## Values

The Elfen hold four values (and only four):

1. **Reverence for all taken life** — *honor the death, waste nothing.*
2. **Wisdom outranks power** — *knowing what is worth doing is higher than being able to do it.*
3. **Find each day new** — *to repeat a day is to begin to die.* (Novelty and joyful over-pursuit as necessary life, not indulgence.)
4. **Tend the balance, take no side** — *neither the wild nor the works may consume the other.* (Distrust of all zealotry, including pro-nature zealotry.)

## The vice — "Lost in Smoke"

The innocent thing is **smoke-weed**: hundreds of flavor varieties of a mild narcotic/hypnotic weed, dried, packed in wooden or stone pipes, the clean white smoke swirled for mild euphoria and, rarely, non-prophetic visions. There is no shame in the pipe itself — it is part of the Elfen joyful-excess family alongside the teas and blossom wines.

**The vice** is the **counterfeit** — *Lost in Smoke* — and it is shameful: using the pipe to **simulate** the two things that must be *earned* — the visionary depth of true land-attunement (which takes centuries to ripen) and the freshness of *finding each day new* (the ennui-defense). The Lost repeat the same chemical day while the euphoria tells them it is new — **dying of sameness while feeling fresh**. It attacks the highest Elfen virtue at its root, feels from inside like Sylph's whim until it isn't, and is fenced by **shame** like the disgraced cullers (the culture has a name for it because it happens). At the deepest level it is the **anti-ascension**: ascension — the one escape from the Law-6 wisdom-tragedy — requires the *real* attunement, the costly climb, the true vision. The Lost reach for the counterfeit and get a wisp of smoke, and crack at the wall having spent their centuries counterfeiting the very thing that could have saved them.

## The Three Progenitor Races

For over twenty thousand years, the Elfen, [[velrathi|Velrathi]], and [[tarans|Taran]] lived in cooperative civilization with complementary roles:

- **Elfen** — the living world: wardens of balance, growers and song-shapers.
- **[[velrathi|Velrathi]]** — magic: studying and advancing all arcane knowledge.
- **[[tarans|Taran]]** — order: protecting against external threat and internal corruption.

This harmony broke down in the centuries before ~18,000 years ago, as the Taran grew arrogant — directly causing the founding of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]].

## Cradle, expansion, and the late triad

The ancient-homelands map (~50,000 BP) shows **cradles, not territory**. The Elfen cradle was the green forested heartland of the large **eastern continent**; the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] cradle was the arid southwestern lobe of the **same** continent, neighbors separated by a mountain spine; the [[tarans|Tarran]] cradle was the **moon [[gaia|Gaia]]**. Over roughly the first 10,000 years all three peoples **expanded to occupy the whole world** (and Gaia), and the clean separation blurred into a fully-intermixed world long before the Firethorn era.

This means the **full three-hand interdependence is post-colonization, not founding.** In the founding era the three peoples were separated and cooperation was looser; the Elfen's metal-incapacity is **intrinsic nature** (a people of living-song making), and the complementary trade that exploits it formed *later*, when expansion brought them up against peoples who could do metallurgy. The dependency was **discovered, not engineered** — Mark did not build a deliberately incomplete people. (In the homeland era, the materials-for-watershed relationship was specifically between the Elfen heartland and the upstream-adjacent Velrathi cradle, fed by the same mountain-spine watershed; post-expansion it generalized, since all Elfen everywhere are water-bound. The same 10,000-year intermixing that let the three peoples *build* together is what later let them *destroy* each other.)

## Engineered Child Races

The Elfen themselves engineered three child races. Per established canon, [[the-three|The Three]] created only the Elfen, [[tarans|Tarran]], and [[velrathi|Velrathi]] directly; child races were shaped by the progenitor parents themselves, and the Three did not direct, assist, or oversee that work (canon clarification 2026-05-10). For the Elfen the engineering mechanism worked through the [[womb-trees|womb-tree]] process — see [[peoples-of-eadras#How the engineering worked]].

## Banishment

At the conclusion of the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the [[great-banishment|Elder Gods banished all three progenitor races]] from the Prime Material of Eadras. The Elfen were sent to the **[[gaia|Wyldglimmer version of the moon Gaia]]** — a bright, positive-tainted reflection where wild magic runs rampant and living architecture blooms with impossible speed. Their descendants there leaned fully into wild-song magic. (Of the three nature-keyed exiles, the Elfen drew the *resonant-wild* mirror — fitting for a keeper-race of the living world.)

Modern elves and fey on the Prime are diluted descendants of the surviving servant lines and El'mer/Sidhe/Sea'tua bloodlines that remained on Eadras during the Banishment. Pure Elfen no longer exist on the Prime Material — though hidden Elfen deep wilds remain on Eadras as part of the "enclaves remained behind" exception to the Banishment (see [[great-banishment]]).

## Naming

Elfen names are elegant, melodic, flowing, and nature-inspired with a distinctive **Hawaiian phonetic influence** — soft consonants and abundant vowels giving the language a rhythmic, musical quality. Common sounds: l, m, n, r, s, h, w, th. **Feminine names always end in -a** (or a diphthong ending in an "a" sound — *-ia, -ua, -ea*); **masculine names always end in -o** (or *-io, -uo, -eo*). Examples (F): *Elenara, Liraena, Thalira, Miralua, Kaelana, Aulani.* Examples (M): *Thalor, Elorio, Silvaro, Kaelo.* See [[naming-conventions#elfen-created-by-mark|Racial Naming Conventions — Elfen]].
