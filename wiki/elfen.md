# Elfen

**Summary**: The Elfen are the first progenitor humanoid race created by [[the-three|Mark]], designed as wardens of all wildlands — tall, magically resonant beings who shaped living matter with their voices and served as the world's ecological caretakers.

**Sources**: Elfen.txt, Peoples of Eadras.txt, Playground generation 1st part.txt, Pre-empire foodways.txt, Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, Naming conventions.txt, Extra large grok chat 5-9-2026.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-09

---

## Overview

The Elfen were created by [[the-three|Mark]] — the eldest of the Three — as the wardens of all wildlands. They possessed a profound, innate connection to the land and all living things, feeling the pulse of forests, rivers, and mountains as if they were part of their own body. They also had strong natural connections to the elemental planes of earth and water.

**Physical description**: 3 meters tall, graceful, slender, strong, and durable. An almost ethereal presence.

## Culture and Abilities

**Building**: The Elfen worked only with living materials — living plant matter, ancient root systems, and great fungal networks. They never worked with dead materials, and they never used worked metal or quarried stone at structural scale. Crystal as a structural material was an [[arathi|Arathi]] art that died with the Arathi; it does not appear in Elfen sites. See [[elfen-architecture]] for the full architectural tradition.

**Song Magic**: Using their voices and their connection to [[eldritch|Eldritch]], the Elfen could sing directly into living matter and reshape it from within. A lone Elfen could slowly grow a home over days or weeks. When power was needed, **Circles of up to a hundred Elfen** would sing in harmony and raise entire districts or cities in just a couple of hours. They could also **vitrify living wood** — transforming it into stone while preserving its natural grain — creating fireplaces, pillars, or hard surfaces without using dead material.

**Leadership**: Circles of Wardens — groups of elders who listened to the needs of the land itself before making decisions.

**Foodways**: Strictly plant-based — no meat. Cuisine is extraordinarily diverse thanks to nature magic that grows plants with any desired flavor profile. Meals function as **communal workshops** where grievances, news, and group decisions are aired together. Travel rations are **Waypetals** — compressed fruit/nut/seed/herb wafers that provide complete daily nutrition. See [[pre-empire-foodways]] for full detail.

**Drink**: The Elfen's signature alcoholic tradition is the family of **[[elfen-blossom-wines|blossom wines]]** — fermented from berries, edible flowers, and herbs, with each clan keeping its own closely-guarded recipe. Aged in **sung living-wood barrels** (made by the same song-shaping magic the Elfen use for their architecture) and gently de-alcoholized by magic for children at celebrations. The Elfen also produce **[[aqua-vitae|Elven Aqua Vitae]]**, the universal alchemical solvent distilled from blossom wine; its bubbly floral-herbal character makes it especially prized for nature, growth, healing, and life-magic potions. The Elfen do not, however, drink especially heavily — blossom wines are light and only mildly intoxicating.

**Tea**: Centuries before the [[firethorn-empire|Empire]], the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] commissioned the Elfen to engineer a small handful of new tea varieties. The Elfen fell deeply in love with the project and **vastly overshot** the commission, returning every decade for centuries with 12–15 brand-new varieties at a time, until they had produced **roughly 500** before the Velrathi politely told them to stop. Seven major Elfen clans grow the most popular varieties in volume; seventeen minor clans grow rarer specialty varieties. The Elfen also developed their own much more casual tea culture as a side effect of the project. See [[velrathi-tea-culture]].

## The Three Progenitor Races

For over twenty thousand years, the Elfen, [[velrathi|Velrathi]], and [[tarans|Taran]] lived in stable, cooperative civilization with clearly defined roles:
- **Elfen** — Wardens of the Wildlands; shaping and protecting natural ecosystems
- **[[velrathi|Velrathi]]** — Masters of Magic; studying and advancing all arcane knowledge
- **[[tarans|Taran]]** — Keepers of Order; protecting the world from external threats and internal corruption

This harmony began breaking down in the centuries before 18,000 years ago, as the Taran grew arrogant. Their shift directly caused the founding of the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn Empire]].

## Relationship with animals and nature

The Elfen **do not keep traditional pets**. Wild animals nonetheless move freely through Elfen villages and settlements because the Elfen do not hunt for sport, do not hunt for food, and present no threat to wildlife under ordinary circumstances. The local fauna learns this quickly.

**Herbivores in particular** are very comfortable around Elfen. Deer, rabbits, songbirds, small grazers — they will often approach Elfen openly, and not infrequently *beg for food*, the way a town squirrel might at a generous picnic. Elfen happily feed them by hand. This is one of the small textures of daily Elfen life that surprises non-Elfen visitors most: the dignified Elfen warden walking through the heart of a settlement with a stag dropping its nose into her hand for a piece of fruit.

**Carnivores are not welcome** in Elfen settlements. If a predator enters a populated village, the Elfen kill it — but they kill it with **regret**, performed with ritualistic respect, and never for food or sport. The dead predator is not eaten, hung as a trophy, or used for skin-trade. It is given a proper return to the wild.

### Guardians of natural balance

Elfen are not absolute pacifists toward animals. Their self-conception is as **guardians of natural balance**, not as protectors of all life regardless of consequence. When the local ecosystem requires it, the Elfen will hunt **both herbivores and carnivores** — to thin a population that has grown too large, to remove a diseased animal that would harm the herd, to relocate a species displaced by environmental change. Such hunts are performed with the same reluctance and ritualistic respect that characterizes the killing of village-invading predators. The killings are necessary; they are not, however, taken lightly.

This produces a recognizable Elfen field-craft: the warden who can move silently through a forest, draw a bow with a master's accuracy, and use the kill to mend the local web rather than tear at it. The same warden, in a different ecological condition, would feed the same species by hand without hesitation.

### Agriculture without clear-cutting

Elfen agriculture follows the same balance-maintenance principle. **They do not clear-cut.** They plant and tend cultivated crops **between existing trees** in a harrow-style integration with the surrounding forest, and they use their nature magic to **ensure cultivated plants get enough sunlight and nutrients** without damaging the surrounding ecosystem.

The result is an Elfen agricultural landscape that, to a non-Elfen visitor, often does not look agricultural at all — there are no fields in the open-plot sense, no monocultures, no obvious orchards. The crops are simply **woven into the existing forest**, growing where the song-shaped magic has prepared the ground for them. Yields are smaller per unit area than industrial agriculture, but the practice is sustainable across millennia and produces no ecological debt.

## Engineered Child Races

The Elfen themselves engineered three child races. Per established canon, [[the-three|The Three]] created only the Elfen, [[tarans|Tarran]], and [[velrathi|Velrathi]] directly; child races were shaped by the progenitor parents themselves, and the Three did not direct, assist, or oversee that work (canon clarification 2026-05-10).

| Race | Description | Modern Status |
|---|---|---|
| **[[el-mer\|El'mer]]** | Created to oversee natural balance on land | **Totally extinct** — died at the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves\|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] as one of the [[three-who-stood\|Three Who Stood]]. Diluted blood survives in modern Elves and Fae. |
| **Sidhe** | Created to regulate and rule the [[wyldglimmer]] | Ancestors of all modern sapient Fae |
| **[[sea-tua\|Sea'tua]]** | Created to oversee natural balance in the oceans; created roughly **ten million years after the [[arathi-cataclysm\|Arathi Cataclysm]]**, well after Eadras's hydrology had stabilized in its post-Drowning configuration | Ancestors of all modern sapient sea peoples — the Five Modern Oceanic Races ([[merrow\|Merrow]], [[sirens\|Sirens]], [[shimmercloaks\|Shimmercloaks]], [[ink-lords\|Ink Lords]], [[tideclad\|Tideclad]]) plus two alien-branch peoples ([[kuo-toa\|Kuo-toa]], [[sahuagin\|Sahuagin]]) |

Modern descendants include Elves (various types), Fae, the Five Modern Oceanic Races (collectively known on the surface as [[merfolk|Merfolk]]), [[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]], [[sahuagin|Sahuagin]], and others.

## Banishment

At the conclusion of the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the [[great-banishment|Elder Gods banished all three progenitor races]] from the Prime Material of Eadras. The Elfen were sent to the **[[gaia|Wyldglimmer version of the moon Gaia]]** — a bright, positive-tainted reflection where wild magic runs rampant and living architecture blooms with impossible speed. Their descendants there leaned fully into wild-song magic.

Modern elves and fey on the Prime are diluted descendants of the surviving servant lines and El'mer/Sidhe/Sea'tua bloodlines that remained on Eadras during the Banishment. Pure Elfen no longer exist on the Prime Material — though hidden Elfen deep wilds remain on Eadras as part of the "enclaves remained behind" exception to the Banishment (see [[great-banishment]]).

## Naming

Elfen names are elegant, melodic, flowing, and nature-inspired with a distinctive **Hawaiian phonetic influence** — soft consonants and abundant vowels giving the language a rhythmic, musical quality. Common sounds: l, m, n, r, s, h, w, th. **Feminine names always end in -a** (or a diphthong ending in an "a" sound — *-ia, -ua, -ea*); **masculine names always end in -o** (or *-io, -uo, -eo*). Examples (F): *Elenara, Liraena, Thalira, Miralua, Kaelana, Aulani.* Examples (M): *Thalor, Elorio, Silvaro, Kaelo.* See [[naming-conventions#elfen-created-by-mark|Racial Naming Conventions — Elfen]].
