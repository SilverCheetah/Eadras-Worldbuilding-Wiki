## Status

The Spear of Crystallized Entropy has been impaled into the original First Breach Wound at the conceptual heart of the [[../worldbreaker-scar|Worldbreaker Scar]], beneath the modern continent of [[../arathyn|Arathyn]], since the close of the [[../demon-war|Demon War]] approximately 10,000 years ago. This is the **fourth and current hiding** in the Spear's containment history; it is the gods' last and most desperate containment, executed in panic and fury after three prior attempts had failed.

The Spear has remained in place for 10,000 years and has not been moved. The [[../black-hand|Black Hand]] has not located it; the [[../lost-zenith|Lost Zenith]] failed at the previous Grand Conjunction. The location is **not common knowledge** but is â€” uniquely among the four hidings â€” *deducible from first cosmological principles* by sufficiently committed scholarship, the [[../unicorns|unicorn]] voice, or the deepest [[../order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] theology. The Tome of Baldaran's sealed chapters record it. Public canon retains: *"Current location: Unknown. The Spear has been hidden by the gods for the fourth time."*

This is **the central plot secret** of the Eadras setting. Quest for the Spear can drive an entire campaign arc.

## The alien-artifact principle (sealed framing)

The load-bearing cosmological framing that organizes the entire four-hidings sequence â€” partially summarized in the public [[../spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear page]] but with the full implications kept here â€” is that **the Spear is alien to the Mythos. It is not under the gods' direct jurisdiction. They cannot destroy it, cannot eject it, and cannot hide it absolutely.** This is *why* the fourth attempt is what it is: by the time of Xar'vion's defeat, the gods had exhausted every option compatible with their authority and had to commit an act *against the Mythos itself* â€” wounding the Mythos in order to constrain the Spear within it.

The principle has consequences that are not entirely public:

- **The Elder Gods are not omnipotent within the Mythos.** They have spent ten thousand years quietly working around this limit. They do not publicize the limit because doing so would invite exploitation and undermine mortal faith.
- **The Tome's sealed chapters record the principle explicitly.** The Tome's open scholarship admits only that *the precise mechanism remains unknown*.
- **The unicorns intuit the principle correctly.** Their mythic voice (*some wounds do not close â€” they only wait*) is the canonical inside-Eadras articulation of the alien-artifact principle; they are the canary in the cosmological shaft.
- **The Black Hand does not yet know** about the alien-artifact principle. If they did â€” if Xar'vion knew, through the Motionless King's connection to the Inner Hand, that the gods *cannot destroy the Spear* and that any containment is therefore in principle temporary â€” his entire grand strategy would be conditioned on that knowledge. As of canonical present, this is **open canon** (the Black Hand probably does not know; whether Xar'vion knows from within his Icon is a separate, unsettled question).

## The fourth-attempt impalement â€” sealed details

After [[../xarvion|Xar'vion's]] defeat and Total Icon Binding at the close of the [[../demon-war|Demon War]], the gods inherited the Spear back. They had now failed three times to contain it. The first attempt (destruction-and-illusions) had been broken by Tarloth seeing through the illusion. The second (time-displacement in the Wyldglimmer) had been broken by Darkhoof seizing a time-displaced artifact. The third (the reverse-time-bubble god-prison in the Glimmerdark) had been broken by Xar'vion, who had killed the [[#The volunteer dragon god â€” sealed identity|volunteer dragon god]] to take the Spear and trigger the Demon War.

**They were out of options.**

What they did was an act of **divine panic and fury** â€” not careful planning, not iterative design, but a desperate decision to commit a cosmic violation in order to constrain something they had now failed to contain three times.

They took the Spear and **rammed it into the original First Breach Wound** â€” the same hole Kudur-eá¹­ir had drilled through the Mythos Shell to call down the [[../worldender|Worldbreaker]] 10 million years before. They impaled the alien-marked artifact directly into the lingering scar tissue of the world's most fundamental wound, using the original damage as the prison itself.

### Why this worked when nothing else had

The impalement worked, at least in the operational sense that the Spear has not escaped for 10,000 years, for reasons specific to the alien-artifact principle. The earlier hidings tried to contain the Spear *in something else* â€” an illusion, a time-displacement field, a god-prison. Each was a container, however exotic. The Spear could escape from a container; the alien-artifact principle ensures that no container the gods can build is absolutely durable.

The fourth attempt did not put the Spear in a container. It **welded the Spear into the substrate of the Mythos itself**, at the one point where the Mythos was already wounded enough to accept a foreign body. The Spear is no longer hidden somewhere; it is **embedded in the world's wound**, and removing it from the wound would require either:

- Replicating the divine violence that put it there (no mortal force can replicate this), or
- Working with the wound on its own terms (the alien-artifact principle, applied in reverse â€” a being that understands the principle deeply enough might find an extraction pathway that does not require divine violence).

The Black Hand does not yet know this. If they did, their strategy would shift from *locate the Spear* to *understand the alien-artifact principle well enough to extract the Spear via principle-aware operation*. The latter is far harder; but it is the only path that does not require divine intervention they cannot command.

### Visible cost â€” Arathyn

The impalement **triggered global volcanic upheaval** and accelerated the pre-existing [[../arathyn|Arathyn]] Large Igneous Province into its modern continental shape in a single catastrophic phase. Before the impalement, Arathyn was a slowly-emerging LIP that had broken the ocean surface around 1 mya. After the impalement, Arathyn was forced into its modern reach within a geological eyeblink â€” by the ongoing volcanism that the impalement reactivated.

Arathyn is therefore not merely a continent that happens to have grown over the Worldbreaker impact site. It is **scar tissue grown over the gods' desperate fourth-attempt containment**. The continent's existence has a *single cause*: the impalement. The pre-existing LIP geology is the *mechanism* by which the cause expressed itself; the cause is the impalement.

The continent is still growing â€” slowly, over geological time â€” as the scar continues its very long not-quite-healing. The Spear sits at the conceptual center.

## The volunteer dragon god â€” sealed identity

The volunteer of the third hiding â€” the [[../nine-embers-of-renewal|Ascended Mortal Dragon God]] who voluntarily entered Total Icon Binding to seal the Spear inside a reverse time bubble in the deepest [[../glimmerdark|Glimmerdark]] â€” is preserved here for plot-development purposes.

### Identity

The volunteer's specific name, clan, and place in the dragon-god tradition are **open canon**. Working candidates (any of which could be locked when this becomes campaign-relevant):

- One of the [[../nine-embers-of-renewal|Nine Embers of Renewal]] whose ascension is recorded as having *"withdrawn"* or *"taken up an unspoken duty"* in dragon esoteric tradition.
- A younger Ascended Mortal Dragon God of the era who had ascended legitimately and had a reputation for severity, duty, and willingness to bear unreasonable cost.
- A dragon god whose loss is explicitly mourned in [[../katharim|Katharim]] memory under a name that has otherwise dropped from public scholarship.

Naming the volunteer should be done when their identity becomes campaign-relevant.

### The reverse time bubble and what it did to them

The volunteer entered the prison knowing the cost. They expected to maintain the seal for as long as the binding endured. They did **not** expect to enter what amounted to an eternity of solitude.

The seal-mechanism was a **reverse time bubble** â€” time inside ran *vastly faster* than time outside. Outside, ten thousand years passed between Darkhoof's use and Xar'vion's retrieval. **Inside the bubble, the volunteer subjectively endured roughly 10^37 years** (ten thousand undecillion years â€” a number so vast that the comparison to mortal duration loses meaning entirely).

Working sealed canon on what this did to them:

- **Vastly aged.** A dragon god is long-lived even by divine standards; 10^37 subjective years places the volunteer's accumulated experience and power beyond any frame of reference. By the time Xar'vion broke the seal, the volunteer had subjectively existed for vastly longer than the Mythos itself.
- **Vastly more powerful.** Compressed into the Icon with the Spear at their core, they had eternity to refine their relationship with the artifact, to develop techniques unique to seal-maintenance, to study the Spear's nature in ways no other being has had time for.
- **Vastly more experienced.** Whatever a being can learn alone, with the Spear's low-grade sentient presence for company, over 10^37 years â€” they learned. Multiple times over. They may have invented entire philosophies, theologies, mathematical disciplines, and forgotten them. They may have rebuilt their own mind from first principles, repeatedly.
- **Vastly more isolated.** No outside contact ever reached them. The gods could not check on them without risking the seal. They held the line entirely alone, longer than the Mythos itself has existed.
- **Likely fractured.** **Divine schizophrenia** is the working sealed frame for the volunteer's late-stage mental state: a consciousness that has spent so long alone with itself that it has split, multiplied, forgotten, rebuilt, and renamed its own identity many times over. By the time Xar'vion arrived, the being inside the prison was no longer cleanly the dragon god who had volunteered. Multiple internal personas existed in tension; elaborate inner cosmologies had been constructed, forgotten, and reconstructed; the volunteer's relationship to the Spear had become its own private theology. Different personas may have held different positions on whether the seal should ever be broken, whether the Spear was an enemy or a long companion, whether the volunteer's original commitment was still binding.

### Xar'vion's act

When Xar'vion broke the seal and killed the volunteer to take the Spear, he killed a being whose effective power and experience had grown to a scale no other entity in the Mythos can match. **What Xar'vion absorbed in the act is open canon.** Some possibilities (any combination):

- **Power.** A direct transfer of some fraction of the volunteer's accumulated divine power into Xar'vion's own ascension. This would explain some of the specific intensity of Xar'vion's later operations.
- **Knowledge.** The volunteer had subjectively studied the Spear for 10^37 years. Some of that understanding may have leaked into Xar'vion at the moment of killing. He may carry, deep in his Icon-bound consciousness, knowledge of the Spear no one else has.
- **A fragment of the volunteer's fractured selves.** Divine schizophrenia produces multiple internal personas. One or more of those personas may persist, in some shadow form, inside Xar'vion's Icon â€” a divine presence the Motionless King is not the sole inhabitant of.
- **The Spear's attunement.** The Spear had been the singular companion of a 10^37-year solitude. Whatever the Spear became under that attention, Xar'vion now wields. The Spear that left the Glimmerdark with Xar'vion was substantially more *responsive* to its wielder than the artifact Darkhoof had stolen from the Wyldglimmer.

The dragons remember the volunteer as a sacred martyr. The fact of their death â€” *a god who voluntarily sacrificed themselves to hold the line was killed by a mortal wielder who afterward triggered the Demon War* â€” is one of the heaviest specific moral injuries in Eadran cosmology. The dragons' grief at this loss is encoded in their esoteric tradition; the public *"grim satisfaction over Xar'vion"* register of the dragon mythic voice is, at the deepest dragon level, *complicated* by the awareness that the Demon War cost them a martyr-god as well as a war.

## What the world publicly knows

Almost nothing. No mortal has reached the impalement site and returned with verifiable testimony. The public framing in the [[../spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear page]] is *"Current location: Unknown. The Spear has been hidden by the gods for the fourth time."*

Sailor hearsay and Arathyn-traveler stories carry breadcrumbs (drop these as in-world flavor only when desired):

- Strange dreams that intensify the deeper one travels into Arathyn's interior.
- Compasses, divination tools, and magical instruments behaving wrongly the closer one approaches the continent's deep heart.
- Reality-fray phenomena reported by surviving deep-interior expeditions â€” causality slipping, time skipping, identity drifting.
- The persistent magical disturbance and ongoing volcanism that mark the continent as *not yet settled* in geological terms.
- Theological / philosophical fragments from the unicorn tradition pointing at *the heart of the world where the wound still bleeds*.

None of these is corroborated; none appears in the open chapters of the [[../tome-of-baldaran|Tome]]. The Tome contains a sealed chapter on the matter that has never opened in this age.

## Plot vectors

The active impalement site can drive several distinct narrative tracks:

- **Vector A: The Black Hand learns of the alien-artifact principle.** Some scholarly discovery â€” a recovered fragment of Tome scholarship, a unicorn revelation, a leaked Order Sharanel theological text â€” surfaces the principle. The Black Hand's strategy shifts from *locate the Spear* to *find a principle-aware extraction method*. This is far harder but more cosmologically interesting; the Black Hand becomes the heretical-theological faction it has always quietly been.
- **Vector B: PCs approach Arathyn for some other reason and encounter the reality-fray rings.** The deep interior of Arathyn becomes a dangerous-but-accessible adventure zone, with fraying rings that progressively distort experience. The Spear is the conceptual center but is reached only by the most committed (or doomed) parties.
- **Vector C: The Wound stresses.** Something in the impalement-prison shifts â€” perhaps connected to the next [[../grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]], perhaps a side-effect of Black Hand magical experiments somewhere else in the world, perhaps the Spear's own animal cunning beginning to operate against the wound itself. The impalement is *not* as stable as the gods would prefer to believe.
- **Vector D: A fragment of the volunteer's fractured selves reaches the world.** One of the divine schizophrenia personas that survived in Xar'vion's Icon (per the open canon above) finds a way to communicate with the outside. The implications could include a hidden ally inside the Motionless King's binding, a cult that worships an unknown divine voice, or a faction within the Black Hand whose true loyalty is to a fragmented martyr-god rather than to Xar'vion.
