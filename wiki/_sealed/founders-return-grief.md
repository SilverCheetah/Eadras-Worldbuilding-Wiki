# The Day the Three Come Home — Dao's Withheld Grief (SEALED)

**This is OOC author/plot-spine canon. It is NOT in the public Tome.** Do not wikilink to this page from any public page; do not summarize its contents into [[dao]], [[the-three]], or any other public page. The **public** layer holds only the serene-pendulum metaphysics (see [[dao#Dao's Pendulum]] and [[principles-of-eadras]] #37) and the plain fact that the founders withdrew to explore — never that Dao deliberately hides the catastrophe from them.

**Summary**: [[dao|Dao]] **knows** everything that befell the progenitor peoples after [[the-three|the Three]] departed — the Tarran decay, [[velena|Velena's]] murder, [[xarvion|Xarvion]], the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the [[great-banishment|Banishment]] — and **deliberately keeps it from them** so they can keep exploring in peace. It is triage-as-mercy (I.A.7), not strategy. It is also the setting's **single most fragile** concealment: Dao hides from **living, freely-returning gods** who could walk back through the door at any time and discover it. The latent apocalypse-of-grief this sets up — *the day the Three come home* — is left deliberately unresolved; it is stronger as a fuse than as an event.

## What Dao withholds, and why

All three founders stepped away to explore other Mythoi — an expression of the **seeker-nature** the 11,000-year unshielded childhood gave them (seekers eventually go seek). Each walked *with* their people for a long span first (Mark among the [[elfen|Elfen]] for millennia; Melania with the [[tarans|Tarran]] ~10,000–11,000 years), and left believing — reasonably, at the time — that their peoples were mature enough to stand alone. Melania additionally simply *wanted* to go explore. **(All of the above is public; it is on [[the-three]].)**

What is **sealed** is this: Dao, avatar of the hidden [[omniversal-creator|Creator]] and the one who raised them, **knows the whole aftermath and says nothing.** The silence is **triage-as-mercy** (I.A.7: hiding truth is sometimes mercy, not conspiracy), **not** a strategic withholding gated on some ripening condition. The "later" is simply *whenever the Three happen to return on their own*. It is a father declining to phone traveling children with terrible news.

The mercy is **rational**, not merely sentimental: by the Law of Justice (I.C.16), catastrophe in Eadras is corrected by **consequence, not restoration**. The Three rushing home could not regrow the Elfen groves, un-break the world, or return Velena. Calling them home would **cost** them everything (their peace) and **buy** their children nothing. Dao withholds because the truth purchases no repair — only grief.

## Why this is the most fragile fuse in the founding mythos

Every other concealment in the setting is **passive**: the [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] seals records, [[rpg/closed-time-bubble|closed time bubbles]] excise events, [[ixthal|Ixthal]] silences himself. Dao conceals from **living, freely-returning gods** who could walk back through the door at any moment and discover it. The immune-system mechanism here can fail **by someone's free choice**, not by a seal breaking. It is therefore the single most story-loaded fuse in the founding mythos.

## The seed — *the day the Three come home* (held open per I.G.32; do not over-resolve)

Whenever the founders return (or otherwise learn the truth), three gods who left their children grown and good discover at once: the [[elfen|Elfen]] banished, the [[velrathi|Velrathi]] exiled, the world half-killed — and that one of **Melania's [[tarans|Tarran]]** murdered the Velrathi woman whose brother broke the world.

[[the-three|Melania]] especially would learn that the people she built **from her mother's burning** (her foundational human wound) **became the fire** — the correction eroding back to its own default nature (I.D.18) — while she was away, and that Dao hid it from her out of love. A latent **apocalypse-of-grief**, gated only by Dao's mercy and the Three's free choice not to return or ask. Left deliberately unresolved; more powerful as a fuse than as an event.

## Note on Melania / the Tarran (for the eventual Tarran Grainbound run)

Her foundational wound = witnessing her mother's death by burning as a young child → she built the [[tarans|Tarran]] as **guardians against chaos** ("never again does an innocent burn for want of protection"), on the moon [[gaia|Gaia]] as a guarded cradle whose access she controls (order-as-**safety**, not domination). Per I.D.18 the Tarran are a **correction to a default tendency**, and "societies are most fragile where they correct their own nature" — so the [[firethorn-empire|Firethorn]] decay / Velena's murder is the canonical fragility pattern: the correction eroding back toward the dominating, entitled, chaotic instinct the order was built to suppress. Calibrate her wound as **foundational-but-human** — it is **not** the setting's deepest trauma (that floor is [[ixthal|Ixthal]]: foresaw his civilization's extinction, could not prevent it, then ~2 million years alone as the last mind of a dead species).
