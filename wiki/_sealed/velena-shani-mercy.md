# Shani's Mercy — the True Root of the Demon War (SEALED)

**This is OOC author/plot-spine canon. It is NOT in the public Tome.** Do not wikilink to this page from any public page; do not summarize its contents into [[velena]], [[xarvion]], [[demon-war]], or any other public page. The **public** pages may state only the surface fact that [[xarvion|Xarvion]] pursued a lawful resurrection first and was refused (see breadcrumbs).

**Summary**: The Demon War's true root is a **merciful, correct act of a death-goddess** that caused a billion deaths. [[xarvion|Xarvion]] tried the **lawful resurrection route first**; [[velena|Velena]] was **willing** to return; **[[shani|Shani]] refused — as mercy** (Velena's murder was so brutal that her soul does not remember it in the afterlife's peace, and returning would force her to relive it). Xarvion misread mercy as denial, seized the Spear, and the [[dao|Dao's]]-curse inversion turned grief into the [[demon-war|Demon War]]. No villain: everyone chose love or mercy and it broke the world anyway.

**Sources**: velrathi_consolidated.txt (Section 6), 2026-06-08 ingest; user direction 2026-06-08 (adopt the retcon publicly for the lawful-route attempt; keep the mercy-causation sealed). Supersedes the simpler [[velena]] framing ("content, does not yet wish to return") at the deep level.

## The four-fold tragic sequence

1. **Lawful route, and a willing soul.** Resurrection IS possible under Law 5 with all consents (both death-goddesses + the soul + proper structure) — rare, not impossible. Xarvion tried it first. [[shali|Shali]] (the gentle half) would almost always say yes. **[[shani|Shani]]** (the stern half, "Death Itself," an administrator of the Infernal Bureaucracy alongside [[nox|Nox]], [[daj|Daj]], Erendor, Ignara, Morveth) is the real gate, and routinely refuses. Velena, at peace in the Verdant Fields of [[solarion|Solarion]], **was willing to return.**
2. **The merciful refusal.** Shani **refused** — as **mercy**. Velena's death (raped and murdered by Tarran noble youths) was so brutal that her soul does **not remember it** in the afterlife's peace. Returning would force her to **relive her own murder**. Shani refused to drag a violated soul out of merciful forgetting. **The stern goddess did the kind thing — and the kind thing caused the Demon War.**
3. **The misread.** Xarvion never knew it was mercy. He experienced it as **Death denying him.** He seized the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] to *force* what mercy had withheld — to break the world and give Velena back a life Death had spared her from re-entering. He thought he was rescuing her; **Shani had already rescued her *from* the rescue.**
4. **The inversion.** [[dao|Dao's]] curse stripped his love-motive: he remembers Velena and the vow, but the *need* is gone ("the name had weight but no warmth"). The man who could not accept **one** death caused a **billion**.

## Who holds the proof (stratified by record-keeper)

- **Some living exiled [[velrathi|Velrathi]]** know → a **theological grievance**: the Demon War (for which they were banished) began as Death refusing a grieving brother; a faction believes the **gods share the guilt**. Fuel for the exile's stranger arts and their refusal to accept the justice of the Banishment.
- **The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]]** records it → a **time-release** truth: preserved, discoverable, awaiting a reader (the Curator-Not-Speaker loophole — the Tome may *show* what gods may not *speak*).
- **The Celestial Bureaucratic Archives** record it. **The Infernal Archives** record it → **major seam**: Hell holds **blackmail-grade proof that Death Itself drove a mortal to summon a Demon Prince** — divine fault documented in the one institution whose horror is that *the system works*.
- **Probably not** the Draconic Archives; a **few extremely ancient dragons may or may not** know from personal memory. Unverifiable wildcards.

## Present-day fault line (the resurrection heretics' goal is a horror)

The **Resurrection Heretics** (see [[velena#Her remains and modern interest]]) believe resurrecting Velena would restore Xarvion's severed motive. Per this canon, their goal is **a horror**: to undo divine mercy and **force the memory of her own murder back onto a soul at peace.** Her contested remains (Black Hand remnants, heretics, anti-Xarvion wardens, curse-scholars, demon cults) are sought toward an end far worse than the public framing knows.
