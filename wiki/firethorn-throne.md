# Firethorn Throne

**Summary**: The Firethorn Throne was the seat of [[firethorn-empire|Tarran imperial sovereignty]] — crafted by [[the-three|Melania]] from the leftover [[worldender-crystal|alien crystal]] [[dao]] gave her after the [[runeswords|Runeswords]]' forging. Its alien resonance made the rightful Emperor near-untouchable by direct divine intervention. The Elder Gods explicitly demanded it be **emptied** to turn the [[demon-war|Demon War]] — and after the [[great-betrayal|Emperor was poisoned]] in his bed, the Throne **withdrew itself from history** and has not been seen since.

**Sources**: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt, user direction 2026-05-01

**Last updated**: 2026-05-01

---

## Origin and Forging

The Firethorn Throne was **[[the-three|Melania's]] masterpiece and her warning**.

The Throne, the two Tarran [[runeswords|Runeswords]] (**Brightthorn** and **Darkthorn**), and the [[tarans|Tarran]] race itself were all created **simultaneously** at approximately **39,000 years before present**. (User direction 2026-05-01.) Melania did not first create the Tarran and then later forge their royal artifacts — she crafted all three works as a single act, so the Tarran existed with their throne and royal blades from the moment of their making. The Firethorn Empire's founding ~21,000 years later (~18,000 BP) merely formalized a sovereignty whose emblems already existed.

For the materials, Melania approached her adoptive father [[dao]], who gave her a measured portion of the **[[worldender-crystal|leftover alien crystal]]** he had retrieved from the [[worldender|Worldender's]] remains — enough to create a single great artifact, but not enough to upset the delicate equilibrium he was trying to restore.

The prime crystal had already gone to the **nine original [[runeswords|Runeswords]]'** living hearts. The crystal Melania received was the **secondary portion** — still potent, still alien, but ranked below the prime cuts (source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt).

From this leftover crystal Melania shaped the Firethorn Throne and the two Tarran Runeswords together. **Living crystal thorns** — grown from the same alien matrix — coiled around the seat and backrest of the Throne, glowing with inner fire when a legitimate heir sat upon it.

## Power and Properties

While occupied by the rightful Emperor, the Throne provided extraordinary protection:

- **Difficult for the Elder Gods to influence directly** — the alien resonance disrupted divine perception
- **Shielded against mass arcane assault**
- **Partially veiled even from the eyes of the Omniversal order**
- **Created a pocket of "other-law"** that even the Elder Gods struggled to pierce

The thorns on the throne were said to **bloom with living fire** when the Emperor was just and true, and to **wither and drip black sap** when his rule faltered. On the night of the poisoning, witnesses swore the thorns bled openly for the first time — a crystal weeping for the man who had once worn the crown and for the world that needed him gone.

The Throne sat upon a dais of fused **orichalcum and black [[rathari|Rathar]] stone** in the heart of the imperial domed citadel, displayed publicly. (See [[firethorn-empire]] for context on the imperial hall.)

## Legitimate Claim

The Throne was supernaturally selective:

- It accepted only the **rightful monarch** — the legitimate Firethorn heir by blood and claim
- It would not bend to usurpers, pretenders, or external manipulation
- Its enchantments were **keyed to the Emperor's life and legitimate claim**

This selectivity is why it could not be simply seized by victorious enemies, conquered, or coerced into yielding.

## The Crisis of Wartime

During the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the Throne's protective resonance became a **strategic problem for the Elder Gods themselves**. As long as the Emperor sat upon it:

- The gods could **encourage, empower, and guide** but could not easily **reach in and force outcomes**
- Their direct intervention was throttled
- Coordinated celestial omens and full divine aid were impossible at scale

After the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]] — where the [[three-who-stood|Three Who Stood]] died holding a line the Emperor refused to reinforce — it became clear that the Emperor's leadership would lose the war. **The gods made their demand crystal clear** through oracles and direct visions to key priests and archmagi:

> **The Throne must be empty.**

Only then could divine intervention shift from influence to decisive aid.

## The Great Betrayal

The Throne was not stormed or shattered. It was **emptied** — by the Emperor's own [[velrathi|Velrathi]] princess fiancée, in his shared bed, with a poison perfectly calibrated to bypass the Throne's protective field through intimate contact. He died in his sleep, crown still upon his brow.

By morning the Throne was empty. The gods' constraint was lifted. The final phase of the war began.

See [[great-betrayal]] for the full account.

## The Disappearance

After the Emperor's poisoning emptied the Throne, the Throne **vanished**.

In the chaos of the empire's final collapse — as Tarran loyalists fled through the newly opened rifts to [[gaia|Prime-like Gaia]], as Velrathi scholars sealed their archives, and as the last Firethorn forces burned their own records — the great crystal seat **disappeared from its dais in the domed citadel**. No eyewitness account survives of its removal. No reliable record marks its destination.

> One night it stood empty beneath its vaulted hall, thorns dim and lifeless. By morning it was gone, leaving only a perfectly circular scar in the orichalcum dais where its base had rested for centuries.

(source: Grok WB Generation 4-29.txt)

## Why It Withdrew

The disappearance is explained by the alien crystal's nature:

> The alien crystal answers only to **legitimate sovereignty**. Without a legitimate Firethorn monarch to sit upon it, the crystal **refused to remain a passive relic**. It folded away into whatever liminal space such foreign matter retreats to when it withholds its power.

It is **not** stolen, hidden by mortals, taken by [[dao]] in secret, or destroyed. It is simply **absent — waiting, dormant, and sovereign in its own alien way**.

The circular scar on the dais in the old imperial citadel — and the matching scar in the [[hall-of-kings|Hall of Kings]] — remains as a perfect, slightly warm indentation, as though the crystal **stepped sideways out of reality** the moment its purpose as a bulwark ended.

## Where It Is Now

The Throne is **lost to the ages** in the public record — its actual location unknown to mortal scholars, to the [[katharim|Katharim]] who have searched for centuries, and to the dragon **[[she-who-shall-not-be-named|"She"]]** in the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy]]. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

> **Canonical location** (closely held; not generally known in-universe): the Throne resides in the **[[hall-of-kings|Hall of Kings]]**, **fully cloaked and non-detectable until a worthy claimant appears**. Access is by entering the Hall **through the royal palace on Eadras itself** — *not* through any [[gaia|Gaian reflection]]. The Hall's Door normally manifests in [[firethorn-hold|Firethorn Hold]] on Prime-like Gaia for Swordwielder access, but the Throne's chamber-of-cloaking is reached only via the original imperial citadel on Eadras.

The cloaking is total: no scrying, no divine sight, no Tome consultation has located it. Even She Who Shall Not Be Named — who is contemporary with much of post-Cataclysm history and might be expected to know — does not know where the Throne is. The cloak makes the Throne sovereign in a stricter sense than dormancy alone could: not merely passive but invisible to anyone who is not the rightful claimant.

The Throne is **somewhere within the Hall of Kings** — a place [[dao]] forged — waiting for a worthy claimant who has not yet appeared. Public canon (the centuries of Katharim search records, the Tarran Rite of Empty Claim, the dreams granted to unicorn seers) describes the Throne as *outside the normal map of reality*. That description is true at the level of public access; the deeper canon is that *outside the normal map* means *cloaked within the Hall of Kings, accessed only from the right surface point on Eadras by the right person*.

## Search and Speculation

The Throne's vanishing has become one of the most potent modern myths in Eadras:

- **Exiled Tarran on Prime Gaia** treat it as their greatest shame and greatest promise. Each new generation performs the **Rite of Empty Claim**, standing before the old scar and declaring their lineage. The crystal has never answered.
- **[[katharim|Katharim]]** maintain exhaustive but fruitless search records. Every few centuries a new expedition is quietly funded; all return empty-handed. The scar in the old dais is treated as a holy wound.
- **[[beast-folk|Laughing Third]]** flytings often end with someone dramatically declaring they have found the Throne — usually under a tavern table.
- **Some believe** the rightful claimant still lives — perhaps an unknown descendant hidden among humans, orcs, or even beast-folk — and that the Throne will return the moment that person consciously claims the legacy.

In rare dreams granted to **unicorn seers** or **powerful beast-folk shamans**, the Throne appears as a **great crystal seat floating in a starless void**, its thorns retracted, its inner fire banked to a sullen glow. *It waits. Patient. Alien. Sovereign. It will not return until someone can sit upon it without being burned by its own power.*

## Forgeries

Crystal thorns claiming to match the Throne's signature flame-veining occasionally surface in black markets or ancient ruins. Each time, they draw fortune-hunters, [[black-hand|Black Hand]] agents, and desperate would-be emperors. Every such thorn has proven to be:

- A **clever forgery**, or
- A **lesser fragment that burns out within days**

The true Throne **remains unseen** — a sleeping sovereign that refuses to be found until (or unless) the world once again needs its terrible protection.

## The Modern Shrine

The empty scar where the Throne once stood in the old imperial citadel on Eadras is now a **sealed memorial chamber** visited only by [[katharim|Katharim]] archivists and the occasional daring [[beast-folk|beast-folk]] pilgrim. The scar in the floor remains perfectly smooth, warm, and untouched.

In [[firethorn-hold|Firethorn Hold]] on [[gaia|Prime-like Gaia]], a **second matching scar** in the [[gray-matriarchy|Gray Matriarchy's]] central hall is the **holiest shrine of [[she-who-shall-not-be-named|She Who Shall Not Be Named]]** — where young matrons swear never to let male ambition rise again. The alien crystal scar is said to **pulse faintly in time with the chants of the Circle of Mothers**, as though the lost Throne itself approves of the new matriarchal order.

**Resolved (user direction 2026-05-01)**: She Who Shall Not Be Named does **not** know where the Throne actually is. The Throne resides in the [[hall-of-kings|Hall of Kings]], fully cloaked, accessed only from the imperial palace on Eadras itself; even an ancient dragon of her stature cannot find it.

## Summary

> Melania shaped the leftover crystal into a seat meant to endure. The war emptied it. History lost it. And somewhere, in a place beyond even the Gaian reflections or the deepest vaults, the Firethorn Throne waits — empty, undiminished, and still faintly burning with the alien fire [[dao|Dao]] pulled from the [[worldender|Worldender's]] broken heart.

> It is the one great artifact of the old empire that **refused to become a relic**. It simply **walked out of the story**.
