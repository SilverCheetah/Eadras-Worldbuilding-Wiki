# Trolls

**Summary**: Trolls are living geological constructs — bodies composed of metallic ores and gemstones — engineered by the [[arathi|Arathi]] to be perfect miners. Some ancient elder trolls survived the Cataclysm in hibernation; the modern relationship between trolls and [[dwarves]] is defined by dwarven mining of dormant elders, treated by living trolls as desecration.

**Sources**: Trolls.txt, Open Questions Answers May 1 2026.txt, Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt, notes on various topics 2.txt, Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## Origin

Trolls were created by the [[arathi|Arathi]] (who called themselves the **Nerathi** in their own tongue) to be the perfect miners — **silicon-based, self-replicating, self-repairing constructs** whose bodies are composed of ores and gems and whose minds are housed in **memory crystal brains**. They join the lineage of named Arathi-engineered races, alongside the Arathi servant lineages that preceded the [[the-three|Three]]'s creation of the progenitor races. (sources: Trolls.txt; notes on various topics 2.txt)

Their original Nerathi programming gave them three operational mandates: **locate, refine, and deliver rare minerals and gems**. Every behavior trolls still exhibit — the wandering, the consumption of stone, the budding to fill specific mineral roles, the long hibernations of the elders — descends from those mandates.

## Anatomy

A troll's body is a layered mineral organism:

- **Unrefined ore and raw gems** function as fat and gristle.
- **Highly refined metals and gems** form the active tissues — muscles, circulatory system, sensory organs, and power sources.
- A troll **eats stone, metal, and gems** and grows continuously throughout its life.
- A **memory crystal brain** at the body's core houses cognition and inherited memory.
- A troll possesses **an extraordinary sense of taste/smell for minerals across the entire body** — not localized in a head or a snout, but distributed through the whole organism. This is how a troll feels its way to the next vein. (source: notes on various topics 2.txt)
- Trolls **"swim" through land like fish through water** — the body's mineral substrate flows past native rock the way a swimmer's body parts the sea. They do not tunnel in the dwarven sense; they pass through stone as a living current. (source: notes on various topics 2.txt)

Trolls reproduce by **budding** and have **only one gender**. There is no troll equivalent to gendered reproduction or sexed lineage; troll genealogy is strictly parent-bud. **Buds inherit genetic memory** from their parent — meaning a young troll arrives in the world already carrying the lived experience of its predecessor, and through that the cumulative memory of every troll before, all the way back to the Arathi-era originals. This is one of the reasons trolls are not vengeful: living trolls remember (in genetic-memory form) being mined, being harvested, being woken before time. Memory and grievance both pass down the bud-line; so does the cultural decision not to retaliate. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

## What trolls represent

Beyond their mineral function, trolls are **the philosophical embodiment of geological continuity** in modern Eadras: inherited memory, structural patience, and the conviction that long-lived things should hold their grievances without acting on them. The Great Balance is non-theistic, continuity-oriented, and **anti-vengeful** — it is not a code of restraint applied from outside, but the natural disposition of beings who literally remember every harm done to them and have decided that response would only feed disturbance. (source: Worldbuilding Session Ingest — Eadras Cosmology, Cultures, and Metaphysics.txt)

The Hibernation Horror, taken in this light, is **not malicious invasion but geological event** — *mountains literally waking and walking* — and the dragons' unease about it is unease in the face of a force that is older, slower, and more patient than they are.

## Mineral palette and diet

Troll body composition is **both inherited and acquired**. A troll is what it eats — and a troll can change over time as its diet changes. A troll grown over a vein of orichalcum will incorporate orichalcum into its refined tissues; a troll relocated to a copper-rich mountain will gradually shift its mineral mix as it consumes the local stone and metal. There is no fixed troll palette; there are bodies that record their feeding history. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

This makes individual trolls geologically informative — a master Shada'din miner who knew how to read troll composition could (in principle) reconstruct centuries of a troll's wandering from its body. That such knowledge is mostly used for harvesting rather than reading is one of the quieter tragedies of the Shada'din-troll relationship.

## The hibernation cycle

When a troll reaches the age of **Elder** (roughly **3,000 years**), it enters a long hibernation. This cycle is the reason any ancient trolls survived at all: during the [[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]], **most active trolls were shattered**; the trolls that endured the disaster were already in hibernation when the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] fell. **No resistance was possible** — the Cataclysm was a planetary extinction-level event and active trolls had no greater chance of weathering it than any other large creature. (User direction 2026-05-01.) (source: Trolls.txt)

Hibernation is not permanent. Across the millions of years since the Cataclysm, some elder trolls have **woken multiple times** before entering new dormancies.

### Population — exact numbers

The total troll population is **fixed by the Arathi programming at exactly 2,000** (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt). This cap is not a current census figure — it is a design constraint of the lineage itself, baked in by the original Arathi engineers. Trolls bud, but the global population does not climb above 2,000; the budding rate self-regulates against the population ceiling.

The current distribution of those 2,000:

- **Active: 300–400** — the trolls walking the world, processing ore, drifting between veins, occasionally encountered by [[dwarves|Shada'din]] miners.
- **Hibernating: ~1,600** — the dormant elders, distributed across the planet.

Within the hibernating ~1,600:

- **Fewer than a dozen** are truly colossal, mountain-sized elders.
- The remainder hibernate at sizes ranging from **large hill** to **half a mountain**.

**In the 39,000 years since the younger civilizations arose, no elder troll has awakened.** The oldest dragons — those who remember the pre-civilization world — still recall the **last time** an elder troll woke. They speak of it with **visible unease**. (source: Trolls.txt)

### The Hibernation Horror

Elder troll awakening is rare but not impossible, and what it looks like has acquired a name in dragon and (rarer) mortal record: **the Hibernation Horror**. (source: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt)

The pattern:

- An elder troll has hibernated for so long, and grown so large, that it is no longer recognizable as a creature. It is a **hill** or a **small mountain**. Centuries of weather, soil, and vegetation accumulate over its body. Forests grow on it. Streams cut around it. **Settlements are sometimes built beside it without anyone alive knowing what is sleeping there.**
- When awakening comes, **the mountain splits open**. The elder emerges from inside its own accumulated landscape. It **reshapes into towering humanoid form** — the body remembers its original troll geometry and reconstitutes from the layered mineral mass.
- The elder then **walks the land**. Sometimes for years. Sometimes into the ocean and out the other side. Walking trolls of this scale are not malicious in the modern era — the Great Balance and the genetic memory of grievance both pass down to them — but a walking living mountain is a reshaping force on its own.
- Eventually the elder **returns to dormancy** somewhere new, often a remote range chosen for stability, and begins the long accumulation again.

This is the ancestral horror behind why dragon record-keepers (see [[ratharil#Sealed Hall of First Flame]]) find elder troll awakenings worth memory-crystal preservation: not because the trolls are hostile, but because the awakening itself is the kind of event that erases unwary settlements from the map without intent.

## The dwarven conflict

[[dwarves|Dwarves]] have only existed for less than 39,000 years and never knew the Arathi or saw a troll wake. They view trolls **not as people, but as walking treasure chests**: a lone wandering troll is a walking refinery; a dormant elder is a mountain of refined ore waiting to be quarried. (source: notes on various topics 2.txt)

Standard dwarven practice when a lone troll is encountered is to **hunt it, kill it, and break its body apart** to harvest the refined minerals and gems. Dwarven mining concerns have also **repeatedly mined into dormant elder trolls**, treating the elder body as ore strata.

Trolls **generally flee** rather than fight. They are extremely difficult to trap once they begin to swim through land, and the Great Balance disposes them away from confrontation in any case. **A troll can also be traded with**: anyone who offers something the troll finds genuinely interesting may negotiate passage or material, rather than receive flight or refusal. The single most reliable form of trade-offer is **new music or unfamiliar tonal sequences**, especially anything resembling the **ancient Nerathi language**, to which trolls respond strongly across all ages. A novel song can purchase what no amount of force could compel.

The living trolls have never forgiven the dwarven hunts and mining. They call the ancient ones **"the Sleeping Ancestors"** and **"Grandfather Stone,"** and consider every such killing or mining operation a grave desecration. Each dwarven hold's mining history is, to a troll, a roster of grave robberies. (source: Trolls.txt)

## Religion — the Great Balance

Trolls hold to a religion of **the Great Balance** — a quiet, abstract conviction that all things move in equilibrium and that the role of long-lived beings is to refrain from disturbing it. Trolls are **not particularly vengeful**: even toward [[dwarves|Shada'din]] who kill lone trolls and mine into the dormant Sleeping Ancestors, living trolls take such things as part of the Balance, not as moral injuries that demand redress. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

There are **no troll ascended mortal deities.** The Balance has no named god. Worship — to the extent troll practice is recognizable as worship at all — is contemplative and generational, not personal or transactional. There are no troll temples, no troll clergy, no festival days, no figure to invoke. **There is also no institutional vendetta-structure**: no council, no lineage-house, no vendetta-keeper that pursues redress against dwarven holds. The grievance is real and remembered (passed down through bud-genetic-memory); the response is not retaliation. See [[religion-in-eadras#Troll religion — the Great Balance]].

## Resolved canon (user direction 2026-05-01)

- Cataclysm: **no resistance was possible** (ELE).
- Bud reproduction: **inherits genetic memory** (and through it cumulative ancestral memory).
- Mineral palette: **both** — inherited from feeding context, and changes over time as diet changes.
- Dragon archive of troll awakenings: yes, [[ratharil|Ratharil]]'s deep archives preserve both stone/metal-tablet records and (in a few cases) memory-crystal recordings of eldest dragons who personally experienced past elder-troll awakenings. The dragons' unease is grounded in genuine remembered events, not folklore. See [[ratharil#Sealed Hall of First Flame]].
- Living-troll institutional structure: none. Trolls are not vengeful; the Balance precludes formal grievance bodies.
