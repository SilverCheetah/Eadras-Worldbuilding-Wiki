# The Century of Sorrows

**Summary**: The **Century of Sorrows** was the [[black-hand|Black Hand's]] hundred-year operational ramp-up to the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] — a deliberate campaign of engineered magical disasters that slowly turned the populace of Eadras against all magic, supplying the doctrinal evidence for the *all magic is wicked* framing. Hundreds of major incidents and thousands of smaller ones, escalating across the century and reaching a crescendo in the final 15–20 years, were the operational substrate the Black Hand built so the Crusade could become self-sustaining without continuous direct manipulation. Once the Crusade reached self-sustaining violence, the Black Hand largely stopped manufacturing new disasters and shifted to *directing* the resulting popular fury toward [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] and the [[conduit-cities|Conduit Towers]].

## Strategic purpose

The Century of Sorrows was the **operational substrate** of the Black Hand's larger Spear-search diversion. As the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]] approached, the Black Hand needed:

- **Reduced institutional scrutiny** on their own movements (achieved by directing all institutional attention toward magic users)
- **Doctrinal cover** for mass mortal action against [[netharim|Netharim]] and other magic users (achieved by the *all magic is wicked* framing the disasters proved)
- **A self-sustaining cycle** that would not require continuous Black Hand intervention to maintain (achieved by letting the persecution exceed its initial seeding)

The Century of Sorrows provided all three. The Crusade that followed could be safely left to its own internal momentum while Black Hand cells conducted the Spear search with minimal institutional attention on themselves.

## Operational shape

The campaign **slowly ramped up over approximately one hundred years**, reaching a crescendo in the final 15–20 years before the Crusade properly began. There were:

- **Hundreds of major incidents** — public catastrophes of city-scale or village-extinction scale
- **Thousands of smaller ones** — individual atrocities, family-scale tragedies, district-scale dissolutions

Each incident was designed to provoke **horror, revulsion, and ultimately anger at magic itself**. The Black Hand cell-system meant most operators worked one or two incidents in a generation; few mortals at the operator-level could see the pattern. The Inner Hand maintained the pattern; the Outer Hand executed individual cells without knowing what they fit into.

## Representative major disasters

The following are among the canonical major-incident references that survived the era in some form, reaching modern record:

- **The Vaelis poison cloud** — a magical poison cloud rolled over the city of Vaelis at night; **18,000 dead** by morning. Hand-engineered, framed publicly as the work of a coven of weather-mages. The mages were lynched within days.
- **The Dun Lira undead plague** — a plague of *hungry* undead — sapient, organised, ravenous — ravaged the trading town of Dun Lira before being put down at heavy cost. Necromantic catalyst seeded by a Hand operative weeks earlier; framed as the work of a single named necromancer who had passed through the town. The named necromancer was burned alive while still claiming innocence.
- **The Threeforks chaos pocket** — a chaos pocket ruptured at the caravan village of Threeforks, **dissolving reality before hundreds of witnesses**. Bodies, buildings, soil, sky melted into the local void; the surviving witnesses could not afterward explain what they had seen. Framed publicly as the inevitable consequence of unregulated magical practice in border communities.

Other recurring disaster modes the Century preserved in public memory:

- Rivers transformed into **liquid shadow** — water flowing as black motion, swallowing all who tried to drink
- Fields growing **screaming flesh-crops** — staple grains replaced overnight with sapient meat-tissue that cried out when harvested
- **Wild magic surges** warping entire districts — geometries failing, time misbehaving, language collapsing

The pattern was deliberate: each disaster was *the kind of horror that magic could plausibly produce*, presented in a context where the witnesses' only available explanation was *some magic user did this*.

## Self-sustaining phase

Once the Crusade became self-sustaining — typically dated to the final years of the Century — the Black Hand largely **stopped manufacturing new disasters** and focused on **directing the resulting violence** toward the targets the larger operation required:

- Channeling popular fury toward [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] and the [[conduit-cities|Conduit Towers]] (the priority targets for the Spear-search-diversion's political-revolution dimension; see [[anti-magic-crusade#A Political Revolution Against Tower Rule]])
- Sustaining the *all magic is wicked* doctrine through receptive churches and martial orders
- Allowing local violence to escalate organically once seeded

The shift from manufacturing-disasters to directing-fury was the operational signature of a successful Hand campaign: the populace continued the work the Hand had begun, while the Hand returned to its original objective.

## Plausible deniability

The Century of Sorrows is documented in modern scholarly record as a series of **separate, unrelated catastrophes** rather than a coordinated campaign. Even modern anti-Hand investigators struggle to reconstruct the pattern; the cell-system, the long temporal spread (a hundred years of operations spanning multiple generations of operators), and the lack of any surviving central coordination document have all preserved the deniability. The connections between specific disasters and Black Hand orchestration are inferred from pattern and motive rather than from direct evidence.

The deepest [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] inner-circle scholarship believes the pattern is genuine — that the Sorrows *were* coordinated as a single campaign — but cannot prove it conclusively. The [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome of Baldaran]] preserves the truth in its sealed chapters; mortal certainty must remain inference-based.

## Why the populace turned

A defining feature of the Century is that the populace's eventual turn against magic was **not unreasonable in context**. The Hand did not need to convince a happy stable population that magic was evil; they needed only to inflict so many specific magical horrors on so many specific communities that *every mortal had been touched by one*. The argument *"all magic is wicked"* needed no sophistication when every grandparent could name a town the Sorrows had eaten.

The horror of the Century is partly that the populace's eventual choice to act against magic users was, in their own terms, sound: the world had been demonstrably falling apart, and the immediate cause appeared in every case to be a magic user. The deeper horror is that the entire chain — from disaster, to popular reaction, to Crusade, to Weave damage, to Thousand-Year Dimming — was a single instrument the Black Hand played, with the populace unknowingly forming the orchestra.

## Open canon

- The **complete catalogue** of major Century-of-Sorrows incidents — most are open canon; the three named above are the canonical examples in current record
- The **specific Black Hand cells** that executed each major incident — operational detail, open canon
- Whether any **Century survivor** lived long enough to recognise the pattern across decades — the kind of figure modern anti-Hand investigators are still hunting for in old chronicles
- The **geographical distribution** of the Sorrows — open canon; modern scholarship suggests the campaign deliberately spread incidents across all major populated regions to ensure no part of Eadras felt safe from magic
- The exact **start-date** of the Century — open canon; the closing 15–20-year crescendo is well-attested, but the Hand's initial seeding could have begun substantially earlier
