# Ink Lords

**Summary**: The Ink Lords — *the Story Eaters* — are one of the **Five Modern Oceanic Races** descended from the **Sea'tua**, the [[elfen|Elfen]]-engineered overseers of oceanic balance. They are communal octopus-like scholars who travel among other societies to collect and consume well-told, emotionally true stories. Feeding does not erase the memory from the teller — only the Ink Lord is fed. They maintain hidden Memory Groves where particularly delicious stories are stored in suspended ink droplets.

**Sources**: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt; Peoples of Eadras.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-01

---

## Origin

Ink Lords descend from the **Sea'tua**, the Elfen-engineered race created to oversee natural balance in the oceans. They are one of the five Sea'tua-descended modern oceanic peoples (source: Peoples of Eadras.txt; Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt).

## Anatomy

Ink Lords have **octopus-like upper bodies with heavily armored crab-like lower shells**. They can expel **massive clouds of magical ink** that serve as both defense and as libraries: an ink cloud properly authored is a temporary suspended archive that an Ink Lord (or a trained reader) can move through and "read" for as long as the cloud persists.

## Diet: stories

Ink Lords **feed on well-told, emotionally true stories** for nourishment. The story must be both:

- **Well-told** — the telling itself matters; a brilliant story badly told is poor food.
- **Emotionally true** — fabrications, lies, and stories the teller does not believe are not nourishing. A skilled liar can serve an Ink Lord empty calories, but the Ink Lord will know.

**Feeding does not remove or erase the memory from the teller.** This is a critical canonical fact and the basis of Ink Lord ethics. The teller retains the story in full; only the Ink Lord is fed. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt)

## Society and travel

Many Ink Lords live for years among other races — surface or oceanic — specifically to gather new stories. A long-resident Ink Lord usually positions themselves as a scholar, librarian, archivist, or quiet bystander at gatherings. Communities that have grown used to a particular Ink Lord come to treat them as a sort of collective memory.

They maintain **hidden Memory Groves** where particularly delicious stories are stored in **suspended ink droplets**. A Memory Grove is an Ink Lord's library and pantry both; the location of one is rarely shared, and the contents are often shared only on an honor basis between Ink Lords.

## Honor

Ink Lords are **sardonic, hungry, and surprisingly honorable**. They consider stories to be sacred — both for what they are and for what they cost the teller to tell well — and **they will never break a story-debt**. An Ink Lord who has accepted a story owes the teller something, and that debt is settled or it festers; the Ink Lords have no equivalent of a friendly default.

The corollary: an Ink Lord who lies *about* a story (claiming it for nourishment when it was a fabrication, or misrepresenting it to a third party) is shamed across the entire diaspora. There is no faster way for an Ink Lord to lose standing.

## Relations with other oceanic peoples

- **[[merrow|Merrow]]** — welcoming. A long-resident Ink Lord in a Merrow Growth Circle may eventually earn a polyp-arrangement of their own.
- **[[sirens|Sirens]]** — eager for long-form stories whose internal echoes can fold into Siren composition.
- **[[shimmercloaks|Shimmercloaks]]** — wary collegial relationship; both deal in memory but disagree on which medium is the higher form.
- **[[tideclad|Tideclad]]** — Ink Lords are frequent embedded chroniclers of Tideclad warden schools.
- **[[sahuagin|Sahuagin]]** — rare. A Blood Fleet that has traveled with a resident Ink Lord at any point in its history is considered, by other Sahuagin captains, to be marked in some indefinable way.
- **[[kuo-toa|Kuo-toa]]** — avoided. The Kuo-toa interest in "corrections" makes their stories actively dangerous to absorb.
