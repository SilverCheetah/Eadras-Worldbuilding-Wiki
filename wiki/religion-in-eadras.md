# Religion in Eadras

**Summary**: Religion in Eadras spans 100+ million years of theological development, from the dragons' first worship of the Source through the post-Weave realization that not all magic is divine; today, worship crosses racial lines freely, with Elder Gods accepting devotion they do not crave and Godborn / Ascended Mortal gods (such as [[inki|Inki]] and [[baldaran|Baldaran]]) actively encouraging it.

**Sources**: May 1 revisions.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-06

---

## The Early Ages â€” all magic was divine

In the deep time after the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]], when the [[dragons|first dragons]] awakened, **all magic was understood as divine in nature.** The first dragons worshiped only the **Source**. Worship was direct, personal, and open-air; there were no temples, idols, or organized priesthoods.

When the dragons later learned of the Elder Gods, they extended the same understanding to them. When **[[unicorns|unicorns]]** later learned from the dragons, they too developed priests who could call forth miracles through structured divine spells. For a very long time â€” through the long ages of dragon dominance, the [[chaos-crusades|two Chaos Crusades]], and the entirety of the **First Age** â€” *all magic was considered the province of the divine*. Wizards and Netharim as we now know them did not yet exist; the only practitioners of magic were priests, druids, and divinely touched dragons and unicorns. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

## The Turning Point â€” Lirra's Weave

Ten thousand years ago, during the [[demon-war|Demon War]], the goddess [[lirra|Lirra]] created the **[[magic-in-eadras|Weave]]**. This was both a wartime defense against demonic siphoning and an act of cosmological reform. Its theological consequence was as significant as its martial one:

> **The creation of the Weave proved beyond doubt that not all magic is divine.**

By fundamentally restructuring the fabric of arcane magic, Lirra showed that magic could be **engineered and wielded by mortal minds without direct divine intercession.** Netharim â€” who can natively touch the Weave â€” became living proof. Wizards followed soon after, training to read patterns the goddess herself had laid down.

The theological upheaval continues to echo today. Some priesthoods still maintain that all magic remains, in some hidden sense, *of* the divine â€” even if not always *from* a specific deity. Others accept the new world: divinity gives blessings, the Weave gives Netharim practice, and the two intersect only where Lirra (and a few other gods) actively choose. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

## Temples and worship

| Tradition | Temples | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| **Dragons** | Originally none. Eventually built **one** Temple in [[ratharil|Ratharil]], dedicated to the worship of all the gods (see [[ratharil#The Temple|Ratharil â€” The Temple]]) | Personal, open-air, restrained |
| **Unicorns** | Originally none. | Personal, open-air, herd-shared |
| **Younger humanoid races** | Built temples, shrines, and complex institutions from the beginning | Communal, ritualized, institutionalized |

Dragon and unicorn worship still tends toward the older mode even where temples now exist. The single Temple in Ratharil is a notable exception â€” and notable because, as a dragon working, it honors **all the gods** rather than any one. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

## How the gods receive worship

| Tier | Stance toward worship |
|---|---|
| **[[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]]** | Do not crave worship. They accept it when freely given and have allowed their priesthoods to develop their own traditions, but are not strengthened or diminished by the volume of devotion. |
| **Elderborn** (Erendor, Handwright, Agron, Elyria, Vaelor, Ignara, Thalira, Morveth, Aureon, Fortuna) | Actively desire and encourage worship. Their faiths are more personal and fervent. |
| **Ascended Mortal Gods** (e.g. [[inki|Inki]], [[baldaran|Baldaran]], [[din-shadar|Din'shadar]], [[aramet|Aramet]], the Three Last Lights, the Dragon Gods, the Unicorn Gods, the Elfen, Velrathi, Tarran, and Human ascended) | Actively desire and encourage worship. Their faiths are typically the most personal and fervent of all. |

This pattern matters in practice: a temple of [[lirra|Lirra]] runs differently from a temple of [[inki|Inki]]. The Elder God's hall accepts attendance and offering at worshipper-set rhythms; the Ascended Mortal's hall is dense with festival days, feast-day calendars, and rituals designed to gather, name, and remember the faithful. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

## Cross-racial worship

**There is no strict race-based religion in Eadras.** Worship crosses racial lines freely. A single city may contain temples to many different gods, with priests and devotees of many races mingling in shared rites. Race-specific Ascended Mortal gods (Inki for [[gnomes]], Din'shadar for [[dwarves|Shada'din]], Korvath for the original Tarran-line, Vexara for the Velrathi, the Elfen ascended, the Dragon and Unicorn ascended, etc.) generally welcome worshippers of other races; some have notable cross-race minority cults.

Two consequences:

1. **Pantheon membership, not racial identity, drives priesthood.** A human can serve as a priest of the Shada'din god Din'shadar in good standing. A goblin may pour libations at a Beast-Folk's [[flyting|flyting]]-hall. A dwarf and an elf may share a single Aramet shrine in a frontier outpost.
2. **Cities reflect their layered histories.** An old port city built atop a Rathar foundation may have a [[katharim|Katharim]] necropolis below, a Lirran tower in the marketplace, an Inki workshop-shrine in the gnomish quarter, a Din'shadar foundry-altar in the smithy district, and an open-air Source observance on the highest terrace where the dragons land.

## The Source as ur-deity question â€” resolved

The standing theological dispute is **resolved in canon**: *the dragons mistook a force for a god*. The Source is **not a deity** â€” and it is **not an expression of [[lirra|Lirra]] or of the [[magic-in-eadras#The Weave|Weave]]** either. The Source predates both: it is the **leftover creation energy** of [[primordial-duo|Evye and Fryr]] throughout the [[mythos|Mythos]] of Eadras, left there when they sacrificed themselves into it (see [[magic-in-eadras#1. The Source (The Sun)|magic-in-eadras]] and [[magic-in-eadras#Quintessence, Eldritch, Mana|the three-state model]]). The dragons of the Early Ages perceived the Source as the highest ultimate and accorded it the deference owed to a god â€” they were not entirely wrong about its primordial standing, only about its identity as a deity. (User direction 2026-05-01 / refined 2026-05-06.)

Modern Lirran scholars regard the dragons' Source-veneration as a sincere act of worship that arrived at the wrong altar â€” and treat it generously for that reason. Lirra has *governance* over magic and Netharim practice as her divine portfolio; she is not its origin, and the Weave is a structure she wove during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] *atop* the planetary magical infrastructure (Source, [[seven-conduits|conduits]], [[eldritch-cycle|cycle]]) that already existed.

## Troll religion â€” the Great Balance

The [[trolls]] hold to a religion of **the Great Balance** â€” a quiet, abstract conviction that all things move in equilibrium and that the role of long-lived beings is to refrain from disturbing it. Living trolls are *not particularly vengeful*, even toward [[dwarves|Shada'din]] who kill lone trolls and mine into the dormant Sleeping Ancestors; they take such things as part of the Balance, not as moral injuries that demand redress. (User direction 2026-05-01.)

There are **no troll ascended mortal deities.** The Balance has no named god. Worship â€” to the extent troll practice is recognizable as worship â€” is contemplative and generational, not personal or transactional. This makes troll religion structurally unique among Eadras's faiths: it has no clergy, no temples, no festival days, and no figure to invoke.
