# The Shunned

**Summary**: The **Shunned** are Woodkin-adjacent races or beings who **prey upon humans and reject [[fae-da-nar|Fae-da'Nar]] law**. They are *not merely disliked* — they are outside the compact. The [[four-laws-of-fae-da-nar#III. The Third Law — Oppose predatory Shunned races|Third Law of Fae-da'Nar]] authorises Woodkin action against them when their predation threatens humans (and therefore the hidden Woodkin society's secrecy). Specific identities vary by region; the category is functional, not racial.

**Sources**: faedanar-01/02/03 (2026-05-15 ingest from Elder Gods chat 1.txt).

**Last updated**: 2026-05-15.

---

## What makes a being Shunned

A race, lineage, or being is Shunned when **two conditions** are met:

1. **Predation on humans** — they take human victims (consumption, harvesting, child-theft, blood-tribute, soul-feeding, or other forms of extractive harm) as a regular part of their existence
2. **Rejection of Fae-da'Nar law** — they do not accept the [[four-laws-of-fae-da-nar|Four Laws]] as binding on themselves

The conditions are **conjunctive**. A being that preys on humans but is willing to be bound by Fae-da'Nar Law (and therefore restrains their predation) is *not* Shunned — they are a Woodkin under discipline. A being that rejects Fae-da'Nar Law but does not prey on humans is *not* Shunned — they are simply outside the compact in a non-threatening way.

The Shunned are specifically the **dangerous-and-unbound** combination.

## Not all dangerous beings are Shunned

The word must be **precise**. Many beings in the Frontier wildlands are dangerous without being Shunned:

- **Wild animals** — even very dangerous ones — are not Shunned. They are weather.
- **Solitary fey of unpredictable temperament** — fierce, occasionally hostile, but bound to the compact when the threshold matters — are Woodkin, not Shunned
- **Predatory species that take only Woodkin victims** are not Shunned (the Third Law specifies *humans*; intra-Woodkin predation is governed by other arrangements)
- **Beings whose hunger occasionally crosses into human predation** but who accept restraint when warned are Woodkin under disciplinary scrutiny, not Shunned

The category is **functional and behavioural**, not racial. Two members of the same species may differ in status — one bound by Fae-da'Nar, one Shunned — based on their relationship to the compact.

## Examples of typical Shunned categories

Regional and species-specific; not exhaustive. Common Shunned categories across Eadras include:

- **Child-eaters** — beings whose survival depends on taking human children specifically; almost universally Shunned because the threat to settlements is unmistakable
- **Soul-feeders** — entities that consume human soul-substance rather than (or in addition to) flesh; particularly dangerous because their predation may produce [[unreceived|Unreceived]] cases
- **Bog-walkers / fen-claimers** — entities that pull travellers into specific marsh or fen locations as ritual prey
- **Forest-luring fey-types** — beings who use glamour to lead humans into wildlands they don't return from; the deliberate-luring distinguishes Shunned from accidental fey-encounter
- **Pact-broken fey** — fey who entered binding pacts with Fae-da'Nar and subsequently violated them; some are formally Shunned after second-violation hearings
- **Demon-touched lineages** — Woodkin whose ancestry includes demonic contamination from the [[demon-war|Demon War]] era and whose predation has become demon-shaped; a complicated edge case that the [[druids|Druids]] handle individually

Specific named groups vary by region. The Council declined to name comprehensive examples on the grounds that the category's *function* matters more than any particular naming.

## How Fae-da'Nar acts against the Shunned

The Third Law's enforcement is **specifically threshold-activated**: action against the Shunned occurs *when their predation threatens humans*, not as proactive extermination. The reasoning:

- **Constant hunting creates constant war**, and constant war creates exposure that the First Law forbids
- **Some Shunned are difficult to distinguish** from lawful Woodkin until they act
- **Shunned populations may be partially recoverable** — some have been brought into compact-discipline historically, though the success rate is low

When action is required, it is conducted by:

- **Druid-blessed hunting parties** — small, specifically commissioned, often mixed-species
- **Fey strike-groups** — for cases where the Shunned threat is itself fey
- **Settlement-edge boundary-keepers** — long-standing watchful presence that intercepts Shunned at the human-Woodkin boundary
- **Quiet violence** — the typical mode; Shunned attacks must be stopped before they become a human story large enough to summon soldiers

The action is sometimes **very loud** (fey-style), but the outcome is the same: the Shunned threat is removed; human awareness of the removal is minimised.

## The Shunned and the Anti-Magic Crusade

The [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade's]] ideology did not distinguish Shunned from Woodkin. Crusaders attacked all *strange* beings on the assumption that they were demonic.

The consequences:

- **Some Shunned were killed** by crusaders, accomplishing in a few decades what Fae-da'Nar would have taken centuries to handle
- **Some Shunned hid more deeply** during the Crusade and have not re-emerged; they may be effectively extinct or merely better-concealed
- **Some Shunned benefited** from the Crusade's destruction of the lawful Woodkin populations that had constrained them; the post-Crusade decades saw temporary Shunned-pressure increases on frontier settlements
- **Fae-da'Nar's anti-Shunned function was disrupted** during the Crusade and took decades to rebuild

The complex aftermath produced the modern asymmetric situation: Shunned are *rarer* than before the Crusade but the **Fae-da'Nar enforcement infrastructure** that contained them is also weaker. The [[woundwatch|Woundwatch]] addresses part of this gap on the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] specifically.

## The Shunned and demons

A persistent edge case: **demonic possession or demon-shaped predation** can occur in beings who would otherwise be lawful Woodkin. Such beings become functionally Shunned during the demonic influence; they may or may not return to compact-binding if the influence ends.

The [[druids|Druids]] handle these cases individually. The general principle:

- **A Woodkin who acts as Shunned under demonic influence** can sometimes be saved if the influence is treated quickly
- **A Woodkin who has been Shunned long enough** for the demonic influence to integrate into their identity is treated as Shunned regardless of origin
- **A Woodkin born into a demon-touched lineage** is judged by their own behaviour, not by lineage alone

This is one of the most morally difficult areas of Fae-da'Nar judgment. Some Druids have built reputations specifically as judges of demon-edge Woodkin cases.

## What the Shunned think of Fae-da'Nar

(Speculative — the Shunned do not maintain accessible records.)

The Shunned presumably view Fae-da'Nar as a coalition of cowards who have sold their nature for false safety with humans. The compact's restraint reads, from the Shunned perspective, as **self-erasure**: a being that does not eat what it was born to eat is not the being it was born to be.

This narrative — *the compact is a betrayal of true nature* — is the recurring Shunned ideology when one surfaces. Compare the [[flesh-eaters|Flesh-Eaters']] appeal to "true dragon nature." The pattern is general: antagonists across Eadras tend to frame restraint as denial of essence.

The Fae-da'Nar counter-position, by contrast, is that the compact is **a being choosing what kind of being to be**. The choice does not erase nature; it disciplines it. The two framings cannot be reconciled.
