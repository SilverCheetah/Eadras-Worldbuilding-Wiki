# The Orden

**Summary**: The Orden are a seafaring, clan-based culture of the modern era â€” Viking-analogue in feel but distinct in lineage and lore â€” known for blunt honesty, martial tradition, respect for elders, the rite-of-passage called the **Tale-Earning**, and the parallel rite-of-passage of the **whale-honey harvesting voyage** that produces their signature **whale-honey mead** in cooperation with the [[merrow|Marrow]]. They have largely transitioned from raiding to trading and diplomacy across the last four centuries, and that transition has matured into something genuinely consequential: the **Conduit Compact**, a Hanseatic-style league of conduit-anchored Orden trade-cities that today controls the lion's share of overland and riverine ley-trade in the modern world. The Orden hold a deep symbiotic alliance with the [[merrow|Merrow]], a steadily growing presence at the great [[seven-conduits|conduit]] termini, and a sharp commercial rivalry with the [[bold|Bold Conduit Republic]] (whose merchants nickname them *the Gray Ledger*).

The signature [[orden-whale-honey-mead|whale-honey mead]] is produced from a thick amber substance that the great northern whales squirt into the cold sea to lure schools of small prey; Orden harvesters lure the whales to the surface (with [[merrow|Marrow]] guidance) and draw the substance via long ivory-and-teak syringes sealed with whale blubber. A typical 6-day voyage harvests from dozens of whales; participation is the **dangerous rite of passage** every young Ordon is expected to make at least once. The finished mead is always shared with the Marrow, who carry bottles back to their underwater cities. See [[orden-whale-honey-mead]] for the full treatment.

The village-scale **cast-iron communal hot-tub** â€” the heaviest object in any Orden village, heated over fire and used for daily bathing â€” also doubles as **emergency shelter for the children if the village comes under attack** (drop the kids in, drop a heavy iron lid over the top, makeshift bunker). See [[bathing-customs#Orden]].

## Society and Governance

Orden society is organized into independent **clans**, each ruled by a **Jarl** (clan king). Jarls command significant loyalty within their own territories.

At the national level, the clans elect a **High King** (sometimes **Overking**) through a **clan moot**. The position rotates among the Jarls and is treated as both a political honor and a burdensome duty. The High King serves as the primary representative when dealing with outsiders and foreign powers â€” and because the Orden dislike outsiders' flowery language, hidden motives, and political maneuvering, the role is frequently regarded as a punishment.

In times of existential threat â€” when an external force angers all the clans, or when the gods declare a new Unrestricted Elder God a danger to the world â€” the High King becomes the **national war leader**. If a better strategist exists among the Jarls, the High King will defer command to them. Such unified mobilizations are rare but extremely effective.

## The Tale-Earning

Young Orden (primarily men, though not exclusively) are expected to leave their home clan for several years in a rite of passage called the **Tale-Earning** (also *gaining glory*, *earning stories*). The purpose is to gain experience, build friendships across clans, and **return with tales worthy of the longhouse fires**.

Common paths during the Tale-Earning include:

- Serving in another Jarl's longhouse
- Joining trading or exploratory voyages
- Serving at the High King's court to learn politics and diplomacy
- Handling local problems and adventures within Orden lands

Success is measured by the quality and impact of the stories one brings back.

## Martial and Social Culture

The Orden revere combat as **sport and training**. Inter-clan duels and brawls â€” especially drunken winter hall fights â€” are common and respected. Duels are rarely to the death and serve to improve skill and settle disputes. **Elders** â€” particularly veteran fighters and mariners â€” are deeply respected, even when they become somewhat addled with age. When an elder speaks from experience, younger Orden listen.

The culture is remarkably open: **any individual willing to live by Orden ways** (plain speech, respect for elders, participation in Tale-Earning, etc.) **can be accepted regardless of race**.

## Non-Human Members

Two groups are notable within Orden society:

- **A dwarven clan** lives among the Orden â€” small in number but deeply respected, valued especially for **metalworking and shipbuilding**.
- **The [[merrow|Merrow]]** (sea elves) have adopted many Orden customs. They maintain their own underwater civilization but regularly come ashore for celebrations, moots, and diplomacy. They cannot live permanently on land but are fully integrated allies.

## The Conduit Compact â€” Hanseatic-style trade league

The Orden's transition from raiding to trading across the last four centuries did not stay informal. Across the modern era it has matured into the **Conduit Compact**: a loose but ironclad confederation of **six to eight major Orden conduit-anchored trade cities** bound by shared tariffs, mutual defense charters, and the standing principle that an attack on one Compact city is treated as an attack on all.

### Structure

The Compact's central institution is the **High Council of Guildmasters** â€” a periodically convened assembly drawn from each member city's senior guild and clan leadership. The Council meets in **rotating neutral spires** (whichever member city is currently hosting the year's assembly), and its decisions on tariff harmonization, mutual-defense activations, treaty-level external dealings, and major shared infrastructure are binding on the member cities until the next Council overrules them.

The Council overlays â€” and does not replace â€” the older Orden clan-and-Jarl governance. Each member city is still ruled internally by its own Jarl and clans; the **High King** is still elected at the national-level [[#Society and Governance|clan moot]] and still mediates between Compact cities and external civilizations. The Compact handles **trade** and **mutual defense among the member cities**; the older Orden political structures handle **everything else**. The two systems coexist because the Orden are temperamentally suited to running both â€” Jarls who enjoy ruling a clan can leave the Compact's tariff arithmetic to the Guildmasters, and Guildmasters who prefer ledgers to politics can leave the High King to deal with foreign princes.

The Compact's standing principle is captured in a phrase its merchants like to repeat:

> *A contract is a spell â€” break it and the Weave breaks you.*

Compact cities take contracts seriously. Their notarial system is famously rigorous (every major deal triple-recorded in crystal ledgers, with a Windspeaker-witnessed copy held in the city's [[order-sharanel|Tower of Lirra]] for permanent record), and their reputation for paying what they owe and collecting what they are owed is a major reason Compact merchants have outpaced their rivals across the modern era.

### Order Sharanel integration

A defining feature of the Conduit Compact is its tight working relationship with the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]]. **Every major Compact city contains a [[seven-conduits|Tower of Lirra]] or a Spire adjunct**, and Compact custom is to use Order Sharanel Windspeakers as **neutral arbitrators** in disputes between member cities, between Compact merchants, and (where the Order will accept the role) between Compact merchants and outside civilizations.

The relationship suits both parties. The Order gets a politically central role and a reliable funding base; the Compact gets independent third-party authority that no single member city can corrupt. The standing Order Sharanel objection to Compact decisions is rare but has happened, and when it has, the Compact has tended to defer.

### Economy

The Compact controls the lion's share of:

- **Overland and riverine ley-trade** across the modern continents, including [[mana-metals|mana-metals]], pre-Cataclysm relics, and rare crystalmancy components
- **Bulk industrial goods** flowing between [[gnomes|gnome]] workshops and [[dwarves|Shada'din]] factories on the long routes
- **Magitek-supported caravan logistics** for overland routes that would otherwise be impassable
- **Ley-anchored stable trade routes** that pass between conduit termini, where the Weave's ambient stability makes magical transport unusually reliable

The Compact deliberately does not chase **sea-ley trade** with the same intensity. That domain is dominated by the [[bold|Bold Conduit Republic]]'s aggressive privateer-merchant fleets and short-distance Spire-Step routes; Compact merchants concede the sea and own the land. The two operations rub against each other constantly.

### Cultural character

Compact merchants are pragmatic, contract-obsessed, and ostentatiously wealthy by Orden standards (which is to say, wealthy in a much-grumbled-at way that the older Jarls find faintly offensive). Their festivals are **trade fairs with mechanical parades and public auctions**. Their feasts include public ceremonial accountings of the year's debts paid and contracts honoured. Their high holiday is the day each member city's books close at the end of the trade year.

By Bold's reckoning the Compact is **stodgy, monopolistic, and joyless**. The Compact's standing reply is that joylessness is a small price to pay for solvency, and that Bold can play with sea-foam fortunes if it wants while the Compact owns the land routes. The mutual contempt is a defining feature of modern continental commerce.

The older, non-Compact Orden â€” the inland clans and the smaller coastal Jarldoms that did not become trade cities â€” sometimes view the Compact with mild embarrassment. The reply from the Compact cities is that nothing about the Compact prevents anyone from doing the Tale-Earning, the brawls, the elder-respect, and the proper raids when called for; Compact merchants are still Orden, they just spend more time on ledgers.

### Civilizational stage

Under the [[modernity|modernity framework]], the Conduit Compact cities are **Stage 3 â€” mercantile-urban civilizations**: notarial systems backed by Tower-of-Lirra-witnessed crystal ledgers, mutual-defense charters, treaty-grade tariff harmonization, and conduit-anchored freight networks. The Orden are unusual in maintaining a **mixed-stage profile across one people**: the Compact cities are firmly Stage 3, while the older inland clans and smaller coastal Jarldoms remain **Stage 2 â€” agrarian-and-martial** in configuration. Both are legitimately Orden, and the framework's anti-linearity principle applies cleanly: the inland clans have not "failed to develop." They are solving different problems under different pressures, and the cultural compact between Compact and clan explicitly preserves that.

## The Merrow Alliance

The Merrow and Orden share a deep symbiotic bond. The Merrow assist Orden ships with **navigation, storm warnings, and sea-creature defense**. Most importantly, they **rescue Orden sailors who go overboard**: using water-breathing magic, they bring the sailors to underwater villages as honored guests, show them their civilization (including underwater brawling, much appreciated by Orden tastes), and eventually return them to the surface.

### Deep Friends â€” and *Fish Friends*

Sailors who earn the Merrow's highest respect are named **Deep Friends** and given a **reusable charm that allows them to breathe underwater**. These individuals often become lifelong cultural ambassadors, frequently returning to visit the Merrow.

Among the Orden, Deep Friends are affectionately (and somewhat teasingly) called **Fish Friends**. Fish Friends usually become valued advisors to coastal Jarls.
