# The Thousand-Year Dimming

**Summary**: The Thousand-Year Dimming was the millennium-long magical dark age following the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]], during which no mortal spells above the Fifth Circle could be cast because three of the seven Weave conduits had ruptured.

**Sources**: Playground generation 2nd part.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-29

---

## Overview

After the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] engineered the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] roughly **9,000â€“10,000 years ago** â€” in the decades immediately preceding the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]], following the hundred-year [[century-of-sorrows|Century of Sorrows]] ramp-up â€” three of the seven [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] were killed and three of the seven Weave conduits ruptured. The Weave could no longer carry complex high-density magical structures.

For approximately one thousand years, no mortal spell above the **Fifth Circle** could be cast in Eadras. This era is called the **Thousand-Year Dimming**, also remembered as:

- The Anti-Magic Crusade (popular name for the era)
- The Broken Weave
- The Fifth-Circle Age
- The Magefall
- The Crusade of Ashen Wards
- The Fifth-Circle Limit (technical name for the cap)

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## The Fifth-Circle Limit

During the Dimming, attempts to cast above the Fifth Circle failed, backlashed, or collapsed into lesser effects. This meant:

- No Sixth, Seventh, Eighth, or Ninth Circle magic
- No grand spell architectures of mortal origin
- No great restoration magic
- No high warding
- No large-scale teleportation
- No grand anti-divine bindings
- No major planar seals
- No great resurrection miracles
- No high ritual architecture
- No empire-scale magical infrastructure

Divine miracles could still occur, but even clerical magic through mortal vessels was affected where it relied on Weave mediation. Direct divine action remained constrained by the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] and Icon risk. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## The Breaking Itself

When the Weave broke during the previous [[grand-conjunction|Grand Conjunction]], physical and cultural scars formed:

- Skies shimmered with torn spell-light
- Ley lines howled
- Magical storms crossed kingdoms
- Wards shattered
- Enchanted cities dimmed
- Spellbooks burned from the inside
- Familiars died or transformed
- Portals collapsed
- Ritual circles inverted
- Magical items cracked or went dormant
- Conduits overloaded

Some places were merely dimmed. Others were annihilated. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

A mythic record of the moment of breaking:

> The first Windspeaker died beneath a bell that would not stop ringing.
> The second burned in a circle drawn by hands that called themselves holy.
> The third was drowned in silence, so no spell could hear her name.
>
> Then three conduits screamed.
> The Weave lurched like a net cut from its poles.
> Spells above the Fifth Circle fell stillborn from mortal hands.
> And for a thousand years, magic remembered the taste of murder.

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Civilization During the Dimming

The Dimming reshaped civilization globally:

- Cities dependent on high magic collapsed
- Floating fortresses fell
- Ley-powered irrigation systems failed
- Magitek networks went dark
- Old wards around demon-tainted regions weakened
- Trade routes changed
- Magical academies lost prestige
- Mage aristocracies fell
- Anti-magic churches gained power

Nonmagical crafts, engineering, military organization, and local priesthoods gained prominence. Civilization had to live beneath a lowered sky for ten centuries. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

### Conduit Cities Destroyed

Cities built around the three broken conduits often died. Examples described in canon:

- A city of floating glass fell into its own harbor
- A mountain academy sealed itself and never reopened
- A desert oasis-state lost the magic that drew water
- A dwarven magitek forge went cold for generations
- An elven song-city became silent wood
- A coastal ward-line failed, letting old Demon War corruption seep out

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

These ruins became modern adventure sites, haunted by broken spell-geometry and anti-Netharim atrocities. See [[conduit-cities]].

### Beneficiaries

While most of the world suffered, certain peoples adapted or thrived:

- **Gnomish magitek** had to become more efficient and less dependent on high-energy spells
- **Dwarven engineering** rose in prestige
- **Orcish caravan networks** became essential because teleportation and high transport magic failed
- **Dragons** became disproportionately valuable as mercenaries â€” their innate elemental power was not capped the same way as mortal Weave spellcasting (formal dragon spellcasting through the Weave was affected, but their breath, body-magic, and ancient Source-born nature were not)
- **Unicorn psionics** gained political importance because psionics is not fully dependent on the Weave

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

### Magical Beasts

Some species dependent on high ambient magic declined. Others adapted. Some dormant Demon War creatures stirred where wards weakened. **Glassharts** and other regulating animals became more important in unstable regions. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Lirra's Response

[[lirra|Lirra]] could not simply remake the Weave instantly. The Weave is vast â€” it interfaces with ley lines, Source-reflected [[eldritch|Eldritch]], mortal minds, spell traditions, conduits, and planar boundaries. Repair required:

- Surviving Lirran priesthoods
- Mortal mages
- [[windspeakers|Netharim]] bloodlines
- Dragon arcane assistance
- Unicorn psionic harmonics
- Gnomish magitek stabilizers
- Elven song-magic
- Dwarven conduit anchors
- Possibly Qoxlian ecological magic in ley-wild regions

The Thousand-Year Dimming was the repair period. Lirra guided, inspired, and empowered, but under divine law and Icon constraints, she did not simply overwrite the Prime's magical architecture by fiat. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

A Lirran lament from that age:

> I wove the world a net against demons,
> and frightened hands cut it to catch their neighbors.
> Now all must learn again the knot.

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Netharim Culture During the Dimming

Netharim culture from this era still echoes today. Possible terms preserved from the Dimming:

- **Ash-Born** â€” descendants of crusade survivors
- **Quiet Towers** â€” prisons where Netharim were suppressed
- **The Brand of Unweaving** â€” mark burned onto accused Netharim
- **Hidden Blood Houses** â€” lineages that concealed Netharim inheritance
- **The Lantern Roads** â€” escape routes used to move Netharim to safe territories
- **Fifth-Circle Orphans** â€” children born during the Dimming with magic no one knew how to train safely

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

Some states protected Netharim. Some temples hid them. Some dragon patrons sheltered them for a price. Some unicorn herds allowed Netharim refuge near the plains because psionic coordination could calm unstable casting. Some orc clans carried Netharim children across borders.

## Recovery and Modern Legacy

The Weave was eventually repaired enough for high magic to return â€” but not to its former strength. Repair created a second architecture over the first. Modern magic bears scars:

- Spells above Fifth Circle are possible again, but harder
- High magic requires more formal safeguards
- Conduit maintenance is a recognized profession
- Netharim are now understood as living Weave factors
- Some regions remain permanently low-magic
- The three broken conduits have never fully recovered
- Wild magic zones persist at crusade atrocity sites

(source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

This is why modern Eadras is not as magically grand as the immediate post-[[demon-war|Demon War]] age. Magic is pervasive but degraded compared to its height. The world remembers high magic the way it remembers lost empires â€” through ruins, forbidden spells, and impossible stories.

## Lirran Reform

After the Dimming, the [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] became more serious about:

- Magical ethics
- Netharim protection
- Anti-panic education
- Weave maintenance
- Spell licensing
- Conduit stewardship
- Forbidden ritual detection

Some became compassionate reformers. Others became rigid magical bureaucrats. A modern Lirran reform vow: *"Never again shall secrecy feed the pyre."* (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)
