# Demon War

**Summary**: The Demon War was a cataclysmic conflict triggered 10,000 years ago when [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] summoned a [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]] to Eadras by true-name, opening a wound to the alien [[demons|Demon Mythos]] and unleashing an invasion of energivorous demons that killed roughly half of all living creatures and forced [[lirra|Lirra]] to weave the Weave as a defensive Ward to keep outsider entities from drinking Eadras's magic dry. **It was not won; it was survived.** It is the **foundational societal/existential trauma of modern Eadras**, paired with the [[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]] as foundational geological/metaphysical trauma. The war's residue is still load-bearing: institutional fear, [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] doctrine, [[thousand-year-dimming|Dimming]] scars, [[woundwatch|Woundwatch]] patrols, rituals for [[unreceived|the Unreceived]], and the modern doctrinal tension between [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|the Unheld Hand]] and [[doctrine-of-open-palm|the Open Palm]].

## Survived, not won

The Demon War is canonically described as **survived, not won**. The phrasing is load-bearing: *victory* implies closure, while *survival* makes the war's residue an inheritance the modern era still carries. Modern Eadras is not the world after the Demon War; it is the world **inside the Demon War's long aftermath**. The institutions, fears, Dimming scars, anti-magic prejudice, [[woundwatch|Woundwatch]] patrols, and rituals for [[unreceived|the Unreceived]] are all the war continuing in different registers.

This framing also matters because *active* demons are not always required. The Demon War remains current weather even when no demon is biting anyone; the doctrine, the geography, the magical system, the divine bureaucracy, and the politics of magic all reflect it.

## Demons are not devils

A doctrinally critical distinction: **demons are not devils.**

- **Devils** are infernal bureaucracy â€” contracts, punishment, extraction, procedure, efficient suffering. They are native to the Eadran Mythos's lower plane ([[nyxar|Nyxar]]) and operate within the cosmological order. See [[devils]].
- **Demons** are *alien-Mythos energivores* from the collapsed Mythos called **Nethar** (also recorded as **Vorath**). They are adaptive appetite, ontological predation, magical consumption, and destabilization. They are not part of the Eadran Mythos's structural cosmology. See [[demons]].

Mortal traditions frequently conflate the two. Mortal religions almost universally do. The conflation is doctrinally dangerous because the responses required are categorically different: devils are negotiable through contract and procedure; demons cannot be negotiated with at all.

## What kind of war it was

The Demon War should be understood as more than a military or planar invasion. It was **ontological warfare** â€” the [[demons|demons]] threatened **magical structure, coherent existence, identity stability, the [[magic-in-eadras|Weave]] itself, and metaphysical continuity**, not merely lives and territory. This distinguishes the Demon War from ordinary historical conflicts: civilizations have survived wars without being changed at the structural level; they did not survive the Demon War without changing what they are.

In the modern philosophical synthesis, two foundational traumas anchor the Eadran present:

- The **[[arathi-cataclysm|Arathi Cataclysm]]** â€” the foundational *geological and metaphysical* trauma. The world itself was broken.
- The **Demon War** â€” the foundational *societal and existential* trauma. The conditions for stable selfhood, civilization, and magical structure were attacked.

Nearly every modern civilization can be interpreted as a response to one or both. (source: Commentary Ingest â€” Demon War, Chaos Crusades, Demons, and Aberrations.txt)

## Cause

The Demon War began as a consequence of [[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] war against all the peoples of Eadras. When [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] was losing â€” with every [[gods-of-eadras|god]] encouraging every race to fight him â€” he made a desperate and catastrophic miscalculation: he summoned a **[[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]]** to Eadras. This rash action opened a portal to the **Demon Mythos** (a Mythos specifically built around hellish planes and demonic conquest) for the first time.

The [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]] had no intention of honoring any agreement with Xar'vion. It simply wanted to consume everything.

## The Demon Prince of the Open Wound

The [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince of the Open Wound]] was not merely the largest demon. It was an **adaptive sovereign appetite** â€” an ancient, massively fed, catastrophically intelligent entity that made demonic adaptation more coherent. It did not simply command demons; it *made hunger strategic*. The demon-host's terrifying ability to invent magic dynamically, mutate under pressure, and destabilise coherent orders by consuming the conditions those orders required was the Demon Prince's coordinating presence amplified across every front.

This framing matters because "demon" reads as plural in mortal speech; the Prince was the unifying intelligence that turned a swarm into a *war*. Without it, the Demon Mythos's hunger would have been catastrophic but disorganised. With it, the hunger learned.

Key ecological contrast: *life eats and returns; demons consume and do not return.* The Prince was the unification of that principle in adaptive form.

## The War

The Demon War lasted **twenty years** after the portal opened, ending with the sealing of the Demon Prince and the closing of the major incursion. Roughly half of all living creatures in Eadras died. Several entire races were completely wiped out. The war's pivotal moment â€” the [[battle-of-the-broken-conclaves|Battle of the Broken Conclaves]], in which the [[orugi|Orug]], [[el-mer|El'mer]], and [[aetherins|Aetherin]] held the Final Wall to the death of their last individuals â€” occurred in **year 15 of the twenty-year war**, and the Tarran reinforcements that had been promised did not arrive.

All peoples â€” [[dragons|dragons]], [[elfen|elves]], [[velrathi|Velrathi]], [[tarans|Tarran]], [[orcs|orcs]], unicorns, sea peoples, and every other race â€” fought alongside each other against the demonic invasion. [[xarvion|Xar'vion]] himself, after five years, finally understood what he had unleashed. He turned to the Elder Gods and joined the alliance against the demons. **His knowledge of breach-patterns saved fronts** that would otherwise have collapsed; the war's later survival depended materially on the perpetrator's later usefulness.

With Xar'vion's divine power added to the defenders, the [[demon-prince-of-the-open-wound|Demon Prince]] was forced back through the portal. The moment the portal was sealed, the Elder Gods betrayed Xar'vion and imprisoned his essence in his own Icon. The imprisonment was **lawful under emergency divine compact, but not clean** â€” it was partly necessity (Xar'vion remained dangerous), partly mercy (preventing further harm), partly convenience (avoiding the political mess of trial and judgment), and partly fear of what an unimprisoned Xar'vion might become as banner, martyr, redeemer, or second wound. The mortal record sanitised him into pure monstrosity. The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] grew in the space that lie created.

### Xar'vion under tiered disclosure

Modern divine policy on Xar'vion uses **tiered disclosure** â€” the canonical framework for handling figures whose full historical truth cannot be safely public:

- **First Tier â€” Public Doctrine**: Xar'vion unleashed catastrophe, later aided the war's end, and remains imprisoned because he is dangerous.
- **Second Tier â€” Vowed Scholarship**: trusted historians, [[katharim|death-priests]], war colleges, and divine auditors may learn that his imprisonment involved divine betrayal and emergency necessity beyond clean justice.
- **Third Tier â€” Sealed Judgment**: full [[icons|Icon]] details, binding mechanisms, current [[black-hand|Black Hand]] objectives, and the alien-artifact framing of the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]] remain restricted to the highest [[order-sharanel|Order Sharanel]] / [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] tier.

The framework is itself a [[baldaranic-council-cycle|Baldaranic Council Cycle]] output â€” the [[doctrine-of-open-palm|Open Palm]] reform's anti-secrecy pressure was not allowed to override the cosmologically-necessary partial-concealment of Xar'vion canon. Both doctrines coexist around the same case.

## Consequences

### Mortality
Half of all living creatures died. Multiple races were entirely wiped out.

### Magic: The Weave
During the war, demons were siphoning magic from Eadras. In response, the Goddess of Magic **Lirra** created the **Weave** â€” a web of magic woven from seven strands, each a different aspect of magic, which restricted the ability of outsiders to draw on Eadras's magical energy. The Weave also gave rise to a new type of magic user: **[[magic-in-eadras|Netharim]]**, who can natively use the Weave by composing strands into flows.

### Beast-Folk
The [[beast-folk|Beast-folk]] â€” anthropomorphic mammals, birds, and amphibians â€” emerged from the war as the **Laughing Third** of the [[rathari|Rathar]] (see [[three-thirds-of-the-rathar]]). When perfect-memory Rathar survivors knelt and begged for relief, **[[sylph|Sylph]] answered as the primary deity of the transmutation**, settling the **Veil of Forgetting** over them; **[[qoxli|Qoxli]] assisted**, using her wild magic to protect the petitioners' biological coherence under the demonic ecological pressure of the war zones. The first Beast-Folk communities arose in war-scarred refuge zones and around the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]] where the first great breach opened. See [[beast-folk#Origin]]. (User direction 2026-05-06; sources: Playground generation 2nd part.txt, Commentary Ingest â€” Demon War, Chaos Crusades, Demons, and Aberrations.txt)

### [[aramet|Aramet's]] Ascension
The Elfen mage Aramet ascended to godhood during the Demon War while scouting a secondary gate.

### The Netharim Secret
Lirra wove the Weave **as a defensive Ward** â€” not as the universal source or machinery of all magic, but as the protective overlay that carries, circulates, shields, filters, and contains the majority of [[eldritch|Eldritch]] in Eadras so outsider entities cannot simply drink the world open. Every living magic user's life sustains the Ward's coherence; **[[windspeakers|Windspeakers]]** (Netharim of exceptional innate capacity) anchor the conduit junctions specifically. This truth is kept secret from the public â€” see [[anti-magic-crusade#The Hidden Truth|The Hidden Truth]] for the load-bearing canon.

### The Unreceived

Many Demon War casualties were **consumed wholly by demons â€” body, spirit, and soul**. These victims are not dead in the ordinary sense; demonic consumption is **interruption of death itself**. Ordinary souls pass through afterlife, accounting, rest, judgment, recalibration, and eventual return to the cycle. The Unreceived were *stolen from the known cycle*. They do not enter judgment. They do not return.

[[unreceived|The Unreceived]] are a distinct cosmological category â€” neither dead nor living, neither saved nor damned. The [[katharim|Katharim]] and death-priests maintain ledgers; war-temples maintain the **Black Banner Roll**; households maintain the **Empty Cot Vigil** in the seven days after a confirmed Unreceived loss; [[beast-folk|Beast-Folk]] communities maintain **Laugh With No Echo** rites; cities maintain **Ashless Shrines** for memorials without remains. See [[unreceived]] for the full treatment.

The ritual phrase that anchors the entire category: ***May theft be remembered.***

Canonical constraint on every common form: **the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] is not commonly known to be associated with Xar'vion**; cult-of-the-Still-King rumors exist, but the connection to the modern criminal-political organization is held by the Inner Hand and the deepest scholarship only. ## Long-Term Impact

The Demon War fundamentally reshaped Eadras society:

- **Political histories** use "After the Demon War" as their primary calendar epoch, until the formal adoption of [[first-rebrightening|FR dating]] within the [[order-sharanel|Sharanel]] / [[tome-of-baldaran|Tome]] scholarly register.
- The war gave rise to the Netharim tradition and its eventual cultural tensions.
- The subsequent [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] â€” Black-Hand-engineered as a [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy|Spear]]-search diversion â€” caused the **[[thousand-year-dimming|Thousand-Year Dimming]]** by mass-killing magic users whose lives sustained the Weave, and specifically by killing three [[windspeakers|Windspeakers]] whose loss structurally damaged three conduit junctions. *The Crusade came first. The Dimming was the wound it left.*
- Xar'vion's use of grief-magic as a weapon of domination permanently stigmatized [[necromancy-and-undeath|necromancy]] in many cultures.
- [[unreceived|The Unreceived]] became a permanent theological category. The Demon War Memory Crisis (~25-35 FR) emerges as living memory of the war fades and the Unreceived theology must be reconstructed for descendants who never met a survivor.
- The proposed [[woundwatch|Woundwatch]] institution and *The Wound That Must Not Be Fed* doctrine emerge during the [[first-rebrightening|First Rebrightening]] as institutional responses to lingering demonic boundary risk in the [[demonfall-marches|Demonfall Marches]].
- The [[black-hand|Black Hand]] spy network, originally built to acquire the [[spear-of-crystallized-entropy]], survived the war and continues under Xar'vion's secret direction from within his Icon.
