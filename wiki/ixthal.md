# Ixthal

**Summary**: Ixthal — Arathi mortal name **Ašar-eṭir** ("Order-Opener") — is the first Ascended Mortal Deity of Eadras, the First and Last Arathi. He is the sole surviving Arathi who chose to preserve future possibility rather than spend himself futilely in the cataclysm, and who secretly created dragons and unicorns in violation of divine law. **Ixthal** is his post-ascension divine name (and the form by which he is most widely known); **Ašar-eṭir** is his canonical Arathi mortal name (User direction 2026-05-01).

## Overview

Ixthal was one of three Arathi who ascended to godhood in the final age of Arath. While his fellows [[three-last-lights|Auralis and Thamior]] spent their divine essence trying and failing to save their people, Ixthal possessed **probabilistic foresight** — the ability to read the shape of approaching consequences from existing truths — and knew the Arathi could not be saved. He chose to survive instead, becoming the last mind of a dead civilization.

He is remembered by many names:
- **Ašar-eṭir** — his original Arathi mortal name ("Order-Opener" — fitting both for his role in opening the way to dragon and unicorn creation, and for his persistence after the Cataclysm shattered the established order)
- **Ixthal** — his post-ascension divine name; the form by which he is most widely known
- **The Last Arathi**
- **The God Who Remained**
- **The Ash-Reader**
- **Father of Dragons' Dawn**
- **Caller of the First Herd**
- **The Unforgiven Preserver**
- **Source-Kindler**
- **He Who Chose the Afterward**

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## The Foresight

Ixthal's gift was not true time-sight, which would violate [[omniversal-laws|Omniversal Law]]. He possessed **pattern-perception** — the ability to read how present facts lean toward future outcomes. Truth is indivisible and interlocking; a sufficiently powerful mind can read tomorrow's shadow in today's flame.

He saw the [[arathi-cataclysm|Falling Star]] was an avalanche already loosed once Kudur-eṭir's ritual crossed a threshold. No mortal evacuation, no shield, no prayer, no city-vault, no ascended intervention by his peers could preserve the Arathi people. The catastrophe was planetary, crustal, atmospheric, oceanic, magical, and entropic simultaneously.

He chose not to expend himself in vain. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## The Two Million Years of Solitude

After the [[arathi-cataclysm|Extinction-Level Event]], Ixthal existed alone for **approximately two million years** as the last surviving mind of a dead civilization. Life required millions of years to recover; during the long ecological reset he witnessed a world emptied of mortal sapience. He found this intolerable — Evye and Fryr had become the world so life could flourish within it, not merely so gods could watch animals graze beneath ruins.

Across that span, Ixthal **repeatedly petitioned the [[gods-of-eadras|Elder Gods]] to intervene** in mortal affairs and restore sapient life to Eadras. Each petition was refused. The [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]] precluded direct creation; the Elder Gods would not break it.

After the petitions were exhausted, Ixthal chose to **act independently**. His goal was not power, not control, not vengeance, and not the restoration of Arath specifically — it was simply the **restoration of the presence of intelligent mortal life** to the world. (source: Addition — Ixthal motivation.txt)

## The Forbidden Kindling

When ecosystems had finally recovered enough to support apex magical life, Ixthal committed the **Forbidden Kindling**: he secretly violated the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Edict of Closed Genesis]] by amplifying natural [[source-surges|Source surges]] into **X-class Source Flares**.

He **did not create life directly**. He amplified and shaped natural [[source-surges|Sourceflare]] events to produce specific outcomes — performed in a manner indistinguishable from natural phenomena. The species that resulted ([[dragons]] and [[unicorns]]) are therefore widely believed by both gods and mortals to have emerged through ordinary planetary processes. The intervention is a **violation of cosmic constraints carried out through concealment**, not through direct defiance of the Edict. (source: Refinement — Ixthal Sourceflare influence.txt)

### The Dragon Flare

Ixthal detected an incoming natural Source surge through his pattern-sight. At the critical moment, he amplified it — not by creating an artificial flare, but by widening what was already opening. The X-class flare was focused through nine carefully selected giant reptile eggs, transforming them into the first nine dragon progenitors: one for each of the nine color clans.

These nine progenitors — the **[[nine-embers-of-renewal|Nine Embers of Renewal]]** — went on to **all ascend to godhood** across the deep dragon era, becoming the first generation of dragon gods. Three are publicly named in the modern pantheon (Cindirith, Davian, Koruss); the other six remain in draconic esoteric tradition only. (User direction 2026-05-06; source: notes on various topics 1.txt.)

The public myth, remembered as the **First Great Flare**, is not wholly false. Dragons truly were born during a natural Source event. The lie is that the event remained natural. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### The Unicorn Flare

Two million years after the dragon flare — approximately **6 million years before present**, four million years after the Cataclysm — Ixthal observed that dragonkind was dangerously unbalanced (powerful, solitary, competitive) and caused a second amplified surge across a herd of plains-dwelling proto-equines. A diffuse, harmonic flare awakened collective resonance in creatures already bound by social trust. The result was the [[unicorns|unicorns]], the second sapient race. The geological setting was the [[eldravath|Eldravath]] supercontinent — its formation forced both species into one shared landmass.

This second event, the **Silver Plains Flare**, may have been what finally prompted divine suspicion, as the pattern matched the first too precisely. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt; map explanation.txt)

### The Cover

The concealment worked because:
- He amplified real Source surges rather than fabricating flares, leaving no external divine signature
- His regulation worked within the surge's own parameters
- The post-ELE world was still magically unstable, making extraordinary life developments plausible
- Divine noninterference discouraged overly intrusive investigation by the Elder Gods
- Ixthal withdrew and never taught, repeated openly, or explained himself

The Elder Gods currently believe dragons and unicorns arose from rare but natural X-class Source events. **Ixthal has not been caught.** (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### The seals Ixthal placed

Active concealment is not only silence. Ixthal placed his own divine seals on the artifacts and ancestral memories that could in principle expose the Forbidden Kindling — and because Ixthal *is* a god, those seals are genuinely divine work. What is concealed is **which divine being** placed them.

The seals fall into three principal categories:

- **The [[ratharil#The Sealed Hall of First Flame|Shard of First Shell]] in Ratharil's Sealed Hall.** A divine seal prevents the magical signature of the first Source Flare from being decompiled into a reproducible method. Dragon institutional tradition attributes this seal to [[lirra|Lirra]]. Lirra may indeed have placed a routine high-Source-resonance precautionary seal — that part is hers. The targeted seal *against the method* is Ixthal's. The dragons read both as one seal; Ixthal allows that reading.
- **Divine seals around unicorn origin-memory.** Unicorn psionics preserve a herd-field memory of their awakening — the closest extant evidence of the Forbidden Kindling. Divine seals exist around that memory layer, preventing a sufficiently deep psionic descent from reconstructing the method. These too are Ixthal's. They are not in place to oppress unicorns (the standing dragon-unicorn reading); they are in place because the herd-field is the most accessible reproducible record of how he did it.
- **The "empty spaces by order of the gods" in the Sealed Hall records.** The Sealed Hall's deepest tablets and crystals carry redacted regions — names removed, lines erased, sections blanked. The dragon archivist tradition records these as *"by order of the gods."* That phrasing is literally accurate: a god ordered the redactions. The phrasing is also imprecise: it implies a divine council acted in concert, when in fact a single god acted alone and let the dragons assume the rest.

The pattern is consistent across all three categories: **the seals are divine because Ixthal is divine, but the divine intent behind them is one god's strategic concealment, not a pantheon consensus**. The mortal and dragon tradition that attributes the work to "the gods" plural is not wrong, only undiagnosed. (sources: ForbiddenKindlingAndUnheldHand_01_InWorld_Facts.txt; DragonsAndUnicorns_01_InWorld_Facts.txt; user direction 2026-05-13)

## Divine Silence

Ixthal does not communicate with mortals through speech. He barely communicates with other deities. He does not attend divine councils unless the matter touches extinction, Source-level renewal, or the possible repetition of Arath's catastrophe. When he does speak, even gods listen, because he has conserved speech for ages.

He is **fully capable of speech**. The silence is not a divine condition imposed upon him, not a wound from the Cataclysm that took his voice, and not a religious vow. It is a **deliberate strategic choice**, maintained because extended verbal communication increases the risk of revealing the causal relationships that would expose the Forbidden Kindling. Words make connections explicit. Connections, once explicit, can be inferred. (source: Refinement — Ixthal silence.txt)

He maintains the *appearance* of being unable or unwilling to speak — letting most observers conclude one or the other — to keep even the question of his silence from becoming interesting. A god who chooses not to speak invites scrutiny of the choice. A god who *cannot* speak does not.

His silence has multiple compounding causes:
1. **Strategic concealment of the Forbidden Kindling** — the load-bearing reason; speech risks revealing causal relationships that could expose his actions
2. **PTSD from surviving Arath's extinction** — he is the last mind of a dead civilization
3. **Survivor guilt** — knowing Auralis and Thamior spent themselves in vain
4. **Moral injury** — enduring ages when the Prime Material lacked mortal sapient races
5. **Recognition that confession could destabilize** dragons, unicorns, divine law, and mortal faith

Crucially, Ixthal's silence is **self-imposed** — there is no divine gag upon him. He is his own prison. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## How Ixthal Communicates

When Ixthal does communicate — with mortals, with the rare petitioner he chooses to acknowledge, with another god in moments of crisis — he does so through **direct conceptual transmission**, not spoken language.

Conceptual transmission conveys **meaning, intent, and emotional context** without the use of words. The recipient understands him without requiring verbal explanation. A mortal touched by such a transmission may report later that they "knew what he meant" but cannot reconstruct any specific phrasing — because there was no phrasing. The understanding arrived as itself.

The method has **structural limits**:

- It conveys meaning, intent, and emotional context well
- It is **poor at complex sequences and detailed causal relationships** — exactly the content that would risk exposure
- It is therefore **suited to guidance, not to instruction** — a recipient may receive the *what* and the *why this matters*, but rarely the detailed *how*

The choice of method is itself part of the concealment. By avoiding explicit verbal statements, Ixthal naturally avoids articulating the kind of complex causal claim that could reveal the Kindling. Even in moments where he chooses to communicate, the medium itself filters out the dangerous content. (source: Addition — Ixthal communication method.txt)

## His Moral Position

Ixthal **does not regret his actions**. He knows the acts violated divine law. He considers them justified despite that violation, and would not undo them if the choice were offered again. (source: Addition — Ixthal motivation.txt)

His private conviction:

*"The law forbade interference. But the law also permitted a world where only gods remembered speech."*

He sees dragons and unicorns not as his inventions or property, but as Eadras' own life hastened across the threshold into sapience. If confronted, he would not repent their existence. His defining possible statement:

**"There were no mortals. I changed that."**

His philosophical objection to the [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand|Doctrine of the Unheld Hand]]: before mortals exist, whose destiny is being protected? Noninterference can become divine abandonment of the very possibility of mortal history.

The Elder Gods would reject this argument, because creating races determines destiny at the grandest scale. Ixthal holds the distinction anyway. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Divine Magic

Ixthal's divine power is rare and manifests through circumstances resembling his portfolio rather than direct prayer:
- Extinction events and last chances
- Renewal of life after catastrophe
- Preservation of future possibility
- The unbearable calculation of what must be preserved when all cannot be saved

A priest begging Ixthal for wealth receives nothing. A lone healer protecting the last clutch of an endangered species during a wildfire may find their hands filled with pale Source-light. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Worship

Ixthal's clergy, where they exist, are not prophets. They are **keepers of aftermaths** — they serve disaster survivors, war refugees, extinct languages, last members of bloodlines, orphaned children, and those who must rebuild without closure. Their prayers are not requests for intervention; they are acts of witness.

Major sects:
- **The Coal Keepers** — hold that Ixthal's choice was the highest sacrifice: not death, but the sacrifice of being misunderstood forever so life could have a future
- **The Ashen Accusers** — believe Ixthal should have died with his people; do not deny dragons came through him but argue beauty born later does not absolve abandonment

His worship is strongest among disaster survivors, dragon historians, ecologists, druids, orphans, and those who must make triage decisions. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Relationship to Dragons and Unicorns

Dragons remember him (in esoteric traditions) as the **Kindler** — not as a creator in the parental sense, but as the brightness before dragonkind. Most dragons do not know Ixthal created them.

In Ratharil, the **Court of the Unanswered Sun** silently honors the mystery of draconic origin. To most dragons, it honors the unknown force behind the First Great Flare. To a very few initiates, the obscure older inscription reads: *A silent god stood somewhere in the dawn.* (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

Unicorns know Ixthal only as the **Silent Brightness** or **Pain Behind the Sun** — not as a named god. Their ancestral memory contains the Silver Plains awakening as sensation: light, fear, unity, running, the birth of the herd-mind. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

Ixthal does not need worship from either race. His greatest satisfaction is that they exist independently of him. If the truth emerged and someone said "Ixthal made you," his likely stance would be: **"And they belong to themselves."**

## The Three Last Lights

The three ascended Arathi are collectively remembered as the [[three-last-lights|Three Last Lights]]:
- **Auralis of the Thousand Shelters** — spent herself on shelter; failed
- **Thamior the Upholder** — spent himself on endurance; failed
- **Ixthal the Last Arathi** — carried the coal; endured

*"One opened the doors. One held the stones. One carried the coal. The doors broke. The stones fell. The coal endured."*
