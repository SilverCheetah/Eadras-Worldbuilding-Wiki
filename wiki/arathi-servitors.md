# Arathi Servitors

**Summary**: The Arathi Servitors were dozens of engineered servant lineages built by the [[arathi|Arathi]] for specialized labor across the planet-spanning civilization — from deep mining to fine craftsmanship. **Only one lineage survived the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]: the [[trolls|Trolls]].** All other lineages were organic or semi-organic and died out completely when the world broke.

**Sources**: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt; Trolls.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-05

---

## The lineage system

The [[arathi|Arathi]] engineered **dozens of distinct servitor lineages** during their tenure as Eadras's first civilization. Each lineage was purpose-built for a class of labor and tuned to its function — body plan, biology, sensory apparatus, and (in the magical lineages) integrated [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]] all selected for the work the lineage was meant to do.

This was not a uniform slave caste under a different name. The lineages were specialized peoples in the same way the [[arathi|Arathi]] used specialized [[crystalmancy|crystal lineages]] for different functional roles: a system of differentiated, designed populations, each excellent at one band of work.

Two lineages are now named in canon. The rest are remembered in aggregate.

## Trolls — the only survivors

**[[trolls|Trolls]]** were the **hardy general-purpose constructs for deep mining, processing, vein detection, and excavation**. Unlike most other Servitor lineages, trolls are **living mineral organisms** — bodies composed of ores and gemstones, sapient, self-repairing, and self-replicating through budding.

This material composition is the reason trolls survived the Cataclysm. The few that endured did so by being already in deep hibernation when the [[worldender|Worldbreaker]] fell. Active trolls were shattered alongside the rest of Arathi civilization. The hibernating elders inherited the entire Servitor legacy by default.

For everything about troll biology, hibernation cycles, the Great Balance religion, and the dwarven-conflict aftermath, see [[trolls]].

## Household Servitors — extinct

**Household Servitors** were the most common Servitor lineage in Arathi daily life. They were engineered from **insectoid stock** — the design choice was elegance, dexterity, and unobtrusive integration into Arathi domestic spaces. Canonical features:

- **Mantis-like limbs** — long, articulated, capable of fine precision work
- **Beetle carapaces** — protective, self-cleaning, and ornamentally treated
- **Iridescent wings** — both functional (light flight, room-traversal) and aesthetic, matching the iridescent scale-and-crest aesthetic of the Arathi themselves

Their assignments covered:

- **Domestic duties** — cleaning, food preparation, household management
- **Personal assistance** — attendance on individual Arathi, especially scholars and ranking citizens
- **Laboratory work** — assisting in [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]] and [[magitek|magitek]] research
- **Fine craftsmanship** — precision work too delicate for unaided hands

Household Servitors were ubiquitous in Arathi cities. They are the lineage most often imagined when Eadras scholars try to visualize daily Arathi life.

**They are extinct.** Like every other organic and semi-organic Servitor lineage, they died with the [[arathi-cataclysm|Cataclysm]]. None survive in any preserved form, though their general body plan may have inspired downstream golem-craft (see [[golems]]).

## The other lineages — extinct

Beyond Trolls and Household Servitors, **dozens** of other lineages existed. They are not individually named in current canon. What is known about them as a group:

- **Almost all were organic or semi-organic.** This is the load-bearing detail. Living tissue did not survive the [[arathi-cataclysm|White Deluge]], the planet-wide firestorms, the year-plus oven-temperature ejecta re-entry, the magical deregulation, or the climatic displacement. Where troll mineral bodies in hibernation could endure, organic Servitor bodies could not.
- **They covered a wide range of specialized functions** — agriculture, transit operation, [[crystalmancy|crystalmancy]] support, military duties, ceremonial roles, and more. The exact distribution is undefined.
- **They died out completely during the Cataclysm.** No documented survivors, no modern descendant peoples claiming Servitor lineage. Like everything else of Arathi daily life, they are gone.

## Relationship to engineered races

Servitors are part of a broader Arathi engineering tradition that included:

- **Living mineral constructs** — Trolls (sapient) and proto-golems (see [[golems]])
- **Organic engineered lineages** — the bulk of the Servitor population (extinct)
- **Crystal-mind constructs** — preserved through gnome golem-craft and the modern descendants thereof (see [[golems]])

The post-Cataclysm engineered races created by the [[the-three|Three]] and their progeny — the [[orugi]], [[grugi]], [[el-mer]], [[aetherins]], [[sea-tua|Sea'tua]], [[rathari]], and [[mitan]] — are a **separate lineage**, created by the Three's progenitors after the Cataclysm. They share the conceptual frame of *engineered descent* with Arathi Servitors but are not Servitors and have no Arathi ancestry.

## Open canon

- **Names of any other Servitor lineages.** Currently only Trolls and Household Servitors are named.
- **Whether any organic Servitor lineage produced descendants** that survive in modern Eadras under another name. Current canon is firmly *no*, but the question is large enough to be worth flagging.
- **Specific design lineage relationships** — whether all Servitor lineages were independently engineered or whether some descended from earlier Servitor stock.
- **Population distributions and total Servitor numbers** at the height of Arathi civilization.
- **Servitor culture, agency, and sapience levels.** Trolls are clearly sapient; Household Servitors' degree of self-determination is undefined.
