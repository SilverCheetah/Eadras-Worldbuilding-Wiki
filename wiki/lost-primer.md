# The Lost Primer

**Summary**: A foundational [[arathi|Arathi]]-era teaching text on [[crystalmancy]] and [[magitek]], recovered by a joint [[velrathi|Velrathi]]-[[rathari|Rathari]] expedition that breached one of the [[deep-vaults|Arathi Deep Vaults]] in the early [[firethorn-empire|High Firethorn Era]]. Its low- and mid-level instruction was enough to seed Velrathi and Rathari magitek/crystalmancy traditions across the Empire's golden age. The vault was overrun and emptied by demons during the [[demon-war|Demon War]]; nothing of the Primer survived. **The [[grugi|Grug]] who attended the original expedition kept meticulous notes, which they passed to their two child races — the [[gnomes]] and [[goblins]] — and that inherited memory is the canonical reason gnomes have made a racial obsession of finding more vaults.**

## The discovery

Early in the [[firethorn-empire|High Firethorn Era]], a **joint Velrathi-Rathari expedition** breached one of the sealed Arathi [[deep-vaults|Deep Vaults]]. The exact location is not recorded in any surviving Velrathi or Rathari archive, and the vault itself has not been re-located in modern times. (The vault was overrun in the Demon War; the survivors who knew its location did not survive the war either.)

Inside the vault, the expedition found something far more valuable than artifacts or weapons. They found a **primer**: a foundational Arathi instructional text laying out the basic principles, philosophy, and low-to-mid level techniques of Arathi arcane engineering. It was, in effect, a teaching manual — the kind of thing the Arathi might have used to school an apprentice, not the kind of thing they would have written for an outsider.

The Primer did **not** contain master-level material. Living-crystal songs, the true conduit-regulation methods, the highest-tier living-crystalline workings — none of that was present. What was there was sufficient to start, not sufficient to finish.

It was enough.

## What it enabled

The [[velrathi|Velrathi]] used the Primer as a springboard. Working from its low- and mid-level material, they reverse-engineered, refined, and built upward, recovering working — though shallower — versions of [[crystalmancy]] and [[magitek]] across the centuries that followed.

This is the **canonical explanation** for how the Velrathi and the [[rathari|Rathari]] possessed advanced crystalmancy and magitek throughout the High Firethorn Era despite ten million years separating them from the Arathi. They were not reinventing the wheel from nothing. They were extrapolating outward from a child's instruction manual recovered from a buried library, and they were good enough at it to pass for masters within their own millennia.

The same body of recovered theory underwrote much of the early Empire's grand magical engineering and architectural sophistication, including:

- The integration of spell-foci into [[velrathi-architecture|Velrathi architecture]] (the entire-building-as-spell-lattice principle)
- Early imperial [[tarans|Tarran]] crystalcraft, especially the spell-crystal minaret towers that would later define [[taran-architecture|imperial Tarran architecture]]
- The Velrathi technical vocabulary that the [[katharim|Katharim]] still use in their archive-priest curriculum

What the Empire could not derive from the Primer remained genuinely lost. Modern crystalmancy reaches further than the High Firethorn version did in some directions — particularly thanks to subsequent gnomish vault-archaeology — but no modern practitioner has ever matched what the Arathi themselves were capable of.

## The Demon War destruction

The vault's location was overrun **early** in the [[demon-war|Demon War]] — apparently in the war's first wave, before any organized salvage attempt could be mounted. When scholars finally returned to the site after the war's end, the vault was completely empty. Every scroll, every crystal tablet, every schematic, every annotated manual: gone.

The damage pattern indicated **demonic consumption** rather than ordinary plunder. Demons feed on structured magical content. The vault's library had been Arathi crystallographic notation at its densest — a particularly rich meal. Nothing was left.

The vault has stood empty since. It is still discoverable in principle — its location is not lost, only forgotten — but no living scholar knows where it is, and the [[katharim|Katharim]] who would have been positioned to remember it inherited the bitterness rather than the coordinates.

## The Grug records

There is one detail the original Velrathi and Rathari overlooked, and it has shaped a great deal of subsequent history.

The expedition included a contingent of [[grugi|Grug]] — the Velrathi's engineered servant-race of small, clever, mechanically adept maintainers. The Grug were present in their usual capacity: handling the practical work of the dig, tending the magical instruments, and keeping the operation running. They were not invited to participate in the scholarly study of the Primer.

They took notes anyway.

Grug habits of meticulous, complete record-keeping were a cultural inheritance from their function as Velrathi imperial-system maintainers, and they applied those habits to anything they were exposed to. While the Velrathi and Rathari mages worked through the Primer, the Grug attendants quietly transcribed everything they could see: diagrams, glyph sequences, the readings shouted out from the cataloguing tables, the offhand exclamations of the Velrathi scholars when something especially delighted them.

These Grug notes were preserved within Grug culture and passed forward as part of their working memory. After the Demon War shattered the Grug, their two child races — the [[gnomes]] and the [[goblins]] — inherited the notes as part of their cultural baggage.

**The Velrathi and Rathari never thought to ask whether the Grug had taken notes.** It did not occur to them that the Grug would have been studying alongside them. The notes were preserved, partially translated, partially garbled, and partially augmented by gnome and goblin commentary across the millennia, but at no point did anyone in mainstream Velrathi-Rathari scholarship acknowledge that the Grug record existed.

This is one of the great quiet ironies of post-war scholarship. The civilization that lost everything had handed it down to the servant race they did not think to ask. The [[katharim|Katharim]] still speak of the Lost Primer with quiet bitterness — *"the knowledge we were allowed to taste, but never permitted to keep"* — and they have never, to date, asked the gnomes or goblins what those people remember.

## The gnome obsession explained

The canonical reason **gnomes have made a racial obsession of finding and opening more Arathi Deep Vaults** is the inherited Grug record of the Lost Primer expedition. (See [[gnomes]].)

The obsession is driven by two coupled forces:

1. **Ancestral trauma.** The Grug attended the moment when their masters held the keys to a lost civilization in their hands and let it slip away. The notes preserve the awe; they also preserve the horror of the eventual loss. Gnomish cultural memory of the Primer is not "look what we once had," it is "look what we held and could not protect."
2. **A near-religious desire to restore what was lost.** Gnome golem-craft, the food-fabricator-derived cuisine, the post-vault Arathi-Babylonian naming style, the catalog-crystal-and-teleportation-box logistics empire — every trade gnomes excel at modernly is, at root, an attempt to put pieces of the Arathi back into the world. Each new vault opened is another shot at recovering what the Lost Primer almost gave them.

This reframes the gnomish vault-obsession from "natural inventor curiosity" to **inherited cultural mission**. Gnomes are not merely interested in Arathi technology because it is interesting. They are trying to finish a job their progenitor-line attended at and watched fail.

[[goblins|Goblins]] inherited the same notes, but processed them differently — into the goblin tradition of opportunistic salvage, the cross-city information network, and the cultural conviction that any cache anywhere may eventually be theirs to crack. The goblin reading of the Primer story is closer to *"never let the next find slip"* than the gnomish *"restore the original."*

## Status of the Primer's content today

Modern crystalmancy and magitek descend from three streams of recovered knowledge:

1. **High Firethorn Velrathi/Rathari extrapolations from the Lost Primer.** Most surviving Empire-era texts on these subjects derive ultimately from this stream. Many of them remain in [[katharim|Katharim]] necropolis-archives at [[kharathen]] and in scattered Velrathi exile-archives in [[veyr-keth|Veyr'Keth]] (Glimmerdark Gaia).
2. **The Grug-derived gnome and goblin notes.** Far more partial and garbled than the Velrathi extrapolations, but **older** and closer to the original Arathi presentation in places. Sufficiently respected gnome workshops keep transcribed copies as foundational reference material.
3. **Subsequent direct vault-recovery.** [[inki|Inki's]] First Vault opening (the food fabricator), [[unicorns#Darkhoof|Darkhoof's]] vault (the construct workshop and Nerathi crystalmind), and the other gnome openings have each yielded fresh, primary Arathi material — none of it the Lost Primer itself, but pieces that fit alongside its inherited fragments.

Cross-referencing the three streams is the kind of work serious gnome-Katharim collaborations might one day undertake. They so far have not. (See [[katharim]].)

## Open canon

- **The location of the Lost Primer vault.** Lost; recoverable in principle.
- **Whether any complete Grug copy of the original notes still exists**, or whether the surviving record is purely derivative gnome and goblin transcriptions.
- **Whether the [[katharim|Katharim]] would accept gnomish-held notes if formally offered.** The bitterness is documented; the cultural willingness to bridge it is not.
- **Whether the Primer's master-level material existed at all in any vault.** The High Firethorn assumption was that the Primer was a teaching manual and that more advanced texts existed elsewhere. The assumption has never been verified.
- **Whether other Velrathi-Rathari joint expeditions opened other vaults** during the High Firethorn — and whether other Grug record sets persist somewhere unexamined.
