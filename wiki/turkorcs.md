# Turkorcs

**Summary**: The Turkorcs are wholly individualistic, powerful warriors and casters of [[varkhul|Varkhul]] — amoral, extremely dangerous, and contemptuous of honor. **Manufactured by demons during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] as shocktroopers and exiled to Varkhul after it**, they are one of the principal reasons the [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] exists, and they are strictly contained to the prison continent.

**Sources**: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt; varkhul_society_and_native_elves_ingest.txt (2026-06-02, demon-manufacture origin and the Game of Status)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-02

## Origin

Turkorcs are **demon-manufactured**: a shocktrooper race built by demons during the [[demon-war|Demon War]] to bulk out the forces arrayed against the mortal world. They were engineered for individual combat power and broad magical aptitude, and deliberately made *without* the social bonds — honor, loyalty, obligation — that might have made them unreliable instruments or rivals to their makers. When the war ended, the surviving Turkorcs were a manufactured warrior population with no place in the peace; rather than attempt their extermination, the victorious powers exiled them to [[varkhul|Varkhul]], and the [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] has held them there since.

This origin explains the traits that define them. The amorality, the honor-contempt, and the wholly individualistic disposition are not a fallen culture but the **designed character of a weaponized race** — the residue of the Demon War made flesh. It also settles the long-open question of their relationship to the [[orugi|Orug]] / [[orcs|Orc]] line: Turkorcs are **not** Orug-descended despite the surface resemblance; the shared "-orc" is convergence, not kinship.

## Nature

Turkorcs are individualistic to a degree that other Eadras peoples find unsettling. There is no inherent collective duty in Turkorc culture, no clan-loyalty above self-loyalty, no expectation of obligation among them that survives any moment of self-interest. Where collective behavior exists, it is transactional — assembled for a purpose, dissolved when the purpose ends.

They are **powerful warriors and casters**. Magical aptitude is not exceptional among Turkorcs — it is widespread, and combined with their warrior tradition produces individuals capable of taking on most of what an unprepared world would send against them.

## Honor

Turkorcs **have no concept of honor and consider honor stupid**. This is not a learned cynicism or a fallen state — they regard the entire framework of honor as wasted thought. Promises, codes, oaths, codes of war, debts owed: in Turkorc reasoning these are levers used by the weak to constrain the strong, and the strong have no reason to pick them up.

This is the trait that most distinguishes Turkorcs from [[orcs|Orcs]] — the honor-bound warriors descended from [[orugi|Orug]] bloodlines. Where Orcs are legendarily honor-driven, Turkorcs are legendarily honor-rejecting. The two are not kin: Turkorcs are demon-manufactured (see [[#Origin]]), not an Orug lineage, and their honor-rejection is a designed trait rather than a rejected inheritance.

## Amorality and danger

Turkorcs are **amoral and extremely dangerous**. They are not malicious in the ideological sense — they have no doctrine of cruelty, no pursuit of evil for its own sake. They simply do not weigh moral consequence as a factor in decisions. Whatever serves them is done; whatever does not is not.

The combination of individual power, magical proficiency, and amoral decision-making is what makes them genuinely dangerous to civilized peoples. A single Turkorc loose in a populated region is a serious problem; a band of them is a catastrophe.

## Strict containment

Turkorcs are **strictly contained on [[varkhul|Varkhul]]**. The [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] exists in significant part to prevent them from reaching the wider world. Their containment is not negotiated, not phased, and not seen as cruel by the world's nations — it is treated as the only stable option.

The few historical breach incidents (see [[continental-ward#Open canon]]) provide the empirical basis for this stance.

## Relationship to other Varkhul inhabitants

Turkorcs share Varkhul with the native [[varkhul-native-elves|elf tribes]], assorted **exiled clans** (notably the [[dwarves|dwarf]] clans), and the mercenary economy that connects them. Relations are not friendly. Turkorcs do not form coalitions easily, and treat exiles as resources or obstacles depending on the day. The internal political ecology of Varkhul, to the extent there is one, includes Turkorcs as a perpetually destabilizing element.

## The Game of Status

Turkorcs play Varkhul's [[varkhul#Society — the Game of Status|Game of Status]], and play it hard. This is no contradiction with their honor-contempt. Honor is a *rule-bound* status system — it constrains permissible moves and asks you to lose rather than cheat — and Turkorcs reject precisely that self-limitation. The Game of Status is status with the rules removed: the only questions are whether a move worked and whether it was undeniable. A people who threw out honor as a frivolous handicap are naturally drawn to a status contest that has no handicap, and for a manufactured warrior-caster population the Game doubles as continuous fitness training.

Turkorc status is **cold and objective** — did you win, did you survive, was the play undeniable. The connoisseurship layer that more cohesive Varkhul groups may cultivate (betrayal admired as a fine art, with a shared canon of taste) has no purchase among Turkorcs, whose individualism leaves no room for a shared aesthetic.
