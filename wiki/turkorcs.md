# Turkorcs

**Summary**: The Turkorcs are wholly individualistic, powerful warriors and casters of [[varkhul|Varkhul]] — amoral, extremely dangerous, and contemptuous of honor. They are one of the principal reasons the [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] exists, and they are strictly contained to the prison continent.

**Sources**: Eadras Cannon Expansion.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-05-05

---

## Nature

Turkorcs are individualistic to a degree that other Eadras peoples find unsettling. There is no inherent collective duty in Turkorc culture, no clan-loyalty above self-loyalty, no expectation of obligation among them that survives any moment of self-interest. Where collective behavior exists, it is transactional — assembled for a purpose, dissolved when the purpose ends.

They are **powerful warriors and casters**. Magical aptitude is not exceptional among Turkorcs — it is widespread, and combined with their warrior tradition produces individuals capable of taking on most of what an unprepared world would send against them.

## Honor

Turkorcs **have no concept of honor and consider honor stupid**. This is not a learned cynicism or a fallen state — they regard the entire framework of honor as wasted thought. Promises, codes, oaths, codes of war, debts owed: in Turkorc reasoning these are levers used by the weak to constrain the strong, and the strong have no reason to pick them up.

This is the trait that most distinguishes Turkorcs from [[orcs|Orcs]] — the honor-bound warriors descended from [[orugi|Orug]] bloodlines. Where Orcs are legendarily honor-driven, Turkorcs are legendarily honor-rejecting. It is not clear from current canon whether Turkorcs descend from the same Orug heritage and rejected its honor inheritance, or whether they are an unrelated lineage.

## Amorality and danger

Turkorcs are **amoral and extremely dangerous**. They are not malicious in the ideological sense — they have no doctrine of cruelty, no pursuit of evil for its own sake. They simply do not weigh moral consequence as a factor in decisions. Whatever serves them is done; whatever does not is not.

The combination of individual power, magical proficiency, and amoral decision-making is what makes them genuinely dangerous to civilized peoples. A single Turkorc loose in a populated region is a serious problem; a band of them is a catastrophe.

## Strict containment

Turkorcs are **strictly contained on [[varkhul|Varkhul]]**. The [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]] exists in significant part to prevent them from reaching the wider world. Their containment is not negotiated, not phased, and not seen as cruel by the world's nations — it is treated as the only stable option.

The few historical breach incidents (see [[continental-ward#Open canon]]) provide the empirical basis for this stance.

## Relationship to other Varkhul inhabitants

Turkorcs share Varkhul with **[[dragonkin|dragonkin]]** and assorted **exiled clans**. Relations are not friendly. Turkorcs do not form coalitions easily, do not respect dragonkin territorial claims, and treat exiles as resources or obstacles depending on the day. The internal political ecology of Varkhul, to the extent there is one, includes Turkorcs as a perpetually destabilizing element.
