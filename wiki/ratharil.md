# Ratharil

**Summary**: Ratharil — officially **Rhtrakhadara** in [[draconic-language|Draconic]], "The Eternal Radiant Gathering" — is the City of Dragons. A genuine neutral city built by and for dragonkind across multiple periods of post-war recovery, raised from monolithic seamless stone fused by dragon breath and magic, home to the Council of 99 Eldest, the Treaty of Ratharil's population registry, and the singular Temple of all the gods that the dragons know about.

**Sources**: Playground generation 1st part.txt, May 1 revisions.txt, Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt, Draconic Capital City name change.txt, The Flesh-Eaters.txt, DragonsAndUnicorns_01_InWorld_Facts.txt, ForbiddenKindlingAndUnheldHand_01_InWorld_Facts.txt, conduits-towers-order.txt (2026-06-03, the conduit shrine — Ratharil is the one never-Tower conduit end)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-03

## Names

- **Common:** *Ratharil* — the surface-races' name, used in treaties translated for non-dragons and in mortal scholarship.
- **Draconic (official):** **Rhtrakhadara** — the name dragons themselves use, and the only proper name in formal Council speech and in [[draconic-language|Draconic]] writing. (source: Draconic Capital City name change.txt)

| Form | Use |
|---|---|
| **Rhtrakhadara** | Full formal name |
| *Rhtrakha* / *Rhtrakh* | Common short forms among dragons |
| *Rhtrakhadara, Seat of the Ninety-Nine* | Formal Council speech |
| *Rhtrakhadara RhTrKh* — "Eternal Convergence of the Fire" | Poetic / ritual register |

**Pronunciation:** Ruh-**TRAKH**-ah-**DAH**-rah — heavy emphasis on the second syllable, deep rumbling *kh*, rolling *r*. Spoken by an elder dragon, the name resonates like distant thunder.

**Etymology:** RhTrKh (Eternal / Ancient / Unyielding Memory) + Rh (Radiant / Noble / Gathering) + Dr (Guard / Stone / Enduring Place), under a vowel pattern meaning "a place where many come together in peace." Literal translation: *The Eternal Radiant Gathering*. Poetic dragon translation: *The Place Where All Fires Burn as One Without Consuming.*

How dragons speak of the city:

| Phrase | Register |
|---|---|
| "I fly to Rhtrakhadara" | Neutral, factual |
| "I seek the Fire at Rhtrakhadara" | Going for Council business or treaty |
| "The Flames of Rhtrakhadara do not devour their own" | Proverb on the city's peace injunction |

For the wider language — phonology, root template, naming conventions, and runic abjad script — see [[draconic-language]].

## Overview

Ratharil is not merely a treaty site or symbolic caldera. It is a genuine city built by [[dragons]], for dragons — with quarried stone buildings, meeting halls, streets wide enough for elder bodies, aerial terraces, landing courts, vaults, archives, towers, and neutral sanctuaries. Its existence proves that dragons are also architects, lawmakers, masons, archivists, and urban beings when necessity demands.

The city and the [[dragons|Treaty of Ratharil]] grew into one another. The treaty was not signed in a finished city; both developed simultaneously from a wary camp of elders into a civilization's spine. Ratharil is both place and promise. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Location

Ratharil sits atop a high basalt plateau surrounding a dormant volcanic throat, placed at the convergence of old draconic territories so that no single clan can claim the surrounding region as natural home. This neutrality is structurally enforced by geography.

Within flight, the city has access to:
- Mountains for Golds
- Dry lands for Blues and Reds
- River-fed wetlands for Blacks
- Forests for Greens
- Cold peaks for Whites
- Deep aqueducts leading to marine access for Silvers
- Old devastation fields for Coppers

(source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### Modern placement — the Northern Mountains of Halaran

On the modern world map, Ratharil's high basalt highlands are the **Northern Mountains of [[halaran|Halaran]]** — the cold, glacially scarred northern reach of the eastern continent (see [[eadras-world-map]]). The city sits at roughly **58°N** (map coordinate ~1332, 190). This is consistent with the [[#Construction timeline|founding horizon]] below: the Eldravath plateau that first hosted Ratharil rode the northern pole-crossing fragment that later fused into Halaran, carrying the city to its present northern latitude.

Its **antipode** — the point exactly opposite on the planet — is a cold coastal point on the sealed **Dark Continent** ([[varkhul|Varkhul]], the prison continent inside the [[continental-ward|Continental Ward]]) at ~58°S, *"the shore opposite Rhtrakhadara."* The dragons built their one neutral city as far from the forbidden land as the planet allows. What that antipodal coordinate signifies — a ward-meets-sea point, an unreachable pilgrimage mark, or a weak point in the seal — is open canon. (source: EADRAS WORLDBUILDING — MAP SESSION.txt, 2026-05-31)

## The conduit shrine

Ratharil sits atop one of the **fourteen [[seven-conduits|conduit]] ends** — a drain of the planetary [[eldritch-cycle|Eldritch cycle]], and so one of the richest standing pools of magic in the world. Of all fourteen ends, Ratharil is **the single one that has never carried a [[order-sharanel|Tower of Lirra]].** (user direction 2026-06-03)

Where every other Tower-bearing end is held by Lirra's humanoid [[order-sharanel|Order]], Ratharil's conduit is marked instead by a **shrine** — a primordial, dragon-made acknowledgment of the conduit and the divine, raised in the dragons' own ancient mode. Three things set it apart from the Towers:

- **It is dragon-made and dragon-kept.** A Tower of Lirra is built by and for Lirra's Order and human spellcasters; a dragon cannot use one, and the Order has no standing to build on sacred dragon-ground. The shrine is the dragons' own answer to the conduit at their door.
- **It is older than the Order.** The shrine predates Lirra's Order — and the Towers — by **millions of years.** It is the *primordial* form of conduit-acknowledgment, established long before the Order laid its first stone. There is **no Order presence at Ratharil.**
- **It expresses the dragons' own relationship with the gods.** Dragons keep an older, **direct** relationship with the divine — unmediated by the Order, the Towers, or any humanoid religious apparatus (the dragon-gods, the [[nine-embers-of-renewal|Nine Embers]], are themselves ascended dragons). The shrine is the outward sign of that relationship at the conduit-end.

The shrine and the Towers are thus **two separate lineages of conduit-relationship** — the dragon shrine primordial and dragon-divine, the Towers recent and human-Order — that merely sit at the same kind of cosmological feature. They are not two versions of one thing. The dragons were here, sacred and self-sufficient, for millions of years before the Order existed. The shrine stands in continuity with the rest of Ratharil's older religious life: the open-air personal worship that long predated [[#The Temple|the Temple]], and the silence kept at [[#The Court of the Unanswered Sun|the Court of the Unanswered Sun]]. (source: conduits-towers-order.txt, 2026-06-03)

## Architecture

A dragon city is not a human city enlarged. [[dragons|Dragons]] fly, coil, and perch. Ratharil's architecture is vertical and radial:

- Vast landing plazas
- Terraced roofs strong enough for Golds
- Deep cisterns and saltwater halls for Silvers
- Sunning courts for Reds and Coppers
- Mist gardens for Blacks
- Canopied stone conservatories for Greens
- Ice-vault embassies for Whites
- Resonance chambers for Blues
- Unpredictable, mutable festival courts for Purples

The buildings are genuine quarried stone, constructed rather than grown or conjured — giving dragons shared civic pride. Ratharil is proof that dragons can build something lasting without burning it down. A dragon insult: *"You have never laid stone in Ratharil."* (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### Scale

Every building in Ratharil is constructed on a **truly colossal scale** — comfortably accommodating multiple dragons moving around inside, perching on internal ledges, and conducting full-body ritual. The Council Chamber alone is **larger than any enclosed structure built by the younger races.** Outsiders permitted to enter the city often misjudge proportions: a chamber that reads as a temple plaza by human scale is a single hall by dragon reckoning. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

### Construction method

Ratharil was raised without mortar. Dragons quarried the building stone using **dragon breath, magic, and claw**, then **fused entire buildings into single monolithic pieces of solid stone** using dragon breath and magic. There is no mortar anywhere in the city — each structure is **one seamless, continuous mass of stone.** (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

The technique gives the city its characteristic appearance: walls that flow without seams into floors and roofs, internal arches grown from the same monolith as the chamber they support, and a hush in the corridors that comes from the absence of joint-noise — there are no joints to speak.

### Mosaics

Dragons have a particular love for **mosaics made from pretty colored rocks.** Many buildings feature intricate mosaics created from carefully selected stones of many different colors set into the monolithic walls. To a dragon's eye these are **breathtakingly beautiful**; surface races often find them strange or visually overwhelming. The mosaic tradition is the most public expression of the otherwise austere Ratharil aesthetic. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

The grandest mosaic in the city is in [[#The Temple|the Temple]] (see below).

### Construction timeline

Ratharil was not built in one sustained period. The city was raised across **multiple periods of recovery**, whenever the dragon population began to rebound after devastating wars. This is consistent with the destructive cycle that defined draconic prehistory (see [[dragons]]: war → recovery → stability → overcrowding → war), and with the Treaty's later attempt to break that cycle by capping the population at 999,999. The city's oldest cores predate the Treaty by a wide margin; later additions span the cycle's recovery peaks. The most recent significant building is the [[#The Temple|Temple]]. (source: May 1 revisions.txt)

**Founding horizon: ~6 million years before present.** The canonical timeline places Ratharil's founding around the same horizon as unicorn emergence (the **Silver Plains Flare** at ~6 mybp), on Eldravath itself. By this point dragons were ~2 million years old as a species and the supercontinent provided large stable plateaus on which to build. The earliest cores of Ratharil therefore date to the Eldravath landscape; the city expanded across each subsequent recovery cycle, surviving the Breaking of Eldravath at ~5 mybp and continuing on the fragment of the supercontinent that hosted it. (source: Updates afternoon 5-1-26.txt; user direction 2026-05-04 reverting unicorn dating to ~6mybp BP on Eldravath.)

## The Nine Clan Halls

Each color clan maintains a sovereign hall in Ratharil, subject to neutral-city law:

| Hall | Notable Features |
|---|---|
| **Hall of Red Stone** | Heat-dark basalt, geothermal vents, sparse and austere, many private ledges |
| **Hall of Blue Glass** | Desert stone and fused sand, crystalline memory chambers that hum when touched by lightning |
| **Hall of Green Root** | Quarried stone deliberately overgrown; hides itself from at least three angles |
| **Hall of Purple Chance** | Architecturally inconsistent by design; corridors bend unexpectedly; murals change |
| **Hall of Black Water** | Low, wide, humid; channels, pools, shadowed alcoves; dark river-stone |
| **Hall of White Breath** | Cold-vaulted, severe; guest chambers always stocked; hearths burn blue-white |
| **Hall of Copper Ash** | Part memorial, part forge, part ruin garden; stones from devastated regions built visibly into walls |
| **Hall of Silver Tide** | Connected by water tunnels and salt pools; lower chambers flooded for marine dragons |
| **Hall of Gold Height** | Most formal; elaborate guest laws; among Ratharil's most secure archives |

Each hall smells distinct — an important cultural dimension for sensory beings. The clan scent profiles are well-documented in council elder memory:

| Hall | Characteristic scent |
|---|---|
| **Red** | Mineral heat |
| **Blue** | Ozone and dry dust |
| **Green** | Sap and old rain |
| **Purple** | Different every visit |
| **Black** | Peat, tannin, and slow water |
| **White** | Snow, blood-warm food, and clean stone |
| **Copper** | Ash and metal |
| **Silver** | Salt and cold depth |
| **Gold** | Incense, mountain air, and polished scale-oil |

Hospitality protocol within each clan hall is similarly distinct. All nine clans practice some form of welcome toward same-color members, but the meaning varies dramatically:

- **Red** — heated cave and a carcass, often without conversation.
- **Blue** — formal recitation of lineage may be required before a guest chamber opens.
- **Green** — guests are often shown a hidden way in, then left to find their own corners of the canopy hall.
- **Purple** — the welcome itself is unpredictable; the host's mood determines the form.
- **Black** — hospitality is genuinely offered, but the host quietly tests whether the guest deserves to leave with all their possessions intact.
- **White** — *every* arriving clanmate must be fed and sheltered, even an enemy, because the ice permits no lesser law. A White who violates this risks becoming clanless.
- **Copper** — guests are taken among the memorial-stones and ruin-gardens; the welcome is part history lecture, part shared mourning.
- **Silver** — slow, formal, with long pauses; staid Silver hospitality is difficult for non-Silvers to read.
- **Gold** — ceremony so elaborate that young dragons need coaching from their elders to avoid grave breaches of etiquette.

(sources: Playground generation 1st part.txt; DragonsAndUnicorns_01_InWorld_Facts.txt)

## Neutral Zone Law

Ratharil is sacred neutral ground. No inter-clan vendetta may be prosecuted within its bounds. The laws include:

- No inter-clan vendetta within city bounds
- No dragon may hunt another's guest under neutral protection
- No egg may be destroyed within the city
- No duel except in sanctioned arenas
- No dragon may claim Ratharil as personal territory
- No mortal army may enter without Council invitation
- No clan hall may be besieged by another clan

These laws are magically enforced through oaths bound into the city stones. Breaking them causes consequences: loss of shapeshifting within the city, suppression of breath weapon, public scale-marking, or denial of Ratharil's protections. The city creates costs that even dragons respect — it does not mind-control them. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## The Temple

The **Temple** is the **newest building in the city** — and the only temple dragons have ever built. Originally, dragon and unicorn worship was personal and open-air; dragons built no temples for the entire span of their early religious life. Eventually, in the most recent recovery period, the dragons of Ratharil raised a single Temple, **dedicated to the worship of all the gods.** (sources: May 1 revisions.txt; see [[religion-in-eadras]] for the broader context)

The Temple's interior centerpiece is a **massive mosaic** — the most famous mosaic in Ratharil, and a defining work in the dragon stone-art tradition (see [[#Mosaics|Mosaics]]). The mosaic contains the **holy symbols of all the gods** the dragons know about (which is most, if not all), arranged in a **grand harmonious pattern**. This makes the Temple a **truly neutral sacred space** where any deity may be honored:

- Visiting clergy of any god may worship there.
- No deity is given primacy by altar position; the harmonious pattern is deliberate.
- The mosaic is updated when dragons learn of a god previously unknown to them — an unhurried, multi-generational process.

The Temple is a meaningful late-period statement by dragonkind: that the older form of personal open-air worship has not been abandoned, but that the city of dragons should also possess a single shared place where every god is acknowledged.

## Council of 99 Eldest

The **Council of 99 Eldest** is Ratharil's governing body and, insofar as dragons accept governance, the voice of dragonkind. It consists of the eleven oldest willing and capable living dragons from each of the nine color clans (11 × 9 = 99), honoring the sacred number beneath 999,999.

Age is the central qualification. Dragons automatically defer to those older than themselves — the council uses this instinct as political structure.

### The Council Chamber (Ninety-Ninefold Hall)

The council chamber is the **Ninety-Ninefold Hall**: a **vast, tiered dome** with **nine tiers, each designed to hold exactly eleven dragons** (totaling 99). The chamber is open to the sky at the central oculus, and its monolithic structure follows the city-wide construction pattern — a single seamless mass of black quarried stone fused by dragon breath and magic. Elders sit according to **age, not color**, on massive stone ledges arrayed around the tiers.

At the **center of the floor lies a speaking well** — the focal acoustic point from which a speaker addresses the entire Council. Beside it stands the **Ratharil Stone** — a single quarried slab bearing the nine progenitor names, magically tied to draconic bloodline and maintaining the population count. When a dragon dies, one mark dims. When an egg quickens, one mark ignites. The Council Chamber is **larger than any enclosed structure built by the younger races**. (sources: Playground generation 1st part.txt; May 1 revisions.txt)

### Tensions

Because dragons defer to age, younger reformers struggle to challenge elder policy. If an ancient dragon is wrong, the whole species may hesitate. Overriding an elder in the Hall of 99 is a psychologically difficult and culturally grave historic act. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### Notable standing Council action — the Flesh-Eaters

The Council of Ninety-Nine has formally **declared the [[flesh-eaters|Flesh-Eaters]] enemies of dragonkind.** The Flesh-Eaters are a small, secretive cult of traditionalist dragons who reject the Council itself, hold that all non-draconic sapients are prey, and **raid other dragons' nests to steal and indoctrinate hatchlings** — a direct violation of Ratharil's neutral-zone law against destroying eggs (see [[#Neutral Zone Law|above]]) and a deeper offense against draconic norms generally. The cult operates as small mobile cells from remote wilderness and ancient draconic ruins, and survives its own destruction through buried doctrinal caches that let it be reborn from a single new finder (motto: *"Always reborn"*); the Council's denunciation has not eradicated them. Speaking the name *Flesh-Eaters* in polite Ratharil company is considered extremely poor taste, and the Council itself usually refers to the matter by indirection. (source: Flesh eaters revised.txt)

## The Sealed Hall of First Flame

Beneath or behind the Council archives lies the **Sealed Hall of First Flame**, where the oldest records of the Nine Progenitors are stored. Its stones are lined with gold-veined black basalt that does not echo; no sound leaves it.

The records inside are layered:
- Draconic scale-script
- Psionic impressions learned from unicorns
- Elemental seals
- Divine redactions
- Empty spaces where names were removed

The deepest record is a protected memory-crystal called the **Shard of First Shell**: a fragment of one of the original nine eggshells that records the magical signature of the first Source Flare. Entry requires consent from at least one elder from each of three opposed clan blocs.

The Shard proves dragons were made. It does not teach how. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

### The Sealed Hall oath

Dragons admitted to the deepest level of Sealed Hall access — those few who hold the suspected truth of [[ixthal|Ixthal's]] [[doctrine-of-unheld-hand#The Great Violation|Forbidden Kindling]] — swear an oath whose phrasing has been preserved verbatim across very deep time. It is sworn before three opposed clan blocs, on the [[#Sealed Hall of First Flame|Shard of First Shell]], by every dragon granted entry:

> *By scale, by Source, by the nine first shells,*
> *I shall not call the Kindler by plea or command.*
> *I shall not teach the forbidden flare.*
> *I shall not unmake my people with the truth of their making.*
> *I shall carry the coal and close my jaws.*

The phrase *"carry the coal"* deliberately echoes [[three-last-lights|the Three Last Lights]] mythic line — *Ixthal carried the coal; the dragons who know the truth carry the coal of his secret in turn*. The oath has no metaphysical enforcement; it is honor-bound. The Sealed Hall's continuity across millions of years depends on the discipline of every oath-taker. (source: ForbiddenKindlingAndUnheldHand_01_InWorld_Facts.txt)

### Why divine seals exist on dragon-side artifacts

The Sealed Hall contains "empty spaces where names were removed by order of the gods," and the Shard of First Shell carries a divine seal that allegedly prevents anyone from deriving the Source-flare method from it. To the dragons who maintain the Hall, this attribution is correct: a god ordered the redactions, a god placed the seal. They do not know *which* god.

Per established canon, the Elder Gods do not know of the Forbidden Kindling. Lirra may have placed routine precautionary seals on the Shard for any high-Source-resonance artifact — that part is real, and it is hers. The targeted redactions and the specific seal *against the method* are [[ixthal|Ixthal's]] own work. Ixthal is himself a god; seals placed by a god are divine seals. The concealment is in *which* divine being placed them, not in whether they are divine. The dragons attribute the work to "the gods" in the plural; they are not wrong, only imprecise. (sources: ForbiddenKindlingAndUnheldHand_01_InWorld_Facts.txt; user direction 2026-05-13)

### The deep archive vaults

Beneath the Sealed Hall — and structurally connected to it — Ratharil maintains **deep, deep, deep vaults for archives**: layered subterranean chambers carved into the basalt under the city, accessible only by long descents through the monolithic stone. These archives are not the stuff of any one clan's memory; they are **the shared dragon record, kept for the benefit of all nine clans.** (User direction 2026-05-01.)

Standard format and protocol:

- **Magically preserved stone and metal tablets, written in Draconic scale-script.** Tablets are sized for dragon eyes and dragon claws; they are not designed to be read by smaller races, even if smaller races could be admitted.
- **Each clan posts an archivist permanently, on a rotating basis.** No single dragon is the archivist for more than a small fraction of geological time — the rotation prevents the post from becoming a clan power base, and gives many elders a turn at the deep work. The archivist's task is comprehensive: indexing new tablets, maintaining preservation magic, advising the Council when a current matter has historical precedent in the vaults.
- **Records of past elder-troll awakenings** are among the archive's standing entries. Each such awakening produced an episode of dragon experience worth preserving — and was duly recorded in tablet form. (See [[trolls]] for the troll-side of the relationship.)
- **A very small number of records** are not stone or metal but **memory crystals** — preserving the *entire memories* of the eldest dragons who experienced specific events firsthand. The crystal-recording technique was **gained from [[arathi|Arathi]] ruins**, making memory crystals a rare instance of Arathi-magitek heritage held continuously by dragons rather than by gnome golem-craft. The Council uses memory crystals sparingly: most events do not warrant full-memory preservation, and few dragons consent to having a complete memory recorded.

This is the venue in which the dragons' uneasy memory of past elder-troll awakenings is anchored — not folklore or rumor, but tablets and (in a few cases) memory-crystal recordings of dragon witnesses who were there.

### What Ratharil Knows

The Council of 99 does not know the full truth of Ixthal's Forbidden Kindling. The Sealed Hall contains relics, shell fragments, flare charts, contradictory myths, and unanswered anomalies. Dragons debate whether the First Great Flare was divine, natural, or something stranger.

Public draconic calendar uses **F.F. — First Flare** as the origin epoch. The forbidden archive adds: *"The pre-flare readings indicate A-class. Amplification began three breaths before zenith."* But the amplification is not definitively attributed. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## The Court of the Unanswered Sun

A silent sacred space in Ratharil's highest eastern terrace, where the first Source-light of dawn enters. No idol, no altar, no carved face, no roof. Just sunlight and silence.

To most dragons, it honors the First Great Flare and the mystery of draconic origin. To a very few obscure archivists, an older hidden inscription reads: *A silent god stood somewhere in the dawn.* This inscription is not official doctrine.

The public inscription reads: *From first light, scale. From sealed shell, sky. Ask not the sun why it burned.*

Dragons enter alone. They do not ask for favors. They sit beneath direct sunlight and remain silent — for one hour, one day, or one season depending on grief. Attempts to force a divine response are among Ratharil's greatest taboos. Young dragons dare each other to whisper requests there; elders react with horror. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## The Ixthal Taboo

Among dragons who know of [[ixthal]] at all (through esoteric elder traditions), a taboo exists:

**Do not call Ixthal lightly.**

Not because he will punish transgressors. Because asking him to look directly upon the world is cruelty. When Ixthal does attend to reality, the Source behaves strangely: solar halos, sudden pale flares, reptiles falling silent, unicorn herds turning their heads at once toward no visible threat, dragons feeling ancestral pressure behind the eyes. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)

## Draconic Calendar Epoch

Ratharil's official calendar uses **F.F. (First Flare)** as its origin. Dragon historians reconstruct astronomical records of the First Great Flare and debate endlessly whether it was natural, divine, or both. Public scholars are decades, sometimes centuries, behind the forbidden archive anomalies. (source: Playground generation 1st part.txt)
