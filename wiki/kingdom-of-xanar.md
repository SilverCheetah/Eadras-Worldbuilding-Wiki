# Kingdom of Xanar

**Summary**: The Kingdom of Xanar was [[xarvion|Xar'vion's]] pre-ascension realm â€” a real and legitimate kingdom built on funerary arts, remembrance, and the hidden obsession of its ruler to resurrect his dead sister, which served as the institutional foundation for the [[black-hand|Black Hand]].

**Sources**: Playground generation 2nd part.txt

**Last updated**: 2026-04-29

## Overview

Xanar was a genuine kingdom with towns, farms, laws, courts, temples, schools, roads, fortresses, scholars, poets, merchants, and ordinary families. It was not a cartoonish evil empire from its founding. Xar'vion's grief, ambition, and hidden cult corrupted it from within, but many Xanari citizens believed they lived in a strong, visionary realm seeking mastery over death for the good of all. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

After Xar'vion's ascension and transformation, hostile names arose:

- **The Black Dominion** â€” war name used by enemies
- **The Hand-Kingdom** â€” referring to the cult
- **Xanar the Hollow** â€” after Dao's curse
- **The Kingdom Under the Black Hand**

But the proper political name was always Xanar.

## Location and Character

Xanar occupied a strategically important region with:
- Strong roads and diplomatic reach
- Trade access and wealth
- Magical academies and research centers
- Military organization
- Access to ancient ruins and death-related sites
- A capital with major funerary and necromantic institutions

It likely controlled a crossroads between multiple peoples, which allowed the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] to spread under cover of diplomacy and commerce. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Public Ideology

Xanar's ideology centered on **order, continuity, remembrance, and the conquest of grief**.

Possible motto: *"No Name Forgotten."*

Innocent and even noble in intent â€” a reference to Xar'vion's original grief over his sister. After ascension and Dao's curse, the same motto became sinister:
- Names recorded for surveillance
- Dead catalogued for necromantic use
- Bloodlines tracked for leverage
- Oaths preserved as chains

## Magical Institutions

Xanar specialized in legitimate necromantic arts:
- Funerary preservation and corpse restoration
- Spirit communication and ancestral legal testimony
- Soul theory and memory crystals
- Anti-decay magic and relic recovery
- Comparative studies of divine death rites

None of this was inherently evil. Much was legitimate scholarship. But the [[black-hand|Black Hand]] redirected the highest levels toward Xar'vion's impossible goal: true resurrection of his older sister after Death had claimed her. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## The Capital: Xanareth

The capital city **Xanareth** was built of pale stone, black banners, funerary avenues, and monumental archives â€” not gothic for its own sake, but obsessed with permanence.

Notable locations:
- **The Hall of Names** â€” where births, marriages, deaths, and lineages were recorded
- **The Sister's Sepulcher** â€” built for Xar'vion's older sister [[velena|Velena]]
- **The Obsidian Court** â€” where royal decrees were issued
- **The Black Hand's Hidden Sanctum** â€” likely beneath a public memorial
- **The Colleges of Final Mercy** â€” legitimate necromantic and medical institutions later exposed as recruitment grounds
- **The Royal Ossuary Archives** â€” preserved ancestral remains and legal death-records

The Sister's Sepulcher was particularly significant: before ascension, it was a sympathetic public monument to a grieving ruler. After Dao's curse stripped Xar'vion's love, he stopped visiting. Priests noticed the change. Black Hand inner members noticed. That absence may have been one of the first signs something fundamental in him had been amputated. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Heraldry

Xanar's official heraldry was distinct from the Black Hand's symbol, allowing the kingdom to maintain a legitimate face:
- Possibly a silver crown above a black river
- A white tower over a closed tomb
- A pale flower associated with Velena, later used by the Black Hand as a recognition token

## After Xar'vion's Ascension

When Xar'vion became an Unrestricted Elder God, Xanar changed immediately at the center:
- Necromancers studying restoration were ordered to weaponize corpses
- Diplomats became blackmailers
- Death registries became soul-targeting maps
- Mourning houses became recruitment centers
- Memory crystals became surveillance devices
- Healing dependencies became loyalty chains

The Kingdom of Xanar became the staging ground for world conquest. During the war, its roads carried armies, its hidden cells opened gates, and its necromantic colleges fueled war engines.

When Xar'vion summoned the [[demon-war|Demon Prince]], Black Hand networks became demonic conduits. Xanar suffered terribly from the disaster its king unleashed. (source: Playground generation 2nd part.txt)

## Fall and Legacy

Xanar likely fell as a kingdom during or after the Demon War, but its people survived in fragments. Modern nations may descend from former Xanari provinces.

The term **"Xanari"** carries multiple modern meanings:
- Former citizen of the kingdom
- Descendant of refugees
- Loyalist to Xar'vion
- Practitioner of old funerary arts
- Ethnic or cultural identity
- Political slur or accusation of Black Hand ties

A modern Xanari-descended family might say: *"Our ancestors were victims of Xar'vion too."*

Survivor culture may preserve:
- Funerary songs for those conscripted into undead armies
- Hand-washing rituals to reject the Black Hand
- White gloves worn at weddings to symbolize clean vows
- Public name-recitation to reclaim "No Name Forgotten"
- Legal bans against secret mourning societies

## The Black Throne of Xanareth

The most-frequently-occupied location of Xar'vion's [[icons|Icon]] is beneath or within the ruins of Xanareth â€” a site known as **The Black Throne of Xanareth**, also called:

- The Still Court
- The Unmoving Court of Xanar
- The Black Throne

The Icon itself is **mobile**, however â€” Xar'vion is Total-Bound but not externally imprisoned, and the [[black-hand|Black Hand]]'s Inner Hand has moved the Icon repeatedly across ten thousand years (see [[black-hand]] and [[xarvion]]). The Black Throne is therefore the cult's nominal center and the Icon's home location, but where Xar'vion currently sits is a Fivefold Inner Hand secret. (sources: Playground generation 2nd part.txt; More corrections.txt; Black hand revised.txt)
