# The Free Cities

**Summary**: The Free Cities are a loosely-affiliated cluster of tropical city-states on the eastern continent ([[halaran|Halaran]]) with **no single sovereign** — a tropical Holy-Roman-Empire-*without-an-emperor*, sitting in the fertile equatorial band in the gap between the central mountains and the eastern polities. They are one of the [[halaran#The five trading cultures|five trading cultures]] of the eastern continent, grown rich on high-value luxury goods and on brokering the overland caravan routes. Their name is a hard-won political badge: they were owned by past empires and **broke free twice** — anciently from [[amunorians|Amunor]], and again, five millennia later, from the warlord [[bold#The Ismander Scar (~500 years ago)|Ismander]]. Having thrown off two sets of masters, they reflexively refuse to crown a new one.

## Geography and climate

The Free Cities occupy the **tropical / equatorial band** of [[halaran|Halaran]] — the equator runs just south of [[arathyn|Arathyn]] — on the **wet, fertile windward side** of the central transverse [[halaran#The Collision Range|Collision Range]]. Tropical fertility needs the rain, and the Free Cities sit where the rain falls.

Their position is also strategic: they lie **in the gap between the eastern mountains and the eastern polities**, which places them astride the overland trade. The two caravan passes through the Collision Range — a **northern** and a **southern** pass — are chokepoints, and the Free Cities are geographically positioned as **brokers of the land routes** that thread between them. (source: trading-cultures-halaran.txt)

## Economy — luxury goods and brokerage

Free Cities wealth rests on two pillars:

- **Extreme land fertility** producing **high-value luxury goods** — the tropical, high-margin, low-bulk commodities that maritime trade exists to move: spices, exotic fruits, hardwoods, dyestuffs, sugar, fine textiles, aromatics.
- **Brokerage of the overland routes** between the mountain passes and the eastern polities.

The combination makes them disproportionately rich for their size, and that wealth buys the **means to defend their freedom**: mercenaries, walls, and ships.

## Why "Free" — freedom won twice

The name is a political badge, not a geographic description. The Free Cities were **owned by past empires and broke free** — and they did so on two separate occasions, five millennia apart:

1. **The ancient liberation — from [[amunorians|Amunor]].** Plausibly at Amunor's fall in the [[anti-magic-crusade|Anti-Magic Crusade]] era, when peripheral cities seized independence rather than be inherited by the squabbling [[amunorians|Amunorian successor states]].
2. **The recent liberation — from [[bold#The Ismander Scar (~500 years ago)|Ismander]].** Roughly five centuries ago, when Ismander's one-generation continental empire — which had conquered the Free Cities among much else — **dissolved at his death** and the cities broke free again.

Freedom won twice, five millennia apart: ancestral and historically reaffirmed.

## Why they stay free — and un-unified

Having thrown off two sets of imperial masters, the Free Cities **reflexively refuse a single sovereign** — unification smells like the empire they escaped. Their fertility and wealth give them the **means** to defend that refusal; they will **combine militarily against an external threat** but will never **centralize**. The Holy-Roman-Empire-style refusal to unify and the fierce collective defense are the *same* impulse: *never again a single master, within or without.*

This is the sharp **contrast with the [[amunorians|Amunorian successor states]]**: both emerged from Amunor's collapse, to opposite fates. The successor states fractured and stayed weak, inheriting borders they cannot hold; the Free Cities broke clean and grew rich. The successors may well **resent** the Free Cities as the provinces that got away.

## The Thosh-cult frontier (open)

The breakaway [[snake-folk|snake folk]] of the **Thosh cult** seek a tropical home *away from* the northern dragon-lands — the warm, wet, equatorial-south reaches of the eastern continent, toward and near the equator. That places their sought territory close to, or brushing, the Free Cities' band. Whether the trading cultures know the Thosh cult exists, avoid it, or warily deal with it is open canon. (source: trading-cultures-halaran.txt; cross-reference snake-folk.txt)

- **Relations with the Thosh-cult snake folk** to their equatorial south.
